## Event Overview (drawn from the source)

The 1917 Marian apparitions at Fátima, Portugal, which Vallée analyzes in *The Invisible College* as a textbook UFO event. Three shepherd children, preceded (1915–16) by visits from an "Angel of Peace," reported monthly apparitions of a luminous "Lady" from May to October 1917, culminating in the "Miracle of the Sun" on October 13 before a crowd of ~70,000.

## Participants and Their Roles

- **The three children** (Lucia and her cousins) — percipients who received prophecy, fell into trance, and relayed messages.
- **The Lady / the globe of light** — the apparition, which Vallée reads as the UFO; even a skeptical priest called it "a heavenly vehicle."
- **The crowds** — growing from 50 to 70,000, including remote witnesses miles away.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Vallée catalogues the physical features matching UFO reports: a luminous globe/disk with falling-leaf and zig-zag motion, buzzing/humming sounds, heat waves, "angel hair" that dissolves on reaching the ground, a thunderclap and roaring departure, plus the psychic component — paralysis, amnesia, prophecy, conversions, and healings (e.g., the cure of Mario Godinho's mother's eye tumor). He notes the recurring motif of Earth as a "forbidden wasteland," the seriousness-plus-absurdity of the messages, and the children's instinct to keep the experience secret.
