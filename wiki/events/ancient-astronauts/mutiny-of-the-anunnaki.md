## Event Overview (from the source)

After laboring at the gold mines for **40 shars (144,000 years)**, the rank-and-file Anunnaki rebelled, refusing further toil ("No more!"). In the labour epic "When the gods as men," the gods convene and resolve the crisis by deciding to **create a 'primitive worker' (lulu)** to bear the yoke in their place — the immediate trigger for the creation of man.

## Participants and Their Roles

The Anunnaki — the laboring gods who mutiny; [[Enlil (Sitchin)]] — commander confronted by the revolt; [[Enki (Sitchin)]] — proposes the solution (a worker fashioned from an existing creature) and, with Ninhursag, carries it out.
