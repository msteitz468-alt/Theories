## Event Description from Source

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], TWA Flight 800 departed JFK Airport bound for Paris on July 17, 1996, with 230 people aboard. Shortly after receiving clearance to climb to cruising altitude, the Boeing 747 exploded and crashed into the Atlantic Ocean, killing everyone aboard. The official NTSB conclusion was that a center fuel tank ignition caused the explosion.

Jeffries alleges the plane was struck by a missile — most likely from the US Navy's Cooperative Engagement Capability system then running exercises in the area — and that the FBI and NTSB conducted a cover-up, suppressing over 150 eyewitness accounts of a streaking object hitting the plane, falsifying a CIA animation of events, and suppressing physical evidence.
