## Event Summary (per Berlitz & Moore)

In early July 1947, something crashed on a ranch near Roswell (Corona), New Mexico. The intelligence office of the **509th Bomb Group** at Roswell Army Air Field, alerted by rancher Mac Brazel via Sheriff George Wilcox, sent intelligence officer **Major Jesse Marcel** to recover debris. On July 8, base public-information officer Lt. **Walter Haut** issued a press release announcing the Army "was fortunate enough to gain possession of a disc" — carried worldwide by the Associated Press. Within hours a news blackout descended and a substitute story was issued: the object had been merely a **weather balloon**. Brig. Gen. **Roger Ramey** (Eighth Air Force), alerted by Deputy Air Force Chief Lt. Gen. **Hoyt Vandenberg**, ordered the debris flown via Fort Worth to Wright-Patterson, and went on the radio to reassure the public it was a downed balloon.

Per the authors, witnesses (ranchers, soldiers, a civil engineer, student archaeologists, law officers) described a second site with the remains of "half a dozen or so humanoid creatures, pale… about four feet tall," plus metallic wreckage Marcel called "nothing made on this earth" and "hieroglyphic-like" markings. A radio teletype to Albuquerque was cut off mid-transmission ("DO NOT TRANSMIT… STOP COMMUNICATION IMMEDIATELY"), and witnesses were told to "forget about it."
