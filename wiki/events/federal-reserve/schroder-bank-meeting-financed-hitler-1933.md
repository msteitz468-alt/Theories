## Event Overview (from the source)

Mullins (Ch. 7, "The Hitler Connection") states that by December 1932 Adolf Hitler faced ruinous debts and political decline, having "spent all the money from his usual sources." Quoting Otto Lehmann-Russbeldt's *Aggression*: "Hitler was invited to a meeting at the Schroder Bank in Berlin on January 4, 1933. The leading industrialists and bankers of Germany tided Hitler over his financial difficulties and enabled him to meet the enormous debt he had incurred ... In return, he promised to break the power of the trade unions," which Mullins says he did on May 2, 1933.

Per the source, present at the meeting were **John Foster Dulles and Allen W. Dulles** of Sullivan & Cromwell, "which represented the Schroder Bank." [[Allen Dulles]] "later became a director of J. Henry Schroder Company." Mullins frames the motive as profit: "if Hitler did not become Chancellor of Germany, there was little likelihood of getting a Second World War going, the war which would double their profits."

## Participants and Their Roles

The Dulles brothers (Schroder's U.S. counsel); J. Henry Schroder Bank; German industrialists and bankers; Hitler (recipient). Related: per the source, [[Montagu Norman]]'s Bank of England gave the Nazis financial backing.
