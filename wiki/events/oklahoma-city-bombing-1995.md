## Event Description from Source

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], the bombing of the Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building in Oklahoma City on April 19, 1995 — the second anniversary of the Waco fire — killed 168 people, including 19 children from the building's day care center. Timothy McVeigh, an ex-soldier, was convicted and executed for the crime; Terry Nichols was also convicted.

Jeffries alleges the bombing was a government-connected conspiracy in which McVeigh did not act alone, that the FBI had foreknowledge through informants including Carol Howe and Cary James Gagan, that physical evidence shows a fertilizer bomb alone could not have caused the structural damage, and that the trial was a politically predetermined railroad job.
