## Event Overview

On December 23, 2022, President Biden signed the National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2023, which contained 15 pages of UAP-specific legislation — the most extensive statutory treatment of the subject in US history to that point. The legislation established legal framework for UAP reporting, whistleblower protections, NDA waivers (allowing cleared personnel to discuss UAP evidence with Congressional oversight committees), and the formal authorization of AARO (All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office).

Senator Harry Reid, who had championed UAP legislation since funding AAWSAP in 2008, died shortly after President Biden signed the bill into law. Senator Marco Rubio was a key architect of the whistleblower and NDA waiver provisions.

The UAP Task Force (UAPTF), established in 2020 with Jay Stratton as its first director, had reported over 400 UAP cases by the time the May 2022 public Congressional hearing occurred. AARO was formally established in July 2022 to replace the UAPTF.
