## Event Overview

Between December 26 and 28, 1980, US Air Force personnel stationed at RAF Bentwaters and RAF Woodbridge in Suffolk, England, encountered a UAP in Rendlesham Forest on multiple nights. The base housed US tactical nuclear weapons at the time. Witnesses included Staff Sergeant Jim Burroughs and Staff Sergeant John Penniston, among others. The incident is documented in official USAF records including a memo by Lt. Col. Charles Halt.

Elizondo references Rendlesham primarily in two contexts within *Imminent*: (1) as a UAP-nuclear interface event (Chapter 4), given the presence of nuclear weapons at the base, and (2) as a documented biological effects case (Chapter 10) — Burroughs suffered permanent cardiac damage linked to his proximity to the UAP, and a Congressional intervention was ultimately required to obtain VA medical treatment for his injuries.
