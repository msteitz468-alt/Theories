## Event Overview (from the source)

During a **2002 archaeological dig** at the historic Mac Brazel / Roswell debris site, [[Chuck Zukowski]] and his sister [[Debbie Ziegelmeyer]] recovered a small metallic fragment they describe as the only such artifact obtained "under pristine conditions" at the proposed site. SEM analysis suggested a damaged aluminum-silicon alloy. Unable to fund deeper laboratory analysis, Zukowski went public via a **Roswell press conference (2008/2009)**, posing three open questions about whether the fragment was extraterrestrial, a military remnant, or evidence the site had been "cleaned." The release quoted Apollo 14 astronaut Edgar Mitchell and drew modest UFO-community attention but little mainstream coverage.

## Participants and Their Roles

[[Chuck Zukowski]] and [[Debbie Ziegelmeyer]] — recovered and publicized the artifact; the City of Roswell museum PR department — drafted the release (partly to promote the July 4th festival); Edgar Mitchell — quoted endorser.
