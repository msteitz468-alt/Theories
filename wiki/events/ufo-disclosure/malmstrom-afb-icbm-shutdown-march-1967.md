## Event Overview

In March 1967, while a UAP was observed in the vicinity of Malmstrom Air Force Base, Montana, multiple Minuteman ICBMs in their launch facilities went offline simultaneously. No human command or known equipment failure caused the shutdown. Elizondo presents this as the earliest major documented case of UAP interference with US nuclear weapons systems.

The event is part of a three-event pattern documented by AATIP: Malmstrom (1967, US missiles disabled), Rendlesham (1980, British nuclear base incursion with witness biological effects), and Ukraine (1982, Soviet ICBMs activated without command).
