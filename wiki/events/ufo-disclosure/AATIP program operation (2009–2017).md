## Event Overview

AATIP (Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program) operated as a classified Pentagon program from approximately 2009 (when Elizondo joined) through his resignation on October 4, 2017. It was preceded by AAWSAP (Advanced Aerospace Weapons System Applications Program), the DIA-funded program with $22 million in Congressional appropriations championed by Senators Harry Reid, Ted Stevens, and Daniel Inouye. AAWSAP contracted with Bigelow Aerospace Advanced Space Studies (BAASS) and commissioned 38 Defense Intelligence Reference Documents (DIRDs) from academic researchers.

Elizondo operated AATIP from Pentagon office 3C503A. During his tenure the program reviewed hundreds of UAP cases worldwide, developed the Five Observables analytical framework, documented biological effects on witnesses, reviewed classified material on crash retrievals, and investigated the Nimitz (2004) and Roosevelt (2014–2015) carrier encounters. Elizondo worked directly with Jay Stratton, Harold Puthoff, Jim Lacatski, Robert Bigelow, and Christopher Mellon.

The program ended for Elizondo on October 4, 2017, when he resigned and delivered his resignation letter to SECDEF Mattis. The DoD subsequently denied his role in AATIP through spokesperson Christopher Sherwood.
