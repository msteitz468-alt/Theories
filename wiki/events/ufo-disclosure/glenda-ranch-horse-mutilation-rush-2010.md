## Event Overview (from the source)

On the ranch of a third-generation rancher named **Glenda** near Rush, Colorado, two mature horses (Princess and Buck) were found dead and mutilated, with a surviving traumatized horse and dog bearing quarter-sized burn-like marks. [[Chuck Zukowski]] — alerted by TV reporter Andy Koen, not by the department — investigated days later. He documented the signature mutilation pattern (bloodless carcasses, eyes/tongue/organs excised by precise cuts, no tracks or scavenger signs) and recorded anomalous EMF readings rising toward the skulls. Investigating county officers had offered "predator" and then "lightning strike" explanations, which Glenda rejected. The book states Zukowski subsequently **lost his reserve-deputy position** over his public involvement in the case.

## Participants and Their Roles

[[Chuck Zukowski]] — independent investigator; Glenda — rancher and witness; Andy Koen (KKTV) — reporter who tipped Zukowski; county sheriff's officers — offered the conventional explanations.
