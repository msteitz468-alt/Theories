## Event Overview

In late December 2023, President Biden signed the UAP Disclosure Act as part of the FY2024 National Defense Authorization Act. The act was sponsored by Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer (New York), Senator Mike Rounds (South Dakota), Senator Marco Rubio, and Senator Kirsten Gillibrand. House opposition led by Rep. Mike Turner (whom Elizondo identifies as closely aligned with Legacy Program contractors) stripped the proposed presidential review board from the legislation.

Key provisions as signed: government eminent domain over nonhuman biological evidence and nonhuman-origin technology held by contractors; National Archives collection mandate for all UAP records; 25-year public disclosure timeline for UAP documents; prohibition on unauthorized Legacy Program funding from Congressional appropriations.

Senator Schumer publicly stated that the House's failure to adopt the review board proposal was "really an outrage" that left declassification "largely up to the same entities that have blocked and obfuscated their disclosure for decades."

Elizondo spent much of fall 2023 traveling privately to Washington to assist Senate leadership in clarifying the evidence record for legislative drafting, working alone in SCIFs with senators.
