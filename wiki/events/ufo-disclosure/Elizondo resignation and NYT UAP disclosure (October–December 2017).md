## Event Overview

On October 4, 2017, Luis Elizondo resigned from the Department of Defense, delivering his resignation letter directly to SECDEF James Mattis. Elizondo had been unable to secure a briefing for Mattis, blocked by three gatekeepers in the SECDEF's office. He cited the institutional failure to address the UAP threat as the reason for his departure. AATIP colleague Garry Reid threatened him after the resignation.

Elizondo immediately joined To The Stars Academy (TTSA), co-founded by Tom DeLonge, joining Hal Puthoff and Steve Justice. He connected with New York Times reporters Leslie Kean and Ralph Blumenthal, who had been investigating the AATIP story. On December 16, 2017, the New York Times and Politico simultaneously published stories on AATIP. The Times article included the FLIR (Tic Tac) video.

The DoD confirmed Elizondo's AATIP role through spokesperson Dana White in initial responses to the December 2017 stories, before reversing that position under subsequent spokesperson Christopher Sherwood.

An Air Force Office of Special Investigations (AFOSI) inquiry into the video declassification process found no impropriety. Elizondo's electronic files were allegedly deleted from Pentagon servers after his departure.
