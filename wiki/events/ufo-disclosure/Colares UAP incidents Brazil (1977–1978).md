## Event Overview

Between 1977 and 1978, the island of Colares and surrounding communities in Pará, Brazil, experienced a prolonged wave of UAP incidents. Craft of various shapes emitted intense beams of light that struck civilians, causing burns, puncture wounds, and symptoms consistent with radiation exposure. The Brazilian Air Force conducted an official investigation designated Operation Prato (Operation Saucer), led by Lt. Col. Hollanda. According to AATIP case files reviewed by Elizondo, approximately 10 people died, more than 300 experienced documented biological injuries, and 371 distinct incident locations were catalogued.

Lt. Col. Hollanda later gave an extensive interview about his investigation findings before dying under disputed circumstances.

## Participants and Their Roles

Elizondo describes learning about the Colares events in a briefing-style dinner attended by himself, General Paulo Roberto Uchôa (Brazilian Air Force, ret.), Robert Bigelow, and Hal Puthoff. General Uchôa provided the scope of the Brazilian investigation. Bigelow and Puthoff were present in their capacity as civilian researchers with deep access to international UAP case files.
