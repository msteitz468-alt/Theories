## Event Overview

During pre-deployment workup exercises conducted by the USS Theodore Roosevelt Carrier Strike Group along the US East Coast in 2014 and 2015, Navy personnel documented 22 separate UAP encounters. The encounters were detected on newly upgraded AN/SPY-1 Aegis radar systems — the radar upgrades are credited with making the objects detectable where previous equipment could not resolve them — and were confirmed visually by multiple pilots.

Notable encounters include: a transparent orb with a visible internal cube that split a fighter formation; the object captured in the GoFast video (approximately 18-foot object at low altitude moving with unusual velocity); and the fleet of five lenticular craft captured in the GIMBAL video, including one craft rotating in a 120-knot wind without aerodynamic disruption.

In response to the Roosevelt encounters, Elizondo and Jay Stratton developed OPLAN Interloper — a proposal to use a nuclear-capable asset as bait to lure UAP to a controlled engagement zone for closer observation. The Joint Staff rejected the operation.
