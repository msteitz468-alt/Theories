## Overview from Source

According to Elizondo, in May 2022, Congress held what he describes as a "historic public hearing on UAP," fulfilling one requirement of a UAP bill signed into law by President Biden in late 2021. The hearing lasted ninety minutes.

## Key Developments Reported by Elizondo

Elizondo characterizes the hearing as monumental because it was the first such public hearing. He states that DoD witnesses at the hearing revealed they now had more than 400 UAP cases logged in the preceding year — compared to the 143 unresolved cases covered in the earlier 180-day government report. According to Elizondo, the hearing "confirmed UAP are indeed real and not a glitch of technological systems or a weather anomaly" and confirmed "UAP are not our technology and are a potential threat to air safety and our national security."

Elizondo notes several specific disclosures from the hearing:

- When asked about research into other UAP programs, Ronald Moultrue (identified as "the head of the Pentagon's intelligence efforts") stated under oath: "Other than AATIP and Blue Book, no." Elizondo calls this "a silent victory" because it constituted official Pentagon acknowledgment of his old program, AATIP.
- Congressman Mike Gallagher introduced the Wilson/Davis memo into the Congressional Record on national television during the hearing.
- The Pentagon admitted it was unaware of UAP incursions near sensitive nuclear facilities, including instances of ICBMs being taken offline, despite the fact that — as Elizondo notes — the Pentagon itself had written reports on these incursions. Elizondo characterizes this as "an embarrassing moment for my old office, OUSD(I)."

Elizondo also states that the hearing "made it clear to many civilians and elected leaders that they needed to press the UAP issue going forward, and that DoD was covering up the topic."

## Context in the Source

The hearing is presented by Elizondo as part of a sustained years-long effort, conducted by himself and colleague Christopher Mellon, to bring UAP disclosure before Congress and the American public following Elizondo's 2017 resignation from the Pentagon in protest. Elizondo notes that he contributed language to UAP legislation and that the hearing was one product of that legislative campaign. He also connects the hearing to the subsequent July 2022 announcement of AARO (the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office), the new permanent UAP reporting office required to report to Congress.
