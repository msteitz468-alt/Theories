**Event Overview**

During afternoon watch (1200–1600 hours) while serving as port lookout aboard the submarine USS Tiru (SS-416), William Cooper observed a large disk-shaped metallic craft emerge from the Pacific Ocean, tumble, and re-enter the clouds. The craft appeared and disappeared multiple times over approximately ten minutes. Multiple witnesses were present including the Officer of the Deck (Ensign Ball) and the ship's captain, who personally took the conn. The incident was photographed by Chief Quartermaster Quintero using the ship's 35mm camera.

Upon return to Pearl Harbor, all witnesses were confined to the ship and interrogated by an ONI commander, who required signed non-disclosure agreements threatening fines up to $10,000 and up to ten years imprisonment.

**Participants and Their Roles**

Per Cooper's account:
- **Cooper** — port lookout, first to observe the craft, primary narrator
- **"Geronimo"** — starboard lookout, second confirmed witness after Cooper's alert
- **Ensign Ball** — Officer of the Deck, called captain to bridge, confirmed the sighting
- **The captain** — personally took the conn, ordered photography and radar tracking, confiscated the camera afterward
- **Chief Quartermaster Quintero** — took 35mm photographs of the craft
- **Unnamed ONI Commander** — boarded at Pearl Harbor, conducted intimidation-style debriefings, took signed non-disclosure agreements
- **Sonar operator** — confirmed underwater contact at the same bearing
