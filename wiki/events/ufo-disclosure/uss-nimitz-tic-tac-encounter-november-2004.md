## Event Overview

On November 14, 2004, during training exercises by Carrier Strike Group 11 approximately 100 miles southwest of San Diego, USS Princeton's Combat Information Center vectored fighters toward a UAP contact that the cruiser's upgraded Aegis radar had been tracking intermittently for more than two weeks. The contact had been observed descending from above 80,000 feet to approximately 50 feet above the ocean surface, then ascending again, repeatedly and without any associated aircraft signatures.

Cmdr. Dave Fravor (FASTEAGLE 01) and Lt. Cmdr. Alex Dietrich (FASTEAGLE 02, callsign Noodle) were scrambled to investigate. Fravor visually acquired a white, oblong, roughly 40-foot object shaped like a large Tic Tac, hovering over churning water with no apparent propulsion. When Fravor maneuvered to intercept, the object accelerated away at extreme speed — no sonic boom, no exhaust, no visual propulsion. The Tic Tac reappeared at the prearranged CAP rendezvous point (a classified coordinate) approximately 60 miles away. Cmdr. Jim Slaight in a second aircraft witnessed the object from distance. Kevin Underwood, a weapons systems officer, captured FLIR infrared video of the object subsequently.

The carrier strike group comprised: USS Nimitz (CVN-68), USS Princeton (CG-59), USS Higgins (DDG-76), USS Chafee (DDG-90), and USS Louisville (SSN-724). Princeton's radar operators had tracked more than 100 UAP objects over the two-week period preceding the visual encounter.
