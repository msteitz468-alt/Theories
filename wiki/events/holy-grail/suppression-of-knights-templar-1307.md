**Event Summary**

On Friday 13 October 1307, King Philip IV of France ordered the mass arrest of Knights Templar throughout France. Pope Clement V formally dissolved the order at the Council of Vienne in 1312. The last Grand Master, Jacques de Molay, was burned at the stake in 1314.

In *The Holy Blood and the Holy Grail*, the suppression is treated as the culmination of secular and papal pressure on an order that had accumulated enormous wealth and influence. The source argues the Prieuré de Sion had already formally separated from the Templars at the Cutting of the Elm (1188), and therefore survived the suppression as a distinct entity while the Templars were destroyed.
