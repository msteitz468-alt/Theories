## Event Description from Source

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], the fifty-one-day standoff between federal agents and the Branch Davidian religious sect, led by David Koresh, at their Waco, Texas, residence. The siege began on February 28, 1993, when ATF agents assaulted the compound to serve a warrant on weapon-stockpiling and child-abuse charges. After the initial confrontation, the FBI took over in a "siege" lasting until April 19, 1993, when the compound was destroyed by fire, killing approximately 86 people including 24 children.

Jeffries alleges the assault was unjustified, the child-abuse charges were later acknowledged to be baseless by the Justice Department's own report, that incendiary devices were used contrary to FBI denials (admitted in 1999), and that the government bore full responsibility for the deaths.
