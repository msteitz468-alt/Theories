## Event Overview (from the source)

Per Quigley, on February 5, 1891, Cecil Rhodes and W. T. Stead "organized a secret society of which Rhodes had been dreaming for sixteen years." In it, "Rhodes was to be leader; Stead, Brett (Lord Esher), and Milner were to form an executive committee; Arthur (Lord) Balfour, (Sir) Harry Johnston, Lord Rothschild, Albert (Lord) Grey, and others were listed as potential members of a 'Circle of Initiates'; while there was to be an outer circle known as the 'Association of Helpers' (later organized by Milner as the Round Table organization)." Brett accepted the same day, Milner a couple of weeks later; "Thus the central part of the secret society was established by March 1891."

Quigley traces the society's funding (Rhodes's fortune, Beit, Bailey), its purpose (to "federate the English-speaking peoples and to bring all the habitable portions of the world under their control"), and its growth into the Round Table Groups, the Royal Institute of International Affairs, and the Council on Foreign Relations.

## Participants and Their Roles

Cecil Rhodes (leader, financier), W. T. Stead (co-founder), Alfred Milner (executive committee, later organizer of the Round Table), with Lord Rothschild among the "Circle of Initiates."
