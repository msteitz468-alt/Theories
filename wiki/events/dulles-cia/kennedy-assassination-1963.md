## Event Overview

The assassination of President John F. Kennedy in Dealey Plaza, Dallas, on November 22, 1963; his alleged assassin Lee Harvey Oswald was himself shot dead by Jack Ruby two days later in the Dallas police basement. Talbot presents the killing as the culmination of his deep-state thesis: an "establishment crime" with Allen Dulles as the figure who possessed "the stature, connections, and decisive will" to organize it.

## Participants and Their Roles (per Talbot)

- **Allen Dulles** — alleged organizing hand; spent the assassination weekend at "the Farm," a CIA facility in Virginia, despite having been removed from the agency two years earlier.
- **Lee Harvey Oswald** — argued to be a likely-innocent intelligence "patsy."
- **William King Harvey / David Morales / E. Howard Hunt** — named in Hunt's deathbed account as operational figures of "the big event."

## Source-Specific Portraits

Talbot marshals: Oswald's "patsy" claim and intelligence behavior; ballistic/forensic inconsistencies; the suppressed CIA photo-analysis conclusion of conspiracy; foreign-press skepticism; and de Gaulle's private judgment that Oswald was "a front" and "security forces were in cahoots with the extremists."
