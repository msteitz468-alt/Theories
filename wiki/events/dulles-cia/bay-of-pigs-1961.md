## Event Overview

The failed CIA-organized invasion of Cuba by exile forces in April 1961, intended to topple Fidel Castro. Per Talbot it was "the biggest disaster in the CIA's history" and "the blackest day" of Dulles's life, leading directly to his forced retirement.

## Participants and Their Roles

- **Allen Dulles / Richard Bissell** — planned and ran the operation; Talbot argues the CIA expected to "lure" Kennedy into committing U.S. military force once the invasion faltered.
- **John F. Kennedy** — refused to escalate, publicly took responsibility, and afterward moved to remove Dulles.

## Source-Specific Portraits

In unpublished drafts ("My Answer to the Bay of Pigs"), Talbot says, Dulles inadvertently revealed how the CIA "contrived to lure the young president" into Cuba, confident JFK would fall in line — but "this time, the president… stood his ground." Talbot argues Dulles's reign nonetheless continued "deeply cloaked."
