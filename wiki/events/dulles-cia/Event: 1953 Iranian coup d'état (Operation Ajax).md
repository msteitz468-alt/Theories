## Event Overview

The CIA-and-MI6 coup that overthrew elected Iranian Prime Minister [[Mohammad Mossadegh]] on August 19, 1953, and restored Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi, after Mossadegh nationalized the Anglo-Iranian Oil Company. Per Talbot, Dulles considered it one of the two greatest triumphs of his career.

## Participants and Their Roles

- **Kermit "Kim" Roosevelt Jr.** — ran the operation on the ground, bribing officers and hiring mobs.
- **Allen Dulles** — monitored from the Rome embassy near the Hotel Excelsior, where he arrived the same day as the exiled Shah to "stiffen the shah's spine"; flew the Shah back to Tehran.
- **Mossadegh** — arrested, tried for treason, and confined; died under house arrest in 1967.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Talbot stresses Dulles's personal/financial stakes (Sullivan & Cromwell oil clients; J. Henry Schroder board seat; the killed Overseas Consultants Inc. deal) and the violence (loyalist General Afshartous murdered). Afterward Iran's oil was denationalized (40% to U.S. firms) and the Shah's SAVAK, with CIA help, crushed the Tudeh Party.
