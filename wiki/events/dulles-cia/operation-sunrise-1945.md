## Event Overview

Operation Sunrise was the secret surrender negotiation that OSS Bern chief Allen Dulles conducted with SS general [[Karl Wolff]], commander of SS forces in Italy, in the war's final months. Per Talbot, Dulles aimed to secure a separate German surrender in northern Italy before the Red Army reached the region, blocking Soviet/Communist influence in postwar Italy — in defiance of FDR's unconditional-surrender policy and an explicit Washington order to break off contact with Wolff.

## Participants and Their Roles

- **Dulles** — orchestrated the negotiations, misrepresenting himself to Wolff as President Roosevelt's "personal representative."
- **Karl Wolff** — Himmler's former chief of staff; on April 26, 1945, Dulles dispatched his aide Gero von Schulze-Gaevernitz and OSS agent Don Jones on a cross-border mission to rescue Wolff from Italian partisans at the Villa Locatelli, Lake Como.
- **Lyman Lemnitzer** — U.S. Army point man on the talks; later JCS chairman under Kennedy.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Talbot says Sunrise "saved few lives and had little impact on the course of the war," but deepened Stalin's distrust of the West (some German divisions were reportedly told to prepare to fight the Red Army alongside the Allies) and became "Allen Dulles's creation myth." Truman tried unsuccessfully to shut it down.
