## Event Overview

The presidential commission appointed by Lyndon Johnson to investigate the Kennedy assassination, which concluded Oswald acted alone. Talbot argues Allen Dulles — appointed despite having been fired by Kennedy — effectively steered it toward that conclusion and away from the CIA; "the man who should have been in the witness chair wound up instead in control of the inquiry."

## Participants and Their Roles

- **Allen Dulles** — commissioner; alleged to have worked with former CIA colleagues to direct the inquiry.
- **John McCloy** — commissioner; quoted that the goal was to "lay the dust… all over the world."
- **Lyndon B. Johnson** — appointed the commission; Talbot disputes LBJ's later claim that RFK requested Dulles and McCloy.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Talbot pairs the Commission with Dulles's parallel campaign to suppress Harry Truman's December 22, 1963 *Washington Post* op-ed criticizing the CIA, including a falsified Dulles memo claiming Truman disowned his own article.
