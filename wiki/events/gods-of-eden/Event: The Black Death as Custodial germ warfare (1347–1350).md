## Event Overview (drawn from the source)

The 14th-century Bubonic/pneumonic plague that killed roughly a third of Europe (25–40 million), which Bramley reinterprets as a Custodial biological-warfare campaign. He assembles period records (largely via Johannes Nohl) in which outbreaks were preceded by aerial lights/"comets," foul "mists" and "pestilential fogs," and "men dressed in black" wielding scythe-like spraying instruments.

## Participants and Their Roles (per Bramley)

- **The Custodial society** — alleged source of the aerial phenomena and the disease-laden mists.
- **The "men in black"** — figures seen on town outskirts before outbreaks, whose scythes Bramley reads as aerosol sprayers and as the origin of the Grim Reaper image.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Bramley ties the event to the Mesopotamian tablets' Custodial plagues and defoliation, and to the Biblical plagues of Egypt. The aftermath — Inquisition, the massacre of Jews falsely blamed for the plague (often instigated by Brotherhood-descended trade guilds), and apocalyptic fervor — sets up his account of the Reformation.
