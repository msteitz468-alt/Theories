**Geographical and Historical Context from Source**

Dulce is the northern New Mexico town that, in *The 37th Parallel*, anchors the most intense cluster of "high strangeness" Zukowski investigates. The book's opening (Ch. 1) places his 2000 nighttime sighting — arcing lights, drained camera batteries, a screaming animal "run down" in the dark — on tribal land in the mountains controlled by local Native American tribes near Dulce, a site he returns to for the deliberately ambiguous climax. Mezrich notes that [[Robert Bigelow (aerospace entrepreneur)|Bigelow]]'s [[National Institute for Discovery Science (NIDS)]], after Skinwalker Ranch, "had begun investigating another location ... near Dulce, New Mexico," which "some of his scientists believed ... had clearer connections to some sort of secretive or military project, perhaps involving underground facilities."

**Textual Appearances**

The framing chapters (Ch. 1 and the climactic chapter), and the discussion of underground-base/tunnel theories Zukowski entertained (linking Area 51, Cheyenne Mountain, White Sands, Los Alamos and Dulce).
