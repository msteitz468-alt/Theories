**Geographical and Historical Context from Source**

Eridu is, in Sitchin's account, the **first settlement the Nefilim built on Earth** — the "Earth Station" established by [[Enki (Sitchin)]] in southern Mesopotamia at the head of the Persian Gulf ~445,000 years ago, when the Gulf's waters reached much farther inland. Sitchin notes that Eridu is also remembered as the first Sumerian city (excavated as Tell Abu Shahrein), and that the Sumerian king lists begin "when kingship was lowered from Heaven" with two long-reigning kings at Eridu (A.LU.LIM, 28,800 years; A.LAL.GAR, 36,000 years).

**Textual Appearances**

Featured in the landing narrative (Ch. 9), the cities-of-the-gods sequence (Ch. 10), and the pre-Diluvial king lists (Ch. 6). It anchors the chronology table that culminates in the Deluge.
