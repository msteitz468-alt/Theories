## Geographical and Historical Context from Source

The source identifies Stenay (formerly called Satanicum) as "the old capital of the Merovingian dynasty, near which Dagobert II was assassinated in 679." It is described as being located "on the fringe of the Ardennes."

## Role in the Source's Narrative

The source emphasises Stenay as one of two locations (alongside Gisors) that the "Prieure documents" have stressed repeatedly as being of significance commensurate with Rennes-le-Château itself: "One of these is Gisors, a fortress in Normandy... The other is Stenay, once called Satanicum, on the fringe of the Ardennes — the old capital of the Merovingian dynasty, near which Dagobert II was assassinated in 679."

## Connection to Dagobert II

The source describes how Dagobert II, the last direct Merovingian king before the Carolingian usurpation, was assassinated while hunting near Stenay — pierced through the eye by a lance — on December 23, 679. The source presents this assassination as having been carried out with the complicity of the Roman Church. Stenay appears in the source's account of Dagobert's biography and of the subsequent efforts to suppress knowledge of his bloodline.

## Orval and Stenay

The source notes that in 1070 a band of Calabrian monks arrived in the Ardennes and "obtained the patronage of Mathilde de Toscane, Duchess of Lorraine." From her they received "a tract of land at Orval, not far from Stenay, where Dagobert II had been assassinated some five hundred years earlier." The source treats the geographical proximity of Orval to Stenay as potentially significant to its thesis about the continuity of the Merovingian bloodline and the Prieuré de Sion.

## Church of Saint Dagobert at Stenay

The source's index notes "Dagobert, Saint, Church of (Stenay), 264," indicating that Dagobert II was venerated as a saint at Stenay and that a church was dedicated to him there.

## Edouard de Bar and Stenay

The source notes in its appendix on the alleged Grand Masters of the Prieuré de Sion that Edouard de Bar "on attaining his majority he purchased the seigneury of Stenay from one of his uncles, Jean de Bar" — connecting Stenay to the chain of families the source links to both the Merovingian bloodline and the Prieuré.
