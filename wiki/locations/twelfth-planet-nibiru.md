**Geographical and Historical Context from Source**

The "Twelfth Planet" is the planet Sitchin claims the Sumerians knew as a member of the solar system, the home world of the [[Nefilim (Sitchin)]]. The count of twelve comes from the Sumerian reckoning of the Sun, the Moon, and ten planets (the nine known plus this extra body). Sitchin names it **Nibiru**, "the Planet of the Crossing," and identifies it with **Marduk** of the *Enuma Elish*. He describes it as a large planet on a vast, comet-like elliptical orbit: its perihelion brings it through the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter (the site of the ancient collision with Tiamat), while its aphelion lies far beyond Pluto. Per Sitchin the planet generates its own internal heat and may sustain an atmosphere and life.

**Textual Appearances**

The planet is read out of cylinder seal VA/243 (a twelve-body solar-system depiction), the *Enuma Elish*, and astronomical texts. Its **orbital period is 3,600 Earth-years** (the [[Shar (3,600-year orbital period)]]), which Sitchin equates with the Sumerian *shar* and with the reign-lengths of the pre-Diluvial kings.
