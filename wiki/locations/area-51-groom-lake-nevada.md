## Geographical and Historical Context from Source

Jacobsen describes Area 51 as the nation's most secret domestic military facility. It sits inside the Nevada Test and Training Range, approximately five miles northeast of the northernmost corner of the Nevada Test Site. Facilities were constructed over roughly sixty years (as of publication) around Groom Lake.

The restricted airspace over Area 51 covers 575 square miles and has been nicknamed "the Box." Air Force pilots at nearby Nellis Air Force Base are said never to enter it. At the center sits a near-perfect six-mile-diameter endorheic basin (dry lake) used as a natural runway.

Physical base structures begin at the southernmost tip of Groom's dry lake and spread south down the Emigrant Valley floor.
