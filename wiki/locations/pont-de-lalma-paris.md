**Geographical and Historical Context from Source**

The Pont de l'Alma is the Paris road tunnel where Diana, Princess of Wales, died on 31 August 1997. Icke claims the site was, in antiquity, an **underground chamber used by the Merovingians for human sacrifice to the goddess Diana**, situated on a major "vortex" point of the Earth's ley-line grid — and that it remains "by design, an underground chamber" today.

His etymological argument: "*pont* relates to Pontifex, a Roman high priest, and ... means passage or bridge. *Alma* comes from **Al-Mah, a Middle Eastern name for ... the Moon goddess**. So the Pont de l'Alma translates as 'Bridge of the Moon Goddess' ... And the Moon goddess is Diana. It can also translate as 'Bridge of the Soul,' and a 'Maiden' of the goddess Diana was called an Al-mah." He adds that the spot is "a maze of crossroads" sacred to **Hecate** (an aspect of Diana), whose Satanic day of sacrifice he claims falls on 31 August "under the law of reverse symbolism," and that Diana's car struck the **13th pillar**. The "shrine" above the tunnel — a gold flame — he reads as the Brotherhood's signature, matching the eternal flame on JFK's grave.
