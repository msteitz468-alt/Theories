**Geographical and Historical Context from Source**

Sumer is the land of southern Mesopotamia where, per Sitchin, both the **first cities of the gods** and humanity's first high civilization arose. He locates the Nefilim's first installations here — Eridu, Bad-Tibira (their metallurgical/smelting centre), Sippar (the "spaceport"), and Nippur (Mission Control) — and argues the abrupt appearance of Sumerian civilization ~6,000 years ago reflects knowledge handed to man by the gods after the Deluge. He reads the very name *Shumer* as the "Land of the Watchers/Guardians."

**Textual Appearances**

Chapter 4 ("Sumer: Land of the Gods") and the chronology table (Ch. 13–14), which places the gods' cities and the post-Diluvial re-founding of civilization here.
