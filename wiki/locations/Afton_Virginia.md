## Geographical and Historical Context from Source

According to [[Robert Monroe (1915–1995)|Robert A. Monroe]] in *[[Journeys Out of the Body]]*, Afton, Virginia was his residence and the location of the [[Monroe Institute|Monroe Institute of Applied Sciences]]. Monroe identifies his location in the 1977 foreword as "Robert A. Monroe Afton, Virginia" and provides the Institute's address: "Monroe Institute of Applied Sciences, P.O. Box 57, Afton, Virginia 22920."

Monroe describes his environment at the beginning of his experimentation as "a country environment" with appreciation for "nature and quiet," which aligns with Afton's location in the Virginia foothills.

## Textual Appearances

Afton appears in Monroe's work as:

1. **Primary residence and experiment location**: Monroe conducted most of his out-of-body experiments from his home in Afton during his initial phase of investigation (1958–1971)

2. **Board meeting location**: Monroe recounts a board meeting in Fort Lauderdale, Florida where discussion of his book took place, but Afton is referenced as his home base

3. **Institute headquarters**: By 1972, Afton became the official location of the [[Monroe Institute|Monroe Institute of Applied Sciences]], Monroe's research facility dedicated to investigating out-of-body experiences

4. **Mailing address for researchers**: Monroe provided the Afton address as the contact point for individuals interested in the Institute's activities or who had spontaneous out-of-body experiences

## Related Claims and Events

Monroe documented out-of-body experiences initiated from Afton as well as experiences involving visits to other locations (Locale I apparently encompassing various familiar places) and more distant non-physical realms.
