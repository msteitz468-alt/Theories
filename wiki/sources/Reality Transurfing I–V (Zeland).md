## Source Description

*Reality Transurfing I–V* is a five-volume work by Russian author Vadim Zeland, first published in Russian from 2004 to 2007, compiled into a single English-language volume (ISBN 978-5-9573-2880-3; OJSC Ves Publishing Group, 2012). The work presents an esoteric and self-help framework called "Transurfing," asserting that human thought energy interacts with an infinite information field containing all possible life scenarios ("alternatives"), and that individuals can consciously navigate among these alternatives by managing their attention, intention, and level of importance.

The text comprises nineteen numbered chapters plus an Afterword and Glossary. Topics progress from foundational cosmology (the alternatives space, life lines, pendulums) through practical techniques (reducing importance, working with slides, visualisation, transfer chains) to advanced conceptual material (outer intention, the unity of heart and mind, personal goals).

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

The source presents Guardian Angels (Ch. IX) as potentially both real independent beings and as thought-energy constructs created by belief, explicitly declining to adjudicate: "it does not really matter whether the Angel exists independently or whether it is created by your thoughts."

The source acknowledges throughout that its cosmological claims lie beyond conventional materialism, framing this as a feature of the model rather than a tension within it.
