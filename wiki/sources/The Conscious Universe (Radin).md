## Source Description

*The Conscious Universe: The Scientific Truth of Psychic Phenomena* (1997) by parapsychologist **[[Dean Radin]]** argues that **psi phenomena are real** and that the accumulated, replicated laboratory evidence — assessed by the statistical tool of **meta-analysis** — already establishes them beyond reasonable chance. Radin reviews a century of controlled experiments across the major categories of psi: **telepathy** (especially the ganzfeld studies), **perception at a distance** (remote viewing), **perceiving the future** (precognition and physiological "presentiment"), **mind–matter interaction** (psychokinesis on dice and electronic random number generators), and **field consciousness** (deviations in random systems during moments of mass group attention).

A central methodological argument is that meta-analysis, by pooling many small studies, reveals a small but highly significant cumulative effect that no single experiment makes obvious — and that the **"file-drawer"** and chance objections cannot explain it away. Radin closes by examining why mainstream science nonetheless resists psi, framing the resistance as sociological rather than evidential. The book sits in the wiki's **post-materialist science** cluster ([[Post-materialist science]]) and is cited approvingly by the Hermetic-revival authors.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Radin is a parapsychologist arguing an unorthodox case; he engages the standard skeptical objections directly (chance, the file-drawer problem, experimenter effects, fraud) and rests his claim on cumulative meta-analytic effect sizes rather than any single decisive experiment. Mainstream psychology and statistics dispute both the robustness and the interpretation of these meta-analyses.
