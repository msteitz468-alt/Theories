## Source Description

*When Time Began* (1993) is Book V of the *Earth Chronicles*, devoted to **time, astronomy, and the calendar**. Sitchin argues that humanity's measurement of time was a gift of the [[Nefilim (Sitchin)|Anunnaki]], encoded in megalithic observatories and calendrical systems built to their astronomical design. He reads **Stonehenge** (and a claimed "Stonehenge on the Euphrates") as Anunnaki-engineered instruments for tracking the heavens, and develops a scheme of **three "times"**: *Celestial Time* (the precession of the equinoxes and the orbit of [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)|Nibiru]]), *Divine Time* (the gods' reckoning in [[Shar (3,600-year orbital period)|shars]]), and *Earthly Time* (the human calendar that began at Nippur).

A central theme is the **zodiac and the precessional Ages**: Sitchin contends the shift from the Age of the Bull (Taurus) to the Age of the Ram (Aries) corresponded to real upheavals among gods and men (notably the rise of Marduk/Ra). Per the wiki's strict-extraction policy, claims are catalogued **as presented**; the readings are rejected by mainstream archaeoastronomy.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Sitchin's archaeoastronomy ties disparate monuments (Stonehenge, Near-Eastern ziggurats, a claimed Euphrates circle) to a single Anunnaki design tradition; he presents the precession-encoded zodiac as deliberate divine timekeeping rather than the later Greek astronomical convention mainstream historians describe.
