## Source Description

*America's Secret Establishment: An Introduction to the Order of Skull & Bones* (1983; rev. 2002) by economist Antony C. Sutton argues that Yale's **Skull & Bones** society ("The Order," "Chapter 322") is a genuine American secret establishment whose members have occupied commanding positions in education, finance, foreign policy, and government — and that, guided by **Hegelian philosophy**, they manipulate society by *creating and controlling conflict* ("the clash of opposites"). Sutton's signature application is that The Order (via Wall Street Bonesmen) financed **both** the creation of the Soviet Union (thesis) **and** the rise of the Nazis (antithesis), and that it imported the statist Prussian/Hegelian education system to "dumb down" America.

The book grew from an anonymous package of documents — the society's membership lists — delivered to Sutton in the early 1980s. It is framed as a series of "memoranda." Distinctively, Sutton's method is documentary (membership rosters, corporate directorships, State Department files) and his thesis is **Hegelian-statist, not ethnic** — a sharp contrast with the antisemitic conspiracism of Nesta Webster.

## Structure

Introduction (2002 edition) + numbered "Memoranda": the membership/organization of The Order; How The Order Creates [conflict]; Created Conflict and the Dialectic; Operational Vehicles for Conflict Creation; *Thesis: The Order Creates the Soviet Union*; *Antithesis: Financing the Nazis*; control of education; and the closing memos on "The Secret Cult of The Order" (ritual, "satanic aspects," and "Is The Order also Illuminati?").

## Chapter Summaries

The book is organized as four "volumes" of numbered Memoranda. Summaries below follow that structure.

### Introduction to The Order — Memoranda 1–10
Sutton lays out his case and method. **Memo 1** ("Is There a Conspiracy Explanation for Recent History?") attacks the "official Establishment history" (noting The Order created the American Historical Association in the 1880s, with Andrew Dickson White as first president) and defines the strict legal criteria for a conspiracy, exempting the CFR as a whole while asserting a secret group *within* it controls it. **Memo 2** ("The Order — What It Is and How It Began") identifies The Order as Chapter 322, a branch of a German secret society imported to Yale in 1832–33 by William Huntington Russell and Alphonso Taft, incorporated as the Russell Trust (1856), once called the "Brotherhood of Death"; 15 juniors are tapped each year. **Memoranda 3–10** describe how much is documented, who the members are (the recurring family names Taft, Bush, Stimson, Lovett, Harriman, Whitney, Bundy), the organizations The Order has penetrated (foundations, the CFR, think tanks), the "chain of influence" (Whitney–Stimson–Acheson–Bundy), and a worked example, "The Bundy Operation," tracing Harvey Hollister, William, and McGeorge Bundy through CIA, the Pentagon, and the Ford Foundation.

### How The Order Controls Education — Memoranda 1–9
Sutton argues that controlling the future society requires controlling education. **Memo 1** ("It All Began at Yale") follows a "revolutionary trio" of Bonesmen — Timothy Dwight, Daniel Coit Gilman, and Andrew Dickson White — who studied post-Hegelian philosophy at Berlin in the 1850s (alongside Wilhelm Wundt) and returned to import the Prussian/Wundtian model into American universities. **Memo 2** traces the "look-say" reading method (Gallaudet, *Mother's Primer*, Horace Mann). **Memo 3** ("The Illuminati Connection") links the educational theorist chain (Herbart → Pestalozzi, Herder, Fichte, Goethe) to named or reputed Illuminati, and notes the Illuminati's parallel aim of world domination. **Memoranda 4–9** ("The Leipzig Connection," "The Troika Spreads Its Wings," The Order's objective for education) argue The Order's "troika" (Gilman, White, Dwight) installed a statist, Hegelian education system designed to "dumb down" America.

### How The Order Creates War and Revolution — Memoranda 1–5
The dialectical core. **Memo 1** ("Created Conflict and the Dialectic Process") explains the Hegelian thesis–antithesis–synthesis model and the doctrine of the State as "God on earth," illustrating with J.P. Morgan's funding of both left and right (quoting Carroll Quigley's *Tragedy and Hope* on George Peabody and the Morgan firm). **Memo 2** ("Operational Vehicles for Conflict Creation") attacks the "mind set" that capitalists and communists are enemies. **Memo 3** ("Thesis — The Order Creates the Soviet Union") links Bonesmen (Thacher, William Boyce Thompson) and the 120 Broadway firms (Guaranty Trust, American International Corporation) to Wall Street financing of the 1917 Bolshevik Revolution. **Memo 4** ("Antithesis — Financing the Nazis") traces funds via Fritz Thyssen, the Harriman interests, and Guaranty Trust to Hitler. **Memo 5** ("The New Dialectic — Angola and China") extends managed conflict to the present, aimed at a synthesized New World Order.

### The Secret Cult of The Order — Memoranda 1–5
**Memo 1** ("An Introduction to the Secret Cult of the Order") argues secrecy in politically active organizations is historically tied to coercion (citing the suppressed Bavarian Illuminati and Masonic abuses), and recounts the 1876 break-in at the "Bones" Temple that revealed skulls, bones, the "SPERRY" tombstone, and Illuminati-style inside names. **Memoranda 2–3** describe the organization and ritual of The Order. **Memo 4** ("Satanic Aspects of The Order") presents photographic evidence of members posed around skulls and crossed bones and reads the imagery symbolically. **Memo 5** ("Is The Order Also Illuminati?") concludes that The Order's German origin, code-names, and objectives parallel Weishaupt's Illuminati, though Sutton stops short of asserting a direct organizational descent.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Sutton rests the case on the leaked membership documents and the documented careers of named Bonesmen (Bush, Harriman, Stimson, Lovett, the Gilman–White–Dwight education "troika"), and frames the motive as Hegelian statism and power, explicitly *not* ethnicity — consistent with his rejection of the "Jewish-conspiracy theory" in *Wall Street and the Bolshevik Revolution*. The 2002 introduction also extends the "managed conflict" thesis to contested modern claims (suppressed "free energy," cold fusion, Monsanto), presented as inference.
