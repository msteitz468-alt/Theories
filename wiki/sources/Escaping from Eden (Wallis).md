## Source Description

*Escaping from Eden: Does Genesis Teach That the Human Race Was Created by God or Engineered by ETs?* (2020) by Paul Wallis — an Australian pastor, former archdeacon, and theologian — re-reads the early chapters of Genesis through their Hebrew "anomalies." Wallis's central move is to take the plural form of the word **elohim** literally: rather than a singular God, he argues Genesis originally described a plurality of physical, technologically capable beings he calls the **"Powerful Ones,"** later monotheized by a redactor ("J") who inserted the divine name Yahweh into older "elohim" stories. Read this way, Genesis becomes an account of humanity being genetically engineered and managed by a non-human species — a reading Wallis says *confirms* rather than contradicts the older Sumerian/Babylonian narratives.

Notably, Wallis writes as a person of faith: he does not abandon Christianity but distinguishes the non-divine "Powerful Ones" (the "false gods," "thieves and robbers") from the true God revealed in Jesus. The book is openly the "preacher's journey" of an amateur in linguistics, archaeology, and genetics who "stands on others' shoulders." It converges on the ancient-astronaut thesis of [[The Twelfth Planet (Sitchin)]] and [[The Gods of Eden (Bramley)]] from a biblical-studies direction.

## Chapter Summaries

### Introduction & Chapter One: Injuries & Anomalies
Sidelined by a frisbee injury and convalescing in a backyard shipping-crate study, Wallis re-reads Genesis 1–11 in his interlinear Bible and catalogues the "anomalies": the plural form of *elohim*, the "let us make" and "become like one of us" passages, the geographically specific gold deposits of Eden, the unexplained Snake, Cain's fear of "whoevers" outside the garden, and the *benei elohim* / Nephilim. He notes the redactor "J" inserting the name Yahweh into older *elohim* stories (e.g. the near-sacrifice of Isaac), and surveys the orthodox explanations he finds inadequate (Joshua Ben Levi, Rabbi Ammi, the Targum of Palestine's "heavenly council," Dom Henry Wansbrough's collective-noun reading). He concludes the plural form points to plural entities and proposes translating *elohim* by its root meaning, "Powerful Ones."

### Chapter Two: The Humans and the Others
Wallis retells Genesis as a "Plural Genesis." The Powerful Ones hover over the waters, separate land and sea, fashion humans from clay to resemble themselves, breathe spirit into them, and set them to work in an enclosed zone. The Snake — identified as a Council member, one of the Powerful Ones — gives the humans a forbidden consciousness upgrade (moral awareness, sexuality) led by the female, and is exiled to Earth for breaking rank. A war divides the Council; the *benei elohim* abduct human females, producing the Nephilim/giants; the Council resolves on a flood-cull, warns Noah, and later confuses human language at Babel. Wallis stresses this retelling is not invented fiction but matches the older Mesopotamian cuneiform narratives.

### Chapter Three: Strange! I've Seen That Face Before
The decipherment of cuneiform: Henry Rawlinson copying the trilingual Behistun Inscription in 1835 (Wallis invents the name "Eylo" for the boy who abseiled to it) provided the key. Wallis summarizes the Sumerian stories of beginnings (Enki and Enlil, Qingu's blood in the clay, Ninhursag, the upgrade of the wild man, the flood-warning to Ziusudra/Utnapishtim/Atrahasis, the dispatch of observers to stations in the sky from a "stargate" at Babel) and finds them parallel to Plural Genesis. He cites Joshua 24:14 ("put away the elohim whom your ancestors served beyond the river") as his "smoking gun" that the *elohim*/Sky People are distinct from Yahweh, and notes the Sumerian King List's impossibly long reigns and the 2003 claimed discovery of Gilgamesh's tomb (Jorg Fassbinder), arguing the Sky People are what we would call extraterrestrials.

### Chapter Four: A Lot of Forgetting
Framed as a pub debate with his sceptical friend Brad, Wallis answers the objection "we'd remember if ETs engineered us." He argues forgetting is enforced: the *Planet of the Apes* "CA-LI-MA / Caution Live Animals" motif, the firing of Nathaniel Schmidt for heresy in the 1890s, the harassment of Harvard's John Mack, and the excommunication of Marcion (whose churches read the *elohim* stories as not-God-stories) versus Origen's allegorical "wriggle room." He notes that the Babylonian exile (three-plus generations) coincides with the period scholars believe Genesis was redacted — "time and reason for a lot of forgetting."

### Chapter Five: Knowing in Our Bones
Anomalies in the bones: the Paracas long skulls (heavier, larger-brained, single parietal plate) which DNA testing nonetheless confirms as human; Neanderthal, Denisovan and *Homo floresiensis* ("Hobbit") finds; Ötzi's sophistication; Pliny's and Josephus's reports of giants; six-fingered skeletons in Noble County, Ohio. Wallis argues human prehistory was far more diverse than the "single-line" evolution he was taught, and introduces the Younger Dryas / Clovis Comet extinction-level event (~12,800 years ago), suggesting Genesis 1 may be a story of *recovery* rather than original creation.

### Chapter Six: The Great Re-emergence
Wallis develops the "Genesis as recovery" thesis. He describes the Clovis Comet impact on the Laurentide ice sheet, the soot canopy, sea-level rise, and Younger Dryas freeze (citing Richard Firestone, Albert Goodyear, James Kennett), and submerged or buried sites (Göbekli Tepe, Dwarka in the Gulf of Cambay, Yonaguni, Malta). He reads Genesis 1's pre-existing dark, water-covered earth (*tohu wa bohu*) as a post-cataclysm planet being rehabilitated by the Powerful Ones, paralleling the Maya *Popol Vuh* (Feathered Serpent engineers "hovering over the waters," staged creation of humans from maize, the deliberate limiting of human sight), and the sudden localized domestication of crops near Karaca Dağ as a "helping hand."

### Chapter Seven: The Memory of Us
Wallis adds the Efik people of Nigeria (Abassi and Atai, the Sky-Beings who fear the humans becoming "like one of us" and send death) as a fourth parallel to Genesis, the cuneiforms and *Popol Vuh*. He reads the Tower of Babel as the violent shutdown of an advanced, spacefaring human civilization — the Powerful Ones extracting language "like inducing a collective stroke" — and argues that the repeating motif of creators pitting themselves against human progress is too demeaning and too consistent to be mere fiction; it is collective traumatic memory. He reiterates that the True God of Jesus must be rescued from confusion with the "monstrous acts" of the Powerful Ones.

### Chapter Eight: The Evidence of Things
The "tangible artefacts" chapter. Wallis examines the Pakal sarcophagus lid at Palenque (debated astronaut/serpent-jaws interpretation; Linda Schele's Milky Way reading), Mayan "bluetooth" headgear carvings, the Vedic *vimanas*, Ezekiel's vehicle as read by NASA engineer Josef Blumrich (*The Spaceships of Ezekiel*, 1974), Iapetus as a "Death Star" doppelganger, Usermontu's iron orthopedic pin, Baalbek's 800–1,650-ton stones, Sacsayhuamán's joinery, and Michael Tellinger's southern-African sonic stone circles. He closes with Apollo 15 astronaut Al Worden's 2017 TV remark that "we are the aliens... go get books on the ancient Sumerians."

### Chapter Ten: Loving the Alien
Wallis surveys the modern "soft disclosure" climate: the post-2008 winding-down of government UFO desks, The Disclosure Project, the 2001 National Press Club event, NASA astronauts speaking openly, the Gary McKinnon extradition case, and especially Vatican statements (Fr José Gabriel Funes on a "brother alien," Benedict XVI's 2009 astrobiology colloquium, Monsignor Balducci, Guy Consolmagno). He then presents the DNA argument — Makulov and shCherbak's claim of a non-random "prime-number" (multiples of 37) signature in human "junk" DNA, and the Crick–Orgel panspermia paper — alongside the *Rosetta* comet probe's detection of glycine and the Zulu Unkulunkulu seed-pod legend, framing humanity as a seeded, engineered hybrid.

### Chapter Eleven: Father God, I Wonder
A theological interlude (again sparring with Brad). Wallis argues that genetic engineering or hybrid ancestry would not exclude God's fatherhood: God did not "create" the German Shepherd, Grolar bears, or Dolly the cloned sheep, yet remains the source of every atom and of consciousness. He reads Psalm 139 as affirming God's love "whether I was conceived in utero or in vitro... human, pure and simple, or human with a few drops of ET." He surveys the Hebrew Bible's lingering plural Powerful Ones (the guardian of Persia in Daniel 10, the Lord of Ekron, the Ten Commandments' "no other gods") to show monotheism bedding down only gradually, and the morally troubling treatment of King Saul as a "not-God-story."

### Chapter Twelve: I Have Called You Friends
Wallis re-examines Marcion and Origen, who both saw the same problem — the incompatibility of Jesus's God with the violent Yahweh/elohim of the Hebrew texts — and chose different solutions (Marcion excising the Old Testament; Origen reading it allegorically). He argues the Mesopotamian "slave-wiring" thesis (humans engineered to bring tribute to the Sky People) explains the deep religious impulse to sacrifice and the human tendency toward subservience to elites, and contrasts it with Jesus's reversal: "I have called you friends," not slaves. He suggests the Church's gluing of the Hebrew canon onto the New Testament imported a confused, morally dissonant picture of God.

### Chapter Thirteen: Please Sir, I Want Some More!
Wallis links the mythologies' claim that the engineers deliberately "downgraded" human perception to the neuroscience of Acquired Savant Syndrome — Orlando Serrell's calendar calculation after a head injury, Ben McMahon's sudden fluent Mandarin after a coma, and the frontotemporal-dementia cases catalogued by Bruce Miller, Mark Lythgoe and Darold Treffert in which brain "disinhibition" releases latent abilities. He asks "what is an inhibitor doing in our brains?" and reads Jesus's promise that believers will do "greater things" as an invitation to switch off the slave-programming and the inhibitors.

A closing dialogue with Jesus in the shipping crate. ## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Wallis surveys the orthodox explanations he rejects — the "royal we," a "heavenly council" of created angels (the Targum of Palestine), elohim as an abstract/collective noun (Dom Henry Wansbrough) — and concedes each "might be enough to forgive the grammatical glitches" but argues a plurality is still implied. He frames the ET-engineering reading as the answer to his title's question while keeping a theistic frame, treating the matter as one he could not "not-publish" rather than a proven certainty, and acknowledging he lacks formal credentials in the fields he draws on.
