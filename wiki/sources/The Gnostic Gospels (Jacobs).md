## Source Description

*The Gnostic Gospels* (2005), edited and rendered by Alan Jacobs, is a modern **poetic** presentation of
the principal Gnostic scriptures — the Gospels of Thomas, Philip, Mary, Truth, the Apocryphon of John,
the Thunder, and others — most of which derive from [[The Nag Hammadi Library]]. Jacobs recasts the
Coptic texts as free verse to foreground their devotional and contemplative force, with brief framing
introductions.

This is a **companion** to the primary Nag Hammadi ingest: the wiki's doctrinal extraction (the Father,
Sophia, Yaldabaoth and the archons, the divine spark, gnosis) is drawn from
[[The Nag Hammadi Library]]; this page records Jacobs's edition for the poetic/popular reception of the
same gospels rather than re-extracting the cosmology. Recorded descriptively, not adjudicated.
