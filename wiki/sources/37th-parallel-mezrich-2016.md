## Source Description

*The 37th Parallel: The Secret Truth Behind America's UFO Highway* (2016) is a work of **narrative nonfiction** by Ben Mezrich. It dramatizes the two-decade investigation of **Chuck Zukowski**, a microchip engineer and part-time reserve sheriff's deputy from Colorado Springs, into cattle/animal mutilations and UFO sightings across the western United States. The book's organizing thesis is Zukowski's claim that a disproportionate share of these anomalies — together with major military installations and Native American sacred sites — fall along a corridor near the **37th parallel** of latitude, which he dubs the "UFO Highway."

Mezrich writes in his characteristic reconstructed-scene style (reconstructed dialogue, interior monologue), drawing on Zukowski's case files, MUFON reports, his sister and co-investigator **Debbie Ziegelmeyer**, and the work of **Robert Bigelow**'s [[National Institute for Discovery Science (NIDS)]] at Skinwalker Ranch.

**Note on extraction:** This is a popular, dramatized UFO book, not peer-reviewed science. The mutilation–UFO–"runway" thesis is not accepted by mainstream science; cattle mutilations have conventional explanations (predation, decomposition bloat, blowfly/scavenger removal of soft tissue) per the 1980 Rommel/FBI report, which the book does not foreground. The "37th parallel" correlation is unverified and selection-biased (Zukowski himself only plots reports he could verify, and concedes many anomalies sit nearer the 36th or 38th). Per the wiki's strict-extraction policy, claims are catalogued **as the source presents them**, without endorsement.

## Chapter Summaries

*The book's thirty chapters are untitled, identified only by location/date datelines. Three braided storylines run in parallel: Chuck Zukowski's present-day investigations (mostly Rush, Colorado, 2010–11); his origin story as a UFO investigator (1994–2002); and historical/biographical interludes (1944 Foo Fighters; Robert Bigelow's childhood). Adjacent chapters are grouped below by storyline and arc.*

### Chapter 1: Ute Mountains, Colorado (Sept. 2000)
The book opens with Chuck Zukowski, his wife Tammy, and their kids on an RV trip near the Colorado–New Mexico border. After parking at a motel, Chuck slips out with his equipment (flashlight, video recorder, EMF counter, Glock) to a remote mountain clearing with his friend Joe Fex and a psychic who tipped him to the location. They witness brilliant lights flashing and arcing in the sky; all three of Chuck's camera batteries drain inexplicably, and an animal is heard being run down and killed mid-scream. The framing scene establishes the "UFO Highway" mystery the book will explore.

**Persons**
- [[Chuck Zukowski]]

### Chapter 2: Rush, Colorado (Aug. 2010)
Chuck, now a reserve sheriff's deputy, responds to rancher Glenda's call about two horses (Princess and Buck) found dead and partially stripped. The carcasses show precise, surgical-looking wounds: eyes and tongues removed via clean incisions, organs excised, and no blood at the scene. Chuck frames the kill as one of more than ten thousand unexplained animal mutilations documented across the western US over fifty years.

### Chapter 3: Anza, California (Late Spring 1994) — Origin story
Chuck's origins as a UFO investigator: a microchip engineer who attends Orange County Astronomers "Star Parties," quietly asking attendees whether they've ever seen a UFO and recording the answers. He recounts the history of the term (Project Blue Book, Kenneth Arnold's 1947 "flying saucers," the Condon Report) and his disillusionment with **MUFON** despite joining it, leading him to adopt a grassroots, individualist investigative approach.

### Chapter 4: Rhine Valley (Nov. 1944) — Historical interlude
A dramatized account of WWII "Foo Fighters": US Army Air Force pilot Ed Schleuter and his crew over Germany encounter ten orange fiery balls of light flying in controlled formation that register on no radar, vanish, and reappear at impossible distances. The chapter reproduces a 1945 New York Times report on the phenomenon, establishing a decades-deep history of unexplained aerial objects.

### Chapter 5: Roswell, New Mexico (June 1994)
Chuck visits the International UFO Museum in Roswell on a family trip and narrates the 1947 Roswell incident to Tammy: radar tracking of objects, rancher Mac Brazel's debris field, Jesse Marcel's investigation, the "RAAF Captures Flying Saucer" press release and its retraction as a weather balloon, and the alleged movement of debris (and bodies) to Area 51. He meets mortician Glenn Dennis, a living Roswell witness.

### Chapter 19: National Institute for Discovery Science HQ, Las Vegas (2003) — Bigelow and NIDS

NIDS concluded the frequently-reported "Big Black Delta" triangles were most likely experimental Air Force lighter-than-air craft. ### Chapter 28: Colorado Springs (Aug. 2011) — The 37th parallel pattern
After two strong earthquakes occur the same day on the 37th parallel (Colorado and Virginia), Chuck strips his case-file map of all unverified pins and re-plots only confirmed mutilations and sightings, revealing that a "surprising majority" cluster along a band near the **37th parallel** — which he and Debbie dub the "UFO Highway." Reviewing his coordinates (and noting that Area 51, NORAD/Cheyenne Mountain, Fort Knox, and many Native American sacred sites also fall along it), Chuck concludes the alignment is "mathematically significant," though he concedes some is coincidence and that anomalies sit nearer the 36th or 38th.

### Chapters 29–30: Rush, Colorado (Aug. 2011) / Ute Mountains (37.2841° N) — The runway
A year on, rancher Glenda watches an unmarked dark helicopter circle the exact spot where Princess and Buck died, then speed away. In the final chapter, Chuck returns alone to the Ute Mountain site of the book's opening, again experiences the cold electric stillness and battery-draining effect, and rushes to where the animal died years earlier. In deliberately fragmentary, ambiguous prose he finds a burned area, half an elk's bones, curved metallic material (echoing Roswell and Bigelow), and concludes that the 37th parallel is "real — not a highway, but a runway." Left unresolved, he chooses whether to "ride down that runway, wherever it might lead."

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

The book records the conventional counter-explanations as they surface in Zukowski's cases — investigating officers proposing "predators" then "lightning strike"; a CIA contact dismissing Zukowski as "just another UFO nut"; NIDS resolving the Big Black Deltas as prosaic Air Force craft. The climactic chapter is deliberately written in fragmentary, ambiguous prose, leaving the ultimate "secret truth" unresolved.
