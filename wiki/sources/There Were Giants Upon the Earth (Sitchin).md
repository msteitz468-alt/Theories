## Source Description

*There Were Giants Upon the Earth: Gods, Demigods, and Human Ancestry* (2010) is Zecharia Sitchin's late, capstone volume in the *Earth Chronicles*, taking its title from Genesis 6:4. Its argument is that the [[Nefilim (Sitchin)|Nefilim/Anunnaki]] were **physically real flesh-and-blood beings of great stature** — the biblical "giants" — and that their interbreeding with humans produced **demigods** (such as Gilgamesh, "two-thirds divine") and the unnaturally long-lived **patriarchs** of Genesis. Sitchin retraces the quest for immortality (opening with Alexander the Great), the antediluvian kings, Sumer, and the planet Nibiru, before turning to human ancestry, the Anunnaki/Igigi, and "A Serf Made to Order" (the genetic creation of man).

The book's distinctive contribution is its **demand for physical proof**: in the closing chapters and the Postscript ("Mankind's Alien Origins—The Evidence"), Sitchin argues the question can be settled by **DNA testing of the recovered remains of the gods** — above all the richly buried Sumerian "queen"/goddess (the lady of the Ur tomb) — and calls for such testing. Per the wiki's strict-extraction policy, claims are catalogued **as presented**; the readings and the genetic claims are not accepted by mainstream science.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Unusually for the series, Sitchin here stakes his thesis on a falsifiable test — DNA analysis of recovered remains — implicitly conceding that the textual case alone has not convinced mainstream science. The "giants" and demigod genealogy rest on his literal reading of Genesis 6, the Sumerian King List's vast reigns, and the Gilgamesh epic.
