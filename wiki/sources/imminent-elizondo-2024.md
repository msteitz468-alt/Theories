## Source Description

*Imminent: Inside the Pentagon's Hunt for UFOs* (Bonnier Books, 2024) is a first-person memoir by Luis Elizondo, the former director of the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP) at the Pentagon. Elizondo is a career counterintelligence officer who worked for the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence (OUSD(I)) and, before that, Army Intelligence. He resigned from the Department of Defense on October 4, 2017, and joined To The Stars Academy (TTSA) to pursue public UAP disclosure.

The book's foreword is written by Christopher Mellon, former Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and former minority staff director of the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence.

The title derives from Elizondo's assessment that UAP contact is "imminent" in the sense of being an already-present and escalating reality, not a future event. The book covers Elizondo's recruitment into AATIP, his investigation of UAP evidence, key cases (Nimitz Tic Tac 2004, USS Roosevelt 2014–2015, Colares 1977–1978, nuclear interference incidents), the broader Legacy Program of contractor-held UAP materials, and the Congressional disclosure battle from 2017 through 2023.

## What the Source Contributes

Elizondo provides an insider account of the US government's classified UAP program from 2009 to 2017 and the subsequent public disclosure effort. Key contributions:

- Firsthand description of AATIP's founding, mandate, budget, and methodology inside the Pentagon
- Details on AAWSAP (predecessor program), funded by the Defense Intelligence Agency with Congressional support from Senators Harry Reid, Ted Stevens, and Daniel Inouye
- The Five Observables analytical framework Elizondo developed within AATIP
- Multi-source documentation of the 2004 USS Nimitz "Tic Tac" encounter
- Multi-sensor documentation of USS Roosevelt UAP encounters (2014–2015), including the GoFast and GIMBAL videos
- Claims about a Legacy Program maintained by defense contractors (Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman, TRW, Boeing, McDonnell Douglas, Raytheon, BAE Systems, Aerospace Corporation, Monsanto) holding nonhuman materials and technology
- Secondhand claims about crash retrievals at Ciudad Acuña, Mexico (December 1950) and Kazakhstan (1989)
- Secondhand claim about a CIA autopsy of a nonhuman body with anomalous anatomy
- Dr. Garry Nolan's neurological research on 105 UAP experiencers showing enlarged caudate-putamen
- Hal Puthoff's warp bubble unified theory explaining all Six Observables
- Isotopic analysis of recovered material from Council Bluffs, Iowa (1977) showing engineered isotope ratios
- UAP interference with nuclear weapons at Malmstrom AFB (1967), Rendlesham Forest (1980), and a Ukrainian ICBM site (1982)
- The Colares, Brazil, incidents (1977–1978) resulting in deaths and biological injuries
- Elizondo's declassification of three videos via DoD Form 1910 and their publication via the December 2017 New York Times / Politico articles
- Congressional trajectory: UAP Task Force (2020), AOIMSG (2021), AARO (2022), NDAA FY2023 UAP legislation, UAP Disclosure Act (signed December 2023)
- David Grusch's 2023 Congressional testimony about crash retrieval programs and nonhuman bodies

## Internal Tensions

- Elizondo uses pseudonyms for some colleagues (e.g., "Will Livingston," "Rosemary Caine") and cannot disclose the real names of certain classified programs, creating gaps that cannot be independently verified within the source.
- The source is a memoir written after Elizondo's departure from the DoD; some claims about the Legacy Program and crash retrievals are attributed to third parties (colleagues, unnamed officials) and are clearly secondhand within the text.
- Elizondo acknowledges the DoD's public denial of his role in AATIP (via spokesperson Christopher Sherwood) while describing private confirmation by multiple senior officials.
