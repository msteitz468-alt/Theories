## Source Description

*UFOs and Nukes: Extraordinary Encounters at Nuclear Weapons Sites* (2008; expanded 2017) by researcher **[[Robert L. Hastings]]** assembles three decades of interviews and declassified documents to argue that **UFOs have persistently monitored, and at times interfered with, U.S. (and Soviet) nuclear weapons**. Hastings — son of an Air Force officer who himself saw UFOs tracked on radar at Malmstrom AFB in 1967 — gathers the on-the-record testimony of more than 150 former and retired military personnel (missile-launch officers, security police, missile maintenance crews) describing UFO incursions over ICBM fields, weapons-storage areas, and bomber bases.

His central cases include the **March 1967 Malmstrom "Echo Flight" and "Oscar Flight" incidents**, in which entire flights of Minuteman missiles allegedly went off-alert as a UFO hovered nearby, and the **November 1975** wave of overflights at SAC bases (Loring, Wurtsmith, Malmstrom, Minot) — the same events documented by [[Richard M. Dolan]]. Hastings interprets the pattern as evidence of non-human concern about humanity's nuclear arsenal, and argues the U.S. government has covered up the incidents and pressured witnesses to silence. He presented the testimony publicly at the **2010 National Press Club conference**. The book ties directly to Elizondo's claim that UAP monitor nuclear sites in *[[Imminent: Inside the Pentagon's Hunt for UFOs (Elizondo, 2024)]]*.

## Structure

Introduction + thematic case-study chapters organized by site and era (Malmstrom, Minot, Loring, Wurtsmith, Vandenberg, Soviet/Russian sites, weapons-storage and test ranges), the 2010 National Press Club witnesses, and appendices (incl. UFO sightings at commercial nuclear power plants).

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Hastings relies heavily on the recollections of military witnesses, often decades after the events, and acknowledges the Air Force's official denials (e.g., that the Echo Flight shutdown was caused by an electrical-noise/engineering fault rather than a UFO). He is candid that no single piece of physical proof closes the case, resting his argument instead on the volume and consistency of independent testimony.
