## Source Description

*Wall Street and the Rise of Hitler* is a book by economist and historian Antony C. Sutton, first published in 1976 by '76 Press (with a reprint published by Clairview Books in 2012). The book is the final volume of Sutton's trilogy examining the role of the American corporate socialists and the New York financial elite in major 20th-century historical events.

Sutton utilizes primary source materials, particularly transcripts and exhibits from the post-World War II Nuremberg Military Tribunals and German government archives, to argue that the rise of Adolf Hitler and the military capability of Nazi Germany were heavily assisted and funded by the New York financial elite (especially Morgan and Rockefeller interests). He demonstrates that Wall Street reparations loans (under the Dawes and Young Plans) consolidated the German industrial cartels (I.G. Farben and Vereinigte Stahlwerke) that built the Nazi war machine, and that American multinational corporations (such as General Electric, Standard Oil of New Jersey, I.T.T., and Ford) directly funded Hitler's accession, transferred vital military technologies, and collaborated with the Nazi regime before and during World War II.

## Chapter Summaries

### Preface and Introduction: Unexplored Facets of Nazism
Introduces the book as the third volume in the Wall Street trilogy. Sutton outlines how U.S. business interests backed Bolshevik, New Deal, and National socialism. He discusses how the post-war Allied Control Commission protected collaborating businessmen, citing James S. Martin's *All Honorable Men*, and quotes U.S. Ambassador William Dodd warning President Roosevelt of corporate complicity (Standard Oil, DuPont, GM, Ford).
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[Wall Street reparations loans under the Dawes and Young Plans consolidated the German industrial cartels that built the Nazi war machine]]

### Chapter 1: Wall Street Paves the Way for Hitler
Analyzes how the reparations loans under the Dawes Plan (1924) and Young Plan (1928), both managed by Morgan representatives (Charles Dawes and General Electric's Owen D. Young), were used to finance the consolidation of the major German cartels (I.G. Farben and Vereinigte Stahlwerke). Sutton identifies the Bank for International Settlements (B.I.S.) as the apex of international financial control, where central bankers collaborated during World War II.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[Wall Street reparations loans under the Dawes and Young Plans consolidated the German industrial cartels that built the Nazi war machine]]

### Chapter 2: The Empire of I.G. Farben
Documents the creation of the I.G. Farben chemical cartel in 1925 by Hermann Schmitz using Wall Street capital. Details the role of its U.S. subsidiary, American I.G., which had prominent American directors (Paul Warburg, Edsel Ford, Walter Teagle, Charles E. Mitchell) and facilitated massive technology transfers to Germany.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[Wall Street reparations loans under the Dawes and Young Plans consolidated the German industrial cartels that built the Nazi war machine]]

### Chapter 3: General Electric Funds Hitler
Examines how German General Electric (A.E.G.), in which the U.S. General Electric company held a 25–30 percent interest, directly funded Hitler's rise, donating 60,000 RM to the Nazi trusteeship account in 1933. Sutton details the roles of GE's Owen D. Young and Gerard Swope.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[Wall Street multinational corporations, including General Electric, directly funded Adolf Hitler's accession to power in 1933]]

### Chapter 4: Standard Oil Fuels World War II
Details the extensive cartel cooperation between Standard Oil of New Jersey (Rockefeller controlled) and I.G. Farben. Standard Oil transferred vital patents and technical data for synthetic gasoline (coal hydrogenation) and synthetic rubber (Buna), and supplied tetraethyl lead to the German air force (Luftwaffe), making the motorized German war effort possible.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[Standard Oil of New Jersey transferred vital synthetic gasoline and rubber technology to I.G. Farben, enabling the Nazi Wehrmacht to wage war]]

### Chapter 5: I.T.T. Works Both Sides of the War
Documents International Telephone and Telegraph's (under Sosthenes Behn) collaboration with the Nazi regime. Kurt von Schröder, an S.S. Senior Group Leader and private banker, hosted the January 1933 Cologne meeting between Hitler and von Papen and acted as a financial conduit for S.T.T. (I.T.T.'s German subsidiary) funds to Heinrich Himmler's S.S. Sutton also notes I.T.T.'s ownership of German Focke-Wulf aircraft production.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[International Telephone and Telegraph worked both sides of World War II, directing funds to Himmler's S.S. and manufacturing German combat aircraft]]

### Chapter 6: Henry Ford and the Nazis
Details Henry Ford's financial support of the Nazi party from the 1920s, his receipt of the Grand Cross of the German Eagle in 1938, and how Ford Motor A.G. of Germany merged with I.G. Farben and produced military transport vehicles for the Wehrmacht during World War II utilizing forced labor.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[Ford Motor Company's German subsidiary utilized forced labor and manufactured military vehicles for the Nazi Wehrmacht during World War II]]

### Chapter 7: Who Financed Adolf Hitler?
Analyzes the political contributions made by German cartels at the May 1932 Kaiserhof meeting and the February 1933 National Trusteeship meeting. Shows Fritz Thyssen (of W.A. Harriman Company) and others bankrolling Hitler's final election campaign.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[Wall Street multinational corporations, including General Electric, directly funded Adolf Hitler's accession to power in 1933]]

### Chapter 8: Putzi: Friend of Hitler and Roosevelt
Examines Ernst "Putzi" Hanfstaengl, an elite figure close to both Franklin D. Roosevelt (at Harvard) and Hitler. Discusses his role as Foreign Press Chief and his presence during the Reichstag fire. Sutton compares Roosevelt's New Deal to Hitler's New Order.

### Chapter 9: Wall Street and the Nazi Inner Circle
Documents the Keppler Circle (later the S.S. Circle of Friends / Himmler's Circle), showing that directors of Wall Street multinationals (Standard Oil, I.T.T., Farben) were heavily represented, providing direct financial subsidies to the S.S. through Kurt von Schröder.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[International Telephone and Telegraph worked both sides of World War II, directing funds to Himmler's S.S. and manufacturing German combat aircraft]]

### Chapter 10: The Myth of "Sidney Warburg"
Evaluates the 1933 Dutch pamphlet *Financial Sources of National Socialism* by "Sidney Warburg," claiming New York bankers funded Hitler. Sutton concludes that while the pamphlet contains fabrications and is likely a political tract, the underlying cartels (e.g. American I.G. Farben) indeed funded Hitler.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[The suppressed 1933 book by 'Sidney Warburg' detailed a series of secret meetings and funding transfers from Wall Street bankers to Adolf Hitler]]

### Chapter 11: Wall Street-Nazi Collaboration in World War II
Outlines how American multinational branches continued production in Germany during the war, and how post-war investigations into corporate collaboration were muffled by elite businessmen on Control Commissions.

### Chapter 12: Conclusions
Summarizes how the corporate socialists of the New York financial elite manipulated central banking and cartels to build Bolshevik Russia, New Deal America, and Nazi Germany, working toward a controlled, monopoly-based world order.
