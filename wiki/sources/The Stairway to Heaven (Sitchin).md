## Source Description

*The Stairway to Heaven* (1980) is Book II of Zecharia Sitchin's *Earth Chronicles*. Its theme is humanity's age-old yearning for **immortality** — which Sitchin reads not as religious metaphor but as a literal quest to reach the abode of the [[Nefilim (Sitchin)|Nefilim/Anunnaki]] and share their long lifespans. From the Epic of Gilgamesh and the Egyptian pharaohs' "journey to the afterlife," he builds toward the physical infrastructure of the gods' spacefaring: the cedar-platform **Landing Place** at Baalbek, the Sinai spaceport (**Tilmun**, "land of the rocketships"), and the **Giza pyramids**, which he argues were built by the Anunnaki as **navigational beacons** for that spaceport — not as tombs by the pharaohs. He marshals the "Inventory Stela" to argue the Sphinx and Great Pyramid predate Khufu, and contends the only royal-name inscription in the Great Pyramid (a Khufu cartouche) is a 19th-century forgery by Colonel Howard Vyse.

Per the wiki's strict-extraction policy, claims are catalogued **as the source presents them**; Sitchin's readings and the Vyse-forgery charge are rejected by mainstream Egyptology.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Sitchin's pyramid-as-beacon and Vyse-forgery arguments run directly against the Egyptological consensus that the Giza pyramids are Fourth-Dynasty royal tombs; he presents his case as the suppressed or overlooked reading of the same evidence.
