## Source Description

*The Book of Enoch* is an ancient Jewish apocalyptic/pseudepigraphal work attributed to the
antediluvian patriarch [[Enoch (antediluvian patriarch)|Enoch]] ("the seventh from Adam"). This
"complete volume" edition (compiled by Joseph B. Lumpkin) collects **1 Enoch** (the Ethiopic Book of
Enoch — Book of the Watchers, Book of Parables, the Astronomical Book, the Book of Dream Visions, the
Epistle of Enoch), **2 Enoch** ("The Secrets of Enoch" / Slavonic Enoch — Enoch's ascent through the
heavens and the secrets of creation), and a brief note on **3 Enoch** (the Hebrew Book of Enoch). See
the umbrella [[The Book of Enoch: Watchers, Nephilim, and apocalyptic revelation]].

This is a key **primary root** for the wiki: its account of the **Watchers** (angels who descended on
Mount Hermon, took human wives, and begot the giant **Nephilim**) and their forbidden teachings is the
source text behind the Watchers/Nephilim material that recurs across Sitchin, Bramley, the Wallis "Eden"
books, Bane's demonology, the Rex Deus tradition, and Blavatsky's re-reading of the "fallen angels."

## Strict-extraction note

The text is recorded as *what the source narrates/claims* — attributed and **not adjudicated** as
historical fact (nor as scripture). It is a primary ancient document; the wiki catalogs its narrative
and doctrines descriptively.

## Structure

**1 Enoch:** Book of the Watchers (ch. 1–36) · Book of Parables/Similitudes (37–71) · the Astronomical
Book / Book of the Luminaries (72–82) · the Book of Dream Visions incl. the Animal Apocalypse (83–90) ·
the Epistle of Enoch incl. the Apocalypse of Weeks (91–108). **2 Enoch** (the Secrets of Enoch): Enoch's
ascent through the ten heavens, the creation account, and Melchizedek. **3 Enoch:** brief closing note.
