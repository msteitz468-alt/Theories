## Source Description

*Hidden History: An Exposé of Modern Crimes, Conspiracies, and Cover-Ups in American Politics* (2014)
by Donald Jeffries ([[Donald Jeffries]]), with a foreword by [[Roger Stone]], argues that modern
American history is best understood as a "deliberate systematic plan" — a sustained
**[[Suppressed conspiracies and cover-ups in modern American politics (Hidden History thesis)]]** —
in which assassinations, scandals, electoral fraud, and a recurring pattern of suspicious deaths
([[The political "body count" pattern (Jeffries)]]) have been concealed or distorted by the
mainstream media. Beginning with the 1963 assassination of John F. Kennedy ([[JFK Assassination]]),
it proceeds decade by decade through to the Obama years and concludes with chapters on world
government, the Federal Reserve, and the justice system.

## Strict-extraction note

This is a popular conspiracy/exposé work. Its assertions about specific deaths, events, and officials
are recorded as *what Jeffries claims/alleges*, attributed to the source, **and not adjudicated** as
fact; many concern living or named individuals and are explicitly the author's contested
interpretations of disputed events. The wiki catalogs them descriptively and neutrally.

## Structure

Seventeen chapters plus introduction and postscript: (1) the JFK assassination · (2) the Sixties
(MLK, RFK, Chappaquiddick) · (3) the Seventies (Watergate, the Wallace shooting, the Church
Committee/HSCA) · (4) the Reagan years (the assassination attempt, October Surprise/Iran-Contra,
Inslaw, Rex 84) · (5) Bush the Elder · (6) the Clinton years (Ruby Ridge, Vince Foster, Waco,
Oklahoma City, TWA 800, the "body count") · (7) the death of JFK Jr. · (8) Dubya and "Votescam" ·
(9) 9/11 · (10) more Dubya (dead scientists, the Wellstone crash, Iraq/Afghanistan, Gary Webb) ·
(11) Obama · (12) the Franklin Cover-Up and child-sex scandals · (13) world government / the New
World Order (CFR, Bilderberg, Bohemian Grove) · (14) the Federal Reserve · (15) charity ·
(16) the justice system · (17) where we stand now.
