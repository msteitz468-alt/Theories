## Source Description

*Stanislav Grof, LSD Pioneer* by **Brigitte Grof** is a biographical/expository account of the life and work of the psychiatrist **[[Stanislav Grof]]**, a founder of transpersonal psychology and the leading researcher of non-ordinary ("**holotropic**") states of consciousness. It traces Grof's path from early **LSD psychotherapy** research in Prague and the United States to the development (with Christina Grof) of drug-free **holotropic breathwork**, and presents the model of the psyche he built from thousands of sessions: an expanded **cartography** spanning the biographical, **perinatal** (the four Basic Perinatal Matrices organized around birth), and **transpersonal** domains.

The book documents his theories as a record of Grof's ideas; per the wiki's policy, claims are catalogued as presented. Grof is an influence cited elsewhere in the wiki (John Mack's abduction method; Graham Hancock). See [[Holotropic states of consciousness (Grof)]].

## Structure

A biography interleaving Grof's life story with exposition of his core ideas — LSD research, the birth of holotropic breathwork, the cartography of the psyche, perinatal matrices, transpersonal experience, and spiritual emergency.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

The book is written by Grof's wife and collaborator and is sympathetic; it presents his cartography and perinatal model as established findings, whereas mainstream psychiatry regards them as unproven and tied to the contested status of psychedelic-assisted therapy.
