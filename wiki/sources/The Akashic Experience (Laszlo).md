## Source Description

*The Akashic Experience: Science and the Cosmic Memory Field* (2009), edited and framed by systems philosopher **[[Ervin Laszlo]]**, argues that a fundamental cosmic information field — the **Akashic field** ("A-field") — records and conveys the experience of all things and connects everything nonlocally. The book pairs Laszlo's scientific framing (drawn from quantum, vacuum/zero-point-field, and systems theory) with some twenty first-person testimonies, from scientists, healers, artists, and others, of **"akashic experiences"**: episodes of transpersonal connection, intuition at a distance, spontaneous healing, past-life and near-death recall, and a felt unity with a larger whole.

Laszlo presents these not as anomalies to be explained away but as evidence that consciousness can access the A-field, and that science is converging on a **post-materialist** paradigm of an integral, in-formed reality. It belongs in the wiki's [[Post-materialist science]] cluster alongside Radin, Sheldrake, and Lanza.

## Structure

Laszlo's framing chapters (the concept of the Akashic field; the science of an in-formed universe) bracketing roughly twenty first-person "akashic experience" accounts contributed by various authors, followed by Laszlo's synthesis on the emerging paradigm.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

The book mixes Laszlo's theoretical framing with personal anecdote; the evidential weight rests on subjective testimony interpreted through the A-field hypothesis, which mainstream physics does not recognize. Laszlo presents the A-field as a unifying inference rather than a directly measured entity.
