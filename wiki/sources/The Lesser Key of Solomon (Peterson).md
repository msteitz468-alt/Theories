## Source Description

*The Lesser Key of Solomon (Lemegeton Clavicula Salomonis)*, edited by [[Joseph H. Peterson]] (Weiser
Books, 2001), is a critical edition — collated chiefly from British Library manuscripts (Sloane 3825,
Sloane 2731, Harley 6483) — of the seventeenth-century grimoire compilation attributed to King
[[Solomon]]. It gathers **five distinct books of spirit magic** under one cover, "Detailing the
Ceremonial Art of Commanding Spirits Both Good and Evil":

1. **Ars Goetia** — the evocation and constraint of 72 spirits;
2. **Theurgia-Goetia** — the 31 aerial spirits of the compass, mixed good and evil;
3. **Ars Paulina** — the angels of the 24 hours and the 360 degrees of the zodiac;
4. **Ars Almadel** — the angels of the four Altitudes, contacted via a wax tablet;
5. **Ars Notoria** — the prayer-based art of instantly acquiring the arts and sciences.

Peterson also appends **Johann Weyer's *Pseudomonarchia Daemonum*** (1577), an earlier demon-catalogue
that is the direct textual ancestor of the Goetia's spirit list. See the umbrella concept
[[The Lemegeton (Lesser Key of Solomon): a compilation of five books of Solomonic spirit magic]].

## Strict-extraction note

Recorded as *what the Lemegeton and Peterson's edition present* — attributed and **not adjudicated**.

## Structure & scope

Peterson's Introduction (textual history + manuscript sources), then Parts I–V, then appendices (Sloane
addenda, Weyer's *Pseudomonarchia*, a Goetia/Weyer comparison, and plates). *Scope: extract the
character of each of the five Arts, the Goetic system of 72 spirits and its ritual apparatus, and the
Weyer ancestry — not every individual spirit, seal, or oration.*
