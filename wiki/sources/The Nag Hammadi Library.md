## Source Description

The **Nag Hammadi Library** is the collection of some fifty-two Gnostic and related texts found in
Coptic codices near Nag Hammadi, Egypt, in 1945 — the primary corpus for reconstructing **Gnosticism**
from its own scriptures rather than from the hostile summaries of the Church Fathers. It contains the
keystone Sethian cosmology of the **Apocryphon (Secret Book) of John**, the **Hypostasis of the
Archons** and **On the Origin of the World**, the **Gospel of Thomas**, **Gospel of Philip**, **Gospel
of Truth**, **Gospel of Mary**, **The Thunder: Perfect Mind**, **Trimorphic Protennoia**, and many more.
See the umbrella [[Gnosticism: the unknowable Father, Sophia's fall, and the archon-creator (Nag Hammadi)]].

This is a major **primary root** for the wiki. Its picture of a world made and ruled by a blind, arrogant
creator-god (**Yaldabaoth**) and his **archons**, beneath a true unknowable Father, is the direct source
of David Icke's "archons"/Demiurge material, parallels Bramley's Custodians, and underlies Bane's
[[Gnostic demons (Bane)]]. (Two of its tractates — the Hermetic *Discourse on the Eighth and Ninth* and
part of the *Asclepius* — also tie it to [[The Corpus Hermeticum]].)

## Strict-extraction / sensitivity note

Recorded as *what the texts teach/narrate* — attributed and **not adjudicated** (neither as history nor
as heresy). A primary ancient corpus; the wiki catalogs its doctrines descriptively.

## Structure & scope

Introductory essays (on gnosis, the 1945 discovery, the characteristics of Gnosticism) + the fifty-two
tractates. *Scope: heavy on the Sethian/Valentinian cosmology (the Father/Pleroma, Sophia's fall,
Yaldabaoth and the archons, the Gnostic Genesis, the divine spark) and the major gospels; selective on
the fragmentary Platonizing ascent texts (Zostrianos, Marsanes, Allogenes) and skipping the duplicated
Hermetic tractates (covered under [[The Corpus Hermeticum]]) and non-Gnostic fragments (Plato, Sextus).*
