## Source Description

*Custodians of Truth: The Continuance of Rex Deus* (2005) by Tim Wallace-Murphy
([[Tim Wallace-Murphy]]) and Marilyn Hopkins ([[Marilyn Hopkins]]) is an alternative-history work
elaborating the **[[Rex Deus]]** hypothesis: that a network of "elect families" descended from the
Davidic and Hasmonean royal lines and the twenty-four *ma'amadot* (hereditary high-priestly families)
of the Jerusalem Temple secretly preserved a suppressed tradition for two thousand years — outwardly
professing the prevailing religion while inwardly following "the true way."

It is an explicit continuation of the authors' earlier *Rex Deus* (2000) and builds on
*[[The Holy Blood and the Holy Grail (Baigent, Leigh, Lincoln 1982)]]*, prompted by an informant
("Michael," a claimed descendant of Hugues de Payen, founder of the [[Knights Templar]]). The book
traces the bloodline narrative chronologically from ancient Egyptian gnosis through biblical Israel,
Jesus, the Templars, the Cathars, the Inquisition, the survival of the tradition, and the St. Clairs
of Roslin and the foundation of Freemasonry.

## Strict-extraction note

This is a speculative alternative-history thesis. Its claims (the Temple schools, Jesus's priestly
parentage, the Rex Deus families, the Templars as a Rex Deus creation) are recorded as *what
Wallace-Murphy & Hopkins argue*, attributed to the source, **not adjudicated** as fact. Where the
book asserts contested historical or religious claims, the wiki records them as the authors' position.

## Structure

Nineteen chapters in a chronological arc: **(1)** Tep Zepi — the origins of Egyptian gnosis ·
**(2)** the Egyptian origins of Judaism · **(3)** Exodus to the Babylonian exile · **(4)** the exile
and the rebuilding of the Temple · **(5)** Biblical Israel, John the Baptist, and Jesus ·
**(6)** James the Just, St. Paul, and the destruction of the Temple · **(7)** the founding of
Christianity, Rabbinical Judaism, and Rex Deus · **(8)** the Dark Ages and the repressive Church ·
**(9)** the rise of the Rex Deus aristocracy in Europe · **(10)** the Knights Templar ·
**(11)** the beliefs of the Knights Templar · **(12)** genocide and repression (the Cathars) ·
**(13)** the Holy Inquisition · **(14)** the decline and fall of the Templars · **(15)** survival and
resurgence · **(16)** the re-authentication of a famous "fake" · **(17)** spreading the light from
Lombardy · **(18)** the St. Clairs of Roslin and the foundation of Freemasonry · **(19)** spiritual
awakening in the 21st century.
