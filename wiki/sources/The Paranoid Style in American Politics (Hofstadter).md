## Source Description

*The Paranoid Style in American Politics and Other Essays* (1965) by historian Richard Hofstadter
([[Richard Hofstadter]]) collects his landmark title essay — which names and analyzes the **"paranoid
style"**, a recurring mode of political expression marked by "heated exaggeration, suspiciousness, and
conspiratorial fantasy" — with related studies of the American Right. It is the wiki's key
**meta-counterweight**: rather than debunking any single claim, it characterizes the *rhetorical and
cognitive mode* shared by much of the wiki's conspiracy corpus (Cooper, Icke, Marrs, Coleman, Webster,
Allen, Jeffries). See the umbrella [[The paranoid style in politics (Hofstadter)]].

Hofstadter stresses the style is about **how** ideas are held, not whether they are true; that it is
used by "more or less normal people," not clinical paranoiacs; that its central image is a **vast,
sinister conspiracy as the motive force of history**, framed apocalyptically as an all-out crusade; and
that it **recurs** across U.S. history with the same form but changing villains — the anti-Illuminati
panic (Robison and Barruel), anti-Masonry, anti-Catholic nativism, the Populist "international bankers"
conspiracy, McCarthyism, and the John Birch Society.

## Structure

The collection has two parts. **Part I — Studies in the American Right:** "The Paranoid Style in
American Politics" (the title essay, in sections I–V, with extensive endnotes), "The Pseudo-Conservative
Revolt" (1954), and "Pseudo-Conservatism Revisited / Goldwater and Pseudo-Conservative Politics" (1965).
**Part II — Some Problems of the Modern Era:** "Free Silver and the Mind of 'Coin' Harvey," "What
Happened to the Antitrust Movement?", and "Cuba, the Philippines, and Manifest Destiny."

*Scope note: the wiki ingests Part I (the title essay in depth, plus the pseudo-conservative/status-
politics thesis), which is what bears on its conspiracy material. The Part II economic- and
imperial-history essays are out of scope for this wiki and are not extracted.*
