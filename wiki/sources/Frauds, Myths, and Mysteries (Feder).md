## Source Description

*Frauds, Myths, and Mysteries: Science and Pseudoscience in Archaeology* by archaeologist Kenneth L.
Feder is the standard scholarly text debunking **pseudo-archaeology**. It is the wiki's principal
**counterweight to the ancient-astronaut and lost-civilization cluster** — directly rebutting the kinds
of claims made by von Däniken, Sitchin, Hancock, Hapgood, Temple, Bramley, and the Emerald Tablets. See
the umbrella [[Pseudo-archaeology and how to detect it (Feder)]].

Feder teaches the epistemology of "how you know what you know" about the past, dissects classic hoaxes
(the Cardiff Giant, Piltdown Man), and works through the major myths chapter by chapter: lost
continents (Atlantis/Mu), pre-Columbian "discoverers" of America, the Mound Builder myth, ancient
astronauts, the supposed "mystery" of the pyramids and Nazca, and psychic archaeology — showing in each
case how real archaeology explains the evidence without exotic claims.
