## Source Description

*Magick in Theory and Practice* (Part III of *Liber ABA / Book 4*, 1929) is [[Aleister Crowley]]'s
systematic treatise on ceremonial **Magick**. It opens with his famous definition — "**Magick is the
Science and Art of causing Change to occur in conformity with Will**" — and a set of numbered Theorems,
then works through the **magical theory** (Will, the Qabalistic universe, correspondences), the
**instruments** (the Temple, Circle, Wand, Cup, Sword, Pantacle), and the **operations** (banishing,
consecration, invocation, evocation, the formulae such as IAO and Tetragrammaton, divination, and the
attainment of the Great Work). See the umbrella [[Thelema and Crowley's system of Magick]].

This is the **systematic core** of Crowley's method and the primary root behind later operative magic in
the wiki, including [[Liber Null & Psychonaut (Carroll)|Carroll's chaos magick]].

## Structure & scope

An Introduction (Definition, Postulate, Theorems) and ~25 chapters (0–XXI plus appendices). *Scope:
extract the core doctrines — the definition of Magick and the Will, the magical/Qabalistic universe, the
weapons and Temple, invocation/evocation and the formulae, and the Great Work — rather than every
chapter and footnote.*
