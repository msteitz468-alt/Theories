## Source Description

*Science and the Akashic Field: An Integral Theory of Everything* (2004) is **[[Ervin Laszlo]]**'s foundational, scientific statement of the **Akashic field** hypothesis — the theoretical book behind the testimony-driven *[[The Akashic Experience (Laszlo)]]* (2009). Laszlo argues that mainstream science faces a growing catalog of **"puzzles of coherence"** across four domains — quantum physics (entanglement/nonlocality), cosmology (fine-tuning and large-scale coherence), biology (the improbable coherence of organisms and evolution), and consciousness (transpersonal and nonlocal mind) — that the standard fragmented theories cannot explain.

His proposed solution is an **in-formation field** rooted in the **quantum vacuum** (zero-point field): a cosmic "plenum" that records and conveys the in-formation of all things, making the universe a coherent, self-remembering whole. Reviving the Sanskrit *Akasha*, he names this the **A-field** and argues it is the missing element of a genuine **integral "Theory of Everything"** — one that must include consciousness, not merely matter. He extends it cosmologically to a cyclic **Metaverse** in which information is carried from one universe to the next. The book anchors the wiki's [[Post-materialist science]] cluster alongside Radin, Sheldrake, and Lanza.

## Structure

Introduction + Part One: *The Foundations of an Integral Theory of Everything* (the challenge of a TOE; "puzzles and fables"; the catalog of the puzzles of coherence in quantum physics, cosmology, biology, and consciousness; "in-formation in nature" — the quantum vacuum and the Akashic field) + Part Two: *The In-formed Universe* (how the A-field informs matter, life, mind, and the cosmos; the cyclic Metaverse) + an autobiographical afterword.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Laszlo presents the A-field as the most economical hypothesis that unifies the coherence puzzles, while conceding it is a frontier proposal that reinterprets the quantum vacuum in a way mainstream physics does not accept; he frames it as a research program (an "integral TOE") rather than a finished, experimentally confirmed theory.
