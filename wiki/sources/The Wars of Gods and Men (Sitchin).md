## Source Description

*The Wars of Gods and Men* (1985) is Book III of the *Earth Chronicles*. Where Book I established the Nefilim and Book II their spaceport infrastructure, this volume narrates the **conflicts among the gods themselves** — read as literal history behind the myths of warring deities (Horus and Seth, Zeus and Indra). Sitchin reconstructs the "Olden Gods'" rivalries, the division of Earth's regions among the Anunnaki lineages of [[Enki (Sitchin)|Enki]] and [[Enlil (Sitchin)|Enlil]], and the **Pyramid Wars** for control of the spaceport facilities, culminating in the ambition of [[Marduk (Sitchin)|Marduk]] to seize supremacy.

The climax is Sitchin's most dramatic claim: that around **2024 BC** the Anunnaki used **nuclear weapons** to destroy the Sinai spaceport and the "cities of the plain" (Sodom and Gomorrah), the radioactive cloud from which doomed Sumer — events he reads into the biblical account of Abraham and Lot. Per the wiki's strict-extraction policy, claims are catalogued **as presented**; these readings are not accepted by mainstream scholarship.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Sitchin presents the warring-gods narrative as the literal history myth only "remembers"; he is candid that he is synthesizing fragments from many cultures into one chronology, and that the nuclear-destruction reading hinges on his interpretation of specific Sumerian lament texts and the biblical Sodom account.
