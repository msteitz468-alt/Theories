## Source Description

*The Emerald Tablets of Thoth the Atlantean* is an esoteric text presented by **M. Doreal** (founder of the Brotherhood of the White Temple) as his English translation of tablets written by **Thoth**, an Atlantean priest-king. Per Doreal's preface, the tablets date "back some 36,000 years B.C.," were authored by Thoth — builder of the Great Pyramid of Giza ("erroneously attributed to Cheops") — and were physically recovered by Doreal, who claims a connection to the "Great White Lodge," translated them in 1925, and was permitted to publish only part.

The work consists of a preface and interpretive introduction by Doreal, followed by the tablets themselves, rendered as free verse in an archaic, King-James-inflected idiom. Doreal states there are ten tablets "divided into thirteen parts for the sake of convenience," with the final two withheld; this edition prints Tablets 1–13 plus a Supplementary (Tablet 14) and Tablet 15 ("Secret of Secrets"). The doctrine blends Atlantis lore, Egyptian motifs, Hermeticism ("as above, so below"), Theosophy (the Great White Brotherhood, Shamballa, root-race cycles), and yogic/astral-projection technique.

## Chapter Summaries

### Preface & Introduction (Doreal)
Doreal frames the provenance: the tablets are the work of **Thoth**, an Atlantean priest-king who founded a colony in Egypt after the sinking of the mother country, ruled Egypt for some 16,000 years (c. 52,000–36,000 B.C.), and was the true builder of the Great Pyramid of Giza ("erroneously attributed to Cheops"). Thoth was an immortal who conquered death; he later incarnated as Hermes "thrice-born." Doreal claims the tablets are physically imperishable, fashioned of an alchemically-transmuted green material inscribed in Atlantean, that he recovered them and translated them in 1925, and was permitted to publish only part.

### Tablet 1: The History of Thoth, the Atlantean
Thoth recounts his life in the great city of KEOR on the island of UNDAL in Atlantis, where the mighty ones renewed their lives in the Halls of Amenti through which "the river of life flows eternally onward." He describes his father THOTME, the temple, and the "Children of Light" who dwelt among the Atlanteans. He tells how the Atlanteans turned to darkness until the Dweller called down the power that sank Atlantis, sparing only the Temple. Thoth then led his people by "the great ship of the Master" to the land of KHEM (Egypt), where he subdued the hairy barbarians by his "magic-science," raised a pyramid over a gateway to Amenti, and set within its apex a crystal channelling force from the ether.

### Tablet 2: The Halls of Amenti
Deep in Earth's heart, beneath sunken Atlantis, lie the Halls of Amenti, built by the Children of Light who descended and took on human bodies to free men from bondage. Thirty-two of the Children sit on thrones around a central "flower" of life-giving radiance; one hundred years out of each thousand they bathe their first-created bodies in the cold fire of life. Above them sit the Seven Lords from the Space-Times — the Veiled Presence (the "infinite Nine") and the Lords of the Cycles Three through Eight — who guide the destiny of men. The Dweller leads Thoth before the figure of Death, who reveals souls as flames "coming and going like fireflies," and Thoth is made free of the Halls and chooses to be a teacher of men.

### Tablet 3: The Key of Wisdom
A book of moral and gnomic instruction: wisdom is power and power is wisdom; follow thine heart; silence is profit; debate a friend alone to test his heart. Thoth then teaches that "man is a star bound to a body," freed only through strife, and that one must "divine the material from the immaterial, the fire from the earth." He names the Seven Lords of Amenti by their numbers Three through Nine and assigns each a function (Three binds souls and creates the Halls of the Dead; Four looses the power of life; Five is Lord of all magic; etc.).

### Tablet 4: The Space Born
Thoth recounts how in his Atlantean youth he longed to "conquer the pathway that led to the stars," and how his soul broke free of the body and travelled through the cosmos, finding Law working among gigantic planets and many races of men. He encounters beings who, "free of space," built a planet from condensed ether by thought alone. The tablet teaches that "man is truly space-born, a Sun of the Sun, a child of the stars," and gives a technique of astral projection (fasting, darkness, centring the soul-force, vibrating it free, and projecting a mind-formed image of the desired place).

### Tablet 5: The Dweller of Unal
Thoth tells of **HORLET, the Dweller of UNAL**, a master "from a cycle beyond us" who appeared when Atlantis first grew as a power, divided the kingdom into ten sections, and built a black temple out of the ether by the power of his mind, choosing Three as his gateway to the kings. Thoth is summoned before the Dweller and made keeper of records. When Atlantean adepts "deep delved into the forbidden" and opened the gateway "that led to below," the Dweller called on the Seven Lords' power and sank Atlantis, shattering the gateway and sparing only UNAL. Thoth is commanded to take the records to KHEM, where he buries his spaceship beneath a lion-shaped marker (the Sphinx) and builds the pyramid.

### Tablet 6: The Key of Magic
Thoth describes the "age old Battle of Darkness and Light." The **Brothers of Darkness** — adepts banded "as in order" — work secretly through dark magic to enslave the souls of men and reach into the mind-space, while the **Brothers of Light**, "Suns and Lords of the morning," guard men unseen. He gives protective techniques: testing whether a dark feeling comes from within or is directed at one; sending a wave of vibration from head to foot; and a circle-ritual invocation calling the Seven Lords by both their numbers and their secret names (UNTANAS, QUERTAS, CHIETAL, GOYANA, HUERTAL, SEMVETA, ARDAL) to free oneself from the Brothers of Darkness.

### Tablet 7: The Seven Lords
Thoth records his dialogues with the Seven Lords of the Cycles, who declare they came "from beyond Space-Time… from the place of Infinity's end," were "formed forth in order by Law," and were once as men. Each (Three through Nine) has a duty and a force to control but is "ONE with the SOUL of our cycle." The Lord of the Nine speaks of a future in which life and death become one in the ALL; the Lord of the Eight says all man knows is "but part of a little." The tablet closes with an invocation to the Light to make the seeker "One with the ALL-SOUL."

### Tablet 8: The Key of Mystery
Thoth teaches that "mystery is but hidden knowledge" and that man is "in process of changing to forms that are not of this world," becoming formless before becoming one with the Light. He then tells of the **serpent-headed "children of shadows"** — beings of another vibration called up by dark magic from "the great deep below," who could take form only through blood, crept into the Councils of Atlantis disguised as men, slew the kings, and ruled in their place until the Masters lifted the veil and cast them out with a Word of power. He warns they still live in a hidden place and will again "take the semblance of men." He also describes the **Hounds of the Barrier** ("Dwellers in Angles") that pursue souls who pass the barrier of time-space, escapable only by moving through curves and circles.

### Tablet 10: The Key of Time
The Dweller answers Thoth's question "What is time?" — in the beginning was a timeless, spaceless nothingness into which came a purposeful all-pervading thought, and "for thought to be eternal, time must exist," so the Law of Time grew. Time does not move; consciousness moves through it from event to event. Thoth learns to move through time by "strange angles" and curves. He gives the word of power "ZIN-URU," and recounts an invocation by which, robed and crowned within a circle of cinnabar, he called the "Lady of Light" out of ARULU (calling on the dark Lords by their secret names). He closes: "Man is only what he believeth, a brother of darkness or a child of the Light."

### Tablet 11: The Key to Above and Below
Addressed to the children of KHEM. Thoth recalls finding them "in caves of rocks," barely above beasts, and raising them by his power. He reveals that keys are hidden in the pyramid (a line drawn from the great image/Sphinx to the apex, and another opposite, locating an underground entrance to ancient secrets). He expounds the cycles of consciousness: there are nine cycles above and fourteen below, each a "cell in the process of growth," each following its own path; the Lords of the Cycles are "units of consciousness" working in harmony with the Law toward a future "joining" when all become "One in a perfected Infinity."

### Tablet 12: The Law of Cause and Effect and the Key of Prophecy
Thoth declares he has "conquered the Law of time-space" and that "all of the future is an open book to him who can read," though the future is not fixed but varies with man's free will as cause produces effect. He prophesies the rise and fall of races, that the land of KHEM will be inhabited by barbarians, that the Dark Brothers will open a great warfare when man "shall conquer the ocean and fly in the air… and harness the lightning," that weapons of force will destroy half the races, and that then the Sons of the Morning will intervene, Atlantis will rise again, and an Age of Light under the Brothers of Light will unfold. He gives the name of power for summoning him: "Chequetet, Arelich, Volmalites."

### Tablet 13: The Keys of Life and Death
Thoth reveals that "the gateway to life is through death," and that the body is "dual, balanced in polarity"; death comes "only because thy balance is shaken… one pole has been lost." He gives a technique to hold off death by orienting the head to the north (positive) and south (negative) poles for an hour each per day, balancing once in seven, so that an aged body "will freshen." He gives a second technique for willed death (lying with head to the east, dividing the consciousness north and south, releasing the "silver spark" to fly to the Sun of the morning), and a third for retaining memory of past lives across the transition of death by holding the soul-flame between the eyebrows.

### Tablet 14: Supplementary
Thoth teaches that Earth is "but a portal" guarded by the Dark Lords, and that the way to the sphere of **Arulu** is opened only to the Light-born. He establishes the Mysteries (to be veiled in symbols and made into a religion) so the secrets endure. He details Egyptian-style cosmology: the regions Duat and Sekhet Hetspet, Osiris as guard of the portal, the seven mansions, the Forty-Two great powers who judge the dead, the Sons of Horus, Isis "Queen of the Moon," and the distinction of Ba (eternal Essence) and Ka (the shadow man calls life). He sets out Druidic-style triads (three circles of existence, three paths of the Soul, three hindrances) and recounts standing before the Holy One enthroned in the Flower of Fire, glimpsing primeval chaos, and pledging to renounce his "Great right" until his Earth-work is done.

### Tablet 15: Secret of Secrets
Promising to "plainly speak of the Unveiled Mysteries" with "no dark sayings," Thoth teaches that darkness is disorder and light is order, and man's purpose is "transmutation of darkness to light." Man is threefold (physical, astral, mental) — nine qualities in all, "as above, so below" — directed by the fourfold Spiritual Self, "thirteen in one." He gives the words of power for releasement: "Dor-E-Lil-La" to pass the barriers of space for six hours, "La-Um-I-L-Ganoover" to free the self from the flesh, the formula "Mekut-El-Shab-El Hale-Sur-Ben-El-Zabrut Zin-Efrim-Quar-El" to enter the Halls of Amenti, and the Key to **Shamballa** where his Brothers dwell ("Edom-El-Ahim-Sabbert-Zur Adom").

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

The work itself flags omissions and gradations: Doreal says the two greatest tablets are "forbidden to release"; Thoth declares "the highest knowledge is unutterable" and that his Hermes-incarnation writings (the Emerald Tablets known to "modern occultists") are "a later and far lesser exposition of the ancient mysteries" than the tablets translated here. Tablet 15 promises to "plainly… speak of the Unveiled Mysteries" with "no dark sayings," in contrast to the deliberately veiled earlier tablets.
