## Source Description

*Liber Null & Psychonaut* is a foundational text of **[[Chaos Magic]]** by Peter J. Carroll
([[Peter J. Carroll]]), combining two works — *Liber Null* (1978) and *Psychonaut* (1981) — in one
volume (the ingested copy is a modern edition with a 2022 foreword by historian
[[Ronald Hutton]]). Written originally as a sourcebook for a secretive group of chaos magicians
(the precursor of the Illuminates of Thanateros), it presents magic as "an intensely practical,
personal, experimental art" stripped of dogma: "altered states of consciousness are the key to
unlocking one's magical abilities," and "these abilities can be developed without any symbolic
system except reality itself."

Carroll draws techniques (not dogma) from shamanism, the Golden Dawn, Aleister Crowley
([[Aleister Crowley]]), and especially Austin Osman Spare's Zos Kia Cultus
([[Austin Osman Spare]]). *Liber Null* is structured as graded syllabi (Neophyte → Initiate →
Adept) in four sub-books — **Liber MMM, Liber LUX, Liber NOX, Liber AOM** — while *Psychonaut*
applies the same approach to group ritual, consciousness, and a "scientific"/chaos-theory framing of
magic.

## Strict-extraction note

This is a primary esoteric/instructional text. Its assertions (Kia, Chaos, the aether, magical
powers, the aeons) are recorded as *what Carroll teaches*, attributed to the source, without
adjudicating their truth or endorsing any practice. Techniques are catalogued descriptively, not as
instructions to follow.

## Structure

**LIBER NULL** — *The Quest* (the chaos tradition, its grades and aeonic purpose) ·
**Liber MMM** (Neophyte syllabus: mind control / magical trance, metamorphosis) ·
**Liber LUX** (Initiate syllabus, "white" magic: the theory of Chaos, Kia, duality, aether, mind; and
the practical arts) · **Liber NOX** (the "black"/shadow side) · **Liber AOM** (the work of the Adept:
the light & dark powers, aetherics, the magician as microcosm).

**PSYCHONAUT** — Introduction · New Aeon Magic · Group Magical Experiments · Levels of Consciousness ·
Magical Combat · The Rites of Chaos · Magical Time · Chemognosis · Magical Perspectives · Animism and
Shamanism · Gnosticism · Occult Priestcraft · Magical Weapons · Magical Paradigms · Anecdotes ·
Catastrophe Theory and Magic · The Transitions · Chaos Theory and Magic.

## Section Summaries

### LIBER NULL

**The Quest / Liber MMM (Neophyte syllabus)** — Defines magic ("the science and art of causing change
to occur in conformity with will") and grounds it in **[[Gnosis (chaos magic)]]** — the focused
altered state reached through mind control (motionlessness, breathing, not-thinking, concentration)
and through inhibitory/excitatory trance including **[[The Death Posture]]**. Core theses:
[[Magic is causing change in conformity with will; gnosis (the gnostic state) is the necessary condition]],
[[Lust of result short-circuits magic; desire must be encoded and forgotten to work]],
[[Metamorphosis: the magician must restructure belief and habit deliberately, using will as the instrument of self-transformation]],
and the technique **[[Sigil magic (Carroll)]]** —
[[Sigilization encodes desire into an abstract glyph to bypass the conscious mind and deliver it directly to the subconscious will]].

**Liber LUX (Initiate syllabus, "white" magic)** — The metaphysics: **[[Chaos (Carroll's metaphysical principle)]]**
(the meaningless source-force, "God or Tao"), **[[Kia (Carroll)]]** (the unified will-perception "I," a
fragment of the cosmic life force), and **[[The Aether (Carroll)]]** (the probabilistic medium magic
manipulates). See [[The Chaos–Kia–aether metaphysics: a three-tier ontology underlying magical causation]].

**Liber NOX (the "black"/shadow side)** — The dark methods and the aeonic frame; see
[[Carroll's five-aeon historical scheme: from shamanism to a nascent Chaoist fifth aeon]] (**[[The Five Aeons (Carroll's aeonics)]]**).

**Liber AOM (the work of the Adept)** — **[[The work of the Adept (Carroll)]]**: the twin light and dark
forces (*solve et coagula*) of chaos and the aim of becoming "a center of creation and destruction … a
god"; the adept's aetheric powers. See
[[The magician aims to become a god by balancing the twin light and dark forces of chaos]] and
[[The adept develops aetheric powers over dense and subtle matter, from levitation to telepathy]].

### PSYCHONAUT

**New Aeon Magic** — **[[New Aeon Magic (Carroll)]]**: experimental technique plus a chaos-aligned
spirituality. See [[New Aeon Magic proposes experimental technique and a chaos-aligned spirituality as the next advance of humanity]].

**Group Magical Experiments / Levels of Consciousness** —
[[Group magical work achieves synergistic power, and consciousness operates across five distinct levels]].

**The Rites of Chaos** — **[[The Rites of Chaos]]**: a ceremonial toolkit (Mass of Chaos, Initiation,
Exorcism, Extreme Unction, Ordination). See
[[The Rites of Chaos provide a complete ceremonial structure for group chaos magic practice]].

**Chemognosis** — **[[Chemognosis]]**: chemical means of attaining gnosis, with the source's own
cautions. See [[Chemognosis: chemical agents assist receptive magic but cannot substitute for training]].

**Magical Paradigms** — **[[Magical Paradigm shifting (Carroll)]]**: belief as a pragmatic instrument.
See [[Belief is a magical tool: the chaos magician adopts and discards paradigms instrumentally]].

**Animism and Shamanism** —
[[Animism and shamanism are the original substrate from which all later magical traditions derive]].

**Catastrophe Theory and Magic** — **[[Catastrophe Theory and Magic]]**:
[[Catastrophe theory mathematically models how magical and mystical initiations produce sudden worldview shifts]].

**Chaos Theory and Magic** — **[[Chaos Theory and Magic]]**:
[[Chaos theory reveals that sorcerers must enchant long and divine short]].
