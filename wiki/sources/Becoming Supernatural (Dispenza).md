## Source Description

*Becoming Supernatural: How Common People Are Doing the Uncommon* (2017) by Dr. Joe Dispenza
([[Joe Dispenza]]), with a foreword by [[Gregg Braden]], teaches that ordinary people can transcend
their material conditioning and "become supernatural" — healing disease, altering their biology,
accessing a "quantum field" of possibility, having mystical/interdimensional experiences, and
collectively changing the world — through a specific practice combining **brain coherence**,
**heart coherence** (developed with the HeartMath Institute), "blessing the **energy centers**,"
mental rehearsal ("Mind Movies"), walking meditation, and activation of the **pineal gland**. See
the umbrella [[Becoming supernatural (Dispenza's method)]].

Dispenza presents the book as "bridging the world of science with the world of mysticism," supported
by his organization's EEG brain scans, heart-rate-variability data, and case studies from multi-day
"advanced workshops." It sits alongside the wiki's other consciousness sources —
[[Stalking the Wild Pendulum (Bentov, 1977)]], [[The Holographic Universe (Talbot)]],
[[Reality Transurfing I–V (Zeland)]], and the Monroe material.

## Strict-extraction note

Dispenza makes strong, contested scientific-sounding claims (self-healing of disease, changes in gene
expression, group-meditation effects on crime, photon/energy-field measurement). He himself notes
critics "might call my work pseudoscience." The wiki records these as *what Dispenza teaches/claims/
reports*, attributed to the source and **not adjudicated**; case studies are recorded as "Dispenza
reports," not as verified outcomes.
