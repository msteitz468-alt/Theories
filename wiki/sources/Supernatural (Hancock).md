## Source Description

*Supernatural: Meetings With the Ancient Teachers of Mankind* (2005) by **[[Graham Hancock]]** turns from lost-civilization archaeology to the origins of the human mind, religion, and the "spirit world." Hancock — recounting his own experiences with iboga, ayahuasca, and DMT — argues that the "creative explosion" of symbolic culture ~40,000 years ago coincided with humans learning to enter **altered states of consciousness**, often via psychoactive plants, and that the **painted caves of the Upper Paleolithic** (Chauvet, Lascaux, Altamira) are records of visions seen in trance, interpreted through David Lewis-Williams's neuropsychological model.

His most provocative claim is one of **continuity**: the part-human/part-animal "therianthropes" and the strange beings of cave art, the spirits of shamanism, the fairies and elves of folklore, the "machine elves" of the DMT state, and the "greys" of modern alien-abduction reports are, he argues, encounters with the *same* category of seemingly autonomous intelligences — the "ancient teachers of mankind." Drawing on Lewis-Williams, Terence McKenna, Jeremy Narby, Rick Strassman, and Jacques Vallée, the book bridges the wiki's consciousness, entheogen, and UFO-contact material; it is a direct companion to *[[Communion (Strieber)]]*, *[[Passport to Magonia: From Folklore to Flying Saucers (Vallée, 1969)]]*, and the DMT-entity literature.

## Structure

Five Parts across seventeen chapters: I The Visions and the Caves (cave art and the neuropsychological model) · II The Beings (therianthropes; fairies, spirits, and aliens) · III The Codes (DNA and Jeremy Narby's cosmic-serpent thesis) · IV The Religions (shamanism as the root of religion) · V The Mysteries (DMT, the "machine elves," and the reality status of the spirit world).

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Hancock holds two readings in tension and declines to fully adjudicate: that the beings are *internal* (products of brain chemistry and the neuropsychological "hard-wiring" of the nervous system) or *external* (objectively real entities in a parallel dimension that hallucinogens allow us to perceive). He repeatedly frames the question as open, while leaning toward the reality of the encounter.
