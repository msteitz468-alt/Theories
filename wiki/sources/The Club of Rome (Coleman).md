## Source Description

*The Club of Rome* (2008; first edition 1970) is a short conspiracy tract by **Dr. John Coleman** (former British intelligence officer turned conspiracy author, best known for *The Committee of 300*). In 16 chapters Coleman argues that the Club of Rome (COR) — a real environmental think-tank founded in 1968 — is in fact "the chief 'think tank' for the New World Order," a secret society set up "on the orders of the **Committee of 300**" to engineer the **deindustrialization and depopulation** of the United States and the world under a one-world government.

Coleman frames the COR as the modern successor of the French and Bolshevik Revolutions and the Illuminati, staffed by the **Black Nobility** of Venice and Genoa, pursuing a "Post-Industrial Zero-Growth" policy and a program of "global genocide" embodied in the *Global 2000 Report*.

**Note on extraction:** This is a conspiracist polemic. Its central claims — a controlling "Committee of 300," a planned cull of 2 billion people, the Club of Rome as author of the Pol Pot genocide, environmentalism as a depopulation weapon — are rejected by mainstream scholarship; the real Club of Rome is a sustainability think-tank, and *The Limits to Growth* (1972) is a systems-dynamics study, not a genocide blueprint. The book also contains antisemitic and anti-Masonic occult framing. Per the wiki's strict-extraction policy, claims are catalogued **as the source presents them**, without endorsement.

## Chapter Summaries

### Chapter 1: Echoes of the French Revolution
Coleman argues that the explosive events of the 20th century were planned "according to a well laid out blueprint" by occult-based secret societies controlled by the Committee of 300. He traces the Club of Rome back to the French Revolution, which he says had its roots in England where the Earl of Shelburne trained Malthus, Adam Smith, Danton and Marat. He gives a history of the **Illuminati** — sprung from the Rosicrucians, founded in Bavaria by Adam Weishaupt on 1 May 1776 — whose principal business is "carrying on war against Christianity" and overthrowing existing governments to bring about a One World Government, beginning with the French Revolution.

### Chapter 2: Crowley, Pike and Mazzini
Coleman asserts that Freemasonry and Illuminism never died out and are stronger now than at the time of the French Revolution. He claims Anglo-Saxon and European Grand Orient Masonry are one occult system, names the three main Masonic rites, and identifies **Aleister Crowley** as a Patriot Grand Master who "greatly influenced the policies of the Club of Rome," quoted Malthus and Adam Smith (servants of the British East India Company, "today known as the Committee of 300"), and allegedly took part in over 150 ritual murders of children. He concludes the COR is an "unbroken succession of secret societies" working toward a New Dark Age under the Committee of 300.

### Chapter 3: What is the Club of Rome?
Coleman states the name "Club of Rome" was chosen to deceive and has nothing to do with the Vatican; the COR consists of the oldest **Black Nobility** families of Europe, descendants of those who ran Genoa and Venice in the 12th century (the Guelph dynasty; Queen Elizabeth II as a "Black Guelph"). He describes a secret two-part COR conference in Washington D.C. in November–December 1980 (financed by the German Marshall Fund, chaired by David Rockefeller), recorded by an intelligence officer, whose agenda was "how best to dismember the industrial heart of the United States" and rid it of its "surplus population," nullify the Reagan presidency, and radicalize the Democrat Party. He names attendees (Benn, Brandt, Palme, Mitterand, Gonzalez, Agee) and cites the Pol Pot regime in Cambodia as the first experiment in breaking up cities.

### Chapter 4: Connection to Global Genocide
Coleman claims the COR is anti-national and suppresses scientific development, maintaining connections with terrorist organizations like the Red Brigades and the Baader-Meinhof gang through Bettino Craxi. He says the December 1980 follow-up meeting "endorsed and accepted the COR's Global 2000 Report — a blueprint for global genocide," which called for the death of 2 billion people by 2010, adopted euthanasia as policy, and embraced Russell's term "useless eaters." He insists U.S. industrial workers, not just non-white races, are targets, and ties COR planning to the retention of Paul Volcker at the Federal Reserve and to deliberate destabilization of nations.

### Chapter 5: Men Are Like Insects
Coleman quotes Mitterand at the 1980 meeting ("Industrial capitalist development is the enemy and the opposite of freedom") and COR founder **Aurelio Peccei** at a March 1982 Paris meeting ("Men are like insects. They proliferate too much"). He lists the COR's objectives: retard industrial development, hold back scientific research, depopulate cities, move populations to rural areas, cull at least 2 billion people, and destabilize the United States through layoffs and racial wars. He likens the COR to the Jacobin terror and warns of mobs to be unleashed on America, citing real 1981 anti-nuclear riots in Paris and Hamburg.

### Chapter 11: NASA and the Club of Rome
Coleman claims one of the American COR's main achievements was moving the space program from the military to the civilian NASA, then attacking NASA itself after a 1967 Tavistock study found it had become a major employer of industrial and science personnel — "the exact opposite of the de-industrializing plans of COR." He describes a Deauville conference attended by Peccei and Brzezinski (inspiring Brzezinski's *Between Two Ages*) that called for US–USSR "convergence" into a One World Government, and argues that WWII was a contrived situation in which the international bankers set up and then destroyed Hitler.

### Chapter 12: The Mess of the Money Systems
Coleman attacks the **World Dynamics / Limits to Growth** model built for the COR by Jay Forrester and Dennis Meadows, calling it a flawed Malthusian study that ignores human ingenuity and the "magic of the fusion torch." He blames "un-backed paper money" and the Federal Reserve for depleting resources, and quotes Peccei's pessimism about nuclear power, arguing the COR rejects nuclear energy precisely because it would create jobs, prosperity, and population growth — all "undesirable" to the COR. He cites the 1973 oil embargo as a "contrived" event that brought people into alignment with COR thinking.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Coleman writes as an intelligence insider citing "minutes of the meeting secretly recorded by an intelligence officer" and his own newsletter *World in Review*; the sourcing is largely his own assertion. He treats real figures' real statements (Peccei, Mitterand, Willis Harmon) as confessions of a genocidal plan, while folding in the occult/Illuminati framing of his other works (the [[Committee of 300 (Coleman)]], the "Olympians," the Cult of Dionysus).
