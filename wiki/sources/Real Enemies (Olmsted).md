## Source Description

*Real Enemies: Conspiracy Theories and American Democracy, World War I to 9/11* (2009) by historian
Kathryn S. Olmsted ([[Kathryn S. Olmsted]]) argues that modern American conspiracy theories gained
their power because the U.S. government itself **really did plot, lie, and cover up** — from WWI
propaganda through Pearl Harbor, McCarthyism, the JFK assassination, Watergate, COINTELPRO/MKULTRA,
Iran-Contra, and 9/11. Her thesis: "to understand contemporary theories, we need to examine the
history of *proven* government conspiracies, because … when [theorists] charge that the government
has plotted, lied, and covered up, they're often right." See the umbrella
[[Real government conspiracies and the rise of conspiracy theories (Olmsted)]].

This makes Olmsted a distinctive **counterweight with a twist**: where Hofstadter, Barkun, Sagan, and
Shermer analyze or debunk conspiracy *belief*, Olmsted (a historian) takes seriously which government
conspiracies were *real* — and shows how official secrecy and deception corroded democratic trust and
fed the conspiracy culture. She opens with **Operation Northwoods** (the 1962 Joint Chiefs plan to
stage terror attacks on Americans and blame Castro) as the emblematic *proven* plot.

## Strict-extraction note

Olmsted's positions — including her assessments of which conspiracies were real vs. imagined — are
recorded as *what she argues*, attributed and **not adjudicated**, the same neutral protocol used for
the other sources.
