## Source Description

*Why People Believe Weird Things: Pseudoscience, Superstition, and Other Confusions of Our Time*
(1997; exp. 2002) by Michael Shermer ([[Michael Shermer]]), founder of the Skeptics Society, is a
companion skeptical counterweight to [[The Demon-Haunted World (Sagan)]]. It both **debunks specific
weird beliefs** (psychics and ESP, alien abduction, recovered-memory witch-hunts, Ayn Rand's
Objectivist "cult," creationism, Holocaust denial) and advances a **psychological thesis** for *why*
people — including smart people — hold them. See the umbrella
[[Why people believe weird things (Shermer's thesis)]].

Shermer's tools include his list of **25 ways thinking goes wrong**, the **"Belief Engine"** (the mind
as a pattern- and meaning-seeking organ), and his key claim that **smart people are *better* at
defending beliefs they arrived at for non-smart reasons.** As with Sagan, this source's value to the
wiki is that its arguments **directly counter** claims made elsewhere in the corpus.

## Strict-extraction note

Shermer's positions are recorded as *what he argues* — attributed and **not adjudicated**, the same

## Structure

Five parts, 18 chapters. **Pt 1 Science & Skepticism** (1 I Am Therefore I Think · 2 The Most
Precious Thing We Have · 3 How Thinking Goes Wrong). **Pt 2 Pseudoscience & Superstition** (4
Deviations · 5 Through the Invisible · 6 Abducted! · 7 Epidemics of Accusations · 8 The Unlikeliest
Cult). **Pt 3 Evolution & Creationism** (9 In the Beginning · 10 Confronting Creationists · 11 Science
Defended, Science Defined). **Pt 4 History & Pseudohistory** (12 Doing Donahue · 13 Who Says the
Holocaust Never Happened · 14 How We Know the Holocaust Happened · 15 Pigeonholes and Continuums).
**Pt 5 Hope Springs Eternal** (16 Dr. Tipler Meets Dr. Pangloss · 17 Why Do People Believe Weird
Things? · 18 Why Smart People Believe Weird Things).

[[Shermer argues that psychics and ESP fail every controlled test and that high scores in uncontrolled experiments are statistical artifacts]] ·
[[Shermer argues alien abduction experiences are explained by sleep deprivation, altered states, hypnotic false memory, and cultural feedback]] ·
[[Shermer argues recovered-memory accusations and satanic-panic are modern witch crazes driven by feedback loops, not evidence of real events]] ·
[[Shermer argues Ayn Rand's Objectivism became a cult of personality despite its philosophy of individualism and reason]].

**Pt 3 — Evolution & Creationism** (**[[Creationism's arguments and why they fail (Shermer)]]**).
[[Shermer argues creationism attacks all science — not just evolution — and its 25 standard arguments all fail]] ·
[[Shermer argues what separates science from pseudoscience is testability, naturalism, and the willingness to be wrong]].

**Pt 4 — History & Pseudohistory** (**[[Holocaust denial as pseudohistory (Shermer)]]**).
[[Shermer argues Holocaust denial shares its rhetorical methods with creationism and both are paradigm pseudohistory]] ·
[[Shermer argues the Holocaust is proved by convergence of evidence and deniers must disprove eighteen independent data streams]] ·
[[Shermer argues pigeonholes distort reality — belief, race, and sexuality exist on continuums not in binary categories]] ·
[[Shermer argues suppressing fringe claims backfires and public rebuttal is more effective than censorship]].

**Pt 5 — Hope Springs Eternal** (**[[The Belief Engine — why people believe weird things (Shermer)]]** · **[[Why smart people believe weird things (Shermer)]]**).
[[Shermer argues wishful cosmology — the Physics of Immortality case — shows how hope shapes even rigorous scientific argument]] ·
[[Shermer argues the mind is a Belief Engine that finds pattern and meaning in noise, generating weird beliefs as a normal cognitive output]] ·
[[Shermer argues that intelligence defends weird beliefs rather than preventing them — smart people are better at rationalizing conclusions reached for non-smart reasons]] ·
[[Shermer argues the confirmation bias and intellectual attribution bias are the core mechanisms by which smart people sustain weird beliefs]].
