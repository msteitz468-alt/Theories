## Source Description

The *Encyclopedia of Demons in World Religions and Cultures* (2012) by Theresa Bane
([[Theresa Bane]]) is an A–Z reference catalog of well over a thousand demons, devils, and malevolent
spirits drawn from the mythology, folklore, and religion of cultures worldwide. Bane — a
"vampirologist" and compiler of myths — compiled it from primary mythologies and grimoires after her
vampire research kept turning up demonic entities. It is a *reference work*, not an argument: each
entry briefly gives a demon's culture of origin, alternate names, attributes, and (where relevant)
its rank, domain, or means of summoning/banishing.

## How this source is catalogued in the wiki

Because the book is an A–Z list of 1,000+ entries, the wiki does **not** create a page per demon
(that would be pure bloat). Instead it is catalogued as **one concept page per major tradition/
culture** — see [[Demonology across world religions (Bane)]] — each summarizing how Bane characterizes
that tradition's demonology and naming a selection of its notable demons, with cross-links into the
wiki's existing occult/esoteric material (Gnostic [[Archons (Gnostic entities)]], the
[[Enochian Magick (Gallery of Magick approach)|Enochian]] and Goetic grimoire demons, the
Jewish–Kabbalistic current, the Mesopotamian [[Anunnaki (Icke)|Anunnaki]], etc.).

## Strict-extraction note

Entries are records of *what the mythologies/grimoires say*, as compiled by Bane — folklore and
religious belief, recorded descriptively and not as factual claims about existing entities.

## Structure

Preface · Introduction (the history and classification/hierarchy of demons — elemental categories,
the *Testament of Solomon*, medieval "departmentalization" of demons by sin, planet, and rank) ·
THE ENCYCLOPEDIA (the A–Z entries) · Bibliography · Index.
