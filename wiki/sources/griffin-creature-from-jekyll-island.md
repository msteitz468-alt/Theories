## Source Description

*The Creature from Jekyll Island: A Second Look at the Federal Reserve* is a book-length critique of the Federal Reserve System by G. Edward Griffin (American Media). It comprises 26 chapters in six sections, ranging from the 1910 Jekyll Island meeting through monetary theory, banking history, war finance, and projected future scenarios. Each chapter opens with an abstract and closes with a SUMMARY in the author's own words.

The book's central thesis is that the Federal Reserve is **a banking cartel with a government facade** — "not federal, with no reserves, and the Federal Reserve Banks are not even banks." Griffin frames the Fed as the institutional realization of a cartel agreement reached secretly at Jekyll Island, and extends the analysis to the IMF/World Bank, the Council on Foreign Relations, and a projected drive toward world government.

**Note on extraction:** Like the Mullins works, this is a partisan, conspiracist account. Many claims (Rothschild/Rhodes control, deliberate engineering of depressions and wars, Wall Street financing of the Bolshevik Revolution, the *Report from Iron Mountain*, and projected world-government scenarios) are contested or rejected by mainstream scholarship. Per the wiki's strict-extraction policy, claims are catalogued as the source presents them, without endorsement.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Griffin's account of Jekyll Island matches Mullins's in substance; he notes one documented dispute — whether [[Benjamin Strong]] actually attended (Andrew Gray's correspondence vs. Vanderlip's memoir) — and sides with Vanderlip. He cites a wide range of mainstream sources (Galbraith, Greider, Chernow, Kolko) selectively, treating their factual findings as corroboration while rejecting their benign interpretations.
