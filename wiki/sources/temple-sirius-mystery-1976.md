## Source Description

*The Sirius Mystery* is a book by researcher Robert K. G. Temple, first published in Great Britain in 1976 by Sidgwick & Jackson, with an abridged edition published in 1977 by Futura Publications. The book presents a detailed historical and astronomical synthesis proposing that Earth was visited in prehistory by intelligent, amphibious extraterrestrial beings from the Sirius star system who established human civilization.

Temple’s thesis is centered on the Dogon tribe of Mali, who possess highly specific cosmological lore concerning the Sirius system. This includes knowledge of Sirius B—a tiny white dwarf companion star that is invisible to the naked eye and was only discovered by modern astronomers in the 19th century. The Dogon describe this companion star as the smallest and heaviest star, made of a super-dense metal, and possessing a 50-year elliptical orbit. Temple argues that this information is over 5,000 years old, inherited from pre-dynastic Egypt, where the Sirius system's astronomy was encoded in the myths of Isis, Osiris, Nephthys, and Anubis, as well as the Greek myth of Jason's ship *Argo* and its fifty Argonauts.

## Chapter Summaries

### Foreword, Author's Note, and To the Reader
Temple outlines his research, which began in 1965 after Arthur M. Young introduced him to Griaule and Dieterlen's reports on the Dogon. He explains how the Dogon's secret traditions concerning the star *Po Tolo* (Sirius B) match the astronomical realities of the white dwarf star. He argues that this knowledge was inherited from ancient Egypt, where the Sirius mysteries were preserved by esoteric priesthoods.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[The Dogon tribe of Mali possess highly accurate astronomical knowledge about the star Sirius and its invisible companion stars (Sirius B and C) that could only be verified with modern telescopes]]

### Chapter One: The Knowledge of the Dogon
Examines the Dogon's specific claims about the Sirius system. The Dogon describe Sirius B (*Po Tolo*, or Digitaria) as the smallest and heaviest star, containing the "germs of all things" and composed of a heavy metal called *sagala*. They state that it revolves around Sirius A in an elliptical orbit every 50 years. They also describe a third star, *Emme Ya* (Sirius C), which has a planet orbiting it. The planet is inhabited by the Nommos, a race of amphibious, merman-like beings.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[The Dogon tribe of Mali possess highly accurate astronomical knowledge about the star Sirius and its invisible companion stars (Sirius B and C) that could only be verified with modern telescopes]]
  * [[Amphibious, intelligent beings named the Nommos visited Earth from a planet in the Sirius system, imparting astronomical and cultural knowledge to humans before returning]]

### Chapter Two: A Fairytale
Written from the perspective of an ancient Egyptian priest, this chapter describes the heliacal rising of Sothis (Sirius) after 70 days in the Underworld (Duat), which formed the basis of the Sothic calendar and the 70-day embalming rites. Temple connects the Egyptian pantheon to the Sirius system: Isis represents Sirius A; her sister Nephthys represents the dark, invisible companion (Sirius B); and Anubis represents the dividing circle or orbit that separates them.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[The ancient Egyptians possessed detailed knowledge of the invisible Sirius star system, identifying Isis with Sirius A, Nephthys with Sirius B, and Anubis with the orbit of the companion star]]

### Chapter Three: The Sacred Canal
Investigates the shared astronomical and mythological foundations of ancient Egypt and Mesopotamia. Temple shows that the oldest Egyptian and Sumerian star lore is identical, particularly regarding the dog and jackal symbols associated with the "Dog Star" Sirius.

### Chapter Four: The Fifty Argonauts
Examines the Greek myth of Jason's ship, the *Argo*, which represents the celestial ark of Isis and Osiris. Temple shows that the ship's fifty oarsmen (the Argonauts) and the fifty daughters of Danaus represent the 50-year orbital period of Sirius B. The ship is steered by Canopus, which the Arabs named *Wazn* ("weight"), reflecting the super-heavy nature of the white dwarf companion.
* **Claims Extracted:**
  * [[The Greek myth of Jason's ship, the Argo, and its fifty Argonauts symbolizes the fifty-year orbital period of the star Sirius B]]

### Chapter Five: The Oracle Centres
Explores the geodetic distribution of ancient oracle centers across Greece, Egypt, and Libya. In this abridged edition, Temple outlines how these centers were mathematically aligned to represent a geodetic grid connected to ancient astronomical orientations.

### Chapter Six: The Syrian Connection
Traces the migration of the Egyptian and Mediterranean Sirius traditions to other parts of West Africa, such as the Akan people of Ghana, who also preserve cosmological customs and kingly rituals derived from ancient Egypt.

### Chapter Seven: The Amphibious Civilizers
Focuses on the Babylonian priest Berossus's accounts of Oannes, the amphibious creature who emerged from the Erythraean Sea to teach civilization, writing, and arts to the Sumerians. Temple equates Oannes with the Dogon Nommos. He analyzes the physical conditions of the Sirius system, proposing that the Nommos' home planet orbits Sirius C (*Emme Ya*) and is a moist, watery environment. He describes the Dogon accounts of the Nommos' ark landing on Earth with a thunderous noise, rushing wind, and fire.
