## Source Description

*Echoes of Eden: What Secrets of Human Potential Were Buried with Our Ancestors' Memories of ET Contact?* (2022) is the third book in Paul Wallis's "Eden series," after [[Escaping from Eden (Wallis)]] and [[The Scars of Eden (Wallis)]]. Where the first two argued that the Bible and world mythology encode ancient ET contact ("paleocontact"), this volume turns to **human potential**: Wallis argues that the ET engineers who modified our ancestors deliberately *downgraded* humanity — switching off latent abilities such as self-healing, telepathy, precognition, and remote viewing to make a more manageable workforce — and that indigenous **initiation** rites and shamanic traditions preserve ancient methods for reawakening that buried potential.

The book is structured as a travelogue of "experiencers and researchers, scholars and sages," and is also personal: it opens with Wallis undergoing a regression session (with abduction therapist **Barbara Lamb**, continuing the thread that closed *The Scars of Eden*) in which he recovers the opening moments of a 1985 close encounter, identifying himself as an "experiencer." It carries endorsements from Erich von Däniken and George Noory.

## Structure

Introduction & Chapter One (When Troy was Taken) + Chapters Two–Fourteen — moving among Native American and southern African initiation ceremonies, Australian Aboriginal narratives (Steven & Evan Strong), Mesoamerican texts (the Popol Vuh), modern experiencers (John, Troy), and Washington "Senate briefings," to the question of why governments study indigenous ritual and archaeology.

## Chapter Summaries

### Introduction & Chapter One: When Troy was Taken
Opens with Wallis emerging from a September 2021 regression session in San Diego with abduction therapist Barbara Lamb, in which he recovers the opening moments of a 1985 close encounter and accepts the label "experiencer." He recaps how translation anomalies in Genesis led him out of consensus Christianity into "paleocontact," then introduces Troy, a Navajo boy taken from his Arizona home at thirteen for a months-long wilderness initiation that physically and mentally transforms him — the first of many rites preparing him as a seer/guardian. Frames the book's thesis: human history is a "treasure house of disclosure and suppression."
