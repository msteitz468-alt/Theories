## Source Description

*UFOs and the National Security State: The Cover-Up Exposed, 1973–1991* (2009) by historian **[[Richard M. Dolan]]** is the second volume of his document-based history, continuing the chronology begun in *[[UFOs and the National Security State (Dolan)]]* (Vol. 1, 1941–1973). Covering 1973 to 1991, it argues that the UFO phenomenon is genuine, anomalous, and "most assuredly 'not us,'" and that it is "actively being covered up" by a power structure whose secret-keepers, Dolan now suggests, are "beyond nations" — no longer wholly answerable to the U.S. government alone.

Prioritizing cases "with military implications" (drawing on FOIA documents, U.S./Soviet military and intelligence reports, journalistic interception accounts, and MUFON investigations), Dolan threads them into a history of the deepening **national security state** ([[The national security state (Dolan)]]): the November 1975 incursions over SAC nuclear bases, the failed promise of Carter-era openness, the Cash–Landrum and Rendlesham (Bentwaters) cases, the Bennewitz disinformation affair, the surfacing of the MJ-12 documents amid the late-1980s "explosion" of abduction research, and the emergence of Area 51 / Bob Lazar into public view. He argues UFO secrecy was a key driver of the "black budget" and a corrosive force on democratic government.

## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

Dolan distinguishes "ours" from "theirs" — conceding that some UFOs are "secret breakthroughs by all-too-human groups" while arguing the core phenomenon is genuinely non-human — and admits this separation is "difficult and… thankless." He remains a measured historian, inferring the cover-up from documented behavior rather than claiming direct proof of a managing control group.
