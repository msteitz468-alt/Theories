## Source Description

*Faucian Bargain: The Most Powerful and Dangerous Bureaucrat in American History* (2021, Post Hill Press) is a polemical book by conservative talk-radio host **Steve Deace** and his co-host **Todd Erzen** (of *The Steve Deace Show* on The Blaze). Written in early 2021, it argues that the United States' COVID-19 lockdown policy — personified by **Dr. Anthony Fauci** — was an unconstitutional, data-defying, and economically and socially catastrophic mistake, enabled by an unaccountable administrative state and a compliant media.

The book's framing thesis (introduction and conclusion) is Benjamin Franklin's "A republic, if you can keep it" and his "those who would give up essential Liberty, to purchase a little temporary Safety, deserve neither." Deace and Erzen repeatedly stress that **Fauci is "not the disease but the symptom"** — a "construct of a swampy bureaucracy" that would have produced an equivalent figure had he not existed. They state the book "will not argue that COVID-19 isn't a serious disease," but charges that the *response* — especially indefinite lockdowns and mask mandates — was unjustified by the data and corrosive to constitutional self-government. The book is heavily endnoted, drawing on mainstream press, medical journals, dissenting academics, and social-media posts.

Per this wiki's strict-extraction protocol, this page records the claims **as the source presents them**, with attribution; it does not adjudicate their accuracy.

## What the Source Contributes

- A chronicle of alleged **Fauci self-contradictions** on COVID-19 (risk, masks, asymptomatic spread, models, herd immunity).
- The argument that **lockdowns were "the dumbest management decision in American history"** and the cure was worse than the disease.
- The case that **credentialed experts dissented** from Fauci (Katz, Ioannidis, Gupta, Pegden, Atlas, the Great Barrington Declaration).
- A whistleblower interview ("Veritas") alleging Fauci changed positions inside the White House without new data, driven by media.
- **Sweden** presented as a no-lockdown "control group."
- Fauci's NIAID **funding of gain-of-function research at the Wuhan Institute of Virology**.
- An attack on **mask mandates** as unsupported by real-world data, and on **"Branch Covidian"** alarmism as a cult.

## Chapter Summaries

### Introduction: A Republic, If You Can Keep It
Frames the book as a warning about an unaccountable administrative state, invoking Franklin, the Book of Esther (Haman's bureaucratic genocide plot), Tolkien's Sauron/Wormtongue, and Eisenhower's 1961 farewell warning that "public policy could itself become the captive of a scientific-technological elite." Insists Fauci is "the symptom," not the disease — "a construct of the Matrix, at worst an Agent Smith." States the book will not deny COVID-19 is serious but will "not exaggerate the seriousness, either."

### Chapter 1: A Man of Many (Contradictory) Opinions
A "cited chronicle" of Fauci's shifting positions: January 2020 ("very low risk... not something the American people need to be frightened about"); the February 28, 2020 *New England Journal of Medicine* piece comparing COVID's case-fatality rate to "a severe seasonal influenza"; March 8 ("no reason to be walking around with a mask"); the March 11 congressional testimony that COVID is "10 times more lethal than the seasonal flu" that "launched us into lockdowns." Catalogs reversals on models (the Imperial College and IHME models he later said he distrusted), asymptomatic spread, and herd immunity (Rubio: Fauci "selectively lied"), plus narratives that allegedly shifted after Biden's inauguration ("the Biden vaccine"; the WHO's January 20, 2021 testing-threshold notice).

### Chapter 2: The Great Unanswered Questions
Calls for "9/11-type tribunals" to answer two questions: what caused Fauci's shift from calm to panic, and how one can "flatten the curve" without knowing when the curve began. Stresses the age skew (Kaiser: 8 in 10 COVID deaths were 65+; CDC median age of death ~78) against the median worker age (~42), and that schools stayed largely closed. Argues the virus's true U.S. arrival is unknown (Patricia Dowd's February 6, 2020 death; a USC antibody study suggesting 28–55× more LA County infections than recorded), so lockdowns came "too late."

**Claims Extracted** — [[Lockdowns were "the dumbest management decision in American history"]]; [[COVID-19 mortality was concentrated in the elderly, so focused protection was possible]]; [[The virus's true start date was unknown, undermining the rationale for lockdowns]]

### Chapter 3: Trust the Experts, Yes, but Which Ones?
Argues credentialed experts dissented from Fauci: **David Katz** (Yale, "is our fight worse than the disease?"), **John Ioannidis** (Stanford), **Sunetra Gupta** (Oxford), **Wesley Pegden** (Carnegie Mellon, "the duration of containment does not matter"), and **Scott Atlas** (Stanford, who joined Trump's task force and itemized lockdown harms). Recounts the **hydroxychloroquine** dispute (the retracted *Lancet* study; Harvey Risch and Stephen Smith vs. Fauci) and quotes the **Great Barrington Declaration** in full. Notes Fauci himself opposed "draconian" quarantines during the 2014 Ebola outbreak.

Cites comparisons (as of February 2021) showing Sweden outperforming various lockdown countries on deaths/cases per million, and a Sweden–Michigan comparison (similar populations; Michigan harsher lockdown, higher deaths and economic damage). ## Internal Tensions or Multiple Accounts

The book repeatedly hedges its central charge against Fauci's motives. It explicitly declines to call him a mastermind ("he contradicts himself way too much... to be considered that sort of mastermind") and offers "only two possible explanations" for his conduct — deliberate manipulation *or* incompetence/"BSer" — saying "both possible explanations are bad." On hydroxychloroquine the authors stress "we're not doctors or scientists" and "don't know what to think" about its efficacy, resting their argument on the existence of expert disagreement rather than on a verdict. They affirm throughout that "the virus is very real. It has taken many lives," and concede the initial lockdown was forgivable given unreliable Chinese data. They also acknowledge Fauci "has said things the science agrees with" at times. On the lab origin they state "there's a lot we still do not know for certain about the true origins."
