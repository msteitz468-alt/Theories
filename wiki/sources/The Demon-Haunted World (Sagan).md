## Source Description

*The Demon-Haunted World: Science as a Candle in the Dark* (1995) by astronomer Carl Sagan
([[Carl Sagan]]) is a defense of **science and skeptical inquiry** as the best tools against
credulity, superstition, and pseudoscience. It is the wiki's most important **counterweight source**:
where most of the corpus advances UFO, occult, ancient-astronaut, and conspiracy claims, Sagan
supplies the rigorous skeptical and naturalistic rebuttals — making it a natural opposing voice on
many existing comparison and controversy pages. See the umbrella
[[Science and skeptical inquiry as a candle in the dark (Sagan)]].

Sagan argues alien-abduction reports are better explained by hallucination, sleep paralysis, false
memory, and cultural narrative; that "the Face on Mars" and similar wonders are **pareidolia**; that
the medieval witch-craze and the modern demon/abduction experience share a psychology; and he offers
the famous **"Baloney Detection Kit"** and the **"Dragon in My Garage"** parable on unfalsifiable
claims. He pairs unflinching skepticism with genuine wonder, and argues a scientifically literate
citizenry is essential to a free society.
