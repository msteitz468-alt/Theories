## Source Description

*The Book of the Law* (*Liber AL vel Legis*) is the foundational scripture of **Thelema**, which
[[Aleister Crowley]] reported receiving by dictation in Cairo in April 1904 from a discarnate
intelligence named **Aiwass**. Its three short chapters are spoken by the deities **Nuit**, **Hadit**,
and **Ra-Hoor-Khuit**, and proclaim the **Law of Thelema** — "Do what thou wilt shall be the whole of
the Law" — and the dawn of the **Aeon of Horus**. See the umbrella
[[Thelema and Crowley's system of Magick]].

This is the seed-text of Crowley's entire system and a key **primary root** for modern occulture. Its
companion in this ingest, *[[The Book of Lies (Crowley)]]*, is a separate work of mystical aphorisms.

## Strict-extraction / sensitivity note

Recorded as *what the text proclaims* — attributed and **not adjudicated**. Liber AL contains martial
and antinomian verses (e.g. on "the slaves shall serve"); such content is recorded descriptively and
flagged as the text's, never endorsed.
