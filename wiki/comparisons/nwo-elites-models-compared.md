## Overview

This page compares the models of a global elite conspiracy and hidden government developed by three key authors in the alternative history and political synthesis literature:
1. **[[Carroll Quigley]] (*Tragedy and Hope*)**: An academic historian who documents the historical existence of a secret network of Anglophile elites (the Rhodes-Milner Round Table Group) that sought to unify the English-speaking world and influence foreign policy from behind the scenes.
2. **[[Gary Allen]] (*None Dare Call It Conspiracy*)**: A political analyst who frames the elite as a self-perpetuating clique of international bankers and monopolists (the "Insiders") utilizing socialism and central banking to establish a centralized world government.
3. **[[John Coleman]] (*Conspirators' Hierarchy*)**: A former intelligence officer who presents a highly structured model of a supreme hidden world government (the "Committee of 300" or "Olympians") rooted in European aristocracy, utilizing social engineering, deindustrialization, and population reduction.

## 1. Structural Comparison of Elite Models

The three authors describe overlapping networks of elite power but attribute different leadership structures, origins, and ultimate endpoints to the conspiracy:

## 2. Core Differences in Motive & Methodology

### A. The Nature of the Elite Group
* **Quigley (The Anglophile Network):** Describes a secret society founded by Cecil Rhodes in 1891, structured as a "circle of initiators" and an "outer circle" (Milner's Kindergarten). It was cooperative, intellectual, and driven by a paternalistic desire to extend British imperial values and coordinate U.S.-British foreign policy. See [[Cecil Rhodes founded a secret society in 1891 to extend British rule]] and [[Milner's Kindergarten organized the Round Table Groups]].
* **Allen (The Banking Cartel):** Frames the elite as a cold-blooded financial cartel ("The Insiders") of international bankers who manipulate central banking systems to protect their wealth from free-market competition. See [[History is planned and engineered by a self-perpetuating clique of international bankers known as the Insiders]] and [[The Federal Reserve is a banking cartel with five objectives]].
* **Coleman (The Oligarchical Hierarchy):** Asserts the existence of the "Committee of 300," a supreme hidden world government composed of 300 individuals descended from the old oligarchical families of Venice and Genoa (the "Black Nobility") alongside British and European royalty. See [[The Committee of 300 ("the Olympians") is the supreme hidden world government]].

### B. The Motive: World Government vs. Monopoly Control
* **Quigley (Financial Capitalism):** Argues the elite aimed to build a "world system of financial control in private hands able to dominate the political system of each country and the economy of the world as a whole," managed by the world's central banks acting in concert. See [[Financial capitalism aimed to create a world system of control via the central banks]].
* **Allen (Corporate Socialism):** Argues that the Insiders do not fear socialism; rather, they finance and use it to establish a centralized world socialist government. By eliminating competitive free-enterprise, the Insiders can establish global corporate-state monopolies. See [[Socialism is not a movement of the downtrodden masses, but a tool utilized by the super-rich to establish monopolistic control over governments, resources, and individuals]].
* **Coleman (Feudalism & Depopulation):** Outlines a direct program ("21 goals") to establish a one-world feudal state. Coleman's elite seeks to deindustrialize the United States, eliminate national sovereignty, restrict scientific progress, and execute a massive program of global depopulation under the guise of environmentalism. See [[The Committee of 300 pursues a stated program of '21 goals' to impose world government]] and [[The Committee of 300's "Global 2000" plan is a doctrine of deliberate depopulation]].

### C. The Methodology: Pincer Strategy vs. Social Engineering
* **Quigley (Behind-the-Scenes Influence):** Describes the network working through personal influence, academic institutions, newspapers (like *The Times*), and foreign-office appointments, using wealth to quietly guide public opinion and policy from behind the scenes. See [[Investment bankers controlled the central banks from behind the scenes]].
* **Allen (The Pincer Strategy):** Argues the Insiders use a "pincer strategy." They apply "pressure from above" through government regulations, taxation, and tax-exempt foundations (which they control), coordinated with "pressure from below" by funding radical street movements and social agitation to force the middle class to accept the socialization of society. See [[The conspirators utilize a pincer strategy of pressure from above (government regulation) and pressure from below (street mobs) to force the socialization of America]].
* **Coleman (Tavistock & Crisis Engineering):** Focuses on the Tavistock Institute for Human Relations, which Coleman claims uses mass brainwashing, television, and social profiling to manipulate public opinion. He also argues that the Club of Rome engineers economic crises (such as the 1973 oil embargo) and funds radical experiments (such as Pol Pot's agrarian genocide in Cambodia) as test cases for global social control. See [[The Club of Rome runs mass brainwashing and social engineering through the Tavistock Institute]] and [[The Club of Rome used the Pol Pot Cambodia genocide as a social experiment]].

## 3. Historical and Conceptual Overlaps

1. **The Role of the CFR and RIIA:**  
   All three systems identify the Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) in the United States and the Royal Institute of International Affairs (RIIA) in Britain as key front organizations. While Quigley sees them as front groups for Anglophile cooperation, Allen sees them as vehicles of Insider socialization, and Coleman sees them as direct operational branches of the Committee of 300. See [[The Round Table Groups founded the RIIA and the Council on Foreign Relations]] and [[The Council on Foreign Relations and the Round Table groups were established by international bankers to guide U.S. and British policy toward a centralized world socialist government]].
2. **Banker Control of Central Banking:**  
   All three models agree that international bankers used their wealth to capture the central banking systems (specifically the Federal Reserve and the Bank of England), removing credit creation from democratic or congressional oversight.
3. **The Engineering of Crises:**  
   All three models assert that major historical crises (WWI, WWII, the Great Depression, the 1973 oil crisis) were not accidental but were deliberate, managed conflicts designed to shift societies toward centralization and international governance.
