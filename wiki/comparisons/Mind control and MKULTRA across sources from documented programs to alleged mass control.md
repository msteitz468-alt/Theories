## Overview

Several sources address government **mind control**, but they sit at very different points on an
**evidentiary spectrum** — from archivally documented CIA/military programs to sweeping claims of
programmed assassins and population-wide control. This page lines them up so the line between
*documented record* and *conspiracist extrapolation* is explicit. Each claim is attributed and
**not adjudicated**; the documented-vs-alleged distinction is itself the point of comparison.

## Where they converge

1. **The programs were real.** Even the cautious sources agree the CIA (MKULTRA) and US military
   (remote viewing / Project Stargate) *did* run mind-control and psychic programs — this is
   documented, not alleged.
2. **LSD was a central tool** and its covert history overlaps the 1960s counterculture
   ([[LSD has a covert CIA history that converges with the 1960s counterculture]]).
3. **Secrecy and deniability** are common to all accounts.
4. **The documented programs are treated as the tip of an iceberg** — the conspiracist sources extend
   them; the investigative sources note how much was destroyed or hidden.

## Where they sharply diverge — the evidentiary axis

- **Documented / archival:** *Acid Dreams* and *Goats* rest on declassified records and named
  participants.
- **Investigative-circumstantial:** O'Neill assembles striking anomalies (Manson's leniency, CIA-linked
  researchers) but **explicitly declines to claim proof** of a Manson–MKULTRA link.
- **Conspiracist extrapolation:** Cooper, Jeffries, and Icke move from "programs existed" to
  programmed assassins (Jeffries), a society-wide "Quiet War" (Cooper), and reality-defining mass
  control via "Project Monarch" (Icke) — claims far beyond the archival record.
- **Attributed aim:** scientific/interrogation use (Acid Dreams) → manufactured social chaos
  (O'Neill's hint, Cooper) → political assassination (Jeffries) → total population control (Icke).

## Notes on source posture

Investigative journalism / archival history (Lee & Shlain, O'Neill, Ronson) vs movement-conspiracy
literature (Cooper, Jeffries, Icke; Marrs bridges the two via the Illuminati frame). The wiki records
the documented programs as established and the expansive claims as *attributed allegations* — see each
claim page's limiting notes.
