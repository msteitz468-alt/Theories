## Overview

Four scientifically credentialed authors in this wiki argue that the **materialist/physicalist worldview is incomplete** and that consciousness is fundamental rather than a by-product of matter — but each reaches that conclusion from a *different discipline and a different kind of evidence*. This page lines up their **arguments and evidential strategies**: what each treats as the decisive data, what they say materialism gets wrong, and what they put in its place. Per the wiki's contradiction protocol, positions are attributed to their sources and **not adjudicated**.

This is the **evidential-strategy** companion to [[Metaphysical Reality Models Compared]], which compares *experiential / esoteric* reality systems (Campbell, Zeland, Talbot, Hicks, Monroe) by their cosmology and manifestation mechanics. The two overlap only at **Donald Hoffman**, who appears in both because his work is simultaneously an empirical-science argument and a full metaphysics.

## The four arguments

1. **Empirical / parapsychological — Dean Radin, *[[The Conscious Universe (Radin)]]*.** The case is *data-first*: a century of controlled psi experiments, pooled by **meta-analysis**, shows mind reaching beyond the brain and biasing matter — effects materialism says are impossible, therefore materialism is incomplete.
2. **Philosophy of science — Rupert Sheldrake, *[[Science Set Free (Sheldrake)]]*.** The case is *diagnostic*: materialism rests on **ten unproven dogmas**; once they are reopened, nature looks like an evolving organism with inherent memory (**morphic resonance**) and minds that extend beyond brains.
3. **Physics / cosmology — Robert Lanza, *[[Biocentrism (Lanza)]]*.** The case is *interpretive*: the quantum **observer effect** and cosmic **fine-tuning** are best resolved by making the observer primary — life and consciousness create the universe, and space and time are modes of perception.
4. **Evolutionary cognitive science — Donald Hoffman, *[[The Case Against Reality (Why Evolution Hid the Truth from Our Eyes)]]*.** The case is *formal*: his **[[Fitness-Beats-Truth (FBT) Theorem]]** argues natural selection hides objective reality behind a perceptual "interface," so space-time and objects are species-specific icons, and what underlies them is a network of **conscious agents** ([[Conscious Realism]]).

## Where they converge

- **Consciousness is the ground, not the product.** All four invert the materialist order. Compare [[Lanza: biocentrism holds that life and consciousness create the universe]], [[Consciousness is fundamental; the world is a network of conscious agents (conscious realism)]], and [[Sheldrake: materialism is a belief system, not a proven fact]] with Radin's evidence that mind is not brain-bound ([[Radin: the accumulated scientific evidence shows psi is real]]).
- **Space and time are not the bedrock.** Lanza ([[Lanza: space and time are forms of animal perception, not external realities]]) and Hoffman ([[Interface Theory of Perception (ITP)]]) independently demote space-time to a perceptual construct — a point the esoteric systems on the [[Metaphysical Reality Models Compared]] page reach experientially.
- **Materialism is a commitment, not a result.** Sheldrake ([[Sheldrake: modern science is constrained by ten unproven dogmas]]) and Hoffman both argue the physicalist default is an unexamined assumption rather than a finding.
- **The mind is not confined to the brain.** Sheldrake's extended mind ([[Sheldrake: minds are extended beyond the brain (telepathy and the sense of being stared at)]]) and Radin's non-local psi land on the same conclusion from philosophy and from lab data respectively.

## Where they differ — the real fault lines

- **How much weight rests on contested data.** Radin's case stands or falls on the validity of **meta-analysis** and the answer to the file-drawer objection ([[Radin: meta-analysis defeats the chance and file-drawer objections to psi]]); Hoffman's rests on a **mathematical theorem** about perception, not on anomalous phenomena at all. They could both be right, but almost no evidence is shared between them.
- **Does the argument need the paranormal?** Radin and Sheldrake actively embrace psi/telepathy as evidence; Lanza and Hoffman build their cases entirely from *mainstream* results (quantum mechanics; evolutionary game theory) and do not require psi — a sharp strategic divide over respectability and risk.
- **How far the metaphysics goes.** Hoffman and Lanza offer a complete ontology (conscious agents / biocentric universe); Sheldrake offers a reformed science (habits + morphic fields) that stops short of idealism; Radin is the most cautious, arguing the *data* without committing to a full theory of reality.
- **Observer-created vs. observer-revealed.** Lanza says the observer *brings reality into being* ([[Lanza: the observer brings physical reality into being]]); Hoffman says evolution *hides* a pre-existing reality of conscious agents behind the interface — creation versus concealment.

## Notes on source posture

The cluster spans a parapsychologist (Radin), a biologist-heretic (Sheldrake), a physician–stem-cell scientist with an astronomer co-author (Lanza), and a tenured cognitive scientist (Hoffman). Their shared adversary is the same — physicalism — but they agree least on *what counts as evidence*, which is exactly what this comparison surfaces: the case "against materialism" is really four different cases that happen to point the same way. All four are disputed by the scientific mainstream, on grounds specific to each (the robustness of psi meta-analyses; the status of morphic resonance; whether the observer effect requires consciousness; whether the FBT theorem licenses its metaphysical conclusion).
