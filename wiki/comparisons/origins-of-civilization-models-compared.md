## Overview

Many ingested sources reject the orthodox account that civilization arose independently in the
river valleys of the Bronze Age, and instead claim that humanity's earliest high knowledge came
from *somewhere else* — a lost human civilization, extraterrestrial teachers, or a vanished
Atlantean tradition. This page compares those models. Per the wiki's contradiction protocol, each
position is attributed to its source and **not adjudicated**; the page records the shared anomalies
they invoke and where their explanations part ways.

## The three families of explanation

1. **Lost-human-civilization model** — *[[Fingerprints of the Gods (Hancock)]]* and
   *[[Maps of the Ancient Sea Kings (Hapgood, 1966)]]*: an advanced *human* civilization existed in
   the Ice Age and was destroyed by cataclysm (earth-crust displacement); its survivors seeded
   knowledge into later cultures as "teacher-gods." No aliens required.
2. **Extraterrestrial-teacher model** — *[[Chariots of the Gods (von Däniken)]]*,
   *[[The Twelfth Planet (Sitchin)]]*, *[[Escaping from Eden (Wallis)]]*: civilization (and humanity
   itself) was given by non-human visitors later remembered as gods. (This overlaps the
   [[Reptilian / Anunnaki 'gods-created-and-rule-humanity' models compared across sources]].)
3. **Atlantean / esoteric-tradition model** — *[[The Emerald Tablets of Thoth the Atlantean (Doreal)]]*
   and *[[The Secret Teachings of All Ages (Hall)]]*: a literal Atlantis and an initiatic Mystery
   tradition (Thoth/Hermes) are the fountainhead of the arts and sciences.

## Where the sources converge

A shared set of anomalies, recorded as their common assertions (not as established fact):

1. **A real global cataclysm is remembered in flood myths.** Hancock's
   [[Worldwide flood myths are a shared memory of a real global cataclysm (Hancock)]] and Wallis's
   [[A Clovis Comet / Younger Dryas cataclysm makes Genesis 1 a story of recovery, not original creation]].
2. **High astronomical/cartographic knowledge predates the orthodox timeline.** Hancock's
   [[The precession of the equinoxes is deliberately encoded in ancient myth (Hancock)]] meets
   Hapgood's [[The Piri Reis and Oronteus Finaeus maps show the coasts of Antarctica before they were covered by the current ice cap, indicating they were mapped during a pre-glacial or ice-free period]]
   and [[An advanced worldwide maritime civilization with global commerce and sophisticated navigation instruments existed at least 20,000 years ago during the Ice Age]].
3. **"Teacher" figures civilized humanity.** Hancock's lost-culture survivors
   ([[A lost advanced human civilization seeded knowledge into later cultures (Hancock)]]), von
   Däniken's worshipped visitors ([[Extraterrestrials visited Earth in prehistory and were worshipped as gods (von Däniken)]]),
   and Doreal's [[Thoth was an Atlantean priest-king who built the Great Pyramid]] all play this role.

## Where they sharply collide

- **Human survivors vs. extraterrestrials.** Hancock and Hapgood explicitly explain the anomalies
  *without* aliens — a lost but fully *human* civilization — whereas von Däniken and Sitchin make the
  teachers non-human (see [[Modern man was genetically engineered by the Nefilim from Homo erectus about 300,000 years ago]]).
  The same evidence (ancient maps, an older Sphinx) is claimed by both camps: Hancock cites Hapgood's
  Antarctic maps as proof of a lost *human* cartography, while ancient-astronaut authors read similar
  data as ET.
- **Mechanism of the cataclysm.** Hapgood's
  [[The entire crust of the Earth shifts as a single unit over the liquid-like mantle beneath, driven by the centrifugal forces of eccentric ice caps]]
  is a *geophysical* mechanism; Doreal's [[Atlantis was sunk after adepts opened a forbidden gateway]]
  is an *esoteric/occult* one; Sitchin's deluge is a natural Antarctic ice-slippage tidal wave.
- **Naturalistic vs. occult framing.** Hancock/Hapgood argue as (heterodox) empirical researchers
  marshaling physical evidence; Doreal and Hall present revealed/initiatic knowledge —
  [[Atlantis was a real sunken continent and home of the gods of antiquity (Hall)]] — that does not
  rest on the same evidentiary basis.

## Notes on source posture

The cluster ranges from a geologist/historian (Hapgood) and an investigative journalist (Hancock)
building empirical, alien-free arguments, through the popular ancient-astronaut author (von Däniken)
and language-theorist (Sitchin), to frankly esoteric/revealed texts (Doreal's *Emerald Tablets*,
Hall's *Secret Teachings*). They converge most on the *anomalies* (a lost age, a cataclysm,
knowledge too early) and diverge most on *what fills the gap* — lost humans, aliens, or Atlantis.
