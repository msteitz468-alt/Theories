## Overview

The wiki's sources repeatedly invoke the same cast of secret societies — the Knights Templar,
Freemasonry, the Bavarian Illuminati, the Rosicrucians, the Priory/Ordre de Sion, Rex Deus, the
Committee of 300. This page compares how each source treats these organizations, and the central
dispute: **is there an unbroken, continuous secret design steering history, or are these discrete
groups with ordinary historical explanations?** This is distinct from
[[Models of the New World Order Elites Compared]] (modern banks/think-tanks). Each position is
attributed and **not adjudicated**.

## Sensitivity note

Webster's account (and, in a different key, Marrs's "ancient lineage" framing) ties the alleged
conspiracy to "Cabalism"/Judaism; this is an antisemitic trope. The wiki records *that the source
makes the claim*, flags it as such, and does not endorse or amplify it.

## Side-by-side comparison

| Source | Societies emphasized | Continuity thesis | Posture |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Webster** | Templars → Freemasonry → Illuminati | One continuous "secret force of revolution" from occult/Cabalist roots | Alarmed polemic (antisemitic) |
| **Heckethorn** | 160+ societies, catalogued | **No** grand design — discrete, often decayed groups | Sober Victorian history (skeptic's control) |
| **Marrs** | Illuminati, Masonry, modern elites | "Unbroken chain" back through the mysteries to Sumer/the Anunnaki | Conspiracist synthesis |
| **Coleman** | The Committee of 300 ("Olympians") | A supreme modern controlling body with "21 goals" | Conspiracist |
| **Custodians** | Rex Deus, Templars, chivalric orders | A sacred **bloodline** network seeding nobility | Alternative-history (sympathetic to the societies) |
| **Holy Blood** | Ordre de Sion / Priory, Templars | Priory secretly created & steers events for a bloodline | Investigative alternative-history |
| **None Dare** | CFR / Round Table | Banker front for a world collectivist state | Conspiracist |

## Where they converge

1. **Secret societies are real and historically influential** — even Heckethorn, the skeptic,
   [[Heckethorn catalogues 160-plus secret societies as sober history]].
2. **A recurring cast** — the Templars and Freemasonry appear in almost every account.
3. **Several assert an *unbroken chain*** from antiquity to modern elites — Webster's
   [[A continuous secret force of revolution runs through history (Webster)]], Marrs's
   [[An unbroken chain of secret societies connects modern elites to the ancient mysteries (Marrs)]],
   and the bloodline continuity of [[Rex Deus survived the suppression of the Knights Templar by infiltrating multiple chivalric and military orders]].
4. **Initiatic or bloodline transmission** is the shared mechanism of continuity.

## Where they sharply diverge

- **Continuity vs. discreteness — the core split.** Webster, Marrs, Custodians and Holy Blood see one
  continuous design; **Heckethorn rejects it**, deriving Freemasonry prosaically (
  [[Freemasonry derives prosaically from medieval operative masons' guilds]]) and reading the
  societies as "the conscience of history," not its puppet-master.
- **The engine.** Revolutionary *subversion* (Webster) vs an *ancient-mystery / Anunnaki* lineage
  ([[The secret tradition traces back through the mysteries to Sumer and the Anunnaki (Marrs)]]) vs a
  *sacred bloodline* (Custodians, Holy Blood —
  [[Claim: Merovingian dynasty was descended from Jesus's bloodline via the Magdalene]]) vs a *modern
  banker/political control body* (Coleman, None Dare).
- **Origin point.** Medieval guilds (Heckethorn) vs the Templars/Crusades (Webster, Custodians, Holy
  Blood) vs Sumer (Marrs).
- **Valence.** A threat to be exposed (Webster, Marrs, Coleman, None Dare) vs *guardians of suppressed
  truth / a sacred line* (Custodians; partly Holy Blood) vs a neutral catalogue (Heckethorn).

## Notes on source posture

Victorian sober history (Heckethorn) sits at one pole; alarmed conspiracy-polemic with antisemitic
content (Webster) and movement-conspiracy synthesis (Marrs, Coleman, None Dare) at another;
bloodline alternative-history (Custodians, Holy Blood) at a third. The wiki records each as an
attributed account.
