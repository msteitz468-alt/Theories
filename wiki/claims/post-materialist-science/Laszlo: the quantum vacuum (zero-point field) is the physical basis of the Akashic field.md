## Claim Statement

Laszlo argues that the quantum vacuum — the zero-point field underlying all physical reality — is not an empty void but a "physically real and active plenum" that serves as the physical substrate of the Akashic field. This plenum transports light, energy, and pressure, endows particles with mass through the Higgs field, and — crucially — conserves and conveys a holographic form of "in-formation" that connects all things in the universe. Laszlo identifies the A-field directly with this vacuum medium, equating it with the ancient concept of Akasha as the all-pervading substratum from which all matter and force emerge.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Laszlo first establishes the physical reality of the vacuum as a "plenum":

> "The vacuum turns out to be a cosmic medium that transports photon-waves (light) as well as density-pressure waves, exerts the force that may ultimately decide the fate of the universe, and endows the particles we know as 'matter' with mass. Such a medium is not an abstract theoretical entity. It is not a vacuum, but a physically real and active plenum." (line 1741)

He then poses the key question linking the vacuum to information:

> "The physical vacuum, effectively a cosmic plenum, subtends the observed universe. It transports light, energy, and pressure. Could it also be responsible for the remarkable coherence discovered at all scales in nature, from the quantic to the cosmic? Could it conserve and convey an active form of information?" (line 1749)

Laszlo cites Edgar Mitchell's formulation to bridge the vacuum and in-formation:

> "The quantum vacuum is the 'holographic information mechanism that records the historical experience of matter.' The information meant here is evidently active: it is 'in-formation.'" (line 1757)

He describes the physical mechanism by which the vacuum stores in-formation — via torsion-wave vortices forming interference-pattern holograms — and concludes:

> "The information carried in the vacuum is not localized, confined to a single location only. As in a hologram, the vacuum carries information in distributed form, present at all points where the wavefields have propagated. The interfering wave-fields in the vacuum are natural holograms. They propagate quasi-instantly, and nothing can attenuate or cancel them. Thus nature's holograms are cosmic holograms: they link—'in-form'—all things with all other things." (line 1835)

He explicitly names the A-field as grounded in the quantum vacuum, equating it with Akasha:

> "In the new physics the unified, physically real vacuum is the equivalent of Akasha. It is the original field out of which emerged particles and atoms, stars and planets, human and animal bodies, and all the things that can be seen and touched. It is a dynamic, energy-filled medium in ceaseless fluctuation. The vacuum is Akasha and Prana rolled into one—the womb of all the 'matter' and all the 'force' in the universe." (line 1939)

And from the Part Two synthesis:

> "The most fundamental element of reality is the quantum vacuum, the energy- and in-formation-filled plenum that underlies, generates, and interacts with our universe, and with whatever universes may exist in the Metaverse." (line 2381)

> "In the last count matter is but a waveform disturbance in the quasi-infinite energy- and in-formation-sea that is the connecting field, and the enduring memory, of the universe." (line 2409)

## Contexts Within the Source

This identification is the pivotal conceptual move of Part One. Laszlo builds toward it by surveying evidence for the vacuum's physical reality (Higgs field, cosmological constant, zero-point interactions in water), then introduces "in-formation" as a distinct mode of connection carried by the vacuum, and finally names the resulting field the Akashic field. The claim is reinforced throughout Part Two and in the concluding scientific appendix on coherence (lines 2929–3222), where in-formation in the A-field is offered as the unified explanation for nonlocal coherence across quantum, biological, cosmological, and consciousness domains.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Laszlo acknowledges that the A-field's properties "transcend those currently thought to be associated with that [zero-point] field" and that "we do not yet possess a mathematical description" of it (line 1909). He is thus careful to distinguish the A-field from the zero-point field proper while still grounding it in the vacuum medium. The specific torsion-field theories he invokes (Shipov, Akimov, Gazdag) are presented as cutting-edge but not mainstream-accepted physics.
