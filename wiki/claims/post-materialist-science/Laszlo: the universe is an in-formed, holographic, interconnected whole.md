## Claim Statement

Laszlo argues that the universe is not a collection of isolated material objects but an integral, in-formed, holographic whole in which every element is nonlocally connected to every other through an underlying Akashic field. He holds that information — not matter — is the most fundamental constituent of reality, that this information is conserved holographically in the unified field, and that it is accessible to consciousness across all distances and times. The cosmos is, in his formulation, a "superhologram" in which nothing that has ever happened is ever lost.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Laszlo's postscript makes his metaphysical core explicit: "A clear and elementally certain concept has always been the integral unity of the world in all its diversity. The universe is not a layer cake of inherently different kinds of things and processes but a self-evolving system" (line 6063). He adds that "psyche and physis, mind and matter, are not separate realities, or even separate elements of the same reality. They are the same reality" (line 6083), and that "the interconnections are intrinsic and mutually constitutive … more fundamental than the causes and effects that relate things and events in the manifest dimensions of space and time" (line 6075).

On information and holography, the "Summing Up" chapter argues: "The most logical and least speculative hypothesis is that the Akashic field records, conserves, and conveys information in a holographic mode … the entire contents of the Library of Congress … could be coded in a multiplex hologram the size of a cube of sugar. Functioning in a holographic mode, nature's information field could record and convey information on all the things that take place in space and time, from the big bang (or perhaps before) until the big crunch (and perhaps beyond)" (lines 5865–5866). The resulting structure is "a field of quantum holograms, a kind of superconducting cosmic medium … The quantum holograms created by the waves emitted by objects in space and time entangle throughout the field — that is, throughout space and time. They produce sequences of interference patterns, culminating in the superhologram that is the integration of all other holograms. The superhologram carries information on all the things that exist, and have ever existed: it's the 'hologram of the universe'" (lines 5881–5882).

Laszlo is equally explicit about nonlocality as the mechanism: "Nonlocality means that physical effects do not propagate over space at finite speeds but spread instantly (or at any rate faster than measurable by existing instruments)" (lines 5808–5809), and repeated laboratory experiments confirm "that particles … remain correlated with one another over all finite times and distances. Changes in the state of one of the particles instantly result in changes in the other particle, even when they are no longer connected in any conventional way" (line 5813). He draws out the cosmological implication: "everything that has ever happened in the universe remains present in some way; nothing is entirely evanescent" (line 6079).

On the role of information as the primary constituent of reality: "A further insight concerned the fundamental elements of physical reality. These, I realized, are energy and information. Matter, on the other hand, is an illusion, created only by our observation of the open systems that coevolve through energy and information" (line 6067). Stanislav Grof, whose chapter Laszlo endorses, echoes this holographic cosmology: "each of us thus seems to be a microcosm that also has access to the information about the entire macrocosm" — which Laszlo quotes approvingly in the synthesis (line 5925).

The living organism itself exemplifies the holographic interconnection: "Through its biofield, the living organism interacts with all the fields that surround it … the organism is in communication with more than its immediate environment: it is in communication with other organisms whether near or far. In the final count it is connected with the entire sphere of life" (lines 5841–5842).

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim constitutes the ontological backbone against which the empirical testimonies are evaluated. It is worked out most fully in Laszlo's own "Summing Up" chapter (lines 5751–6011) and postscript (lines 6013–6092), but is anticipated in every contributor's chapter. Stanislav Grof invokes the Avatamsaka Sutra's "Jewel Net of Indra" — "if you look at one, you see all the others reflected in it. In the same way, each object in the world is not merely itself, but involves every other object and, in fact, is everything else" (line 5044) — as the traditional analogue for Laszlo's quantum-holographic model. The claim grounds the mechanism proposed in [[Laszlo: "akashic experiences" are evidence of an interconnected in-formed reality]] and motivates the paradigm shift argued in [[Laszlo: science is converging on a post-materialist paradigm of an integral reality]]. The concept hub is [[The Akashic field (Laszlo)]].

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Laszlo acknowledges that "the final word has not been spoken" on quantum coherence at biological temperatures and macroscopic scales: "the fundamental divide between the microworld of the quantum and the world of macro-scale objects appears to have been breached" but is not definitively closed (line 5825). He also concedes that his account of after-death communication — where the Akashic field must sustain consciousness without a functional brain — remains "a major challenge for science" and requires "sustained research … in the physics of quantum holograms and nonlocal coherence as well as the theory of self-regulation and evolution in complex systems" (line 5999). He does not claim the hypothesis is proven, only that it is the most scientifically coherent available framework.
