## Claim Statement

Laszlo argues that nature operates through a form of active information he calls "in-formation" — distinct from Shannon information or knowledge about facts — that is a "subtle, quasi-instant, non-evanescent and non-energetic connection between things at different locations in space and events at different points in time." The quantum vacuum stores and transmits this in-formation as holographic wave-interference patterns, feeding the accumulated experience of past systems back into present ones. This accounts for the otherwise inexplicable speed and coherence of biological evolution, the fine-tuning of the universe's physical constants, and the nonlocal coherence observed across all scales of nature. Without in-formation conveyed by the A-field, Laszlo argues, neither the evolution of complex life nor the large-scale structure of the cosmos could have emerged within the time available.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Laszlo defines "in-formation" precisely in a dedicated background brief:

> "In-formation is a subtle, quasi-instant, non-evanescent and non-energetic connection between things at different locations in space and events at different points in time. Such connections are termed 'nonlocal' in the natural sciences and 'transpersonal' in consciousness research. In-formation links things (particles, atoms, molecules, organisms, ecologies, solar systems, entire galaxies, as well as the mind and consciousness associated with some of these things) regardless of how far they are from each other and how much time has passed since connections were created between them." (line 1787)

He distinguishes it sharply from Shannon information:

> "The 'theory of in-formation' is not the same as standard 'information theory,' because in-formation is not information in any of the scientific or everyday definitions. It is neither knowledge received about some fact or event, nor a pattern imposed on a transmission channel, nor yet the reduction of uncertainty regarding multiple choices." (line 1781)

On the mechanism — vortices in the vacuum building up into holograms — Laszlo uses the parable of the sea:

> "As fresh waves superimpose on those already present, the sea becomes more and more modulated; it carries more and more information. On calm days we can see that it remains modulated for hours, and sometimes for days. The wave patterns that persist are the memory of the ships that traveled on that stretch of water. If wind, gravity, and shorelines did not cancel these patterns, the wave-memory of the sea would persist indefinitely." (line 1827)

And applied to the vacuum itself:

> "In this way the vacuum carries information on atoms, molecules, macromolecules, cells, and even on organisms and populations and ecologies of organisms. There is no evident limit to the information that interfering vacuum wave-fields could conserve. In the final count, they could carry information on the state of the whole universe." (line 1831)

On the consequence for evolution and cosmic coherence, from the Part Two introduction:

> "In the in-formed universe the A-field is a fundamental element. Thanks to in-formation conserved and conveyed by the A-field, the universe is of mind-boggling coherence. All that happens in one place happens also in other places; all that happened at one time happens also at all times after that. Nothing is 'local,' limited to where and when it is happening. All things are global, indeed cosmic, for all things are connected, and the memory of all things extends to all places and to all times." (line 1985)

On biological evolution specifically:

> "The evolution of life on Earth did not rely on chance mutations, nor did it require the physical importation of organisms or proto-organisms from elsewhere in the solar system, as the 'biological seeding' theories of the origins of life suggest. Instead, the chemical soup out of which the first proto-organisms arose was in-formed by the forms of life that had evolved elsewhere in the universe. Life on Earth was not biologically, but in-formationally seeded—and its evolution continues to be in-formed by life wherever it exists in the universe." (line 2297)

On why in-formation resolves the fine-tuning problem:

> "There is no need to ascribe nonlocal coherence, the remarkable space-and time-transcending connection of everything with everything else, to the action of divine will, or to forces above or beyond the natural world. Nonlocal coherence is a bona fide scientific phenomenon. . . . A-field in-formation is the logical explanation of nonlocal coherence: of the mysterious way in which quanta are connected across space and time, of the evident but nonetheless astounding fact that we and other organisms have evolved and can live on this planet and, last but not least, of the seemingly miraculous capacity of the universe to bring forth human beings." (line 3221–3222)

## Contexts Within the Source

The concept of in-formation is introduced late in Part One as the resolution to all the puzzles of coherence surveyed in earlier chapters (quantum nonlocality, biological evolution, cosmic fine-tuning). In Part Two it is applied systematically: Chapter 5 deploys it to explain how our universe inherited its fine-tuned parameters and how life evolved without reliance on blind chance; Chapter 6 uses it to explain nonsensory access to information in human consciousness. The scientific appendix (lines 2929–3222) reviews the empirical evidence for nonlocal coherence in quantum physics, cosmology, biology, and consciousness research, presenting each as evidence for A-field in-formation.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Laszlo acknowledges that random mutations and natural selection remain operative; his claim is that they are insufficient on their own to explain the speed of speciation ("the 'search space' of possible genetic rearrangements within the genome is so enormous that random processes would take incomparably longer to produce new species," line 3109). He presents in-formation as complementary to, not a wholesale replacement of, Darwinian mechanisms. He also concedes that the specific torsion-field physics he uses to model in-formation is not mainstream-accepted.
