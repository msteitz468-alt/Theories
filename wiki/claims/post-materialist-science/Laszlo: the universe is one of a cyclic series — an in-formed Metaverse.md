## Claim Statement

Laszlo argues that our universe is not a one-off event but one cycle in an enduring "Metaverse" — a temporally quasi-infinite matrix of sequentially born-and-dying universes. The quantum vacuum is not destroyed when a local universe ends; it persists, carrying the holographic in-formation imprinted by all the systems that evolved within the previous universe. Each successor universe is therefore "in-formed" by its predecessors: it inherits fine-tuned physical constants, a head start on the evolution of complexity, and an increasingly favorable substrate for life. The cycle is not repetitive but progressive — evolution across the Metaverse itself moves toward greater coherence and complexity in each successive universe.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Laszlo introduces the Metaverse as a cosmological necessity implied by current multi-universe theories:

> "There is also a meta-universe or Metaverse that was not created in the Bang that created our universe (which was but one of many explosions, so it no longer qualifies for the adjective 'Big'); nor will the Metaverse itself come to an end when the particles created by this particular Bang vanish in the collapse of the last black holes. . . . The universe is the Metaverse, the mother of our universe and perhaps of myriad other universes." (line 2039)

He argues that the vacuum — as the A-field medium — bridges the cycles:

> "The quantum vacuum, the subtle energy and in-formation sea that underlies all 'matter' in the universe, did not originate with the Bang that produced our universe, and it will not vanish when the particles created by that explosion fall back into it. The subtle energies and the active in-formation that underlie this universe were there before its particles appeared and will be there after they disappear. The deeper reality is the quantum vacuum, the enduring in-formation and energy sea that pulsates, producing periodic explosions that give rise to local universes." (line 2043)

On how each successor universe is in-formed by its predecessor:

> "Instead of marveling at this improbable scenario and giving up on a scientific explanation of it, we can contemplate the possibility that at its birth our universe was in-formed by a universe or universes that existed prior to it. . . . Thus, through the vacuum an ongoing transfer of in-formation came about between the universes. In the cycle of universes in the Metaverse each universe is in-formed by its predecessor, and in-forms in turn its successor." (line 2067)

On the mechanism by which inherited in-formation shapes the new universe's parameters:

> "The in-formation transmitted from a precursor to a successor universe affects the value of the energy of the vacuum and determines the amount of matter in the successor universe. It also affects the distribution of the virtual states that particles, atoms, molecules, and systems and assemblies of molecules can occupy. . . . In this way each universe 'inherits' the physical properties of its precursor. It neither collapses back on itself shortly after its birth nor expands so fast that only a dilute gas of particles survives. It evolves more and more efficiently, and hence further and further into the otherwise improbable realms of coherence where complex systems such as organisms, societies, and ecologies can emerge." (line 2071)

On the progressive (non-repetitive) character of the cycle:

> "We begin by noting that the evolution of universes within the Metaverse is cyclic but not repetitive. One universe informs another; there is progress from universe to universe. Each universe is more evolved than the one before. The cycle itself evolves from a random initial universe, to universes where the physical parameters are more and more tuned to the evolution of complexity." (line 2221)

On life's continuity across the cycle:

> "Each universe in-forms the vacuum in which it arose, and its in-formed vacuum in-forms the next universe. Thus in each universe life evolves more and more efficiently, and in equal times evolves further and further, toward coherence and complexity. Cosmic evolution is a cyclical process with a learning curve." (lines 2357–2361)

The poetic summary in Chapter 7 describes the mechanism vividly:

> "And now another shaft of light rends the plenum, breaking its quiescent turbulence and reviving it with another formative burst: a new universe is born. This time the ripples and structures that form on the surface do not appear randomly, at the mercy of chance: they derive from a plenum in-formed with the holo-trace of prior ripples and waves." (line 2901)

> "The cosmic drama repeats time after time. Further shafts of light radiate outward from the epicenter, another multitude of ripples moves outward to dance, to cohere, to feel, and to reflect. . . . Time after time, the cosmic drama repeats, but it does not repeat in the same way. It builds on its own past, on the memory of the ripples and waves that appeared and then disappeared in prior universes." (line 2905)

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 5 of Part Two applies this cosmological thesis to the "great questions" of cosmic origin and destiny. Laszlo uses it to dissolve both the standard Big Bang puzzle (why our universe is so improbably well-tuned for life) and the creationist/evolutionist controversy: design and evolution are reconciled because the Metaverse's primordial vacuum was itself "in-formed" through a chain of predecessors, pointing ultimately to what Laszlo calls an original act of "metaversal Design." The thesis recurs in Chapter 7's prose poem and in the concluding summary (lines 3215–3222), where A-field in-formation is presented as the unified scientific explanation linking cosmic, biological, and conscious coherence.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Laszlo acknowledges that the question of the Metaverse's own primordial origin — what in-formed its vacuum before any predecessor universe existed — is "transempirical" and cannot be resolved by scientific reasoning: "This greatest of all mysteries is 'transempirical'; it is not amenable to resolution by reasoning based on observation and experiment" (line 2087). He does not claim to resolve it, only to assert that the Metaverse's primordial vacuum must itself have been in some sense "in-formed" for the progressive cycle to begin. He also acknowledges disagreement among cosmologists about whether universes are causally isolated from one another.
