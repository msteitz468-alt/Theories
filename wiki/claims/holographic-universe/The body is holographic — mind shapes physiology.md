## Claim Statement

Talbot extends the holographic model to the body, arguing that if reality is mentally constructed from a deeper order, then mind and belief can reshape physiology far more profoundly than mainstream medicine allows — making sense of "miraculous" phenomena such as placebo effects, spontaneous remissions, stigmata, and the abrupt physiological changes seen in multiple personality disorder.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

A striking case Talbot cites is **multiple personality disorder (MPD)**: different sub-personalities inhabiting one body can display distinct, measurable physiologies — different brain-wave patterns, allergies, eye-prescriptions, even diseases that appear and vanish as personalities switch — which he reads as evidence the "body holographic" is plastic to mind. He marshals the placebo response and documented "miracles" (the chapter "A Pocketful of Miracles") to argue belief literally restructures the body, consistent with a reality projected from the implicate order rather than fixed in dead matter.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

As with the rest of the book, Talbot presents these as supportive illustrations of a contested paradigm, not as proof, and draws on the interpretations of clinicians (e.g., Bernie Siegel) who are themselves controversial within mainstream medicine.
