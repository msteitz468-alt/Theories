## Claim Statement

On February 5, 1891, Cecil Rhodes and W. T. Stead organized a secret society — with Rhodes as leader, an executive committee (Stead, Lord Esher, Alfred Milner), a "Circle of Initiates" (including Lord Rothschild and Lord Balfour), and an outer "Association of Helpers" — dedicated to federating the English-speaking peoples and bringing "all the habitable portions of the world under their control."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Quigley: Rhodes's inaugural inspiration came from Ruskin (whose lecture Rhodes copied out and "kept with him for thirty years"). "This association was formally established on February 5, 1891, when Rhodes and Stead organized a secret society of which Rhodes had been dreaming for sixteen years. In this secret society Rhodes was to be leader; Stead, Brett (Lord Esher), and Milner were to form an executive committee; Arthur (Lord) Balfour, (Sir) Harry Johnston, Lord Rothschild, Albert (Lord) Grey, and others were listed as potential members of a 'Circle of Initiates'; while there was to be an outer circle known as the 'Association of Helpers' (later organized by Milner as the Round Table organization)."

Quigley notes Rhodes was financed by "Lord Rothschild and Alfred Beit," monopolized South African diamonds (De Beers) and much of its gold (Consolidated Gold Fields), and left part of his fortune to found the Rhodes Scholarships "to spread the English ruling class tradition throughout the English-speaking world."
