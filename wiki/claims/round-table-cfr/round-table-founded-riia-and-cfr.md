## Claim Statement

In 1919 the Rhodes-Milner Round Table group founded the Royal Institute of International Affairs (Chatham House), financed chiefly by Sir Abe Bailey and the Astor family; sister "Institutes of International Affairs" were established in the British dominions and in the United States — where it is known as the Council on Foreign Relations — in the period 1919–1927.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Quigley: "In 1919 they founded the Royal Institute of International Affairs (Chatham House) for which the chief financial supporters were Sir Abe Bailey and the Astor family (owners of The Times). Similar Institutes of International Affairs were established in the chief British dominions and in the United States (where it is known as the Council on Foreign Relations) in the period 1919–1927."

He adds that these units interlocked with the Round Table Group and the RIIA in each country, and that the network controlled *The Times* and numerous journals and academic chairs. This is the precise statement Griffin and Mullins cite when asserting that the CFR is "an outgrowth of the secret society formed by Cecil Rhodes."
