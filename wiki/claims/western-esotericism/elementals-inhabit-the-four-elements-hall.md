## Claim Statement

Hall sets out the doctrine of **elementals** — living nature spirits that inhabit and embody the four elements: **gnomes** (earth), **undines** (water), **sylphs** (air), and **salamanders** (fire) — as a serious component of the esoteric tradition's "occult pneumatology" (the philosophy of spiritual substances).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Drawing chiefly on **Paracelsus** — whom he calls the source of "the most comprehensive and lucid exposition of occult pneumatology… extant" — Hall explains that "just as visible Nature is populated by an infinite number of living creatures, so… the invisible, spiritual counterpart of Nature" is populated by elemental beings. He treats the four orders of elementals, along with the related lore of dæmons and the incubus/succubus, as the invisible inhabitants the ancient Mysteries recognized behind the visible world. He quotes Paracelsus's tripartite scheme: "The body comes from the elements, the soul from the stars, and the spirit from God."

## Contexts Within the Source

Part of Hall's survey of the invisible cosmos the secret doctrine describes ([[The secret doctrine (perennial wisdom)]]); the elementals are the living agencies behind the ceremonial-magic and Hermetic-pharmacology chapters.
