## Claim Statement
Hanegraaff argues that nineteenth-century psychology, following Ludwig Feuerbach's "reversal" thesis (that "Man made God in his own image"), reversed the traditional hierarchy in which God/spirits exist independently outside the mind, instead locating the divine within the human psyche. This produced, in modern esotericism (beginning with Mesmerism), a "double dialectics": an increasing "psychologization of the sacred" paralleled by an increasing "sacralization of psychology," such that practitioners can speak of divine realities while meaning their own psyche, and vice versa — with figures like Jung's collective unconscious representing sacralized psychology, and Israel Regardie's Freudian reading of the Golden Dawn representing the psychologized-sacred pole, while Crowley and Chaos Magic each negotiate the boundary differently.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"Ludwig Feuerbach's Das Wesen des Christentums... culminating in the radical statement that 'Man made God in his own image' instead of the reverse. Feuerbach became a major influence on the great 'masters of suspicion' (Marx, Nietzsche and Freud)." "The gradual psychologization of Western esotericism since the end of the nineteenth century can be described in terms of a double dialectics: the increasing psychologization of the sacred is paralleled by an increasing sacralization of psychology. This means that in much of modern and contemporary esotericism, authors and practitioners can talk about divine realities while really meaning their own psyche, and about their own psyche while really meaning the divine." On Jung: "the 'numinous' energies of the collective unconscious are seen as mysterious forces somehow more primary and original than the products of human reflection... the sacralization of the psyche trumps the reductionist trends of psychologizing the sacred mostly associated with Freud." On the practical upshot: "for many of those who are involved in esotericism today, perhaps it does not matter so much whether those spiritual entities or realities 'out there' really exist... More important is whether it works to get in contact with them."
