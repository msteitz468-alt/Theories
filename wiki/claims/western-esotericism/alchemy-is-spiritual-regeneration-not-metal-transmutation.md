## Claim Statement

Hall argues that alchemy is, at root, a **spiritual science of regeneration** — a divinely revealed art for restoring man to "his lost estate" — and that laboratory chemistry is only its "earthly body." Read materially, alchemy yields at most "half of the mystery"; its true object is the transmutation of the soul.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He calls alchemy "the secret art of the land of Khem… one of the two oldest sciences" (with astrology), "divinely revealed to man so that by their aid he might regain his lost estate." Per the Rabbinic legend he cites, "the angel at the gate of Eden instructed Adam in the mysteries of Qabbalah and of alchemy," promising that mastery of "the secret wisdom concealed within these inspired arts" would lift "the curse of the forbidden fruit." Hall warns that chemists "who… study only material elements… can at best discover but half of the mystery," since "half of The Book of Torah is forever concealed behind the veil of Isis," and that one passes "behind the veil which divides the seen from the unseen" only "in the way appointed — the Mysteries."

## Contexts Within the Source

A core application of the symbol-as-cipher principle ([[The secret doctrine is deliberately encoded in symbol and allegory]]): the alchemical "Philosopher's Stone" and metallic transmutation veil a doctrine of spiritual rebirth. Stems from the Hermetic tradition of [[Hermes Trismegistus is the fountainhead of the Hermetic arts and sciences]].
