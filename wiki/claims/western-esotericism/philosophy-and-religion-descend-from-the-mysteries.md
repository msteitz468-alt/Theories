## Claim Statement

Hall argues that Western philosophy and religion did not arise independently of the Mysteries but emerged *from* them — that "philosophy emerged from the religious Mysteries of antiquity, not being separated from religion until after the decay of the Mysteries," and that the founding philosophers were Mystery initiates.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He states that "he who would fathom the depths of philosophic thought must familiarize himself with the teachings of those initiated priests designated as the first custodians of divine revelation," and that the great schools drew their doctrines from this hidden source. Plato, he notes, "traveled widely and was initiated by the Egyptians into the profundities of Hermetic philosophy" and "derived much from the doctrines of the Pythagoreans"; Pythagoras likewise is treated as a transmitter of Mystery teaching. Over time, Hall says, the teachings became "inextricably linked with the names of their disseminators" so that "the actual but recondite source — the Mysteries — came to be wholly ignored."
