## Claim Statement

Hall argues that behind the world's religions lies a single **solar theology**: the visible sun is only a "reflector" of an invisible **spiritual Sun** that is the true source "of life, light, and truth," and the mystics taught a doctrine of **three suns** — spiritual, intellectual ("soular"), and material — corresponding to the triune Deity and to man's threefold nature of spirit, soul, and body.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He writes that "there are three suns in each solar system… the spiritual sun manifests the power of God the Father; the soular sun radiates the life of God the Son; and the material sun is the vehicle… for God the Holy Spirit" — symbolized, he says, by Freemasonry's three candles and the three tabernacles of the Transfiguration. Citing Paracelsus ("There is an Eternal Sun, which is the source of all wisdom") and Franz Hartmann, he holds "the material visible sun was a reflector rather than a source of power," reflecting "the invisible spiritual sun, which was the true source of life." He notes the Rosicrucian appellations of the three suns (Vulcan; Christ and Lucifer; and "the Jewish Demiurgus Jehovah"), where Lucifer is "the false light" (intellect without spirit) redeemed by the "true light" of Christ — a transmutation he calls "one of the great secrets of alchemy."
