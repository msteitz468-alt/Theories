## Claim Statement

Hall treats **Atlantis** — Plato's sunken continent — as a real antediluvian land, the home of "the gods of antiquity" and a wellspring of the wisdom later carried into the historic civilizations, arguing the tradition deserves serious scientific attention rather than dismissal as myth.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He cites a 1915 Smithsonian Institution report by the French geologist M. Pierre Termier, who told the Institut Océanographique that "science is returning to the study of Atlantis," that "it seems more and more evident that a vast region… has collapsed west of the Pillars of Hercules… in the not far distant past," and that "the question of Atlantis is placed anew before men of science." Hall pairs Plato's account with this geological argument to present Atlantis as the probable seat of the prehistoric "gods" and a source of the perennial wisdom.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hall leans on Plato and a single cited geologist; Termier himself concedes "no affirmation is yet permissible" and that the question cannot be settled "without the aid of oceanography."
