## Claim Statement

Hall presents Pythagoreanism as "a system of metaphysical speculation concerning the relationships between numbers and the causal agencies of existence" — the doctrine that number, ratio, and harmony are the underlying structure of the cosmos — and credits this school with first expounding "the music of the spheres."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He reports that the Pythagorean school "first expounded the theory of celestial harmonics or 'the music of the spheres'," required "the discipline of silence" as "the first rudiment of contemplation" before any teaching, and accepted "the theory of transmigration" (reincarnation) of the soul. Hall develops the doctrine across chapters on Pythagorean mathematics (the symbolism of the numbers and the tetractys) and "the Pythagorean theory of music and color," treating number as a sacred key to cosmic order. He frames Pythagoras as an initiate transmitting Mystery teaching.
