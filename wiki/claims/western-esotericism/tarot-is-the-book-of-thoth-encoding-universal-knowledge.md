## Claim Statement

Hall presents the **Tarot** as the "Book of Thoth" — a résumé of Egyptian esoteric learning crystallized in hieroglyphic/pictorial form — a complete encyclopedia of universal knowledge encoded in cards, which degenerated into fortune-telling and card games only after its true meaning was forgotten.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Quoting Éliphas Lévi (via Westcott), Hall describes the Tarot as "a Key to the Ancient Book of Thoth… a résumé of the esoteric learning of the Egyptians," whose pictured symbols, once "forgotten or misunderstood… fell into the hands of the sham diviners." He details the structure — 78 cards, of which the **22 major trumps** ("the lineal descendants of the Hieroglyphics of the Tarot") correspond to the letters of the Hebrew sacred alphabet, plus 56 minor cards in four suits (Swords, Cups, Wands, Coins). The cards function "as separate and complete hieroglyphs," as a "philosophic alphabet," and as "the key to the magical constitution of man," cross-indexing Masonic, Qabbalistic, Pythagorean (numerological), and alchemical knowledge. He cites Lévi: "An imprisoned person, with no other book than the Tarot, if he knew how to use it, could in a few years acquire universal knowledge."
