## Claim Statement

Hall expounds the ancient doctrine that **man is a microcosm** — a miniature of the universe — so that the human body mirrors, part for part, the structure of the cosmos (the **Macrocosm** or "Grand Man"); to know the body rightly is to read the universe, per the Hermetic axiom "as above, so below."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He writes that "the pagans believed that the zodiac formed the body of the Grand Man of the Universe… which they called the Macrocosm (the Great World)," with the human form as its microcosmic counterpart, and quotes **Paracelsus**: "The body comes from the elements, the soul from the stars, and the spirit from God." The chapter "The Human Body in Symbolism" maps "the philosophical manikin," "the three universal centers," and the correspondences by which the parts and members of man answer to the planets, signs, and divine principles.

## Contexts Within the Source

A core expression of the symbol-as-cipher principle ([[The secret doctrine is deliberately encoded in symbol and allegory]]) applied to anatomy; it underlies Hall's reading of the Tarot, the Qabbalistic Tree, and the Hiramic legend as maps of man-as-cosmos. The macrocosm/microcosm correspondence is the same principle behind [[World religions encode a solar theology of three suns and an invisible spiritual Sun|the three-suns/three-centers analogy]].
