## Claim Statement

Hall argues the Mysteries deliberately chose **symbolism and allegory** to preserve their secret doctrine — a method that simultaneously reveals the teaching to the worthy and conceals it from the profane — so that the world's myths, scriptures, art, and rituals are, in effect, ciphers of the hidden wisdom.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He writes: "Rejecting man-conceived dialects as inadequate and unworthy to perpetuate divine ideas, the Mysteries thus chose symbolism as a far more ingenious and ideal method of preserving their transcendental knowledge. In a single figure a symbol may both reveal and conceal, for to the wise the subject of the symbol is obvious, while to the ignorant the figure remains inscrutable." Accordingly, the seeker "must search for that doctrine not upon the open pages of books which might fall into the hands of the unworthy but in the place where it was originally concealed" — the symbol. The book's surveys of the cryptogram, the Tarot, numerology, and the symbolism of plants, stones, and the human body apply this principle.

## Contexts Within the Source

The methodological key to the whole work and to [[A single secret doctrine has been preserved by the Mystery schools across all ages]]; it justifies Hall's reading of literal myth/scripture as encoded esoteric teaching — the same hermeneutic Jonathan Black uses in [[The secret history must be read as an imaginative exercise in altered consciousness]].
