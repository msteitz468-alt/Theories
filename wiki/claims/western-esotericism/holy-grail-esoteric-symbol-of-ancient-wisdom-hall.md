## Claim Statement

Hall reads the **Holy Grail** (and "Mystic Christianity" generally) esoterically: the Grail is not merely a relic but a symbol preserving the Ancient Wisdom under Christian forms — at once a solar/Nature-myth allegory, the human heart and "the real Self," and a variant of the Masonic "Lost Word."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He relays the legend that the Grail was fashioned from the **Lapis Exilis**, the green stone struck from Lucifer's crown by Michael, later the cup of Christ's blood carried by Joseph of Arimathea — the relic behind the Arthurian Round Table quests. Hall interprets: "the quest for the Holy Grail is the eternal search for truth," that some hold "the Knights of the Holy Grail to have been a powerful organization of Christian mystics perpetuating the Ancient Wisdom," that Mackey "sees in it a variation of the Masonic legend of the Lost Word," and that it is "undoubtedly… an early pagan Nature myth" engrafted on Christianity — "a type of the ark or vessel in which the life of the world is preserved." To the seeker, finding the Grail is "the consummation of the *magnum opus*."

## Contexts Within the Source

Hall's esoteric Christianity, applying [[The secret doctrine is deliberately encoded in symbol and allegory]] to the Grail and crucifixion; the "martyred Sun Gods whose blood… caught in the cup of matter" reading ties to [[World religions encode a solar theology of three suns and an invisible spiritual Sun]] and the "Lost Word" links it to [[The Hiramic legend encodes the soul's descent into matter and initiatory resurrection]]. Contrast the literal bloodline reading of the Grail in the *Holy Blood, Holy Grail* material.
