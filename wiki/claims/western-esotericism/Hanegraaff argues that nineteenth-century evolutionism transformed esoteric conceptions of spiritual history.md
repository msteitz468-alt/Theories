## Claim Statement
Hanegraaff argues that before the eighteenth century, Western esotericism conceived time either as degeneration from an original perfect state (prisca theologia) or as static, ever-available truth (philosophia perennis) — not as progress. German Idealist evolutionary models (Schelling, Hegel), fed by both the "alchemical paradigm" and the Platonic "Great Chain of Being" (Arthur Lovejoy's plenitude reinterpreted temporally), together with Darwinian biology and colonial-era anthropology (Tylor, Frazer's magic-religion-science progression), fused into a new "super-story" of spiritual evolution. This reshaped esotericism most visibly in H. P. Blavatsky's Theosophy, whose cosmology of seven evolving "root races" (with contemporary humanity as the "Teutonic" subrace of the "Aryan" root race) was, Hanegraaff stresses, an ethnocentric and racially charged doctrine. Evolutionism also drove the spread of reincarnation as a mainstream esoteric belief from the nineteenth century on.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The notion of spiritual progress by means of evolution is extremely familiar to us today, but before the eighteenth century it did not yet exist... the dominant model of the prisca theologia implied a process of degeneration... whereas the conception of philosophia perennis emphasized that nothing new was to be expected." On Blavatsky: "Blavatsky developed an ambitious evolutionary cosmology... built around the concept of seven 'root races'... She claimed... that we had now progressed up to the fifth 'Teutonic' subrace of the fifth 'Aryan' root race." On ethnocentrism: "All these theories of evolution were based on the confident and obviously ethnocentric assumption that the white middle and upper classes of modern Western civilization... stood at the pinnacle of the evolutionary process." On reincarnation: "her eventual defence of it is inseparable from her evolutionist beliefs and her discovery of karma as a universal and impersonal 'natural law'... during the twentieth century reincarnation has become one of the most universal and widespread beliefs in esoteric milieus."

## Contexts Within the Source
