## Claim Statement

Hall reads Freemasonry's central myth — the murder of **CHiram (Hiram) Abiff**, the Master Builder of Solomon's Temple, and his "raising" — as an esoteric allegory on two levels at once: a **solar myth** (the sun "slain" by the autumn signs and "raised" at the vernal equinox) and a drama of the **human soul**, which "dies" by descending into matter and is "resurrected" through initiation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He calls CHiram "the martyred sun… the Universal Benefactor, the Fiery Architect of the Divine House, who carries with him to the grave that Lost Word which, when spoken, raises all life," discovered by Aries and raised "by the Lion of Judah" — "Each year the great tragedy is repeated, and the glorious resurrection takes place." On the soul level, CHiram is "the third, or incarnating, part" of the triune spirit: "he dies at the gates of matter… buried in the elements of creation," so that "to the average individual, physical birth actually signifies the death of CHiram, and physical death the resurrection of CHiram" — but "to the initiate… the resurrection of the spiritual nature is accomplished without the intervention of physical death." The three murderers represent "birth, growth, and decay."

## Contexts Within the Source

Hall's signature demonstration of [[The secret doctrine is deliberately encoded in symbol and allegory]]: the Masonic and Christic "savior-god" dramas are the same solar/initiatory allegory. His "death into matter / initiatory rebirth" reading parallels Jonathan Black's [[History is a descent of spirit into matter]], and gives an esoteric counterpart to Heckethorn's prosaic [[Freemasonry derives prosaically from medieval operative masons' guilds|operative-guild account of Masonry]].
