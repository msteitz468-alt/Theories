## Claim Statement

Hall presents **Hermes Trismegistus** ("Thrice Greatest") — the divine sage of Egypt — as the fountainhead of the Hermetic tradition and the revealer to humanity of the arts and sciences, whose teaching (the *Divine Pymander* and the Hermetic books) carries "the sum of the Abyss, and the divine knowledge to all posterity."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He recounts the initiatory vision of the *Divine Pymander* — the candidate beholding a transfigured Hermes bearing "a winged rod, entwined with serpents," who vanquishes the dragon Typhon — and reports the tradition that Hermes authored tens of thousands of books and "revealed to mankind… medicine, chemistry, law, art, astrology, music, rhetoric, Magic, philosophy, geography, mathematics (especially geometry), anatomy, and oratory." Hall notes Hermes was identified with the Egyptian **Thoth**, the Hebrew **Enoch** ("Second Messenger of God"), and the Latin **Mercury**, and was "nearest to God… the Messenger of the Gods."
