## Claim Statement

A lost religion and civilization belonged to a people who lived on a planet occupying the same space that the Earth now occupies, "before" the Earth existed. These people destroyed their own planet through their own error and, after the Earth was formed and prepared, they reincarnated on it. Their subconscious, historical memories of their lost world and its destruction became the psychological template for the birth of human religion.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Seth outlines this in the book outline given in Session 510:
> "...a lost religion, belonging to a people of which you have no information. These people lived on a planet in the same space that your earth now occupies, 'before' your planet existed. They destroyed it through their own error, and were reincarnated when your planet was prepared. Their memories became the basis for the birth of religion as you now think of it."

He reiterates this in Chapter 21:
> "These people destroyed their own planet... and were reincarnated when your planet was prepared. Their memories became the basis for the birth of religion... The stories of the Garden of Eden and the Fall, for example, are distorted memories of their home planet and their loss of it."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is presented as a primary key to understanding the deep psychological roots of human religious impulses. Seth frames religious dogmas, myths of cataclysms (like the Deluge or the Fall of Man), and stories of paradise lost not as divine revelations or historical Earth events, but as genetic and subconscious memories of a completely different planetary existence that ended in self-destruction.
