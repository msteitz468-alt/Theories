## Claim Statement

The book's personal driver: Monroe's search for "the missing Basic" and "the new direction" was intensified when his wife **Nancy** was diagnosed with cancer, "the knowledge that for Nancy's sake there was little time to spare." Ronald Russell records that Monroe completed the exploration "while Nancy was still with him in physical reality, and that he and others were able to apply the knowledge he gained to help her in her own ultimate journey."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Russell's foreword frames the whole book around this race against time; the book is dedicated to Nancy Penn Monroe. The Lifeline retrieval framework (Focus 23→27) and the conviction of survival are presented not as abstractions but as tools applied to a real death in Monroe's own family. Monroe himself died in 1995, the year after publication — making the title's "ultimate journey" also his own.
