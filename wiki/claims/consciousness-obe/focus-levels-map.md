## Claim Statement

Monroe developed a numbered "Focus level" system to designate reproducible, instrumentally verifiable states of consciousness along a spectrum from normal waking (C-1) through deep non-physical states. Each Focus level represents a consistent, trainable state that can be achieved and re-achieved through Hemi-Sync guidance. The Gateway Program introduces participants to these levels sequentially. The levels form a practical map of consciousness rather than a purely theoretical schema: they are operationally defined by EEG brain-wave patterns and subjective phenomenology.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Monroe defines the principal levels as follows in Chapters 2–3 and the Explorer transcripts:

**C-1**: Normal waking consciousness — "a clearing in a dark forest."

**Focus 10**: "Mind awake, body asleep." All physiological responses are those of light or deep sleep, but the brain-wave pattern shows a distinctive mix of sleep waves overlaid with beta (wakefulness) signals. "We could tell easily from the instrument readout when they were and when they were not in Focus 10. It could not be imagined or faked."

**Focus 12**: All physical-data input is shut off; consciousness reaches out beyond the five senses. The action of the Gateway Program begins here — "perspectives and overviews change drastically" and the participant "truly understands that he is 'more than his physical body.'"

**Focus 15**: Referenced in participant testimonials as involving "increased vibrations" — one participant described feeling "projected like a Sputnik into the universe."

**Focus 21**: Referenced in Explorer session transcripts. One entity communicating through an Explorer described it: "Twenty-one can be either physical or nonphysical, depending on where you choose to focus the energy."

**Levels 22–28**: Described by a communicating entity in session #366 as "your bridge. They are your levels that you enter upon death." Level 28 marks the crossing of the bridge, beyond which consciousness "would not again assume human form of any kind, not even as a learning experience."

**Higher levels**: The same entity described a seven-circle system (49 levels) and then seven-circle systems nested within larger circles, implying an infinite hierarchy of consciousness development.

**Level 27**: Referenced as a location within the rings where participants find deceased relatives or persons who have "graduated."

In the Appendix, Monroe's first book coined "Locale I/II/III" terminology for the same spectrum; *Far Journeys* uses Focus levels as the primary framework, with the rings of souls around Earth corresponding to the inner focus levels above C-1.

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe emphasizes that the Focus levels are navigational guideposts rather than metaphysical assertions — they serve the practical function of allowing trainers and subjects to orient relative to one another. The system is explicitly designed to be non-dogmatic: "There is no dogma related to the Gateway Program — except that when you begin the session, you should seriously consider that you are 'more than your physical body.'"

Explorer transcripts suggest the numerical assignments are human-side conventions. One entity communicating through subject SHE implied a different mapping ("Twenty-two through twenty-eight are your bridge") that does not correspond exactly to Monroe's training-program numbering, suggesting some interpretive drift between the experiential reality and the labeling system.
