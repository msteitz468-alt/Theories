## Claim Statement

Physical matter is not continuous, but behaves like an oscillator. Atoms and bodies vibrate at high frequencies, blinking off and on. At the turning point of each vibration (the null point or point of rest), matter temporarily halts and disappears from three-dimensional physical reality, entering a state of infinite velocity and nonlocality in the non-physical void.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Bentov outlines this in Chapter 3:
> "...our physical bodies and all matter is made up of interacting electromagnetic fields vibrating at tremendous frequencies... Each of the points of rest is reached through a period of motion... The nervous system translates this in a Morse code of action or motion and rest..."

He explains the disappearance of the oscillator at the turning point:
> "...any oscillator will tend to disappear and reappear at its own rate. The atoms of our bodies are such oscillators; they vibrate at a rate of about 1015 Hz. It is possible that our bodies blink on and off at this very high rate." (Ch. 4)

At the turning point, velocity becomes infinite:
> "...at that moment of rest, when the pendulum changes direction... the distance to be traveled is zero, and the time taken is zero... velocity becomes infinite, and the pendulum is nonlocal—it is present everywhere in the universe at once." (Ch. 3)

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim explains how out-of-body travel and altered states are physically possible. Because our bodies blink off and on at $10^{15}$ Hz, we are constantly entering absolute space-time. Normally, our sensory focus keeps us locked into physical coordinates. By quietening the mind, the observer (psyche) can detach during the off-periods, traveling nonlocally without physical restriction.
