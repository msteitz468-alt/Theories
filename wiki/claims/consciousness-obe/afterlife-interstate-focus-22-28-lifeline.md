## Claim Statement

Monroe maps the post-death "Interstate" as a sequence of **Focus levels (22–28)** and reports that, through the Institute's **Lifeline** program (begun June 1991), ordinary trained participants — not just Monroe — could reach these states and perform "retrieval": helping the recently dead who are stuck to move onward. This collaborative, teachable retrieval work is the "new direction" the book is named for.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Building on the earlier Focus levels (10, 12, 15, 21), Monroe defines: **Focus 22** (humans in partial consciousness — coma, delirium, intoxication); **Focus 23** (the recently dead who cannot accept or free themselves from the Earth Life System); **Focus 24–26** the "Belief System Territories" (the nonphysical dead grouped by religious/philosophical creed); **Focus 27**, the "Reception Center" or "Park," an artificial way-station easing the transition; and **Focus 28**, beyond human thought. He reports ~200 people (physicians, psychologists, engineers, etc.) completed Lifeline in its first fourteen months, almost all reaching Focus 27 and many "knowing for certain that they would survive the physical death process." Participants phase to Focus 27 for guidance, then return to Focus 23 to coax stuck individuals onward; some peel off into the Belief System Territories, others reach the Park to be met by loved ones and counseled on next steps (reincarnation, reunion, retrieval roles, nonhuman existence elsewhere).
