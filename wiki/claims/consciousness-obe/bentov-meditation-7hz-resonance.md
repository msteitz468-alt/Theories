## Claim Statement

Bentov asserts that during deep meditation the heart-aorta system enters a standing-wave resonance of approximately 7 cycles per second (Hz), which entrains the entire body in coherent micro-motion, locks that motion into phase with the Earth's electrostatic (Schumann) resonance, and can further entrain other meditating individuals worldwide.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Heart-aorta resonance mechanism (Ch. 1 / Appendix):** Bentov explains that each heartbeat sends a pressure pulse down the aorta; when breathing stops, the timing of reflected pulses synchronises with outgoing pulses so that the aorta acts as a tuned half-wavelength resonator:

> "It causes the body to move harmoniously up and down about seven times a second, hence the nice, regular, large-amplitude sinewave pattern... The amplitude or height of this signal is about three times the average of the normal signal. Another characteristic of resonant behavior is that it requires for its sustenance a minimum amount of energy."

**Meditation extends the resonant state (Ch. 3):** Bentov states that meditative techniques evolved precisely to sustain this resonance:

> "Techniques of extending this harmonious resonant state have been known for thousands of years. These are the different meditative techniques. They slow down the metabolic rate of the body so that much less oxygen is required to keep the body going... The resonant state will naturally apply to the whole body. The skeleton and all the inner organs will move coherently at about 7 cycles per second."

He adds that the body's natural frequency makes this low-effort: "It therefore takes very little effort on behalf of the heart-aorta system to drive the body at this rate. It is similar to pushing a swing in correct timing. The normally occurring destructive interference ceases, and the body starts acting in an increasingly coherent fashion."

**Coupling to the planetary field (Ch. 3):** Bentov notes that the Schumann resonance of the Earth–ionosphere cavity is approximately 7.5 cycles per second, matching the body's micro-motion of 6.8–7.5 Hz:

> "We may say now that in deep meditation the human being and the planet system start resonating and transferring energy. This is occurring at a very long wavelength of about 40,000 km., or just about the perimeter of the planet. In other words, the signal from the movement of our bodies will travel around the world in about one-seventh of a second through the electrostatic field in which we are embedded. Such a long wavelength knows no obstacles, and its strength does not attenuate much over large distances."

**Global entrainment effect (Ch. 3):** He further asserts that a meditating body acts as a broadcast emitter:

> "A nucleus of meditating body or bodies will emit a simple harmonic motion or a 'sound' of approximately 7 Hz. through the electrostatic field of the planet. This emission will entrain others and help them in their effort to achieve the resonant state. The more bodies are thus locked in, the stronger the signal becomes. Due to the different time zones around the globe, there are always some people resonating and keeping this 'sound' going."

**Kundalini and pathological triggering (Appendix):** Bentov notes that sustained exposure to external vibrations in the 4–7 Hz range (e.g., from a car's suspension or air-conditioning ducts) can inadvertently trigger a similar physiological sequence in susceptible individuals, which he associates with the kundalini phenomenon.

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim forms Bentov's central physiological bridge between ordinary biology and altered states of consciousness. He uses his background in bio-medical engineering to present the 7 Hz resonance as the physical mechanism that allows out-of-body and heightened-awareness states: once the body moves coherently and locks to the planetary field, the "observer" can be ejected. The claim recurs across chapters: the mechanics are laid out in Ch. 1, the meditative extension in Ch. 3, and the cortical / kundalini dimension in the Appendix.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Bentov does not offer counter-evidence within the text. He acknowledges that the resonant state is disrupted as soon as normal breathing resumes and that sustained resonance during breathing requires considerable meditative skill.
