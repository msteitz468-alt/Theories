## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that one of the three distinct non-physical environments he encountered is "Locale I," which he also calls "the Here-Now." Locale I is defined as the space that is co-extensive with ordinary physical reality at the present moment — the same world perceived by the five physical senses, but experienced from the vantage point of the Second Body rather than the physical body.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Monroe defines the concept in Chapter 4:

> "One of the most common questions that arises during any discussion of the Second Body and the Second State is: where do you go? In evaluation of all experiments, there evolved what seemed to be three Second State environments. The first of these was identified as Locale I, for lack of a better nomenclature. More appropriately, it could be called the 'Here-Now'."

> "Locale I is the most believable. It consists of people and places that actually do exist in the material, well-known world at the very moment of the experiment. It is the world represented to us by our physical senses which most of us are fairly sure does exist. Visits to Locale I while in the Second Body should not contain strange beings, events, or places. Unfamiliar, perhaps, but not strange and unknown. If the latter is the case, then perception is distorted."

Monroe describes Locale I as the environment where the only externally verifiable (evidential) data can be obtained, since it corresponds to the known physical world:

> "Thus it is that the only evidential results provable by standard methods of confirmation have taken place while moving about via the Second Body in Locale I."

The statistical chapter confirms: "Locale I conforms strictly to the physical material world in all respects."

Monroe notes that travel to Locale I is in a sense "forced" — the Second Body's natural environment is actually Locale II, and keeping it in Locale I requires deliberate concentration: "travel to points in the physical world is a 'forced' process in the Second Body state. Given the opportunity of the slightest mental relaxation, the Supermind will guide you in your Second Body into Locale II."

## Contexts Within the Source

A major experimental obstacle in Locale I travel is navigation: without physical landmarks visible from overhead, orienting to a specific target such as a specific person's house is extremely difficult. Monroe also notes perceptual distortions — visual input in Locale I "registers in shadings of black and white" under most conditions, and strong light sources can produce misleading impressions (e.g., dark hair appearing blond under overhead lighting).
