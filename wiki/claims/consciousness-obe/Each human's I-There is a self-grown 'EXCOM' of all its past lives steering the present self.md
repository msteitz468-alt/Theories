## Claim Statement

In *Ultimate Journey* Monroe develops the **I-There** from a cluster of incarnations into an active inner organization he names **EXCOM** (Executive Committee). Investigating his own I-There from the inside, he finds it layered (Memory, Fear, Emotional layers behind a "broken barrier") and populated by hundreds of past-life personalities — among them "Talo," "Cass," "Asha," and a ninth-century French court jester. This committee, he reports, selected the time, place, and "DNA mix" of his birth, has steered and rescued him throughout his life, plants the dreams that triggered his out-of-body experiences, and is now working through him to reach "escape velocity." Crucially, EXCOM insists it is not an external guardian: "We aren't your anything. We and you are the same." Monroe concludes that **every human possesses such an individual I-There with its own self-grown Executive Committee** — the experiential basis of "the multiplicity of self."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The framework unfolds across two chapters. In Ch. 11 ("Turning Inward") Monroe penetrates his own I-There, finding "a layer… containing every moment of my life to date" and beyond it the "Repertory" of past-life beams. In Ch. 12 ("Inside the Inside") he holds an extended dialogue with it:

- Naming: "How about… an abbreviation of Executive Committee—that's close to what we are." / "I'll settle for that—**EXCOM!**"
- On origin: "We made the decision to become human again. We selected the time and place and organized the **DNA mix**—elements from the physical and elements from us."
- On identity: "We aren't your anything. **We and you are the same.** You've been helping yourself all the time. We are just the part that helps you remember."
- On multiple lives: "Up to now human lives have been and are used in such a random way that it's not possible to get wide enough experience in any one life. So we keep on returning until we have what we need."
- On purpose: "You are the best chance we have ever had…. The way out. **Escape velocity. Not just eternity, but infinity.**"
- Scope: members include "Talo… one of us—one of you—who lived in another energy system," and the speaker "was a court jester in France, in the ninth century."

Monroe summarizes: "I am now sure that **every human has his or her own individual I-There, complete with a particular self-grown Executive Committee.**" In Ch. 13 he adds that in sleep "my EXCOM took over and we worked together," handling retrieval of the dead and the "helping mode" inserted into others' lives.

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe says the discovery "radically altered my own Different Overview" and "filled in many holes in my Known file." He reframes the multiplicity of self he had glimpsed in earlier chapters (retrieving figures who turn out to be "earlier versions of himself") as a single organized inner committee, and likens his physical self to "a shuttle craft or a scout probe operator for the mother ship." He notes a "reserve or substitute" — a female member of the same I-There simultaneously incarnate as his backup.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Monroe records EXCOM resisting the comforting "guardian angel" reading he initially reaches for ("Oh no. We aren't your anything"), and the committee repeatedly declining to supply the final answer he seeks: "We cannot give you that answer…. it lies with you." He presents the whole exchange as a self-to-self dialogue in which "there is no barrier of any kind," not an encounter with a separate being.
