## Claim Statement

The historical figure of Jesus Christ was not a single historical individual, but a composite figure formed in history by the overlapping lives of three distinct psychically gifted individuals who belonged to a single entity: John the Baptist, Jesus of Nazareth, and Saul of Tarsus (Paul). Seth also claims that the third personality (Paul/Saul) will return in the future (around 2075) as a reformer to correct the organizational and dogmatic distortions he introduced.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Seth outlines this in Chapter 21 (Session 585):
> "There were three men whose lives became confused in history and merged, and whose composite history became known as the life of Christ... The three men were a part of one entity, gaining physical existence in one time."

He lists the three personalities:
> "The entity was born once as John the Baptist, and then he was born in two other forms. One of these contained the personality that most stories of Christ refer to..."

In Session 586, he clarifies that the third personality was Saul of Tarsus:
> "The third historical personage... took upon himself the role of a zealot... Historically now, was Paul or Saul... It was his job to form a physical framework; and even then he was afraid that the framework would strangle the ideas, but he saw no other way."

Seth predicts the return of the third personality:
> "He will return as the third personality, just mentioned, in your future... By 2075, all of this will be already accomplished... he will emerge once again, this time to destroy those distortions... carrying within him the characteristics of all the three personalities."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim occurs in Seth's discussion on the meaning of religion and the nature of All-That-Is. He explains that an entire entity's energy is too vast to fit into a single physical vehicle, which is why the Christ entity chose to manifest across three simultaneous lives. He frames Saul/Paul's dogmatic errors as necessary for the survival of the ideas in that violent era, but states that Saul's future return is required to dissolve the church structures and re-emphasize individual spiritual contact with All-That-Is.
