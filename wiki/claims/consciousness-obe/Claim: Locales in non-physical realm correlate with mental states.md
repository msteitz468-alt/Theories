## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that within Locale II — the primary non-physical environment he encountered — one's destination, companions, and experiences are determined not by conscious intent but by one's deepest emotional states, motivations, and personality. The locale effectively mirrors or enacts the inner mental life of whoever enters it.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Monroe states the governing principle of Locale II directly:

> "Superseding all appears to be one prime law. Locale II is a state of being where that which we label thought is the wellspring of existence. It is the vital creative force that produces energy, assembles 'matter' into form, and provides channels of perception and communication."

The rule of correspondence between inner state and experienced locale is captured in the phrase "Like attracts Like":

> "A facet learned in this medium of thought explains much. It is: Like attracts Like. I didn't realize there was such a rule that acted so specifically. It had been to me nothing more or less than an abstraction. Project this outward, and you begin to appreciate the infinite variations found in Locale II. Your destination seems to be grounded completely within the framework of your innermost constant motivations, emotions, and desires. You may not consciously want to 'go' there, but you have no choice. Your Supermind (soul?) is stronger and usually makes the decision for you. Like attracts like."

Monroe further states:

> "In Locale II, reality is composed of deepest desires and most frantic fears. Thought is action, and no hiding layers of conditioning or inhibition shield the inner you from others, where honesty is the best policy because there can be nothing less."

The "nearest" areas of Locale II (closest to the physical world in "vibratory frequency") are described as populated by beings governed by raw emotion — including both living people sleeping and the recently dead who have not yet adapted:

> "This implies that the areas of Locale II 'nearest' the physical world (in vibratory frequency?) are peopled for the most part with insane or near-insane, emotionally driven beings. For the most part, this seems to be true."

Monroe also describes this in terms of heaven and hell:

> "Your destination in the heaven or hell of Locale II seems to be grounded completely within the framework of your deepest constant (and perhaps non-conscious) motivations, emotions, and personality drives. The most consistent and strongest of these act as your 'homing' device when you enter this realm."

He reports personal confirmation of this principle: "I am sure of this because it always works this way when I have traveled non-physically in Locale II. It works this way whether I want it to or not. The least stray desire at the wrong time, or a deep-seated emotion I wasn't aware of, diverts my trip in that 'like' direction."

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe connects this principle to the difficulty of controlling OBE travel in general: since deep emotional and unconscious motivations override conscious intent, the traveler in Locale II is constantly being redirected by their own inner states. This same "thought-as-homing-device" mechanism is why visits to specific people in Locale I also fail when stray thoughts intervene.
