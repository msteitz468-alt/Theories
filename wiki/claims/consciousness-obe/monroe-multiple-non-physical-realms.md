## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that he encountered not one but (at minimum) three distinct non-physical environments during his OBE experiments, each with qualitatively different physical laws, inhabitants, and experiential qualities. He names these Locale I, Locale II, and Locale III.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Monroe introduces the classification in Chapter 4:

> "In evaluation of all experiments, there evolved what seemed to be three Second State environments."

### Locale I — "The Here-Now"

The physical world at the present moment, accessed via the Second Body. Governed by the same physical laws as the waking world. The only locale in which externally verifiable (evidential) observations are possible. Monroe describes it as containing "people and places that actually do exist in the material, well-known world at the very moment of the experiment."

### Locale II — The Primary Non-Physical Environment

Monroe's statistical summary: "Locale II is many-faceted, but evidences only a few of the patterns common to Locale I. It is an area of energy fields both familiar and strange, where there is no gravity, but, significantly, several of the more vital laws of physics do apply. Socially, historically, and philosophically, there is very little parallel to Locale I."

Its defining characteristic is that thought is the operative force: "Locale II is a non-material environment with laws of motion and matter only remotely related to the physical world… Superseding all appears to be one prime law. Locale II is a state of being where that which we label thought is the wellspring of existence." Time as understood physically is non-existent. Locale II "is inhabited, if that is the word, by entities with various degrees of intelligence with whom communication is possible." It contains what Monroe describes as analogs of heaven and hell.

### Locale III — A Parallel Physical World

Accessed through a specific technique (a 180-degree rotational separation), Locale III appeared as an almost-physical realm: "Locale III, in summary, proved to be a physical-matter world almost identical to our own. The natural environment is the same. There are trees, houses, cities, people, artifacts, and all the appurtenances of a reasonably civilized society." Yet it differs in ways that rule out its being any known period of Earth history — no electrical technology, different vehicle designs, different customs and history.

Monroe's statistical chapter states: "Locale III poses inscrutable questions. It has almost identical characteristics to Locale I, except for several definite departures which are inexplicable."

### The Distinction Between Locales

Monroe emphasizes that the three locales are not merely different places but environments with fundamentally different operative laws: "Locale I conforms strictly to the physical material world in all respects. Locale II is many-faceted… Locale III poses inscrutable questions."

He also notes that a fourth category — an "intertime state" — was discovered but not classified within the three locales.

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe acknowledges the scientific difficulty of these claims: "Only extended exploration of the Second Body phenomenon by an expanded research group can bring about truly comparative studies of these areas. All that is needed is motivation."
