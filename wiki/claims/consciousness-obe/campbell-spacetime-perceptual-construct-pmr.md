## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that space and time are not fundamental features of reality but are perceptual constructs generated by the space-time rule-set that AUM evolved to constrain a subset of its consciousness (PMR). Space-time is a "consciousness interface filter" that defines the experience of physical reality for individuated consciousness units enrolled in PMR. It has no independent existence outside that rule-set.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell defines space-time's nature directly:

> "Space-time, in this context, is a construct of consciousness. It is not a physical substance or a thing — it is not a physical construct — it is created by imposing a set of constraints upon a subset of the larger reality."

He elaborates on the mechanism:

> "Space-time is the construct within which our fleshy bodies appear to live and interact. It is a consciousness interface filter that defines the perception (experience) of Physical Matter Reality (PMR) to participating sentient conscious entities. The experiences of PMR space, PMR time, as well as PMR mass, energy, and gravity are all derived from the space-time constraints placed on interacting consciousness."

On the ontological status of space itself:

> "It is not that we are separate from space-time and live within it, as we live in a house but are not part of the house. We experience space-time as our minds interpret and process the limited perceptions gathered by our senses. Contrary to popular belief, space-time is not a physical construct that we physical beings live within, but rather the result of a rule-set that defines the experiential virtual reality that we perceive."

And more radically: "There is no such thing as physical space!"

On the relationship between space and time: "Time, or equivalently, frequency is a fundamental attribute of AUM, whereas the notion of space is derived from time by specifying a constant velocity of propagation of information. That is why the citizens of space-time must live with c as the celestial speed limit. Time is fundamental; space is derived from time by specifying the constraint c."

He also notes alignment with Einstein: "As Einstein pointed out more than half a century ago, space and time, as we interact with and experience them, are illusions."

## Reasoning and Argument Structure

Campbell builds this conclusion from his model of AUM's digital structure:
1. AUM's fundamental substrate is non-spatial consciousness organized in reality cells.
2. AUM evolved a subset of its consciousness with a rule-set that defines how information propagates between cells at a constant maximum rate (c, the speed of light).
3. This constant propagation rate — time quantum divided by propagation rate — gives rise to the concept of spatial extent and therefore space.
4. All PMR phenomena (mass, energy, gravity) are consequences of this rule-set, not independent physical facts.

Therefore space-time is a "shadow" of AUM's deeper structure: "Our time, measured in seconds ... is a shadow of Fundamental Time; it is our sensory perception of a specific constrained and limited implementation of the more fundamental time defined by AUM-consciousness."
