## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that the entire universe — physical and nonphysical — derives from a single foundational assumption: a primordial, undifferentiated consciousness-energy he calls AUO (Absolute Unbounded Oneness). Campbell states that AUO, along with the Fundamental Process of evolution, is one of only two assumptions needed to derive "physics, the nature of your existence, your physical universe and experience, and provide a comprehensive working model of the larger reality."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell defines AUO as "a finite primordial-consciousness-potential-energy-thing" — the "One Source" or "Absolute" familiar from philosophy and theology, but stripped of dogma and given a minimalistic scientific treatment. He describes AUO as it appears before evolution has acted upon it:

> "You will soon discover that this seemingly infinite Absolute Unbounded Oneness (AUO) is not infinite. Nor is it absolute, unbounded, or a oneness — but only appears to be an infinite absolute unbounded oneness from a limited point of view within PMR."

Campbell explains that AUO is "primordial consciousness — an energy form that, like earth's early carbon based bio-forms, has the potential to evolve awareness." At its initial state, AUO is characterized as:

- Undifferentiated: "a seemingly infinite primordial consciousness whose beginning depends upon a causal requirement that is so far beyond our local reality that it is outside our rational consideration"
- Dimly aware at best: "not capable of forming or holding a single coherent thought"
- Digital in nature: constituted of "reality cells" capable of flipping between states
- A structurable potential: "an immense unstructured, but structurable form of digital potential energy"

He draws the explicit ontological conclusion: "All are connected through (and are a part of) the One Source: AUO consciousness. AUO is the foundation of everything, because everything exists in the relation of AUO to itself."

Campbell acknowledges this premise is "mystical from the PMR point of view" but argues it is the logically necessary starting point for any Big TOE that reaches beyond physical causality. He compares humanity's inability to know AUO's origins to bacteria being unable to comprehend the stock market: the question is outside our rational purview.

## Reasoning and Argument Structure

Campbell's argument proceeds as follows:
1. Any Theory of Everything must reach beyond Physical Matter Reality (PMR), since PMR's own causal logic cannot explain its own beginning.
2. The only things known to exist outside the constraints of space and time are "thought, mind, consciousness or spirit."
3. Therefore, the most parsimonious foundational assumption is a primordial consciousness — AUO — existing prior to space and time.
4. From AUO plus the Fundamental Process of evolution, all else is logically derived.

## Contexts Within the Source

The AUO premise is introduced and developed in Book 1, Section 2. Campbell notes the concept is not new — "it has always been (at least since about 600 [BCE]) a fundamental concept in various philosophic, spiritual, metaphysical, and religious traditions" — but states he uses only its "bare basics": "no frills, no dogma, and no extraneous conditions or attributes."
