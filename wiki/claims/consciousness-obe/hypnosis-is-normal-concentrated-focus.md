## Claim Statement

Hypnosis is a natural, daily mental state of highly concentrated attention on specific ideas or beliefs, rather than an artificial, medically induced, or sleep-like state. All individuals are in a continuous state of natural hypnosis regarding their core beliefs about reality, which directly instructs the subconscious to construct corresponding physical experiences.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Seth defines natural hypnosis in Chapter 16:
> "Let us first of all discuss the nature of hypnosis, quite natural hypnosis, and the ways in which you use it now. A trance is a trance is a trance... Hypnosis is merely a state of concentrated attention, in which you focus upon a specific idea or belief to the exclusion of other stimuli."

He explains that all beliefs are hypnotic commands:
> "You are already in a state of hypnosis, focused upon the beliefs you hold. Every belief is a hypnotic suggestion... The conscious mind directs these suggestions, and the inner mind executes them."

In Chapter 17, he adds that deliberate hypnosis is a tool to replace limiting suggestions with constructive ones:
> "You can utilize natural hypnosis deliberately in your present point of power... to bypass old limiting beliefs and introduce new, healing suggestions."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim demystifies the concept of hypnosis, reframing it as a normal biological and psychological mechanism of focus. Seth explains that when an individual concentrates on a single idea (such as fear of disease or confidence in success), they automatically enter a "trance" where their biological systems and environment organize to materialize that idea.
