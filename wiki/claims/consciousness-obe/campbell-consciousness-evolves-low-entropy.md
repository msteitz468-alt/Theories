## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that consciousness systems — unlike physical matter — naturally and spontaneously evolve toward lower entropy states (greater organization, brightness, and quality) when acted upon by the Fundamental Process of evolution. He treats entropy reduction as the technical measure of consciousness quality and spiritual growth.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell defines the directional arrow for consciousness evolution in direct contrast to physical matter:

> "Just as evolving non-living, non-growing entities naturally move toward higher entropy, consciousness naturally moves toward lower entropy. Dimness gradually gives way to brightness as entropy is lowered. The quality of consciousness also increases as entropy is decreased — we call this growth of quality, spiritual growth."

He states the identity between spiritual, personal, and technical terms:

> "Spiritual growth, personal growth, improving the quality of your consciousness, evolving your being, increasing your capacity to love, and decreasing the entropy of your consciousness are all essentially synonymous and equivalent."

Campbell defines the mechanism: the Fundamental Process of evolution explores available states, retains what produces greater organizational profitability, and drives consciousness toward lower entropy configurations:

> "The constant evolutionary pressure to maximize profitability by reducing entropy, made AUO progressively more complex and self-aware."

He defines "right growth" technically: "Right growth and right learning are technically defined as growth or learning that leads to a decrease in the entropy of your consciousness."

For the consciousness ecosystem overall:

> "Entropy reduction is the name of the game at all levels and within all dimensions of the consciousness-evolution fractal wherever evolution is succeeding."

## Reasoning and Argument Structure

Campbell uses the thermodynamic concept of entropy borrowed from physics and applies it as an analog to consciousness:

1. In PMR, physical matter (closed systems) trends toward higher entropy (more disorder) — the second law of thermodynamics.
2. In mind-space, where AUO/AUM exists, "seeking the lowest energy state is never a goal." Instead, for consciousness systems "the most profitable next-state is one that enables that system to minimize its entropy."
3. Therefore, consciousness evolution is the inverse of physical entropy increase: living, growing systems decrease their entropy through profitable self-organization.
4. The Fundamental Process explores all states and preserves those that achieve lower entropy — this is how AUO evolved into AUM, and how individuated consciousness units (people) evolve over time.

He distinguishes growing systems from non-growing ones: "Typically, growing things (things that are in a state of becoming) can naturally and spontaneously — for at least some limited period of time — decrease their entropy."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Campbell acknowledges entropy can also increase within consciousness if growth stops or regresses: "Every evolving complex system will eventually either keep growing (entropy decreases) or run out of evolutionary gas (entropy increases or stays the same)." He describes fear, ego, and self-destructive choices as mechanisms that raise entropy within an individuated consciousness.
