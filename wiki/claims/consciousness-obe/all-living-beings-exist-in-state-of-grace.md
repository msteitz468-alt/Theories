## Claim Statement

All living creatures, including humans, exist in a natural "state of grace" as their default biological and spiritual condition. This state automatically maintains physical health, emotional balance, and alignment with the universe, but in humans it can be blocked or disrupted by self-conscious, limiting beliefs of unworthiness, guilt, or fear.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Seth defines the state of grace in Chapter 9:
> "A state of grace is a condition in which all growth is effortless, a state of alignment in which the self and the universe are one... It is a natural state of grace, which provides biological health and spiritual alignment..."

He contrasts human self-consciousness with animal grace:
> "Animals exist in a state of natural grace. They do not worry about their worth, or whether they have a right to exist. They simply exist... The human body is also designed to exist in that state of grace, but your conscious mind can block it through beliefs in unworthiness, guilt, or fear."

In Chapter 12, he explains how natural conscience supports this state:
> "Natural grace and natural conscience are biological attributes... they are built-in feedback loops designed to keep you in alignment with your own entity and the natural environment."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim qualifies the nature of health and illness. Seth frames disease not as a natural biological default or a mechanical breakdown, but as a disruption of the inherent state of grace caused by the conscious mind adopting limiting or negative creeds. Re-entering grace is described as a matter of consciously rejecting artificial guilt and affirming the self's connection to All-That-Is.
