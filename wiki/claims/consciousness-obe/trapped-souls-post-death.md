## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that a significant portion of souls after physical death become trapped in post-death states from which they cannot independently exit. The primary trapping mechanism is the inability or refusal to accept that one is no longer physically alive. Secondary trapping arises from habituated compulsions — particularly sexual fixation — that are so absorbing that the soul remains unaware of any other dimension of existence. Monroe describes this not as divine punishment but as a consequence of the blanking process combined with cultural conditioning that leaves humans unprepared for non-physical existence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 7 (Surveys and Schematics), Monroe describes what he calls the innermost post-physical zone: "an enormous pile of writhing forms. It reached up, slanting back as high as I could see. In each direction, right and left, it swept off into the distance. It reminded me of nothing so much as the interweaving of huge fishing worms in the bottom of a can after being left there overnight. The motion was continuous, thousands upon thousands, each wet slippery form wiggling in and out among the others in the pile, searching, trying to do something… but never achieving satisfaction." Monroe identifies the forms as human, physically dead, emanating powerful sexuality. He attempts to extract an individual by pulling him by the foot out of the pile, but the man is completely unresponsive to spoken words, shows no recognition of Monroe's presence, and tries to crawl back in. Monroe cannot provide the radiation that would attract the man's attention and releases him.

In Chapter 11 (Rescue Mission), Monroe describes guiding BB through the innermost post-death ring: "These were the ones who had just been released from their physical body via death and vaguely knew they had but didn't have the rote to do much, if anything, about it. Occasionally as we passed, one would lean in our direction, which I had learned indicated the beginning of remembering — or the last vestiges of the forgetting process."

Monroe also describes an adjacent zone: "The inhabitants of this ring couldn't. They didn't or couldn't or wouldn't realize they were no longer physical. They were physically dead, no more physical body. Thus they kept trying to be physical… Bewildered, some spent all of their activity in attempting to communicate with friends and loved ones still in bodies or with anyone else who might come along, all to no avail. Others were held attracted to physical sites in which they had instilled great meaning or importance during their previous human lifetime. All had long forgotten or blocked deeply the technique that once was so basic: the M Band."

From a Gateway participant's testimonial (Chapter 3, subject 1644-CM): she reached a focus level approximately 100 and encountered "many people milling around: They looked like holograms, but conveyed the message of being 'alive.'" Some said they were "just exploring," some were "waiting for their bodies to die, so they could be free," and some said "they got there inadvertently, and were not able to return on their own." She adds: "Fact: There are people in various institutions who are catatonic or comatose and whom medical science has not been able to reanimate."

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe expresses intense compassion for those trapped in these zones and "anger at a system that could so inhibit, repress, and distort as to create the situation in front of me." He asks whether "these the castoffs of the human process, to remain so throughout eternity?" He does not resolve this question but implies that beings from outer rings do enter the inner rings specifically to assist — "Others are working on the effect, this end of the blockage" — and that Monroe's own INSPEC training is preparing him to help "cut back the cause."

Monroe frames the Gateway Program's function partly in terms of this problem: by establishing while alive that one survives death and can navigate non-physical reality, Gateway graduates are better equipped not to become trapped in the post-death inner rings.
