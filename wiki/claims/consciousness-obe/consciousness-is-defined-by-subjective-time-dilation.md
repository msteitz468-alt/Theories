## Claim Statement

The "quality" or level of an observer's consciousness is determined mathematically by the ratio of subjective time to objective clock time. In expanded or altered states of consciousness, the observer experiences time dilation—stretching subjective time relative to physical clock time—allowing them to observe reality in greater detail, process more information, and navigate non-physical space-time.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Bentov outlines this in Chapter 4:
> "We stretch our subjective time so that we are facing a subjective situation... Under altered states of consciousness, these two become separated, and we can function in our subjective space-time. This explains many phenomena..."

He defines the quality of consciousness in Chapter 5:
> "We defined levels of consciousness as being somehow connected with the ratio of subjective to objective time... The higher the level of consciousness, the larger the amount of subjective time available to the observer relative to a unit of objective time."

He notes that in deep meditation or altered states, the observer has more time to observe and act:
> "Clearly, what happens is that we somehow have more subjective time, and we can observe the act... in greater detail. If we can stretch our subjective time, then we have more time to observe events..." (Ch. 4)

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim defines Bentov's metric for consciousness. Rather than treating consciousness as an unmeasurable quality, he defines it mathematically. As an entity's consciousness evolves (from mineral to plant, animal, human, and spiritual bands), its capacity to perceive subjective time increases, allowing it to process information at higher rates and ultimately achieve omnipresence.
