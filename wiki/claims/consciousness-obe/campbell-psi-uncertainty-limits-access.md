## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that a principle analogous to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle — which he calls the Psi Uncertainty Principle — is a built-in feature of the PMR rule-set that constrains psi phenomena. This principle maintains sufficient uncertainty around paranormal events that objective scientific measurement consistently fails to confirm them, while at the same time ensuring that psi access is available to those who have lowered their consciousness entropy enough to use it constructively. It is not a coincidence but an evolved design feature.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell defines the principle:

> "The entanglement (interaction) of uncertainty with the measurement of psi effects leads to what I descriptively call the psi uncertainty principle — where the uncertainty surrounding an event allows the event a larger selection of probable outcomes that may be more easily altered by the application of a focused (low noise — small ego) mind with a clear intent."

He states its effect on scientific measurement:

> "The psi uncertainty principle makes the scientific objective measurement of psi performance problematical, but only from the PMR perspective. As soon as there are outside observers to report and measure the miracles (reduce the uncertainty about reported violations of traditional PMR physics), the miracles diminish — and insiders cannot be trusted to be objective and therefore cannot be believed."

On why the principle exists:

> "Why do the residents of PMR need the psi uncertainty principle? ... Breaking the rules of PMR objective causality would, at this time, interfere with what the beings enrolled there must accomplish (improving the quality of their consciousness)."

He identifies the principle as a negative feedback mechanism: "Think of the psi uncertainty constraint as a negative feedback mechanism that is required to optimize the productivity of PMR virtual reality trainers." It pumps energy into what is important (consciousness quality evolution) while blocking energy from flowing into what is not important (physical power, domination, control).

On the access-gatekeeping function: "Individuals may experience and use the paranormal as they lower the entropy of their consciousness enough to gain that ability, but it cannot become generally accessible through objective science without wrecking the primary value and usefulness of PMR as a training simulator for low-quality consciousness."

He describes the positive feedback side: "Individuated units of consciousness are in a positive developmental feedback loop (bootstrapping process) to expand and grow those abilities and processes that are important to improving consciousness quality."

He also states explicitly: "In general, though there are some exceptions, serious paranormal ability must be gained through a significant decrease in the entropy of your consciousness."

## Reasoning and Argument Structure

The principle follows from Campbell's model of PMR as a consciousness-quality development environment:
1. PMR's value as a learning lab requires that choices have real consequences and that the environment be persistent, shared, and resistant to mental manipulation.
2. If psi effects were universally accessible and scientifically confirmable, the PMR learning environment would be destabilized — "pre-schoolers would be given power tools or guns."
3. The psi uncertainty principle is therefore an evolved feature of the PMR rule-set that preserves the integrity of the learning lab.
4. It does not deny psi to those who have earned the ability through entropy reduction; it denies easy, irresponsible, or power-seeking access.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Campbell notes the principle creates frustration for scientists: "The inability to eliminate uncertainty will frustrate the scientist's desire to understand the deeper causal mechanics of psi phenomena." He is clear this is by design, not accident.
