## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that precognition is not mysterious or impossible but is a natural consequence of the structure of his model: The Big Computer (TBC) within NPMR continuously computes probable future reality surfaces for Our System (OS/PMR) based on current actualized states. Consciousness that is sufficiently developed and operationally aware in NPMR can access these computed probability surfaces, experiencing the most probable futures as precognitive impressions.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell describes the computing mechanism:

> "Only one of the significant possible future states will eventually be actualized and take its place on our seemingly continuous OS-PMR history thread. The state that eventually becomes actualized will probably be the one that was previously given the highest probability of being actualized — but not necessarily."

He defines the probable reality surface:

> "The probable reality surface represents the most likely future possibilities."

On how precognition occurs within this framework: "Only if your intuition finds a way to vent its content to the surface will you experience a psi event such as precognition — and then only if you are paying attention. The crack between your deeper consciousness and the surface of PMR awareness must establish an adequate, continuous (but not necessarily straightforward) connection of some type before the information-magma can find an acceptable way to express itself within your conscious awareness."

He describes the typical character of precognitive access: "Most precognition occurs in the margins — like a hot spring, or unpredictable geyser. As a result we experience fuzzy, difficult to interpret, precognitive moments that seem to come and go by their own volition as stresses, pressures, and personal significance constantly fluctuates and changes."

He also notes that conscious, deliberate access to probable future surfaces is possible through NPMR: "Future probable reality surfaces for a particular group, activity, or happening can be computed. Specific summations can be taken over all the choices made by sentient beings and all the changes of objects and energy that have, or are projected to have, an impact or influence on a particular group."

On the relative accuracy of group vs. individual prediction: "The rate of change (fluctuations on the surface) of the probability functions for expected events for individuals is much faster than the fluctuations for a large group of individuals. Thus, a nation's future is easier to predict than an individual's (is a more slowly varying and stable surface)."

## First-Person Evidence in Source

Campbell describes early experiments: "We traveled into the future and into the past... reading next week's newspaper headlines." He describes himself and Nancy Lea having "read three and four digit numbers written on a blackboard next to the control room" while out-of-body — a form of remote viewing — as well as explorations of probable future states during Monroe Institute research.

## Relationship to Psi Uncertainty

Campbell notes that the Psi Uncertainty Principle limits the clarity and repeatability with which precognitive information can be brought into PMR: "A paranormal event (precognitive dream or vision, for example) is without uncertainty, the number of people who can objectively verify this perfect demonstration of psi will always be small enough to produce no major or lasting impact on the larger society."
