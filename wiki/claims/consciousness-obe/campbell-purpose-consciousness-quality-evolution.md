## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that the purpose of human existence — and more broadly of all individuated consciousness within PMR — is to evolve the quality of one's consciousness by reducing its entropy. Physical life, experience, free will choices, and the learning environment of PMR all exist to serve this goal. Campbell frames this not as a belief or spiritual metaphor but as a technical conclusion derived from his model of AUM's structure.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell states the purpose directly and technically:

> "The evolution of our individuated unit of consciousness is the point of our existence. This is how the AUM-digital-mind-thing consciousness organism survives and grows. This is how the fundamental-consciousness-energy-ecosystem evolves, lowers its entropy, and avoids stagnation, regression (increasing entropy), and dissolution (death)."

He equates purpose with entropy reduction:

> "Your consciousness quality rises and falls based upon the intent that animates your everyday interactions."

On what constitutes growth: "Spiritual growth, personal growth, improving the quality of your consciousness, evolving your being, increasing your capacity to love, and decreasing the entropy of your consciousness are all essentially synonymous and equivalent."

He identifies what makes PMR a purposeful environment: "PMR is designed to serve as a learning lab — a place where units of individuated consciousness (sometimes referred to as beings, entities, or souls) can grow up (improve their quality) by exercising their free will intent within a virtual system of direct interactive experience and feedback."

He also frames the individual contribution to the whole: "Your personal contribution is not limited by a group function or a slow uncertain pass-along process with strong random components; it is simply measured by the quality that your consciousness brings to the table."

On the mechanism: "It is learning and personal growth that eventually enable you to make the right choices for the right reasons — naturally and intuitively. Every day of every year we are forced to make hundreds of significant choices as we interact with others... We are the sum total of those choices made over a lifetime, or a series of lifetimes. The substance of our being and the rate of our growth are defined by the quality of our choices."

He distinguishes the true purpose from surface-level material goals: "Consciousness does not exist to promote physical evolution within PMR; physical evolution within PMR exists to promote consciousness."

## Contexts Within the Source

Campbell develops this claim by drawing on the macro-level (AUM's need to reduce its system entropy through the participation of individuated units) and the personal level (each individual's opportunity to grow through free will choice in the learning environment of PMR). He notes that "growing up within a larger reality has much more to do with raising the quality of your consciousness than accumulating information. What matters most is the development of wisdom, understanding, and the capacity to love."
