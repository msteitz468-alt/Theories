## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that out-of-body experiences (OBEs, also called OOBEs) are not hallucinations, dreams, or artifacts of a dysfunctional brain, but instances of individuated consciousness directly accessing NPMR while the physical body (the PMR-interface) remains in a low-activity or sleep state. In his model, OBEs are entirely consistent with the structure of reality: consciousness is primary, the body is a projection interface, and NPMR is the larger reality within which PMR is embedded.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell describes the basic theoretical framing:

> "Out-of-body, as a phenomenon or happening, is much more mundane and common than it is strange. OOBE seems strange because the limitations we place on our concepts of consciousness and reality force us to reject many of the mental functions that are naturally available to our species."

He describes the early Monroe Institute research that established the evidential basis: "In about eight months we were ready for the world to give us a try. Bob put out the word that we needed a limited number of guinea pigs to try out our binaural-beat brainwave entrainment techniques for facilitating the projection of one's consciousness into the nonphysical as an aware operational entity."

On the personal experience of NPMR access during OBEs: "PMR and NPMR seemed to blend into a continuum and I found I could live in both realities simultaneously; it was no longer a matter of leaving one and going to the other. Now, it was merely a matter of shifting and splitting my focus — I lived and was continuously aware, sentient, and conscious (except when sleeping) in both reality systems simultaneously and permanently."

He describes specific evidential experiments conducted in NPMR: "We went places — to people's homes — and saw what they were doing, then called them or talked to them the next day to check it out. We traveled into the future and into the past. We read three and four digit numbers written on a blackboard next to the control room. Somebody would write a random number and we would read it while our bodies lay asleep."

He notes OBEs are also common in children: "Like most kids that age, he had frequent spontaneous out-of-body-experiences (OOBE)... At about five years of age, most children naturally and spontaneously have lots of OOBEs."

The physiological marker: "One key datum Bob had derived from his personal study of out-of-body experience (OOBE) was the perception of a 4 Hz oscillation within his body and consciousness just before exiting his body and sometimes just after returning to it."

## Theoretical Framing

Within Campbell's model, the OBE is straightforwardly explained: because consciousness is primary and PMR is a virtual reality within AUM's larger consciousness, an individuated consciousness is not fundamentally "inside" a body. The body is the Physical User Interface (PUI) — a constrained projection of consciousness into the PMR rule-set. During OBE, the focus of consciousness shifts from the PMR rule-set interface to direct experience of NPMR. This is not a departure from the body so much as a shift of attentional focus between layers of the same larger reality.
