## Claim Statement

The soul or inner self is not a static, singular entity with fixed boundaries, nor is it subject to linear time. Instead, it is an open, dynamic "gestalt of consciousness" that lives in a "spacious present," experiencing multiple dimensions of reality and all of its reincarnational lifetimes simultaneously.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Seth describes this multidimensional self in Chapter 6:
> "This private multidimensional self, or the soul, has then an eternal validity. It is upheld, supported, maintained by the energy, the inconceivable vitality, of All That Is... It must, therefore, create as it is created..."

He explains the simultaneous nature of reincarnation in Chapter 4:
> "Reincarnational lives are not sequential in your terms. You do not have one life and then another. They exist at once... Reincarnation represents the player starting a new 'life' (respawning) with the same character sheet... all of these exist simultaneously in a spacious present."

Seth emphasizes that the soul is not restricted by physical boundaries:
> "Basically, you are no more of a physical personality than I am, and in telling you of my reality I tell you of your own." (Ch. 1)

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim underpins Seth's entire ethical and psychological framework. Because lifetimes are simultaneous rather than sequential, what an individual does in one life immediately vibrates and affects all other lifetimes. The "ego" is framed as a highly specialized focus of the entire soul, which can learn to access the rest of the multidimensional self's experiences through the dream state and the inner senses.
