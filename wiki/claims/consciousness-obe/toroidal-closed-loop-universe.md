## Claim Statement

The physical universe is a closed-loop, curved system shaped like a self-reflecting torus (or smoke ring) that constantly turns itself inside out. In this model, matter is projected outward from a central core (a white hole), moves along lines of force across the outer surface of the torus, and is eventually pulled back into the central core (a black hole) to be recycled.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Bentov describes the shape of the universe in Chapter 8:
> "Such a body can be visualized as an elongated donut with a long thin hole through its center. This hollow ovoid shape is called a torus. We have here a torus which is constantly turning itself inside out, with matter flowing into the center core, through it, and out, thus forming the outgoing jet."

He compares it to a smoke ring:
> "...our model of the universe is a flattened form of that smoke ring, having a much smaller hole through it... We have a closed-loop model of the universe... showing how matter is projected, travels, and is recycled."

He notes that this loop curvature joins the beginning and end:
> "The universe is a closed system... matter moves out from the center, cools, aggregates, forms galaxies, and is eventually drawn back into the central core... This means that there is no absolute start or end, only a continuous cycle."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim provides the spatial cosmology of Bentov's model. He explains that space is curved by gravity and electromagnetic fields into a torus. This geometry allows the absolute void (the center core) to connect all points of the universe, providing a physical mechanism for instant, non-local telepathic communication and energetic recycling.
