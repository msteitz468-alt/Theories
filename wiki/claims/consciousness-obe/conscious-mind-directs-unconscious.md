## Claim Statement

The conscious mind is not weak or subordinate to the subconscious. Rather, it possesses complete control over all subconscious and unconscious processes, which are biological and psychological execution engines that translate the conscious mind's beliefs and suggestions into physical reality.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Jane Roberts highlights this in her Introduction:
> "The message is plain: We are not at the mercy of the subconscious, or helpless before forces that we cannot understand. The conscious mind directs unconscious activity and has at its command all of the powers of the inner self. These are activated according to our ideas about reality."

Seth explains the relationship in Chapter 5:
> "...the conscious mind is a portion of the inner self; that part that surfaces, so to speak, and meets physical reality more or less directly... The inner self uses the conscious mind as a window to look outward, and as a director to choose which realities to construct."

In Chapter 11, he reiterates that the subconscious only obeys the conscious focus:
> "The conscious mind is the carrier of beliefs. Your subconscious is not a dark dungeon of hidden horrors; it is simply a repository of energy that acts upon the conscious mind's beliefs. What you believe consciously is the blueprint that activates unconscious creation."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is designed to restore a sense of agency to the reader. Seth rejects models of psychology that present the subconscious as a hostile, independent force that dictates behavior. He frames the subconscious as an obedient, highly efficient organic database and processor that simply executes whatever instructions are sent by the conscious mind's focus and beliefs.
