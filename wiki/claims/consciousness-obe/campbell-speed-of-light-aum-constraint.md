## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that the speed of light (c) is not a fundamental constant of nature but a constraint that AUM evolved through the Fundamental Process to define the propagation rate of information within the space-time virtual reality. It is the defining upper limit of the PMR rule-set, and its value was arrived at through evolutionary optimization, not predetermined design.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell states the function of c in his model:

> "The constant upper limit of c (speed of light) defines the concepts of both distance and space within the space-time subset of AUM's consciousness."

He explains how space itself is derived from this constraint:

> "Time, or equivalently, frequency is a fundamental attribute of AUM, whereas the notion of space is derived from time by specifying a constant velocity of propagation of information. That is why the citizens of space-time must live with c as the celestial speed limit. Time is fundamental; space is derived from time by specifying the constraint c."

On how AUM arrived at the value of c:

> "How did AUM come up with the constant c? By occupying all the possibilities and building on the best results, by trial and error, by evolution."

He describes c's role in the formal structure of his model:

> "Distortions can appear to move through space-time at a maximum velocity of one space-time cell per DELTA-t... We have measured the upper limit on information transfer in PMR as 'c,' which is the symbol commonly used by scientists to represent the speed of light."

Campbell also notes what this implies for faster-than-light phenomena: "The fact that we might be clever enough to take a space-time shortcut through a 'wormhole' does not change the fact that c remains the upper speed limit on information propagation through space that defines our local experiential space within AUM's consciousness."

He frames c as one of several defining constants: "Applied mathematicians, scientists, and engineers have a tendency to define their reality in terms of its constraints expressed as physical constants. For example, c (the speed of light in a vacuum) defines the speed limit of matter in PMR. Think of c as one of several local PMR space-time constants that constrains our physical reality to a certain experience-set."

He notes that NPMR has its own, much larger upper speed limit for information propagation, since it has no spatial constraint analogous to c.

## Reasoning and Argument Structure

Campbell's argument is:
1. AUM's digital structure has a fundamental time quantum (smallest possible time increment).
2. By specifying a constant maximum rate at which information can propagate between space-time reality cells — expressed as one cell per time quantum — AUM defines both a time metric and a spatial metric simultaneously.
3. This constant propagation rate is what we observe as the speed of light.
4. The specific value of c was determined by evolutionary optimization (the Fundamental Process explored many values and stabilized on one that best served the learning environment for consciousness evolution).
