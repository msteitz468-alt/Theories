## Claim Statement

The universe operates as a giant holographic system. On every level of reality (including physical matter, biological cells, and spiritual planes), information about the entire whole is stored in every individual part. This holographic structure allows an observer to access universal knowledge instantly, bypassing spatial and temporal distance.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Bentov outlines the hologram as nature's storage in Chapter 1:
> "The chaotic-looking ice surface [interference pattern] is actually an information-storage device... This is the basic principle of the hologram. However... it is nature's way of storing information. There is already evidence that our brains store information in a holographic form."

He compares it to genetic codes:
> "This kind of storage device is the most compact known in Nature. An example of this is the genetic code carried in our chromosomes. Each cell in our bodies carries all the information required to make an additional copy of our bodies."

In the Introduction, he describes the universe as a hologram:
> "When taken together, all these realities form a large hologram of interacting fields in my model."

In Chapter 8, he explains how this allows instant access:
> "...because the universe is a hologram, any part of it contains information about the whole... By matching the frequencies of the mind to the universal hologram, we can access intuitive knowledge about the whole instantly."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim links Bentov's physics to his model of intuition. He explains that individuals do not need to physically travel to acquire knowledge of distant systems. Because the universe is holographic and interconnected, a quiet, meditative mind can align its vibrations with the cosmic hologram, retrieving details about any part of the cosmos from its own localized consciousness.
