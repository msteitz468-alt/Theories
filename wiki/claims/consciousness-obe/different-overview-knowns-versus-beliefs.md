## Claim Statement

Monroe holds that genuine knowledge of reality comes only from direct personal experience ("Knowns"), not from faith or doctrine ("Beliefs"), and that the out-of-body experience is "the most efficient means we know to gather Knowns to create a Different Overview" — a transformed perception of existence. The driving purpose is to convert fear-producing "Unknowns" into Knowns, because "fear is the great barrier to human growth."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Monroe defines "belief" as "a currently popular label for anything that cannot be fully understood or identified," and insists his Different Overview "can be at most only a belief until you begin to test it for validity within your own ongoing experience." The first and "perhaps most important" Known is **survival of physical death** — "not just hoping, having faith, or believing, but knowing." He recounts that his own 1958 onset of involuntary OBEs forced this revolution upon him: he fell "panic-stricken, into the process that engendered the new recognition of reality." He cites near-death experiences as producing "a genuine Different Overview" in others.
