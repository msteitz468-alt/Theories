## Claim Statement

Bentov asserts that the universe stores all information—including the accumulated experience of every consciousness—in a holographic interference pattern he calls the "universal mind." Because every element of a hologram contains the information of the whole, any sufficiently developed consciousness can access this repository by expanding its "observer" into what Bentov terms the inner torus (protospace) of the universe. A quiet, meditative, or high-consciousness mind can retrieve this knowledge; the sharpness of the information received scales with the individual's level of consciousness.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Holography as nature's storage mechanism (Ch. 1):** Bentov introduces the hologram as the foundational model for information storage in nature:

> "This is the basic principle of the hologram... it is nature's way of storing information. There is already evidence that our brains store information in a holographic form. This kind of storage device is the most compact known in Nature. An example of this is the genetic code carried in our chromosomes. Each cell in our bodies carries all the information required to make an additional copy of our bodies."

**All realities form a single hologram (Introduction):** Bentov states the scope of this storage explicitly:

> "When taken together, all these realities form a large hologram of interacting fields in my model."

**The universal mind as the information hologram (Ch. 8):** He describes how psyches expand into protospace and form an interference pattern with one another, against the background of the "absolute" as a reference beam — directly paralleling how a hologram is recorded with a reference and working beam:

> "For a tiny fraction of a second we form an information hologram with them, and this is repeated many times a second. The reference frequency against which all this interaction occurs is the absolute. Since all information that has been generated in this universe appears there, we can call that area the 'universal mind.'"

**Retrieval by a developed consciousness (Ch. 8):** Bentov asserts that access to this stored information is real and practically demonstrated in phenomena such as clairvoyance and clairaudience:

> "People having 'intuitive insights' get solutions from there, and people with high levels of consciousness, who are able to extend their subjective time, can learn and bring back useful information about the goings-on in the 'universal mind.' This communication appears to one as simply 'knowing.' Such a person just seems to know things that other people usually don't know."

**Quality of access scales with consciousness level (Ch. 8):** Drawing on holographic optics, Bentov explains that a larger illuminated area of a hologram yields a sharper image, and applies this to consciousness:

> "It is known from optics of holograms that although all the elements of the hologram contain complete information about the objects portrayed in the hologram, if we illuminate just a small area of the hologram, the image will appear fuzzy... while if we illuminate a large area of the same hologram, the image will appear much sharper. Therefore, persons with higher levels of consciousness will see past or future events more sharply than those occupying lower levels of consciousness."

**Summary statement (Ch. 8 summary):** Bentov encapsulates the claim:

> "This interference pattern or hologram of knowledge information we can call the 'universal mind.' The knowledge in the universal mind is open to anyone who can extend his stay there by stretching out his subjective time while there so as to gain useful information and decipher it upon his return."

**Psychic information preserved after death (Introduction):** Bentov extends the claim to include post-mortem continuity of information:

> "Nature... needs all this information, which is organized energy, and will not allow it to go to waste. It will be stored in Nature's large information-storage hologram: the universal mind."

**Living bodies as four-dimensional holograms (Ch. 10):** He further asserts that physical bodies themselves are holographic interference patterns:

> "We human beings consider ourselves to be made up of 'solid matter.' However, by now we know that we are just an interference pattern of waves that is changing with time. Or, in other words, we are a four-dimensional hologram. The base for the hologram is the void that permeates and connects all creation."

**Instant universal communication (Ch. 8):** Bentov claims information transfer within this hologram is instantaneous across any distance:

> "All these consciousnesses communicate with each other and make up part of the information hologram: Communication throughout the universe is continuous and instantaneous."

## Contexts Within the Source

The holographic storage claim is the cosmological backbone of Bentov's model. He uses it to explain clairvoyance, telepathy, precognition, and the intuitive insights of advanced meditators as legitimate information retrieval from a physically grounded repository rather than supernatural phenomena. The claim is introduced via the physics of holography in Ch. 1, formalised in the cosmological model of Ch. 8, and restated in Ch. 10 when discussing the nature of the Creator and the absolute.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Bentov acknowledges a theoretical obstacle: the theory of relativity states information cannot travel faster than light, yet his model requires faster-than-light propagation for the universal mind to be accessed instantaneously. He notes this as an open problem:

> "The theory of relativity states that information cannot travel faster than the velocity of light. I hope that this will eventually be shown not to be the case as ways are found to get around these limitations. Workers in the field of faster-than-light particles (tachyons) are presently grappling with these problems."
