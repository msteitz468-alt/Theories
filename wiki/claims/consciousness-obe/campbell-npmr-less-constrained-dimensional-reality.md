## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that NPMR (Nonphysical Matter Reality) is a dimension of AUM's consciousness that contains PMR as a subset, operates under fewer constraints than PMR, has its own causality and physics, hosts a far more varied set of life-forms than PMR, and can interact with PMR in ways that from PMR's perspective appear acausal or paranormal.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Campbell defines the relationship between NPMR and PMR structurally:

> "PMR and its causality is a subset of NPMR and its causality. The rules that govern NPMR physics and the interactions between NPMR beings are of a higher order (more general, less restrictive). Thus, NPMR can interact with PMR in ways that violate PMR's causality (such an interaction may produce paranormal activity from the viewpoint of PMR), yet maintain NPMR's own causality."

He identifies NPMR as containing PMR:

> "Visualize a reality dimension (like PMR) within a larger reality dimension (like NPMR) within a larger reality dimension (like AUM)."

On NPMR's lack of spatial constraints: "NPMR has no space and therefore no distance — it exists outside of PMR's space-time." Information propagation in NPMR is therefore "near instantaneous compared to poky light speed in PMR's space-time construct."

Campbell notes the biological diversity implication of reduced constraints:

> "It should now seem more obvious to you why the less constrained NPMRs should evolve a more varied set of life-forms than the more constrained PMRs."

He also notes that NPMR was AUM's first experimental environment for consciousness evolution — prior to PMRs: "Before there were PMRs, there were only NPMRs. As it turns out, NPMR realities are not optimized for evolving basic or elementary consciousness." He explains the limitation: in NPMR thought-space, "if a sentient entity does not like what is going on in NPMR thought-space, it can drop out and disappear or block out (filter) specific interactions. In NPMR, your external environment is, to a large extent, controlled directly by your mind."

This made NPMR a less effective learning lab for consciousness quality evolution than the more constrained, persistent PMR environment where choices carry real consequences.

Campbell also establishes a further level: "NPMR" in his system refers to the broader nonphysical dimension containing PMR, while "beyond-NPMR" or AUM itself represents yet higher-order levels with still fewer constraints.

## First-Person Evidence in Source

Campbell describes entering NPMR directly in his autobiographical Section 1: "I was spending almost as much time in NPMR as I was in physical-matter reality (PMR), and it showed." He describes learning to live in both simultaneously: "It was merely a matter of shifting and splitting my focus — I lived and was continuously aware, sentient, and conscious (except when sleeping) in both reality systems simultaneously and permanently."
