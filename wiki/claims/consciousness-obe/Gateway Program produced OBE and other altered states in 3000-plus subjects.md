## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that the Gateway Program — a Hemi-Sync-based residential training program — was administered to over 3,000 participants across more than 60,000 individual test exercises over approximately ten years. The program teaches Focus 10 (mind awake, body asleep) and Focus 12 (five physical senses shut off, non-physical perception accessible). Monroe claims the program consistently produced in participants: dissolution of the "Fear Barrier" regarding the unknown; peak experiences and life-changing insights; OBE states or altered-state perception; encounters with deceased friends and family; and encounters with "other forms of intelligent energy." Monroe states that "the individual, of his own accord, without suggestion but through direct experience, begins to know, rather than believe, that he does survive physical death."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Monroe provides demographic data in Chapter 3: 41% male (double the norm for self-awareness workshops, attributed to the scientific framing of Hemi-Sync); 29% professionals (psychologists, psychiatrists, educators, scientists, engineers); average age 39 (well above the age range of "faddist" groups); 83% attend for one reason and gain another more valuable result.

Monroe cites 60,000-plus individual test/exercises as his statistical base, noting: "At the very least, we know that we have been able to put people to sleep 60,000 times and awaken them. That is a pretty good statistic for product testing, especially one that is noninvasive in its physiological impact."

The chapter includes four extended participant testimonials. One (1644-CM) describes navigating focus levels beyond 21, reaching level 27 where he found his deceased father, then moving to "uncharted" territory at approximately level 100, encountering "many people milling around: They looked like holograms, but conveyed the message of being 'alive.'" Some approached him believing he could guide them back; some said they were "just exploring, and would return to their bodies when they felt like doing so, while others were waiting for their bodies to die, so they could be free."

Monroe describes the Gateway methodology as progressive desensitization: Focus 10 as a first guidepost in the "forest," Focus 12 as deeper but still reachable, with successively deeper guideposts established until "with the Fear Barrier dissolved, one of the greatest gifts to the human species comes into full play. Curiosity."

Monroe also describes a Gateway experiment in which 24 participants simultaneously projected mental energy approximately 1,000 feet above a darkened motel. Four outside observers saw soft, red, neon-like waves against the starlit sky at the exact timing specified by the exercise tape. A California replication (with no visible light observed by witnesses) produced two Polaroid photographs of "a round ball with a marbleized effect much like the earth seen from a distance" taken during the exercise window, with blank exposures before and after.

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe frames the Gateway Program primarily as a research tool rather than a commercial product: "The basic value to us of the Gateway Program — a test base of 60,000." Participants signed statements acknowledging the experimental nature and agreed to report results. There was no promotion; attendance was entirely by word of mouth from graduates.

Monroe emphasizes that OBE is not the typical outcome of a Gateway session: "Most of the time this expectation is not fulfilled during the session itself. Instead, they gain profound insights and understanding of the purposes behind their being and doing."
