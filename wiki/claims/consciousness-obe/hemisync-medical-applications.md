## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that the Hemi-Sync technology has demonstrated effectiveness across multiple medical and educational contexts, including: surgical pain control (reducing or eliminating need for anesthetic and post-operative pain medication); accelerated surgical recovery; terminal care (enabling a drug-dependent dying patient to spend his final week pain-free without medication); temporary symptom reduction in at least one schizophrenic patient; stress reduction in psychiatric patients; and significant learning enhancement across multiple populations. Monroe describes these as a "training series" called Emergency Treatment developed for serious illness, accident, and surgery contexts.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Monroe provides the following specific cases in Chapter 2:

- A kidney transplant patient on his sixteenth successive operation used the Emergency Treatment tape series. His surgeon nearly cancelled due to low but stable blood pressure. The patient called Monroe from his hospital room at 4 PM the same day as the surgery, sitting up in bed. Recovery time was cut in half compared to all previous operations; he subsequently became an advocate for the technique at a State Rehabilitation Agency.

- A psychologist who had been ill for two years and drug-addicted for pain management was treated with Hemi-Sync daily by a psychiatric associate. By the second week's Wednesday, he slept without pain or sleep medication for the first time in two years. He died months later but spent his final week "completely free of pain, without any medication, and had a pleasant and peaceful last few days with his family."

- A schizophrenic patient "lost many of the symptoms of his illness" under certain Hemi-Sync patterns, reverting to the psychotic condition when taken off the sound.

- An Air Force colonel being treated for stress denied any change, then mentioned he had taken his wife to dinner for the first time in six months and taken his son fishing as promised.

Educational results: 75% increase in mental-motor skills in a government training school; 30% improvement in Morse code learning; primary-grade pupils in Tacoma learned in four weeks what normally took a full semester.

Surgical applications: "A young woman had major abdominal surgery and was sky-diving one week later." A vascular surgeon used it with over thirty patients. A corporate president refused all post-operative pain or sleep medication and discharged himself three days after surgery.

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe acknowledges variability — "varying degrees of success and no failures whenever it has been properly used" — and emphasizes the biggest obstacle is "gaining the consent of the surgeon and the hospital personnel." He notes that an inquiry from the Federal Rehabilitation Facility at Hot Springs, Arkansas, for training at rehab centers in all fifty states never resulted in a follow-up, apparently because "it was too unorthodox to fit into a federal budget."

Monroe also acknowledges his personal inability to use the system for his own post-surgical pain: "I knew if I consciously went out of body, I would not have the courage to return to that sea of scalding pain."
