## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that in the non-physical state, thought and action are identical — there is no mechanical intermediary between intending movement and movement itself. The direction of travel is determined entirely by what one thinks about, including conscious intent and involuntary or unconscious thoughts; the person one thinks about becomes the destination, not any physical place.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Monroe states this principle as an empirical observation from the Second State:

> "Thought-action synchronicity. Whereas in the physical state action follows thought, here they are one and the same. There is no mechanical translation of thought into action. One gradually appreciates the existence of thought as a force in itself rather than as a trigger or catalyst. It is primarily an emotional thought force, which is gradually molded into coherent action. It is the thought of movement that creates the action. It is the thought of the person to be visited that determines the destination."

For Locale II specifically, Monroe states that "you think movement, and it is fact" and that "'Mere' thought is the force that supplies any need or desire, and what you think is the matrix of your action, situation, and position in this greater reality."

In the navigation instructions for Locale I visits, Monroe emphasizes that one must think of a person, not a place: "you 'think' of the person at the end of your destination — never a place, but a person — and use the method prescribed. In a few moments, you are there." The mechanism he describes is "a built-in directional sense" that acts "by what and of whom you think."

The practical problem Monroe identifies is that **any stray thought instantly diverts travel**: "Let one small stray thought emerge dominantly for just one microsecond, and your course is deviated." He describes this as "the chief barrier to the production of consistency and repeatability."

Monroe's separation technique instructions state explicitly: "you must 'go' to a person, not to a place. It may be possible to achieve the latter if you have a deep emotional attachment to the locale, but the experiments to date have shown little success along this line."

In Locale II, the principle extends beyond conscious thought to deep emotion: location in Locale II is determined by one's "innermost constant motivations, emotions, and desires" — even those below conscious awareness.

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe frames the thought-as-movement principle as the operational foundation for learning OBE travel: once the vibrational state is achieved and the Second Body separates, "the technique of separation evolved into a simple uncluttered thought of 'up' or 'away'." He cautions that "any non-physical movement in the Second Body was instigated by desire or thought alone." This makes thought control a critical prerequisite skill.
