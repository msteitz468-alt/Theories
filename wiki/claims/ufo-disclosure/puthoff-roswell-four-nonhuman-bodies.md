## Claim Statement

Elizondo states that Harold "Hal" Puthoff — physicist, co-founder of AAWSAP, and former CIA Project Stargate contractor — told him in classified SCIF briefings that the Roswell, New Mexico, 1947 crash was real and that four nonhuman bodies were recovered from the crash site.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 4, Elizondo describes classified briefings conducted within AATIP's SCIF setting. He states that Puthoff, drawing on his decades of involvement with the US government's classified UAP and consciousness research programs, confirmed that Roswell involved a genuine nonhuman craft and that four nonhuman bodies were recovered. This is presented as Puthoff's direct assertion to Elizondo rather than as a secondhand claim.

Elizondo also notes that in Chapter 9, Puthoff separately offered an EMP-based theory for the Roswell crash mechanism — the craft's electromagnetic propulsion system interacted with US military radar, causing a systems failure that resulted in the crash.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo presents Puthoff as one of the most credentialed and connected insiders in the classified UAP world — with simultaneous ties to the CIA (Project Stargate), DIA (AAWSAP), and private research (Skinwalker Ranch, TTSA). Elizondo treats Puthoff's confirmation of Roswell as significant precisely because of Puthoff's institutional position, not as rumor but as classified-source disclosure within a cleared setting.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The number "four" bodies differs from Cooper's claimed count (65 bodies total from multiple crashes, 1947–1952) and the Jacobsen source's claim (child-sized bodies from a Stalin/Mengele aviation experiment). Elizondo does not address these discrepancies.
