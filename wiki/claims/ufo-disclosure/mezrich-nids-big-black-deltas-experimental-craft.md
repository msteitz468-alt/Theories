## Claim Statement

Robert Bigelow's [[National Institute for Discovery Science (NIDS)]] concluded that a frequently reported UFO type — the **"Big Black Delta,"** a large black triangle that hovers, maneuvers sharply, and accelerates — was **most likely an experimental US Air Force lighter-than-air craft** (a blimp powered by electrokinetic engines under development since the early 1980s).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mezrich: NIDS "believed that his team had identified the source of at least one of the more frequent types of sightings: Big Black Deltas ... Using sophisticated mapping techniques, NIDS scientists had shown a correlation between the sightings and the locations of known air force bases that were conducting research into 'lighter-than-air' craft." Employing "an aircraft design specialist, and using military sources, the NIDS had concluded that the Deltas were most likely experimental blimps, powered by electrokinetic engines."

The book frames NIDS's aim as turning "UFOs into IFOs — Identified Flying Objects," and presents this as "a clear success for the NIDS's methodology," though it "barely edged into the mainstream media." NIDS deputy administrator Colm Kelleher is quoted on the field's "aura of ridicule."

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the book's principal example of a **mundane resolution** of a UFO category — a counterweight to the unexplained cattle-mutilation and Skinwalker material that NIDS could not resolve.
