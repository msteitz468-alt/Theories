## Claim Statement

By the book's end Zukowski reinterprets the 37th parallel not merely as a corridor of coincidental sightings but as **"a runway, not a highway"** — a single connecting thread implying a sustained extraterrestrial (or otherwise non-human) presence operating across the UFO sightings, animal mutilations, Roswell, and Native American sacred grounds.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In the deliberately fragmentary climactic chapter — Zukowski returning alone to the Dulce-area site of his 2000 sighting, encountering a fresh high-temperature burn area, a dead elk "head turned up toward the sky," and curved metallic material — Mezrich writes: "His 37th parallel was real — not a highway, but a runway. sightings, lights in the sky, Roswell, the animal mutilations, the Native American sacred grounds—." The passage gestures at "**Bigelow Aerospace**" and leaves the ultimate agency unresolved; a CIA contact had earlier dismissed Zukowski as "not a threat, just another UFO nut ... part of a cottage industry of belief aimed at believers."

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the "secret truth" promised by the subtitle. Mezrich renders it ambiguously — neither fully confirming an extraterrestrial conclusion nor retreating from it — closing on Zukowski's choice whether to "turn around and head back ... to his life, and his research."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The chapter's broken syntax, the unresolved metallic object, the gesture toward a terrestrial actor (Bigelow Aerospace / a "secretive or military project"), and the CIA debunking all keep the conclusion **open** rather than asserting a proven extraterrestrial fact.
