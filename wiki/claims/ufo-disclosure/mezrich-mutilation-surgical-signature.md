## Claim Statement

Animal mutilations across the western US exhibit a consistent and anomalous signature that, per the book, cannot be explained by ordinary predators: carcasses are **drained of blood**, organs and soft tissue are removed by **precise, apparently cauterized "surgical" incisions**, and there are **no tracks, struggle, or scavenger signs**.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

At Glenda's ranch (Rush, CO, 2010), Zukowski finds two horses "stripped to the bone in various places," with eyes "carefully removed," organs "excised," and tongues "taken — cleanly, via perfectly straight incisions, far back in the throat." The animals "had been drained of blood ... For whatever reason, this horse had been exsanguinated." He notes "No signs of cauterization at all" by burn yet wounds that are otherwise "precise, small, and ... seemingly surgical," and "No footprints, tire tracks, or signs of violence."

In the Duran cow case (Criminal Case #09-00155), the report records "reproductive organs and udder removed via circular cuts, with no blood or signs of physical trauma. The wounds seemed cauterized, and the closest cow prints ended four feet away ... No noticeable vehicle tire prints, no signs of scavengers." The necropsy vet found "the animal's heart had collapsed, telling her that the blood had been drained," with no blood at the site — "Where had the blood gone?"

Zukowski's expert judgment: "the scene ... wasn't the result of a predator. No animal could have caused these bizarre wounds. Nor did Chuck see any evidence that pointed to human culpability."

## Contexts Within the Source

Presented as the empirical core of Zukowski's two decades of fieldwork; the consistency of the signature is what convinces him a single unexplained phenomenon is at work.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Investigating officers in the Glenda case successively proposed "predators" and then a "lightning strike" — which Glenda rejects ("Lightning had cut out my horses' tongues and left bloodless corpses behind?"). (The book does not foreground the mainstream blowfly/scavenger and decomposition explanations of the 1980 Rommel report.)
