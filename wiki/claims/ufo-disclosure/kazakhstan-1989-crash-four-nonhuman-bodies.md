## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that in 1989, a large Tic Tac-shaped nonhuman craft crashed in Kazakhstan (then part of the Soviet Union), and that four nonhuman bodies were recovered from the wreckage. He states that information about this event reached US intelligence channels.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 11, Elizondo lists the Kazakhstan 1989 event as a documented crash-retrieval case in AATIP's holdings. He specifies the craft type as a large Tic Tac — consistent with the shape described in the 2004 Nimitz encounter — and states that four nonhuman bodies were recovered. The specific number matches the body count from Puthoff's account of the Roswell recovery.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo presents this event as part of the multivariate crash-retrieval record across multiple decades and multiple nations, reinforcing the claim that the phenomenon is global and has been ongoing. He does not specify the US intelligence pathway by which knowledge of a Soviet-territory crash was acquired.
