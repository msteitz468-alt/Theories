## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that US Air Force Staff Sergeant Jim Burroughs, one of the military witnesses to the December 1980 Rendlesham Forest UAP incident at RAF Bentwaters/Woodbridge, suffered permanent cardiac damage as a documented result of his proximity to the UAP. Elizondo presents Burroughs's injury as a concrete biological effect case in AATIP's evidentiary record.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 4, Rendlesham Forest is listed among the three key UAP-nuclear interference incidents, as the base at Bentwaters housed US tactical nuclear weapons at the time. In Chapter 10, Elizondo specifically cites Burroughs's cardiac damage as a confirmed, documented biological effect — one of the AATIP case histories used to establish the sixth (unofficial) observable. Elizondo notes that Burroughs's medical records and the congressional intervention required to obtain VA treatment for his injury are part of the public record.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo frames Rendlesham as triply significant: it is a UAP-nuclear interface event, a high-credibility multi-witness military case, and a documented biological effects case. Burroughs's cardiac damage, he argues, is not consistent with psychological stress or conventional illness — its onset, timing, and nature are consistent with exposure to unusual electromagnetic radiation fields.
