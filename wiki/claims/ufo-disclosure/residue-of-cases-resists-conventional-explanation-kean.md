## Claim Statement

Kean's central evidentiary claim: after the great majority of sightings are explained away, a small residue of rigorously investigated cases remains genuinely unexplained — physically real yet beyond identification — and intellectual honesty requires treating this residue as an open scientific question rather than dismissing it. She keeps "the phenomenon" carefully distinct from the extraterrestrial hypothesis.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

She frames the Belgian objects as "something physically, technologically real, yet completely unknown to any of us," argues a secret terrestrial craft of such capability is implausible (it "would have revolutionized both air travel and modern warfare… and probably physics"), and reports the French **COMETA Report** (1999) — authored by retired generals and senior officials (General Bernard Norlain, General Denis Letty, Jean-Jacques Velasco of SEPRA/GEPAN, et al.) — which concluded the extraterrestrial hypothesis was "the most logical explanation" for the hardest cases, while she herself withholds that conclusion.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Kean explicitly declines to endorse the ET hypothesis, presents COMETA's stronger conclusion as the view of others, and stresses that the unexplained residue is precisely what warrants investigation, not a settled answer.
