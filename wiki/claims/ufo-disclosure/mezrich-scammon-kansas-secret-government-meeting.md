## Claim Statement

While investigating a reported house-sized orange UFO at Scammon, Kansas, MUFON/BAASS Star Team leader Debbie Ziegelmeyer found that local police denied any report existed and that a **caravan of federally-plated SUVs together with county and state police convened secretly** at a closed Mexican restaurant — which the book frames as a government cover-up operation tied to the sighting.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

At the sheriff's office an officer "walks out rather than discuss the 'sky lighter,'" and the receptionist insists, "There isn't any report from that night," though the witness had described the craft "Big as a house." Returning to her motel, Debbie finds a car that "was black, big — and had government plates," its windows tinted. Pursued from a farmhouse by a tinted-window pickup, she then sees "four more cars coming toward her ... three of them black SUVS, one a dark blue sedan ... All of them had federal designations."

The caravan pulls into "the parking lot of a Mexican restaurant," joined by "even more cars ... Some of them ... unmarked like the SUVs ... others were cop cars, county and state," including the earlier officer's car. Inside, "At least twenty people were seated ... some of them in suits and ties, a few in police uniform," around a man "half out of his seat" wearing "obvious FBI-black pants, white shirt, and black shoes" with "a walkie-talkie ... chirping at his belt," holding open a file. He orders Debbie out — "Restaurant is closed" — and a deputy adds "The bathroom is closed, too." The source's chapter summary frames the subsequent six-hour tail to "within three miles of her home" as "a clear warning to end her investigation."

## Contexts Within the Source

Mezrich presents the meeting as physical evidence for Zukowski's belief "that strange vehicles had been shadowing him," and Chuck reads it as proof "a military detachment cleaning up a debris field" was at work — "Something is going down here."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The book preserves a skeptical register. Debbie first allows the motel car "Could be a coincidence," and the text concedes that with this work "you started to see things behind every rock and tree." Bigelow's own agents were supposed to meet her in Scammon; she reflects "for all we know some of these guys are Bigelow," leaving the convened group's identity unresolved.
