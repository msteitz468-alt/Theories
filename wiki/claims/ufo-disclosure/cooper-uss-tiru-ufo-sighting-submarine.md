**Claim Statement**

While serving as port lookout aboard the submarine USS Tiru (SS-416) during a transit from the Portland-Seattle area to Pearl Harbor, William Cooper witnessed a large disk-shaped metallic craft emerge from the ocean multiple times over approximately ten minutes. The sighting was corroborated by at least three other witnesses. Sonar confirmed an underwater contact at the same bearing. The captain personally took the conn and had photographs taken. An Office of Naval Intelligence (ONI) commander subsequently boarded at Pearl Harbor, interrogated all witnesses under threat of imprisonment, and required them to sign non-disclosure agreements.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**

Cooper describes the sighting in the Introduction to *Behold a Pale Horse*. He was on the 1200–1600 watch as port lookout; Ensign Ball was Officer of the Deck (OOD); "Geronimo" was starboard lookout. Cooper's initial observation:

> "I turned, raising my binoculars to my eyes just in time to see a huge disk rise from beneath the ocean, water streaming from the air around it, tumble lazily on its axis, and disappear into the clouds."

He states he initially withheld the report, then reported a vague sighting to Ensign Ball. Before Ball could respond:

> "The giant saucer shape plunged out of the clouds, tumbled and, pushing the water before it, opened up a hole in the ocean and disappeared from view."

This second sighting was witnessed naked-eye by Cooper, and Ensign Ball confirmed it with binoculars. The OOD called the captain to the bridge. Chief Quartermaster Quintero brought the ship's 35mm camera. Cooper describes three observations total. His physical description: "It was a metal machine, of that there was no doubt whatsoever. It was intelligently controlled, of that I was equally sure. It was a dull color, kind of like pewter. There were no lights. There was no glow." He believed he saw a row of portholes but was not certain. Radar reported contact at the same bearing, range 3 nautical miles.

After returning to Pearl Harbor, all witnesses were confined to the ship. An unnamed commander from the Office of Naval Intelligence (ONI) boarded and interrogated each witness separately. During Cooper's interrogation, the ONI commander "began to visibly shake" when Cooper said "I believe it was a flying saucer, sir," and threatened him with imprisonment. Cooper states he was shown laws and regulations governing classified information and signed an agreement never to communicate "in any manner any information regarding the incident with anyone."

**Contexts Within the Source**

Cooper presents this as the first of several formative incidents that convinced him the U.S. government conceals UFO reality. He connects it to earlier barracks stories from Air Force sergeants (whom he then disbelieved) about recovering crashed saucers, establishing a cumulative personal experience of encounters and cover-up. The sighting is presented as a matter-of-fact memory, not as speculation.

Cooper explicitly notes he did not report the sighting honestly at first ("I was torn between my duty to report what I had seen and the knowledge that if I did, no one would believe me"), and that the ONI interrogation confirmed to him that the cover-up was institutional.

**Opposing Information Within the Source**

None. Cooper does not present alternative explanations or qualify the identification.
