## Claim Statement

Elizondo states that Puthoff offered a specific hypothesis for why UAP are consistently observed in the vicinity of nuclear weapons facilities and nuclear test sites: NHI may be monitoring humanity's approach toward the energy levels required for warp bubble propulsion. In this interpretation, UAP nuclear site monitoring is not primarily a military or weapons concern but a technological threshold concern — NHI track whether humanity is developing the energy capacity to become an interstellar civilization.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 16, after presenting the warp bubble unified theory, Puthoff addresses the UAP-nuclear correlation. He proposes that nuclear reactions (particularly weapons-grade fission and fusion events) produce energy densities in the terahertz range relevant to warp bubble formation — the same energy range he identified as required for warp propulsion. UAP are thus watching nuclear sites not because of the weapons themselves but because nuclear technology is the closest humanity has come to the energy threshold for warp-capable technology.

Elizondo uses the metaphor of "a gorilla with a shotgun" to describe the NHI threat assessment problem: a gorilla is not deliberately threatening in intent, but the combination of limited understanding and devastating capability makes it dangerous. The question is whether NHI view humanity's nuclear development the same way.

## Contexts Within the Source

This hypothesis is presented as a theoretical framework within AATIP, not as a confirmed NHI communication. Elizondo uses it as one of three possible NHI intent scenarios — benevolent (monitoring to protect), malevolent (monitoring to control), or neutral (monitoring with scientific curiosity).
