## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that during pre-deployment workup exercises conducted by USS Theodore Roosevelt Carrier Strike Group along the US East Coast (Virginia to Florida) in 2014 and 2015, Navy personnel documented 22 separate UAP encounters. Objects were confirmed on upgraded radar systems and visually observed by multiple pilots.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 15, Elizondo describes the Roosevelt UAP encounters as a sustained series — 22 documented events — spanning the ship's pre-deployment training period. Key details:

- Objects were initially detected on newly upgraded AN/SPY-1 radar systems; the upgrades made the objects detectable where older equipment could not
- Multiple pilots visually confirmed the objects, which were also tracked on radar, eliminating the possibility of sensor artifact
- A **transparent orb with an internal cube** visible inside split a formation of fighter aircraft — the object physically occupied the formation's flight path
- The **GoFast video** (approximately 18-foot object at low altitude, moving with unusual velocity) was captured during this period
- The **GIMBAL video** (fleet of five lenticular craft, one rotating in a 120-knot wind without aerodynamic disruption) was captured during this period
- Elizondo describes a proposal called **OPLAN Interloper** — using a nuclear-capable asset as bait to lure UAP to a controllable engagement zone — that was developed in response to the Roosevelt encounters

## Contexts Within the Source

The Roosevelt encounters provide the evidentiary basis for the GoFast and GIMBAL videos subsequently declassified by Elizondo via Form 1910. Unlike the Nimitz encounter (one confirmed visual engagement), the Roosevelt series represents 22 documented events with consistent characteristics over an extended period, strengthening the pattern established by the Five Observables framework.
