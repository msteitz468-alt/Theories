## Claim Statement

Elizondo states that the formal Five Observables framework was extended internally by AATIP to include a sixth observable — biological effects on human witnesses — but that this sixth was never formally codified because doing so would have compounded the stigma surrounding UAP research within the government. He presents the sixth observable as real, documented, and supported by both case records and Dr. Garry Nolan's neurological research.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 12, after enumerating the Five Observables, Elizondo identifies biological effects as a sixth unofficial observable: radiation burns, unexplained cancers, neurological changes, implants, altered brain anatomy (caudate-putamen), and in extreme cases death (Colares). He notes the deliberate decision not to formalize this sixth observable in official program documentation to protect the program from further ridicule.

In Chapter 10, the case histories used to establish biological effects as a genuine sixth observable include:
- Cash-Landrum Texas 1980 — diamond-shaped UAP, three civilians with acute radiation exposure
- Jim Burroughs — Rendlesham Forest 1980, permanent cardiac damage
- Davis, California, 2007 — blue orbs, breast cancer cluster among witnesses
- 105 UAP experiencers studied by Nolan — uniformly enlarged caudate-putamen

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo frames the omission of the sixth observable from formal documentation as a calculated strategic decision, not a scientific judgment. He was confident the evidence supported it but believed that including "human bodies harmed by UAP" in formal program documents would invite institutional shutdown.
