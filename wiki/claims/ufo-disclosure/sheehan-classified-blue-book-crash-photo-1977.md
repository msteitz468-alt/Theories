## Claim Statement

Attorney Daniel Sheehan told Coulthart that in 1977, while assisting a Congressional Research Service inquiry for President Carter, he was allowed to view classified Project Blue Book microfiche and saw photographs of a crashed domed disc in a snowy field with etched symbols, one of which he traced onto his notepad.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sheehan recounts the cloak-and-dagger access, the ban on note-taking, and his claim that the CIA Director (George H. W. Bush) had earlier refused President-elect Carter a UAP briefing on 'need to know' grounds. Coulthart notes Sheehan has told this unchallenged for decades and that Carter/Marcia Smith never denied it.

## Contexts Within the Source

Central to the chapter's argument that even a US president was denied UAP information by the CIA.
