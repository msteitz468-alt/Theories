## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that UAP incidents in and around Colares, Brazil, between 1977 and 1978 resulted in approximately 10 deaths and biological injuries to more than 300 people across 371 documented locations. The Brazilian Air Force conducted an official investigation. Lt. Col. Hollanda, who led the investigation, later gave detailed testimony about his findings before dying under disputed circumstances.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 2, Elizondo describes a dinner attended by himself, General Paulo Roberto Uchôa (Brazilian Air Force, ret.), Robert Bigelow, and Hal Puthoff. General Uchôa briefed the group on the scope of the Brazilian Air Force investigation — designated Operation Prato (Operation Saucer). Key figures cited:

- **Lt. Col. Hollanda** led the investigation and documented approximately 371 incident locations, biological effects on more than 300 civilians (burns, puncture wounds, blood drawn from witnesses by light beams), and approximately 10 deaths attributed to UAP interactions
- Witnesses described bright beams of light emanating from craft, striking people, and causing radiation-like burns
- Hollanda died shortly after giving a lengthy interview about his investigation findings; Brazilian investigators considered the timing suspicious

Elizondo presents Colares as the most severe mass-casualty UAP biological effects event in AATIP's international case files.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo uses the Colares case to establish that UAP biological effects (the sixth observable) are not rare, individual, or psychosomatic. The Brazilian investigation provides an official government record of mass UAP-induced injury and death, corroborating AATIP's domestic case files on biological effects from Chapter 10.
