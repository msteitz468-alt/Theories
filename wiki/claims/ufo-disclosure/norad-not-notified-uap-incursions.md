## Claim Statement

Christopher Mellon, in his foreword to *Imminent*, asserts that during the period of AATIP's operation, neither NORAD (North American Aerospace Defense Command) nor US Strategic Command was being systematically informed of UAP incursions into controlled US airspace. Individual military units reported encounters through their own chains of command, but no unified reporting structure existed that would bring the data to the organizations responsible for air defense.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mellon's foreword states explicitly that NORAD and Strategic Command were not receiving notification of UAP events that US military personnel were documenting. This structural gap meant that even if individual carrier strike groups (like the Nimitz and Roosevelt groups) filed reports, those reports did not flow to the commands responsible for defending US airspace against intrusions.

## Contexts Within the Source

Mellon frames this as the central national security failure — not that UAP are hostile, but that the government had no mechanism to evaluate whether they were. The absence of unified reporting meant that 22 Roosevelt encounters, 100-plus Nimitz radar contacts, and numerous other military reports were siloed within individual commands without triggering the air defense response that an unknown intrusion of US military operating areas would normally produce.
