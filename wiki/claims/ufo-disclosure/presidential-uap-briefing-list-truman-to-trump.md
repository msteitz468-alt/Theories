## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that a presidential briefing list on UAP — compiled through AATIP research and congressional engagement — identifies which US presidents were briefed on UAP reality and which were not. The list reveals that UAP information was withheld from several presidents.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 23, Elizondo identifies the presidents who received UAP briefings:

**Briefed**: Harry Truman, Dwight Eisenhower, John F. Kennedy, Lyndon B. Johnson, Jimmy Carter, Ronald Reagan, George H.W. Bush, Donald Trump

**Not fully briefed**: Gerald Ford, Richard Nixon

The Reagan information is corroborated elsewhere in the source by Reagan's 1987 United Nations General Assembly speech in which Reagan speculated about a hypothetical alien threat uniting humanity — which Elizondo interprets as an informed reference rather than idle speculation.

Elizondo notes that Carter filed a formal UAP sighting report as governor of Georgia (1969) and specifically requested a UAP briefing as president but was denied by CIA Director Stansfield Turner.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo presents this list as evidence that UAP knowledge was controlled not by the Office of the President but by a separate institutional structure — implying that the presidency itself does not automatically confer access to the most sensitive UAP information. The omission of Nixon and Ford is presented as particularly striking given Nixon's tenure through the height of Cold War UAP reporting.
