## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that David Grusch — a National Reconnaissance Office officer and former NGA representative to the UAP Task Force — testified under oath before Congress in July 2023, stating that the UAP Task Force was refused access to a crash-retrieval program involving nonhuman origin vehicles, and that nonhuman bodies had been recovered. Grusch testified alongside Cmdr. Dave Fravor (USS Nimitz Tic Tac witness) and Lt. Ryan Graves.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 24, Elizondo quotes Grusch's congressional testimony directly:

> "The UAP Task Force was refused access to a broad crash retrieval program, retrieving nonhuman origin technical vehicles, you know, call it spacecraft, if you will, nonhuman exotic origin vehicles that have either landed or crashed. . . . There's a sophisticated disinformation campaign targeting the US populace, which is extremely unethical and immoral."

Elizondo notes that Grusch had previously filed complaints with the Inspector General, which was the mechanism enabling him to go public as a protected whistleblower. The hearing was conducted by the House Oversight Committee.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo frames Grusch's testimony as the culmination of the whistleblower protection legislation he had advocated for, demonstrating that the statutory mechanisms created by the NDAA FY2023 UAP provisions enabled exactly the kind of official, on-record disclosure he had been working toward since 2017. He describes Fravor, Graves, and Grusch as "heroes."
