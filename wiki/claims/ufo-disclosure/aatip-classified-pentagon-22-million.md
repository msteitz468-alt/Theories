## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that AATIP (Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program) was a real, classified US government UAP investigation program housed inside the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence (OUSD(I)) at the Pentagon. Its predecessor, AAWSAP (Advanced Aerospace Weapons System Applications Program), was funded by the Defense Intelligence Agency with $22 million appropriated through the efforts of Senators Harry Reid (Nevada), Ted Stevens (Alaska), and Daniel Inouye (Hawaii).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Christopher Mellon's foreword references the $22 million appropriation for AAWSAP. Elizondo describes being recruited into the program in early 2009 by Jay Stratton and a colleague he calls "Rosemary Caine," and meeting program director Jim Lacatski. Elizondo ran AATIP from a Pentagon office, room 3C503A. He describes his role as director of the program and states that he briefed findings to senior DoD officials throughout his tenure (2009–2017).

Senator Harry Reid's official request to place AATIP under Special Access Program (SAP) protection is referenced in the appendix and entered into the public record. The DoD confirmed Elizondo's AATIP role through spokesperson Dana White in 2017, before later reversing that position under spokesperson Christopher Sherwood.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo frames AATIP as operating within normal DoD intelligence structures — using SCIF facilities, standard classification protocols, and formal briefing processes — not as a rogue or informal effort. He commissioned 38 Defense Intelligence Reference Documents (DIRDs) from academic scientists through AAWSAP. The program investigated UAP cases worldwide including the Nimitz encounter, the USS Roosevelt encounters, and foreign incidents.
