**Claim Statement**

Cooper asserts that a live alien recovered from the 1949 Roswell crash site was designated EBE (Extraterrestrial Biological Entity), a name suggested by Dr. Vannevar Bush. EBE was chlorophyll-based, processed food into energy like a plant, and excreted waste in the same manner. EBE spent over a year giving only desired answers to questions, then began to open up. His revelations became the "Yellow Book." EBE died June 2, 1952. Cooper asserts the film *E.T.* is "the thinly disguised story of EBE."

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**

"The live alien that had been found wandering in the desert from the 1949 Roswell crash was named EBE. The name had been suggested by Dr. Vannevar Bush and was short for Extraterrestrial Biological Entity."

On behaviour: "EBE had a tendency to lie, and for over a year would give only the desired answer to questions asked. Those questions which would have resulted in an undesirable answer went unanswered."

On biology: "EBE's system was chlorophyll-based and he processed food into energy much the same as plants. Waste material was excreted the same as plants."

On illness and death: "In late 1951 EBE became ill. Medical personnel had been unable to determine the cause of EBE's illness and had no background from which to draw... A botanist, Dr. Guillermo Mendoza, was brought in to try and help him recover. Dr. Mendoza worked to save EBE until June 2, 1952, when EBE died."

On Project SIGMA: "In a futile attempt to save EBE and to gain favor with this technologically superior race, the United States began broadcasting a call for help early in 1952 into the vast regions of space."

On *E.T.*: "Dr. Mendoza became the expert on at least this type alien biology. The movie E.T. is the thinly disguised story of EBE."
