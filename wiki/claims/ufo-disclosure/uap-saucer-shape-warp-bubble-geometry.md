## Claim Statement

Elizondo states that Puthoff's warp bubble theory predicts specific craft geometries as mathematical consequences of the bubble topology, explaining why the three most commonly reported UAP shapes (disc/saucer, cigar, triangle) appear precisely as observed.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 16, Puthoff presents the geometric predictions:

- **Disc/saucer shape**: A single spherical warp bubble, when flattened to generate the directional propulsion axis perpendicular to the disc plane, produces an oblate spheroid — the classic "flying saucer" silhouette
- **Cigar shape**: Two overlapping or conjoined spherical warp bubbles produce an elongated ellipsoid — the cigar or cylinder shape reported in many cases
- **Triangle shape**: Three overlapping warp bubbles arranged in a triangular array produce the triangular silhouette reported in large, slow-moving UAP ("TR-3B"-type) cases

Puthoff's model makes these shape predictions independently of observed UAP reports — they emerge from the mathematics of warp bubble topology, not from fitting the theory to existing sighting data.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo presents this predictive power as evidence in favor of the warp bubble model. The fact that Alcubierre-derived warp bubble geometry independently generates the three dominant UAP shapes is treated as significant corroborating correspondence between theory and observed phenomenology.
