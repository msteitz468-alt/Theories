## Claim Statement

Mutilated animals and their immediate surroundings register **abnormally high electromagnetic-field (EMF) readings** — far above natural background — which Zukowski treats as tangible physical evidence that "something truly unnatural had occurred."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Using a $400 **Trifield EMF reader** (set to filter out baseline natural and man-made fields), Zukowski scans the horse Princess at Glenda's ranch: "the electromagnetic levels jumped, starting around eight microteslas at the bottom ribs, rising past ten microteslas as Chuck got closer to the skull." In a related case the reader over a traumatized dog's lacerations "spiked over one hundred microteslas. Twice the magnetic field of the Earth. Chuck had run the EMF meter over dogs before, never recording anything above six microteslas."

The book also repeatedly reports **drained/dead camera and EMF batteries** in proximity to the lights at Dulce ("all three batteries were now, inexplicably, completely drained"), which Zukowski reads as part of the same energetic anomaly.

## Contexts Within the Source

The EMF data is Zukowski's answer to the demand for "real, tangible evidence"; he contrasts it with MUFON's witness-collection approach and with the lab analysis he could not afford.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The book presents these readings as Zukowski's interpretation; it does not report independent replication, and notes elsewhere that even NIDS's well-funded instrumentation "had not yet picked up anything conclusive."
