## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that a classified program he calls the "Legacy Program" — operating within and contracted to major US defense and aerospace corporations — has been in possession of nonhuman technology and biological materials since at least the late 1940s, funded by US taxpayers without Congressional oversight or awareness.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapters 4, 11, and 13, Elizondo names the Legacy Program contractors as:

- **Lockheed Martin**
- **TRW** (TRW crash-retrieval program at Wright-Patterson AFB, per Chapter 11)
- **McDonnell Douglas**
- **Northrop Grumman**
- **Boeing**
- **Raytheon**
- **BAE Systems**
- **Aerospace Corporation** (Eric Davis is identified as a current employee; Davis is quoted saying "Off-world vehicles not made on this earth")
- **Monsanto** (biological samples)

In Chapter 13, Elizondo states that a Legacy Program contractor directly acknowledged to AATIP analysts that they held UAP materials, but stated that release would require permission from the Secretary of the Air Force — permission that was never granted. Elizondo describes the Air Force as "part of the cover-up."

The Wilson/Davis memo (Chapter 4 and Appendix) is cited as contemporaneous documentation of a 2002 meeting in which a Legacy Program contractor confirmed to astrophysicist Eric Davis that a crash-retrieval and reverse-engineering program was underway within a Special Access Program inaccessible even to the sitting Deputy Director of the DIA (Admiral Thomas Wilson).

In Chapter 24, David Grusch (NRO/NGA, UAPTF representative) publicly testified under oath in July 2023 that the UAPTF was refused access to "a broad crash retrieval program, retrieving nonhuman origin technical vehicles."

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo frames the Legacy Program as having developed from the original post-Roswell response (1947 onward) and as having migrated from direct government control into contractor custody specifically to evade Congressional oversight, Inspector General review, and FOIA requests — all of which apply to government agencies but not to private contractors operating under classified contracts.

He also notes that in Chapter 24, the UAP Disclosure Act (signed December 2023) was specifically designed to give the US government eminent domain authority over Legacy Program materials held by contractors.
