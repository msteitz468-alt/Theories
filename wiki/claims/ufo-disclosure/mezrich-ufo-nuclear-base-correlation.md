## Claim Statement

At the National Atomic Museum, Zukowski argues that **UFO sightings correlate with nuclear bases** — a relationship he calls "well documented" — and that the Manhattan Project proves the U.S. government is capable of keeping enormous secrets (including, by implication, about UFOs).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mezrich writes: "The relationship between UFO sightings and nuclear bases was well documented. Chuck had an entire section of his file cabinet dedicated to sightings near bases." His leading example is "the famous story of craft spotted and tracked by radar over Malmstrom Air Force Base in Montana in 1967, witnessed by base security officers and nuclear engineers, and the unexplained, sudden shutdown of at least ten ICBM missiles housed in silos beneath the UFO activity." He adds that Los Alamos, "where the atomic bombs were first designed, had sightings going back ... to 1948 ... reportedly documented in FBI files, since liberally redacted and designated as top secret," some pages "mostly black ink."

The chapter ties this to the secrecy theme: the **Manhattan Project** "involved more than 130,000 people" yet "maybe two dozen had actually known the details," and the Office of Censorship even "took words out of the English language" ("atomic," "nuclear"). Chuck's inference: "If a government that wasn't very good at keeping secrets had been able to keep such a mammoth undertaking covert ... who knew what else it was capable of?" — and "a year later, Roswell happened," near the base that housed the atomic-bomb B-29s.

## Contexts Within the Source

The correlation supports the book's broader cover-up thesis: that demonstrable government secrecy (the Manhattan Project) makes a UFO cover-up plausible. Two interpretations are offered — that benevolent visitors "watch over the developing nuclear program," or that "nuclear weapons were some sort of calling card to UFOs."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Mezrich voices the skeptic directly: "nuclear weapons, developed in secret, would be particularly interesting to foreign governments ... Observation craft, or even primitive drones and the U-2s designed at Area 51, would certainly appear like UFOs." Chuck concedes the benevolent-alien reading "seemed a mix of hubris and science fiction," admitting "there was no arguing with the fact that cover-ups did exist" only as the secure ground.
