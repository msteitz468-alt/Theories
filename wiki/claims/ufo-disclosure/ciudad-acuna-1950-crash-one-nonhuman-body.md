## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that in December 1950, a nonhuman craft crashed near Ciudad Acuña, Coahuila, Mexico (directly across the US–Mexico border from Del Rio, Texas), and that one nonhuman body was recovered from the crash site by US forces.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 11, Elizondo lists the Ciudad Acuña, Mexico, December 1950 crash as one of the known crash-retrieval events in AATIP's case files. He states that one nonhuman body was recovered. The event is presented as part of the series of recoveries that established the Legacy Program's holdings.

## Contexts Within the Source

The Ciudad Acuña event is presented alongside the Kansas (1989) and Roswell (1947) retrievals as part of a pattern of US government collection of nonhuman biological and technological material. Elizondo does not provide the name of the military unit or agency responsible for the recovery.
