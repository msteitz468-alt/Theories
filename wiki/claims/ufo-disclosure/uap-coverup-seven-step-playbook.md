## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that the US government's suppression of UAP information follows a consistent, identifiable seven-step playbook that AATIP analysts were able to reverse-engineer from historical case records. He presents this not as speculation but as a documented operational pattern.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 4, Elizondo identifies the seven steps of the coverup playbook:

1. **Immediate classification** of the event and all associated evidence
2. **Witness intimidation** — military personnel warned not to discuss what they saw, often under threat of legal sanction or career consequences
3. **Evidence confiscation** — physical materials, recordings, and reports removed from witnesses and classified
4. **Disinformation** — alternative explanations seeded (weather balloons, swamp gas, psychological phenomena)
5. **Ridicule** — institutional and media mockery deployed to discourage further reporting
6. **Compartmentalization** — information distributed on strict need-to-know basis across multiple agencies with no single office having full picture
7. **Denial** — official government spokespeople deny the event, deny the program, deny the witness's role

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo frames this playbook as having been in continuous use since at least Roswell (1947) and as operating automatically — not requiring active conspiracy but institutional reflex built into classification culture. He himself experienced steps 2, 6, and 7 after his resignation when the Pentagon denied his AATIP role.
