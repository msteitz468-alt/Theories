## Claim Statement

According to Bob Lazar, on his first day at an outpost called S-4 he was shown a flying saucer and told to reverse engineer its antigravity propulsion system. He says nine saucers were at S-4 and he was given a manual stating they came from another planet, with drawings of alien-like pilots.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "According to Lazar, that first day he was at Area 51 he was driven... to a mysterious complex of hangars... called S-4... He was shown a flying saucer and told it would be his job to reverse engineer its antigravity propulsion system. All told, there were nine saucers at S-4, Lazar says."

Lazar also says he glanced through a window and saw a small gray alien-like figure between men in white coats (early March 1989).

## Contexts Within the Source

Jacobsen frames Lazar's account alongside media frenzy, credential disputes, inconclusive lie detector tests, and Stanton Friedman's rejection. The source does not adjudicate truth.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Stanton Friedman calls Lazar a "total fraud" and disputes his MIT credentials. Jacobsen notes MIT had no record of Lazar. Lie detector tests were inconclusive but the administrator said Lazar appeared to believe his statements.
