## Claim Statement

A small metallic fragment that Zukowski and his sister recovered from the historic **Mac Brazel / Roswell debris site** is, per their account, "the only artifact ... acquired scientifically under pristine conditions at the proposed debris site," whose composition and recovery raise unresolved questions about a possible extraterrestrial or covered-up military origin.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The fragment was found during a **2002 archaeological dig** at the crash site; SEM (scanning electron microscope) analysis "hinted at an aluminum silicon alloy that had been damaged." Unable to afford further lab work (and after MUFON connections "had gone nowhere"), Zukowski went public via a **2008/2009 Roswell press conference** framed around three questions:

> 1. Is this artifact from an extraterrestrial craft which crashed at this site in July 1947?
> 2. Is this artifact left over from some type of military presence in this area?
> 3. Why was it so difficult to find this small piece? Could the site ... have been cleaned at some point? If so, Why?

The associated press release quoted Apollo 14 astronaut and Roswell native **Edgar Mitchell** ("We're not alone") and billed the find as "more proof that extraterrestrial life visited Earth." (See existing page [[Edgar Mitchell (Apollo 14 astronaut)]].)

## Contexts Within the Source

Zukowski sought "Undeniable, smoking-gun, physical evidence"; the artifact, the EMF data, and the carcasses were, in his mind, evidence — but "to the rest of the world ... none of that was enough." The going-public decision also cost him standing (and, the book says, his reserve-deputy position).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The SEM result (a damaged aluminum-silicon alloy) is consistent with terrestrial materials; the book leaves the artifact's origin explicitly **unresolved**, posing it as open questions rather than a proven non-terrestrial finding.
