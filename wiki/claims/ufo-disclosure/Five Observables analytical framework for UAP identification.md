## Claim Statement

Elizondo developed a Five Observables framework within AATIP to provide an objective, intelligence-community-based methodology for distinguishing genuine UAP from misidentified conventional aircraft or natural phenomena. A sixth unofficial observable was added later.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 12, Elizondo enumerates the Five Observables:

1. **Hypersonic velocity** — sustained speeds of Mach 17 or greater at sea level, without sonic booms
2. **Instantaneous acceleration** — immediate stops from hypersonic speed; right-angle turns at velocity impossible for any known aircraft
3. **Low observability** — no radar cross-section, no heat signature, no emissions, no contrail; described as "low observable" or effectively cloaked
4. **Transmedium travel** — seamless transition between air, water, and space without structural modification or deceleration
5. **Positive lift** (antigravity) — flight without conventional wings, rotors, or visible propulsion systems

**Sixth unofficial observable**: **Biological effects** on human witnesses — radiation burns, neurological changes, cancer clusters, implants, and altered brain structure (enlarged caudate-putamen as documented by Garry Nolan). Elizondo states this was not formally codified because it risked further stigmatizing the program.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo frames the Five Observables as a deliberately conservative, verifiable threshold — an object must exhibit at least one of these characteristics to merit classification as a genuine UAP, and most confirmed cases exhibit multiple. He designed the framework to be usable by analysts without any prior UFO-related background, grounding the investigation in known physics and engineering limits.
