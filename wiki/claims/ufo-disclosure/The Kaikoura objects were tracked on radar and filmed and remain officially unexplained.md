## Claim Statement

Coulthart reports that the December 1978 Kaikoura objects were tracked simultaneously on Wellington radar and an Argosy aircraft's radar, seen by crew and reporters and filmed, and that the Royal NZ Air Force's Venus / squid-boat explanation does not fit the evidence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Air-traffic controller John Cordy ('there was nothing wrong with the radar') and reporter Dennis Grant told Coulthart the objects were solid radar targets that parallel-tracked the plane for 40 miles, and that key witnesses (including Cordy) were never interviewed by investigators.

## Contexts Within the Source

Used to show a rushed official explanation applied to a well-recorded radar-visual case.
