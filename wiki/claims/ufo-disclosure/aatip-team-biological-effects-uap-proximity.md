## Claim Statement

Elizondo asserts that multiple members of the AATIP analytical team, and in some cases their family members, experienced direct biological effects apparently connected to their UAP investigative work. He describes a pattern of unexplained health events that began for team members after extended engagement with UAP evidence and, in some cases, after direct proximity to UAP phenomena at Skinwalker Ranch and other locations.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 6, Elizondo describes his own family's experience: green orbs appeared at the Elizondo family home during the period of his AATIP work. In Chapter 9, Jennifer Elizondo (his wife) is struck by a car and sustains a traumatic brain injury; while this is not attributed directly to UAP, it is contextualized within a period of escalating strangeness affecting the family. Elizondo also describes AATIP team members experiencing unexplained health issues.

In Chapter 10, Elizondo describes the broader biological effects case history: the Texas 1980 diamond-shaped UAP encounter resulting in radiation burns, the Rendlesham/Burroughs cardiac case, and a 2007 Davis, California, case linking blue orbs to breast cancer in witnesses. He also states that virtually all AATIP team members have Cherokee DNA and share the enlarged caudate-putamen brain feature documented by Nolan — suggesting the team was not random but possibly selected by factors related to UAP susceptibility.

The "hitchhiker effect" — coined by Jay Stratton — refers to the phenomenon of UAP-related anomalies following witnesses home, persisting in their environment after the initial encounter. Dogs were reportedly vaporized by a blue orb at Skinwalker Ranch.

## Contexts Within the Source

Elizondo presents this cluster of personal experiences not as proof of malicious NHI intent but as evidence that UAP interaction carries real physical risk — and that the biological effects observable is not limited to civilian bystanders but also affects trained intelligence professionals.
