## Claim Statement

UFO sightings, animal mutilations, and related "high strangeness" cluster disproportionately along a corridor near the **37th parallel** of latitude — Zukowski's "UFO Highway" (or "Paranormal Highway").

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Zukowski noticed that incident and earthquake coordinates kept falling near 37° N (e.g. the Sangre de Cristo fault event at 37.0412° N) and began pinning verified cases — "cattle mutilations, UFO sightings, most of your MUFON reports" — to a map. He concedes the corridor is a **band**, not an exact line: "many, if not most of the phenomena seemed to have occurred on the geographical 37th latitude, a number were closer to the 36th or 38th," so he pictures "a highway extending between the 38th and 36th parallels."

[[Debbie Ziegelmeyer]] independently confirms Missouri cases (Norwood, Piedmont) "both on the 37th," and lists "Joplin Spook Lights ... 37.090514. The Mantell Incident, Owensboro, Kentucky, 37.7737 ... Cape Girardeau, Missouri, 37.313656. Taos. Quite a highway." Roswell (33rd) is rationalized as on the path from Kenneth Arnold's Mount Rainier sighting. Mezrich's summary: "There was clearly a pattern here: A highway of anomalies spreading from one end of the country to the other, along the 37th parallel."

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the organizing claim of the book; everything before Chs. 27–28 supplies the data points, and the conclusion ([[The 37th parallel is 'a runway, not a highway']]) reinterprets the corridor's meaning.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Debbie warns: "Some of this has got to be coincidence ... related to the reporting mechanisms. Where we're situated is informing the kind of reports we see." Zukowski himself wonders, "Am I just going crazy? Or is this something?" — and only plots reports he "could reasonably verify," a self-acknowledged selection effect.
