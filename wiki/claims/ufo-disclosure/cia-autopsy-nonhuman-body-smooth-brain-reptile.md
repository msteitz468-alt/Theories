## Claim Statement

Elizondo states that an AATIP colleague received an official CIA autopsy report on a nonhuman body. The autopsy documented three anatomical features sharply distinct from human biology: a smooth cerebral cortex lacking the folds and convolutions of the human brain; a gastrointestinal system in which the gut and liver were conjoined into a single organ; and a cardiovascular system featuring a three-chambered heart consistent with reptilian rather than mammalian anatomy.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 11, Elizondo attributes this information to a colleague who was provided access to the CIA autopsy document within a classified setting. Elizondo does not name the colleague. The autopsy is described as an official CIA document, not a leaked or informal record. The three anatomical findings (smooth brain, conjoined gut/liver, three-chambered heart) are each stated as specific autopsy findings.

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is presented within a chapter that also names TRW's crash-retrieval operations at Wright-Patterson AFB and the Ciudad Acuña (1950) and Kazakhstan (1989) crash retrievals. Elizondo treats the autopsy report as consistent with these other recovery events — physical confirmation of nonhuman biology recovered from crash sites.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Elizondo is explicit that this is secondhand: he personally reviewed his colleague's account of the document, not the document itself. He does not specify which crash or recovery event the autopsy is associated with.
