## Claim Statement

Bramley claims the snake/serpent of ancient symbolism was the logo of a Brotherhood originally founded by the god **Ea** ([[Enki (Sitchin)]]) and dedicated to liberating humanity through spiritual knowledge. After Ea's faction was defeated by rival Custodians, Ea was demonized (recast as "Satan," "Prince of Darkness"), and the Brotherhood was captured and "corrupted" into "the world's most effective tool for preserving mankind's status as a spiritually ignorant creature of toil." The corrupted Brotherhood became a far-flung network of secret societies and religions controlled, at its hidden top, by the Custodians.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Bramley cites Mesopotamian depictions of a snake coiled around the Tree (with Ea's and Anu's symbols), and Sitchin's note that the Hebrew for snake, *nahash*, derives from a root meaning "to decipher, to find out." He argues the corrupted Brotherhood ran the Egyptian "Mystery Schools," elevated Custodians to the status of God, and through secrecy "disguised its organizational hierarchy," allowing the Custodians to "hide at the top… behind veils of myth."

## Contexts Within the Source

The Brotherhood is the engine for nearly every later claim — the Mystery Schools, monotheism, Freemasonry, the religions (Jehovah, Jesus, Mohammed), the plagues, the banks, and the revolutions. Bramley maintains sincere humanitarians kept working "through Brotherhood channels" throughout. See [[The Mystery Schools corrupted and suppressed spiritual knowledge]], [[Freemasonry descends from the Brotherhood's Egyptian mason guilds]].

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Bramley insists on a persistent distinction between the *original benevolent* Brotherhood and its *corrupted* form, and records "lingering humanitarian influences" (e.g., Moses, reform movements) surfacing across history.
