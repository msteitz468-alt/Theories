## Claim Statement

Bramley's concluding doctrine: each person is fundamentally a spiritual being — a "timeless and indestructible unit of awareness" — and all such units are individuated "units of awareness" put forth by a single original Primary/Supreme Being so it could experience countless unique viewpoints and creations (he likens it to a control booth feeding trillions of cameras). Entrapment in matter shrinks a being's perspective until it forgets it is a "camera" and believes it is mere "foliage." Salvation is recovering one's unique self-awareness and perspective — not "merging" with a Supreme Being (which he calls a false goal), and not going to the near-death "being of light" (which he warns may induce post-death amnesia).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "Individual spiritual beings are actually the units of awareness of a Primary, or Supreme, Being, yet each unit is possessed of its own self-awareness, personality, freewill… and infinite creativity." (Ch. 40)

He argues repressing or entrapping any spiritual being is therefore "the act of trying to reduce a Supreme Being." His practical remedies (Ch. 39): debt-free government money, seeking and negotiating with the Custodial society to end the conflict, "security through friendship" rather than arms, and expanding small everyday freedoms.

## Contexts Within the Source

This is Bramley's answer to the enslavement thesis — it reframes Custodial spiritual repression as a cosmic "psychosis" of awareness units repressing one another, and grounds his optimism that "a core of good and decency" exists in everyone, even despots.
