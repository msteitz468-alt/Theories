## Claim Statement

Bramley argues humans "appear to be a slave race languishing on an isolated planet," created and still owned by an extraterrestrial society he calls the **Custodians**. Drawing on Sumerian tablets (via Sitchin), he states the Custodians first inhabited Earth to exploit its resources, found the labor "backbreaking," and genetically engineered *Homo sapiens* as a work race — bred in Custodial wombs from a god's body mixed with "clay" (genetic material). Humans were treated as livestock and culled by famine, plague, and the Flood when too numerous.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Bramley adopts Charles Fort's formula — "I think we're property… this earth… is owned by something" — and quotes the tablets: "When the Gods like men / Bore the work and suffered the toil… the work was heavy." He credits the god **Ea** (= [[Enki (Sitchin)]]) with engineering man, and his rival **Enlil** ([[Enlil (Sitchin)]]) with the cruelties (cutting off food, the plague, the deluge). He points to the abrupt appearance of *Homo sapiens* and disappearance of Neanderthals as evidence of "intelligent intervention."

## Contexts Within the Source
