## Claim Statement

Bramley reads the Genesis stories of Eden, the Fall, and Babel as symbolic records of Custodial control, derived from older Mesopotamian sources in which the "Lord God" is the Custodial rulers. Adam (first man) was a servant forbidden two kinds of knowledge — the "tree of knowledge of good and evil" (ethics) and the "tree of life" (spiritual identity/immortality) — because spiritually free beings cannot be enslaved. The serpent that offered the fruit symbolizes the original [[The Brotherhood of the Snake was founded by Ea then corrupted into a tool of spiritual repression]], and the Tower of Babel was a "divide and conquer" scattering of a unified humanity.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Bramley quotes Genesis 3:22 ("the man has become as one of us, knowing good from evil… what if he… takes also of the tree of life, and… lives forever?") and argues the "flaming sword" placed at Eden represents "the no-nonsense measures" keeping spiritual knowledge from humans. Following Sitchin, he reads the Babel "name" (*shem*) as "sky vehicle/rocket," so the tower was a human attempt to match Custodial technology — prompting the Custodians to confuse human language. He notes Adam and Eve's shame at nakedness signified their *enslavement* (Custodians were depicted clothed, human laborers naked), not sexuality.
