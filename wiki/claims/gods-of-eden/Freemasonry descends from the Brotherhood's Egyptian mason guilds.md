## Claim Statement

Bramley traces Freemasonry to elite mason guilds sponsored by the Brotherhood in ancient Egypt — guilds with "Warden" and "Master" ranks and mystical traditions that built temples (including Solomon's). Because guild members were often free men, they were called "free masons." He uses the ceremonial **apron** as a visible thread linking the Custodial "gods" (depicted wearing aprons), the Egyptian priesthood, the Melchizedek Priesthood (which began making white-lambskin aprons c. 2200 B.C.), and modern Freemasonry.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He cites the Egyptian *Book of the Dead*, in which the god **Thoth** calls himself "Grand Master of the craftsmen who set up the sacred arch" — evidence, Bramley says, that a Custodial god led the ancient guilds and initiated higher degrees. Archaeologist Petrie's finds of a c. 2000 B.C. guild with duties "very similar to those of 'Warden' and 'Master'" in Freemasonry, and the apron statuette (c. 3400 B.C.) described by Rosicrucian H. Spencer Lewis, are offered as support.
