## Claim Statement

Bramley argues the Old Testament Jehovah was not a Supreme Being but "a succession of Custodial management teams" using their aircraft to impersonate God and enforce obedience. The Biblical descriptions — smoke, fire, thunder, a moving "pillar of cloud/fire," and especially Ezekiel's metallic flying object with humanlike occupants in helmets — read, he says, like sightings of rocket craft and their pilots. Moses was a high-ranking Brotherhood initiate (per the Egyptian priest Manetho) who established a Brotherhood branch among the Hebrews.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Bramley quotes Exodus/Genesis descriptions of Jehovah and the whole of Ezekiel 1, concluding the "wings" were "retractable engines" and the heads were "covered by glass… that reflected the sky." He cites Jehovah's mass killings (up to 14,000 at a time), the genocidal conquest of Canaan under Joshua, and the Bible's own admission that "it was the Lord who hardened their hearts, that they would go against Israel" (Joshua 11:20) — which Bramley reads as Custodians manipulating victims into war (the Machiavellian technique). He credits the humane Ten Commandments to human Brotherhood humanitarians like Moses, per Manetho.
