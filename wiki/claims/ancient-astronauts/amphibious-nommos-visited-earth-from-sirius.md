**Claim Statement**  
Earth was visited in prehistory by advanced, amphibious, merman-like beings called the Nommos from the Sirius system, who arrived in a circular ark, civilized early humans, and returned to space, leaving behind oral traditions detailing the physics of their home star system.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *The Sirius Mystery* (Chapters One and Seven):
* **Amphibious Nature:** The Dogon portray the Nommos as semi-aquatic or amphibious beings with fish-like lower bodies, scales, and human-like upper bodies. They are described as "masters of the water" who needed to live in a moist, liquid environment, which Temple links to Sumerian depictions of **[[Oannes]]**.
* **The Landing of the Ark:** The Dogon describe the landing of the Nommos' vessel (the "Ark of Nommo") on dry land, which caused a great noise, vibrating earth, and rushing wind. The ark eventually slid into water, and the Nommos emerged.
* **Sirius C Environment:** Temple speculates that the Nommos' home planet orbits Sirius C (*Emme Ya*) in a figure-eight orbit alternately wrapping around Sirius A and B, creating a watery, moist environment suitable for amphibious life.

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Temple frames the Nommos as actual extraterrestrial visitors rather than mythological gods. He emphasizes that they are never called "gods" by the Dogon, but rather "monitors" or "teachers." He asserts that they established the initial foundations of civilization in Egypt and Sumer and that their astronomical disclosures explain the Dogon's detailed knowledge of Sirius B.
