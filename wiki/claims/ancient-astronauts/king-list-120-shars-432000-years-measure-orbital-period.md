## Claim Statement

The "impossibly" long reigns of the pre-Diluvial kings in the Sumerian king lists are not exaggeration: they are recorded in units of the **shar (3,600 years)** because they are tied to the **orbital period of the Twelfth Planet**. The ten rulers before the Deluge reigned a combined **120 shars = 432,000 years**.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sitchin cites Berossus (via Alexander Polyhistor): "the history of the ten kings of the Chaldeans, and the periods of each reign, which consisted collectively of an hundred and twenty shar's, or four hundred and thirty-two thousand years; reaching to the time of the Deluge." Abydenus likewise gives "120 shar's," noting "a shar is esteemed to be three thousand six hundred years."

These match the Sumerian king list **W-B/144**: "When kingship was lowered from Heaven, kingship was first in Eridu. In Eridu, A.LU.LIM became king; he ruled 28,800 years. A.LAL.GAR ruled 36,000 years," and so on through Bad-Tibira (EN.MEN.LU.AN.NA, 43,200 years; etc.). All figures are exact multiples of 3,600.

Sitchin's inference: "what is a year? ... the orbit of the Nefilim's planet equaled a *shar*, or 3,600 Earth years," so reign-lengths were measured in orbital cycles of the home planet.
