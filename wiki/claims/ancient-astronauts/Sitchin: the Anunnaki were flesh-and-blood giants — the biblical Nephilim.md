## Claim Statement

Sitchin argues that the Hebrew word "Nefilim" in Genesis 6:4 — traditionally rendered "giants" in English Bibles — literally means "Those who came down" (from the verb NaFoL, "to fall down, to be downed, to come down"), and therefore refers to the [[Nefilim (Sitchin)]], the same beings the Sumerians called the Anunnaki ("Those who from Heaven to Earth came"). These were not metaphors or mythological symbols but physically real, flesh-and-blood space-travelers from the planet [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]] who possessed large, towering bodies — matching every ancient tradition of "giants upon the Earth." The biblical preamble to the Flood story is, on this reading, a compressed historical record of their presence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The book's animating question is posed in the Introduction: a young Sitchin asked his teacher why Genesis 6:4 reads "giants" when "the word in the original Hebrew text is Nefilim — which, stemming from the Hebrew verb NaFoL, means to fall down, to be downed, to come down — and in no way 'giants'." The teacher's angry rebuke, Sitchin writes, "changed my life's direction to pursue the Nefilim. Who were they, and who were their 'Mighty Men' descendants?" (lines 187–191).

His answer is explicit in Chapter V: "Adding 432,000 + 13,000, we can thus confidently say that 'Kingship was brought down [to Earth] from the heavens' some 445,000 years ago. It was then that astronauts from another planet, whom the Sumerians called Anunnaki, arrived on Earth. They were the biblical Anakim — the Nefilim of Genesis 6." (lines 2137–2141). The word "Anakim" (biblical "giants of the land of Canaan") is thus equated with the Anunnaki as the same flesh-and-blood race of large beings.

Sitchin grounds the physical reality of these beings in Sumerian textual and archaeological evidence. He quotes Enki's autobiographical text — "I am the leader of the Anunnaki. Engendered by fecund seed, the Firstborn son of divine An, the 'Big Brother' of all the gods" (lines 2171–2178) — to show that these were self-described persons with genealogies, ranks, and physical bodies that could tire, desire, and die. The Atra-Hasis Epic is cited to confirm that the Anunnaki themselves bore heavy mining toil "like men" before creating the Adam: "When the gods, like men, bore the work and suffered the toil — the toil of the gods was great, the work was heavy, distress was much" (lines 3521–3527). Their bodily reality is underscored by the fact that the Igigi rebel leader Zu could physically seize the Tablet of Destinies while "Enlil was bathing" (line 3461), and that the Anunnaki mutineers "set fire to their tools" and marched on Enlil's gate (lines 3567–3575).

The Baalbek "Landing Place" is cited as physical evidence of their great stature and engineering capacity: stone blocks weighing "more than 1,100 tons each (!)" — the Trilithon — with "local lore attribut[ing] them to 'the giants'" (lines 3315–3316).

The book's title is itself the central assertion: the biblical phrase "There were giants upon the Earth in those days and also thereafter too, when the sons of God came in unto the daughters of men" (Genesis 6:4, quoted lines 167–179) is read literally. The Nefilim/Anunnaki were physically present on Earth, were physically giant relative to humans, and their offspring were the "Mighty Men of old, Men of Renown."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is the book's title thesis and is developed across virtually every chapter. It connects directly to [[Sitchin: demigods and long-lived patriarchs descended from unions of Anunnaki and humans]], since the giants' interbreeding with human women is the Genesis 6 event that produced the demigods and triggered the Flood. The arrival narrative — the Anunnaki splashing down in the Persian Gulf, establishing Eridu, building mining settlements in Africa — is the physical backstory for why flesh-and-blood beings of extraterrestrial origin were present on Earth to be seen and remembered as "giants." Their six-hundred-strong Earth contingent (lines 3141, 3435) and three-hundred Igigi in space-station duties (lines 3131, 3435) made them a substantial, visible alien population.

The claim also contextualizes the Sumerian King List's pre-Diluvial rulers (Chapter V), who reigned for Sar-unit periods totaling 432,000 years: these were the governing Anunnaki individuals, not mythological abstractions. Their "Shar" reign-lengths distinguish them from mortal humans and mark them as a different, longer-lived species — the physical "giants" of Genesis.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Sitchin is aware that mainstream scholarship treats these stories as myth. He notes with sarcasm that "for reasons that defy understanding, it is the norm in scholarly circles" to classify ancient god-texts as myth while accepting the historicity of Alexander the Great's campaigns despite their equal reliance on divine parentage and oracular interventions (lines 355–371). He does not provide, and does not claim to provide, physical skeletal or fossil evidence of Anunnaki bodies. The mainstream scientific and archaeological consensus rejects the identification of Nefilim with alien astronauts entirely; no peer-reviewed evidence supports the existence of [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]] or of extraterrestrial visitors in the human prehistoric record.
