## Claim Statement

An Akkadian cylinder seal in the East Berlin State Museum (catalogue **VA/243**) depicts the solar system as **twelve bodies** — the Sun, the Moon (as a body in its own right), the nine known planets, and one additional large planet between Mars and Jupiter — proving the Sumerians possessed accurate modern astronomical knowledge millennia early.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sitchin: seal VA/243 "does not show them individually but rather as a group of eleven globes encircling a large, rayed star. It is clearly a depiction of the solar system as it was known to the Sumerians: a system consisting of twelve celestial bodies." Reading the globes in order, he matches them to the planets by **relative size and sequence**: small Mercury, larger Venus, Earth with its small Moon, Mars (smaller than Earth), then "a planet unknown to us — considerably larger than Earth, yet smaller than Jupiter and Saturn," then Uranus and Neptune as a pair, and Pluto placed near Saturn. "The 4,500-year-old depiction, however, also insists that there was — or has been — another major planet between Mars and Jupiter ... the Twelfth Planet, the planet of the Nefilim."

He stresses the Sumerians could not have known of Uranus, Neptune or Pluto by naked-eye observation, so their inclusion implies the knowledge was given by the Nefilim.
