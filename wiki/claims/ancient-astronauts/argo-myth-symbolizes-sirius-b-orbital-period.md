**Claim Statement**  
The Greek mythological ship *Argo* (representing a celestial ark or orbit), its fifty rowers (the Argonauts), and the fifty daughters of Danaus whom it transported from Egypt, were symbolic structures encoding the fifty-year orbital period of the white dwarf star Sirius B.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *The Sirius Mystery* (Chapter Four):
* **Fifty Argonauts:** The ship *Argo* was built specifically for fifty Argonauts, and myth emphasizes that the number of Argonauts was identical to the ship's fifty oars. Temple interprets these oars as yearly markers or stages in the 50-year orbit of the companion star.
* **Fifty Daughters of Danaus:** The *Argo* was also famous for transporting the fifty daughters of Danaus from Egypt, further emphasizing the number fifty in connection with voyages originating in Egypt.
* **Canopus as Wazn (Weight):** The brightest star in the Argo constellation is Canopus, which was historically named *Al Wazn* ("Weight") by Arabic astronomers. Since a white dwarf is defined by its extreme density and gravitational weight, Temple argues this naming represents a garbled memory of the heavy companion star.
* **The Celestial Ark:** The Egyptian predecessor to the *Argo* was the celestial boat of Isis and Osiris, which transported the gods across the heavens. Temple suggests the Greeks historicized this astronomical cycle into Jason's voyage.

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Temple presents this claim to show that the Sirius mystery was not restricted to Egypt and West Africa, but also permeated Greek esotericism. He argues that Greek mythmakers inherited pre-dynastic Egyptian astronomical data and transformed the abstract 50-year orbital period into Jason's quest for the Golden Fleece on a 50-oared ship.
