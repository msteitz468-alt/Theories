## Claim Statement

Sitchin argues that two successive wars—which he calls the First and Second Pyramid Wars—were fought among the [[Nefilim (Sitchin)]] over control of the Giza pyramid complex and the Sinai Spaceport, facilities regarded as the key infrastructure linking Earth with the home planet Nibiru. The wars pitted the clan of [[Enki (Sitchin)]] (the Hamitic/Egyptian gods, including Marduk/Ra, Nergal, and Horus) against the clan of [[Enlil (Sitchin)]] (led in battle by Ninurta and Adad). They ended at a peace conference convened by Ninharsag, after which the Earth's regions were formally divided among the Anunnaki lineages, the Giza pyramid was stripped of its guidance equipment, and a new Mission Control Center was established at Jerusalem under the god Shamash.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**The post-Flood division of Earth (pre-war background)**

The conflict's roots lie in how the Earth was partitioned after the Deluge. Sitchin cites the Sumerian Atra-Hasis Epic: "The gods clasped hands together, then cast lots and divided: Anu to heaven went up; To Enlil the Earth was made subject; That which the sea as a loop encloses, they gave to the prince Enki. To the Abzu Enki went down" (lines 3643–3685). Enlil and his sons took the highland arc of Mesopotamia and the Levant; Enki and his six sons divided Africa. A Sumerian text Sitchin cites then declares: "The Anunnaki who decree the fates sat exchanging their counsels regarding the Earth. The four regions they created" (lines 5757–5775)—three for mankind's civilizations and a fourth, called Tilmun (the Sinai peninsula), reserved as the Anunnaki's own spaceport and guiding-beacon zone.

**The cause of the First Pyramid War**

Sitchin identifies the First Pyramid War with the Horus–Seth conflict narrated in the Edfu temple inscription (Chapter 2). Seth had been allotted Upper Egypt while Osiris (son of Ra/Marduk, himself son of Enki/Ptah) received Lower Egypt, which included control of the Giza pyramid complex. Sitchin argues: "whoever controlled them shared in the control of the space activities, of the comings and goings of the gods, of the vital supply link to and from the Twelfth Planet" (line 6741). Seth conspired to murder Osiris and seize the whole of Egypt, including the pyramids. After a series of battles that ranged from Nubia to the Sinai lakes—with Horus using an aerial Winged Disk and his trained "Metal People" (mesniu) armed with "Divine Iron"—"the Council of the Gods gave the whole of Egypt 'as heritage . . . to Horus'" (lines 1743–1751). Seth was "banished from Egypt and took up abode in Asiatic lands to the east" (line 6757), effectively extending Enki-clan control over Canaan, a territory that properly belonged to the Enlil lineage.

**The Second Pyramid War**

Seth's occupation of Canaan—including the future sites of Jerusalem (Mission Control Center) and Baalbek (Landing Platform)—was an encroachment on Enlil-clan territory that Sitchin says the Enlilites could not accept. "Soon thereafter—300 years later, we believe—they deliberately launched a war to dislodge the illegal occupiers from the vital space facilities" (line 6805). This Second Pyramid War is recorded in the Sumerian epic Lugal-e and related hymns to Ninurta. Its commander was Ninurta, "Enlil's foremost warrior": "Like a flood which cannot be stopped, the Enemyland as with a girdle you tightly bound. Foremost one, who in battle vehemently enters; Hero, who in his hand the Divine Brilliant Weapon carries; Lord: the Mountainland you subdued as your creature" (lines 6839–6863).

Ninurta flew a purpose-built aircraft called the Imdugud (Divine Storm Bird), approximately seventy-five feet in wingspan (line 6903), attacking the retreating Enkite forces across Africa. Adad destroyed food supplies: "In the Abzu, Adad the fish caused to be washed away . . . the cattle he dispersed" (line 6921). Even Ishtar joined: "In the clash of weapons, in the feats of heroship, Ishtar her arm did not hold back" (line 6931), wielding a weapon that "tore apart [the enemy], made him with his hand clutch his heart" (lines 6934–6935), while "the skies were like red-hued wool in color" (line 6935).

The Enki gods retreated to the Great Pyramid as their final stronghold. Nergal broke through the siege lines at night to reinforce the defenders: "Divine Nergal, Lord who by night stole out, had come to the battle! He cracks his whip, his weapons clank" (lines 7041–7059). Ninurta then cut the pyramid's water supply and, with the defenders starving, a young god (identified as Horus) who tried to escape disguised as a ram was "struck by Ninurta's Brilliant Weapon and lost the sight of his eyes" (line 7161). An "Olden God" cried to Ninharsag: "the weapon . . . my offspring with death is accursed" (lines 7183–7189).

**The Peace Conference**

Ninharsag—half-sister of both Enki and Enlil and thus the most neutral senior goddess—crossed the battle lines and mediated. She addressed Enki within the besieged pyramid: "Enki agreed to surrender the pyramid to her: 'The House that is like a heap, that which I have as a pile raised up—its mistress you may be'" (lines 7245–7257). She then brought the gods together at her mountain abode, the Harsag in the Sinai, and lit seven fires—one for each of the seven assembled gods (Enki and two sons; Enlil and three sons)—performing a symbolic ceremony of reconciliation (line 7547).

Enki's peace terms, which Enlil accepted, were direct: "I will grant thee the ruler's position in the gods' Restricted Zone; The Radiant Place, in thy hand I will entrust!" (lines 7615–7627). The "Restricted Zone" was the Sinai Spaceport (Tilmun); the "Radiant Place" was Jerusalem, the future Mission Control Center. In return, Enlil recognized Enki's permanent sovereignty over the Giza complex. Enlil added one condition: the sons of Enki who had fought from the Great Pyramid were barred from ruling Lower Egypt. Enki appointed his young son Ningishzidda (identified with the Egyptian god Thoth) as the new guardian of the pyramid.

**Post-war territorial assignments**

After the conference, the Enlilite lands were parceled out: Ninurta received the "Olden Land" (Mesopotamia); Adad's territory was extended to include the Baalbek Landing Place; and all of Canaan (from the Egyptian border to the Hittite lands) was placed under the aegis of Nannar/Sin and his offspring. Shamash (Sin's son) was appointed commander of Jerusalem: "He called in Shamash the grandchild of Ninlil. He took him [by the hand]; In Shulim he placed him" (lines 7883–7901). Jerusalem's Sumerian name, SHU.LIM, is interpreted as "The Supreme Place of the Four Regions" (line 7907). The Sinai peninsula itself, renamed as Sin's land, became the "Sinai" (line 7875). A new beacon city (Heliopolis/Annu) replaced the stripped Great Pyramid at the northwestern anchor of the Landing Corridor (line 7921).

**The stripping of the Great Pyramid**

Ninurta's first act on entering the surrendered pyramid was systematic dismantling of its guidance and weapons systems. He ordered the SHAM ("Destiny") Stone destroyed: "its strong power was used to 'grab to kill me, with a tracking which kills to seize me' . . . pulled out . . . be taken apart . . . and to obliteration be destroyed" (line 7435). He then removed the GUG ("Direction Determining") Stone from the uppermost chamber: "by the fate-determining Ninurta, on that day was the Gug stone from its hollow taken out and smashed" (line 7453). He announced his purpose: "'Let the fear of thee'—the Great Pyramid—'be removed from my descendants; let their peace be ordained'" (line 7471). Finally "the Apex Stone of the Pyramid" was sent crashing down: "'Let the mother's offspring see it no more,' he ordered" (line 7475).

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is the military and geopolitical climax of the book and the direct application of the thesis stated in [[Sitchin: the "gods" fought real physical wars among themselves over Earth]]. The Pyramid Wars explain, in Sitchin's framework, why the Great Pyramid has no apex stone today, why its upper chambers are bare of inscriptions, and why the ancient Egyptians themselves left no record of who built it. The post-war settlement also explains the later biblical geography: why Canaan is cursed in Genesis (it was illegally seized Enlil-territory), why Jerusalem becomes the "Navel of the Earth," and why the Sinai is called by Sin's name. The entities [[Enki (Sitchin)]], [[Enlil (Sitchin)]], and the [[Nefilim (Sitchin)]] are central throughout.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Mainstream Egyptology holds that the Giza pyramids were built by Pharaohs of the Fourth Dynasty (c. 2560–2510 BC), not by extraterrestrial beings in prehistoric times. Sitchin acknowledges this directly, arguing that the standard attribution to Khufu rests largely on an inscription he claims was forged by Colonel Howard Vyse in 1837 (line 5967), and that the Inventory Stela shows Khufu referring to the pyramid and Sphinx as pre-existing (line 6343). These claims are rejected by the archaeological mainstream. The fragmentary state of Sitchin's primary Sumerian sources (many tablets are broken, mis-ordered, or missing sections) means his reconstructions of battle sequences and peace-conference details involve considerable interpretive filling of gaps. Independent scholars of Assyriology and Egyptology do not support the identification of Ninurta, Marduk, or Nergal with specific historical commanders, nor the reading of the Lugal-e epic as a literal military chronicle.
