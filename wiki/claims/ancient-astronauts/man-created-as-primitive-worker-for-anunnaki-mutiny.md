## Claim Statement

Man was deliberately created to be a **labour force** — a *lulu* ("primitive worker") — so that the rank-and-file Anunnaki, who had toiled at gold mining for 40 *shars* (144,000 years) and finally **mutinied**, could be relieved of their burden.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sitchin quotes the Creation epic's decision (in Marduk's words): "I will produce a lowly Primitive; 'Man' shall be his name. I will create a Primitive Worker; He will be charged with the service of the gods, that they might have their ease." The labour text "When the gods as men" has the Anunnaki cry "No more!" after "10 ... 20 ... 30 ... 40 periods they suffered the toil" — Sitchin reading *ma*/"period" as *shar* (3,600 years), so "Forty shars, or 144,000 Earth years, after their landing, the Anunnaki protested." The proposal: "Let a *lulu*, a 'Primitive Worker,' be created! ... Let him bear the yoke ... Let him carry the toil of" the gods.

The terminology underscores man's purpose: "He was a *lulu* ('primitive'), a *lulu amelu* ('primitive worker'), an *awilum* ('laborer'). That Man was created to be a servant of the gods did not strike the ancient peoples as a peculiar idea at all." The labour itself was **gold mining** in the AB.ZU (the "Lower World").

## Contexts Within the Source

This ties the creation of man (Ch. 12) directly to the economic purpose of the mission (gold) and the social crisis among the gods (the mutiny, Ch. 11).
