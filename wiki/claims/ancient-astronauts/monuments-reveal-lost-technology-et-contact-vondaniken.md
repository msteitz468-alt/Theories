## Claim Statement

Von Däniken argues that many ancient monuments, maps, and artifacts exceed the technical capacity attributed to their builders, and are best explained as the products of — or aids left by — extraterrestrial visitors: "ancient marvels," he asks, or "space travel centers"?

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His exhibits include the **Piri Reis map** (ancient maps that, examined by cartographer Arlington Mallery, he says show impossible geographic knowledge — "as if they had been taken from a height"), the **Nazca lines** of Peru (which he likens to runways or a landing field), the precision of the **Great Pyramid**, the colossal statues of **Easter Island**, the megaliths of **Tiahuanaco**, and the Palenque sarcophagus lid (which he reads as a figure operating controls in a capsule). Each, he argues, points to know-how "we have only partially rediscovered today."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

These are von Däniken's interpretations of anomalies; many (the Nazca-runway and Piri Reis readings especially) have been strongly contested or refuted, and he frames them as questions challenging orthodoxy.
