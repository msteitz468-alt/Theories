## Claim Statement

Our solar system contains one more major planet than astronomy recognises — the **Twelfth Planet** (Sun + Moon + ten planets in the Sumerian count), which Sitchin names **Nibiru**, "the Planet of the Crossing." It moves on a vast elliptical orbit whose perihelion carries it through the region between Mars and Jupiter and whose **period is 3,600 Earth-years**.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sitchin argues the Sumerians knew of a planet "between Mars and Jupiter ... considerably larger than Earth, yet smaller than Jupiter and Saturn." On the orbital period: "the Mesopotamian and biblical sources present strong evidence that the orbital period of the Twelfth Planet is 3,600 years. The number 3,600 was written in Sumerian as a large circle. The epithet for the planet — *shar* ('supreme ruler') — also meant 'a perfect circle,' a 'completed cycle.' It also meant the number 3,600. And the identity of the three terms — planet/orbit/3,600 — could not be a mere coincidence." (See [[Shar (3,600-year orbital period)]].)

He treats the planet as identical with **Marduk** of the *Enuma Elish*, a body that "first encountered Neptune," had its course "bent inward" past Uranus, Saturn and Jupiter, and was set on collision with the planet Tiamat.

## Contexts Within the Source

Sitchin appeals to the (then-recent) discoveries of Uranus (1781), Neptune (1846) and Pluto (1930) to argue that "extra" planets in the Sumerian list, once dismissed, keep turning out to be real — so a twelfth body should likewise be accepted.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Sitchin acknowledges that a 3,600-year period is far shorter than the orbital periods "running into ... thousands of years" of long-period comets, but argues the textual evidence settles the figure at 3,600.
