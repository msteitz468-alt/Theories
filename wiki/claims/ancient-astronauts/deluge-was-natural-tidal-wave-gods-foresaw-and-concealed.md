## Claim Statement

The biblical/Mesopotamian Deluge was a **real, global, natural catastrophe** — an immense tidal wave caused by the sudden slippage of the Antarctic ice sheet into the sea, an event the nearing Twelfth Planet helped trigger. It was **not a punishment willed by the gods**; rather, the gods *foresaw* it and **conspired to keep it secret** from mankind so that humanity would perish — a plan subverted by Enki, who warned his servant Ziusudra (Noah).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sitchin: "the Deluge was not a premeditated punishment ... it was an unavoidable one, a natural calamity in which the gods played not an active but a passive role. We will also show that the secret the gods swore to was a conspiracy against Mankind — to withhold from the Earthlings the information they had regarding the coming avalanche of water so that, while the Nefilim saved themselves, Mankind should perish."

On the mechanism he adopts Dr. John T. Hollin's hypothesis that "the Antarctic ice sheet periodically breaks loose and slips into the sea, creating an abrupt and enormous tidal wave," and A. T. Wilson's (1964) theory that ice ages ended in such slippages: "the Deluge was the result of such a slippage into the Antarctic waters of billions of tons of ice, bringing an abrupt end to the last ice age." The chronology table records: "13,000 [years ago] — The Nefilim, aware of the impending tidal wave that will be triggered by the nearing Twelfth Planet, vow to let Mankind perish. The Deluge sweeps over Earth, abruptly ending the ice age."

[[Enlil (Sitchin)]] imposes the oath of secrecy (he "seeks [mankind's] demise"); [[Enki (Sitchin)]] warns Atra-Hasis/Ziusudra to build the ark. Sitchin dates the event by precession to the "Age of the Lion" (c. 10,860–8700 B.C.), consistent with the end of the last ice age ~12–13,000 years ago.

## Contexts Within the Source

The Deluge wipes out the gods' installations (the African mines, Nippur, the Sippar spaceport), forcing the Nefilim to teach agriculture to the survivors — Sitchin's bridge to the post-Diluvial rise of civilization.
