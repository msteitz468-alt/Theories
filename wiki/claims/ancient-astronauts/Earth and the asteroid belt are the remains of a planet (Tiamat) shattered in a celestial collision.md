## Claim Statement

The Earth and the asteroid belt are the two halves of a former watery planet, **Tiamat**, which was destroyed when the invading Twelfth Planet (Marduk/Nibiru) — or rather its satellites ("winds") — collided with it. The biblical/Mesopotamian phrase "Heaven and Earth" denotes these two products: the "Hammered Bracelet" (asteroid belt) and Earth.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sitchin reads the *Enuma Elish* literally: "An unstable solar system, made up of the Sun and nine planets, was invaded by a large, comet-like planet from outer space. It first encountered Neptune; as it passed by Uranus, the giant Saturn, and Jupiter, its course was profoundly bent inward ... It was unalterably set on a collision course with Tiamat." Crucially, "the two planets did not collide ... It was the satellites of Marduk that smashed into Tiamat." The satellites "distended Tiamat's body, made in her a wide cleavage," and Marduk's "arrow" / "divine lightning" — a bolt of electricity — "extinguished her life-breath."

Of Tiamat's halves: "Of one part of her he made Heaven, and of the other, Earth." Sitchin's gloss: "Our Earth and the asteroid belt are the 'Heaven and Earth' of both Mesopotamian and biblical references." After "Marduk's North Wind had pushed Earth to its new celestial location, Earth obtained its own orbit around the Sun ... and received its axial spin."

He marshals the modern asteroid belt as corroboration: astronomer Giuseppe Piazzi's 1801 discovery of Ceres, the ~3,000 asteroids since found, their single axial rotation (implying one parent body), and the fact that their combined mass is "insufficient to account for even one whole planet" — explained, he says, because the other half became Earth.
