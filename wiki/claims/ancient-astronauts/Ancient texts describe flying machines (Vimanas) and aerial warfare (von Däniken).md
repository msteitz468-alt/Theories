## Claim Statement

Von Däniken argues that the ancient Sanskrit epics describe real flying machines — **Vimanas** — and advanced aerial (even seemingly atomic) warfare, in terms their writers could not have invented without having witnessed such technology, evidence (he says) of the visitors' craft.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He cites the **Ramayana** ("Vimanas, i.e., flying machines, navigated at great heights with the aid of quicksilver and a great propulsive wind… could travel forward, upward and downward") and the **Mahabharata** ("Bhima flew with his Vimana on an enormous ray which was as brilliant as the sun and made a noise like the thunder of a storm"). He stresses the texts distinguish "chariots that fly and those that cannot fly," and asks: "How can the chronicler give descriptions that presuppose at least some idea of rockets and the knowledge that such a vehicle can ride on a ray and cause a terrifying thunder?"

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Von Däniken presents these as suggestive readings of myth ("even imagination needs something to start it off") rather than proof, inviting re-examination.

## Contexts Within the Source

A textual companion to [[Ancient sacred texts literally describe extraterrestrial visitors and craft (von Däniken)]] (Ezekiel), extending the literal-reading method to the Indian epics — a method later echoed across the ancient-astronaut and paleocontact literature.
