## Claim Statement

Von Däniken argues that anomalous "out-of-place" artifacts — objects too technologically advanced for their era — prove that a forgotten high technology (ultimately of extraterrestrial origin) existed in antiquity.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His exhibits include the **Baghdad batteries**: "Electric dry batteries, which work on the galvanic principle, are on display in the Baghdad Museum," with "electric elements with copper electrodes and an unknown electrolyte." He adds the **Helwan cloth** ("a fabric so fine that today it could be woven only in a special factory with great technical know-how"), and a **Kohistan cave drawing** that he says "reproduces the exact position of the stars as they actually were 10,000 years ago," with "Venus and the earth… joined by lines." He sums up the genre in his introduction: "we find electric batteries many thousands of years old… strange beings in perfect space suits."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

These are von Däniken's interpretations of disputed objects (the "Baghdad battery" identification and his other artifact readings have been widely challenged); he offers them as anomalies demanding explanation.
