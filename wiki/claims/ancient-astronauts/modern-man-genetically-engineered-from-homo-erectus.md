## Claim Statement

The Nefilim **did not create man from nothing**; they took an already-evolved hominid (*Homo erectus*) and genetically manipulated it — "bound upon it the image of the gods" — to produce *Homo sapiens*, modern man, about 300,000 years ago. Evolution and the creation accounts are thereby reconciled: man is a product of evolution *upgraded* by the gods.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sitchin: "The key ... lies in the tale of a slumbering Enki, aroused to be informed that the gods had decided to form an *adamu*, and that it was his task to find the means. He replied: 'The creature whose name you uttered — IT EXISTS!' and he added: 'Bind upon it' — on the creature that already exists — 'the image of the gods.'" Therefore "The Nefilim did not 'create' Man out of nothing; rather, they took an existing creature and manipulated it." And: "some time circa 300,000 years ago, the Nefilim took ape-man (*Homo erectus*) and implanted on him their own image and likeness."

He distinguishes the biblical "Adam" from the genus *Homo*: "'The Adam' of the Bible was not the genus Homo, but the being who is our ancestor — the first Homo sapiens." The mother-goddess **Ninhursag** carries out the process "following processes and formulas devised by Enki," after repeated trial-and-error efforts to "fashion a perfect Primitive Worker."

## Contexts Within the Source

Sitchin uses this to dissolve the supposed evolution-vs-creation conflict (Ch. 12 opening), and reads Genesis 2's "Let us make Man in our image" as a plural statement by the Nefilim.
