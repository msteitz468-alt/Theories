## Claim Statement

Sitchin argues that all of history and prehistory is structured by the interplay of three distinct modes of time: Divine Time (the orbital period of [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]], equal to 3,600 Earth-years, which is the Anunnaki's own year — the [[Shar (3,600-year orbital period)]]); Celestial Time (the precessional cycle, in which Earth's wobble causes the spring equinox to shift one zodiacal house — 30° — every 2,160 years, completing a "Great Year" of 25,920 years); and Earthly Time (the ordinary human calendar anchored to the solar year, lunar months, and the equinoxes). These three scales are not independent: Sitchin contends the ratio of 2,160 to 3,600 gave rise to the sexagesimal mathematical system, and that the Anunnaki devised the twelve-house zodiac precisely to create a workable bridge between their own Divine Time and human Earthly Time.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The book opens by framing the three-times schema as its central thesis. Sitchin writes that "since Mankind began to count Earthly Time, it has already experienced the measure of Celestial Time — the arrival of New Ages" (line 82). He then states the hierarchy succinctly: "For Mankind, Earthly Time began in 3760 B.C." (line 334), and "Between Earthly Time and Divine Time there is Celestial Time" (line 336).

Divine Time is anchored to [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]]: "The sar, 3,600 Earth-years, was the orbital period of Nibiru around the Sun; for anyone on Nibiru, it was just one Nibiru-year . . . The unit of that Divine Time, a year of the gods, was the sar" (line 200). Sitchin argues the Anunnaki solved the translation problem via precession: "Devising the division of the ecliptic into twelve — to conform to the twelve-member composition of the Solar System — the Anunnaki invented the twelve houses of the zodiac; that allotted to each zodiac house 30°, in consequence of which the retardation per house added up to 2,160 years (72 × 30 = 2,160) and the complete Precessional Cycle or 'Great Year' to 25,920 years" (line 346). The ratio of 2,160 to 3,600 is identified as "the 10:6 ratio between Divine Time and Celestial Time" and as the mathematical foundation of the sexagesimal system (line 3086).

Celestial Time moves counter to Earthly Time: "Celestial Time does flow backward, as far as we on Earth are concerned; and therefore, in zodiacal terms, the Past is the Future" (line 364). The interplay of all three scales is stated most directly in Chapter 13: when Marduk rewrote the Babylonian creation epic, he claimed it was he who "determined Divine Time by his orbit, Celestial Time by defining the constellations, and Earthly Time by giving Earth its orbital position and tilt" (line 3550). Earlier, Sitchin summarizes: "the calendar — the count of Divine, Celestial, and Earthly Time — played a major role in those events" (line 2898).

## Contexts Within the Source

This three-times framework is the organizing spine of [[When Time Began (Sitchin)]]. It explains why the Anunnaki built astronomically aligned observatories (to track all three simultaneously), why the shift between zodiacal ages such as the Bull and the Ram carried such enormous political and religious weight (Celestial Time governed divine authority), and why human calendar reforms — Sumerian, Egyptian, Mesoamerican, Andean — recurred in roughly 2,160-year intervals. It connects directly to [[Sitchin: the shift from the Age of the Bull to the Age of the Ram drove upheavals among gods and men]], which treats the Taurus-to-Aries transition as a concrete historical instance of Celestial Time overriding both Divine Time claims and Earthly political arrangements.

The [[Shar (3,600-year orbital period)]] is the irreducible unit of Divine Time, and [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]] is its physical basis. [[Marduk (Sitchin)]]'s attempt to seize supremacy was framed explicitly as a dispute over which god correctly read Celestial Time.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Sitchin acknowledges, at line 3086, that "even nowadays, no one can say for sure when one 'Age' has ended and the other begun." The mathematical calculation of 2,160 years per house is exact, but observational astronomy complicates it because "the zodiacal constellations are of varied sizes" and the Sun "can linger longer or shorter periods within them." This indeterminacy was itself, he argues, exploited by rival gods. Mainstream scholarship does not accept that the Anunnaki existed or that precession was invented to reconcile alien time-scales with human calendars; the entire framework is rejected by archaeologists, historians, and astronomers as speculative mythology.
