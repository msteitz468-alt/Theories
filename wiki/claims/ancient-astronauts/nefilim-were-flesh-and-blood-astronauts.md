## Claim Statement

The "gods" of the ancient Near East — the Sumerian DIN.GIR, the Anunnaki, and the biblical Nefilim and "angels" (malachim) — were not myth but **real, physical, technologically advanced humanoids who travelled to Earth from another planet** of our solar system. *Nefilim*, Sitchin insists, literally means "Those Who Were Cast Down" — those who descended to Earth — not "giants."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In the preface Sitchin recounts being reprimanded as a schoolboy for objecting to the gloss "giants": "didn't it mean literally 'Those Who Were Cast Down', who had descended to Earth?" He concludes: "Is it not now time, therefore, to accept the word of these same ancient records regarding the Nefilim as visitors to Earth from the heavens?"

Ch. 5 ("The Nefilim: People of the Fiery Rockets") argues the gods could "rise from Earth and ascend into the heavens," flying "after crossing heaven, crossing earth." The winged depictions are "not natural wings — but rather a decorative attachment to the god's clothing." Sitchin reads the Mari statue of Inanna/Ishtar as a goddess in flight-gear: a special **helmet with earphones**, **goggles**, a necklace of stones, a cylindrical object in hand, straps and an **instrument pack** on the back with a connecting hose — "the complete package of instruments." The SHU.GAR.RA helmet is glossed "that which makes go far into universe," and the attire "was that of an aeronaut or an astronaut." The biblical *malachim* ("emissaries") who visited Abraham and Lot were "divine airmen," instantly recognizable by their helmets/uniforms/weapons.

## Contexts Within the Source

Sitchin frames this as the resolution of the conflict between Evolution and the Bible: the gods who "descended" were physical beings, so scriptural and Sumerian "myth" can be read as literal history.
