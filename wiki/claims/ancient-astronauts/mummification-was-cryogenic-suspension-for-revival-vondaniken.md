## Claim Statement

Von Däniken argues that Egyptian mummification (and embalming/deep-freezing burial generally) was not merely a religious rite but an early attempt at **bodily preservation for future physical revival** — effectively a prehistoric anticipation of modern cryonics. He suggests the "gods" implanted the idea of "corporeal rebirth," promising to return from the stars and reawaken the well-preserved bodies, or that the dead awaited a future in which medical science could restore them to life.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Von Däniken stresses that Egyptian tombs show "the preparation of embalmed corpses for a corporeal return," provisioned for "a life on this side of the grave." He asks "whence came the first audacious idea that the cells of the body had to be preserved so that the corpse… could be awakened to new life after thousands of years?" and imagines the pharaoh reasoning: "The gods promised to return and wake me up (or doctors in the distant future will discover a way to restore me to life again)."

He explicitly links this to modern cryonics, citing **Robert C. W. Ettinger's** *The Prospect of Immortality* (1965), which "suggests a way in which twentieth-century men can have ourselves frozen so that our cells can go on living… but slowed down a billionfold," and notes "bone banks," blood and "living cells… stored almost indefinitely at the temperature of liquid nitrogen." His star exhibit: "In March, 1963, biologists of the University of Oklahoma confirmed that the skin cells of the Egyptian **Princess Mene** were capable of living. And Princess Mene has been dead for several thousand years!" He adds deep-frozen Inca glacier mummies "theoretically… capable of living," revived deep-frozen Russian dogs (1965), and the ice-packed Kurgan V graves near Outer Mongolia, asking whether "unknown intelligences already knew the methods with which to treat bodies so that they could be revived in so many thousand years."

## Contexts Within the Source

Offered in ch. 7 alongside the engineering of the pyramids as evidence that the "gods" possessed and transmitted advanced knowledge; it supports his broader thesis that [[Extraterrestrials visited Earth in prehistory and were worshipped as gods (von Däniken)]] and left technological traces — here the suggestion that the visitors meant to "preserve" leading figures in "artificial deep sleep" to question them on later return visits.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Von Däniken concedes uncertainty, asking "Am I dragging proofs in willy-nilly?" and admitting of the ice graves that any second-life motive "can scarcely be based on the finds in Mongolia," whose deep-freezing was "too much of this world." He calls why the ancients thought such preparation made "reawakening possible" "a puzzle for the time being," framing the whole as speculation requiring "confirmation from contemporary sources."
