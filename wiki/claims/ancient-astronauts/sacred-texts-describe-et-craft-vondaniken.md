## Claim Statement

Von Däniken argues that passages in ancient sacred texts — read literally rather than theologically — are eyewitness descriptions of spacecraft and extraterrestrial visitors, garbled by writers who lacked the vocabulary for technology.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His flagship example is the Book of **Ezekiel**: "Ezekiel gives precise details of the landing of this vehicle. He describes a craft that comes from the north, emitting rays and gleaming," with wheels, noise, and fire — which von Däniken reads as a powered flying machine, not a divine vision. He applies the same literalizing method to other "fiery chariots from the heavens," the "sons of God" who took human wives, and accounts of gods wielding destructive weapons, treating them as memories of real technological visitors.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Von Däniken presents these as provocative re-readings inviting scientific re-examination, conceding they overturn the religious interpretation; the readings are his, and have been widely contested.

## Contexts Within the Source

Scriptural evidence for [[Extraterrestrials visited Earth in prehistory and were worshipped as gods (von Däniken)]]. The same literalizing-of-scripture method is the engine of Paul Wallis's "Eden" books (e.g. reading Genesis's *elohim* as ET "Powerful Ones").
