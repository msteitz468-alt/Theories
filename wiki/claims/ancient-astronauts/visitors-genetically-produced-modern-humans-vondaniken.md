## Claim Statement

Von Däniken claims the visiting extraterrestrials deliberately intervened in human biology — "annihilated part of mankind existing at the time and produced a new, perhaps the first, homo sapiens" — i.e. that modern humanity is, in part, an engineered product of the visitors rather than purely the result of natural evolution.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His introduction states the assertion plainly: "I nevertheless proclaim that these 'strangers' annihilated part of mankind existing at the time and produced a new, perhaps the first, homo sapiens." He calls this "revolutionary" because "it shatters the base on which a mental edifice that seemed to be so perfect was constructed." He reads myths of gods "creating" or destroying men, and of "sons of God" mating with human women, as garbled memories of this intervention.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Von Däniken offers the genetic-intervention claim as a bold hypothesis he intends to argue, not a demonstrated fact, and concedes it overturns established science.

## Contexts Within the Source

A founding form of the ancient-astronaut "engineered humanity" idea, later given Sumerian detail by Sitchin ([[Man was created as a 'primitive worker' to relieve the mutinying Anunnaki of gold mining]]) and echoed by Wallis's "downgrade" thesis ([[Human latent abilities were genetically switched off by the ET engineers]]).
