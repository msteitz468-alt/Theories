**Claim Statement**  
The religious mythology and deity roles of pre-dynastic and dynastic Egypt were designed to secretly preserve astronomical data about the Sirius star system, specifically encoding Sirius A as Isis, the invisible Sirius B as Nephthys, and Sirius B's orbit as Anubis.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *The Sirius Mystery* (Chapter Two):
* **Isis (Ast) as Sirius A:** In Egyptian hieroglyphics and texts, the goddess Isis (Ast) is explicitly identified with Sothis (Sirius A), the brightest star in the sky, whose heliacal rising initiated the Egyptian New Year and Sothic calendar.
* **Nephthys as Sirius B:** Isis's sister-goddess Nephthys represents the "invisible" or "dark" companion of Sothis. Temple cites Plutarch's report (*On Isis and Osiris*, 368F) that Nephthys was regarded as the "invisible part of the world," while Isis was the "visible."
* **Anubis as the Orbit:** Anubis (the jackal- or dog-headed deity) is described by Plutarch as the "horizontal circle" that touches upon both light (Isis) and darkness (Nephthys), serving as the divider between them. Temple interprets this "circle" as the 50-year orbit of Sirius B around Sirius A.
* **The Satis/Anukis/Sothis Boat:** Sothis is depicted in Egyptian astronomical paintings sailing in a celestial boat accompanied by two other Sothic goddesses, Satis and Anukis, which Temple suggests represents a three-star Sirius system (Sirius A, B, and C).

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Temple argues that the Dogon did not receive their Sirius lore in isolation, but inherited it from Egypt. He notes that the Dogon mummification-style Sigui rites, their 70-day intervals, and their terminology (like the Bozo calling Sirius A "seated," which matches Isis's hieroglyph of a throne) indicate a direct cultural migration from pre-dynastic Nile valley civilizations.
