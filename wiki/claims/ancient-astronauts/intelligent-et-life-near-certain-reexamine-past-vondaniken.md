## Claim Statement

Von Däniken's opening premise and methodological call: given the vast number of stars and habitable planets, intelligent extraterrestrial life is statistically near-certain — so the visitation hypothesis is reasonable, and orthodox science must abandon its complacency and re-examine humanity's past for the visitors' traces.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 1 he marshals the statistical argument (citing Harlow Shapley and Willy Ley) to estimate thousands to millions of life-bearing or inhabited planets — "even this last supposition gives our Milky Way the vast number of 18,000 inhabited planets." Methodologically he insists "modern laboratories must take over the work of archaeological research," that "archaeologists must visit the devastated sites of the past with ultrasensitive measuring apparatus," and that "priests who seek the truth must again begin to doubt everything that is established."
