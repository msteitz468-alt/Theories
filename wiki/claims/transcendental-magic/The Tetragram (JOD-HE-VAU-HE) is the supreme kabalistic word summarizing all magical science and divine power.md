## Claim Statement
Lévi asserts that a single four-letter word — the Hebrew Tetragram JOD-HE-VAU-HE (JODHEVA), identified also with the alchemists' Azoth and the Bohemians' Taro/Tarot — comprehends "all things," expresses God to the profane and man to philosophers, and gives adepts "the final term of human sciences and the key of divine power," though its holder must never reveal it.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
In the Introduction: "A single word comprehends all things, and this word consists of four letters: it is the Tetragram of the Hebrews, the Azot of the alchemists, the Thot of the Bohemians, or the Taro of the Kabalists. This word, expressed after so many manners, means God for the profane, man for the philosophers, and imparts to the adepts the final term of human sciences and the key of divine power; but he only can use it who understands the necessity of never revealing it."

In Chapter IV: "The Kabalistic Tetragram, JODHEVA, expresses God in humanity and humanity in God." Lévi states the "incommunicable axiom is enclosed kabalistically in the four letters of the Tetragram, arranged in the following manner: in the letters of the words Azoth and Inri written kabalistically; and in the monogram of Christ as embroidered on the Labarum, which the Kabalist Postel interprets by the word Rota, whence the adepts have formed their Taro or Tarot." He adds: "All magical science is comprised in the knowledge of this secret... To know it and have the courage to use it is human omnipotence; to reveal it to a profane person is to lose it."

A footnote by Waite notes the Zoharic Kabalists actually read ADONAI for the Name rather than pronouncing it, qualifying Lévi's claim about kabalistic usage.

## Contexts Within the Source
The Tetragram doctrine is Lévi's organizing device for the whole Doctrine, tying together the Kabalah, alchemy (Azoth), and the Tarot (Taro) as expressions of one master secret; Waite's footnotes repeatedly flag the scheme as internally inconsistent, noting that the correspondence of Tarot Trumps, Hebrew letters, and chapter numbers "gives rise to... difficulties".

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Waite's footnote to Chapter I states that Lévi's scheme correlating the 22 Tarot Trumps, Hebrew letters, and Roman letters to the Doctrine's 22 chapters produces "stultifying" results, e.g., the Roman letter I equated with Hebrew TETH.
