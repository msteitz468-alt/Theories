## Claim Statement
Lévi divides all revelations about the historical "Sabbath" into three classes: (1) a purely fantastic and imaginary Sabbath, dreamed under narcotic unguents; (2) the secret assemblies of true adepts of "luminous Magic," descended from the persecuted mystery cults of antiquity (Isis, Ceres Eleusinia, Bacchus, the Bona Dea, Druidism); and (3) the criminal, foolish gatherings of actual Black Magic practitioners. He derives the name from the Jewish Sabbath, calling it "the Sunday of Kabalists," and describes the initiatory Sabbath of the adepts as featuring a blindfolded candidate led through frightening trials to a final choice — to kiss the goat's hindquarters — after which, if he consents, he discovers not an obscene idol but "the young and gracious countenance of a priestess of Isis or Maia."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The revelations made on the subject may be classified under three heads: 1. those referring to a fantastic and imaginary Sabbath; 2. those which betray the secrets of certain occult assemblies of veritable adepts; 3. revelations of foolish and criminal gatherings, having for their object the operations of Black Magic."

On the drugged, dreamed Sabbath: Baptista Porta's recipe "of child's fat, aconite boiled with poplar leaves and some other drugs, the whole mixed with soot" for the "sorcerers' unguent."

On the initiatory Sabbath of adepts: "The candidate for the Sabbath was led or rather carried to the assembly, his eyes covered... He was passed before great fires... surrounded by infernal monsters and in the presence of a colossal and hideous goat which he was commanded to adore... The candidate received a brusque command to kiss respectfully the posterior of the goat. If he refused... he was transported to a distance... If he agreed, he was taken round the symbolical idol, and there found... the young and gracious countenance of a priestess of Isis or Maia, who gave him a sisterly salute, and he was then admitted to the banquet."

On the etymology: "The Sabbath was therefore the Sunday of Kabalists, the day of their religious festivals... As to the diabolical Sabbath of necromancers, it was a counterfeit of that of the Magi, an assembly of malefactors who exploited idiots and fools."

On the German offshoot: "The Rites of the Gnostic Sabbath were imported into Germany by an association which took the name of Mopses. It replaced the kabalistic goat by the Hermetic dog... the candidates... were required abruptly to choose between kissing the posterior of the Grand Master and that of a small silk-covered figure of a dog."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi presents this typology as correcting the demonological literature of Sprenger, Delancre, Delrio, and Bodin, whose accounts he treats as records of the third, criminal category mistaken for or conflated with the second. He explicitly separates the "orgies" some Gnostic sects practiced from the discipline of "veritable adepts," listing figures he holds to have been chaste (Apollonius of Tyana, the Emperor Julian, Plotinus, Paracelsus, Raymund Lully) as evidence that true initiation and licence are incompatible.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Waite's footnote states flatly: "It must be understood that there are no historical records of these Sabbaths of Adepti and that Lévi has arrived at his account of their alleged proceedings by eliminating the abominations of the Black Sabbath" — i.e., the "luminous" Sabbath is Lévi's own reconstruction, not an attested tradition. Waite also rejects the Mopses connection: "It is idle nonsense to say that the Mopses revived the Rites of the Gnostic or any Sabbath... The Order of Mopses was either a mock-Masonry or a silly substitution... when a Papal Bull was in force against the Order."
