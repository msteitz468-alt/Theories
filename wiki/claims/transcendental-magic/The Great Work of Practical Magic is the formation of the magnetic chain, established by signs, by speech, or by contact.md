## Claim Statement
After the education of the will and the personal creation of the Magus, the Great Work in Practical Magic is "the formation of the magnetic chain" — a secret proper to priesthood and royalty, whereby a current of ideas is originated that produces faith and draws many wills into a shared circle of action. The chain may be established by three methods: by signs (e.g., the Sign of the Cross, the Masonic square), by speech (eloquence, "chains of gold" issuing from the mouth of Hermes), or by direct physical contact (hand-clasps, circular dances such as the witches' Sabbath round-dance, tilting tables).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"THE Great Work in Practical Magic, after the education of the will and the personal creation of the Magus, is the formation of the magnetic chain, and this secret is truly that of priesthood and of royalty. To form the magnetic chain is to originate a current of ideas which produces faith and draws a large number of wills in a given circle of active manifestation. A well-formed chain is like a whirlpool which sucks down and absorbs all. The chain may be established in three ways — by signs, by speech and by contact."

"The magic chain of speech was typified among the ancients by chains of gold, which issued from the mouth of Hermes. Nothing equals the electricity of eloquence.... Peter the Hermit convulsed Europe by his cry of 'God wills it!'"

"The third method of establishing the magic chain is by contact.... The direct and positive grasp of hand by hand completes the harmony of dispositions.... The great circular dance of the Sabbath, which concluded the mysterious assemblies of adepts in the Middle Ages, was a magic chain: it joined all in the same intentions and the same acts."

"The phenomena of tilting and talking tables have been fortuitous exhibitions of fluidic communication by means of the circular chain."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi frames the chain as the mechanism behind historically effective mass phenomena — Christian conversion by the Sign of the Cross, the "miraculous medal," the vision of Alphonse de Ratisbonne, Napoleon's electrifying speech to his army, Proudhon's paradox "Property is robbery" — and states that printing itself is "an admirable instrument for the formation of the magic chain by the extension of speech," linking his own work to "the great magical chain which began with Hermes or Enoch."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Lévi distances himself from spiritist explanations of table-turning: "we do not believe that it has been in any sense proven" that elementary spirits intervene in such phenomena, attributing the effects instead to fluidic communication and shared imagination.
