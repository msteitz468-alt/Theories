## Claim Statement
Lévi presents Johannes Trithemius's treatise De Septem Secundiis ("Of the Seven Secondary [Intelligences]... spirits moving the spheres after God") as "a key of all prophecies," dividing the entire history of the world into successive reigns of the seven planetary angels of the Apocalypse, each lasting exactly 354 years and 4 months, beginning with Orifiel (Saturn) at the Creation on 13 March: Orifiel (savagery/darkness), Anaël/Venus (love, family, the first cities, culminating in the Deluge), Zachariel/Jupiter (land disputes, founding of towns and empires), Raphael/Mercury (science, invention of writing — hieroglyphic, the Book of Enoch/Thoth, the Tarot), Samael/Mars (corruption preceding the universal deluge), Gabriel/Moon (rebirth after Babel), and Michael/Sun (first dominions, the empire of Nimrod's children, birth of sciences and religions). Trithemius's calculations placed the next reign of Michael/Sun — inaugurating a universal political-religious empire under "him who holds the keys of the East" and lasting another 354 years and 4 months — at November 1879.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"'De Septem Secundiis', id est intelligentiis sive spiritibus orbes post Deum moventibus. It is a key of all prophecies new or old... The duration of each angelic reign is 354 years and 4 months, beginning with that of Orifiel, the angel of Saturn, on 13 March, for, according to Trithemius, this was the date of the world's creation."

"Next came the reign of Anaël, the spirit of Venus, on 24 June, in the year of the world 354, when love began to be the instructor of mankind... presently the exaltation of poetry produced religion, fanaticism and debauchery, culminating subsequently in the deluge."

"[Raphael's reign brought] letters... invented, the first language being hieroglyphic and universal, a monument of which has been preserved in the Book of Enoch, Cadmus, Thoth and Palamedes, the kabalistic clavicle adopted later on by Solomon... the Rota of the Kabalists, the Tarot of Magi and Bohemians."

"His exact calculations lead him to the month of November in the year 1879, epoch of the reign of Michaël and the foundation of a new universal kingdom... We see therefore according to this calculation, that in 1879 — or in twenty-four years' time — a universal empire will be founded and will secure peace to the world. This empire will be political and religious... and will endure for 354 years and 4 months, after which it will be succeeded by the return of the reign of Orifiel."

"All peoples will acknowledge and follow in four-and-twenty years the standard of France, ever victorious or miraculously raised from the dead. Such is the prophecy of Trithemius, confirmed by all our previsions and rooted in all our hopes."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi treats the prophecy with unqualified enthusiasm, calling Trithemius's work "more comprehensive and independent than that of Bossuet" and describing it as "a key absolute to the philosophy of history." He explicitly folds in his own nationalist hope that France would lead the coming Solar empire.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Waite's footnotes are sharply skeptical: "his supposed 'key of all prophecies' by no means deserves the hectic enthusiasm of Éliphas Lévi and has been stultified sufficiently by the events post 1879," and separately, "After all we prefer Bossuet. De Septem Secundiis does little honour to the great name of Trithemius." Waite also notes bluntly regarding the France prophecy: "that which followed this forecast was the year 1870 and the crushing defeat of Sedan."
