## Claim Statement
Lévi closes the Ritual's Introduction by identifying the five-pointed Pentagram (the "Blazing Star") and the Seal of Solomon as the two great "signatures" of equilibrated Astral Light corresponding to the human body's magnetic polarity, capable of enchaining rebellious spirits by proving their folly, while a broken or incorrectly drawn Pentagram signifies "astral intoxication" and the "Signature of Lucifer" in its perverted sense.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"This star was the magical Pentagram, having a sacred letter at each point. It is the symbol of intelligence which rules by unity of force over the four elementary potencies... it represents the Grand and Supreme Athanor of Nature, which is the body of man. The magnetic influence issues in two rays from the head, from either hand and either foot. The positive ray is balanced by the negative."

"This ruling sign of equilibrated light represents the spirit of order and harmony; it is the sign of the omnipotence of the Magus, and hence, when broken or incorrectly drawn, it represents astral intoxication, abnormal and ill-regulated projections of Astral Light and therefore bewitchments, perversity, madness—all that, in a word, which the Magi term the Signature of Lucifer."

"There is another signature which also symbolizes the Mysteries of Light, namely, the Sign of Solomon, whose talismans bear on one side the impression of his seal... and on the other...the hieroglyphic theory of the composition of magnets and represents the circulatory law of the lightning." "Rebellious spirits are enchained by the exhibition of the five-pointed Blazing Star or the Seal of Solomon, because each gives them proof of their folly." Lévi warns that an "ignorant operator" using these signs without understanding "is like a blind man who discourses of light to the blind."

## Contexts Within the Source
This closes the Introduction's theological framing and transitions directly into the practical Ritual chapters (Chapter I follows), summarized in Lévi's closing formula: "In order to DARE we must KNOW; in order to WILL, we must DARE; we must WILL to possess empire and to reign we must BE SILENT."
