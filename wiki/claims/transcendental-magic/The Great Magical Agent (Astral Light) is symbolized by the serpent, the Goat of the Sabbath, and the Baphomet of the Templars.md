## Claim Statement
The Great Magical Agent — described as "the double current of light, the living and astral fire of the earth" — was represented in ancient theogonies by "the serpent with the head of an ox, goat or dog." This same force is identified across traditions as the dual serpent of the caduceus, the serpent of Genesis, the brazen serpent of Moses (twined about the Tau/generating lingam), the Goat of the Sabbath, the Baphomet of the Templars, the Hyle of the Gnostics, and the double-tailed serpent forming the legs of the solar cock of Abraxas. Lévi denies personal existence to the devil, describing this "blind force" instead as a natural, misdirected current that souls must overcome through will in order to be freed, rather than a conscious evil being.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"In this place let us affirm without evasion that the Great Magical Agent—the double current of light, the living and astral fire of the earth—was represented by the serpent with the head of an ox, goat or dog, in ancient theogonies. It is the dual serpent of the caduceus, the old serpent of Genesis, but it is also the brazen serpent of Moses, twined about the Tau, that is, the generating lingam. It is, moreover, the Goat of the Sabbath and the Baphomet of the Templars; it is the Hyle of the Gnostics; it is the double tail of the serpent which forms the legs of the solar cock of Abraxas."

"We have said that there is no personal devil. It is a misdirected force, as the name indicates. An odic or magnetic current, formed by a chain of perverse wills, constitutes this evil spirit, which the Gospel calls legion."

"The whole magical work consists therefore in our liberation from the folds of the ancient serpent, then in setting foot upon its head and leading it where we will."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi frames the initiate's task as analogous to Ulysses resisting Circe: "Circe is Nature, with all her delights and allurements: we must overcome her in order to enjoy." He states the "natural medium" (Astral Light/serpent force) can only be governed by "an extra-natural mediator, which is an emancipated will," and compares the Magus's needed fulcrum to Archimedes' point outside the world.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Lévi qualifies his denial of a personal devil conditionally in a cited footnote from *Le Grand Arcane*: "Should, however, a decision of some future council affirm that the devil exists personally, I should defer because of my principles... when it shall have affirmed not only the real but personal existence of the devil, the devil will exist personally; the Roman Church will have created it." This is presented within the source as Lévi's own qualifying position, not an external critique.
