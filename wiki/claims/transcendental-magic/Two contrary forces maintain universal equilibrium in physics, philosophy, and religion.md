## Claim Statement
Lévi teaches that the universe is governed by two opposing but non-contradictory forces — attraction and repulsion, mercy and justice, Jakin and Boaz — whose balance ("magical equilibrium") produces stability in physics, criticism in philosophy, and progressive revelation in religion; violation of this equilibrium, however slight, destroys it, while its maintenance is a precondition for magical success.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter II: "the universe is balanced by two forces which maintain it in equilibrium, being the force which attracts and that which repels. They exist alike in physics, in philosophy and in religion; in physics they produce equilibrium, in philosophy criticism, in religion progressive revelation." Lévi lists mythic images of the pairing: "the conflict between Eros and Anteros, the struggle between Jacob and the angel... the caduceus of Hermanubis, by the two cherubim of the ark, by the twofold sphinx of the chariot of Osiris and by the two seraphim — respectively black and white."

He explicitly denies dualism: the "undiscerning disciples of Zoroaster divided the duad without referring it to unity, thus separating the Pillars of the Temple and endeavouring to halve God." Good and evil are not "contrary" principles (as "the disciples of Manes" held) but placed by "a singular wisdom" so that "the supreme excellence is above both, applying evil to the victory of good and good to the amendment of evil" (Chapter III).

Chapter VI states directly: "Equilibrium is exact; fulfil its law, and it is there; violate it, however slightly, and it is destroyed... Magical equilibrium is hence one of the first conditions of success in the operations of science."

The two Pillars are named: "BOAZ and JAKIN are the names of the two symbolical Pillars before the principal entrance of Solomon's Kabalistic Temple. In the Kabalah these Pillars explain all mysteries of antagonism, whether natural, political or religious."

## Contexts Within the Source
This doctrine of the duad/equilibrium is presented as the second stage (after the monad/unity of Chapter I) in Lévi's numerological progression toward the triad, tetrad, and so on, and is explicitly tied to gender symbolism (man/woman, active/passive) and to the Kabalistic Sephiroth Geburah (Severity) and Chesed (Mercy).
