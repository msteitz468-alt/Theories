## Claim Statement
Opening the Ritual, Lévi presents an allegory identifying "the Hebrews" name for the "just and terrible force which destroys abortions eternally," Samael/Satan/Lucifer, and quotes at length an unnamed "Gnostic gospel" in which Lucifer is the first-formed Intelligence who freely chose suffering and liberty over unfree bliss — "The Lucifer of the Kabalah is not an accursed and ruined angel; he is the angel who enlightens, who regenerates by fire."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The just and terrible force which destroys abortions eternally was called by the Hebrews Samael; by other easterns, Satan; and by the Latins, Lucifer. The Lucifer of the Kabalah is not an accursed and ruined angel; he is the angel who enlightens, who regenerates by fire; he is to the angels of peace what the comet is to the mild stars." Lévi develops this via an extended allegory of a "fixed star" pleading with a wandering "comet" to rejoin celestial harmony, and the comet's reply: "God hath appointed my path, even as thine... I am the messenger of the suns... I am the proscribed, the eternal wanderer, who has infinity for domain."

He then quotes "A Gnostic gospel, discovered in the East by a learned traveller of our acquaintance": Intelligence, "saluted by heaven under the name of Lucifer," on hearing God's word "Let there be light," replies "I will not be slavery," is told "Then shalt thou be suffering," and answers "I will be liberty... I needs must strive with death to conquer life." The text continues: "hell was indispensable to the happiness of heaven... Never would Intelligence have known the goodness of God if it had never lost Him."

Lévi ties this to the Genesis serpent, read kabalistically via Fabre d'Olivet's method: the Hebrew word Nahash (serpent) signifies, in Lévi's interpretation, the Universal Magical Agent under its names OD (active), OB (passive), and Aour (equilibrated) — "that old serpent which encircles the world and places its devouring head beneath the foot of a Virgin."

## Contexts Within the Source
This Introduction precedes the practical Ritual chapters and functions as a theological framing statement, closing with an extended affirmation of Christ as "priest and king by excellence" and a description of the Pentagram/Blazing Star and the Seal of Solomon as signatures of "equilibrated light" used to command spirits.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Waite's footnote states the quotation's source and endpoint are Lévi's own invention or at least unverifiable: "In the text of Éliphas Lévi, owing to his confused arrangement, it is impossible to say where this alleged quotation ends. The point at which I have closed it is quite speculative."
