## Claim Statement
Before undertaking serious magical work, the operator (Magus) must purify himself, "without and within," for forty days. This purification consists of renouncing coarse enjoyments, adopting a temperate and vegetarian diet, abstaining from intoxicating drink, regulating sleep, maintaining scrupulous external cleanliness of body, clothing, furniture and vessels, and keeping the work entirely secret from others, disguising true researches under a pretended occupation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"He who decides to devote himself seriously to magical works, after fortifying his mind against all danger of hallucination and fright, must purify himself without and within for forty days. The number forty is sacred, and its very figure is magical."

"The purification of the Magus consists in the renunciation of coarse enjoyments, in a temperate and vegetarian diet, in abstinence from intoxicating drink, and in regulating the hours of sleep."

"As already stated, the most scrupulous external cleanliness must be observed: the poorest person can find spring water. All clothes, furniture and vessels made use of must be also washed carefully, whether by ourselves or others. All dirt is evidence of negligence, and negligence is deadly in Magic."

"We must divulge to no one the works that we accomplish, for, as specified in our 'Doctrine', mystery is the exact and essential condition of all operations of science. The inquisitive must be misled by the pretence of other occupations and other researches... but the forbidden name of Magic must never be pronounced."

Lévi also specifies daily practice: "Rise daily at the same hour, and that early; bathe at a spring before daybreak, and in all seasons; never wear soiled clothes: wash them yourself at need; practise voluntary privations."

## Contexts Within the Source
This is the opening instruction of the "Ritual" portion of the book, framed as the "first and most indispensable preparation of the operator," prerequisite to the exercise of will that Lévi treats as the true foundation of magical power. Waite's footnote observes there is "no general law of this kind in the procedure of Ceremonial Magic," suggesting Lévi's forty-day rule is his own formulation rather than a transcription of an older grimoire requirement.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Waite's editorial footnote states: "It should be understood that Éliphas Lévi is laying down a rule of his own manufacture. There is no general law of this kind in the procedure of Ceremonial Magic, though in particular cases there may be a preliminary purification extended over forty days." Waite further notes there is "no authority for either of these figures" regarding Lévi's numerological reading of forty in Arabic and Roman numerals.
