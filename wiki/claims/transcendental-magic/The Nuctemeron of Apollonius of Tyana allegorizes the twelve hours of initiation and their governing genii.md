## Claim Statement
Lévi presents, translated and interpreted "for the first time," the Nuctemeron ("the day of the night, or the night illumined by day") attributed to Apollonius of Tyana — a Greek text first published by Gilbert Gautrimis and reproduced by Laurentius Moshemius (Amsterdam, 1721) — describing twelve symbolic "Hours" of cosmic/initiatory allegory (e.g., First Hour: demons praising God in unity and losing their malice; Fourth Hour: souls revisiting tombs amid enchantments; Ninth Hour: "the number which must not be divulged"; Twelfth Hour: "the works of the light eternal fulfilled by fire"). Lévi's explanation equates the twelve Hours with the twelve labors of Hercules and with sequential stages of magical initiation (overcoming passions, studying balanced natural forces, triadic initiation, conquering illusion, understanding elemental harmony, becoming fearless, directing magnetic light, calculating causes and effects, respecting the hierarchy of mystery and silence, studying astronomy, learning the laws of universal life, and effecting works of Nature through light). Each Hour is further assigned seven named "genii" — understood by Lévi's "ancient hierophants" as "neither angels nor demons, but moral forces or personified virtues" — governing specific faculties (e.g., Papus, physician; Rasphuia, necromancer; Pharzuph, genius of fornication; Tukiphat, genius of the Schamir).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"NUCTEMERON signifies the day of the night or the night illumined by day... This monument of transcendent Assyrian Magic is sufficiently curious to make it superfluous to enlarge on its importance. We have not merely evoked Apollonius, we have possibly resuscitated him."

"The First Hour: In unity, the demons chant the praises of God: they lose their malice and fury... The Ninth Hour: The number which must not be divulged... The Twelfth Hour: The works of the light eternal are fulfilled by fire."

Explanation: "These twelve symbolical hours, analogous to the signs of the magical Zodiac and to the allegorical labours of Hercules, represent the succession in works of initiation. It is necessary therefore (1) To overcome evil passions... (9) To understand the hierarchy of instruction, to respect the mysteries of dogma and to keep silence in presence of the profane... (12) To effect great works of Nature by direction of the light."

On the genii: "Here follow the names and attributions of the genii who preside over the twelve hours of the Nuctemeron. By these genii the ancient hierophants understood neither angels nor demons, but moral forces or personified virtues." Ninth Hour genii include "SABLIL, genius who discovers thieves... ZEFFAR, genius of irrevocable choice," with the explanation: "This number, says Apollonius, must be passed over in silence, because it contains the great secrets of the initiate."

On the SCHAMIR (Eighth Hour): "TUKIPHAT, genius of the SCHAMIR... The SCHAMIR is an allegorical diamond which in magical traditions represents the stone of the sages... The Arabs still say that the SCHAMIR, originally given to Adam and lost by him after his fall, was recovered by Enoch and possessed by Zoroaster; and that Solomon received it afterwards from an angel."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi places the Nuctemeron as the capstone Supplement to the entire "Ritual" volume, presenting his translation as a scholarly and magical recovery act ("we have possibly resuscitated him"). He explicitly parallels it in the same Supplement with "The Nuctemeron According to the Hebrews," treating the two texts as independent confirmations of the same underlying numerical philosophy.
