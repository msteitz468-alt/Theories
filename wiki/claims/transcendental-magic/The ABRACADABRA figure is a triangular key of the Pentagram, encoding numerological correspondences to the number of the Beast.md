## Claim Statement
The "magic triangle of pagan theosophists" was the celebrated word ABRACADABRA, arranged as a descending triangular figure in which the initial letter A is repeated in a pattern giving specific numbers of elements. Lévi assigns kabbalistic meanings to its letters (A = unity/first principle, AB = fertilization of the duad by the monad, R = the triad as emission from the union of two principles), and derives further numbers: 11 (letters in the word) combined with the Pythagorean denary, and 66 (sum of all the letters) reducing kabbalistically to 12, "the square of the triad." He further claims the author of the Apocalypse formed 666, the number of the Beast, by adding 6 to "the double senary of ABRACADABRA," linking it to Tarot card XVIII (the Moon) and to the number 9 as "the key" of initiation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The magic triangle of pagan theosophists was the celebrated ABRACADABRA, to which they attributed extraordinary virtues... This combination of letters is a key of the Pentagram. The initial A is repeated five and reproduced thirty times, thus giving the elements and numbers of the two following figures."

"The isolated A represents the unity of the first principle, otherwise, the intellectual or active agent. A united to B represents the fertilization of the duad by the monad. R is the sign of the triad, because it represents hieroglyphically the emission which results from the union of the two principles. The number 11, which is that of the letters of the word, combines the unity of the initiate with the denary of Pythagoras, and the number 66, the added total of all the letters, form kabalistically the number 12, which is the square of the triad and consequently the mystic quadrature of the circle."

"We may remark, in passing, that the author of the Apocalypse... composed the number of the beast, that is to say, of idolatry, by adding a 6 to the double senary of ABRACADABRA, which gives 18 kabalistically, the number attributed in the Tarot to the hieroglyphic sign of night and of the profane—the moon... a mysterious and obscure number, the kabalistic key of which is 9, the number of initiation."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi presents this as an example of the practical Kabbalah's twin disciplines of Gematria and Themura, asserting that "the absolute kabalistic alphabet, which connected primitive ideas with allegories, allegories with letters, and letters with numbers, was then called the Keys of Solomon" — which he identifies with the Tarot, crediting Court de Gébelin as the first modern scholar to recognize this.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Waite's footnote is skeptical of the entire numerological exercise: "This 'discovery' may be commended to mathematicians—if any—who are drawn towards 'occult science'... The question is whether even a professed Magus could put it forward seriously."
