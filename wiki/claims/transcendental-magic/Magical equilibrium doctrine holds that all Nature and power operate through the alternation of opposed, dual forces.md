## Claim Statement
Equilibrium arises from two forces; if perfectly equal, the result is immobility (a negation of life), so movement instead requires "alternate preponderance" between opposed principles. All Nature is "bisexual," structured by paired contraries (light/shadow, void/plenitude, active/passive), and knowledge of the law of alternation between such forces constitutes "the first principles of the Great Magical Arcanum." The human body itself operates by a dual, androgyne magnetism, attracting and radiating, and the alternate use of contrary forces (warmth after cold, love after anger) is described as the actual secret of sustained magical power.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"EQUILIBRIUM is the consequence of two forces. If two forces are absolutely and invariably equal, the equilibrium will be immobility and therefore the negation of life. Movement is the result of an alternate preponderance."

"All life is composed of an inspiration and a respiration; creation postulates a shadow to serve as a bound for light, of a void to serve as space for the plenitude, of a passive fructified principle to sustain and realize the power of the active generating principle. All Nature is bisexual."

"The human body is subject, like the earth, to a dual law, it attracts and it radiates; it is magnetized by an androgyne magnetism and reacts inversely on the two powers of the soul, the intellectual and sensitive."

"The alternate use of contrary forces, warmth after cold, mildness after severity, love after anger, etc., is the secret of perpetual motion and the permanence of power... To operate always on the same side and in the same manner is to overweight one basin of the balance, and complete destruction of equilibrium is a rapid result."

The magical androgyne on the frontispiece of the "Ritual" bears "SOLVE inscribed upon the right and COAGULA on the left arm," recalling "the symbolical architects of the Second Temple, who bore the sword in one hand and their trowel in the other."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi frames equilibrium as scientifically confirmable through "electric or magnetic phenomena" such as galvanic action between copper and zinc, insisting "ever will the Kabalist explain the discoveries of science." He connects the doctrine to the Pentagram's sympathetic correspondences between the body's extremities (Chapter II), and states a magical operation should always be followed "by a rest of equal length and a distraction analogous but contrary in its object."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
None presented; Waite's footnote merely cross-references Lévi's related statement in *Le Grand Arcane* ("the Supreme Secret is that of unfettered rule over equilibrated movement") without disputing the doctrine itself.
