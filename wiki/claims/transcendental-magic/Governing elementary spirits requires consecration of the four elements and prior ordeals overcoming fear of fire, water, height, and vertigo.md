## Claim Statement
The four elements (air, fire, water, earth) separate created spirits — Sylphs, Salamanders, Undines, and Gnomes — disengaged from the Astral Light by the universal movement of Nature. These elementary spirits are compared to children, without free will and hence irresponsible for the good or evil they cause, but usable by more powerful human wills; the Magus who employs them bears full moral responsibility for their effects. To become "king of the occult elements," the operator must have undergone the four ordeals of ancient initiations, or their modern substitutes: exposure to fire, crossing an abyss on a plank, scaling a mountain in a storm, and swimming a dangerous whirlpool — since inferior spirits will only obey a power that has already overcome them in their own element. Each element has its own cardinal direction, ruling temperament, commanding sign, and sovereign spirit (Gob for Gnomes, Djin for Salamanders, Paralda for Sylphs, Nicksa for Undines), and each is consecrated separately by specific breathings, asperging materials, incense, and spoken names before any conjuration.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"To govern elementary spirits and thus become king of the occult elements, we must first have undergone the four ordeals of ancient initiations; and seeing that such initiations exist no longer, we must have substituted analogous experiences, such as exposing ourselves boldly in a fire, crossing an abyss by means of the trunk of a tree or a plank, scaling a perpendicular mountain during a storm, swimming through a dangerous whirlpool or cataract. A man who is timid in the water will never reign over the Undines; one who is afraid of fire will never command Salamanders."

"It must be borne in mind that the special kingdom of Gnomes is at the north, that of Salamanders at the south, that of Sylphs at the east, and that of Undines at the west. These beings influence the four temperaments of man; that is to say, the Gnomes affect the melancholy, Salamanders the sanguine, Undines the phlegmatic and Sylphs the bilious... Their respective sovereigns are Gob for the Gnomes, Djin for the Salamanders, Paralda for the Sylphs and Nicksa for the Undines."

"Elementary spirits are like children: they torment chiefly those who trouble about them, unless indeed they are controlled by high reason and great severity... they are without free will and are hence irresponsible... Therefore the Magus who makes use of them assumes a terrible responsibility, for he must expiate all the evil which he causes them to accomplish."

"Fire is exorcised by the sprinkling of salt, incense, white resin, camphor and sulphur; by thrice pronouncing the three names of the genii of fire: MICHAEL, king of the sun and lightning; SAMAEL, king of volcanoes; and ANAEL, prince of the Astral Light."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi links contemporary "phenomena established by the criminal trials of Magic" and the work of M. Eudes de Mirville to these elemental spirits, and connects the four elements to divination methods (aeromancy, pyromancy, hydromancy, geomancy) tied to the four temperaments, framing "second sight" as a natural faculty operating through abstraction of the ordinary senses in the Astral Light.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
None presented as internal contradiction; Waite's footnotes are mainly bibliographic (tracing the Prayer of the Sylphs to *Le Comte de Gabalis*, 1680) rather than disputing the doctrine.
