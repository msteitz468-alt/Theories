## Claim Statement
Lévi presents the Kabalah as the symbolical summary of a primitive book of hieroglyphs (attributed variously to Enoch, Hermes Trismegistus, or Cadmus), resting on the one dogma of Magic — "the visible is for us the proportional measure of the invisible" — and expressed through the ten Sephiroth (numbered principles) and the Sacred Tetragram, the fourfold Divine Name.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"This book...was the symbolical summary of primitive tradition, called subsequently Kabalah or Cabala, meaning reception. The tradition in question rests altogether on the one dogma of Magic: the visible is for us the proportional measure of the invisible." Lévi derives the Sephiroth from a doctrine of dual attributes in the First Cause — "stability and motion, necessity and liberty...justice and love" — personified as GEBURAH and CHESED, balanced by the Supreme Crown, MALKUTH.

He enumerates the ten Sephiroth in full: "1. KETHER...2. CHOKMAH...3. BINAH...4. CHESED...5. GEBURAH...6. TIPHERETH...7. NETSAH...8. HOD...9. JESOD...10. MALKUTH," calling these "the ten primary notions attached to the ten first characters of the primitive alphabet, signifying both principles and numbers." He states these ten notions plus the twenty-two Tarot cards "form what the Kabalists term the thirty-two paths of absolute science," further divided into "fifty gates," and into Bereshith (universal Genesis) and MERCAVAH (the Chariot of Ezekiel), yielding GEMATRIA and TEMURAH as the "Notary Art."

On the devil within this system: "in symbolism itself the demon is an angel cast out of heaven for having sought to usurp divinity," and philosophically "the devil is a human idea of divinity, which has been surpassed and dispossessed of heaven by the progress of science and reason."

## Contexts Within the Source
This chapter opens the Kabalah as a unifying key underlying the rest of the Doctrine — Lévi immediately connects the Sephiroth to the Tarot's structure of twenty-two cards and four suits, treating the two systems as a single hieroglyphic alphabet.
