## Claim Statement
Lévi teaches a doctrine of "realization" in stages — a thought is realized by becoming speech, then imprinted on the Astral Light by writing or speech, then translated into acts that modify the world — such that no act, word, curse, or blessing is without consequence, since all are permanently registered ("It is the word made flesh") in the Astral Light and react upon their author and others by natural, quasi-electrical law.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The realization of speech is the logos properly so called. A thought is realized in becoming speech; it is realized also by signs, sounds and representations of signs: here is the first degree of realization. Then it is imprinted on the Astral Light by means of the signs of writing or speech; it influences other minds by reflections upon them... it is translated into acts and modifies the world: this is the last degree of realization. Men who are born into a world modified by an idea bear away with them the mark thereof, and it is thus that the word is made flesh."

"There are no isolated acts, and it is impossible that there should be secret acts; whatsoever we will truly — that is, everything which we confirm by our acts — remains registered in the Astral Light, where our reflections are preserved... Curses and blessings produce their consequence invariably, and every action, whatsoever its nature, whether inspired by love or hate, has effects analogous to its motive, its extent and its direction."

Lévi gives historical examples of this "law of solidarity" as literal cause of death: "the terrible visitations of Nadab and Abihu, of Osa, of Ananias and Sapphira, were occasioned by electric currents of outraged convictions"; he attributes the deaths of Marat, Charles IX, and the poet Gilbert, and the near-ruin of Henry IV, to the same principle — being struck down or sustained by public sympathy or reprobation functioning as a literal "electric current."

## Contexts Within the Source
This doctrine follows the account of the Astral Light as the medium of "magnetic breathing," in which "the Universal Agent... ever seeks equilibrium; it fills the void and draws in the plenitude, which makes vice contagious... and works powerfully in the proselytism of virtue."
