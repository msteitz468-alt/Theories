## Claim Statement
Lévi describes an "exceedingly simple method of finding celestial horoscopes by onomancy": cut a person's name into a black card, view the sky through a tube fitted with the card, and note which stars are visible through the letters at each of the four cardinal points in turn (east for childhood, south for youth, west for maturity, north for decline). The letters and star-counts are converted to numbers and summed in Hebrew, then the resulting star names are matched to an Egyptian planisphere of genii (recommending the atlas of Dupuis) to determine fortunate and unfortunate influences. He applies the same technique historically: the Hebrew Kabalists allegedly read the destruction of Greece, the fall of Jerusalem's temple (calculated to 423 years), the destruction of Persia and Assyria (208 years), the division of Alexander's empire (284 years, PARAD), and the decline of the Ottoman Empire (word CAAH, sum 1025) directly from vertical star-alignments, and interprets Belshazzar's MANE THECEL PHARES as the same onomantic intuition applied to lamps rather than stars.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"Take a black card; cut therein the name of the person for whom you wish to make the consultation; place this card at the end of a tube which must diminish towards the eye of the observer; then look through it alternately towards the four cardinal points, beginning at the east and finishing at the north. Take note of all the stars which you see through the letters; convert these letters into numbers... A good example of the planisphere will be found in the atlas to the great work of Dupuis."

"A short time before the sack of Jerusalem and its temple by Nebuchadnezzar, the Kabalists remarked eleven stars disposed in the following manner vertically above the temple... The sum of the number of the letters is 423, exactly the period of the duration of the temple."

"So also four stars announced to the kabalistic rabbins of another epoch the fall and division of the empire of Alexander; they entered into the word... PARAD = to divide = 284, the number of this word, indicating the entire duration of the said empire."

"The MANE THECEL PHARES which Belshazzar, in his intoxication, saw written on the wall of his palace by the glare of torches, was an onomantic intuition similar to that of the rabbins... Belshazzar went to work mechanically and instinctively upon the lamps of his nocturnal feast, as he would upon the stars of heaven."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi attributes the source of these traditions to "Rabbi Chomer, Rabbi Kapol, Rabbi Abjudan and other masters in Kabalah" via Gaffarel, and closes the chapter by cautioning that such calculations have value "only" when accompanied by "magnetic intuitions," being otherwise "puerile... and completely arbitrary."
