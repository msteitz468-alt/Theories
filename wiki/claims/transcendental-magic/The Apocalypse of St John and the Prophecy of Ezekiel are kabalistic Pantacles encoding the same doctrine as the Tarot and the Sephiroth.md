## Claim Statement
The five books of Moses, the Prophecy of Ezekiel, and the Apocalypse of St John are "the three kabalistic keys of the whole Biblical edifice." Their bizarre figures — the sphinxes of Ezekiel, the wheels within wheels, the Initiator among seven candlesticks, the Woman clothed with the Sun, the angel with pillars of fire for legs, the beast with seven heads — are hieroglyphics comprising a series of Pantacles, each correlated to the Sephiroth (Malkuth/Briah/Yetzirah/Atziluth) and to Masonic symbolism (the Pillars Jakin and Boaz). Commentators who seek historical persons (Napoleon, Luther, Voltaire) encoded in these images, or who calculate the number 666 against celebrated names, are said to waste their effort.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The five books of Moses, the Prophecy of Ezekiel and the Apocalypse of St John are the three kabalistic keys of the whole Biblical edifice. The sphinxes of Ezekiel are identical with those of the sanctuary and the ark, being a fourfold reproduction of the Egyptian Tetrad; the wheels revolving in one another are the harmonious spheres of Pythagoras."

"The apparently bizarre figures presented by the Apocalypse of St John are hieroglyphics, like those of all oriental mythologies, and can be comprised in a series of Pantacles. The Initiator clothed in white, standing between seven golden candlesticks and holding seven stars in His hand, represents the unique doctrine of Hermes and the universal analogies of the light. The Woman clothed with the Sun and crowned with twelve stars is the celestial Isis, or the Gnosis... The mighty angel with the face of a sun, a rainbow for nimbus and a cloud for vestment, having pillars of fire for his legs... is truly a kabalistic PANTHEA. His feet represent the equilibrium of Briah... his legs are the two Pillars of the Masonic Temple, JAKIN and BOAZ; his body... is the sphere of YETZIRAH... his solar head, crowned with the radiant septenary, is the world of ATZILUTH, or perfect revelation."

"The commentators who have sought anything else in this book of the transcendent Kabalah have lost their time and their trouble only to render themselves ridiculous. To discover Napoleon in the angel Apollyon, Luther in the star which falls from heaven, Voltaire or Rousseau in the grasshoppers armed like warriors, is merely high fantasy."

## Contexts Within the Source
Presented as the culmination of the chapter's argument that Pantacles are the universal key of all ancient and modern mythologies, extending the earlier discussion of the Genesis symbols (the Tree, the four rivers, the serpent, the ark, Cain and Abel) into the New Testament.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
A translator's footnote observes that "the arrangement of the Tree of Life in Kabalism passes into utter confusion" here, suggesting Lévi meant the angel's feet to represent Malkuth (external forms) rather than Briah as stated in his own text — an internal inconsistency the source itself does not resolve.
