## Claim Statement
Lévi teaches that the triad — "an intelligent principle, a speaking principle and a principle spoken" — is the "perfect word" and the universal dogma manifested identically as the Christian Trinity, the threefold structure of alchemy (azoth, incorporation, transmutation), the Kabalistic hierarchy of worlds, and the human family (father, mother, child); the two triangles of this doctrine, combined, form the six-pointed Seal of Solomon.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The perfect word is the triad, because it supposes an intelligent principle, a speaking principle and a principle spoken... These two triangles, combined in a single figure, which is the six-pointed star, form the sacred symbol of Solomon's Seal, the resplendent Star of the Macrocosm."

"The triad is the universal dogma. In Magic — principle, realization, adaptation; in alchemy — azoth, incorporation, transmutation; in theology — God, incarnation, redemption; in the human soul — thought, love and action; in the family — father, mother and child."

On the necessity of three rather than one or two: "Were God only one He would never be Creator or Father. Were He two there would be antagonism or division in the infinite... He is therefore three for the creation by Himself and in His image of the infinite multitude of beings and numbers. So is He truly one in Himself and triple in our conception."

Lévi also links the triad to the trigrams of Fohi (I Ching) and to the three worlds: "There are three intelligible worlds which correspond one with another by hierarchic analogy; the natural or physical, the spiritual or metaphysical, and the divine or religious worlds."

## Contexts Within the Source
Presented as the logical successor to the duad/equilibrium doctrine of Chapter II, resolving apparent dualism (Manichaeism, the disciples of Zoroaster) into a governing unity-in-triplicity, and linked explicitly to Christian Trinitarian dogma, which Lévi treats as philosophically necessary rather than merely revealed.
