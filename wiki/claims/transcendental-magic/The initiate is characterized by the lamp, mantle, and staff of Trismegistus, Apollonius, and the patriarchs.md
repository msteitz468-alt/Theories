## Claim Statement
Lévi defines the initiate as "he who possesses the lamp of Trismegistus, the mantle of Apollonius, and the staff of the patriarchs" — respectively symbolizing reason illuminated by science, self-possession isolating the sage from blind tendencies, and the support of Nature's secret forces — granting him knowledge of past, present, and future, discretion, and strength, summarized as: "He knows, he dares and is silent."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The initiate is he who possesses the lamp of Trismegistus, the mantle of Apollonius, and the staff of the patriarchs. The lamp of Trismegistus is reason illuminated by science; the mantle of Apollonius is full and complete self-possession, which isolates the sage from blind tendencies; and the staff of the patriarchs is the help of the secret and everlasting forces of Nature. The lamp of Trismegistus enlightens present, past and future, lays bare the conscience of men and manifests the inmost recesses of the female heart."

On divination as a privilege of initiation: "divination is simply the knowledge of effects contained in causes and science applied to the facts of the universal dogma of analogy... every man bears about him the history of his life, which is legible for the initiate."

On the initiate's temperament under persecution: "So may you find him often melancholy, never dejected or despairing; often poor, never abject or miserable; persecuted often, never disheartened or conquered." Lévi lists a lineage of persecuted adepts the initiate remembers: "the desolation in which Agrippa died... the great Paracelsus... Raymond Lully... Swedenborg simulating madness... Saint-Martin and his hidden life; Cagliostro, who perished forsaken in the cells of the Inquisition; Cazotte, who ascended the scaffold."

## Contexts Within the Source
This chapter closes the Doctrine's numerological sequence (number nine, "the number of divine reflections... the number of initiation"), synthesizing the fourfold formula of Chapter I (to know, to dare, to will, to keep silence) into the figure of the initiate.
