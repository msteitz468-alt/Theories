## Claim Statement
Lévi holds that genuine astrology derives from "the unique and universal dogma of the Kabalah" (the sephirotic doctrine of seven spheres and three mobiles), was later degraded into vulgar superstition by "genethliacal soothsayers," and properly functions not as fatalistic prophecy but as "the scientific and magical calculus of probabilities," since human liberty can override astral influence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"True astrology, that which derives from the unique and universal dogma of the Kabalah, became profaned among the Greeks and Romans of the decline... all this has been materialized and degraded into superstition by genethliacal soothsayers and erecters of horoscopes during the decline and the Middle Ages."

The mechanism: the Astral Light "receives and preserves the impressions of all visible things," so "the daily position of the heaven is reflected in this light," which "transmit[s] to the plastic and indeterminate temperament of a newly-born child the atmospheric impressions and diverse influences" present at birth. "The true adept in astrology must give heed...to the precise time and place of the birth," calculating "the chances of estate," but "will have also to take into consideration human liberty and its initiative, should the child eventually come to be a true man and to isolate himself by strength of will from fatal influences." Lévi concludes: "we do not allow too much to astrology, but so much as we leave it is indubitable: it is the scientific and magical calculus of probabilities."

He credits Paracelsus as "perhaps the last of the great practical astrologers," curing "diseases by talismans formed under astral influences," and cites Gaffarel's celestial planisphere (in which "the constellations form Hebrew letters") as the basis for chiromancy and physiognomy, "the art of reading the writing of the stars in the lines of the hand" and face. He also reports Jerome Cardan's climacteric calculation method (using year-intervals of 4, 8, 12, 19, and 30) as personally verified by Lévi for the year 1855.

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi links astrology's degeneration to the same historical process — the loss of the primitive kabalistic key — described for the Tarot and Kabalah in Chapter X, and connects it forward to phrenology ("The final word of phrenology is to be found therefore in scientific and purified astrology").
