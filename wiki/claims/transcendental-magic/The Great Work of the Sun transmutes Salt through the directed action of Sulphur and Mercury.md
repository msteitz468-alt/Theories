## Claim Statement
Lévi identifies the "Work of the Sun" — Hermes' name for the Great Work — with the discovery of the Absolute across religion, philosophy, and "metallic transmutation," embodied in the Philosophical Stone, which is "decomposed by analysis and recomposed by synthesis," existing as powder before synthesis and as stone after. He gives the alchemical mechanics via Raymond Lully ("before we can make gold we must have gold... wealth is not created absolutely: it is increased and multiplied") and Geber (Sublimation as "the elevation of the dry substance by means of fire, with adherence to its proper vessel"): Salt is "absolute matter," present in everything; Sulphur is the Living Fire/intellectual energy; Mercury (AZOTH, the Astral Light) is the universal fructifying agent; the operation converts Salt to gold through the combined, will-directed action of Sulphur and Mercury, sometimes instantaneously and sometimes over years, and the same Stone, recovered synthetically, is the "PANACEA" curing all diseases of soul and body.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"To discover such Absolute in the infinite, the indefinite and the finite, is the Great Work of the sages, that which is termed by Hermes the Work of the Sun... This Stone is both one and manifold: it is decomposed by analysis and recomposed by synthesis. In analysis it is a powder, the alchemical powder of projection; before analysis and in synthesis it is a stone."

Lully: "before we can make gold we must have gold. Out of nothing we can make nothing; wealth is not created absolutely: it is increased and multiplied."

"Sublimation, which is nothing less, according to Geber, than the elevation of the dry substance by means of fire, with adherence to its proper vessel."

"It is AZOTH, universal magnesia, the Great Magical Agent, the Astral Light, the light of life, fertilized by animic force, by intellectual energy, which they compare to Sulphur on account of its affinities with Divine Fire. As to Salt, it is absolute matter. All that is material contains Salt, and all Salt can be converted into pure gold by the combined action of Sulphur and Mercury."

"To know how to extract from all matter the pure Salt which is concealed in it is to possess the secret of the Stone... Obtained by analysis, it may be termed the UNIVERSAL SUBLIMATE; recovered by the synthetic way, it is the veritable PANACEA of the ancients, for it cures all diseases, whether of soul or body."

On the goat/dog hieroglyphs: "the sages ascribed to their Mercury, personified in Egypt as Hermanubis, a dog's head, and to their Sulphur, represented by the Baphomet of the Temple or Prince of the Sabbath, that goat's head which brought such odium upon the occult associations of the middle ages."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi presents Baron Tschoudy's Hermetic Catechism in The Blazing Star, said to derive from a Paracelsus manuscript in the Vatican Library transcribed by Sendivogius, as the clearest surviving key to this doctrine, and directs advanced students to the "Emerald Table" and the Tarot's kabalistic alphabet for full initiation.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Waite's footnote disputes the transmission chain Lévi asserts: "There is nothing in the work of Baron Tschoudy to indicate that he drew from the source mentioned by Lévi, nor am I acquainted with any evidence for the statement that Sendivogius transcribed the Vatican MS."
