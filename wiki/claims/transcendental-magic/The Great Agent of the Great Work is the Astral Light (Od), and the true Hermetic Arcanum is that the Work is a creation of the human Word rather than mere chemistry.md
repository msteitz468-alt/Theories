## Claim Statement
Sulphur (fire), Mercury (air/water), and Salt (earth), volatilized and fixed alternately, compose the AZOTH of the sages, which is identified with the Great Agent of the solar work described in the Hermetic "Emerald Table": "the Od of the Hebrews and the Astral Light." Lévi claims to reveal, "for the first time clearly and devoid of mystical figures," that "dead substances" are bodies as found in Nature while "living substances" are those magnetized by the operator's science and will — making the Great Work "an actual creation of the human Word initiated into the power of the Word of God Himself," rather than a chemical operation alone.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"For such, gold and silver are the Sun and Moon... Hermetic art is, therefore, at one and the same time, a religion, a philosophy and a natural science... for the Great Agent of the solar work is that force described in the Hermetic Symbol of the 'Emerald Table': it is universal magical power; it is the igneous spiritual motor; it is the Od of the Hebrews and the Astral Light, according to the expression which we have adopted in this work."

"Here then is the great Hermetic Arcanum, and we reveal it for the first time clearly and devoid of mystical figures: that which the adepts term dead substances are bodies as found in Nature; living substances are those which have been assimilated and magnetized by the science and will of the operator. Therefore the Great Work is something more than a chemical operation: it is an actual creation of the human Word initiated into the power of the Word of God Himself."

"Raymund Lully states that in order to make gold we must have gold and mercury... 'By Mercury, I understand that mineral spirit which is so refined and purified that it gilds the seed of gold and silvers the seed of silver.' Doubtless he is here speaking of OD, or Astral Light."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi supports the claim by citing a Hebrew text he says he located in a rare copy (preserved since 1643 in the Protestant church at Rouen) of the rabbinical commentator Abraham's occult commentary on the Sepher Yetzirah — the reputed source of Nicholas Flamel's knowledge.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
The translator (Waite) inserts an extended footnote stating this "Hebrew Arcanum" was later shown by Lévi's own subsequent writing to demonstrate nothing new, and that Lévi's later gloss reducing the secret to "magnetized electricity" is "a proposition from the world of nonsense" — an internal correction the translator attributes to Lévi's own later text (La Clef des Grands Mystères).
