## Claim Statement
Lévi states that he personally performed an evocation using the Pentagram, following the ceremonial preparations described in the corresponding Ritual chapter, on 24 July 1854, repeated it a second and third time, and obtained results he characterizes as "truly extraordinary, but positive and unmixed with hallucination."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"On 24 July in the year 1854, the author of this book, Éliphas Lévi, made an experiment of evocation with the Pentagram, after due preparation according to all the Ceremonies indicated in the thirteenth chapter of the 'Ritual'. The success of this experiment, details of which, as regards its principles, will be found in the corresponding chapter of the present doctrinal part, establishes a new pathological fact, which men of true science will admit without difficulty. The experience, repeated even to a third time, gave results truly extraordinary, but positive and unmixed with hallucination. We invite sceptics to make a conscientious and intelligent trial on their own part before shrugging their shoulders and smiling."

He adds that the Pentagram figure he used "is more perfect than any in the Keys of Solomon or in the Magical Calendars of Tycho Brahe and Duchentau," and warns that "the use of the Pentagram is most dangerous for operators who are not in possession of its complete and perfect understanding."

## Contexts Within the Source
Presented as empirical corroboration of the theoretical doctrine of the Pentagram and the Astral Light set out earlier in the same chapter; Lévi frames it as a "pathological fact" akin to a scientific observation rather than a supernatural claim, consistent with Waite's later characterization (in the Biographical Preface) of Lévi as fundamentally a materialist who explained occult phenomena through natural law rather than literal spirit intervention.
