## Claim Statement
Lévi teaches that the Great Magical Agent (the Astral Light) is controlled by two operations — concentration/isolation and projection/distribution — and that a "Magic Chain" of persons linked in fixed practice and shared enthusiasm generates a magnetic current, which grows stronger with the extent of the chain and can sweep up impressionable individuals, explaining phenomena from religious conversion to table-turning.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"The Great Magical Agent, by us termed the Astral Light...is the key of all empire, the secret of all power...To have control of the Great Magical Agent there are two operations necessary—to concentrate and project, or, in other words, to fix and to move." Isolation "consists in absolute independence of thought, complete liberty of the heart and perfect continence of the senses," exemplified by Pythagoras, Apollonius of Tyana, Julius Caesar, Paracelsus, Raymond Lully, Jerome Cardan, and Agrippa.

"To make the Magic Chain is to establish a magnetic current which becomes stronger in proportion to the extent of the chain," with Mesmer's trough cited as "a chain of an exceedingly imperfect kind," and unnamed "circles of illuminati" and an unnamed Catholic religious association cited as more potent examples.

Lévi attributes the conversion of Saul on the road to Damascus and of "the young Israelite, Alphonus of Ratisbon" to being "struck down by a formidable magnetic flash." He states Jean-Jacques Rousseau's desertion of his children resulted from "the magnetic influence of a libertine circle and a magical current of table-d'hôte," as Rousseau "describes...in his Confessions." Contemporary phenomena of "table-turning and fluidic manifestations" are attributed to the same cause: "the Universal Magnetic Agent in search of a chain of enthusiasms."

Lévi also recounts personal night-time phenomena following magic-chain experiments "performed with persons devoid of good intention or sympathy" — books moved, blows on the ceiling, a sensation of an unknown hand attempting to strangle him — leading him toward the evocation experiments detailed in the thirteenth chapter.

## Contexts Within the Source
Presented as the practical mechanism underlying religious, revolutionary, and mesmeric mass phenomena alike; Lévi frames genius and "great circles" as mutually producing one another via this same current.
