## Claim Statement
Lévi asserts that there exists in Nature a single Universal Agent — the Astral Light — immeasurably more powerful than steam, having equilibrium for its supreme law, known to the ancients under many names, and constituting the physical basis of all magical, mesmeric, and religious phenomena.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
In the Introduction: "there exists in Nature a force which is immeasurably more powerful than steam, and a single man, who is able to adapt and direct it, might change thereby the face of the whole world. This force was known to the ancients; it consists in a Universal Agent having equilibrium for its supreme law... This agent, which barely manifests under the uncertain methods of Mesmer's followers, is precisely that which the adepts of the Middle Ages denominated the First Matter of the Great Work. The Gnostics represented it as the fiery body of the Holy Spirit; it was the object of adoration in the Secret Rites of the Sabbath and the Temple, under the hieroglyphic figure of Baphomet."

In Chapter IV (The Tetragram): "The Great Magical Agent manifests by four kinds of phenomena, and has been subjected to the experiments of profane science under four names — caloric, light, electricity, magnetism. It has received also the names of TETRAGRAM, INRI, AZOTH, ETHER, OD, Magnetic Fluid, Soul of the Earth, Lucifer, etc."

In Chapter V (The Pentagram): "the Astral Light, or terrestrial fluid, which we call the Great Magnetic Agent, is saturated with all kinds of images or reflections. Now, our soul can evoke these, and subject them to its DIAPHANE."

In Chapter VI (Magical Equilibrium): the Astral Light is described as "the universal seducer, typified by the serpent of Genesis," which "lends itself to good as to evil; it transmits light and propagates darkness; it may be called equally Lucifer and Lucifuge."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi presents this as the master-key underlying all of occult philosophy — physics, mesmerism, Kabalah, alchemy, and Christian doctrine (the Holy Spirit, redemption) are all treated as different names or manifestations of this one agent, subject to the law of equilibrium (see [[Two contrary forces maintain universal equilibrium in physics, philosophy, and religion]]).
