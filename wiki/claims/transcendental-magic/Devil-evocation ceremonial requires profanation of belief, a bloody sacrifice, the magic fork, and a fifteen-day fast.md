## Claim Statement
Lévi sets out a "Magical Axiom" — "Within the circle of its action, every word creates that which it affirms" — with the "Direct Consequence" that "he who affirms the devil creates or makes the devil." He then lists the conditions required to evoke the devil successfully (invincible obstinacy, a hardened yet fearful conscience, ignorance, blind faith in the incredible, and a false idea of God) and a detailed grimoire ceremonial: profanation of one's own cultus, a bloody sacrifice, the fabrication of a hazel-or-almond "magic fork," a fifteen-day fast of unsalted black bread and blood, periodic drunkenness on a hemp-and-poppy wine strained through a prostitute's cloth, and an elaborate arrangement of gruesome instruments (candles of human fat, a copper vase of the victim's blood, coffin nails, a cat's head, a drowned bat, goat's horns, a parricide's skull) within a blood-colored circle and triangle.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"MAGICAL AXIOM: Within the circle of its action, every word creates that which it affirms. DIRECT CONSEQUENCE: He who affirms the devil creates or makes the devil."

"Conditions of Success in Infernal Evocations (1) Invincible obstinacy; (2) a conscience at once hardened to crime and most prone to remorse and fear; (3) affected or natural ignorance; (4) blind faith in all that is incredible; (5) an utterly false idea of God."

"We must afterwards (1) profane the ceremonies of the cultus in which we believe; (2) offer a bloody sacrifice; (3) procure the magic fork, which is a branch of a single bough of hazel or almond, cut at one blow with the new knife used for the sacrifice... A fast of fifteen days must be observed, taking a single unsalted repast after sundown... We must get drunk every five days after sundown on wine in which five heads of black poppies and five ounces of pounded hemp-seed have been steeped for five hours, the infusion being strained through a cloth woven by a prostitute."

On the site and instruments: "A solitary and forbidden spot must be chosen, such as a cemetery haunted by evil spirits... A black seamless and sleeveless robe must be provided; a leaden cap... two candles of human fat... a magical sword with a black handle; the magical fork; a copper vase containing the blood of the victim... four nails taken from the coffin of an executed criminal; the head of a black cat which has been nourished on human flesh for five days; a bat drowned in blood; the horns of a goat... and the skull of a parricide."

On the evoking formula: "The Grand Appellation of Agrippa consists only in these words: Dies Mies Jeschet Boenedoesef Douvema Enitemaus. We make no pretence of understanding their meaning; possibly they possess none... since they avail in evoking the devil, who is the sovereign unreason."

On the resulting pact: "Evocations were followed frequently by pacts written on parchment of goat skin with an iron pen and blood drawn from the left arm... one copy was carried off by the fiend and the other swallowed by the wilful reprobate."

## Contexts Within the Source
Lévi presents this ceremonial polemically, as a demonstration that devil-evocation is logically self-defeating (belief in a "creative devil, who is also rival of God" is required before the ritual can function at all) and morally degrading, rather than as an endorsement. He frames the whole apparatus as "the most atrocious mysteries of the Grimoire."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Waite's footnote cautions readers "not to suppos[e] that there is any authority in the records for these diabolical evocations, taken as a whole. Part of the account comes from Grimoires and MSS. of Ceremonial Magic, but Lévi has supplied most of the pictorial effects by his own inventive genius." Waite also identifies Lévi's cited "Grand Appellation of Agrippa" as drawn from "the so-called Fourth Book of Philosophia Occulta, attributed falsely to Cornelius Agrippa," and separately criticizes Lévi for classing the Enchiridion of Leo III "with one of the worst Rituals of Black Magic, to which it by no means belongs."
