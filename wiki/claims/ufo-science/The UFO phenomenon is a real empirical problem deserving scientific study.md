## Claim Statement

Hynek argues that, once misperceptions and hoaxes are removed, there remains a genuine residue of UFO reports made by competent, psychologically normal witnesses that cannot be explained — and that this "UFO phenomenon" is a legitimate scientific problem that has been wrongly neglected. He is explicit that he does *not* know what UFOs are: "I cannot presume to describe… what UFOs are because I don't know; but I can establish beyond reasonable doubt that they are not all misperceptions or hoaxes."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Drawing on twenty-plus years as the Air Force's astronomical consultant — access to "all the reports in the Blue Book files" and "many hundreds of witnesses" — Hynek defines his terms precisely: a **UFO Report** is a credible witness's account of a perception "that does not specify any known physical event, object, or process"; the **UFO Phenomenon** is the class of such reports; **UFOs** are their "existential correlates, if any." He lists possible explanation-classes (undiscovered items obeying known physics, items obeying unknown physics, or even non-physical/psychological ones) and refuses to presuppose the extraterrestrial hypothesis. He pointedly distinguishes this from journalistic "flying saucers" and from the contactee "cults," which he dismisses.
