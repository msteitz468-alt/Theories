## Claim Statement

Hynek argues that both the scientific establishment and the Air Force refused to examine UFO data honestly — scientists dismissing the subject a priori as "unscientific" (the "laughter of science"), and the Air Force's investigation degenerating into public-relations debunking rather than genuine study. "Science is not always what scientists do."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He recounts his own arc from confident debunker to serious investigator, and how Project Sign (1947) became Project "Grudge" (1949) as "the Pentagon began to treat the subject with subtle ridicule," issuing "cryptic news releases" while withholding cases from public examination. He quotes William James on the "ingrained naturalism and materialism of mind" that worships "Science" and kills disfavored ideas by calling them "unscientific." He is especially critical of the Air Force-sponsored **Condon Report** (University of Colorado), used to justify closing Blue Book in 1969 — publishing administrative assistant Mary Lou Armstrong's resignation letter and William Powers's suppressed critique as evidence the study was biased toward a negative conclusion.
