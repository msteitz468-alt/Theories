## Claim Statement

Jose — a pseudonymous former US Marine and Pasulka's former undergraduate student, who grew up near
Roswell, New Mexico — reports that his encounters with aerial phenomena (including an orange-red orb,
black atmospheric objects, and dark entities) are not isolated UFO events but natural features of a
life imbued with spiritual meaning. He frames them within a worldview of ángeles y demonios (angels
and demons) and ongoing spiritual warfare, drawing on Charismatic Christianity, Catholic
sacramentals, and the philosophy of [[Pierre Teilhard de Chardin]]. He explicitly dislikes
the militarised UFO narrative and considers public hysteria around the topic harmful to the deeper
spiritual encounter it actually represents.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Pasulka recounts Jose's experiences across Chapters 5 and 6 of [[Encounters (Pasulka)]]. Key
episodes include:

- Age 3, San Antonio: Jose reports *el demonio negro* — a shape-shifting black entity with glowing
  eyes that grabbed him from a mattress. He could not move or speak and eventually passed out.
- Artesia, NM (childhood): After the family's conversion and move, Jose reports developing a
  "knowing" — spontaneous inner information, including foreknowledge of his mother's cancer — which
  he attributes to prayer and protection from God.
- Church (Artesia): Jose witnesses repeated episodes of congregants apparently possessed and then
  delivered of demons by pastors, which he takes as evidence of "another, invisible reality."
- His mother's death (young adult): Three days after her passing, Jose reports that *los demonios
  negros* returned in force — "floating, moving, with contrasting and shifting blackness." His father
  performed an oil-anointing rite of exorcism, after which, Jose reports, they never came back.
- Ongoing: Jose regularly experiences a "knowing" that prompts him to look up, whereupon he sees
  aerial anomalies (orbs, cartoon-like smudged aircraft shapes in clouds). He shares a phone photo
  with Pasulka. He considers these encounters connected to his sense of mission but refuses to
  discuss them publicly under the UFO label.

Direct quotation: "There is another, invisible reality, and some of the things, let's call them
demonios, they intend to harm you."

Pasulka notes: "Jose's attitude toward the UFOs he often 'feels' and then sees is what UFO
encounters most often look like. They are not like the encounters represented in the media … this is
why Jose doesn't talk about the topic. He doesn't want his spiritual experiences of the UFO
encounters to be appropriated and used as a means to bolster the military UFO."

## Contexts Within the Source

Jose's framing is placed within Pasulka's broader argument (Ch. 6) that [[Contact with nonhuman intelligence (Pasulka)|UFO contact]] is intrinsically a religious and gnostic experience. His case parallels those of [[J. Allen Hynek]] and [[Jacques Vallée]] — who practiced Rosicrucianism and kept extensive libraries on angelology — as instances of "spontaneous esotericism" outside institutional affiliation. See also [[Gnosis and the religious dimension of UFO contact (Pasulka)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Pasulka is careful to present Jose's account phenomenologically. She does not assert that the
demonic entities or the aerial phenomena are objectively real; she records what Jose reports and
believes. She acknowledges that José grew up in an environment saturated with violence, charismatic
religion, and cartel-adjacent trauma, contexts that Pasulka neither dismisses nor treats as
sufficient explanation for his experiences.
