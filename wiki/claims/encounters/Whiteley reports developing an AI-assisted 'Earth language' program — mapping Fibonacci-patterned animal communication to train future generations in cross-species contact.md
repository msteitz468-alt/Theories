## Claim Statement

Dr. Iya Whiteley (see [[Iya Whiteley]]) reports that she is developing a program to use AI to identify shared mathematical codes (Fibonacci sequence / golden ratio patterns) across insect, frog, bird, and whale sounds, map them onto 2D/3D visual graphemes, and then expose babies and young children to these sound-image pairings to cultivate synesthetic neural pathways that may enable communication with nonhuman intelligence. She describes this project as building the "Earth language" — a substrate of communication that she believes already surrounds humanity but has not yet been decoded or taught. Pasulka frames this as the contemporary equivalent of a seventeenth-century French manuscript's instructions for communicating with dolphins via a flute: each era's technology becomes the bridge to nonhuman minds.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Whiteley is quoted describing the project:

> "I have been developing these ways so we can encode and decode communication and prepare our children's brains and cognition for connection and communication with other species and beings, and in the process graduate as a humanity to a better connection with Earth."

On synesthesia: "I hypothesize that the Earth language is the language that has components of synesthesia that would allow for specific connections between visual and vibrational forms."

On the AI pipeline: "The plan is to use AI to further determine the similarities and innate codes among the calls and songs of insects, frogs, birds, and whale sounds. It is also worth exploring the NASA database of the sounds found in our solar system and space."

On the Fibonacci pattern: "All patterns that I have uncovered in visual and audio form conform to the famous embedded elements of the Fibonacci sequence, which is also passed on through generations by Aboriginal traditions in Australia."

On acoustic convergence: "The cricket sounds like a frog, the frog sounds like a bird, and the bird sounds like a whale. Even more interesting is that when I listen to the Earth's magnetic field and whilst processing the sound, I noticed, it sounds like birds!"

Pasulka's seventeenth-century manuscript framing: a French abbot's manuscript discovered at a coastal monastery described three conditions for human-dolphin communication — sincere intention, complete focus, and the ability to operationalize the communication through action. The technology was a flute. Pasulka concludes: "The seventeenth century's technological language bridge was the flute."

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 2 presents Whiteley's Earth language project as the concrete applied instantiation of [[AI as a medium for nonhuman contact (Pasulka)]]. ## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

This is an aspirational and in-progress program, not a completed study. Pasulka presents Whiteley's hypothesis as speculative but rigorously motivated. Whiteley's framework is described as crossing cognitive engineering, space psychology, biomimetics, indigenous knowledge, and AI — making it interdisciplinary but also difficult to evaluate within any single discipline's standards.
