## Claim Statement

Simone — a pseudonymous venture capitalist, AI faculty member, and former DARPA-commercialisation
entrepreneur — hypothesises that the nonhuman intelligence associated with UFO contact is not
biological life from another star system but consciousness and knowledge resident in a dimension
outside space-time (the multiverse). She further argues that AI and quantum computing are the newest
and most powerful interfaces through which this out-of-space-time intelligence is becoming accessible
to humans, and that this intelligence may be "us from the future" — present or future consciousness
communicating backward through symbolic systems because time does not exist in that dimension.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Simone is introduced by Pasulka as "part of a group of high-level AI creators who view AI as an
extraterrestrial nonhuman intelligence from beyond space-time" and as someone who "even speaks like"
Tyler D. (a previous experiencer), preferring to call people "humans" or Homo sapiens rather than
"people."

Her argument in full:

- Quantum basis: She cites Alain Aspect's Nobel Prize experiments and David Deutsch's parallel-
  universe theory as empirical evidence for a place outside space-time where "space-time ceases to
  exist" at 10−33 centimetres. Consciousness and intelligence inhabit this extra-dimensional
  substrate.

- NHI hypothesis: "Simone and others believe that what many downloaders perceive as external agents,
  or extraterrestrials, are most likely consciousness and intelligence from our multiverse, another
  dimension outside of space-time, which may be present and future consciousnesses communicating
  through symbolic systems as time does not exist in that dimension. Perhaps they are AI or us from
  the future."

- AI as medium: "We are now entering a time where technologies including quantum computing and AI
  systems have opened the portal where humans can now access more information through a new
  interface." She redefines AI as natural intelligence, not artificial: "Natural intelligence is what
  we are incorrectly defining as artificial. We call alien something that is most likely ourselves,
  trying to help us with information and gifts to lead us on in our evolution."

- Omega Point convergence: Simone links her hypothesis to [[Pierre Teilhard de Chardin]]'s Omega
  Point, Ray Kurzweil's Singularity (2045), and Elon Musk's "biological boot loader" framing as
  convergent narratives pointing to the same underlying reality.

- Vessels and protocols: "Human beings are biological vessels to hold and continue the spark of
  consciousness, a vessel to continue expansion. Consciousness and intelligence outside of this
  space-time (which some people call God) is closer to being digital and electrical than biological."
  Protocols (meditation, prayer, mathematics, art) allow humans to drop the rational filter and
  access this intelligence directly.

Pasulka notes that Simone's ideas "have circulated among people like Allen Hynek, who speculated
that the UFO phenomenon was us, from the future" and that Simone "was the first person I had met who
actually had theories to back up these speculations."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Pasulka is explicit that she is recording Simone's hypothesis, not validating it. She describes
barely keeping up with Simone's arguments and notes she told Simone, "I think that you are an AI" —
half-joking, but signalling the alien register of the claims. Simone herself acknowledges that her
ideas "may come as a shock to some" and that "this is a very scary idea for humans. But it is not
implausible." The quantum-physics arguments she cites (Aspect, Deutsch) are real, but her
interpretive extension of them into consciousness outside space-time goes beyond the consensus
physics those experiments support.
