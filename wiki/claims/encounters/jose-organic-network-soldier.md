## Claim Statement

Jose reports that in Afghanistan he became aware of an organic information network connecting him to
the mental, physical, and emotional states of his fellow Marines and to knowledge about the terrain —
a network he only later identified with [[Pierre Teilhard de Chardin]]'s concept of the noösphere.
He describes this network as what kept him alive, and he teaches its cultivation as a public-health
intervention for youth and veterans today. He further asserts that UFO phenomena appear specifically
when a person is attuned to this organic network, and that the network is distinct from — and
antecedent to — the internet.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Pasulka recounts Jose's account across Chapters 5 and 6 of [[Encounters (Pasulka)]].

In combat: "Before ever hearing about Chardin's philosophy, Jose identified this current as a network
that linked him to the mental, physical, and emotional states of his brothers, and to information
about the Afghanistan terrain and people. The information it provided was life-giving. He knew who to
trust, where to go, and which areas to leave. He avoided explosions and snipers. His brothers called
him 'lucky.'"

On UFOs and the network: "According to Jose, we are targets and soldiers in a war of which most
people are unaware … the UFO shows up when one is connected to the organic network — the one
described by Chardin. However, one should not under any circumstances focus on the UFO. It is just
one aspect of an ongoing reality that involves other supernatural events."

On youth and veterans: Jose's program wakes digital natives to the organic network through body
protocols (exercise, meditation, biofeedback, time outdoors), arguing that those born after the
internet have never felt the older network and that their phones are "direct connection to the
battlefield, yet we view them as toys." He characterises social media platforms as weapons — "Trojan
horses" — designed to harvest cognitive and emotional data from vulnerable populations.

On epigenetics: Jose frames the organic network in quasi-scientific terms: "Platonic and Aristotelian
ethical frames are epigenetic prescriptions … Memes are social programs, and UFOs, like Saint
Michael, are images that describe an experience that is being had by millions of people." He connects
Chardin's "Omega Point" to the moment when the Homo sapiens lineage merges with a
technological/spiritual sphere.

Simone (Ch. 7) independently validates this account: "I can validate what your student Jose knows
regarding the biological network; Chardin described it well as the noösphere."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is central to Pasulka's argument in Chs. 5–6 that [[Contact with nonhuman intelligence (Pasulka)|contact]] is mediated by an organic, non-digital information field. It links directly to the shared concept page [[The organic network / noösphere (Pasulka)]] and to [[Pierre Teilhard de Chardin]]'s philosophy. Compare also with [[Jacques Vallée]]'s warning (cited in Ch. 6) that social media technologies feed on human behaviour, which Jose extends into a full counter-strategy.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Pasulka presents Jose's framework phenomenologically and does not adjudicate whether the organic
network is an objectively real field or a cognitive/cultural construction. She notes that Jose's
precognitive abilities — "these skills can't be reproduced in laboratories" — fall outside mainstream
scientific validation frameworks, a point Jose himself acknowledges.
