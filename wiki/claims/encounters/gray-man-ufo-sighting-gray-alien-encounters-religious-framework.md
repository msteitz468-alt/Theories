## Claim Statement

Gray Man (pseudonym) reports a UFO sighting on Empire Bay Drive, New South Wales (ca. 1994, corresponding to the documented Brisbane Waters / Gosford mass sighting), multiple encounters with small gray beings (~3 feet tall, large black eyes) in states he insists were not dreams, poltergeist activity in his home affecting himself and his severely autistic son, and recurrent sleep paralysis / lucid-dream visitations. He adamantly refuses the extraterrestrial-spacecraft interpretation of these events, arguing instead that the accompanying paranormal phenomena (time distortion, demonic presence, poltergeist effects, supernatural synchronicities) are incompatible with a simple space-travelers-from-another-planet hypothesis and are better understood through religious and demonic categories. Pasulka cites his case as evidence that [[Jacques Vallée]]'s information-technology / control-system framework provides a more adequate explanatory lens than the ET-hardware model.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

UFO sighting: "It was dark. I was in my car driving along Empire Bay Drive, a narrow undivided country road on the New South Wales Central Coast. Out of nowhere this giant meteor-like thing blasted through the sky above me... The whole sky lit up like daylight; I saw the brown bark on the trees just like it was daytime." Gray Man expected national news coverage; none appeared, though Pasulka documents the Brisbane Waters sighting was in fact reported, with police sergeant Bob Wenning confirming: "All the people I spoke to had exactly the same thing to say; there was absolutely no variance whatsoever in the object that was sighted."

Gray aliens: "There were about three small gray beings. They looked like the gray aliens that people talk about. They were about three feet tall, with big black eyes and big heads... The feeling I got from them was that they were purely evil, filled with pure evil. I tried to use my mind to fight them off. I commanded them in my mind in the name of Jesus to be gone."

Gray Man on the ET hypothesis: "If these events were just the case of an extraterrestrial contact experience, then why would all these other weird paranormal effects occur along with it? When people landed on the Moon, I am reasonably certain they didn't use a Ouija board to communicate with ground control on planet Earth... Then why is it that virtually all of the UFO/alien contact experiences that I am aware of contain some type of paranormal phenomena that goes along with it?"

On poltergeist activity: his autistic son's buttoned shirt was found reversed and all buttons still done up in the morning — something the child (age 5–6) could not have done. Gray Man: "It's like this phenomena takes pleasure in mocking you. It's like something is saying to us, 'We can do anything we want with you, and you can't do anything about it.'"

He also references Jack Parsons's ritual magic and Wernher von Braun's late-life Christian conversion as evidence that advanced scientists working at the frontier have historically encountered these forces and recognized them as real.

Pasulka frames the interpretation through [[Jacques Vallée]]: "Research into the UFO phenomena benefits from a religiously informed interpretive framework... Vallée presaged important theoretical and societal developments. First, he used the burgeoning models of information technology as an interpretive framework to study religious events and the impact that supernatural characters — deities, angels, and demons, and in the modern era, UFOs — have on populations."

Pasulka also notes some members of the Invisible College (scientists who studied UFOs with [[J. Allen Hynek]]) practice Catholic and Episcopal devotions, and that this convergence of scientific rigor and intense religious practice is characteristic of those who study the phenomena closely.

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapters 3 and 4 together constitute the extended Gray Man case study. The religious warfare framework — the UFO as door to a supernatural realm of angels and demons — is the chapter's core argument. See [[The Gray Man (Pasulka)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Pasulka does not endorse Gray Man's specific religious interpretation. She presents his framework phenomenologically, as the experiencer's own interpretive response. She notes the UFO event is "like a door that opens a person to a nonordinary world" — but does not specify what lies beyond that door. She also notes that "we are not meant to figure it out," quoting her colleague Tyler D., suggesting institutional constraints on knowledge.
