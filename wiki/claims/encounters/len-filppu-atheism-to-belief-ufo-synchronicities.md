## Claim Statement

Len Filppu reports that a UFO sighting in Brockport, New York, in 1967, followed by a series of synchronicities, a waking vision of Archbishop Fulton J. Sheen on the night of Sheen's death, and recurring vivid dreams, progressively dismantled his atheist worldview and led him to a form of personal spirituality he describes as "Len's Magical Mystery Tour." Pasulka argues this arc illustrates that UFO encounters must be understood within the full biographical context of experiencers, not extracted from their lives.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**The 1967 sighting**: On August 1, 1967, at approximately 2:00 a.m., Filppu observed a large, silent, orange spherical object approximately the apparent size of a full moon, with flames trailing behind it and a small white orb in its wake, traveling at an estimated altitude of two hundred feet, thirty yards away from his position on a lawn in Brockport, NY. The sighting lasted about thirty seconds and was corroborated by more than thirty witnesses across nearby towns, two Brockport police officers, and two Monroe County sheriff's deputies. NICAP reviewed the reports and ruled out a bolide. The *Rochester Democrat and Chronicle* published multiple follow-up articles.

Filppu reports that during the sighting, as he ran to report it to police, he received a sudden calm, rational redirection:

> "Stop. What's the point? Turn around and continue watching. Enjoy it."

He characterizes this as an external "knowing" — a form of information received rather than generated — which Pasulka connects to the [[The download (spontaneous cognition, Pasulka)|download]] phenomenon.

**Personal impact**: Filppu states: "My UFO sighting forever changed me. It was my first dramatic transcendent experience… It also opened my eyes to the inescapable fact that government authorities and maybe even science were either lying to us about UFOs or they were sorely incompetent. It made me more wary, cynical, and distrustful of pronouncements by our society's institutional authorities."

**The Fulton Sheen synchronicity (December 9, 1979)**: During a "dark night of the soul" in a New York hotel room after a period of heavy drinking, Filppu silently appealed to the universe for help. He then experienced a vivid, full-color waking vision of Sheen's face rising upward in his mind's eye. The following morning he read that Sheen had died in his private chapel the previous night. He describes the moment: "Reality shifted for me as the realization of this 'aha' synchronicity took hold… The unthinkable question forced itself upon me: Had Sheen's spirit visited me?"

**Subsequent experiences**: Filppu reports a Kundalini electrical shock wave during chanting, a poltergeist experienced with witnesses, a deep recognition of a past life, and numerous vivid precognitive dreams (see the [[The dream network (Pasulka)|dream network]]).

**His conclusion**: Filppu does not adopt Christianity but converts from atheism to a form of personally situated spirituality. Pasulka writes: "These experiences eventually led Len to a conversion from atheism. To what did he convert? One could call Len's form of spirituality a variety of 'spiritual but not religious.'"

## Contexts Within the Source

Pasulka uses Filppu's arc to argue that UFO encounters initiate an "enchanted world" entry that unfolds over years or decades and includes paranormal experiences far beyond the original sighting. She frames the sighting as a "window" (drawing on [[Jacques Vallée]] and John Mack), through which Filppu stepped into "another, enchanted world" and "for a time… existed in two worlds."

She also uses his case to critique ufology's tendency to isolate sightings from biographical context, arguing: "One conclusion I've reached is that UFO encounters must be placed within contexts and not extracted from the lives of the people who encounter them."

The Brockport setting — the "Burned-Over district," historically associated with religious revivals, abolitionism, Spiritualism, and Mormonism — is noted as potentially significant to the pattern of spiritual innovation Pasulka sees in the region.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

- Pasulka is careful to note that Filppu "refused to conclude as to what it meant. Ultimately, he doesn't know what it means."
- The Sheen vision is described phenomenologically; Filppu himself speculates: "I truly think it had nothing to do with me… I merely stumbled, hungover, into the trajectory of his transitioning spiritual essence."
- The witness corroboration for the 1967 sighting is strong, but Pasulka does not adjudicate the nature of the object; she records it as a genuine Unidentified Aerial Phenomenon confirmed by NICAP.
