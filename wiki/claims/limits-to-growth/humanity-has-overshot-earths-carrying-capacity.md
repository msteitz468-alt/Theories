## Claim Statement

The authors argue humanity has already entered **overshoot** — its population and material economy have grown past "the support capacities of the earth." Measured by the **ecological footprint**, "since the late 1980s the earth's peoples have been using more of the planet's resource production each year than could be regenerated in that year." Overshoot, they say, results whenever growth meets a limit with delayed or mistaken perception of that limit.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

They cite Mathis Wackernagel's ecological-footprint work (Earth Council 1997; WWF *Living Planet Report*) for ~150 nations, and document exponential growth in population, industrial output, and atmospheric CO₂ (≈270→370+ ppm). "The bad news is that many crucial sources are emptying or degrading, and many sinks are filling up or overflowing… [throughput] cannot be maintained at their current rates for very much longer." They stress the consequences "need not be catastrophic" — overshoot can end in "a crash of some kind" or "a deliberate turnaround," but warn that without a profound correction "a crash of some sort is certain… within the lifetimes of many who are alive today."
