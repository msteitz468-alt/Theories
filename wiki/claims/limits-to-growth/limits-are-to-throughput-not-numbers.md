## Claim Statement

The authors explicitly clarify that "the limits to growth are not limits to the number of people, cars, houses, or factories." They are limits to **throughput** — "the continuous flows of energy and materials needed to keep people, cars, houses, and factories functioning" — i.e., limits to the rate at which humanity can extract resources from planetary **sources** and emit wastes into planetary **sinks** without exceeding their productive or absorptive capacities.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sources named include "mineral deposits, aquifers, and the stock of nutrients in soils"; sinks include "the atmosphere, surface water bodies, and landfills." The good-news corollary: "current high rates of throughput are not necessary to support a decent standard of living for all the world's people," and the footprint "could be reduced by lowering population, altering consumption norms, or implementing more resource-efficient technologies." They invoke Herman Daly's three conditions for a sustainable throughput.

## Contexts Within the Source

A precise restatement of [[Humanity has overshot the Earth's carrying capacity]] meant to forestall the reading that the report calls for capping the number of people; it underwrites the authors' insistence that sustainability need not require population cuts — see [[A deliberate transition to sustainability is possible — and need not be authoritarian or impoverishing]].
