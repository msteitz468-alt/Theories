## Claim Statement

When the World3 computer model simulates the global system "as it might evolve, assuming no profound changes," the result is, "in nearly every scenario, overshoot and collapse of the planet's economy and population." Adding stronger technology and market responses (Ch. 6) "helps considerably" but is "not enough" by itself, because those responses are "themselves delayed and imperfect" and "can be overwhelmed by population and economic growth."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The authors present World3 runs as **scenarios, not predictions** ("10 different pictures of how the twenty-first century may evolve… to encourage your learning, reflection, and personal choice"). The model tracks interacting stocks and flows — population, food, industrial output, pollution, nonrenewable resources — descended from Jay Forrester's World2. Technical fixes (pollution control, recycling, efficiency, land preservation) delay but do not avert collapse unless combined with deliberate limits; see [[A deliberate transition to sustainability is possible — and need not be authoritarian or impoverishing]]. The ozone story (Ch. 5) is offered as a real-world counterexample showing foresighted global cooperation *is* possible.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors repeatedly disclaim prediction, note the data are "often poor in quality and incomplete," and acknowledge their own "worldview" filters what they see. They also diverge among themselves on whether collapse/decline is now avoidable.
