## Claim Statement

Lehrich reports that [[Claude Lévi-Strauss]], in his 1950 *Introduction to the Work of Marcel Mauss*,
reinterpreted mana (and cognate terms wakan, orenda) not as a substantive supernatural force but as a
"floating signifier" — a term with an indeterminate, empty signification that fills a structural gap
between signifier and signified whenever a "relationship of non-equivalence" is established between them.
Lehrich adds his own qualification that "mana really is mana" even in Lévi-Strauss's abstracted account —
i.e., that Lévi-Strauss's reformulation is itself a further floating signifier, not a final explanation,
and that it leaves unanswered why such signifiers become specifically associated with magical power.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"Always and everywhere, those types of notions, somewhat like algebraic symbols, occur to represent an
indeterminate value of signification, in itself devoid of meaning and thus susceptible of receiving any
meaning at all; their sole function is to fill a gap between the signifier and the signified... a
relationship of non-equivalence [inadéquation] becomes established between signifier and signified." Also
quoted: "I believe that notions of the mana type... represent nothing more or less than that floating
signifier which is the disability of all finite thought." Lehrich's own gloss: "It is not only in Mauss
and Durkheim that 'mana really is mana.'"

## Contexts Within the Source

Lehrich treats Lévi-Strauss's proposal as "fascinating and usefully incorrect" — valuable as a structural
reformulation but leaving the ascription of specifically magical power to mana-type notions unexplained,
since deferral of signification does not by itself explain why the deferred sign should carry
supernatural overtones.
