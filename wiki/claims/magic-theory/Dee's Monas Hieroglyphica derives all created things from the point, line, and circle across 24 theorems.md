**Claim Statement**
Lehrich quotes and discusses Dee's *Monas Hieroglyphica*, structured as a series of twenty-four
"theorems" in which Dee argues that every line, curve, or mark composing the hieroglyphic monad
derives from and expresses the deeper hieroglyphic reality of point, line, and circle — the "first and
most simple manifestation... of things." The final, twenty-fourth theorem completes the "permutations"
of the number 24, likened by Dee to the 24 elders and 24 hours of the equinoctial circuit described in
Revelation 4.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
"Theorem I. The first and most simple manifestation and representation of things, non-existent as well
as latent in the folds of Nature, happened by means of straight line and circle." Theorem XXIIII: "As we
made this little book take its beginning from point, straight line, and circle, so also we have made the
last linear effluxion... describe a circle which is almost analogous to the equinoctial completing its
circuit in 24 hours." Theorem VIII derives the cabbalistic significance of the numeral ten and the
rectilinear cross (the "twenty-first letter of the Roman alphabet") from the same generative logic.

**Contexts Within the Source**
Lehrich presents these theorems as evidence that Dee treated the monad not as an arbitrary diagram but
as a "master key" of interpretation — what Dee himself, following Agrippa and Trithemius, called a
"real Cabala" that manipulates things as well as language, grounding writing within a single system of
knowledge uniting Scripture, Nature, and human reason.
