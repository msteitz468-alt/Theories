**Claim Statement**
Lehrich engages Brian Vickers's argument that occult analogical thinking (as in Kircher's correspondence
tables in *Musurgia Universalis*, linking angels, planets, metals, stones, plants, and colors) is a
sterile "matrix into which reality had to be assimilated," unlike scientific analogy which remains
open to falsification. Lehrich argues Vickers unknowingly restates Lévi-Strauss's argument in *La
pensée sauvage* (never citing him) about "savage" classificatory thought, and that Vickers commits the
same fallacy he criticizes: treating Heninger's reading of Kircher as identical with Kircher's own
intent. Citing Stephen Jay Gould's positive reassessment of Kircher's paleontology and the "musurgical
ark" composing-machine, Lehrich argues Kircher (like Bruno and like Eliade or Goethe's morphology)
sought homology — underlying structural constancy discovered in diverse data — rather than superficial,
arbitrary analogy imposed on the world.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Vickers, quoted: "instead of deriving their methods from the physical world by processes of observation,
experiment, quantification, theory, and so forth, the occult imposed traditional thought categories onto
the world and 'read' nature in the light of them." Lévi-Strauss, quoted in reply (written twenty years
earlier): "Classifying, of whatever sort, as opposed to not classifying, has a value of its own."
Lehrich: "For Kircher, however, the links sought lie at a deeper level: he seeks homology, not analogy,
rather like Eliade or Goethe." Kircher, on fossils: he will not dwell on ordinary petrified shells
"because these are obviously found everywhere in such a state, and hardly merit any attention" —
evidence, per Gould and Lehrich, that Kircher sought principled classification, not indiscriminate
correspondence-hunting.

**Contexts Within the Source**
Lehrich also invokes Lévi-Strauss's critique of the concept of "totemism" (a *petitio principii*
imposed by anthropologists) as a caution equally applicable to scholars who presuppose Kircher's
"connections" already form a coherent system before examining whether he achieved one.
