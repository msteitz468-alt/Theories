## Claim Statement

Lee and Shlain document that the CIA covertly funded much of early American hallucinogen research through "cutout" foundations (the Geschickter Fund, the Society for the Study of Human Ecology, the Josiah Macy Jr. Foundation), creating "laboratories of the state." Many subjects were captive or unwitting — prisoners, mental patients, addicts, "foreigners, the terminally ill, sexual deviants, ethnic minorities" — in violation of the Nuremberg Code.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Cited cases: Dr. **Ewen Cameron** at McGill (CIA-funded "depatterning" with massive electroshock, sleep therapy, LSD, and "psychic driving" — taped messages played up to a quarter-million times; nine former patients later sued); Dr. **Harris Isbell** at the Lexington Narcotics Hospital (one of 15 penal/mental institutions used by the CIA), who kept mostly Black inmates on LSD "for more than seventy-five consecutive days," paying volunteers in heroin/morphine; and Drs. Paul Hoch, Carl Pfeiffer, Robert Heath, and "Jolly" West (who killed an elephant with 300,000 µg of LSD). A May 26, 1954 CIA memo ordered domestic field offices to monitor all LSD researchers. The authors note the irony that Cameron had sat on the Nuremberg tribunal.

## Contexts Within the Source
