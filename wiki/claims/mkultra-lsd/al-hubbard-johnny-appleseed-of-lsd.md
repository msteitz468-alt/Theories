## Claim Statement

Lee and Shlain present Captain Alfred M. Hubbard — a former OSS officer and uranium millionaire with "prestigious government and business connections" — as the unlikely first apostle of LSD as a *visionary/transcendental* drug, the man "widely credited" with emphasizing its spiritual potential. Nicknamed "Captain Trips" and the "Johnny Appleseed of LSD," he made it his mission to turn on as many influential people as possible.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The authors note the irony of a career spook becoming an LSD missionary: Hubbard ran a covert pre–Pearl Harbor arms-smuggling operation for the OSS (later pardoned by Truman), then took his first acid trip in 1951 (claiming to witness his own conception). He sought out Dr. Humphry Osmond and, through him, Aldous Huxley. Hubbard exemplifies the book's "interface" between the covert and grassroots histories — the same drug the CIA studied as a weapon was spread by a CIA-linked man as a sacrament.
