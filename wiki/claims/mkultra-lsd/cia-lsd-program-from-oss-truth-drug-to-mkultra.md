## Claim Statement

Lee and Shlain trace an unbroken institutional lineage of U.S. drug/mind-control programs: the OSS "Truth Drug" program (1942, under "Wild Bill" Donovan, using a cannabis extract called "TD"); the Navy's Project CHATTER (1947, mescaline); the CIA's BLUEBIRD (1950) and Operation ARTICHOKE (1951), which first tested LSD as a truth serum; and Operation **MK-ULTRA** (1953), the CIA's principal Cold War drug and mind-control program, run by the Technical Services Staff under chemist [[Sidney Gottlieb]].

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The authors report that [[Allen Dulles]] authorized MK-ULTRA three days after his April 10, 1953 "brain warfare" speech, that it was "the brainchild of Richard Helms," and that LSD was first hoped to be a truth serum ("the secret that was going to unlock the universe"), then deemed unreliable, then reconceived as a weapon. They document the CIA's attempt to buy **ten kilos** of LSD from Sandoz (the result of a milligram/kilogram error) — "enough for a staggering one hundred million doses" — and Eli Lilly's 1954 synthesis of "all-American acid." Nazi-derived precedent (Dachau mescaline experiments, imported via Project Paperclip) is noted.
