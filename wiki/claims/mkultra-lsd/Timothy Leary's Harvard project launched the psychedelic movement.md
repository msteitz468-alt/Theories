## Claim Statement

Lee and Shlain trace the public psychedelic movement to the Harvard project that Timothy Leary and Richard Alpert began in 1960, after Leary's first magic-mushroom trip in Mexico (prompted by R. Gordon Wasson's 1957 *Life* article). They ran psilocybin studies (the Concord Prison recidivism study; the "Good Friday"/Marsh Chapel experiment showing drug-induced mystical states), then moved to LSD after Michael Hollingshead arrived with a mayonnaise jar of acid.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The authors document the competing visions for the movement: Aldous Huxley's elitist "turn on the talented and well-born" versus Allen Ginsberg's egalitarian crusade to give psychedelics to everyone, and William Burroughs's prescient warning that "sinister forces" might use the drugs "to control rather than liberate." They note Harvard students/faculty had already served as subjects in CIA/military LSD studies, that the CIA's Office of Security monitored Leary's group, and that Leary's own "Leary" personality test "was used by the CIA." They quote Leary's later quip: "The LSD movement was started by the CIA."
