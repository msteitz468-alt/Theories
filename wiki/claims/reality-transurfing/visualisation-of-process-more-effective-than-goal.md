## Claim Statement

Visualising the process of moving toward a goal — rather than the goal itself — significantly quickens the work of outer intention. Visualising the goal is the work of desire and does not move the person closer to it. The Transurfing visualisation method is defined as mentally picturing the realization process of the current link in the transfer chain.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. VIII: "Contemplating the end result will expand the limits of your comfort zone and so in this sense it has its value, but visualising the process of moving towards your goal will significantly quicken the work of outer intention."

Ch. VIII: "Visualisation of your goals is the work of desire which will not bring you a single step closer to your goal."

Ch. VIII: "In Transurfing, visualisation is understood to be the act of focusing on a mental image of the realization process of a current link in the transfer chain." (Ch. VIII Summary)

## Contexts Within the Source

The source identifies why conventional visualisation techniques yield inconsistent results: most focus on the end state (the goal), which is desire-based and creates excess potential or simply fails to engage intention. The Transurfing method shifts the focus to process, thereby activating the intention mechanism rather than the desire mechanism.

The goal-image slide (type 3 visualisation) retains value specifically for expanding the comfort zone and attuning thought energy to the target life line — but it is the "workhorse" process visualisation that actually moves the person along the transfer chain.
