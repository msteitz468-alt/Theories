## Claim Statement

A slide is a distorted picture of reality held in the mind — a mental image or belief about oneself or the world rooted in importance. Through [[Outer Intention]], the content of the slide is gradually but consistently realized in physical reality. Outer intention catches the holder of the slide and carries them to a sector where the slide's content is expressed to its full extent.

## Contexts Within the Source

The claim explains a mechanism by which outer intention operates without the person's conscious direction or consent. Because negative self-images (inadequacy, ugliness, unworthiness) create heart–mind unity around negative content — even without conscious positive affirmation — outer intention realizes their content automatically.

The source presents this as the key to both understanding self-sabotage and redirecting it: replace negative slides with positive ones, and outer intention will realize those instead. The Toulouse-Lautrec example (Ch. VIII) illustrates the mechanism: his suffering from his physical deformity created a negative slide which outer intention amplified; when he let go of the importance of his condition, the slide ceased to function and fortune followed.
