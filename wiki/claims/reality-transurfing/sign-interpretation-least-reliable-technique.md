## Claim Statement

In Transurfing, with the exception of a negative response from the inner voice, sign interpretation is considered to be the least reliable technique available and it is not recommended to attribute signs a great deal of meaning. Signs relate only to the current link in the transfer chain and have no reliable connection to distant goals.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. VIII: "In Transurfing, with the exception of a negative response from the inner voice, sign interpretation is considered to be the least reliable technique one can use, so it is not recommended that you attribute them a great deal of meaning."

Ch. VIII: "Signs only relate to your current link in the transfer chain and only have a very tenuous connection to the final goal."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is notable because it directly contradicts a common practice in esoteric and spiritual traditions where sign-reading is treated as a primary method. The Transurfing position is more skeptical: signs are real (they indicate movement along the alternatives flow) but their interpretation is unreliable, especially when the goal is many transfer chains away.

The single reliable form of "sign" is the negative response from the inner voice — i.e., the [[Rustle of the Morning Stars]] in its discomfort mode: "If you have to convince yourself and talk yourself into saying 'yes', then your soul is really saying 'no.'" External signs, by contrast, are easily misread.
