## Claim Statement

Inner intention and outer intention are fundamentally different in kind, not merely in degree. Inner intention achieves a goal by direct effort — it acts within the current life line. Outer intention chooses a goal — it selects from the alternatives space the sector in which the goal already exists.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. VII: "Inner intention achieves the goal, outer intention chooses it."

Ch. VII: "Inner intention strives to impact outside world directly. Outer intention gives green light for autonomous realization of goal." (Ch. VII Summary)

Ch. VII: "Inner intention is resoluteness in the decision to act. Outer intention is resoluteness in the decision to have." (Ch. VII Summary)

## Contexts Within the Source

The source presents this as the most important structural distinction in the Transurfing model. Inner intention is the ordinary human mode — working hard toward a goal through personal effort. It is effective but limited by the boundaries of the current life line.

Outer intention operates at a different level entirely: it does not push through obstacles in the current life line but selects a sector of the alternatives space where the goal is already the natural state. Hence the characteristic outer intention posture: "It turns out that..." or "Circumstances are such that..." — the world configures itself around the goal rather than the person forcing their way to it.
