## Claim Statement

Every soul has its own individual "star" sector in the information field where its exceptional and unique qualities can be manifest in all their magnificence. Copying another person's qualities or script attunes one to a foreign sector where one's own heart potential cannot be fully realized.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. IX: "Every soul has its own individual 'star' sector in the information field."

Ch. IX: "A unique soul has its own sector in the alternatives space where its exceptional qualities can be manifest in all their magnificence."

Ch. IX: "Copying someone else's script always creates a parody."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim appears in "The Mirage" section of Ch. IX, which analyzes the pendulum mechanism of celebrity worship and imitation. The source argues that pendulums promote the imitation of stars specifically because imitation keeps adherents in foreign sectors where their own potential cannot flower.

The practical implication is strong: attempting to emulate another person's success script does not lead to that success but to a parody, because the person is attempting to inhabit a sector of the alternatives space that does not belong to their soul's unique parameters.

The Transurfing prescription: "left unhindered the heart will find the path of self-expression. The mind's task is to forget about other peoples' experience, to acknowledge the heart's extraordinary nature and to allow it to find its own path." (Ch. IX)
