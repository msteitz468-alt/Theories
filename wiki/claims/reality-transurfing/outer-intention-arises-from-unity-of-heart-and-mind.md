## Claim Statement

Outer intention does not arise from an act of will but from the harmony and unity between the heart (subconscious/soul) and the mind (conscious reason). When heart and mind are in agreement about a goal, outer intention realizes it — in both positive and negative directions.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. VII: "Outer intention arises not as a result of will but as a consequence of harmony between the heart and mind."

Ch. VII Summary: "Outer intention is the unity of heart and mind."

Ch. VII: "Outer intention gives the green light for the realization of whatever the soul and mind agree on."

Ch. VIII: "The feeling of clarity without words, knowledge without faith, confidence without hesitation comes from the unity that exists between heart and mind."

Ch. IX: "Achieving unity between heart and mind is enough to bring outer intention under the sway of your will."

## Contexts Within the Source

The claim explains both the difficulty of deliberately using outer intention and the ease with which it operates destructively (via negative slides and negative expectation). Heart–mind unity is already present in states of fear and guilt — hence "heart and mind united in negative expectation → easily realized." The difficulty is achieving positive unity.

The source identifies three conditions for directing outer intention positively: awareness (the Guardian), reducing importance, and letting go of the goal.
