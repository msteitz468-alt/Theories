## Claim Statement

Desire and intention are not the same thing in different amounts but different in kind. Desire concentrates attention on the goal as something lacking; intention concentrates attention on movement toward the goal or on how it realizes itself. Desire creates excess potential; intention does not. It is intention, not desire, that is realized.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. VII: "Realization is brought about not by desire but by one's attitude to that which is desired."

Ch. VII: "It is not desire that is fulfilled so much as intention."

Ch. VII: "Desire always creates excess potential. Desire is potential by definition."

Ch. VII: "Intention neither believes nor desires; it simply does."

Ch. VIII: "Desire is the concentration of attention on a goal. Intention is the concentration of attention on movement towards the goal. The driving force behind any action is intention and not desire."

## Contexts Within the Source

This distinction is the practical centerpiece of the Transurfing method. It reframes the common self-help emphasis on desire, visualization of outcomes, and positive wanting as fundamentally misdirected: all of these concentrate on the goal as a desired but absent thing, thereby creating the excess potential that balanced forces then eliminate.

The solution — replacing desire with intention, total resoluteness in the decision to have and act — is described as "the secret to fulfilling one's desires." (Ch. VII)
