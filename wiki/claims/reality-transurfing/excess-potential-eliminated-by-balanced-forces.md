## Claim Statement

Whenever a person attributes too much importance to something — creating excess potential — balanced forces arise specifically to eliminate that potential. These forces typically work against the person's aims in the area of excess importance.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. IV: "Excess potential: created when too much importance attributed to something; balanced forces emerge to eliminate it."

Ch. VII: "Desire always creates excess potential. Desire is potential by definition."

## Contexts Within the Source

The source provides multiple examples:
- **Perfection**: one who presents themselves as superior to a partner generates excess potential; balanced forces then undermine the relationship
- **Guilt**: guilt is pure excess potential and reliably produces circumstances of punishment
- **Atlantis**: described as having been destroyed when its inhabitants perfected [[Outer Intention]] to such a degree that balanced forces intervened catastrophically (Ch. VII)
- **Desire for goals**: concentrating desire on a goal creates potential that balanced forces then obstruct

The mechanism applies universally — to inner importance (about oneself) and outer importance (about external things).
