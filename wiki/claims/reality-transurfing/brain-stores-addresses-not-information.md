## Claim Statement

The brain does not store information itself. It stores the address to where the information is located in the alternatives space. Memory and knowledge are therefore not contained in the physical brain but accessed through it from the alternatives space.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. VII: "The brain does not store information itself. It stores the address to where the information is located in the alternatives space."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim appears in the "Dream Space" section of Ch. VII, as part of the broader argument that the [[Alternatives Space]] is the true locus of information — not just of possible future scenarios but of all information, including memories and accumulated knowledge.

The claim supports the broader Transurfing cosmology in which the alternatives space/information field is the primary substrate of reality, with the physical world (including the brain) being a manifestation of it rather than the reverse.

It also provides an explanation for the phenomenon of "Knowledge Out of Nowhere" (Ch. VI) — sudden insights that come from the subconscious accessing unrealized sectors of the field.
