## Claim Statement

Thought energy attuned to a negative frequency — through fear, worry, or excessive focus on what one does not want — shifts the person to a life line matching that negative frequency. The feared or unwanted outcome is thereby realized.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. II: "You get what you do not want: thought energy attuned to negative frequency shifts you to matching life line."

Ch. III: "Boomerang: thoughts always return; negative thinking attunes to negative life lines."

Ch. VII Summary: "Heart and mind united in negative expectation → easily realized."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is central to the Transurfing model's account of why conventionally "rational" worry and worst-case planning is counterproductive: it attunes thought energy to the very outcomes being avoided.

The source applies the principle to: negative news consumption (Ch. V — induced transitions via media), fear of failure in relationships (creating the failure one fears), guilt (producing the punishment one fears), and obsessive negative slides (realizing physical deterioration).

The claim is also the basis for the "do not allow" principle: "do not allow = intentionally ignore, not strive to avoid" (Ch. V) — anxious avoidance still attunes thought energy to the feared outcome; calm non-engagement does not.
