## Claim Statement

In reaching a distant goal in the alternatives space, a person must traverse all the intermediate stages (links in the transfer chain) in sequential order. It is impossible to move onto any given stage without having completed the one immediately before it. The alternatives space is "inherently an ordered structure."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. VIII: "Because the individual stages are linked together in a chain it is impossible to move onto any given stage without having completed the one immediately before it. For example, you cannot study for a master's before finishing your first university degree."

Ch. VIII: "The alternatives space is inherently an ordered structure which means that if you try to create a path to your goals in an unordered fashion you will never reach them."

## Contexts Within the Source

The claim has immediate practical consequences for visualisation: it explains why visualising the process of the distant future stages of a goal is not just ineffective but potentially disorienting ("could end up getting you lost"). The correct approach is to visualise only the current link of the transfer chain.

It also contextualizes the relationship between guiding signs and the goal: signs relate to the current link, not to the final objective. Attempting to read signs as indicators of the distant goal will produce misinterpretation.
