## Claim Statement

Pure intention — defined as desire and action without attribution of importance — fulfills the underlying desire without triggering the balanced forces that undermine ordinary desire. Unlike desire, pure intention does not create excess potential and therefore does not activate the [[Law of Balance]] against the person.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ch. III: "Pure intention = desire and action without attribution of importance."

Ch. VII: "Pure intention never creates excess potential. Pure intention assumes that everything is already in the bag."

Ch. VII: "Intention neither believes nor desires; it simply does."

## Contexts Within the Source

The distinction between pure intention and desire is central to the Transurfing model's account of why goals are or are not achieved. Ordinary desire (concentration on the goal as lacking) creates excess potential; pure intention (resolute movement without importance) does not.

The two conditions for achieving pure intention are stated in Ch. VII:
1. Accept the possibility of failure at the outset — removes the stakes that transform desire into excess potential
2. Reduce the intensity of importance — brings the goal within the comfort zone

Once these conditions are met: "move in direction of goal without thinking about it."
