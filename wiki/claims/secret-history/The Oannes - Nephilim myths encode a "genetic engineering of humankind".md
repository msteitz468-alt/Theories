## Claim Statement

Booth argues that the worldwide myths of the fish gods (the Babylonian **Oannes**) and of the **Nephilim** — the "sons of God" who interbred with the daughters of men — are coded accounts of a real, guided intervention of higher beings in human development, which he labels "the genetic engineering of humankind." Crucially, he reads this esoterically: not as literal alien DNA-tinkering, but as the secret tradition's anticipation of the evolution of species and the descent of spirit into animal-then-human form.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Drawing on **Berosus**, Booth recounts Oannes, the monster "whose whole body was that of a fish… His voice and language were articulate and human," who emerged from the sea to teach humanity letters, law, agriculture and geometry, then "retired again into the sea." He links parallel "fish gods who suddenly appear and become the teachers of mankind" — Matsya (Vishnu's first avatar), the Phoenician/West-African Dagon, and the fish-tailed early Zeus/Poseidon.

He pairs this with Genesis 6:1–5 (the "sons of God" taking "wives… of all which they chose," begetting "mighty men… of old, men of renown") and the fuller account in the **Book of Enoch** of the Watchers who "lusted after" the daughters of men and "taught them charms and enchantments." Booth's "astonishing possibility" is "that the ancient myths anticipated the modern, scientific insight that the animal life that would eventually evolve into the human form began life as a fish" — i.e. that "the priests of the Mystery schools knew of the evolution of species many thousand of years earlier."

## Contexts Within the Source

The chapter's stated theme is "The Genetic Engineering of Humankind"; the episode is set against an esoteric "original history of the origin of the species." It is an instance of [[A single secret philosophy was preserved by the Mystery schools and secret societies]] and a stage in [[History is a descent of spirit into matter]] — "the entry into the human condition of the serpent of animal desire."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Booth explicitly rejects the literal extraterrestrial reading of the same myths: "Some modern writers… have seen in this fish imagery evidence for an alien invasion… It's even been suggested that the human race was genetically engineered by alien invaders, which is a good illustration of the way that esoteric traditions are misinterpreted by people trying to impose a materialistic interpretation on them." His "genetic engineering" is thus a spiritual/evolutionary process, not the ancient-astronaut thesis. He also frames the whole book as "an imaginative exercise" and these verses as "one of the murkier episodes in the secret history."
