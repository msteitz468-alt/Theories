## Claim Statement

Booth closes the book with the secret societies' prophecy for the present age: that the great new evolutionary/spiritual impulse will arise in **Russia** (and eastern Europe), that **European civilization will collapse**, and that the "flame of true spirituality" will be kept burning in **America** — all keyed to the Mayan cycle that closes on 22 December AD 2012.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He states the forecast plainly in the Introduction: "This plan will encompass a belief that the great new impulse for the evolution will arise in Russia, that European civilization will collapse and that, finally, the flame of true spirituality will be kept burning in America." In the final chapter he supplies the chronology: "Mayan astronomer-priests pinpointed the incarnation of Lucifer to 13 August 3114 BC… These same priests predicted a similar turning point in history, the closing of one great cycle and the start of another, on 22 December AD 2012."

He grounds it in his model that consciousness changes rapidly across generations and that, since Christ's incarnation was history's midpoint, humanity is now re-ascending through earlier ages in reverse. Looking further ahead, "In AD 3574 we will enter the age called Philadelphia… the next impulse will come from Eastern Europe and Russia," already showing "extremes… both of spirituality and extremes of evil, such as the Russian 'mafia.'"

## Contexts Within the Source

The capstone of [[History is a descent of spirit into matter]] — the prophesied re-ascent after "the mystic death of humanity" — and of [[The cosmic mind and Thought-Beings|the idealist cosmology]], in which "mind created the physical universe precisely with the aim of nurturing human consciousness and helping it to evolve." It is presented as the secret societies' own "great cosmic plan."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Booth conditions the prophecy on the plan "prov[ing] to be successful" and presents the entire book as "an imaginative exercise" rather than empirical prediction, conceding he made "cavalier judgements" and "compromised" his narrative throughout.
