## Claim Statement

Booth argues that a single esoteric philosophy, born in the ancient **Mystery schools** (Thebes, Eleusis, Ephesus), was preserved and transmitted in secret across millennia through a chain of secret societies — the Cabala, Hermeticism, the Knights Templar, the Rosicrucians, esoteric Freemasonry, Martinism, Theosophy, and Anthroposophy — and that "an astonishing number of famous people" who made history were secretly its initiates.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He describes the Mystery schools' initiations (sensory deprivation, breathing, sacred dance, hallucinogens, redirected sexual energy) inducing altered states, guarded so strictly that revealing them was a capital offence; when Christianity closed the temples, the teaching went underground into secret societies. Booth's roster of alleged initiates spans Plato, Alexander, and Augustus to Newton (a lifelong "practising alchemist"), Voltaire, Washington, Napoleon ("guided by his star"), Dante, Shakespeare, and even Pope John Paul II (initiated, he says, by "a modern Rosicrucian master"). He claims the doctrine is "placed in plain view" yet coded — in art, statuary (the rolled scroll), public monuments, festivals, and the names of the days of the week.
