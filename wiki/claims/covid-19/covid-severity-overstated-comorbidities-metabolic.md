## Claim Statement

The authors argue COVID-19's lethality was overstated and that the true driver of severe outcomes is underlying **chronic disease and poor metabolic health** rather than the virus alone. They frame SARS-CoV-2 as "a biological trigger that magnifies and intensifies preexisting chronic diseases and comorbidities."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

They cite CDC data (released Aug 26, 2020) that "only 6 percent of COVID-19 deaths in the US had COVID-19 listed as the sole cause of death," the remainder averaging "2.6 comorbidities"; that ~80 percent of US victims were 65+, "with almost half of all deaths taking place in nursing homes"; and that the disease "didn't turn out to be nearly as lethal as initially predicted." They identify the key vulnerabilities as obesity, diabetes, heart disease, hypertension, and **low vitamin D**, and prescribe metabolic health — whole-food/low-net-carb diet, sunlight/vitamin D, fasting — as the real protection (Chs. 6, 9).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors acknowledge COVID-19 is a "genuine health threat" to the elderly and chronically ill, and that there were "a massive number of hospitalizations and deaths"; their argument is about *proportion and cause*, not denial of the disease.
