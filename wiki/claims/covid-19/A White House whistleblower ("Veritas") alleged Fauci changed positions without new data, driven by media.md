## Claim Statement

The book presents an anonymous, composite Trump White House insider — pseudonymously "**Veritas**" — who alleges that [[Anthony Fauci]] privately reversed his COVID guidance "literally out of nowhere... with no explanation," driven by his media persona rather than new evidence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 5** (a Q&A): Veritas states that early on the medical team (Fauci, Birx, Redfield) was "pretty equal," but Fauci and Birx "started to move forward." Veritas says Fauci told the White House "privately... we didn't need to wear masks" and "the flu was much worse," then "changed his tune out of the blue" with "no studies to point to." On the Feb 28–March 11 severity reversal, Veritas says "No new evidence or data was shared to justify the raised concern. The only thing that changed was the level of media obsession." He describes "the media drug" going to Fauci's head ("from a relatively obscure scientist... to Brad Pitt impersonating you on Saturday Night Live"), and says distrust grew after Fauci "admitted to lying" about masks: "what's to stop him from feeling justified in lying again?"

## Contexts Within the Source

Veritas is offered as the book's "whistleblower," corroborating the unexplained-reversal claim ([[Fauci's February-to-March 2020 reversal on COVID severity was never explained]]) and the media-feedback-loop theme. The authors frame whistleblowers as necessary so they are "not... left to little more than dot-connecting."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The source itself flags the testimony's limits: Veritas "could be one person, could be several," is anonymous, and the Q&A is described as a "composite." The authors warn readers "be forewarned. What you're about to read will not satisfy you," and Veritas concedes early trust was reasonable ("it's a novel virus so at first you want/need to trust your experts").
