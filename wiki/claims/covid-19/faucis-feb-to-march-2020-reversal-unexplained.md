## Claim Statement

Deace and Erzen assert that [[Anthony Fauci]] reversed himself on COVID-19's severity within eleven days — from comparing it to "a severe seasonal influenza" to calling it "10 times more lethal than the seasonal flu" — and that **no new data has ever been identified to explain the change**, making it "one of the great unsolved mysteries of this story."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapters 1, 2, 5**: On **February 28, 2020**, Fauci wrote in the *New England Journal of Medicine* that COVID's case-fatality rate "may ultimately be more akin to those of a severe seasonal influenza (which has a case fatality rate of approximately 0.1%)." On **March 11, 2020**, he testified to Congress that COVID would be "10 times more lethal than the seasonal flu" — testimony the book says "sent shockwaves across the country and launched us into lockdowns" (the NBA shut down the next day).

The authors note the Imperial College model "wasn't published until five days later on March 16," so it cannot account for the shift. In chapter 5 the "Veritas" insider says the White House "also saw a sudden and inexplicable change from Fauci right around that time," with "no new evidence or data... shared," speculating he was "reading the room. The media room."

## Contexts Within the Source

This is one of two questions the book says require "9/11-type tribunals" (the other being the virus's unknown start date — see [[The virus's true start date was unknown, undermining the rationale for lockdowns]]). It anchors the larger contradiction thesis ([[Fauci repeatedly contradicted himself on COVID-19]]).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors acknowledge the possibility that the reversal was a sincere response to a fast-moving novel virus rather than manipulation, presenting "two possible explanations" — deliberate or incompetent — and concluding "both... are bad." They also note Fauci's original *NEJM* estimate "is looking increasingly prescient."
