## Claim Statement

Deace and Erzen assert that the antimalarial **hydroxychloroquine** "was panned and ridiculed by Fauci" and the media — "almost as if they didn't want a cure/treatment early on" — and that "it is entirely possible that this drug does work and lives were lost because it was politicized." They rest the claim on documented expert disagreement, not their own medical judgment.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 3**: The authors note a prominent *Lancet* study critical of the drug was **retracted** for data flaws (a Cleveland Clinic doctor said "a first-year statistics major could tell you about major flaws"), yet Fauci spoke against the drug a month later. They quote Yale's **Harvey Risch** (author of 330+ peer-reviewed papers) calling it "the key to defeating COVID-19" and citing a Swiss "reverse natural experiment," and **Stephen Smith** calling it a "game-changer." They highlight a January 21, 2021 *American Journal of Medicine* paper recommending it was "prudent to deploy" hydroxychloroquine with azithromycin and zinc — after which, they note, "Fauci had yet to amend his past public criticism."

## Contexts Within the Source

The episode is offered as evidence for [[Credentialed experts dissented from Fauci's lockdown approach]] and for the danger "of vesting so much power in the hands of one man... rather than seeking wisdom in a multitude of counsel." It parallels [[Low-cost COVID treatments were suppressed in favour of Big Pharma products]] from another source in this wiki.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors are unusually explicit in hedging: "The authors of this book don't know what to think about hydroxychloroquine's effectiveness... precisely because we're not doctors or scientists," and "We also have seen contradictory studies on the matter." Their claim is that "somebody is wrong and/or lying here — Fauci or Risch," not that the drug is proven effective.
