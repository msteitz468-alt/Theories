## Claim Statement

Deace and Erzen assert that the COVID-19 **lockdowns** — "15 days to flatten the curve," extended into "30 days to slow the spread" and beyond — were "perhaps the dumbest management decision in American history" and that "the cure (consisting of mass shutdowns) is worse than the disease." Lockdowns "don't work, but they do kill."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapters 2–6 & Conclusion**: The book cites Yale's **David Katz** ("Is our fight against coronavirus worse than the disease?"), Stanford's **John Ioannidis** (a March 11, 2020 peer-reviewed paper on "the harms of exaggerated information and non-evidence-based measures"), and **Scott Atlas**'s itemized harms (undiagnosed cancers, missed transplants, skipped chemotherapy and immunizations, UN starvation projections, unreported child abuse, tuberculosis deaths). It notes "15 days to flatten the curve" coincided with millions of jobs lost "in the… health care sector," and that lockdown unemployment exceeded "the second year of the Great Depression."

Carnegie Mellon's **Wesley Pegden** is quoted that "the duration of containment does not matter" — lifting it later yields "an epidemic almost as large" — so lockdowns merely "delay the infection."

## Contexts Within the Source

The claim runs throughout and is sharpened by the [[Sweden's no-lockdown approach was a "control group" indicating lockdowns were ineffective]] chapter and by the age-skew argument ([[COVID-19 mortality was concentrated in the elderly, so focused protection was possible]]). It feeds the conclusion that Americans "gave up our liberty for a little safety, and we ended up with neither."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors grant the **initial** short-term shutdown was defensible — Atlas himself says "a short-term shutdown was appropriate to flatten the curve to stop hospitals from being overcrowded" — and that "erring heavily on the side of caution can hardly be considered an unforgivable sin" given early uncertainty. Their objection is to *indefinite* lockdowns after the data clarified.
