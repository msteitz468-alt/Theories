## Claim Statement

Deace and Erzen assert that [[Anthony Fauci]] publicly blessed New York Governor [[Andrew Cuomo]]'s pandemic record — making him "the poster-child for Faucism" — even though Cuomo's policies were tied to thousands of nursing-home deaths that his administration later admitted were undercounted, and that Fauci then dodged accountability for the endorsement.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 8**: On **July 20, 2020**, Fauci said on PBS that New York "did it correctly by doing the things [I recommended]" — which the authors gloss as "this is my son in whom I am well pleased." One month later Cuomo announced his book *American Crisis* and won an Emmy for his briefings. The authors note the Associated Press had reported unreported nursing-home deaths as early as May 23, 2020, and that Cuomo aide **Melissa DeRosa** admitted the toll was underreported "by more than six thousand people... to minimize potential fallout in the press." When later asked about the scandal, Fauci said "I prefer not to comment." The chapter documents a long Fauci–Cuomo-family relationship (Fauci's daily calls to CNN's Chris Cuomo, whom he had known "since [Chris] was almost a kid").

## Contexts Within the Source

The chapter frames Cuomo as a Fauci "proxy" ("First Bishop of Faucism") to argue that the same self-promoting, gaslighting pattern the book attributes to Fauci also produced Cuomo, with deadly consequences. It supports [[Unaccountable, unelected bureaucrats like Fauci threaten the constitutional republic]].

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors concede a distinction: "Maybe Fauci's duplicity didn't directly lead to the deaths of people as it allegedly did with Cuomo." They also note Cuomo's own defense that he was "merely following federal guidelines." The chapter is explicitly polemical (e.g., comparing the two to "De Niro and Pacino").
