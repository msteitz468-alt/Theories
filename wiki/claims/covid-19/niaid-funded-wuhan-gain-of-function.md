## Claim Statement

Deace and Erzen assert as established fact that [[Anthony Fauci]]'s **National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID)** "funded the Wuhan Institute of Virology in China for 'gain of function research on bat coronaviruses,'" and argue the **lab-leak hypothesis** for COVID-19's origin was prematurely dismissed while Fauci was scarcely questioned about it.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 7** (citing Newsweek): NIAID-backed NIH funding totaled ~$7.4 million across two projects through 2019, including gain-of-function work to test "spillover potential" of bat coronaviruses. The Obama administration pressured a 2014 moratorium; the NIH lifted it in 2017; the Trump administration later cancelled the remaining work. The book cites Steve Hilton's Fox investigation (six payments despite the ban; the genetic "doorway" into human cells) and Tucker Carlson (the intermediate horseshoe bat's native population "no closer than 600 miles away"). It notes the WHO's January 14, 2020 tweet of "no clear evidence of human-to-human transmission," its February 2021 dismissal of the lab-leak theory (Ted Cruz: "The Chinese Communist Party told them it didn't"), and Judicial Watch FOIA emails alleging NIH/WHO "accommodations to Chinese... efforts to control information."

## Contexts Within the Source

The chapter is also used to reinforce that Fauci is "a servant of this leviathan; he is not the leviathan himself" — the funding "goes far beyond him." It parallels [[SARS-CoV-2 was lab-engineered via gain-of-function research]] from another source, though stated more cautiously here.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors are notably hedged on origin: "There's a lot we still do not know for certain about the true origins," and the gain-of-function funding is "one thing we do know for sure." They note the lab-leak idea was "often dismissed by the scientific community," and ask only that Fauci "be asked to give an account," not that the lab origin is proven.
