## Claim Statement

Deace and Erzen close by asserting, via Benjamin Franklin, that COVID-19 policy proved "those who would give up essential Liberty, to purchase a little temporary Safety, deserve neither Liberty nor Safety": Americans "consented to unprecedented government oppression" yet still suffered massive case and death tolls — "We gave up our liberty for a little safety, and we ended up with neither." They call for a "9/11 style of tribunal" and accountability.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Conclusion**: The authors note that despite the lockdowns the country was "reporting over twenty-nine million cases and over a half million deaths," and that "still 95 percent of all COVID-19 deaths were to people over the age of fifty anyway" — "Proving lockdowns don't work, but they do kill." They contrast "free states" (Florida, South Dakota, Georgia, Iowa) as vindicated control groups — citing Kristi Noem (told she'd have 10,000 hospitalized; "never had more than 600") and Ron DeSantis (Florida senior COVID hospitalizations down ~80%). They invoke Elie Wiesel's *Night* on slow-boiled complacency and cite cases of arrests for using a playground, opening a salon, or singing hymns.

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the book's culminating statement, tying the lockdown/mask/cult chapters back to the framing thesis ([[Unaccountable, unelected bureaucrats like Fauci threaten the constitutional republic]]). The authors urge that "our own fear that has become our idol has to go."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors restate that the virus "is very real. It has taken many lives. We must never lose sight of that," and that government action can be legitimate where "the sanctity of life is at stake" — locating their objection in the *disproportion* and *unaccountability* of the response rather than in any claim that COVID was harmless.
