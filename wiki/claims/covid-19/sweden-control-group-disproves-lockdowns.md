## Claim Statement

Deace and Erzen assert that **Sweden** — which under [[Anders Tegnell]] avoided mandatory lockdowns and masks — served as "the greatest control group in the history of Western civilization," and that its outcomes (comparable or better than many lockdown jurisdictions, with far less economic damage) indicate "the virus is gonna virus" regardless of lockdowns, so destroying an economy yields "so little promise of a return on investment."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 6**: As of February 23, 2021, the book says Sweden outperformed lockdown countries (Italy, UK, Spain, U.S., and others) on deaths or cases per million. It draws a **Sweden–Michigan** comparison (similar populations): Michigan imposed "one of the harshest lockdowns in the US" (even banning seed and garden-supply sales) yet had ~28% more deaths than Sweden plus "almost one-third of its businesses... shuttered" and "the worst job losses in the country." Sweden's 2.9% GDP contraction was milder than the Euro-area average. The book also alleges a closed Facebook group of 200+ academics coordinated to "damage Sweden's image."

## Contexts Within the Source

Sweden is the empirical centerpiece of the anti-lockdown case ([[Lockdowns were "the dumbest management decision in American history"]]). The authors quote Alex Berenson ("the virus is gonna virus"; "We can live with the truth... or destroy ourselves with the lie of perfect control").

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors concede Sweden's "herd immunity hopes have yet to materialize," that it "was hit with a second seasonal wave," that its population-adjusted death rate was "in the top 10 worldwide," and that "not even Sweden went without any mitigations." They also stress Sweden does **not** vindicate one-bureaucrat rule: "the answer here isn't as simple as replacing a Fauci with a Tegnell."
