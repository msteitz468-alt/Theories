## Claim Statement

Deace and Erzen assert that a "boisterous bloc" of Americans turned COVID-19 "into the apocalyptic event it never was," forming "perhaps the largest cult in American history" — the "**Branch Covidians**" — with [[Anthony Fauci]] as their "high priest."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 10**: The authors propose seven traits of a cult and mark each "Check" for the Branch Covidians: discouraging critical thinking (de-platforming skeptics); isolating members (lockdowns; an Ohio State poll that 72% planned to keep masking "after COVID-19 is no longer a threat"); claiming special knowledge (treating "novel coronavirus" as unprecedented while ignoring crossover/T-cell immunity studies); loyalty to the leader ("In Fauci We Trust" merchandise; *SNL* sex-symbol treatment; Julia Roberts's "courage award"); detaching families (Fauci's advice against holiday gatherings); crossing moral boundaries (endorsing online hookups while restricting church communion); and separating from the church (declining in-person worship). It then offers seven "antidote" steps (critical thinking, re-engagement, established science, truth-first, family reunification, civil disobedience against "COVID fascism," and "faith, family, and freedom"). See [[The Branch Covidian Cult]].

## Contexts Within the Source

This penultimate chapter generalizes the book's specific charges into a cultural diagnosis, setting up the conclusion's call to "throw that idol into the fire."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors preempt the objection that "describing these panic porn junkies as a cult is going too far, which is fair," and explicitly reaffirm the virus "is at least the worst pandemic to hit our nation since the Hong Kong flu in 1969, and maybe... since the Spanish flu of 1918" — "the most existential threat the average American has faced... since Pearl Harbor." They also concede some poll respondents are "likely virtue signaling."
