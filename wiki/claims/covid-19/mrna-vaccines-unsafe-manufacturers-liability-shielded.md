## Claim Statement

Mercola argues that the Pfizer and Moderna mRNA COVID-19 vaccines are "experimental," never-before-licensed products fast-tracked under Operation Warp Speed without animal trials or long-term safety studies, carrying serious unresolved safety risks — while their manufacturers enjoy a near-total liability shield, so the public, not the companies, bears the cost of any injuries. This combination, he says, creates a "too big to fail" product with no accountability.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 8 Mercola traces the liability regime: the **National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act of 1986** "granted drug makers partial liability protection," creating the federal Vaccine Injury Compensation Program (VICP); the 2011 Supreme Court decision **Bruesewitz v. Wyeth** (Sotomayor and Ginsburg dissenting) "effectively removed all remaining liability from vaccine manufacturers… even if there was evidence the company could have made a vaccine less likely to cause harm"; and for COVID-19 the **PREP Act** has "completely shielded them from having to pay for vaccine injuries." He stresses that when you sue, "you're actually suing the US government," with payouts funded "by the US public via a small fee tacked on to each vaccine," that the VICP has paid "over $4 billion" yet "less than 1 percent of people who are injured ever get to court," and that the new COVID-era CICP makes compensation even harder.

On safety, he argues "animal studies were bypassed entirely… due to fast-tracking" so that "millions of humans… have now become the primary test subjects"; that "mRNA vaccines have never before been licensed for use in humans" and lack long-term human data; and that they "eliminated well over six years of important testing." He raises specific concerns: the lipid nanoparticles (echoing a 2017 Stat News account of Moderna's toxicity "daunting challenge"); the uncontrolled, continual production of spike protein with "no ability to shut down"; potential "paradoxical immune enhancement" resembling the ferret studies; and listed adverse effects (inflammation, autoimmunity, edema, blood clots, anaphylaxis linked to polyethylene glycol). He quotes the framing that with "no liability… No medical product in the world would be able to go forward with a [safety] profile like Moderna has."

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 8 ("Successful Protocols Suppressed") frames the vaccine push as the profit motive behind the suppression of cheap treatments (ivermectin, hydroxychloroquine-with-zinc, vitamin D, the MATH+ protocol). The liability shield and rushed approval are presented as evidence that Big Pharma's products were "prioritized over cheaper, effective treatments," tying this claim to the book's broader suppression thesis.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Mercola notes the 1986 Act was "historic acknowledgment by the US government that… vaccines can cause injury and death," i.e. it created a compensation route, even as he argues that route is inadequate. He repeatedly qualifies the safety claims as provisional — "while data are still limited," "we have no way to accurately predict what the consequences… will be" — framing the case as concern about untested risk rather than proof of harm.
