## Claim Statement

Tablet 15 ("Secret of Secrets"), promising "no dark sayings," supplies explicit spoken **words/names of power** for releasing the self from the body and passing successive barriers, culminating in the **Key to Shamballa**, "the place where my Brothers live in the darkness." The keys given are:

- **"Dor-E-Lil-La"** — spoken after releasing the self from the body and rising to the bounds of the earth-plane; for a time the Light is lifted and one "may pass the barriers of space."
- **"La-Um-I-L-Ganoover"** — sounded over and over in the mind to "free… from the bondage of flesh."
- **"Mekut-El-Shab-El Hale-Sur-Ben-El-Zabrut Zin-Efrim-Quar-El"** — the words of power (pronounced mentally) to enter the Halls of Amenti and "stand before the Lords in their places."
- **"Edom-El-Ahim-Sabbert-Zur Adom"** — the Key to Shamballa: spoken before the gates and their guardians, with the command "Open thou the way of the Twelve and the One," to command the barriers to fall and pass "to the realm of wisdom."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "When ye have released the self from the body, rise to the outermost bounds of your earth-plane. Speak ye the word Dor-E-Lil-La… free may ye pass the barriers of space." (Tablet 15)

> "Think of this word La-Um-I-L-Ganoover and over in thy mind let it sound… Free from the bondage of flesh by thy will." (Tablet 15)

> "Pronounce the words of power I give (mentally); Mekut-El-Shab-El Hale-Sur-Ben-El-Zabrut Zin-Efrim-Quar-El." (Tablet 15)

> "Now give I the Key to Shamballa, the place where my Brothers live in the darkness… Stand before the gates and their guardians… Open thou the way of the Twelve and the One… command them to open by these words of power… Open, I command, by the Secret of Secrets Edom-El-Ahim-Sabbert-Zur Adom." (Tablet 15)

## Contexts Within the Source

The keys are framed as the long-withheld "Secret of Secrets" now plainly spoken. Their efficacy is conditioned: the seeker "Pure must ye be in mind and in purpose, else only failure will come unto thee," and at the gates "if thy words have been Truth of the highest, open for thee the barriers will fall." The passage closes Thoth's instruction with his descent "Down, yet up, to the Halls," inviting his children to "Win ye the way to me."
