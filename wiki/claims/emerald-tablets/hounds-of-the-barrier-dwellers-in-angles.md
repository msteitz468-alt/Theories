## Claim Statement

Tablet 8 describes the **Hounds of the Barrier**, also called the **Dwellers in Angles**, as guardian entities lying in wait at the boundary of space-time ("the great Barrier… on the shores where time exists not"). Thoth recounts encountering them on his journeys "through SPACE-TIME, even to the end of space of this cycle." They scent the approaching soul, give "the great bell cry that could be heard from cycle to cycle," and pursue the soul "moving in strange angles not known to man." A soul that dares the Barrier "may be held in bondage by the HOUNDS from beyond time." Their key limitation: they "move only through angles and never through curves of space," so only by moving "through curves," circles, and the cross-and-circle, and uttering "the WORD," can a soul escape them and flee back to the body. Only "the one who of LIGHT has the fullest" can hope to pass the guardians.

> "Know ye, the HOUNDS of the Barrier move only through angles and never through curves of space. Only by moving through curves can ye escape them, for in angles they will pursue thee." (Tablet 8)

> "Only the one who of LIGHT has the fullest can hope to pass by the guards of the way." (Tablet 8)

## Contexts Within the Source

The passage is delivered as a warning ("heed ye my warning; Seek not to break open the gate to beyond"). Thoth advises that rather than attempting to pass the guards, the seeker "should… seek to gain of thine own Light and make thyself ready to pass on the way." The Hounds are presented as part of the larger Tablet 8 teaching on the angular versus curved geometry of space (continued in Tablet 9's "angles and curves" cosmology).
