## Claim Statement

The text asserts that its author, Thoth, was an Atlantean priest-king and immortal who, after the sinking of Atlantis, founded a colony in ancient Egypt ("Khem") and built the Great Pyramid of Giza — "erroneously attributed to Cheops" — over the entrance to the Halls of Amenti, ruling the ancient race of Egypt for some 16,000 years (c. 52,000–36,000 B.C.).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Doreal's preface: "The writer is Thoth, an Atlantean Priest-King, who founded a colony in ancient Egypt after the sinking of the mother country. He was the builder of the Great Pyramid of Giza, erroneously attributed to Cheops." In Tablet 1, Thoth says: "Builded I the Great Pyramid, patterned after the pyramid of Earth force… In it, I builded my knowledge of 'Magic-Science.'" He built it "using the power that overcomes Earth force (gravity)" and set "the crystal" in its apex.

## Contexts Within the Source

The preface frames Thoth as later deified by the pyramid priests as the Egyptian God of Wisdom, and as reincarnating three times, "in his last being known as Hermes, the thrice-born." This grounds the rest of the tablets' authority. See [[The Great Pyramid of Giza]], [[Halls of Amenti]].
