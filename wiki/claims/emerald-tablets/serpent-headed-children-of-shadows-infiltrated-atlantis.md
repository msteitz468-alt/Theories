## Claim Statement

Tablet 8 asserts that, in ages before and during Atlantis, men who "delved into darkness, using dark magic" called up formless beings "from the great deep below." Of "another vibration" and ordinarily unseen, these entities could take bodily form "only through blood" and could "live in the world" only "through man." Conquered in an earlier age and driven back below by the Masters, some remained hidden. In Atlantis they crept into the **Councils** disguised as men, slew the kings/chiefs by their arts, and "ruled o'er man" in their place. Their true nature was **serpent-headed**, visible only "when the glamour was lifted." The Masters eventually lifted the veil and cast them out by a "Word that only a man can pronounce," but Thoth warns the serpent "still liveth" in a hidden place and "again as time passes onward shall they take the semblance of men."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "Far in the past before Atlantis existed, men there were who delved into darkness, using dark magic, calling up beings from the great deep below us… Only through blood could they have formed being. Only through man could they live in the world." (Tablet 8)

> "Serpent-headed when the glamour was lifted but appearing to man as men among men. Crept they into the Councils, taking forms that were like unto men. Slaying by their arts the chiefs of the kingdoms, taking their form and ruling o'er man." (Tablet 8)

> "But, know ye, the Masters were mighty in magic, able to lift the Veil from the face of the serpent… Came they to man and taught him the secret, the WORD that only a man can pronounce. Swift then they lifted the Veil from the serpent and cast him forth from the place among men." (Tablet 8)

> "Yet, beware, the serpent still liveth in a place that is open at times to the world… Again as time passes onward shall they take the semblance of men." (Tablet 8)

## Contexts Within the Source

The episode is framed as concealed history ("knowledge hidden from man"), introduced after the teaching that "mystery is but hidden knowledge." The text states such beings "Called may they be by the master who knows the white or the black, but only the white master may control and bind them while in the flesh," and repeatedly warns the reader, "Seek not the kingdom of shadows, for evil will surely appear."
