## Claim Statement

Ronson reports the legend — and his pursuit of it — that a Special Forces master sergeant once killed a goat purely by staring at it, as part of attempts to weaponize the "deadly stare." The setting is "Goat Lab," a secret facility at Fort Bragg holding roughly 100 **de-bleated goats**, known only to a few insiders.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per Ronson's source Glenn Wheaton, Goat Lab (originally "Dog Lab," switched to goats because soldiers wouldn't bond with goats) was created to give Special Forces in-the-field surgical training; the goats were de-bleated, shot, operated on, and closed up again. Ronson notes the goat-staring legend is unverifiable — the goat killed "by staring" may already have been "in a shaky medical condition." He could find no documentary confirmation, reaching "a dead end regarding Goat Lab."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Ronson treats the staring-death as folklore he could not confirm, openly entertaining mundane explanations (a sick or wounded goat).
