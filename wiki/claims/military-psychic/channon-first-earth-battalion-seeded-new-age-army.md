## Claim Statement

Ronson identifies Lt. Col. [[Jim Channon]]'s 1979 *First Earth Battalion Operations Manual* as the wellspring of the army's New Age turn. Rather than command a literal unit, Channon let his ideas diffuse "into the fabric of the army," where they inspired real programs even when the soldiers implementing them knew nothing of their "fantastic provenance."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The manual envisioned soldiers as peaceful "warrior monks" with uniforms carrying ginseng regulators and divining tools and loudspeakers emitting "indigenous music and words of peace." Ronson traces concrete descendants: Colonel **John Alexander**'s non-lethal weapons (Sticky Foam; acoustic weapons such as the Blast Wave Projector and infrasound, per a leaked 2002 USAF report *Non-Lethal Weapons: Terms and References*), Dr. Jim Hardt's brainwave "Zen master" machine, and Stubblebine's psychic-soldier push. Channon, Ronson notes, declined a real command because his superiors would have demanded "measurable results" (soldiers literally stopping their hearts) and shut the unit down when they failed.
