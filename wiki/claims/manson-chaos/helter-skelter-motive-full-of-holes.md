## Claim Statement

O'Neill argues that prosecutor Vincent Bugliosi's official "Helter Skelter" motive — that Manson ordered the murders to ignite an apocalyptic black-versus-white race war foretold in a Beatles song — is "an official narrative that few have ever thought to question" yet is full of "contradictions, omissions, discrepancies with police reports." He contends the real story is unknown, but the accepted one is demonstrably unreliable.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

O'Neill reports finding notes "in Vince's own handwriting" indicating that a lead witness (Terry Melcher) lied under oath about how many times he had met Manson; co-prosecutor Stephen Kay told O'Neill the notes "could be enough to overturn all the verdicts." He documents that Bugliosi omitted the Family's formative San Francisco/Haight-Ashbury year from *Helter Skelter*, and kept the Family's heavy **amphetamine** use out of the trial (Kasabian, Atkins, and Watson later said they took speed before the murders) because it suggested the killings were a "paranoid speed delusion" rather than a scripted race-war plot. In a six-hour 2006 interview Bugliosi threatened O'Neill with "a hundred-million-dollar libel lawsuit"; O'Neill stresses he is "not a Manson apologist" and thinks Manson "every bit as evil as the media made him out to be."
