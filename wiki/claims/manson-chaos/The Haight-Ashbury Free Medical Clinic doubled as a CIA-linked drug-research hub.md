## Claim Statement

O'Neill reports that the famous "free" clinic founded by Dr. David E. Smith was also a drug-research operation: patients were, in one critic's words, treated as "research subjects." Both David Smith and Manson's parole officer Roger Smith (no relation) ran amphetamine/LSD studies there — funded by the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH), which O'Neill notes "later acknowledged that the CIA used it as a front for LSD research" — during the exact period when Manson and the Family were frequent visitors and Manson was using LSD to "reprogram" his followers.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

O'Neill highlights the "suspicious" overlap: how did an "uneducated ex-con" who had never taken acid come to use LSD "to such sophisticated ends," while visiting two researchers studying drug-based behavior control? David Smith's rodent studies (dosing crowded mice/rats with amphetamines, and — he first denied, then admitted — with LSD "to make them suggestible") echoed John B. Calhoun's "behavioral sink" (a dominant male, a "harem," and predatory "probers" — which O'Neill notes "sounded a lot like the Family"). Roger Smith's Amphetamine Research Project (ARP) extended this to people; its files, multiple staff (Pittel, Dernburg) told O'Neill, were **stolen right after Manson's arrest**, suspected to be a police or federal removal. O'Neill repeatedly flags that he "never came close to proving" deliberate wrongdoing.
