## Claim Statement

Per the book, the evidence from quantum physics — particularly the observer effect, John Archibald Wheeler's "participatory anthropic principle," and the Global Consciousness Project's REG data — demonstrates that mind and consciousness are not mere by-products of brain chemistry but are intimately and causally connected to the material universe at a fundamental level. The authors contend this is the most direct scientific vindication of the Hermetic teaching that the cosmos is mental in nature and that human consciousness has a special creative role in it.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**The observer effect and wave-particle duality.** Feynman's double-slit experiment is the paradigm case: a photon behaves as a wave or particle depending on whether an observer has information about which slit it passed through. The authors quote Paul Davies: "It's almost as if the photon can be in two places at once." Richard Feynman called wave-particle duality "the mystery which cannot go away." The outcome depends not on physical interaction but on the experimenter's *knowledge* — "the behaviour of the particle itself that seems to depend on what the observer knows."

**Wheeler's participatory anthropic principle (PAP).** John Archibald Wheeler's "delayed choice" experiment shows that an observer's present choice can determine how a particle behaved in the past — including light emitted by stars billions of years ago, bent around black holes. Wheeler concluded: "our very act of measurement not only revealed the nature of the photon's history on its way to us, but in some sense determined that history. The past history of the universe has no more validity than is assigned by the measurements we make — now!" He termed the universe "participatory" and proposed that "observership is the mechanism of genesis" — conscious observers are necessary for the universe to exist. Bernard Carr is quoted: "the universe does not even come into being in a well-defined way until an observer is produced who can perceive it."

The authors note Barrow and Tipler's observation that Wheeler's ideas are "strikingly similar" to Teilhard de Chardin's arguments about the size and age of the universe being necessary to produce life.

**The Global Consciousness Project.** Dick Bierman (University of Amsterdam) accidentally discovered that Random Event Generators (REGs) became non-random during the 1995 UEFA Champions League final when Ajax scored. Dean Radin and Roger Nelson of Princeton subsequently established a permanent network of 65 REGs worldwide and found statistically significant non-random output coinciding with major global events, most dramatically on 11 September 2001, when Radin noted "our bell rang more loudly on this day than any other day in 2001." The authors conclude: "Human consciousness really does seem to have a tangible effect on the material world."

**Hawking's top-down cosmology.** Hawking, Hartle, and Hertog's work extending Wheeler's ideas — "We create history by our observation, rather than history creating us" — is cited as confirming that present observations collapse wave functions reaching back to the big bang itself.

**Connection to Hermeticism.** The authors quote Corpus Hermeticum Treatise XI directly: "So you must think of god in this way, as having everything — the cosmos, himself, <the> universe — like thoughts within himself." Wheeler's participatory universe is identified as expressing "essentially the same thing as the Hermetica: the circular relationship between mind and the universe makes human consciousness necessary for the completion of the universe." Erich Jantsch's conclusion — "God is not the creator, but the mind of the universe" — is cited, with the note that Jantsch explicitly traced this back to Hermetic philosophy.

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 12 is the pivot of Part Two: it moves from cosmology and biology to physics of mind, making the positive case that the Hermetic worldview is not merely compatible with science but is being empirically rediscovered. The chapter also engages the sub-field of parapsychology, presenting Brian Josephson (Nobel laureate) as a credible scientific voice taking psi seriously, and noting the convergence between quantum nonlocality and parapsychological phenomena — without endorsing specific psi claims. Brian Josephson's critique of "pathological disbelief" in the scientific community is quoted.

The authors are careful to note that Wheeler himself was fiercely opposed to parapsychology and explicitly rejected the use of his ideas to validate psi phenomena — yet his own theory is more radical than most paranormal claims.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The authors acknowledge that consciousness-quantum links remain speculative ("still very early days") and that the Global Consciousness Project's results are contested on methodological grounds. They also note that Wheeler's PAP has been termed "PAP" by critics and that its implications are "extreme" and prone to misuse. They do not claim that existing REG data proves a "global consciousness" — only that it shows "something" is happening.
