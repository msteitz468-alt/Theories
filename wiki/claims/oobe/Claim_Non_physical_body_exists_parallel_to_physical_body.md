## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that human consciousness possesses a non-physical body (or "Second Body") that exists parallel to and independently of the physical form. This Second Body can separate from the physical form while consciousness remains intact and aware. The Second Body maintains a precise replica of the physical body's form "down to the last hair follicle and scar" but can be deliberately modified through thought.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Primary source: Chapter 13, "The Second Body"**

Monroe states: "The form of your physical body is carried over in replica down to the last hair follicle and scar, unless you deliberately think otherwise."

He elaborates on its modification capacity: "I suspect that one may modify the Second Body into whatever form is desired. Once the thought is discarded, the Second Body will drop back into its habitual humanoid shape."

**Experiential evidence provided:**

Monroe documents systematic experiments demonstrating non-physical arm independence:
- Release of extremities (Chapter 17): Monroe reaches out with his Second Body arm, encounters objects at distances physically unreachable, and pushes them through solid matter (walls, doors, etc.)
- Hand conjunction test: Monroe moves only his non-physical hands above his physical body while his physical arms remain at rest, observing both simultaneously
- Quote: "At this point, your hand will seem to go right through the object. Keep pushing until your hand is completely through the object and meets some other physical object."

**Alleged verification:**
Monroe claims to have verified the accuracy of these experiences against physical reality by:
1. Noting small physical details of objects felt through non-physical arm contact
2. Comparing his recorded descriptions with physical examination of the same objects
3. Testing whether distances and object positions correspond to physical reality

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe frames this claim within the context of systematic investigation of consciousness:
- Treats non-physical body as scientifically observable phenomenon
- Presents it as result of deliberate experimentation, not imagination or dream
- Emphasizes repeatability and trainability of the experience
- Uses engineering and scientific terminology to describe the phenomenon

**Monroe's methodological stance:**
He argues the claim should be treated as hypothesis subject to empirical verification through systematic experimentation, not as revealed truth or speculation.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Monroe acknowledges challenges to verification:

1. **Perceptual misidentification**: The mind tends to rationalize unfamiliar perceptions, matching them to past experience. Monroe documents his own errors (Van de Graaff generator perceived as "child's automobile") and argues this is inherent to perception of non-physical reality, not unique to understanding the Second Body.

2. **Thought-responsiveness uncertainty**: Monroe notes uncertainty about whether the apparent materiality of the Second Body is genuine or thought-created: "There seems to be nothing that thought cannot produce in this new-old other life."

3. **Incomplete understanding**: Monroe acknowledges that many aspects remain unclear and subject to further investigation. He states: "To put it another way, there seems to be nothing that thought cannot produce in this new-old other life. This invites a note of caution in large red letters..."

4. **Limited verification possibilities**: For experiences in more distant realms (Locales II and III), direct physical verification becomes impossible, limiting confirmability of the Second Body's existence outside Locale I.
