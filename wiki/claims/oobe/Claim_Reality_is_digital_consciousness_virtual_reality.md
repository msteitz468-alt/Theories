## Claim Statement

Thomas Campbell asserts that physical reality — our space-time universe with its matter, energy, forces, and laws — is a virtual reality simulation running within a digital consciousness system called [[Concept: AUM (Absolute Unbounded Manifold)|AUM]]. Physical space, time, and matter are perceptual constructs — the experience delivered to individuated consciousness by a rule-set, not ultimately real in themselves. The underlying reality is digital consciousness, not matter.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Direct statement of the claim:**

> "Physical space, and your experience of it, is an illusion, a trick of your individuated mind, a virtual reality within the actual reality of your consciousness... Space as a mental construct, not physical space itself; there is no such thing as physical space!"

**On space-time as rule-set product:**

> "Space-time is a particular pattern of constraints applied to the energy transfers between individuated consciousnesses. Space-time exists within the digital media that supports the rule-set that defines our perception and thus our physical experience."

**On the digital substrate:**

> "All cellular and digital creations exhibit granularity at some level of detail. Both our reality and our perception of it are granular at the root."

**On the scope of the claim:**

> "Our space-time universe and small blue planet represent only one special-case application of that process [the Fundamental Process]. We, our universe, and other universes in other dimensions are all specialized thought-parts or subsets of AUM."

**On derivation from two assumptions:**

> "From only those two assumptions [AUO and the Fundamental Process], I intend to derive physics, the nature of your existence, your physical universe and experience, and provide a comprehensive working model of the larger reality."

## Contexts Within the Source

### Scientific Framework
Campbell presents this not as mysticism but as a scientific model. He argues:
- The claim is logically derived from minimum assumptions
- It is more consistent with known physics than materialist alternatives
- It resolves the mind-matter dichotomy (both are products of consciousness)
- It provides a working model that explains paranormal phenomena within normal science

### Physics Consistency
Campbell argues his model is **consistent with general relativity and quantum mechanics**:
- Curved space-time (general relativity) is consistent with Euclidean conceptual framework for virtual space in AUM's mind
- Quantum granularity is expected from a digital consciousness substrate
- The speed of light is derived as an evolved constraint, not an arbitrary brute fact

### Personal Verification
Campbell bases his claim on decades of personal exploration in [[Concept: NPMR (Nonphysical Matter Reality)|NPMR]], including experiences within the Monroe Institute's research program, as empirical evidence that consciousness exists independently of the physical body and can access non-physical realities.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Campbell acknowledges significant challenges to verification:

1. **Bootstrap problem**: The perceptual systems available to verify the claim are themselves products of the PMR rule-set being described. Expanding awareness beyond PMR limitations requires the very consciousness development the theory advocates.

2. **Working model disclaimer**: Campbell explicitly presents My Big TOE as a "working model" subject to revision, not as proven fact. He argues it is the most parsimonious explanation of available data but acknowledges alternative possibilities.

3. **Consciousness quality dependency**: Full understanding requires personal consciousness development, not just intellectual analysis. Campbell acknowledges this appears circular.

4. **No direct physical test**: The claim that physical reality is virtual cannot, by design, be definitively confirmed or refuted from within physical reality using physical instruments.

5. **Scale of claims**: Campbell notes his claims are "so far away from the thoughts you are probably used to thinking" and requires willingness to entertain fundamental assumptions unfamiliar in mainstream science.

## Relationship to Monroe's Framework

Campbell was a direct collaborator with [[Robert Monroe (1915–1995)|Robert A. Monroe]] at the [[Monroe Institute|Monroe Institute of Applied Sciences]]. Monroe's empirical out-of-body research provided the experiential data on which Campbell built his theoretical framework. Monroe's [[Concept: The Second State|Second State]] and [[Concept: Locales in Non-Physical Reality|Locales]] correspond to what Campbell formalizes as NPMR. Monroe documented the phenomena empirically; Campbell provides the theoretical framework.
