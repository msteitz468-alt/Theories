## Claim Statement

Monroe asserts that a distinctive energetic phenomenon he terms the "vibrational state" is an essential prerequisite for separating the non-physical body from the physical form. Without achieving the vibrational state, separation cannot occur. The vibrational state consists of a rhythmic, energetic sensation approximately 27 cycles per second that can be felt throughout the body and controlled through mental direction.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Primary source: Chapters 16–17**

Monroe defines the vibrational state: "It is as if a surging, hissing, rhythmically pulsating wave of fiery sparks comes roaring into your head. From there it seems to sweep throughout your body, making it rigid and immobile."

**On necessity:**
Monroe states: "It is essential that you learn and apply this speed-up process. The faster vibration effect is the form that permits disassociation from the physical."

**Frequency specification:**
"The original vibratory rate seems to be on the order of some twenty-seven cycles per second (this is the rate of the vibration itself, not the head-to-toe frequency)."

**On achievement:**
"Consistent achievement of this stage is the sign that you are ready for the first physical disassociation experiments."

**Documented progression:**
Monroe provides extensive documentation of his development of technique to reliably establish the vibrational state through:
1. Breathing techniques (half-opened mouth breathing)
2. Mental concentration (converging lines exercise at specific distances)
3. Reaching and stretching toward established point
4. Gradual frequency increase through "pulsing"

## Contexts Within the Source

Monroe presents the vibrational state as:

1. **Physical phenomenon, not imagination**: "There is no evidence to indicate that this vibrational state has a deleterious effect on either the mind or the physical body."

2. **Learnable and reproducible**: "Once you have learned the process, or the concept, it will not be necessary to go through the entire routine. You need only to think of the vibrations while in a relaxed state, and they come into being. A conditioned reflex has been established, or a neuron path that can be followed again and again."

3. **Subject to systematic investigation**: Monroe documents hundreds of trials developing technique and control procedures.

4. **Trainable in others**: The methodology is presented as teachable; Monroe's later establishment of training programs at the [[Monroe Institute|Monroe Institute of Applied Sciences]] presumes vibrational state induction is trainable to multiple individuals.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Monroe acknowledges limitations and uncertainties:

1. **Variability in reproducibility**: "However, once you have succeeded, it is not always repeatable at will. There are still many variables that interfere which have yet to be isolated and identified."

2. **Individual differences**: Not all individuals achieve results equally easily or quickly. "The probability of success increases with each successive effort. The more often you attempt it, the more likely you are to have positive results."

3. **Requires specific conditions**: Monroe implies certain physical, mental, and environmental conditions optimize success, though he does not fully articulate all variables.

4. **Time requirements**: Monroe indicates significant practice and patience are required; results are not immediate for most practitioners.
