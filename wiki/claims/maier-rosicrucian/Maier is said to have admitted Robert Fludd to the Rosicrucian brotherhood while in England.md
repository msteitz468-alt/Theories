**Claim Statement**
It is said that it was at Maier's instigation that Fludd wrote or published, in 1617, his "Tractatus Theologo-Philosophicus," dedicated to the brethren of the Rosy Cross; and that Maier, having become a member of the Rosicrucian order, admitted Fludd to its privileges while Maier was in England.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
"It is said that it was at Maier's instigation Fludd wrote, or at least published, in 1617 his most excellent 'Tractatus Theologo-Philosophicus,' dedicated to the brethren of the Rosy Cross. We are told that Maier, having become a member of this mysterious order, admitted Fludd to its privileges when in England." Craven adds: "Fludd's 'Apologia' is said to have been written at the instigation or request of Maier. It was published at Leyden in 1616, and again in 1617."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven immediately qualifies the claim: "The whole matter is, however, buried in obscurity, if not in contradiction." He directs readers wishing to pursue the subject further to Dr Wynn Westcott's paper in "Ars Quat. Coron." and to his own "Life and Writings of Dr Robert Fludd."
