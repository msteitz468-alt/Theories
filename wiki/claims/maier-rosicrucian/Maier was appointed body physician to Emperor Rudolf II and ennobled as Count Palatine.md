**Claim Statement**
After graduating in medicine (apparently at Rostock), Maier came under the notice of Emperor Rudolf II, was appointed a body physician to the Emperor, was summoned to Prague, was ennobled by Rudolf as Pfalzgraf (Count Palatine), and served as his private secretary.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven writes: "he soon proved himself to be a man of distinction, and came under the notice of the Emperor Rudolph II. He was appointed a body physician to the Emperor." Later: "Rudolph resided at Prague, to which he summoned Maier, ennobled him, making him Pfalzgraf—Count Palatine—and his private secretary." Craven adds biographical detail on Rudolf II himself: son of Maximilian II and Maria of Austria, born in Vienna in 1552, crowned Emperor and King of Hungary in 1572 and King of Bohemia in 1575, devoted to science, host to Tycho Brahe and Kepler (the "Rudolphine" calendars), and died 20 January 1612 in the 60th year of his age.

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven notes Maier "does not appear, however, to have been in constant attendance on the Emperor," citing that in 1611 Maier was in Amsterdam viewing a shell collection. After Rudolf's death, Maier travelled to England.
