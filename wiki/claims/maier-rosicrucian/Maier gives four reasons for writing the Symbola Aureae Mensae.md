**Claim Statement**
In the preface to the *Symbola Aureae Mensae*, Maier gives four reasons for writing the book: (1) the antiquity and widespread fame of Chemia in different nations and places deserved to be recorded and better known; (2) its true authors, practitioners, and writers should be rescued from malevolent and untrue aspersions; (3) the adversaries of the art should be known and the fallacy of their sayings indicated, so the true place of Chemia as Queen of the Arts should be upheld; and (4) [implicitly] the truth of her labours for thousands of years should be recorded, invoking the Father of lights to shower gifts on those who vindicate the chastity of the "virginal science."

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven paraphrases: "The author gives four reasons for writing his book. 1. The antiquity and the widespread fame of Chemia in different nations and places deserved to be recorded and better known. 2. Its true authors, practises, and writers should be rescued from malevolent and untrue aspersions. 3. That the adversaries of the art should be known, the fallacy of their sayings should be indicated, so that the true place of Chemia, as the Queen of the Arts, should be duly upheld, and the truth of her labours for thousands of years recorded."

**Contexts Within the Source**
The preface describes an ancient Roman custom of celebrating triumphs at a Golden Table, citing Lucius Verus's banquet with twelve guests as a model for the assembly of the twelve alchemists.
