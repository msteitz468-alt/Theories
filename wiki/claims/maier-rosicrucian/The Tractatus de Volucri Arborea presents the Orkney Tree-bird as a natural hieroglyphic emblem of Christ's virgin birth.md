**Claim Statement**
Maier presents the Tree-bird, born without human father through a "divine mother" (the tree), as an emblem of Christ, who was born into the world without a human father, becoming the Mediator between God and man; the tree from which the bird hangs is likened to the Cross by which man is elevated to heaven.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
In his preface, Maier states the birds "are to be to us emblems of the power and knowledge of God, who Himself was born into the world without a human father through a divine mother, and became the Mediator between God and man." Craven records Maier's extended mystical passage: "It is Christ, who, without father or ordinary mother, was born... The tree from which he hangs is the cross, and by it is man elevated to heaven. He bare the cross and our sins on His shoulders, to cast our sins into the bottomless ocean." Maier also allegorizes the classical myth of Alcyone (who with Ceyx was transformed into a bird by the gods) as typifying the Church tossed on the waves of the world.

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven notes that Max Müller and others considered this Christ-comparison "blasphemous," but records Maier's (and Craven's) view that it was not so intended, comparing it to the fish-symbol of the Catacombs teaching baptism, or circumcision as token of an older covenant — a natural illustration of a believed truth, even though the underlying zoological comparison is now known to be false.
