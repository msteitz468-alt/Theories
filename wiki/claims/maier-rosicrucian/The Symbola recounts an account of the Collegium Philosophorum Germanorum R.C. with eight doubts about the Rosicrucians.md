**Claim Statement**
Following the chapter on Albertus Magnus (representing Germany), the Symbola includes a section titled "Collegium Philosophorum Germanorum R.C.," presenting eight "dubia" (doubts) concerning the Rosicrucians, with Maier's answers to each, followed by "Aenigmata a IX. Musis et Apolline de Collegio Germanorum Philosophorum R.C."

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven: "After this comes an account of 'Collegium Philosophorum Germanorum R.C.' A number of 'dubia,' eight in number, are given as to the Rosicrusians, with answers by Maier to these doubts. Follows 'Ænigmata a IX. Musis et Apolline de Collegio Germanorum Philosophorum R.C.' The article ends in the usual form."

**Contexts Within the Source**
This section is embedded within the German chapter (Albertus Magnus), whose "congentiles" listed include Trevisan, Basil Valentine (author of the "Triumphal Chariot of Antimony"), Isaac Holland, Pontanus, and Paracelsus.
