**Claim Statement**
Maier's medical treatise on gout, arthritis, and related joint diseases presents the human body as a "Civitas" (city-state / lesser world) whose "citizens" are its anatomical members; its government is likened not to a pure monarchy but to the Venetian Republic, with the heart as a prince of limited power ruling over an aristocracy of organs (throat, liver, oesophagus, teeth, tongue), while gout and similar diseases arise as a kind of tyranny or sedition afflicting the extremities furthest from the heart (hands and feet), caused by failures of diligence in the liver, stomach, and other organs.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven quotes/paraphrases: "in the body politic there are three States. First, the King or Prince; second, the order of Aristocracy; and thirdly, the Democracy... What form of government then exists in the human centres? It is not exactly regal, but may rather be compared to the order of the Venetian Republic, in which a number of magnates reign under a prince of limited power. The heart is that prince." Further: "It is in the inferior parts of the body furthest from the heart that the tyranny of the enemies of the 'civitas' is to be found, and then the hands and feet suffer from gout and the like diseases."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven notes the treatise attributes gout to intemperance — Maier states "Gout is ever the companion of riches and delicate living... Venus and Bacchus are the greatest friends of gout," and cites Hippocrates's rule of continence, activity, and periodic abstinence from supper. Maier also compares the gradual accumulation of the gouty secretion ("gutta humoris") to a dripping drop wearing away stone, or an ant's continual labor building its home.
