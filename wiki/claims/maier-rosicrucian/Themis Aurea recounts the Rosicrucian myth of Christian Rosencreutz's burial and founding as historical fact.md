**Claim Statement**
Craven notes that in Themis Aurea, "the story of Christian Rosencreutz, at least the history of his burial, discovery, and the alleged dates of the founding of the society, are treated as facts," and that the mysterious "Book M" referenced by the Brethren may signify either "Meus" or "Mundus," and the place "C" (site of the "house of S. Spiritus") may signify "Corpus."

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven: "The story of Christian Bosencreutz [Rosencreutz], at least the history of his burial, discovery, and the alleged dates of the founding of the society, are treated as facts. There may be some reason to suppose these in a measure correct. The 'Book M' may be either 'Meus,' or 'Mundus,' the place 'C,' where the house of S. Spiritus is existent 'Corpus.'"

**Contexts Within the Source**
Themis Aurea describes the Book "M" as containing "the perfection of all the Arts, beginning with the Heavens, and descending to lower Sciences," and describes the Brethren as possessing "the true Astronomy, the true Physics, Mathematicks, Medicine, and Chymistry."
