**Claim Statement**
Maier's *Cantilenae Intellectuales de Phoenice Redivivo* presents nine "Triads" of rhymed songs, each triad structured in three parts (name/allegory/religious application) expounding "the key of the three impenetrable Secrets of Chemistry," using the classical Phoenix myth (the bird that dies in fire on its nest at Thebes and is reborn) as the central allegory for the alchemical Great Work, explicitly paralleled throughout with Christian doctrines of Incarnation, Crucifixion, Eucharist, and Resurrection.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven quotes the work's stated design: "Nine Triads of Intellectual Songs on the Resurrection of the Phoenix: or the most precious of all medicines, the mirror and abridgement of this Universe... presented to the wise as the key of the three impenetrable Secrets of Chemistry," with the plan of each triad given as "1. The names. 2. The allegories. 3. The application of the Mysteries of the Art to those of Religion." Examples cited by Craven: the Seventh Triad likens Adam formed of red earth to the sages' "matter of red earth," and states "He who sees how Jesus Christ saved us from death will understand the art and the purification and colour of metals"; the Eighth Triad ties the alchemical "liquor for a new birth" to baptism and Christ's Passion, noting "streams of blood flowing which, when they penetrate metals, preserve them in violent fires."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven notes the work is dedicated to Frederick, Hereditary Prince of Norway (dated Rostock, 25 August 1622), in which Maier recounts a life spent in hermetic study "at great expense, frequently repeated," enduring "bitter griefs, frequent disappointments," and states he is a native of Holstein who left fourteen years earlier to complete his hermetic studies abroad, intending to return.
