**Claim Statement**
Michael Maier was born at Rendsburg, in Holstein, about the year 1568.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven states: "ALL authorities agree that Michael Maier was born at Rendsburg, in Holstein, about the year 1568. The date is taken from the inscription on his portrait as prefixed to his 'Symbola,' 'Atalanta fugiens,' and 'Septimana Philosophica.' The picture was painted in 1617, when he was forty-nine years of age." Rendsburg is described as "a town on the north side of the now famous Baltic Canal, nineteen miles west of Kiel, with a population of some 15,000."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven notes that the church records of Rendsburg extend only to the seventeenth century and so cannot directly confirm Maier's birth; the 1568 date rests on the portrait inscription. Craven also cites correspondence with Herr R. R. von Lilienstern, first Burgomaster of Rendsburg, noting several individuals named Meyer/Meyger connected to Rendsburg in the sixteenth century (a church official in 1541, a Lutheran clergyman in 1577, a tax-collector, a dyke inspector), though none is positively identified as Maier's father.

**Opposing Information Within the Source**
None presented; Craven states "all authorities agree" on this point, though he acknowledges the documentary basis is indirect (a portrait inscription rather than parish records).
