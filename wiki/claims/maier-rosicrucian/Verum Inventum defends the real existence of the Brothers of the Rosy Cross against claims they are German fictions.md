**Claim Statement**
In the chymia section of Verum Inventum, Maier answers the charge that the Brothers of the Rosy Cross "exist nowhere, but are fictitious phantasms of the German brain merely," asserting this to be false and referring to proofs already given "in the twelfth book, the 'Symbolis Aureae Mensae.'" He argues the Brethren, possessing the divine art for some 600 years from their founder, deserve reverence, being "the very asylum of piety," and that if such virtuous men possess the art, it must indeed be the perfection of science.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven: "Some, indeed, have said that the brethren of the R.C. exist nowhere, but are fictitious phantasms of the German brain merely. We have already shown this to be false. In the twelfth book, the 'Symbolis Aureae Mensae,' we have given proofs to the contrary." Also: "Believing that they have this divine art for the last [6]00 years, having received it from their founder; that they themselves deserve the reverence of all men; that their society is the very asylum of piety; that in it are gathered together all virtue, temperance, strictness, chastity; that they do not give themselves up to ease, but to the assistance of humanity."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Maier defends alchemical transmutation against the charge of impossibility by citing Moses's dissolving of the golden calf into golden water for the Israelites to drink, and argues the calumniators of the art deserve severe punishment, "a doctrine shown to be true from the case of Miriam and from the writings of St Jerome, St Gregory, Origen, St Bernard." He also compares the treatment of the Rosicrucian Brethren to the unjust blame placed on Luther for the Anabaptists of Münster and on St Paul for the Ephesian riot.
