**Claim Statement**
After the death of Rudolf II, Maier visited England, where he made the acquaintance of Doctor Robert Fludd, Sir William Paddy, Sir Thomas Smith, and Francis Anthony. Maier's first publication, the "Arcana Arcanissima," was dedicated to Sir William Paddy (physician to King James I, later President of the College of Physicians of London). Francis Anthony (a Cambridge graduate and self-styled discoverer of "Aurum Potabile") was one of three friends to whom the "Lusus Serius" was dedicated, in a dedication dated at Frankfort "ex Anglia reditu, Pragam abituriens anno 1616, Mense Septembri" (on his return from England, about to depart for Prague, September 1616).

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
"After the death of Rudolph, Maier visited England, where he made the acquaintance of Doctor Robert Fludd, Sir William Paddy, Sir Thomas Smith, and Francis Anthony." Craven gives biographical notes: Paddy was "a fellow of St John's College, Oxford, a graduate in medicine of Leyden, afterwards President of the College of Physicians of London, the friend of Laud, and a benefactor to St John's"; Thomas Smith was ambassador to Russia in 1604, first governor of the East India Company, treasurer of the Virginia Company, and died 4 April 1625; Francis Anthony, son of a London goldsmith, "pretended to be the first discoverer" of "Aurum Potabile" and died 26 May 1623.

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven states that "the most distinguished friend in England whom Maier had was the famous Doctor Robert Fludd," and that "when in England Maier 'lived on friendly terms' with Fludd."
