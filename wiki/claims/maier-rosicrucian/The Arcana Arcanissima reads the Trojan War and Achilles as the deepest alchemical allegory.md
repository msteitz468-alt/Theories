**Claim Statement**
Book 6 of the "Arcana Arcanissima" treats the Trojan Expedition, the golden apple of Discord, and above all the story of Achilles as the highest and most arcane point of the whole allegorical system: Achilles, dipped in fire nightly by Thetis to burn away his mortal parts and trained by the centaur Cheiron, together with the vessel-like enclosure of Helen and Paris at Troy, and the Phoenix rising from Troy's ashes, symbolize the philosophic vessel, its fire, and the culmination of the Work.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
"The overthrow of Troy-town is, under the philosophic and arcane story, the most arcane of all; and the highest points—the very 'arcanissima'—relate to the great and noble Achilles... 'Hoc sit clavis totius artis.'" On Thetis and Achilles: "To make her son immortal, Thetis anoints him with ambrosia by day, and holds him in the fire at night to destroy his mortal elements... Achilles trained in the fire, 'ut vere Pyrisous et salamandra nostra fiat,' is slain at Troy and reduced to ashes." On the vessel: "'Nihil aliud, ab Homero mystice et occulte intellectum est, quam vasis philosophi, in quo Helena et Paris, materia principalis continetur arcte conclusa...'" And: "From the ashes the Phoenix arises, Æneas, and others, who build a new city and found new kingdoms. This is the summary and total of Homer's story." Craven notes this interpretation had "long ago, in 1548," been set out "by Dionysius Zacharius Gallus."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Maier also treats the story of Vulcan and Neptune building Troy's walls, the golden apple thrown by Eris/Discord as cause of the war, and Romulus's founding of Rome, all as fabulous allegory rather than literal history: "The whole tale is so mixed up with fictitious gods and heroes as to be impossible of belief."
