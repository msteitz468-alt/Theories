**Claim Statement**
Each of the fifty emblems of Atalanta Fugiens embodies a mystic alchemical proverb in which natural or mythological imagery — the wind bearing a fruit in its belly, an eagle's nest, an egg struck by a glowing sword, the whitening of Latona, the dragon devouring its own tail, the Salamander living unhurt in fire, the death of Adonis and the reddening of the rose, the Vulture's cry on the mountain, the Coral hardening in air — all symbolize stages or properties of the Philosopher's Stone.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven catalogues numerous emblems with epigram and discursus, e.g., Emblem 14: "The Dragon eating its own tail... he even enjoys a part of his own body for food... 'The strongest of all fortitudes'"; Emblem 29: "As the Salamander lives in the fire, so does the Stone... It is uninjured, becoming cold out of the fire"; Emblem 41: Adonis killed by a wild boar, Venus coloring the roses with his blood; Emblem 39: the Coral, growing under water, hardens when brought to air, "sic lapis"; Emblem 45: "Sol et ejus umbra perficiunt opus," with silver as "but the shadow of gold." Emblem 34 is noted as showing the Sun and Moon in human form "in the act of coition, standing in a pool of water."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven, citing the "Suggestive Inquiry," states the fifty plates together "supply to the adept who holds the Clavicula a complete view of the system of the Universe, the essential unity of all things, the possible transmutation of matter, and the highest form of Theosophy able to be conceived by earthly mortals," and that "Man...is the true laboratory of the Hermetic art, his life the subject...and self-knowledge is at the root of all alchemical tradition."
