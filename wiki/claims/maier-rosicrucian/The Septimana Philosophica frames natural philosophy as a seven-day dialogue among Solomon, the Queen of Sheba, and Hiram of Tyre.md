**Claim Statement**
Maier's *Septimana Philosophica* stages a dialogue in which Solomon, the Queen of Sheba, and Hiram, Prince of Tyre, meet at Solomon's palace in Jerusalem, and Solomon sets apart six days (a philosophic week) for instruction, with a distinct subject each day: the Heaven and Elements (day 1), Meteors/imperfectly mixed bodies (day 2), fossils/things of the earth (day 3), vegetable life (day 4), animals (day 5), and man/the Microcosm (day 6), reserving the seventh as a Sabbath of rest.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Craven's summary: "The work begins with an account of the persons and circumstances at the meeting of Solomon, the Queen of Sheba, and Hiram, Prince of Tyre... He sets apart a week (six days) for a philosophic discussion or instruction, commencing with the subject 'de Cælo et Elementis.'" Each day is structured as numbered "Ænigmata" (enigmas/riddles) posed and answered among the three figures, reaching "Ænigma 319" by the sixth day, after which Solomon concludes: "according to divine precepts they shall rest to-morrow, being the Sabbath day, in which all—angels, men, the world itself—shall see an image of the rest and joy of eternity."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Craven notes the work's full title identifies these as "Ænigmata Aureola de omni Naturæ genere," proposed and resolved among Solomon, the Queen of Sheba, and Hiram "in modum Colloquii," with a folding plate depicting the "centrum mundi" cosmology and further engraved plates for each day.
