## Claim Statement

Wallis extends his Genesis argument worldwide: he claims the indigenous and ancestral traditions of cultures across the planet preserve a shared memory of **"Sky People"** — extraterrestrial visitors who "came and colonised our planet and interfered in our evolution to make us a more useful workforce." He treats folklore and mythology as "indigenous memory," the surviving record of real contact that "didn't make it on to the news."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Wallis links the Hebrew tradition to the Mesopotamian: the giant **Anakim** of the Book of Joshua descend from the *benei elohim*/Nephilim hybridization, and the name "Anakim" is "uncannily close" to the Sumerian **Anunnaki**; he chains parallel giant-progenitor names (Hebrew Og/Uj, Islamic Anaq, Greek Anax/Anactorians). He cites giant-lore across Celtic, Norse, Hindu, Native American, and Aboriginal Australian traditions, and reports anomalous large human remains (7–12 ft) found in the Americas 1819–1959, once held by the Smithsonian and later repatriated without DNA testing. His travelogue spans Greece, Peru, Kenya, Ghana, the Philippines, India, and Australia, drawing on figures such as Aboriginal Elder Shane Mortimer and anthropologist Maria Scholten.
