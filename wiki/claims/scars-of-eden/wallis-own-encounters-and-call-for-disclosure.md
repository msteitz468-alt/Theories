## Claim Statement

Wallis discloses his own unexplained experiences and ends the book by seeking to recover suppressed memories of them — framing personal, "grassroots" testimony-sharing as a route to disclosure that does not depend on governments.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Wallis recounts two 1985 anomalies: at 2 a.m. in his Bath flat he saw "five things… short, grey, almost translucent… not human" at the foot of his bed (he hissed "In the name of Jesus, get out!"); and in a Chichester health-food store he watched a tall, blonde, telepathically-coordinating "Nordic" couple with a toddler whose beauty and "energy" transfixed him. At the time he had "no language" for these or for two episodes of "missing time." The book closes with him entering a regression session with abduction therapist **Barbara Lamb** in San Diego to recover what may lie behind them. He cites the 2009 Vatican statement and Apollo 14 astronaut **Ed Mitchell**'s call to "put away this embargo of truth about the alien presence," and argues that if ordinary people "overcome our embarrassment, break the taboo and talk about this topic," their networks can become "an unstoppable tidal wave of grassroots disclosure."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Wallis repeatedly concedes the Chichester episode "sounds like nothing," that children "can have vivid imaginations," and that he shares his own story chiefly to encourage others to share theirs — not as proof.
