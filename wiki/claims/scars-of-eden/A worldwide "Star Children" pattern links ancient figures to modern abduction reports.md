## Claim Statement

Wallis argues that "the hybridization of humans by Sky People is arguably the oldest and most widely recurring theme in the world's ancestral narratives," surviving as a worldwide **"Star Children"** pattern — anomalous conceptions following a mother's "close encounter" — that he reads as ancient memories of phenomena we now have technical words for (IVF, artificial insemination, genetic engineering, hybridization). He claims this same pattern continues in modern abduction reports.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Ancient cases Wallis cites: the anomalous, post-childbearing-age conceptions of **Isaac**, **John the Baptist**, and **Jesus** (Luke); plus Lao Tzu, the Yellow Emperor, Krishna, Mithras/Mitra, and the Buddha Vipassi — whose mothers conceived after encounters with "objects of light" from the stars; and the **Mami Wata** (African/Caribbean) and Celtic **fay** traditions. He reads the Sky People as appearing human and beautiful (Genesis 18; the Sodom "angels" with their "destructive technology"). Modern cases: the childhood close-encounters of "John from California" (Mount Shasta) and "Juan Perez" (Argentina) — robotic beings, an entity projecting a deceased grandparent, puncture/inoculation-type marks, lasting PTSD/nightmares — and Jane Pooley's televised account, presented as testimony from people "with nothing to gain." He draws on abduction psychologists John Mack and Barbara Lamb.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Wallis records his friend "Brad" arguing the Star-Child stories are likely political/charismatic mythologizing (as Rome and China deified emperors) and that the Sodom story is too bizarre to mine; Wallis concedes "a good deal of logic" to this but rests on the low-profile modern testimonies.
