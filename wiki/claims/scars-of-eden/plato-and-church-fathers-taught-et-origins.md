## Claim Statement

Wallis argues he is "not the first" to read human origins as ET intervention: **Plato** (c. 400 BCE) taught it, and influential **early Church Fathers** — Justin Martyr, Clement of Alexandria, Origen, and Marcion — endorsed Plato, putting the idea into "the mainstream conversation in early Christianity."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per Wallis, Plato described extraterrestrial beings — he called them "children of God" — who "came and adapted our ancestors to give us a higher capacity for consciousness and intelligence"; spoke of others in the universe who are more advanced, longer-lived, knowledgeable about outer space, and who "live on islands in the sky"; held that Earth is a globe in space periodically "blasted" into extinction-level resets (so "we are not the first civilization"). Wallis says the Platonist Church Fathers "had no problem endorsing Plato in the strongest terms." He cites this as one of his three "red pills" (alongside the 2009 Vatican readiness-to-welcome-ETs statement and his own Genesis linguistics).
