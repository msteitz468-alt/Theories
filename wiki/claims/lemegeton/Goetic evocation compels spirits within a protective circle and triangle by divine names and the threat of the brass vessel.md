## Claim Statement
The Goetia specifies a complete ritual system for safely evoking any of the 72 spirits: a nine-foot magic circle inscribed with divine names, a triangle set two feet outside the circle into which disobedient spirits are commanded, protective devices (hexagonal figure, pentagonal figure, magical ring, secret seal), a graduated sequence of conjurations that escalate in force, and — if the spirit still refuses — a formal curse and a symbolic burning of its name and seal in a black box, invoking the governing kings (Amaymon, Corson, Ziminiar, Gaap) and the underlying threat that Solomon originally confined all 72 spirits in a sealed brass vessel.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
On the circle: "The circle of Salomon is to be made nine feet across, & the divine names are to be written around it, from Eheye to Levanah" — a sequence of divine and angelic names correlated to the Qabalistic spheres from Kether/Ehyeh down to the Moon/Levanah.

On the triangle: "The Triangle that Salomon commanded the disobedient spirits into; it is to be made two foot out from the Circle and 3 foot over [across]. Note this triangle is to be placed upon that Coast [quarter] the spirit belongeth to." The text ties specific spirits to the triangle explicitly — of Bileth: "he must hold a hazel stick in his hand, streched forth towards ye South & East quarters making a Triangle without ye Circle, commanding him into it by ye vertue of ye Bonds & chaines of spirits"; of Furfur: "he never speaketh Truth, except he be compelled or brought up wthin a Triangle"; of Shax: "he must be commanded into a Triangle first or else he will deceive him, & tell you many lyes."

On protective figures: the hexagonal figure "is to be made on parchment made of a calfes Skin and worne at the Skirtt of ye white vestment... to be shewed to the spirits when they are appeared that they may be compelled to be obedient and take a humane shape." The pentagonal figure "is to be made in [gold or silver] and worne upon the brest with the seal of the spirit... it is for to preserve [the Exorcist] from danger, and allso to command by it." The magical ring "is to be held before the face of the Exorcist to preserve him from The stinking fumes of spirits."

On the secret seal and brass vessel: "This secreet seal is to be made by one that is deane both Inward and outward, and hath not defiled himself by any woman in the space of a Month... written with the Blood of a Black Cock which never trode hen, on virgins parchment... by this seal Salomon compelled the aforesaide spirits into a Brass vessel, and sealed it up with the same, he by it gained the love of all Manner of persons, and overcame in Battle, for neither weapon fire nor water could hurt him."

On the vestments and preparatory oration: the exorcist requires "a sceptre or sword; a miter or cap, a long white Robe of Linnen... a girdle of Lyons skin 3 Inches broad... perfumes and a chafin dish of Charcoles... anoynting oyles... and fair water to wash yrselves in," accompanied by the oration: "by ye figurative Mystery of these holy vestures... I will doath me with ye armour of Salvation in ye strength of ye highest, Aneor Amaeor Amides Theodonias Anitor."

On the conjurations, an escalating three-stage sequence: the First Conjuration ("I Invocate and conjure you spirit N... by Beralanensis, Baldachiensis, Paumachiae & Apologiae-Sedes... That you forthwtb appear... in a fair and humane shape"); the Second Conjuration (invoking a chain of biblical precedents — "by the name Y & U weh Adam heard... by ye name Joth weh Jacob heard... by the name Anaphexaton weh Aron heard"); and, if the spirit still fails to appear, "The Constraint," which threatens: "I will bind you into Eternall fire & into the lake of fire and Brimstone, unless you come forthwith and appeare heere before this Circle."

On invoking the governing kings: "For to Invocate the Kinge say as ffolloweth — O you great mighty and Powerfull kinge Amaymon, who beareth rule by the power of thy supreame god El over all spirits... that you cause, enforce & compell N. to come unto me." The text notes: "It will depend on the quarter to which the Spirit is attributed, which of the four chief kings is to be invoked" (per Peterson's footnote on Crowley's edition, consistent with the four kings named earlier as ruling the cardinal quarters — Amaymon, Corson, Ziminiar, Gaap).

On the final sanction — the General Curse ("the spirits Chaine") and burning of the name/seal: "if he doth not come still... write his name and seal in [on] virgins parch[ment] and put it into a black Box with Brimstone... and hold it over the fire of Charcoles," accompanied by "The Conjuration of the fire": "I conjure thee you fire... that you Torment Burne and consume this spirit N. everlastingly," escalating to "The curse": "I doe... Curse you and deprive you from all your offices Joy and place and do bind the thee in the debtts of ye Bottomless Pitt... untill the day of the last Judgement."

On the spirit's arrival, dismissal, and the recurring threat of the vessel: the exorcist shows "the Pentacle of Salomon" and says "Behold your conclusion [confusion] if you be disobedient," then, once obedient, offers welcome and later "The licence to depart": "I doe hear licence thee to depart unto thy proper place without doeing any Injury or danger to any man or beast." The text closes Part I noting the vessel could substitute for the triangle: "you may command these spirits into the Brazen vessell as you doe into the Triangle saying That you forthwith appeare before this Circle in this vessell of Brasse."

The section on Observations also specifies lunar timing ("Thou art to observe first ye Moones age for yr working. The best dayes are when ye Moon is 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 or 14 dayes old") and time-of-day restrictions by rank (e.g., "The chife kings may be bound from 9 to 12 of ye Clock at noone & from 3 till sunset. Marquizes may be bound from 3 ofye Clock in ye after Noon till nine at night...").

## Contexts Within the Source
This ritual apparatus follows immediately after the 72-spirit catalog in Part I and is presented as the practical operating manual for the catalog: figures 1–7 (circle, triangle, hexagonal figure, pentagonal figure, ring, secret seal, brass vessel) are described and keyed to specific stages of the working.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
Peterson's footnotes flag several of the ritual's details as garbled in transmission or mistranslated — e.g., he identifies "if his cap be on his head" (in the Asmoday entry) as "a truly bizarre mistranslation of si vero coopertus fuerit ('if he is afraid he will be overwhelmed')," and notes the required hazel stick mentioned for Bileth "is not mentioned here" in the ring's description, suggesting inconsistency within the compiled instructions themselves.
