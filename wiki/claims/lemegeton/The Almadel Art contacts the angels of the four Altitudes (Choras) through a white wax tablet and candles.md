## Claim Statement
By this Rule Solomon obtained great wisdom from the chief angels governing the "4 Altitudes of the world" — the four corners East, West, North, and South, each subdivided into three "parts," for twelve ruling angelic offices total. The operator contacts these angels not through a magic circle and demonic seal but through a purpose-built wax tablet called the Almadel, wax candles, a gold or silver seal, and a fixed Invocation, with the angel appearing in a crystal stone.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"[T]here be 4 Altitudes, representing the 4 corners of the world, East, West, North and South. The which are divided into 12 parts, that is, every part or Altitude into 3, and the Angells of every of these altitudes have their particulars vertues and powers" (Part IV).

The Almadel itself: "Make the Almadel of pure white wax... it is to be four squares, and six Inches over every way; and in every corner a hole and write betwixt every hole with a new pen" the divine names — for the East: "Adonai, Helomi, Pine"; south: "Hellon, Heloi, Hell"; west: "Jod, Hod, Agla"; north: "Tetragrammaton, Shadai, Jab." Between the quarters is written "this word, Anabona," and in the middle "a six angled figure and in the middest off it a Triangle" bearing "Hel, Helion, Adoni." Four wax candles matching the Almadel's color are made from the same wax, each with "a foot made of ye same wax, for to suporte ye Almadel with," and a gold or silver seal is engraved "Helion, Hellujon, Adonai."

The four Choras and their angels, as given in the text: the first, "Chora Orientis" (East), governed on the "day and houre of the Sun," whose office is "to make all things fruitfull & encrease" — its angels "Alimiel, Gabriel, Borachiel, Lebes & Hellison." The second Chora's angels are "Alphariza, Genon, Geron, Armon, Gereinon." The third Chora's angels are "Eliphamasai, Gelomiros, Gedobonai, Saranana, & Elomnia," appearing "in the form of children or little women drest in green and silver collours." The fourth Chora's angels are "Barchiel, Gediel, Gabiel, Deliel, & Captiel," appearing as boys clothed in black mixed with dark green, holding a bird, crowned with ivy and berries.

Ritual mechanics: the operator sets the four candles on four candlesticks, lays the Almadel "between the four candles upon the waxen feet that cometh from the candles," lays the golden seal upon the Almadel, and reads the Invocation from "virgins Parchment." An earthen basin with hot ashes and "3 little grains of Masticke in powder" is fumed beneath the Almadel so the smell rises through its holes; the angel, on smelling it, speaks "with a low voice asking what your desier is." The Invocation itself calls upon "the great mighty and blessed angell of god, N, who ruleth as the cheefe & first governing angell in the first Chora" and constrains him "by this powerfull name Anabona."

The text notes twelve total princes beyond the four chief Altitude-angels, each ruling roughly thirty days per year according to the zodiac sign the Sun occupies: "There are 12 princes rulling besides those in the 4 Altitudes, and they distribute their offices amongst themselves, every one rolling 30 dayes... every yeare."

## Contexts Within the Source
Framed as the fourth of the five books of the Lemegeton, immediately following Ars Paulina and preceding Ars Notoria. Peterson's apparatus notes the Almadel's colors are assigned per Chora ("lilly white" for the first, "Perfect red Rose Collour" for the second, "green mixt with a white silver collour" for the third, "black mixt with a little green of a sadd collour" for the fourth), and that the rite differs from the Goetia/Theurgia-Goetia/Ars Paulina in invoking benevolent, higher-ranked angelic governors rather than binding subordinate spirits.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source
The text itself restricts the rite's scope: "you must not pray for any Angells but those that belong to the same Altitude, you have a desier to call forth," and "doe not pray for anything that is contrary to the nature of their office, or against god and his laws, but what God giveth according to the course of nature." It also notes it is "in vain to call any angell or spirit unless you knew what names of god to call them by."
