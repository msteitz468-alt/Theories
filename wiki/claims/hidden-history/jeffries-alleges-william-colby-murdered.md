## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that former CIA Director William Colby's death on April 29, 1996 — officially ruled accidental drowning following a heart attack or stroke during a canoe outing — was in reality a murder, citing inconsistencies in the official account and Colby's role as mentor to Franklin Cover-Up author John DeCamp.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]]:

- Colby had personally encouraged DeCamp to write about the Franklin child sex scandal, telling him: "What you have to understand, John, is that sometimes there are forces and events too big, too powerful, with so much at stake for other people or institutions, that you cannot do anything about them, no matter how evil or wrong they are…."
- Colby went missing and his body was discovered in Maryland's Wicomico River without his life jacket — which family and friends insisted he always wore while boating.
- The coroner attributed death to drowning from a heart attack or stroke, but the autopsy found no evidence of either condition.
- When police arrived at Colby's home, his computer and radio were both left on, and dinner dishes remained on the table — described as unusual for a man known for his meticulous habits.
- Colby always wore shoes while boating; his body was found without them, on a blustery late-April day.
- An initial Associated Press story prematurely declared Colby "missing and presumed drowned," quoting a source as saying Colby had told his wife he wasn't feeling well "but was going canoeing anyway." A week later, Colby's wife told the Washington Times her husband had been well and had not mentioned canoeing.
- Colby had been a contributing editor to James Dale Davidson's investment newsletter *Strategic Investment*, which covered Clinton scandals in detail; Christopher Ruddy (author of the Vince Foster investigation book) had considered asking Colby to write the foreword.

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 12, "Death of William Colby" section. Jeffries presents Colby's death as part of [[The political "body count" pattern (Jeffries)]] and as evidence of suppression specifically connected to [[The Franklin Cover-Up (Jeffries account)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries does not claim definitive proof. The official cause of death (accidental drowning) is stated; Jeffries catalogues the anomalies rather than naming a specific perpetrator.
