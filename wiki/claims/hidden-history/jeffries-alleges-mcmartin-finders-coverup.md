## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that the McMartin Preschool case (California, 1983–1990) and the Finders investigation (1987) were both genuine pedophile networks whose prosecutions were sabotaged, with physical evidence suppressed and key witnesses dying under suspicious circumstances, because the networks had connections to government and intelligence agencies.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]]:

**McMartin Preschool:**
- Allegations surfaced in 1983; a high-profile trial ran three years, with all charges dropped in 1990.
- An archaeological excavation at the site in May 1990 discovered two elaborate underground tunnel complexes under the preschool building — corroborating what children had described. The tunnels were subsequently filled in by unknown parties before the building was demolished.
- The Children's Institute International concluded "a full eighty percent [of child complainants] displayed physical symptoms, including vaginal or rectal scarring."
- Renegade ex-FBI Los Angeles chief Ted Gunderson stated 460 complaints had been filed with Manhattan Beach police before any charges were brought.
- **Judy Johnson** — the woman whose initial complaint launched the case — was found dead at age 42 on December 20, 1986, with accounts of prior threats and surveillance.
- **Wayne Satz** (KABC reporter who broke the story) — died of a heart attack in January 1993, age 47.
- **Robert Winkler** (identified by children as the "Wolfman"; later arrested for running a sexual abuse babysitting front) — died of an alleged drug overdose just before his trial.
- **Paul Bynum** (private investigator hired by victims' parents) — died of a supposed self-inflicted gunshot wound before his scheduled testimony.

**The Finders:**
- On February 4, 1987, Tallahassee police found six malnourished young children (all under seven, only one could speak) with two adults connected to the Finders, a Washington, D.C.-based organization.
- A subsequent police raid on a Finders warehouse found photographs of naked children, documents with "detailed instructions for obtaining children for unspecified purposes," evidence of ritual animal mutilation, jars of feces and urine, and what appeared to be an altar.
- The Metropolitan Police dropped the case "like a hot rock" after a police official said it "had become a CIA internal matter."
- The FBI and Customs Service ordered further investigation stopped on "national security" grounds; the Metropolitan Police case files were sealed.
- Finders leader Marion Pettie was a retired Air Force officer; his wife had worked for the CIA; his son was employed by Air America (described as "the CIA's airline").
- Despite the materials found, the Finders group was described by US News and World Report as engaging in practices that were "eccentric — not illegal."

**Satanist/Military network (Lt. Col. Michael Aquino):**
- Aquino founded the Temple of Set in 1975 while in the Special Reserves; wrote doctoral dissertation on the neutron bomb and produced "From Psy Ops to Mind War."
- A 1988 San Jose Mercury News story linked Aquino to a nationwide Satanic pedophile ring using military-base day care centers, including the Presidio Child Development Center (San Francisco, US Army).
- Thirty-eight videotapes, photo negatives, and other evidence were found in Aquino's home during a 1987 San Francisco police raid. Ten-count indictment against child care worker Gary Hambright was inexplicably dropped by US Attorney Joseph Russoniello (himself linked by journalist Gary Webb to Contra cocaine trafficking).
- Noreen Gosch testified in a Nebraska courtroom (February 5, 1999) linking Aquino to a nationwide pedophile ring including the disappearance of her son Johnny Gosch (Des Moines, 1982). Per her sworn testimony: "He was in the military. He had top Pentagon clearances. He was a pedophile. He was a Satanist. He's founded the Temple of Set. And he was a close friend of Anton LaVey. The two of them were very active in ritualistic sexual abuse…. They used these kids to sexually compromise politicians or anyone else they wish to have control of."
- Photographer Rusty Nelson's 2000 written statement alleged that King paid Aquino "with a suitcase of bearer bonds and cash earmarked for covert Contra operations" and that Aquino was part of Colonel Oliver North's Contra guns-and-drug program.
- Aquino was never charged or indicted; retained highest-level security clearances throughout Army investigations.
