## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that Commerce Secretary Ron Brown, who was reportedly threatening to "take down" Clinton amid a Justice Department investigation of him, was murdered in his Croatian plane crash and that military pathologists found a circular hole in his skull consistent with a gunshot wound — which was then suppressed and the x-rays and photos of his head disappeared.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], Brown was under investigation by the Justice Department, FBI, FDIC, and his own Commerce Department at the time of his death. After Janet Reno appointed a special prosecutor, Brown angrily told Clinton he wasn't going to "take the rap."

- **Gunshot wound evidence**: Lieutenant Colonel Steve Cogswell, deputy medical examiner with the Armed Forces Institute of Pathology at Dover Air Force Base, observed what he called an "apparent gunshot wound" — a perfectly round hole in the top of Brown's head. He urged an autopsy. Colonel Gormley conducted only an external examination and declared the case closed. Jeffries quotes Cogswell: "To stack up the coincidences: one of thirty-six people has got a hole; the hole is in their head; the hole is dead center in the top of their head; and it just happens to be the most important person on that airplane from a political point of view."
- **Army corroboration**: US Army Lieutenant Colonel David Hause, also present at the external exam, said he heard someone exclaim "Gee, this looks like a gunshot wound" and agreed it looked like one.
- **Evidence destroyed**: All of Brown's head x-rays, photos of the x-rays, and the negatives for the photographs subsequently went missing from his case file.
- **Dr. Martin Fackler**: Former director of the Army's Wound Ballistics Laboratory studied the photographs: "I'm impressed by how very, very round that hole is. That's unusual except for a gunshot wound." Learning no autopsy was done: "My God. It's astounding."
- **Survivor suspicious death**: Air Force sergeant Shelley Kelly, a flight attendant reportedly found alive with cuts and bruises after the crash and able to help herself to the rescue helicopter, was declared dead on arrival at hospital. Her autopsy showed she died of a broken neck.
- **Cogswell suppressed**: After his public statements, Cogswell was placed under "command investigation" by the Air Force, ordered not to speak to the press, and had case material retrieved from his home by military police.
- **Related death**: Barbara Wise's partially nude body was found in a locked Commerce Department office on November 29, 1996, with bruises all over her body; police found "no evidence of foul play." She worked in the same Commerce division under investigation for illegal foreign fund-raising. Jeffries links this to [[The political "body count" pattern (Jeffries)]].

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 6. Jeffries presents the Ron Brown case as illustrating how politically powerful figures connected to Clinton scandals die before they can become government witnesses.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries notes establishment sources "heatedly refuted" the bullet-hole allegation. He relies primarily on Christopher Ruddy's reporting in the Pittsburgh Tribune-Review, an outlet associated with right-wing media.
