## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that the Reagan administration, under the auspices of FEMA and co-authored by
Oliver North, devised a classified emergency plan called Readiness Exercise 1984 (Rex 84) that
established an undisclosed number of concentration camps throughout the United States, permitted
the declaration of martial law by presidential signature alone, and was deliberately concealed from
Congress when raised during the 1987 Iran-Contra hearings.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per Jeffries, Rex 84 "used a problem solely created and enabled by the same corrupt authorities,
illegal immigration, as a pretext" to set up FEMA-run detention camps designed to hold civilian
populations. The plan "permitted FEMA to declare martial law with the mere signature of a president"
and "also allowed the conversion of numerous military bases into prisons."

The existence of the plan was first publicly revealed during the 1987 Iran-Contra hearings and
reported in the July 5, 1987, *Miami Herald*. When Representative Jack Brooks of Texas asked Oliver
North directly about his work on "plans for the continuity of government in the event of a major
disaster," North's attorney "visibly winced." Committee chairman Senator Daniel Inouye of Hawaii
immediately intervened: "I believe that question touches upon a highly sensitive and classified area,
so may I request that you not touch upon that?" Inouye blocked a second attempt by Brooks to
discuss "plans for the continuity of government … that would suspend the American Constitution" in
the event of a declared emergency.

Jeffries connects this to Jimmy Carter's creation of FEMA (Executive Order 12148), which also
incorporated sweeping emergency powers from Nixon's Executive Order 11490, and he cites former
Minnesota governor Jesse Ventura's 2010 television examination of what Ventura alleged were
mysterious "Fusion Centers" proliferating across the country.

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim illustrates Jeffries's broader thesis about the covert construction of a surveillance
and detention infrastructure that could be weaponized against the American public. It links to
[[Suppressed conspiracies and cover-ups in modern American politics (Hidden History thesis)]] and
the chapter's treatment of Reagan's record as diverging sharply from his small-government rhetoric.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries does not cite evidence that Rex 84 detention camps were ever activated or that FEMA
exercised the martial-law authority described. The congressional suppression of questioning about
the plan is documented via the publicly available Iran-Contra hearing record; the full contents of
the plan remain, per Jeffries's account, classified.
