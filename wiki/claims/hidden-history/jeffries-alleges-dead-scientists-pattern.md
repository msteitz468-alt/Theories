## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that an unusual and suspicious cluster of deaths among microbiologists, virologists, and biological weapons experts occurred in the months immediately following 9/11, at the same time as the anthrax letter attacks, and that the pattern suggests deliberate elimination of individuals whose knowledge posed a threat to official narratives or whose research had intelligence value.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], the deaths catalogued within a roughly eighteen-month window beginning November 2001 include:

- **Don Wiley** (November 16, 2001) — Harvard professor and foremost infectious disease researcher; car found abandoned on a Memphis bridge with keys in the ignition, full tank of gas. Body found in January 2002; death ruled "accidental."
- **Dr. Benito Que** (November 12, 2001) — biologist specializing in infectious disease; found dead outside his Miami Medical School lab. Friends reported he had been attacked by four men with a baseball bat.
- **Dr. Vladimir Pasechnik** (November 23, 2001) — former Soviet biological weapons microbiologist who defected to Britain in 1989 and revealed the scale of the USSR's bioweapons program; died suddenly.
- **Dr. Robert M. Schwartz** (December 10, 2001) — DNA researcher and founding member of the Virginia Biotechnology Association; found stabbed in his rural Virginia home. The weapon was a sword; an X had been carved in the back of his neck.
- **Set Van Nguyen** (December 14, 2001) — Australian microbiologist; died in a freak lab accident when deadly gas escaped from a liquid nitrogen cooling system.
- Multiple Russian microbiologists died in January–February 2002 (Ivan Glebov, Alexi Brushlinkski, Victor Korshunov).
- **Ian Langford** (February 2002) — senior researcher at University of East Anglia; found wedged under a chair at his "blood-spattered and apparently ransacked home."
- **Dr. David Kelly** (July 18, 2003) — British biological weapons expert; found dead after being publicly humiliated by the Foreign Affairs Select Committee for his skepticism about Iraq's WMD. Postmortem report and photos classified for seventy years.

**Howard Hughes Medical Institute connection**: Jeffries notes that Wiley, Schwartz, and Que all worked for facilities receiving grants from HHMI, which investigator Patricia Dole, Ph.D., alleged was involved in "black ops" biomedical research for the CIA.

Jeffries also notes the deaths occurred in the context of the post-9/11 anthrax letter attacks, which independent researchers later alleged used anthrax traced to US military sources at Fort Detrick, Maryland.

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 10. Jeffries presents this cluster within [[The political "body count" pattern (Jeffries)]] and notes a parallel phenomenon in the 1980s, when more than two dozen scientific experts associated with "Star Wars" defense research died under mysterious circumstances.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries acknowledges that individual deaths have individual explanations (lab accident, attack by a fired colleague, etc.). He frames the clustering as significant. The connection to any specific government action is implied rather than directly evidenced.
