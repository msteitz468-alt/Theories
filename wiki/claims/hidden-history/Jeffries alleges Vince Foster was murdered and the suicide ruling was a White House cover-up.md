## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that Deputy White House Counsel Vince Foster was murdered and that overwhelming physical evidence of a cover-up — including falsified FBI reports, missing x-rays and photos, forged suicide note, and White House staff ransacking Foster's office — was suppressed by investigators and ignored by the mainstream media. He frames the official ruling of suicide as untenable.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], Foster's body was found on July 20, 1993, at Fort Marcy Park in northern Virginia. Among the anomalies Jeffries catalogues:

- **Different weapon**: The first paramedics on scene reported a different weapon than the 1913 Colt revolver later linked to Foster; the revolver only appeared after police took over the crime scene.
- **Neck wound suppressed**: Paramedics reported a neck wound the authorities denied.
- **No gun in hand**: Confidential witness "CW," who originally found the body, testified to a congressional committee: "As sure as I am standing here … there was nothing in his hands."
- **No shot heard**: Five homes within five hundred feet of the body; no one reported a gunshot; authorities waited months to interview them.
- **Missing x-rays and photos**: Autopsy Dr. James Beyer told police that x-rays showed no fragments, then later claimed he never took x-rays. The photos of Foster's body in its original position disappeared.
- **Forged suicide note**: The alleged note was torn into twenty-eight pieces, found in a briefcase that had been searched twice; the piece where the signature should appear was missing. Three handwriting experts declared it a forgery and held a press conference — which the mainstream media entirely ignored. Foster's fingerprints were not on the note.
- **White House office ransacked**: Hillary Clinton's chief of staff Maggie Williams was observed by a Secret Service agent moving files from Foster's office. Aide Deborah Gorham testified that Bernie Nussbaum demanded the combination to Foster's safe, after which documents known to be there were missing.
- **Prosecutor driven out**: Grand jury prosecutor Michael Rodriguez, assigned by Kenneth Starr, resigned in disgust, saying "I was told what the result was going to be from the get-go" and "The FBI told me to back off."
- **Witness harassment**: Patrick Knowlton, who found the body before it was officially discovered, was subjected to years of harassment — tailing, threatening gestures from FBI agents, 3 a.m. knocks on his door.
- **Clinton insider's admission**: Jeffries quotes Assistant Attorney General Web Hubbell: "Don't believe a word you hear. It was not suicide. It couldn't have been." (*Esquire*, November 1993).

Jeffries describes London journalist Ambrose Evans-Pritchard's reporting on evidence that Foster's body was in his car when it was originally reported discovered — contradicting the crime scene — and notes that Evans-Pritchard's apartment was subsequently broken into and his computers stolen.
