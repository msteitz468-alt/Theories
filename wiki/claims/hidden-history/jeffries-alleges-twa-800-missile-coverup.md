## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that TWA Flight 800, which exploded over the Atlantic on July 17, 1996, killing all 230 aboard, was struck by a missile — most likely fired by the US Navy — and that the FBI and NTSB orchestrated a cover-up, suppressing or falsifying witness testimony and physical evidence, in a manner directly analogous to official suppression in the JFK assassination.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], the key allegations include:

- **Eyewitnesses to a missile**: Over 150 credible eyewitnesses told the FBI they saw a bright object streaking toward the plane before the explosion. The Associated Press (July 19, 1996) reported radar detected a blip merging with the jet. By September 22, 1996, the New York Post reported the FBI had interviewed 154 witnesses who testified to seeing a missile. The Navy's Cooperative Engagement Capability system was running tests in the general area at the time.
- **CIA animation fabricated**: Witness Mike Wire, whose testimony the CIA used as the basis for its animation showing Flight 800 rocketing upward (to explain the "streaking light" as the plane itself), stated publicly: "The video presents a factual reenactment of what I saw … It most definitely didn't start up in the sky like the FBI/CIA story says. I don't know how they could come up with that scenario because it doesn't match what I told the FBI."
- **Navy involvement suppressed**: The Navy contradicted its initial denials and admitted submarines were in the area. NTSB managing director Peter Goelz asked a *Washington Post* reporter to run a story misquoting a journalist who had asked about radar data showing "a score of unidentified vessels in a military warning area twenty-five miles from the site of the crash."
- **Witnesses silenced**: FBI lead agent James Kallstrom asked the NTSB to exclude eyewitnesses from public hearings. An anonymous TWA pilot stated "90 percent of us believe there was a government cover-up." The Pentagon ordered deep-sea salvage vessels to the area and superseded the NYPD, which had legal jurisdiction.
- **Evidence of explosive residue**: Explosive residue was found on the wreckage; authorities attributed it to a bomb-sniffing dog exercise. Independent journalists James and Elizabeth Sanders were prosecuted for obtaining a piece of seat fabric from the wreckage for independent testing.
- **Pierre Salinger's intervention**: Former JFK press secretary and ABC News affiliate Pierre Salinger held a press conference in France on November 8, 1996, stating he believed a US Navy test missile had accidentally struck the plane. He was immediately attacked by the press as gullible; NTSB vice chairman Bob Francis called him "an idiot."
- **Oliver Stone's documentary killed**: ABC canceled Oliver Stone's planned documentary *Declassified* about TWA 800 before it aired, despite Stone having interviewed thirty eyewitnesses who were then sent apology letters.

## Contexts Within the Source

Jeffries treats TWA 800 as a further instance of the pattern identified in Chapter 6: a major incident involving many eyewitnesses, physical anomalies, and government suppression of inconvenient evidence. He frames it within [[Suppressed conspiracies and cover-ups in modern American politics (Hidden History thesis)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries acknowledges the official NTSB conclusion that a center fuel tank explosion caused the crash. He notes that NTSB chairman Jim Hall called Salinger's allegations "unfortunate and irresponsible" and presents the official rebuttal mostly as institutional defensiveness.
