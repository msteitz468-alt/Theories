## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that the [[Council on Foreign Relations]], the [[Bilderberg Group (Cooper)]], and Bohemian Grove are not merely policy-discussion forums or fraternal gatherings but interlocking instruments of a decades-long project to erode national sovereignty and install a world government controlled by a transnational financial elite.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]]:

**CFR:**
- Professor Carroll Quigley (epigraph): "The Council on Foreign Relations is the American Branch of a society which originated in England [and] believes national boundaries should be obliterated and one-world rule established."
- Membership spans ostensible ideological opposites: Dick Cheney, Jesse Jackson, Newt Gingrich, Caroline Kennedy — along with media figures (Tom Brokaw, Katie Couric, Dan Rather, Barbara Walters) who, Jeffries notes, have no "foreign policy" expertise and whose presence undermines the CFR's stated mission.
- Jeffries argues CFR membership explains why mainstream journalists do not cover the organization critically.

**Bilderberg:**
- Founded 1954, named for the Bilderberg Hotel in the Netherlands; annual secret meetings of politicians, corporate leaders, and royalty from around the world.
- Denis Healey (Bilderberg founder, 2001): "To say we were striving for a one world government is exaggerated, but not wholly unfair."
- David Rockefeller (attributed June 1991 Bilderberg meeting, Baden-Baden): "We are grateful to the Washington Post, the New York Times, Time Magazine… it would have been impossible for us to develop our plans for the world if we had been subject to the bright lights of publicity…."
- David Rockefeller (2002 memoirs): "Some even believe we are part of a secret cabal working against the best interests of the United States…. If that's the charge, I stand guilty, and I am proud of it."
- Strobe Talbot (Clinton's roommate at Oxford and deputy secretary of state): "In the next century, nations will be obsolete; all states will recognize a single global authority."
- Clinton sent Talbot a congratulatory message praising his work toward "world government" when Talbot received the Norman Cousins Global Governance Award in 1993.
- Walter Cronkite (1999 UN appearance): declared his desire for world government and said, "I'm glad to sit here at the right hand of Satan." Cronkite also served as the "voice of the giant owl" at Bohemian Grove occult rituals.
- Henry Kissinger: "Today America would be outraged if UN troops entered Los Angeles to restore order. Tomorrow they will be grateful…."
- James Warburg (US Senate testimony, February 17, 1950): "We shall have world government, whether or not we like it."
- [[George H.W. Bush]]'s famous "New World Order" speech was given to Congress on September 11, 1990 — exactly eleven years before 9/11.

**Bohemian Grove:**
- Approximately 2,000 elite men gather in northern California each July; women excluded (historically including as employees).
- The "Cremation of Care" occult ritual is performed before a 40-foot stone owl; members wear red-hooded robes and chant. Jeffries notes the owl and child sacrifice are associated in the Old Testament with the god Moloch.
- Alex Jones infiltrated the Grove in 2000; resulting video Dark Secrets: Inside Bohemian Grove is cited as evidence.
- Past attendees per Jeffries: Nixon ("the most faggy goddamned thing you could ever imagine"), Ford, Carter, Reagan, both Bushes, Clinton, Kissinger, Greenspan, Cheney, Gingrich, John Major, Tony Blair, Arnold Schwarzenegger, Colin Powell, David Rockefeller.
- New York Post (July 2004): gay porn star Chad Savage was flown in to service notable figures at the Grove.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries does not substantially engage with counter-arguments. He acknowledges that a "world government, in and of itself, was probably born out of misguided idealism" and that figures like Woodrow Wilson seemed to have noble intentions — but argues the current network serves elite financial interests rather than humanist goals. He hedges on whether policy decisions are formally made at Bohemian Grove, stating "whether important policy decisions are made at Bohemian Grove or not, it is at the very least disturbing to know that our leaders are gathering together to worship a massive owl."
