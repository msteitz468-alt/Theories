## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that the July 16, 1999, plane crash killing JFK Jr., his wife Carolyn Bessette, and her sister Lauren was a suspicious death warranting investigation, and that key evidence — including a suppressed last phone call, the black box battery removal, missing flight log, manipulated weather narratives, and a smear campaign portraying Kennedy as a reckless pilot — indicate a possible cover-up. He speculates the death was politically motivated, possibly to clear the path for Hillary Clinton's New York Senate bid.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], Chapter 7:

- **Delay in search**: The Coast Guard did not begin coordinating a search until 8:25 a.m. — fifteen hours after the plane went missing. An official Coast Guard statement and multiple TV reports cited a 9:39 p.m. phone contact between JFK Jr. and the FAA, during which he was described as calm, giving his position on final approach. The FAA later denied any such call existed. Jeffries cites uncut television footage of Coast Guard information officer Todd Burgun confirming the 9:39 p.m. call in detail, footage later cut from archived copies released for purchase.
- **Black box battery removed**: The plane's black box battery had been removed, making cockpit recordings unavailable. The NTSB report also noted JFK Jr.'s flight log was missing.
- **Weather fabricated**: FAA flight specialist Edward Meyer, who prepared the FAA's weather report for that night, issued a public statement saying "the weather along his flight was just fine" and called media accounts stressing poor visibility "garbage." All of JFK Jr.'s flight instructors described him as a prudent, experienced pilot with 700+ flight hours and near-instrument-flight-rating qualifications. The mainstream press widely portrayed him as reckless.
- **Explosion witnesses**: A Martha's Vineyard Gazette reporter told TV station WCVB about seeing "a big white flash in the sky" at the time of the crash; the Gazette later denied this and said the story was "completely bogus." Pennsylvania lawyer Victor Pribanic heard "a loud impact like a bomb" from the exact location Kennedy had given in his 9:39 p.m. call. A guest at the Rory Kennedy wedding was also reported by Fox News to have seen an explosion.
- **FBI treated it as a possible murder**: An unnamed friend of JFK Jr. told investigative reporter Wayne Madsen that FBI and Secret Service agents initially investigated the death as a possible murder, and that agents had canvassed stores in Caldwell, New Jersey, asking if anyone had recently purchased epoxy. Madsen also reported FBI found "suspicious boating activity" and an unusual number of batteries in a fishing boat near the crash site.
- **JFK Jr.'s investigative agenda**: Madsen told Jeffries that JFK Jr. had been planning to hire him as an investigative reporter for a revamped George magazine, with "the most significant" assignment being investigation of his father's assassination. Jeffries also cites JFK Jr.'s high school girlfriend Meg Azzoni's account that his "heartfelt quest was to expose and bring to trial who killed his father."
- **Pentagon took command**: The Pentagon, without jurisdiction over a civilian crash, took over the situation and subsequently contradicted all initial reports: no flight instructor on board, no FAA contact during the flight, no flight plan filed, plane not reported missing until 2 a.m. despite family notifying authorities from 10 p.m.
- **Political motive**: Jeffries raises the question of whether JFK Jr.'s planned Senate run would have conflicted with Hillary Clinton's political ambitions.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries acknowledges the NTSB concluded "pilot error." He concedes he cannot prove murder and is working largely from circumstantial anomalies and Internet research. He notes NTSB acknowledged the plane had autopilot that could have guided it to within one hundred feet of the airport.
