## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that while Senator Frank Church's Senate committee (1975) and the House Select
Committee on Assassinations (1976–79) appeared to be reform efforts, both were ultimately
compromised — the HSCA particularly through a CIA secrecy agreement and a change of lead counsel
that redirected the inquiry away from the most damaging conclusions. Jeffries argues a wave of
suspicious deaths among key witnesses just as the HSCA was forming further suggests the conspiracy
was actively protecting itself.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Church Committee.** Jeffries credits the Church Committee with genuinely disclosing CIA
assassination plots against foreign leaders (Castro, Lumumba, Trujillo, the Diems), exposing
COINTELPRO, and touching on CIA performance in the JFK assassination. However, CIA director William
Colby and Defense Secretary James Schlesinger — who had both attempted reforms — were fired by
President Ford on November 4, 1975 (the "Halloween Massacre"). Colby was replaced by future
president [[George H.W. Bush]]; Schlesinger was replaced by Donald Rumsfeld. Jeffries frames these
appointments as the establishment reasserting control after the brief reform window.

**HSCA compromised.** The HSCA's original lead counsel, Richard Sprague, was a "veteran prosecutor
who promised to leave no stones unturned." He was forced out after being attacked by the mainstream
media and a confusing feud with Chairman Henry Gonzalez. New lead counsel G. Robert Blakey then
"limit[ed] the inquiry, agree[d] to a shameful secrecy agreement with the CIA, and back[ed] up
every one of the Warren Commission's untenable and long-discredited conclusions." Jeffries cites his
own personal disillusionment with politics after this outcome. The acoustic evidence finding of a
"probable conspiracy" (a grassy-knoll shot that missed) was dismissed in establishment press
coverage that suggested "two unassociated 'lone nuts' happened, by the most remarkable of
coincidences, to show up in Dealey Plaza."

**Sprague's memo on Burkley.** An HSCA memorandum from Sprague disclosed that JFK's personal
physician Admiral George Burkley had indicated to an attorney that "others besides Oswald must
have participated." The HSCA never questioned Burkley, who was "perhaps the most important witness
that was inexcusably ignored by the Warren Commission." His files were later reported by the law
firm to have been "destroyed over 20 years ago."

**Wave of witness deaths (1976–77).** In the period just as the HSCA was forming, Jeffries catalogs:
mobsters Sam Giancana and John Roselli murdered; CIA operative William Harvey died; ex-ambassador
William Pawley committed suicide; George DeMohrenschildt (Oswald's strange "friend" with powerful
intelligence connections) allegedly committed suicide in March 1977 — the same month scheduled HSCA
witness Carlos Prio Socarras died from a gunshot, and mobster Charles Nicoletti died of a gunshot.
"What are the odds of three people from a witness pool dying unnaturally in the same month?"
Additionally, William Sullivan (former FBI #3 man) was killed in a "hunting accident" in November
1977; Sullivan had told journalist Robert Novak: "Someday you will read that I have been killed in
an accident, but don't believe it, I've been murdered." Three other FBI officials scheduled to
testify (Louis Nicholas, Alan Belmont, James Cadigan) also died in 1977.

## Contexts Within the Source

These sections bridge Jeffries's account of the 1960s assassinations and the Reagan-era scandals,
demonstrating that brief congressional reform windows were systematically closed by establishment
countermoves. The witness deaths link directly to [[The political "body count" pattern (Jeffries)]].
The HSCA is also the setting for one of Jeffries's personal anecdotes (his own involvement with
Mark Lane's Citizens' Committee of Inquiry and meeting with Freddie Prinze, who died after
expressing interest in funding a JFK investigation telethon).

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries acknowledges the Church Committee produced genuinely important disclosures (assassination
plots, COINTELPRO, MK-Ultra). His criticism is not that it was entirely fake, but that its gains
were quickly reversed and that the HSCA — the only body to formally conclude a probable conspiracy
in the JFK assassination — was then undermined by its own limitations and secrecy agreements.
