## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that the announced killing of Osama bin Laden on May 1, 2011, was either a staged political event or that bin Laden had already been dead since 2001, and that the Obama administration fabricated or heavily embellished the operation for maximum political benefit ahead of the 2012 election.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]]:

- The released photograph of bin Laden with a bloody wound was "quickly proven to be a crude, Photoshopped forgery by Internet researchers." The White House acknowledged real photos existed but refused to release them, claiming it would "create a national security risk."
- A photo purported to show Obama, Biden, and Clinton watching "live coverage" of the Navy Seal raid was undercut by CIA Director Leon Panetta's admission that the live feed to the White House had been cut off before the Seals even entered the building.
- Bin Laden's body was disposed of at sea, described as honoring "Muslim tradition" — which Jeffries notes is not actually an Islamic tradition.
- Former deputy assistant secretary of state Steve Pieczenik told Alex Jones that bin Laden had died in 2001 and the entire 2011 announcement was "a total make-believe… American theater of the absurd."
- "Spontaneous" crowds appeared outside the White House immediately after Obama's announcement, which Jeffries describes as seeming scripted.
- Obama repeatedly bragged about the killing for political purposes; the New York Times headlined "Obama Trumpets Killing of Bin Laden" and noted it was unprecedented for a president to boast so openly about killing an individual enemy.

Jeffries also notes the killing of American citizen Anwar al-Awlaki (September 30, 2011) by drone without charge or trial, and subsequently the killing of al-Awlaki's sixteen-year-old American-born son in Yemen — both presented as evidence of the Obama administration's extrajudicial assassination policy (NDAA and "kill or capture" list).

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 11. Jeffries frames the bin Laden announcement as timed to distract from the "birther" controversy (Obama held a press conference on April 30 saying "we don't have time for this silliness," then announced the killing the next day).

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries acknowledges uncertainty: "Either way, the public loses here. If, as seems likely, the entire thing was staged for political reasons… On the other hand, if they really assassinated Bin Laden and aren't even bothering to construct a clumsy cover story…"
