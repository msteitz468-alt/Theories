## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that the Federal Reserve System, established in 1913, is not a government agency but a private corporation that institutionalized "legal counterfeiting" through fractional lending, that this arrangement has concentrated financial power in the hands of a banking elite, and that attempts to expose or audit the Fed have been suppressed — with at least one investigator, journalist Mark Pittman, dying shortly after suing the Fed under FOIA.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]] (see also [[Federal Reserve as a banking cartel]] for the broader wiki treatment):

- Congressman Charles A. Lindbergh Sr.: "This [Federal Reserve Act] establishes the most gigantic trust on earth… the invisible government of the monetary power will be legalized … the worst legislative crime of the ages."
- Congressman Louis McFadden called the Fed "one of the most corrupt institutions the world has ever known," was shot at and nearly poisoned, and died suddenly on October 3, 1936, of "heart failure."
- Congressman Wright Patman: "The Federal Reserve bank buys government bonds without one penny."
- The Boston Federal Reserve Bank's own publication *Putting It Simply* states: "When the Federal Reserve writes a check, it is creating money" — there is no underlying deposit.
- Under fractional lending, banks are required to hold only 10 percent in reserve, permitting them to "lend" $100 for every $10 actually possessed; Jeffries describes this as legal counterfeiting making 90 percent of all principal in circulation counterfeit.
- The "Fed" in Federal Reserve is "purposefully misleading; the Federal Reserve is a private corporation, not a government agency."
- **Mark Pittman** (Bloomberg News): filed a 2008 FOIA suit against the Fed for records of taxpayer-financed loans; won in federal court (August 2009, judge ruled Fed "improperly withheld" information); died suddenly on November 25, 2009, at age 52, of officially undetermined causes, before the data was released. Both of Pittman's parents were alive at the time of his death. His daughter publicly denied conspiracy, but Jeffries notes the cause of death was never established.
- Warren Buffett and Bill Gates publicly advocate higher taxes on the wealthy while structuring their own finances to avoid those same taxes through foundations and specialized income classifications.
- The 2008 bank bailout — opposed by "well over 90 percent of Americans" — was supported by both Barack Obama and John McCain.
