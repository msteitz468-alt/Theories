## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that a pattern of suspicious, often violent deaths surrounds Barack Obama, forming an "Obama Body Count" analogous to the Clinton and Bush body counts described elsewhere in the book. He presents these deaths as consistent with the suppression of political opponents, embarrassing witnesses, or those who possessed damaging information.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], among the deaths catalogued:

- **Bill Gwatney** (August 13, 2008) — Arkansas Democratic Party chair and strong Hillary Clinton super delegate; shot three times by a "lone nut" who was then killed in a police chase.
- **Stephanie Tubbs Jones** (August 19, 2008) — another Clinton super delegate and Diebold voting-machine critic; found dead in her car six days after Gwatney.
- **Donald Young** (December 23, 2007) — choir director at Obama's Trinity United Church of Christ; openly gay; allegedly in contact with Larry Sinclair, who claimed a sexual relationship with Obama. Two other gay Trinity congregation members (Larry Bland, November 17, 2007; Nate Spencer, December 26, 2007) also died within weeks.
- **John Wheeler** (December 31, 2010) — aide to three Republican presidents; found in a Delaware garbage dump, cause: blunt-force trauma; his widow alleged professional assassination.
- **Ashley Turton** (January 10, 2011) — lobbyist; found dead in a burning car.
- **Andrew Breitbart** (March 1, 2012) — right-wing journalist who publicly claimed to possess a damaging college-era video of Obama, promising to release it "March 1st"; died that same day of what was officially ruled a sudden massive heart attack at age 43.
- **Michael Cormier** (April 20, 2012) — the coroner in Breitbart's case; died on the day Breitbart's autopsy results were released; police sources told the LA Times arsenic poisoning was suspected.
- **Loretta Fuddy** (December 13, 2013) — Hawaii State Health Director who verified Obama's birth certificate; killed in a plane crash (only fatality).

The book notes that Breitbart's sole eyewitness, Christopher Lasseter, temporarily vanished from his known address after the event.

## Contexts Within the Source

The "Obama Body Count" section (and follow-up "More Obama Body Count" section) appears in Chapter 11. Jeffries places it explicitly within the recurring [[The political "body count" pattern (Jeffries)]] framework and connects it to the broader thesis of [[Suppressed conspiracies and cover-ups in modern American politics (Hidden History thesis)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Jeffries does not claim definitive proof of murder in each case. He presents the clustering and circumstances as suspicious, and quotes those who raised doubts (e.g., Breitbart's witness Lasseter describing the "bright red" color of Breitbart's face and a "thick white band" on his forehead). He acknowledges some (like the Shacknai mansion deaths) may not directly implicate Obama but notes the non-investigation reflects elite protection.
