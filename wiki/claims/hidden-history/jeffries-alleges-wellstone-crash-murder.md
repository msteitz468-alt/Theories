## Claim Statement

Jeffries alleges that the October 25, 2002, plane crash killing Minnesota Senator Paul Wellstone, his wife, his daughter, and five others was not an accident, but politically motivated murder — citing a prior threat by Vice President Dick Cheney, the FBI's premature arrival at the crash site, and multiple witness accounts of an unusual descent that contradict "pilot error."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], shortly before his death, Wellstone met with Cheney, who was seeking support for the invasion of Iraq. Wellstone later recounted to war veterans that Cheney had warned him: "If you vote against the war in Iraq, the Bush administration will do whatever is necessary to get you. There will be severe ramifications for you and the state of Minnesota." Wellstone voted against the war. He died on October 25, 2002 — twelve days before his reelection — in a plane crash near Eveleth, Minnesota.

Additional allegations:

- **FBI arrived early**: Professor James Fetzer alleged the FBI recovery team arrived to investigate the crash before the plane had even gone down. Emergency workers were denied access to the still-burning plane.
- **Congressional suspicion**: Researcher Michael Ruppert interviewed two Democratic members of Congress who told him they believed Wellstone had been murdered. One stated: "I don't think there's anyone on the Hill who doesn't suspect it."
- **Unusual descent**: The Duluth News Tribune noted "for some still unexplained reason, the plane turned off course and crashed." NTSB acting chair Carol Carmody said: "We find the whole turn curious."
- **Witness Megen Williams** heard "a diving noise and then an explosion."
- **Witness Don Sipola**, former airport commission president: "This was a real steep bank … This is more like a space shuttle coming down. This was not a controlled descent into the ground."
- **Bush's odd response**: Bush referred to the "plain-spoken" Wellstone and expressed "condolences for the loss of the Senate." The Nation quoted a senior Republican aide: "There are people in the White House who wake up each morning thinking about how to defeat Paul Wellstone."
- **NTSB conclusion**: The NTSB blamed pilot error.

Jeffries closes: "Whatever really happened to Wellstone's plane, his death was decidedly unnatural and a convenient boon for the establishment."
