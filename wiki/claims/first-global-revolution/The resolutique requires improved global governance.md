## Claim Statement

In Part II, the authors argue that the "deficiencies of governance are at the root of many of the strands of the problematique," so "improved governance is an essential aspect of the resolutique." They call for a capacity to govern that can manage complex, interacting global problems — what they term "global governance" — across every level from the international down to the local and even the family.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

They define "governance" as "the command-mechanism of a social system" providing "security, prosperity, coherence, order and continuity," and apply the concept broadly rather than only to states. The resolutique is operationalized through the **"Three Immediacies"** — the simultaneous attack on the main problem-clusters (the security/disarmament situation and arms-industry conversion at the Cold War's end; North–South development; and the global environment, "a crucial element in the first global revolution"). They stress no comprehensive methodology yet exists and that "existing institutional structures are singularly inappropriate" — yet insist "there is no alternative."
