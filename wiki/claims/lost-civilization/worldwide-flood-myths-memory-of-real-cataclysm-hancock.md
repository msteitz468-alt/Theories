## Claim Statement

Hancock argues that the near-universal myths of a world-destroying **flood** (and accompanying fire, darkness, and earthquakes) — found from Mesopotamia and the Bible to the Americas, India, and the Pacific — are not coincidence or archetype but a shared cultural memory of a real global cataclysm that destroyed the lost civilization. Humanity, having forgotten its true past, is "a species with amnesia."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Part IV catalogues the worldwide flood/cataclysm traditions and their striking convergences (a warned survivor, a vessel, the repopulation of the earth), arguing the pattern records an actual event at the end of the last Ice Age. He links the catastrophe to the rapid climatic and sea-level changes of that period and to his proposed mechanism, earth-crust displacement.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hancock weighs the mainstream explanation (independent local floods, Jungian archetypes) and argues the specificity and global spread of the motifs point instead to a single remembered catastrophe — an inference, not a demonstration.
