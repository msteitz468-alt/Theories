## Claim Statement

Hancock's concluding answer to "where's the body?": the lost civilization was located on **Antarctica**, which was at temperate latitudes and habitable until **earth-crust displacement** slid the crust and carried the continent to the South Pole at the end of the last Ice Age — burying the civilization under miles of ice and triggering the global floods recorded in myth.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He builds on Charles Hapgood's *Earth's Shifting Crust* (foreword by Einstein) for the mechanism and on the Flem-Aths' Antarctica thesis (*When the Sky Fell*) for the location. He marshals supporting evidence that Antarctica was once warm — e.g. fossil *Glossopteris* forests at 85° latitude with no frost-rings, and trees "killed by a flood or mudflow… another impossibility in Antarctica today" — to argue the continent's glaciation is geologically recent. This unifies the maps (Part I), the flood myths (Part IV), and the missing "body" into one answer.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hancock concedes earth-crust displacement is rejected by mainstream geology (which favours slow plate tectonics) and that no artefacts have been recovered from beneath the ice; he frames Antarctica as the best-fit inference, not an excavated fact.
