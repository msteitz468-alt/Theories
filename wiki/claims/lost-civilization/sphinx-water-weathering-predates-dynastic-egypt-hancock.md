## Claim Statement

Hancock, following John Anthony West (and geologist Robert Schoch), argues that the Great **Sphinx**, the Valley Temple, and the Osireion at Abydos bear unmistakable signs of **water erosion** — possible only during the rainy "pluvial" period at the end of the last Ice Age — implying these monuments were built **before c. 10,000 BC**, far earlier than the orthodox attribution to Khafre (c. 2500 BC).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He summarizes West's case: the monuments "showed many scientifically unmistakable signs of having been weathered by water, an erosive agent they could only have been exposed to… during the damp 'pluvial' period that accompanied the end of the last Ice Age around the eleventh millennium BC," so that "the Osireion, the Sphinx, and other associated structures were built before 10,000 BC." Hancock quotes a journalist's verdict that West is "an academic's worst nightmare… full of data they can't refute," to which the establishment response is to "ignore it."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hancock acknowledges Egyptology attributes the Sphinx to Khafre and rejects the redating; the water-erosion interpretation is West's and Schoch's, contested by mainstream archaeologists (he cites Carol Redmont's incredulity).
