## Claim Statement

Hancock argues the Great Pyramid embodies deliberate advanced mathematics and astronomy: its proportions encode the irrational constants **pi** and **phi** (the golden section), its faces are almost perfectly aligned and right-angled, and its concealed internal **shafts** were precisely targeted on stars — engineering, he contends, beyond the supposed capacity of its builders and bespeaking inherited high knowledge.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He notes the builders achieved near-perfect 90-degree corners and cardinal alignment ("very few modern buildings… have corners that consist of perfect ninety degree right angles"), and that the monument's geometry expresses pi and the golden section phi ("another irrational number like pi that could not be worked out arithmetically"). He recounts the mystery of the King's and Queen's Chamber **shafts** — deliberately sealed off ("the builders had left the last five inches of stone intact… rendering them invisible") — and Rudolf Gantenbrink's 1993 robot discovery of a sealed "portcullis door" up the southern shaft, treating the shafts as purposeful star-pointers and "invitations" rather than mere ventilation.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hancock presents these as anomalies challenging the orthodox view that the pyramid was solely a Fourth-Dynasty tomb; the intentionality he reads into the mathematics and shafts is interpretive.
