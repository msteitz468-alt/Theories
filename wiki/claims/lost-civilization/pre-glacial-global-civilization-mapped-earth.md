**Claim Statement**  
An advanced, worldwide maritime civilization with global trade routes, an organized central government, and sophisticated scientific knowledge (including astronomy, ship-building, and navigation instruments) existed during the last Ice Age, at least 20,000 years ago, prior to the rise of any known historical cultures.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *Maps of the Ancient Sea Kings* (Chapters I, II, V, and VIII):
* **Global Scope of Maps:** The surviving charts deal with the shores of the entire Atlantic Ocean, the Americas, and the Arctic and Antarctic poles. Mapping on such a scale indicates a worldwide maritime commerce and the economic resources of an organized global government.
* **Pre-Glacial Mapping of Antarctica:** Depicting Antarctica's sub-glacial bedrock and ice-free shores requires that the surveying expeditions took place before the continent's coasts froze over, which Hapgood dates to the Ice Age period.
* **Ice Age Land Bridges:** The Hadji Ahmed Map of 1559 preserves a land bridge connecting Siberia and Alaska, indicating that the original geographical observations were made during a period of lower sea level, which occurred when a substantial portion of the world's water was locked in Ice Age glaciers.
* **Non-Linear Development of History:** Hapgood argues that the standard archaeological view of human society progressing linearly from the Old Stone Age (Paleolithic) through the New Stone Age (Neolithic) to the Bronze and Iron Ages is incorrect. Instead, he asserts that advanced societies existed concurrently with Paleolithic hunters elsewhere on Earth.

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Hapgood presents this claim to challenge conventional historical timelines. He frames the existence of this vanished civilization not as a myth, but as a necessary deduction from the existence of the maps themselves. He argues that the maps are "hard evidence" that cannot be dismissed by conventional historians and require a total reexamination of human prehistory.
