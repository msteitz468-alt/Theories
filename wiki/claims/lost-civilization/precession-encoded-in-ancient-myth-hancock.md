## Claim Statement

Hancock argues that the slow wobble of the Earth's axis — the **precession of the equinoxes** (a ~25,920-year cycle) — is deliberately encoded in the numbers and imagery of myths and sacred traditions worldwide, implying that a sophisticated ancient science of astronomy long predates its supposed discovery and was inherited from the lost civilization.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He builds on Giorgio de Santillana (MIT historian of science) and Hertha von Dechend's *Hamlet's Mill*, which argued that ancient myth is a technical language carrying precessional astronomy. Hancock highlights recurring "precessional numbers" (e.g. 72, the years for one degree of precession, and its multiples like 2,160 and 25,920) embedded across mythologies, treating these as a deliberate "code" devised by "serious and intelligent people… ancient scientists" to transmit exact astronomical knowledge.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hancock acknowledges the *Hamlet's Mill* thesis is controversial and that the number-matching is interpretive; he presents it as strong corroboration of a forgotten advanced science rather than as proof.
