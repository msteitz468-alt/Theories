## Claim Statement

Hancock argues that certain early maps — above all the **Piri Reis map** of 1513 — preserve accurate surveys of Antarctica's coastline *as it was before the ice-cap formed*, implying the source charts were drawn by an advanced civilization in deep prehistory.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He centres a 1960 U.S. Air Force letter (Lt. Col. Ohlmeyer to Hapgood) judging it "reasonable" and "in all probability… correct" that the map's lower portion depicts the ice-free "Princess Martha Coast of Queen Maud Land," matching the 1949 seismic survey of the sub-ice coastline: "This indicates the coastline had been mapped before it was covered by the ice-cap." Hancock notes the map is "a genuine document, not a hoax," that Antarctica was not (officially) discovered until 1818, and that Piri Reis said he compiled it from older source maps — leading Hapgood to conclude the charts "must have originated with a" hitherto-unknown civilization.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hancock acknowledges this requires cartography in a window (he suggests c. 13,000–4000 BC) when no civilization is known to have had the capacity, and that the dating is uncertain.
