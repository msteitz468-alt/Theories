## Claim Statement

Hancock, following Robert Bauval's "Orion correlation," argues that the three pyramids of Giza are a precise terrestrial map of the three stars of **Orion's Belt**, and that the layout encodes the sky not of the Fourth Dynasty (c. 2500 BC) but of around **10,500 BC** — pointing to a far older astronomical design inherited from the lost civilization.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He relays Bauval's finding that "the Great Pyramid of Khufu [occupies] the position of Al Nitak, and the Second Pyramid of Khafre… Al Nilam, while the Third Pyramid of Menkaure was offset to the east" — exactly mirroring the belt stars' offset, with the relative pyramid sizes reflecting the stars' magnitudes, "endorsed by mathematicians and astronomers." Crucially, accounting for the precession of the equinoxes, the Giza monuments "as a whole were so arranged as to provide a picture of the skies… not as they had looked in the Fourth Dynasty… but as they had looked – and only as they had looked – around the year 10,450 BC." (In Egyptian myth Orion is the celestial form of Osiris.)

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hancock notes the correlation is Bauval's hypothesis (which he says astronomers endorsed) and that some features of the Great Pyramid do relate astronomically to the Pyramid Age; the 10,500 BC reading is an inference from precessional alignment.
