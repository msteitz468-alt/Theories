## Claim Statement
Thomas argues that the gulf opened by mid-seventeenth-century intellectual change was chiefly between educated elites and the rural lower classes, and that traditional magical beliefs — healing wells, divination, cunning folk, witchcraft, omens, ghosts — persisted vigorously in the English countryside into the nineteenth century and beyond, while modern society itself retains quasi-magical practices (spiritualism, horoscopes, ritualized medicine) that resist a clean distinction between "science" and "magic."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Thomas quotes an 1856 report from Lincolnshire: "Those who are not in daily intercourse with the peasantry can hardly be made to believe or comprehend the hold that charms, witchcraft, wise men and other like relics of heathendom have upon the people." He cites Jacob Burckhardt's verdict that nineteenth-century religion was "rationalism for the few and magic for the many," and notes middle-class fascination with "spiritualism and automatic writing, astrology, haunted houses," plus Mary Baker Eddy's "malicious animal magnetism" as a revival of witchcraft-fear. He cites a modern survey finding "about a quarter of the population… holds a view of the universe which can most properly be designated as magical." He closes by arguing that "modern medicine shares an optimistic bias with the charmers and wise women and it has similar means of explaining away any failure," and offers his final definition: "If magic is to be defined as the employment of ineffective techniques to allay anxiety when effective ones are not available, then we must recognize that no society will ever be free from it."

## Contexts Within the Source
This is the concluding statement of the entire book, following Thomas's extended critique of Malinowski's science-versus-magic dichotomy; he argues the sociological and psychological continuities between "magic" and modern "science" undercut any tidy claim that magic has simply vanished, even as its seventeenth-century intellectual and institutional foundations collapsed.
