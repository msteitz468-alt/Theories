## Claim Statement
Thomas documents that ancient prophecies, especially Galfridian material invoking Merlin, the Mouldwarp and related figures, were invoked in virtually every major English, Welsh and Irish rebellion or popular rising from the Wars of the Roses through the Civil War, functioning as claimed advance sanction for resistance to the Crown.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Thomas traces the "Mouldwarp" prophecy from its use by the Percies against Henry IV, through its revival against Henry VIII during the Pilgrimage of Grace, where "John Hale, vicar of Isleworth, was executed in 1535 after declaring that Henry VIII was the Mouldwarp of whom Merlin had prophesied." He documents the Norfolk rebels under Robert Ket proclaiming a prophecy about "clouted shoon" filling "Dussindale/With slaughtered bodies soon," and Irish and Welsh nationalist prophecies sustaining hopes of expelling English rule, including one used to support Sir Phelim O'Neill during the 1641 Ulster Rebellion.

## Contexts Within the Source
Thomas notes the same prophetic vocabulary was often deployed by both sides — the Crown itself commissioned counter-prophecies (Richard Morison identifying Henry VIII with the Lion of the Book of Esdras) — and that the tradition climaxed during the Civil War through William Lilly's prophetic anthologies interpreting events against Charles I.
