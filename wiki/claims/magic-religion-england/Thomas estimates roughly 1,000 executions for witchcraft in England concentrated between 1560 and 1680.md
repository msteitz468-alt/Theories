## Claim Statement
Drawing on C. L'Estrange Ewen's pioneering statistical work, Thomas documents that on the Home Assize Circuit (Essex, Hertfordshire, Kent, Surrey, Sussex) between 1559 and 1736 there were 513 accused, over 200 convicted, and 109 hanged, and estimates the total English execution figure at "something under 1,000" — high enough to exceed the number of Catholic martyrs executed in the period (264), but low by continental standards and lower than Scotland's per-capita rate. Prosecutions were concentrated overwhelmingly in the reign of Elizabeth I and the Matthew Hopkins campaign of 1645–7, not evenly spread across the two centuries the statutes were in force.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"C. L'Estrange Ewen... guessed the total number of executions for witchcraft throughout the period to be something under 1,000... It greatly exceeded the parallel prosecution of Catholic clergy, for the total number of English Catholic martyrs, lay and clerical, executed during the period was only 264." "On the Home Circuit the trials were at their zenith during the reign of Elizabeth I, when 455 out of the 790 known indictments were made."

## Contexts Within the Source
Thomas offers these figures cautiously, stressing that surviving legal records are patchy and that formal prosecutions represent only "the tip of an iceberg" of informal accusations that never reached court.
