## Claim Statement

The Morgan-controlled Guaranty Trust Company of New York maintained a continuing working relationship with the Bolsheviks before, during, and after the Russian Revolution — chiefly through the Swedish banker Olof Aschberg — culminating in Guaranty Trust vice president Max May becoming a director of the first Soviet international bank, Ruskombank, in 1923.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sutton (Ch. XI synopsis): "We have now gone beyond this evidence to establish a continuing working relationship between Bolshevik banker Olof Aschberg and the Morgan-controlled Guaranty Trust Company in New York before, during, and after the Russian Revolution. In tsarist times Aschberg was the Morgan agent in Russia ...; during 1917 Aschberg was financial intermediary for the revolutionaries; and after the revolution Aschberg became head of Ruskombank ... while Max May, a vice president of the Morgan-controlled Guaranty Trust, became director and chief of the Ruskombank foreign department."

"The first really concrete signal that previous political and financial support was paying off came in 1923 when the Soviets formed their first international bank, Ruskombank.... the Ruskombank promptly appointed Guaranty Trust Company its U.S. agent." Sutton also documents Guaranty Trust backing the Soviet Bureau in New York (1919) and notes British Intelligence chief Sir Basil Thompson's statement that the first Soviet ambassador Ludwig Martens was backed by Guaranty Trust funds.
