## Claim Statement

The same Wall Street interests that supported the Bolsheviks also financed and aided the anti-Bolshevik (White) side — notably Morgan and Rockefeller support for Admiral Kolchak — hedging across both sides of the Russian Civil War in the manner Griffin describes as the Rothschild Formula.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sutton devotes Chapter X ("J.P. Morgan Gives a Little Help to the Other Side") to this hedge, with sections "United Americans Formed to Fight Communism," "United Americans Reveals 'Startling Disclosures' on Reds," and "Morgan and Rockefeller Aid Kolchak." In the synopsis he notes that support for the Bolsheviks "did not cease with consolidation of the revolution" — the American-Russian syndicate (1918) "to obtain concessions in Russia was backed by the White, Guggenheim, and Sinclair interests," whose company directors included Thomas W. Lamont (Guaranty Trust), William Boyce Thompson (Federal Reserve Bank), and Harry Payne Whitney (Guaranty Trust) — "formed to cash in on earlier support for the Bolshevik cause."

## Contexts Within the Source

The "both sides" pattern parallels Griffin's [[The Rothschild Formula]] (financing both belligerents) and underscores Sutton's profit-driven explanation rather than ideological commitment.
