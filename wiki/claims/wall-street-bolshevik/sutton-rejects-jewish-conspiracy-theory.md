## Claim Statement

Sutton examines and explicitly rejects the "Jewish-conspiracy theory" of the Bolshevik Revolution as spurious and unsupported by evidence, and finds that Jacob Schiff — frequently named as a financier of the Bolsheviks — actually opposed the Bolshevik regime.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Sutton (Appendix II) reviews the literature (Churchill's 1920 "Zionism Versus Bolshevism," Wickham Steed, the State Department file "Bolshevism and Judaism," and the *Protocols of Zion* material) and concludes: "There is no concrete evidence that Jews were involved in the Bolshevik Revolution because they were Jewish. There may indeed have been a higher proportion of Jews involved, but given tsarist treatment of Jews, what else would we expect?"

He notes that under "scientific procedure ... the proportion of foreign Jewish Bolsheviks ... falls to less than twenty percent of the total number of revolutionaries — and these Jews were mostly deported, murdered, or sent to Siberia in the following years. Modern Russia has in fact maintained tsarist anti-Semitism." Crucially: "documents in the State Department files confirm that the investment banker Jacob Schiff ... was in fact against support of the Bolshevik regime. This position ... was in direct contrast to the Morgan-Rockefeller promotion of the Bolsheviks."
