## Claim Statement

Barkun argues that the shape-shifting reptilian alien at the center of figures like David Icke's superconspiracy is not an independently discovered truth but a concept assembled over several decades from intentional fiction (principally Robert E. Howard's pulp stories), occultist pamphlets (Maurice Doreal), and pseudoscientific inner-earth writing, before being re-presented as historical fact. Barkun characterizes this as a paradigm case of "fact-fiction reversal" in stigmatized knowledge: material created as deliberate entertainment or occult speculation migrates into the conspiracy subculture as purported documentary reality.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Barkun traces the shape-changing serpent-race concept specifically to Robert E. Howard's 1929 *Weird Tales* story "The Shadow Kingdom," featuring snake-men with human bodies, serpentine heads, and hypnotic shape-shifting powers, who insinuate themselves into positions of authority. H. P. Lovecraft and Clark Ashton Smith, Howard's *Weird Tales* contemporaries, also incorporated serpent-men motifs. Barkun judges it "almost certain" that Maurice Doreal appropriated this fictional serpent race in his pamphlets (notably "Mysteries of the Gobi" and "Flying Saucers: An Occult Viewpoint"), recasting it as a prehistoric historical reality — a Serpent Race with "bodies like man" but serpentine heads and hypnotic powers, identified as extraterrestrials awaiting revival as Antichrist's allies. Doreal's "translation" of the alleged Emerald Tablets — attributed to "Thoth, an Atlantean Priest-King" — introduced the politically resonant warning that the serpent race "took over the control of the nations." Barkun notes explicitly that David Icke used Doreal's Emerald Tablets "extensively" in [[Children of the Matrix (Icke, 2001)]].

By 1951, Robert Ernst Dickhoff (*Agharta*) had synthesized these elements into a narrative of Venusian humanoid serpent men infiltrating human civilization through an antediluvian tunnel system — reptilians in polar suspended animation awaiting their moment to strike. By the time Dulce-base reports appeared in the late 1980s, Barkun concludes, "the fictional scenario of reptoids, presented as fact by occultists like Dickhoff, was available, fully formed." In the 1990s, Branton integrated the material into his "Reptilian-Bavarian Illuminati" superconspiracy, which Icke then absorbed and amplified globally, including through the "Reptilian Connection" section of his website and in [[The Biggest Secret (Icke, 1999)]].

Barkun also identifies the biblical layer added by conspiracists: the Eden serpent is identified as the first reptoid, and the "two-seedline" theology of Christian Identity (the serpent's seed vs. Eve's seed in apocalyptic war) is retained in reptoid conspiracism but typically without Christian Identity's explicit anti-Semitism — though, Barkun notes, it occasionally slides toward coded anti-Jewish scapegoating when figures like Commander X link the "Serpent's seed" with "positions of financial power."

## What This Characterizes in the Wiki

This analysis directly characterizes the reptilian thesis as presented in [[The Biggest Secret (Icke, 1999)]] and [[Children of the Matrix (Icke, 2001)]]. Barkun's genealogy — Howard → Doreal → Dickhoff → Branton → Icke — provides the scholarly account of how material those sources present as revealed truth was in fact assembled from pulp fiction and occultist pamphlets over half a century.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Barkun acknowledges genuine difficulty dating Doreal's pamphlets, since most bear only the date of their most recent reprinting. He therefore hedges on exactly when the serpent-race material first appeared. He also notes that whether Doreal read Howard directly remains unproven, though his appearance in *Amazing Stories* makes pulp-fiction familiarity plausible.
