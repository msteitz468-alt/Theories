## Claim Statement

Barkun argues that Milton William Cooper's [[Behold a Pale Horse (Cooper, 1991)]] is "not only among the most complex superconspiracy theories, it is also among the most influential" — widely available in mainstream bookstores and read in both UFO and militia circles. Barkun characterizes Cooper as building on the Illuminati tradition of Griffin and Todd, but making two distinctive moves: reprinting *The Protocols of the Elders of Zion* in full with instructions to substitute "Illuminati" for Jewish references, and adding actual extraterrestrial beings as the ultimate hidden controllers of the conspiracy.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Barkun notes that Cooper projected the Illuminati back to the medieval Knights Templar (with Weishaupt merely establishing a new "particularly evil outpost"), financed by the Rothschilds. Though Cooper denied antisemitic intent, Barkun observes he followed predecessors in linking the Illuminati to Jewish influences, and reprinted the entire *Protocols* with the instruction: "any reference to Jews should be replaced with the word 'Illuminati'; and the word 'goyim' should be replaced with the word 'cattle.'"

Cooper identified the Bilderbergers as the Illuminati's inner circle, with a "dizzying array" of other organizations — Knights of Columbus, Jesuits, Masons, Communist Party, Nazi Party, Council on Foreign Relations, Vatican, Skull and Bones — all working "toward the same ultimate goal, a New World Order."

Cooper's distinctive contribution was presenting two simultaneous UFO-conspiracy theories: (1) that the Illuminati have been fabricating an alien invasion hoax since 1917 to create a pretext for world government; and (2) that actual extraterrestrials are acting in concert with human conspirators. In the latter scenario, Eisenhower allegedly signed a treaty with aliens in 1954, granting the Illuminati access to alien space technology — enabling an Alternative 3–style escape from earth. Most strikingly, Cooper ultimately subordinated even the Illuminati: "aliens have manipulated and/or ruled the human race through various secret societies, religions, magic, witchcraft, and the occult." The Illuminati, seemingly at the top of the world power hierarchy, are themselves "the dupes of a strange and evil alien race."

## What This Characterizes in the Wiki

This analysis is Barkun's most extended treatment of [[Behold a Pale Horse (Cooper, 1991)]]. Cooper's work sits at the intersection of the NWO and UFO conspiracy traditions that Barkun traces, making this claim also relevant to [[Models of the New World Order Elites Compared]] and [[Secret societies through history compared across sources]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Barkun notes that Cooper's theory has an internal contradiction: at one level Cooper concedes the New World Order "may be necessary" if humanity is to survive ecological and civilizational collapse; at another level he calls on patriots to resist it. This tension — between the NWO as a grim necessity and as absolute evil — is characteristic, Barkun suggests, of the improvisational quality of superconspiracy literature.
