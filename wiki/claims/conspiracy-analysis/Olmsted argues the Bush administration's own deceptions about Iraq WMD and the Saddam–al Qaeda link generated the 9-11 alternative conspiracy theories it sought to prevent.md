## Claim Statement

Olmsted argues that the Bush administration's demonstrable lies — about the Saddam–al Qaeda connection, the Niger uranium claim, and Iraq's weapons of mass destruction — directly caused the surge in alternative 9/11 conspiracy theories. By 2006, 36 percent of Americans believed the government either helped the terrorists or took no action to stop them. Olmsted frames this not as irrational paranoia but as the predictable consequence of an administration that promoted a false conspiracy theory (Iraq-al Qaeda), ran a real conspiracy (manipulating intelligence "fixed around the policy"), and then expected the public to accept its account of September 11 at face value.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Olmsted documents the sequence of official deceptions and their exposure:

- **The Saddam–al Qaeda fabrication:** Cheney stated December 9, 2001, that it was "pretty well confirmed" that Mohammed Atta met with Iraqi intelligence in Prague. CIA analysts knew from the start this was wrong — "We knew right away that's not Atta." By August 2002, after months of administration arguments, 53 percent of Americans blamed Saddam for 9/11. The 9/11 Commission later definitively found no "collaborative relationship" between Iraq and al Qaeda; the percentage of believers fell from 69 percent to 22 percent.
- **The WMD fraud:** Powell's UN speech presented "irrefutable evidence" of reconstituted nuclear programs and WMD stockpiles. UN inspectors found nothing. David Kay told Congress in 2004: "We were almost all wrong." The Downing Street memos (2005) revealed that British intelligence reported: "the intelligence and facts were being fixed around the policy."
- **The insider witnesses:** Ambassador Joseph Wilson exposed the fraudulent Niger uranium claim in a 2003 New York Times op-ed; the administration responded by outing his CIA wife Valerie Plame. Richard Clarke's sworn testimony (March 2004) and *Against All Enemies* showed an administration "obsessed with Iraq" and dismissive of bin Laden. Paul Pillar (CIA's top Middle East officer) said the administration "misused" intelligence "to justify a decision already made." Lawrence Wilkerson (Powell's chief of staff) called the UN speech "a hoax on the American people" and described a "cabal" led by Cheney and Rumsfeld.
- **The political impact:** The proportion of Americans believing Bush deliberately lied about the case for war climbed from 31 percent in June 2003 to 53 percent in October 2005. An unnamed Bush official told reporter Ron Suskind: "We're an empire now, and when we act, we create our own reality."
- **The resulting alternative theories:** As official lies were exposed, LIHOP and MIHOP theories multiplied. *Loose Change* (2005) was watched by ten million people. A 2006 Scripps Howard poll found 36 percent of Americans believed the government helped or allowed the attacks; 16 percent believed explosives brought down the towers.

Olmsted directly links the two: "Real government conspiracies and cover-ups made these revisionist theories more believable. The popularity of alternative 9/11 conspiracy theories rose as Americans' trust in their president fell."

## What this bears on in the wiki

This is the Olmsted account that most directly intersects with [[Jeffries alleges 9-11 was an inside job or allowed to happen, and the official account is a cover-up]]. Olmsted does not endorse the alternative theories, but she documents the same evidentiary anomalies (pre-attack warnings, Saudi connections, commission obstruction) that skeptics like Jeffries treat as evidence of inside involvement. The key difference: Olmsted explains the alternative theories as responses to proven deception, not as accurate accounts of 9/11.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Olmsted is explicit that the alternative 9/11 theories are largely wrong: the controlled-demolition thesis requires an "absurdly convoluted" cast of thousands; MIHOP theorists work backward from cui bono to predetermined conclusions. She treats the Jersey Girls and Richard Clarke as legitimate truth-seekers while treating *Loose Change* as propaganda. She notes the "slow march toward self-delusion" that both official and alternative conspiracists follow.
