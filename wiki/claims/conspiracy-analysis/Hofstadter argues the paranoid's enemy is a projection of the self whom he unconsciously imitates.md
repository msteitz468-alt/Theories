## Claim Statement

Hofstadter argues the paranoid's enemy "seems to be on many counts a projection of the self" — both
the ideal and the forbidden aspects of the self are attributed to him — producing "a fundamental
paradox of the paranoid style: the imitation of the enemy."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His examples: the Ku Klux Klan donning priestly vestments and elaborate ritual in imitation of the
Catholicism it hated; the John Birch Society emulating Communist cells and front groups; secret
organizations founded to fight secret organizations. He also notes the special role of the
**renegade** (the ex-Mason, apostate priest, ex-Communist) as the insider whose "revelations"
verify the suspicions, and the projection of forbidden sexual/sadistic fantasy onto the enemy.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Hofstadter offers this as interpretation of a recurrent pattern, illustrated rather than
systematically proven, drawing on David Brion Davis's study of counter-subversive movements.
