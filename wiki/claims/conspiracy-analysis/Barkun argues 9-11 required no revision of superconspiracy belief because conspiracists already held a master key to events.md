## Claim Statement

Barkun argues that the September 11, 2001 attacks produced no genuine cognitive disruption within the conspiracist subculture, because believers already possessed a comprehensive explanatory framework into which the attacks could be immediately inserted. Unlike the Kennedy assassination — which created a conspiracist subculture where little previously existed — 9/11 occurred in the presence of a flourishing, pre-existing New World Order superconspiracy framework. Conspiracists simply named new actors (Illuminati, grey aliens, "Khazarian Zionist Bolsheviks") as the forces behind the hijackers and interpreted the attacks as confirmation of an already-established truth rather than as evidence requiring new theory construction.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Barkun documents the immediate range of conspiracist responses:

- Duncan M. Roads (*Nexus*) described mainstream accounts as "manufactured reality."
- Texe Marrs attributed the attacks to the Illuminati.
- The Phoenix publications' successor (*The Spectrum*) framed the hijackings as "New World Order Thru 'Terrorism.'"
- Patrick H. Bellringer attributed the attacks to "the Khazarian Zionist Bolshevik (KZB) world controllers" — "robotoids" with "fourth dimensional shape-shifting capabilities."
- TCA (an internet author writing in Branton's idiom) attributed the attacks to "gray aliens" operating through the Illuminati and the CIA, who trained bin Laden; the aim was to ignite world war so aliens could "abduct great amount of bodies to the hybrid project."
- David Icke's initial response attributed the attacks to the Illuminati, with bin Laden "set up," the goal being a nuclear war culminating in Illuminati world government. Icke's channelled source later added that celebrities would be turned into "genetically manipulated zombies" through cosmetic-surgery mind-control operations.
- Milton William Cooper, weeks before his own death in a gunfight with sheriff's deputies, identified the beneficiaries as the Bush family, the oil industry, the UN, and Israel — not Al Qaeda.

Barkun's analytical conclusion: "Their fundamental conclusion did not depend on the confirmation of such legends and reports. Rather, they argued that they already knew who was responsible for the world's evil; therefore, the attacks were seen as merely an additional demonstration of what was already established truth. To the extent that September 11 required any revision of conspiracy theories, it suggested that the plotters were even more brazen and desperate than previously thought."

He also notes that the visible world is, for conspiracists, always deception — hence the rapid identification of the hijackers made no difference to the interpretive framework.

## What this characterises in the wiki

This claim directly characterises the response to 9/11 by authors whose works appear in the wiki: [[Behold a Pale Horse (Cooper, 1991)]] (Cooper's final essay on 9/11) and [[The Biggest Secret (Icke, 1999)]] / [[Children of the Matrix (Icke, 2001)]] (Icke's channelled post-9/11 elaborations). The pattern of inserting events into pre-existing superconspiracy structures is also relevant to [[Is history steered by a deliberate cabal or an emergent network? Models compared]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Barkun notes that some conspiracist voices were simply absent: Jim Keith had died in 1999; Branton was incapacitated by a bicycle accident. The specific actors blamed varied considerably across the subculture (Illuminati, grey aliens, Jewish Khazarians, the U.S. government). Barkun also acknowledges that the scope and televised drama of 9/11 did produce potent new apocalyptic imagery — the London *Daily Mail* ran a one-word headline, "Apocalypse" — that mainstream and esoteric audiences processed differently.
