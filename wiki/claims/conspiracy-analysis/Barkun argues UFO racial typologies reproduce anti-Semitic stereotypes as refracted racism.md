## Claim Statement

Barkun argues that even among UFO conspiracists who explicitly reject anti-Semitism, the racial taxonomies they apply to alien species unconsciously reproduce traditional European anti-Semitic stereotypes in displaced form — what he calls "refracted racism." The evil, dwarfish, hook-nosed Grays correspond structurally to the caricatured Jew of anti-Semitic folklore, while the tall, blond, benevolent Nordics or Aryans correspond to the idealised Aryan of Nazi racial ideology. This displacement allows authors to traffic in racial hierarchy while maintaining deniability about earthbound prejudice.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Barkun observes that writers such as Milton William Cooper, George C. Andrews, and the pseudonymous Branton all posit multiple alien races distinguished by appearance and moral disposition. Cooper identified "two kinds of Grays," "Nordic types, tall blond Aryans," and "Orange ones." Andrews linked short Grays to Rigel and Hitler's CIA collaborators, and tall Blonds to Procyon. Branton divided aliens into "Benevolents" (Blond-Nordic/Aryan-like) and "Malevolents."

Barkun's analytical conclusion: "The evil Grays are dwarfish with grotesque features — not unlike stereotypes of the short, swarthy, hook-nosed Jew of European anti-Semitic folklore. They are contrasted to the tall, virtuous Nordics or Aryans. Although there is little to suggest that those who employ such terms do so to make direct parallels to earthbound categories, the images seem clearly to be refracted versions of older racial anti-Semitism."

He further traces the genealogy of this refracted racism to a set of interlocking personal connections: George Hunt Williamson worked briefly for fascist anti-Semite William Dudley Pelley before becoming a major early contactee; Pelley's *Star Guests* (1950) provided an occult, extraterrestrial framework for racial hierarchy; and channeler networks (Hatonn, Ashtar Command, the Phoenix publications) subsequently institutionalised anti-Semitic tropes under a galactic imprimatur. Barkun concludes that "the domain of stigmatized knowledge in the 1950s was one in which mystic and anti-Semitic teachings mingled freely."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Barkun acknowledges genuine variation: some UFO conspiracists (Branton, Stan Deyo) are unambiguously hostile to Nazism and portray Jews as victims. The refracted racism pattern is strongest in writers who posit multiple alien races; single-alien-race theories are less susceptible to this mapping. Barkun also concedes that direct intent cannot be demonstrated — the racial parallels may be unconscious.
