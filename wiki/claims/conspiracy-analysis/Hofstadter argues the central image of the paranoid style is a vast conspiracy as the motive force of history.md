## Claim Statement

Hofstadter argues the defining feature of the paranoid style is treating "a 'vast' or 'gigantic'
conspiracy as the motive force in historical events" — not merely noting that conspiracies occur, but
holding that "History is a conspiracy, set in motion by demonic forces of almost transcendent power."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He describes the enemy as "a perfect model of malice, a kind of amoral superman" and a free agent who
"manufactures the mechanism of history himself" — making crises, starting "runs on banks," causing
depressions, and profiting from the misery. Decisive events are read not as part of history's stream
but "as the consequences of someone's will."

## What this counters in the wiki

This is the exact shape of the wiki's design-conspiracy claims — see
[[Is history steered by a deliberate cabal or an emergent network? Models compared]] and

secret for influencing the mind" motifs recur in [[Behold a Pale Horse (Cooper, 1991)]] and
[[Everything You Need to Know But Have Never Been Told (Icke, 2018)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Hofstadter explicitly grants "there are conspiratorial acts in history, and there is nothing paranoid
about taking note of them" — the pathology is in making conspiracy *the* engine of all events.
