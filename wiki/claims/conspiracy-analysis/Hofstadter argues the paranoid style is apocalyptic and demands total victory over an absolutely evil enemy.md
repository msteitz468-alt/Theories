## Claim Statement

Hofstadter argues the paranoid style is **apocalyptic**: because the struggle is "always a conflict
between absolute good and absolute evil," it demands not compromise but "the will to fight things out
to a finish" — total victory, the total elimination of a totally evil and unappeasable enemy.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The paranoid "traffics in the birth and death of whole worlds," "constantly lives at a turning point"
where "time is forever just running out" (he quotes Robert Welch: "Time is running out…"). Because the
goals are unrealizable, failure perpetually heightens frustration and reconfirms the enemy's terrifying
power. He likens it to a "secular and demonic version of adventism."

## What this counters in the wiki

The "all-out crusade against an absolute enemy, time running out" register pervades
[[Behold a Pale Horse (Cooper, 1991)]] and [[Everything You Need to Know But Have Never Been Told (Icke, 2018)]];
the apocalyptic framing also appears in the wiki's NWO and end-times material.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Hofstadter notes the apocalypticism "runs dangerously near to hopeless pessimism, but usually stops
short of it," functioning to arouse militancy rather than despair.
