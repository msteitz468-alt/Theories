## Claim Statement

The Apocryphon of John teaches that the archons — prompted by hearing a divine voice proclaim "The Man exists" and seeing the reflected image of the heavenly Man in the waters below — assembled a human body through the combined contributions of 365 angels, each responsible for a specific anatomical feature. This body was inert until the higher Pleroma devised a stratagem: the five lights advised Yaldabaoth to blow his spirit into Adam's face. Yaldabaoth did so, unwittingly transferring Sophia's stolen power (his own animating force) into Adam. Adam thereby received a divine spark superior to all his makers, which panicked the archons into casting him into the lowest matter and fashioning a second, heavier material body to imprison him.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The divine voice triggers the archons' project:

> "A voice came forth from the exalted aeon-heaven: 'The Man exists and the son of Man.' And the chief archon, Yaltabaoth, heard (it) and thought that the voice had come from his mother… And he said to the authorities which attend him, 'Come, let us create a man according to the image of God and according to our likeness, that his image may become a light for us.'" (Apocryphon of John, Codex II)

The assembly by 365 angels is catalogued in extraordinary anatomical detail — head, brain, eyes, ears, nose, lips, teeth, molars, tonsils, uvula, neck, vertebrae, throat, shoulders, elbows, hands, fingers, nails, breasts, belly, navel, ribs, hips, spine, stomach, heart, lungs, liver, spleen, intestines, kidneys, sinews, arteries, genitals, legs, feet, and toes — each assigned to a named angel. The total is given precisely:

> "This is the number of the angels: together they are 365. They all worked on it until, limb for limb, the natural and the material body was completed by them… And all the angels and demons worked until they had constructed the natural body. And their product was completely inactive and motionless for a long time."

The stratagem and the transfer of the divine spark:

> "They said to Yaltabaoth, 'Blow into his face something of your spirit and his body will arise.' And he blew into his face the spirit which is the power of his mother; he did not know (this), for he exists in ignorance. And the power of the mother went out of Yaldabaoth into the natural body, which they had fashioned after the image of the one who exists from the beginning. The body moved and gained strength, and it was luminous."

The archons' immediate terror at what they had created:

> "In that moment the rest of the powers became jealous, because he had come into being through all of them and they had given their power to the man, and his intelligence was greater than that of those who had made him, and greater than that of the chief archon. And when they recognized that he was luminous, and that he could think better than they, and that he was free from wickedness, they took him and threw him into the lowest region of all matter."

The material body as a second, heavier prison:

> "They brought him (Adam) into the shadow of death, in order that they might form (him) again from earth and water and fire and the spirit which originates in matter, which is the ignorance of darkness and desire, and their counterfeit spirit. This is the tomb of the newly-formed body with which the robbers had clothed the man, the bond of forgetfulness; and he became a mortal man."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The tractate later reveals that even after being thrown into the lowest matter, Adam is not abandoned: the Mother-Father sends the "luminous Epinoia" (Life) to dwell hidden within him as a corrective. The divine spark is therefore unkillable by archonic action — it can be suppressed and forgotten but not destroyed. The text also distinguishes between the psychic/material body (which is the archons' work) and the spiritual element (which comes from the Pleroma), maintaining that the two are fundamentally different in kind.
