## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that the body has eight energy centers (seven in the body plus one above the head), each with its own glands, hormones, neuronal plexus ("mini-brain"), and mind. When a meditator slows brain waves to alpha/theta — bypassing the analytical mind — and combines attention to each center with open focus on the surrounding space and sustained elevated emotion, a coherent electromagnetic field builds around each center. This coherent field provides correct "instructions" to the autonomic nervous system, which Dispenza claims then heals the body, restores hormonal balance, and reverses disease caused by incoherent stress-state energy. He calls this meditation "one of the most popular" in his community and reports it has produced a large number of "supernatural results."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Chapter 4 is devoted to this teaching. Dispenza states: "In the Blessing of the Energy Centers meditation, when you learn how to rest your attention in each of these centers and become aware of the space around them, you create coherence in each of these little brains in the same manner as you create coherence in the big brain between your ears."

The mechanism:
1. Chronic stress or stuck emotions keep creative energy confined to the lower three survival-oriented centers (sexuality, digestion/social, will/power), drawing from and shrinking the body's "invisible field of energy."
2. Meditation slows brain waves, accessing the subconscious operating system (the autonomic nervous system in the midbrain).
3. Placing attention in each center (convergent focus) followed by awareness of the space surrounding it (divergent focus) shifts the brain from incoherent beta to coherent alpha/theta.
4. Elevated emotion (love, gratitude, joy) raises the frequency of the coherent field built around each center.
5. A coherent, high-frequency field gives correct instructions to atoms, molecules, cells, tissues, and organs — drawing each toward balance/homeostasis.
6. After completing all eight centers, the meditator rests for 15 minutes in surrender, allowing the autonomic nervous system to integrate the instructions.

Reported community healings attributed to this meditation include: chronic bladder infections, prostate problems, impotence, diverticulitis, Crohn's disease, celiac disease, ovarian tumors, elevated liver enzymes, acid reflux, heart palpitations, arrhythmias, asthma, back problems, thyroid conditions, throat cancer, neck pain, chronic migraines, and brain tumors.

Dispenza also reports using gas discharge visualization (GDV) technology before and after the meditation to measure improvements in energy-center balance and the energy field surrounding participants' whole bodies. He notes the GDV device is "not approved by the scientific community in the United States."

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 4 is the first full-length meditation chapter in the book. This practice is described in the Introduction as the first element of Anna Willems' daily routine, is central to Chapter 1's case study, and is repeatedly referenced in all subsequent chapters.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza's framing of measurable "energy centers" with distinct glands, mini-brains, and coherent electromagnetic fields is not standard medical science. The healing reports are self-reported by community members and presented without controlled study design or independent clinical verification. The GDV device he uses for measurement is acknowledged as not accepted by US scientific authorities. The eight-center model is borrowed from and consistent with ancient Hindu Vedic chakra teachings, which Dispenza acknowledges explicitly.
