## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that reality comprises two interpenetrating domains. **Space-time** is the familiar three-dimensional, Newtonian world of matter, senses, separation, duality, and linear time. **Time-space** is an inverse, five-dimensional quantum multiverse where time (not space) is infinite; there is no linear past or future — only an eternal present moment containing infinite possible dimensions, realities, and "spaces." Moving from space-time to time-space requires that practitioners become "no body, no one, no thing, no where, in no time" — dissolving sensory identity so that consciousness merges with the unified field. Dispenza teaches that once this crossing is made, manifestation accelerates because there is no longer matter trying to influence matter; instead, consciousness directly creates reality from a state of oneness.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Dispenza defines space-time as the realm where "as we move through space we experience time" and time-space as the inverse: "as we become aware of an increase or decrease in the speed of the frequency or vibration of energy, we can go from one space to another space or from one dimension to another dimension" (Ch. 11). He states: "In the world of space-time, when we collapse space, we experience time in the material reality. When we collapse time, we experience spaces or dimensions in the immaterial reality." He invokes:
- Alain Aspect's 1980s Bell-test experiments (quantum entanglement) as proof of a nonlocal field beyond three-dimensional space-time.
- David Bohm's "implicate order" (quantum unified field) and "explicit order" (material realm of separation).
- Einstein's E=mc² and the speed of light as the threshold between matter and energy/dimension.
He also describes the atom as 99.999…% empty space and electrons as "waves of probability" to reinforce the primacy of energy over matter.

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 11 is the theoretical centerpiece of the book, synthesizing the quantum/Newtonian contrast set up in earlier chapters. It frames the "becoming no body, no one, no thing, no where, in no time" language used throughout the meditation instructions. Chapter 13 (Project Coherence) and Chapter 12 (pineal gland) both build directly on this model. The chapter includes a Space-Time, Time-Space Meditation guiding practitioners to move from elevated heart emotion through "pure consciousness" into the unified field.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza does not present opposing scientific views within these chapters. His invocation of mainstream physics concepts (Aspect's experiments, Heisenberg's uncertainty principle, Bohm, Einstein) is framed as supportive of his model, though the interpretive leap from quantum entanglement to macro-scale consciousness effects is not itself standard physics. The book does not cite peer-reviewed studies testing the specific space-time/time-space model. Dispenza himself acknowledges the framework challenges conventional understanding: "I'm going to challenge your understanding and perception of the nature of reality" (Ch. 11). Critics, including voices Dispenza references elsewhere in the book, characterize his synthesis as pseudoscience.
