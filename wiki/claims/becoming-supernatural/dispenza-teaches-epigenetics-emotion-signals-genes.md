## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that emotions are "the chemical end products of experiences in the environment" and that, via the science of epigenetics, the chronic experience of stress emotions down-regulates healthy gene expression and up-regulates genes for inflammation and disease — while deliberately sustaining elevated emotions (love, joy, gratitude, inspiration) can reverse this process, up-regulating genes for growth and repair within days, without any change in the external environment.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Chapter 2 presents this as a cornerstone model: "When you change your emotions, you can change the expression of your genes (turning some on and others off) because you are sending a new chemical signal to your DNA, which can then instruct your genes to make different proteins — up-regulating or down-regulating to make all kinds of new building blocks that can change the structure and function of your body."

He describes two categories of relevant genes: "experience-dependent genes" (activated by new learning; responsible for stem-cell differentiation) and "behavioral state-dependent genes" (activated during high stress or altered states; linked to the mind-body connection, sometimes altering expression within minutes and passing to the next generation).

**Workshop study (IgA / immune function):** Dispenza reports a study at a winter 2016 Tacoma workshop with 117 participants. Salivary immunoglobulin A (IgA) — a primary protein marker of immune function — was measured before and after the four-day event. Participants were asked to move into elevated emotional states (love, joy, inspiration, gratitude) for nine to ten minutes, three times a day. Reported result: average IgA levels rose by 49.5 percent; some participants exceeded 100 mg/dL (above the normal ceiling of 87 mg/dL). He claims this constitutes epigenetic up-regulation of the IgA gene simply through emotional-state change — without any new external experience.

Anna Willems' case (Ch. 1) is presented as the extended illustration: stress emotions over years created her cluster of diseases; replacing them with elevated emotions reversed disease-gene expression and restored health.

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 2 is the primary theoretical chapter for this claim. Chapter 4 applies it to the energy-center model, explaining how incoherent energy in each center produces disease via incoherent hormonal signaling.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza's extrapolation of epigenetics — the mainstream scientific finding that environmental signals influence gene expression — into the claim that deliberate internal emotional states can produce rapid, targeted, medically significant epigenetic changes within days is not supported by mainstream epigenetics research. The IgA study is not described as peer-reviewed or published in a scientific journal; it is presented as a workshop measurement. He notes that his critics consider the framework pseudoscience.
