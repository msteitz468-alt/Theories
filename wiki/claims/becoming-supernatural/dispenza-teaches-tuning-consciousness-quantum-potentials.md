## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that the quantum or unified field is a real, immaterial domain existing beyond space and time in which all possible futures exist simultaneously as electromagnetic frequencies. A meditator who achieves a state of "no body, no one, no thing, no where, in no time" — pure awareness beyond the physical senses — can tune in to any of these potentials by sustaining a vibrational match between their own electromagnetic field (intention + elevated emotion) and the chosen potential's frequency, thereby collapsing that wave-function possibility into a particle (a lived experience). He presents this as a reproducible, learnable skill.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Chapter 3 opens with: "The quantum (or unified) field is an invisible field of energy and information — or you could say a field of intelligence or consciousness — that exists beyond space and time. Nothing physical or material exists there... the quantum field is the state in which all possibilities exist."

Dispenza draws on the standard quantum physics finding that electrons exist as waves (probability fields) until observed, at which point the wave function collapses into a particle. He extrapolates this to human consciousness: "Just as the material electron expands back to immaterial energy in the quantum field once scientists stop observing it, when you no longer observe your pain, your routine life, and your problems they will turn back into energy — into an infinite number of possibilities, into pure potential."

He reports that once in the present moment beyond the senses, a meditator assigns a symbol (a capital letter) to the desired potential, lists specific conditions of the intention beneath it, lists the elevated emotions that will accompany its manifestation, and then sustains both intention and elevated emotion until a "vibrational match" draws the experience to them. He states practitioners "don't have to make anything happen" — the experience finds them.

Case illustration (reported): Jace Dispenza, following this process from New Zealand with "no money," within one month walked into a surf shop and encountered "the top surfboard fin designer in the world" who offered him a one-year independent contract meeting all his specified conditions. Dispenza presents this as the quantum-creation process working without any direct job application.

Dispenza reports workshop participants have used this method to lower cholesterol levels, reduce cancer markers, make tumors disappear, create new jobs, and manifest all-expenses-paid vacations. He also cites René Peoc'h's published study of baby chick intention influencing a random-event-generator robot as scientific support for the principle that conscious intention can influence matter.

## Contexts Within the Source

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza's quantum-field model is a metaphorical extension of quantum physics, not standard quantum mechanics. Mainstream physicists do not accept that individual human consciousness collapses macroscopic wave functions or that vibrational matching with a "unified field" produces specific life experiences. Dispenza himself uses the language of science alongside mysticism and acknowledges the risk of being called pseudoscientific.
