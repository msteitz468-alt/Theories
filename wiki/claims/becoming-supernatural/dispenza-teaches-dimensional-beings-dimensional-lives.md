## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that human beings are "not linear beings living linear lives, but dimensional beings living dimensional lives," and that each person already possesses — latent and waiting to be awakened — all the anatomy, chemistry, and physiology needed to become supernatural: to heal disease, access mystical states, alter gene expression, and create entirely new futures.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In the Introduction, Dispenza states: "we are not linear beings living linear lives, but dimensional beings living dimensional lives. Hopefully, reading it will help you understand that you already have all the anatomy, chemistry, and physiology you need to become supernatural sitting latent within you, waiting to be awakened and activated."

Chapter 1 illustrates this claim through the extended case of Anna Willems (Netherlands psychotherapist), whom Dispenza reports healed herself of esophageal cancer, paralysis from neuritis, multiple autoimmune conditions, and clinical depression through daily meditation practice after conventional medicine had failed. Dispenza presents her story as proof that "common people can do the uncommon." His own mystical experiences — including a claimed vision of experiencing his past and future selves simultaneously, and a recalled past-life incarnation as a Greek/Turkish philosopher tortured for teaching forbidden doctrines — are offered as personal evidence of the dimensional nature of human consciousness.

## Contexts Within the Source

The claim is the overarching thesis of the book and is revisited in almost every chapter. It is framed as the motivation for Dispenza's entire research program: EEG measurements, HRV monitoring via HeartMath Institute, gas discharge visualization of energy fields, salivary IgA studies, and case-history collection.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza opens the Introduction by acknowledging that "certain people in the world—including some in the scientific community—might call my work pseudoscience." He states he has consciously chosen to stop seeking approval from skeptics and instead address audiences who "do believe in possibility." No peer-reviewed independent replication of his specific healing claims is cited; he presents his own workshop data.
