## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that broadcast media (television commercials, news) continuously programs the
subconscious mind by pairing emotionally charged imagery with literal suggestions that bypass the
analytical mind when brain waves are in alpha or theta states — thereby producing stress, disease
susceptibility, and consumer dependence. As a corrective, he advocates a positive inversion of the
same mechanism: a kaleidoscope video to induce alpha/theta trance with eyes open, followed by a
personalised Mind Movie (images + affirmations + music) to reprogram the subconscious with unlimited
beliefs and future-oriented neural maps. When the two are combined in meditation and the student
advances to "dimensionalizing" a scene with all five senses, Dispenza claims the brain fires
identically to a real experience, the body biologically prepares for that future, and new long-term
"memories of the future" are installed.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

On commercial programming: "If you can disorient people with information (or in today's world,
disinformation), shock, or confusion, you just opened the door to programming their subconscious
mind."

On Mind Movies: "Mind Movies program our students' subconscious minds the same way that television
commercials program us, but in more positive, unlimited, and constructive ways."

On pairing music with future images: "If music is the soundtrack of your life, then just as certain
songs transport you to the past, couldn't you bring your future to life in the same way?"

On dimensionalizing: "Without ever using their external senses — they're changing their brain and
body to look like their future has already happened."

On brain scan evidence: A student dimensionalizing a Mind Movie scene showed coherent high-beta and
gamma brain waves approximately 230 standard deviations above normal, described as the equivalent
of a "full-on virtual sensory experience."

Dispenza presents the founding story of Mind Movies software (Natalie and Glen Ledwell, Australia,
2007–2008) as an anecdotal proof of concept: during the 2008 global financial crisis, they generated
approximately $700,000 in sales, eventually reaching their $1 million goal, crediting daily Mind
Movie practice.

## Contexts Within the Source

The chapter opens with a sustained critique of pharmaceutical advertising as subconscious
programming, then establishes the neurological mechanism (alpha/theta suggestibility, left vs. right
hemisphere novelty processing, long-term memory formation), then introduces the two tools and their
combined use. The chapter concludes with a meditation protocol: kaleidoscope → Mind Movie → kaleidoscope
with Mind Movie music → mind-movie dimensionalizing in meditation.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza draws an analogy between his technique and hypnosis and advertising without distinguishing
whether the positive programming is any more durable or effective than the negative kind he
criticises. The business success story (Ledwell) is presented without controls; the contemporaneous
2008 financial crisis and favourable exchange rate are acknowledged as contributing factors. Brain
scan data are from single individuals in workshop settings, not controlled studies.
