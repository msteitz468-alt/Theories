## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that when a group of people achieve sufficient size and heart-centered coherence in shared meditation, the collective electromagnetic field they generate nonlocally influences the Earth's magnetic field and the autonomic nervous systems of others — reducing violence, crime, terrorism, and warfare in the surrounding society. He cites a body of peace-gathering research claiming an average greater-than-70% reduction in such metrics, and presents his own Project Coherence global meditations as an extension of this principle to a planetary scale. The mechanism he proposes is "constructive interference": coherent electromagnetic waves from individual meditators merge into a larger, stronger collective field that entrains others toward coherence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Dispenza cites (Ch. 13): "more than 50 demonstration projects and 23 peer-reviewed scientific studies scrutinized by independent scholars around the world" with results "consistently demonstrating a positive effect in the immediate reduction of crime, warfare, and terrorism by an average of greater than 70 percent." Specific studies cited:
- **Lebanon 1983**: Meditators in Jerusalem; 76% reduction in war deaths on high-participation days; replicated in seven consecutive experiments over two years.
- **RAND Corporation 1983–1985**: ~8,000 meditators; 72% reduction in worldwide terrorism over three 8–11-day periods.
- **India 1987–1990**: 7,000 meditators; Cold War ended, Berlin Wall fell, Iran-Iraq war ended, South African apartheid began to collapse.
- **Washington D.C. 1993**: ~2,500 meditators (June 7–July 30); statistically significant reduction in violent crime per FBI Uniform Crime Reports.
- **Princeton Global Consciousness Project / 9-11**: random event generators worldwide showed anomalous correlation with global emotional response after 9/11.

He also invokes the HeartMath Institute's work (heart-to-heart entrainment study; Global Coherence Initiative and Global Coherence Monitoring System) and the Schumann resonance (Earth's natural 7.83 Hz electromagnetic frequency) as the carrier medium for collective consciousness effects. His own Project Coherence experiments at workshops showed 17 HRV-monitored participants going into heart coherence simultaneously during group meditations of 550–1,500 people, measured across multiple events.

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 13 also covers Alexander Chizhevsky's historical claim (compiled 1749–1926 across 72 countries) that 80% of significant political/social events correlate with solar activity, and the HeartMath GCI's theory that human heart rhythm overlaps with Earth's resonance field. The Afterword extends the moral argument: unless meditators embody and demonstrate peace beyond their meditation sessions, effects revert to baseline.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza presents the peace-gathering studies uncritically and does not discuss methodological criticisms of TM-affiliated research (e.g., selection bias in choosing outcome metrics, multiple comparisons, absence of pre-registered protocols, funding from advocacy groups). The causal attribution of geopolitical events (Berlin Wall, end of Cold War, end of Iran-Iraq war) to 7,000 meditators in India is not addressed as an alternative-explanation problem. The Afterword itself notes a key limitation: "in many of the peace-gathering projects and studies mentioned in Chapter 13, when the experiments concluded, very often the reduced violence and crime returned to their former baseline levels," which Dispenza interprets as calling for sustained embodied peace rather than as a challenge to the causal claim.
