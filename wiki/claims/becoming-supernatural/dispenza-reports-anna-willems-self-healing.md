## Claim Statement

Dispenza reports that Anna Willems, a Dutch psychotherapist, reversed esophageal cancer, neuritis-induced paraplegia, multiple autoimmune conditions (erosive lichen planus, immune collapse, skin conditions), and clinical depression through two-plus years of daily meditation practice — specifically the techniques Dispenza teaches — without completing the conventional medical treatment her doctors had prescribed.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Chapter 1 recounts the account in detail. Anna's husband committed suicide in June 2007; she subsequently entered chronic survival-mode stress which Dispenza says progressively caused: neuritis (paralysis from the waist down, March 2008), erosive lichen planus (ulcerations throughout mucous membranes), immune collapse, clinical depression (2009), and esophageal cancer (January 2011). She began Dispenza's meditations in February 2011 after attending his weekend workshop.

Dispenza reports:
- After roughly two years of twice-daily meditation (Blessing of the Energy Centers, breathing techniques, walking meditation, heart-coherence work), Anna's doctors reported in September 2013 that her cancer had completely resolved and the tumor had vanished, with no cancer markers detectable in blood tests.
- All mucous membrane ulcerations healed; the only remaining issues were mild mouth membrane redness and absent saliva production from earlier medication.
- After attending another workshop in 2014 focused on epigenetics, Anna reports that saliva production also returned to normal.
- By 2016, Anna had founded a psychiatric institution employing more than 20 therapists and was working as one of Dispenza's corporate trainers.

Dispenza frames this case as demonstrating that "emotions of stress" activate disease genes while "elevated emotions" (love, joy, gratitude, compassion) signal new genes for growth and repair, drawing on the science of epigenetics.

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the primary case study of Chapter 1 and is referenced throughout the book as the paradigm example. Dispenza states it was "intentionally chosen" to show that "no matter how bad things can get, you have the power to change them."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza provides no independent medical verification of the cancer diagnosis or resolution beyond his account of Anna's report and the September 2013 checkup. He acknowledges she stopped all conventional medical treatments without telling her doctors, noting that "no one ever called her to ask why" — which he does not comment on critically. Anna's account is presented as self-reported and relayed through Dispenza; no peer-reviewed documentation is cited.
