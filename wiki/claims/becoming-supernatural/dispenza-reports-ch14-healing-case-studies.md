## Claim Statement

Dispenza reports four first-person accounts of workshop participants who claim dramatic, unexpected outcomes following surrender to the meditation practices — outcomes including recovery of hearing from a diagnosed acoustic-nerve tumor, resolution of severe depression, an experience of becoming the universe during a Project Coherence meditation, and survival of near-fatal multi-organ failure through focused intention. He frames all these as examples of "something greater" coming through when the personal ego is surrendered to the unified field.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Stacy (Tampa advanced workshop, winter 2017):** A registered nurse and acupuncturist with a meningioma on her eighth cranial nerve (scheduled for craniotomy), who had lost ~70% of hearing in her left ear. During the pineal gland meditation, she reports "an amazing flash of light" consuming the left side of her head, followed by a "bluish-white light" entering her ear. Her sinuses, left head, and shoulder subsequently cleared, and she reports recovering 100% hearing. Dispenza reports this as a self-described healing.

**Janet (Carefree, Arizona advanced workshop, spring 2015):** A woman in severe depression who had been seeking to re-create a spontaneous light experience from 25 years earlier. During the pineal gland meditation she reports her pineal gland being "illuminated by brilliant white light," an inverted triangle of light descending through the top of her head, and receiving the message "You are mine." She reports that by the time she left the workshop, her depression and exhaustion were "completely gone."

**Sasha and Heather (Project Coherence global meditation, Lake Garda, Italy broadcast):** During a Project Coherence meditation, Sasha in New Jersey reports her heart center generating "shoots and leaves growing... branches, leaves, and blossoms." Simultaneously, her friend Heather in Ireland — not participating in the meditation — photographed heart-shaped moss on a rock and texted it to Sasha, feeling "the overwhelming feeling of your presence." Dispenza presents this as evidence of nonlocal consciousness connection.

**Donna (multiple workshops, 2014–2016):** A technical writer who, across multiple workshops, reports experiences of interdimensional beings — specifically the souls of people killed by her fiancé (a former Army Ranger) during the Iraq War — and facilitating their "crossing over" to golden/blue luminous beings. She also reports becoming "the universe" during a Cancun workshop.

**Jerry (self-managed healing, 2015):** A medical technician hospitalized with near-fatal pancreatitis — pancreatic enzyme levels 20–50× normal, 80% breathing capacity lost, ventilator required, doctors uncertain of outcome — who used meditation and thought management to maintain a positive state. Two days before scheduled surgery, scans showed the obstructing mass had disappeared. His doctors confirmed no surgery was needed. Jerry attributed this to "constantly raising his energy, moving into a feeling of health, and changing the thoughts and belief that he was sick."

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter 14 is the book's final case-study chapter, positioned after the theoretical and practice chapters to illustrate outcomes. Dispenza's introductory framing notes that "none of the people you will read about here tried to make anything happen. They simply had an intention and at the same time surrendered the outcome to something greater." The pineal gland meditation (Ch. 12) features prominently in Stacy's and Janet's accounts. Project Coherence (Ch. 13) underlies Sasha's case.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

None of these cases include independent medical verification presented in the text. Stacy's acoustic-nerve meningioma resolution, if accurate, would be a medically remarkable event; meningiomas can occasionally stabilize or very slowly involute, but rapid hearing restoration is not documented in the mainstream literature as a result of meditation. Jerry's case (resolution of pancreatic mass) is striking but reported entirely through his own account; Dispenza does not include physician statements. Janet's depression resolution following an intense retreat experience, while plausible via known mechanisms (group support, catharsis, expectancy effects), is attributed entirely to the pineal gland metabolite cascade. Donna's interdimensional encounters are presented without evaluation of alternative explanations. Dispenza does not acknowledge that intense multi-day retreats can produce dissociative experiences with dramatic subjective reality.
