## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that seated meditation alone is insufficient because practitioners typically return
to unconscious habitual programs immediately upon opening their eyes. The walking meditation bridges
the internal altered state into ordinary waking activity: the practitioner stands with eyes closed to
cultivate heart coherence and elevated emotions, then opens their eyes and walks in trance — gaze
fixed on the horizon, not focusing on any person or object — embodying their future self through
deliberate changes in stride, posture, breathing, and demeanour. By broadcasting a new
electromagnetic signature while walking, the practitioner is claimed to vibrate at the frequency of
a future reality already present in the quantum field, drawing that reality toward them through
resonance.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"If you can't maintain that energy and awareness throughout the course of your day, you're likely to
fall right back into the unconscious programs that have been running your life for years."

On the mechanism: "The moment your energy changes because you're feeling lower, survival emotions,
there's dissonance and incoherence between you and your future reality... you'll just wind up
creating more of the same reality because your energy is vibrating equal to the reality you're
reacting to."

On embodying the future self: "You might imagine that it's one or two years later and you already
have all the things you want... you already embody the qualities of your future. You are simply
thinking, acting, and feeling your future self."

On future memories: "The more you embody your elevated emotions correctly, the more your brain and
body will look like the future experience has already happened. That means you're technically
remembering your future."

Brain scan evidence: Dispenza presents a scan (Graphic 12) showing a student who "changed his brain
to a high-energy gamma state" after approximately 80 minutes of walking meditation, compared to a
baseline of normal beta/alpha.

## Contexts Within the Source

The chapter situates walking meditation as the third of four postures (sitting, standing/walking,
lying down), specifically designed to extend the altered state created in seated practice into daily
life. The formal protocol involves two cycles of: eyes-closed heart coherence (standing, 2–5 min) →
eyes-open walking in trance (10 min) → recalibration (eyes closed, 5–10 min) → second walking
period (10 min) — finishing with gratitude affirmations and acknowledgement of "the divine within."
Dispenza recommends beginning in nature (minimal distractions) and progressing to public settings.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza acknowledges the practice takes time to master and initially requires a quiet environment.
The quantum-field resonance and "tuning fork" metaphor for attracting future events is Dispenza's
explanatory framework, not a claim with experimental support cited in the chapter.
