## Claim Statement

Dispenza teaches that the body becomes trapped in the past because emotions are "a record of past
experiences" and the body-mind cannot distinguish a thought-created emotion from a real external
experience. A specific breath technique — contracting perineal and abdominal muscles while drawing a
slow breath — mobilises cerebrospinal fluid up the spine, creating an inductance field that liberates
energy stored in the lower three energy centres and delivers it to the brain, producing gamma brain
waves. A subsequent "reconditioning" meditation then cultivates elevated emotions to signal new gene
expression, chemically preparing the body for a future the practitioner has not yet experienced.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"The body believes it is living in the same past experience 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days
a year. The body believes it's in the same past experience because to the body, the emotion is
literally the experience."

"By the time you are in your mid-30s... 95 percent of who we are as adults is so habituated through
repetition that the body has been programmed to be the mind."

On the breath: "As you accelerate charged molecules in one direction up the spine, you produce an
inductance field... [that] will draw the stored energy in the first three centers back to the brain."

On elevated emotions: "When you embrace these elevated emotions in this meditation, what you are
actually doing is signalling your genes ahead of the environment. The body doesn't know the
difference between an emotion created by an experience... and an experience you are creating
internally."

On gene expression: Dispenza cites a 2017 Tampa workshop study of 30 participants in which he claims
expression of 8 genes changed significantly over 4 days, including genes involved in neurogenesis,
anti-aging, stem-cell mobilisation, cancer suppression, and free-radical elimination.

## Contexts Within the Source

The chapter describes the mechanism in three stages: (1) the thinking-feeling loop that keeps the
body conditioned to the past; (2) the anatomy of the breath — sacral movement, cerebrospinal fluid,
intrathecal pressure, the inductance field, and the thalamic gate; (3) the reconditioning meditation
using elevated emotions to signal new gene expression. The chapter also mentions the "prana tube" and
the concept of kundalini energy (which Dispenza avoids naming as such).

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Dispenza does not claim this technique is easy: "Moving this stored energy — energy that has been
stored for years and years, maybe even decades — takes an act of intention and will." He presents
the gene-expression finding as preliminary workshop data with a 1-in-20 chance of being due to
chance, not a controlled clinical trial.
