## Claim Statement

Brand teaches that using Enochian magic for worldly success is not a debasement of a sacred system
but a genuinely spiritual act — that "personal success, personal peace, is one of the greatest ways
to change the world," and that living "with power, success, and control is more spiritual than many
so-called spiritual practices."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Against the objection that "this magick is for opening the gates of heaven… not for lowly desires
such as success," Brand argues "living a meagre life does not give you a ticket to a spiritual
existence," and that "when each person is empowered to live their best life, true greatness becomes
possible." He defines success broadly (the contented baker, the settling-down workaholic) as
"a state of unfolding growth," and ties it to the [[The offering to humanity (Gallery of Magick ritual method)|offering to humanity]]:
"success comes when you discover your unique power and offer it to the world."

## Contexts Within the Source

A defining stance of [[Success Magick (Damon Brand's Enochian system)]], distinguishing it from
purely contemplative magic. Brand insists it is "not about deceiving yourself" or positive thinking
but "manipulating reality."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Brand repeatedly warns against burnout and empty ambition, and concedes some practitioners hold the
opposing view that Enochian magic should be used "only for spiritual work" — a position he
"respectfully disagree[s]" with.
