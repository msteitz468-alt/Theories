## Claim Statement

Brand recounts that Enochian magic originated when angels revealed an encoded magical system to
Dr. John Dee — cartographer, spy, and advisor to Queen Elizabeth I — working with his scryer Edward
Kelley, in experiments beginning 1582 and peaking in 1584.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Per the source: "Dee called to the angels, and Kelley saw visions and heard the angels' instructions,
which in turn Dee recorded by hand." The revelations included "the angelical language itself, an
angelical alphabet, a series of Calls (or Keys), and the names and duties of many celestial entities,"
often given "one letter at a time" and decoded via grids and patterns. Brand says the system "was
nearly taken by the flames of the Great Fire of London" but survived and was "slowly recovered."

## Contexts Within the Source

The historical basis for [[Enochian Magick (Gallery of Magick approach)]] and the
[[The Thirty Aethyrs (Enochian)|Aethyrs]]. Brand candidly notes the record is messier than the
pious legend — leading to [[Which Enochian Watchtower Table is authentic — the original or Kelley's reformed Table]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Brand stresses Dee and Kelley were "contradictory… deceptive," sometimes ignored or bickered with the
angels, and may have introduced errors or deception — yet argues the magic works in practice
regardless of these doubts.
