## Claim Statement

Brand teaches that the rituals are effective regardless of the practitioner's subjective experience —
that "the actions of performing the ritual are all that's required," and results do not depend on
feeling any energy, emotion, or supernatural sensation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"You may perform the whole ritual without feeling anything, and that is fine… don't assume that you
need to feel anything, or that the energies you send out need to be strong. If it just feels like
you're pretending or faking it, that is fine… By doing the magick, you are being the magick." He adds
that some people sense strong presences or visions while others "will never feel anything unusual" —
and that such effects "do not affect or indicate the nature of the results either way."

## Contexts Within the Source

A reassurance central to the book's "anyone can do this" democratizing pitch (see
[[Brand teaches Enochian magic can be simplified and democratized for ordinary people]]) and to the
[[Brand teaches a standardized ritual method of sigil-gazing, divine names, and an offering to humanity|ritual method]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Brand still asks for "some degree of attention" and focus, and a "leap of faith" that the magic
occurs in the brief moments of the ritual — i.e. effortlessness is not the same as carelessness.
