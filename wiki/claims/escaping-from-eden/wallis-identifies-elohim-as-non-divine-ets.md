## Claim Statement

Wallis's resolving position: he does **not** abandon Christian faith but "disentangles God from the activity of others." He concludes the "Powerful Ones" were a non-divine, flesh-and-blood (or "trans-dimensional") species — extraterrestrials who "trespassed," "plundered its resources," "genetically modified us and exploited us as we do with livestock," and ruled prehistoric humans as "heavenly kings" or "false gods" — and that the true God is the one revealed in Jesus, distinct from these elohim.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Wallis reads Jesus's sayings as pointing to this distinction: "All who came before me were thieves and robbers… The thief comes only to plunder, and kill, and destroy" (John 10:8–10); "I have other sheep that are not of this fold." He frames humanity as carrying "neural slavery settings" / "slave programming" (the popular "90 percent of our brains switched off" trope) left by our engineers, and is optimistic about reconnecting with a "galactic family" and its technology ("zero-point energy"). His title question — "created by God or engineered by ETs?" — is answered as *engineered by ETs, under a God who is not those ETs.*

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Wallis foregrounds his own uncertainty and lack of formal credentials, presents the reading as a "preacher's journey" he "could not not-publish" rather than proven fact, and concedes the orthodox grammatical explanations (collective noun, royal "we," created angels) before rejecting them as insufficient.
