## Claim Statement

Wallis argues that the physical record of human bones — the **Paracas "long skulls"** of Peru and persistent ancient reports of **giants** — shows that human prehistory was "rather more diverse and a lot more complicated than the single line of gradual evolution" taught in schools, and that the giants and "little people" of legend may be "more empirical than we gave them credit for."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

On the long skulls (Chapter 5): the Paracas skulls, first found by Julio Tello in 1928, are larger-volumed (with "25 percent larger brains") and have features (heavier weight, a single parietal plate) that Wallis says indicate "the unusual shape was in fact entirely natural… just another type of human being," not the result of artificial cranial deformation. He notes that DNA testing nonetheless "confirms our Paracas cousins as entirely human, a mix of Indigenous South American, Western Asia and Eastern Europe." He observes that other peoples worldwide bound their babies' heads to *imitate* this shape, and that the Spanish Conquistadors "were careful to wipe out the long skulled people as they were recognized as royalty," paralleling the elongated headgear of the Amarna-era pharaohs — asking "who have we all been trying to look like?"

On the giants: he cites **Pliny** (a 9'9" giant brought to Rome from Arabia) and **Flavius Josephus**, who "makes references to the giants of the Biblical narratives — the Nephilim, Emites, Gittites, Anakites and Rephaites" (Goliath at 9'9", Og's 13.5-foot bed) and a six-fingered, six-toed giant of Gath (1 Chronicles 20:5–8). Wallis stresses that "Josephus regarded these mythological references as memories of history rather than as fiction," pointing to skeletal remains "on display" in his own day, and traced the giants to the Genesis 6 offspring of the Powerful Ones and human females. He adds six-fingered skeletons from Noble County, Ohio, and lists a widened roster of hominins (Neanderthal, Denisovan, *floresiensis*/"Hobbit", and his coined "Homo Paracas"/"Homo Nobilis").

## Contexts Within the Source

The material supports Wallis's argument that "human beings come in all kinds of shapes and sizes, and that in times past the diversity was even greater," consistent with the *benei elohim* / Nephilim hybridization and with engineered, non-single-line origins; it feeds directly into his Genesis-as-recovery reading.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Wallis himself reports the countervailing genetic data: DNA testing "confirms our Paracas cousins as entirely human," undercutting the suggestion of "non-human DNA in the long-skulled people"; he presents this candidly while still reading the skulls as evidence of unusual human diversity.
