## Claim Statement

Wallis argues that **Acquired Savant Syndrome** — where brain injury or degeneration suddenly "switches on" extraordinary abilities — is evidence that the normal human brain contains **"inhibitors"** suppressing latent, far greater potential. He links this neuroscience to the mythological motif that the engineers deliberately "downgraded" human capacity, and reads Jesus's promise that believers will do "greater things" as an invitation to switch the inhibitors off.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Wallis gives several cases (Chapter 13): **Orlando Serrell**, who after a 1980 baseball blow to the head gained perfect calendar-calculation ability; **Ben McMahon**, who emerged from a 2012 coma speaking fluent Mandarin he had never mastered. He cites the clinical literature: **Dr. Bruce Miller** (UC, 1996–2000) cataloguing twelve cases where artistic/musical skills were released by frontotemporal dementia through "decreased inhibition of visual systems"; **Prof. Mark Lythgoe** (UCL, 2005) showing degeneration "may release untapped cognitive abilities"; and art judges describing one patient's rising skill as a "disinhibition" of the brain.

The argument turns on the recurring word: "Apparently, entirely by accident, an inhibitor was being switched off… 'What in the world is an inhibitor doing in our brains?'" Wallis answers with the mythologies: "Genesis, the Mesopotamian tablets and Popol Vuh all propose the same answer. It was put there." He quotes **Dr. Darold Treffert** (2018): "Is it possible that such dormant potential resides in all of us?… how to tap those hidden abilities without having endured some CNS catastrophe." He closes by reading Jesus as "what a human life might look like with the slave programming and all the inhibitors switched off."

## Contexts Within the Source

The chapter applies Wallis's thesis that the Powerful Ones deliberately limited human perception and capacity ("the deliberate limiting of human sight" he draws from the *Popol Vuh*), turning the engineering narrative into a hopeful program of "disinhibiting" human capacities — tying the ancient-astronaut reading back to his Christian frame.
