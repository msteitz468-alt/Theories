## Claim Statement

Wallis argues that a comet impact ~12,900 years ago — the **Clovis Comet**, which struck the Laurentide ice sheet and triggered the **Younger Dryas** cold period and a mass extinction — devastated an earlier human world. Read against this cataclysm, Genesis 1's dark, water-covered, formless earth (*tohu wa bohu*) is best understood not as an account of original creation but as **a story of planetary recovery**, in which the Powerful Ones rehabilitate a wrecked Earth.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Wallis introduces the event in Chapter 5 ("the Younger Dryas / Clovis Comet extinction-level event") and develops it in Chapter 6. He describes the impact "on the Laurentide Ice Sheet above the Great Lakes," the meltwater shutting down "the warming Atlantic current," and the world plunging "almost overnight, into an ice age even more severe than before… the Younger Dryas cold period," with "flash-freezing that, famously, could freeze a mammoth mid-mouthful." He cites the supporting research: **Richard Firestone** (2007, Lawrence Berkeley) finding "micro-sized balls of metals (spherules) and nano-sized diamonds" in a 12,900-year sediment layer at a dozen sites; **Albert Goodyear** (2017) confirming the findings; and **James Kennett** among "a growing body of academics" who support the comet trigger, with 75–80% of North America's megafauna (mammoth, mastodon, woolly rhinoceros, sabre-tooth) driven extinct.

He then reads Genesis as recovery: "what we have in the pages of Genesis 1 may be not a creation account at all, but a story of recovery." The "pre-existing dark, water-covered earth" is "planet Earth following the impact of the Clovis Comet… just as Earth was emerging from an ice age," paralleling the Maya *Popol Vuh*'s engineers "hovering over the waters" and the sudden localized crop domestication near **Karaca Dağ** as "a helping hand." He links submerged/buried sites (Göbekli Tepe, Dwarka, Yonaguni, Malta) to "previous technological civilizations arrested by some natural disaster."

## Contexts Within the Source

The cataclysm anchors Wallis's broader thesis that the Powerful Ones repeatedly intervened to rehabilitate Earth and re-establish humanity ("Could it even be that such interventions may have occurred more than once?"). He frames it as why the Genesis, Mesopotamian and *Popol Vuh* narratives all "tell a story of ongoing involvement from the Powerful Ones / Sky People / Engineers."
