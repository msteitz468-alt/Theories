## Claim Statement

Wallis reads Genesis 6:1–4 literally: members of the Powerful Ones (*benei elohim*) "abducted human females and had children with them," whose offspring were the **Nephilim/giants** — "men and women of legend." He argues the Council, alarmed by this hybridization and by humans "living too long" and becoming "too capable," decided on a "final solution": a global **Flood** to wipe out the human menace, with the dissenting faction warning Noah to preserve a breeding stock and "re-boot the human population." Later the Council destroys the Tower of Babel — "a Gateway for the Powerful Ones" — and confuses human language to arrest civilization.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Wallis notes the *benei elohim* are explicitly "not God and not human," and rejects the "land-barons/angels" readings (neither would notice human women as a "novelty" or produce giants). He stresses the demigod/hybrid motif recurs worldwide — Greek (Achilles, Heracles, Perseus…), Hindu, Norse, Celtic, and Mesopotamian lore — and that flood-and-repopulation myths appear across China, Peru, Korea, Polynesia, Aboriginal Australia, and First Nations traditions, implying a shared memory.
