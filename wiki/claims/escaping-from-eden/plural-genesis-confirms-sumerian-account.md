## Claim Statement

Wallis argues that in its received, monotheized form Genesis reads as an *antithesis* to the Sumerian/Babylonian/Akkadian/Assyrian creation and flood narratives — "never mind those old stories, here's what really happened." But once *elohim* is re-read as a plural, "Genesis does an incredible volte-face": instead of critiquing the Mesopotamian account, **"Plural Genesis" confirms it, story after story.** The same engineered-humanity, hybrid-giants, and flood narrative is "etched indelibly in cuneiform" on Sumerian tablets.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Wallis notes Abraham and Sarah came from "Ur of the Chaldees" with a wholly Sumerian education, so Mesopotamian source-material in the Bible is expected. He recounts (Ch. 3) the 19th-century decipherment of cuneiform — Henry Rawlinson and the boy "Eylo" copying the Behistun inscription — as how the older Mesopotamian texts were recovered, and cites the sudden, fully-formed appearance of civilization (writing, mathematics, law, money, banking, plumbing) in Mesopotamia as a further clue.
