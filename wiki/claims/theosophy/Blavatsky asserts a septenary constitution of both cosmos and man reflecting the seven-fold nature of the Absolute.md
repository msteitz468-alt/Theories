## Claim Statement

Blavatsky teaches that the number **seven** is the fundamental law of structure throughout cosmos and man. This septenary principle pervades every level of the Stanzas:

- **Seven Eternities** of Pralaya before re-awakening (Stanza I)
- **Seven Cosmical Elements** — four entirely physical, the fifth (Ether) semi-material, and two "absolutely beyond the range of human perception" — which are conditional modifications of the ONE Element (Proem)
- **Seven Stanzas** describing the seven great stages of cosmic evolution
- **Seven Sublime Lords** (Dhyan-Chohans) who watch over the Rounds and Root-Races (Stanza I Commentary)
- **Seven Sons / Builders** who form the planetary chain (Stanza II)
- **Seven Laya Centres** on which the universe is seated (Stanza VI)
- **Seven Worlds of Maya** through which the Spark journeys from mineral to Man (Stanza VII)
- **Seven Principles** in Nature (Atma-Buddhi-Manas and four lower) corresponding to seven principles in Man
- **Seven Rounds** and **Seven Root-Races** structuring human evolution on Earth

Space is itself called "the Seven-Skinned Eternal Mother-Father," composed from its undifferentiated to its differentiated surface of seven layers. The Stanzas' symbolic language — "from one light seven lights; from each of the seven, seven times seven lights" (Stanza V) — reiterates the fractal self-similarity of the septenary scheme at every scale.

Blavatsky draws on Pythagorean, Kabbalistic ("The number seven is the great number of the Divine Mysteries"), Hindu (the seven Manus, seven Rishis), and Buddhist sources to argue that seven is not an arbitrary number but the authentic key to nature's architecture, an insight preserved in all genuine esoteric traditions.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "Occult Science recognizes Seven Cosmical Elements — four entirely physical, and the fifth (Ether) semi-material… The remaining two are as yet absolutely beyond the range of human perception. These seven elements with their numberless Sub-Elements are simply conditional modifications and aspects of the ONE and only Element."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "Space is called in the esoteric symbolism 'the Seven-Skinned Eternal Mother-Father.' It is composed from its undifferentiated to its differentiated surface of seven layers."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "FROM ONE LIGHT SEVEN LIGHTS; FROM EACH OF THE SEVEN, SEVEN TIMES SEVEN LIGHTS."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Stanza V, verse 6

> "'The number seven,' says the Kabala, 'is the great number of the Divine Mysteries'; number ten is that of all human knowledge (Pythagorean decade)."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Stanza I Commentary

## Cross-source Connections

- The Kybalion similarly structures its seven Hermetic Principles and teaches that "the ALL is SEVEN," connecting to the same Pythagorean-Hermetic root that Blavatsky claims: see [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]].
- Manly P. Hall's *Secret Teachings of All Ages* documents the septenary in Pythagorean, Egyptian, and Kabbalistic traditions as a universal key, corroborating Blavatsky's claim that the number is cross-cultural: see [[The Secret Teachings of All Ages (Hall)]].
- The seven-fold structure attributed to [[Hermes Trismegistus]] in the Hermetic corpus parallels Blavatsky's claim that seven is the number of divine mysteries.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky concedes that "the key to this cannot be given, for herein lies the mystery of esoteric calculations." The septenary scheme is asserted rather than demonstrated through accessible argument; she acknowledges the two highest cosmic elements remain entirely unknown to humanity in this cycle.
