# Blavatsky asserts that apes are degenerate offshoots of early humanity and that Darwinian natural selection cannot account for the origin of man

## Claim Statement

Blavatsky asserts two interlocked theses: (1) The anthropoid apes (gorilla, chimpanzee, orang-utan) are not ancestral to humanity; rather, they are degraded descendants of unions between semi-mindless Third Root-Race men and now-extinct mammalian species, a process later repeated by the Atlantean Fourth Race. (2) Darwinian natural selection is at best a *partial* and *secondary* truth applicable to the middle stages of physical evolution; it is not a creative principle, cannot explain the origin of variations or of language, and systematically ignores the "sub-conscious intelligence pervading matter" — the spiritual/occult direction of evolution — that alone accounts for the organized complexity of living beings, especially man.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**The inversion of descent.** In §§ I and II Blavatsky quotes de Quatrefages' analysis (*The Human Species*) to the effect that man and ape cannot share a common ancestor without violating Darwin's own "law of permanent characterization": a walking animal cannot descend from a climbing one. She adds Gratiolet's finding that in apes the brain *decreases relative to the jaw apparatus as the animal ages*, whereas in man the reverse is true — the frontal lobes develop later and expand, the facial bones diminish. A gorilla, Blavatsky notes wryly, would have a perfectly good claim to address the Evolutionist: "We, anthropoid apes, form a retrogressive departure from the human type." Blavatsky explicitly endorses this reading: the pithecoid species "do descend from the animalized Fourth human Root-Race, being the product of man and an extinct species of mammal."

**The Atlantean sin.** The specific mechanism Blavatsky asserts is the "bestiality of the primeval mindless races" of the Third Race, whose unions with animals produced "huge man-like monsters." As time passed, the descendants dwindled; later, Atlantean Fourth Race members repeated the transgression "with full responsibility," producing specifically the modern anthropoids. This account is presented as the esoteric explanation of the anatomical resemblance between man and ape — not common ancestry but common origin-type, since "all forms which now people the earth are so many variations on basic types originally thrown off by the Man of the Third and Fourth Round."

**Natural selection as non-creative.** In § I Blavatsky states: "Natural Selection is no Entity; but a convenient phrase for describing the mode in which the survival of the fit and the elimination of the unfit among organisms is brought about in the struggle for existence.... Of itself, 'it' can produce nothing, and only operates on the rough material presented to 'it'." The real question — what *causes* the variations themselves — is unanswered by Darwinism. Blavatsky asserts that beyond secondary physical causes (climate, diet, isolation), "a deeper principle has to be sought for": a "sub-conscious intelligence pervading matter, ultimately traceable to a REFLECTION of the Divine and Dhyan-Chohanic wisdom." She aligns this with von Hartmann's "Unconscious" but insists that the actual mechanism is Fohat acting as the vehicle of Dhyan-Chohanic energy.

**Language as a decisive counter-argument.** Blavatsky extensively cites Max Müller's argument that the existence of linguistic roots is an insuperable objection to the ape-ancestor theory. Ape brains — even the gorilla's at 30.51 cubic inches — do not approach the average human brain (99.35 cubic inches even in the "lowest races," per Vogt). The gap cannot be bridged by natural selection operating on gradual variations, since "a human brain is necessary for human speech" and no intermediate speech-capable form has ever been found.

**The "missing link" problem.** Blavatsky observes that not one specimen of an intermediate form between ape and man has been discovered in any stratum — gravel, drift-clay, fresh-water beds, or tertiaries — despite the Darwinian requirement for vast numbers of such transitional forms. She quotes Dr. C. R. Bree: the Neanderthal skull itself "presents... immense differences from the highest known anthropomorphous ape." She turns the absence of evidence against the Darwinians: if the missing links went down with submerged continents, they should still appear in contemporary strata that did not subside — but they do not.

**Darwinism meets evolution "at its midway point."** Blavatsky's synthetic position is: "Darwinism only meets Evolution at its midway point — that is to say when astral evolution has given place to the play of the ordinary physical forces with which our present senses acquaint us. But even here the Darwinian Theory... is inadequate to meet the facts of the case." She accepts gradual change and rejects saltation, but insists that the directing agency is spiritual, not mechanical.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky does not claim that occult science *disproves* Darwinism by materialist methods; she claims it offers an equally or more coherent hypothesis. She concedes that "the partial truth of many of the secondary 'laws' of Darwinism is beyond question," including natural selection as a filtering mechanism, transformation within species, and the slow pace of change. Her quarrel is with using Darwinian evolution as the complete account of man's origin and nature — particularly the claim that "moral consciousness, religious aspirations, genius, and a feeling of one's own immortal nature" could be produced by "physico-chemical forces guided by blind Almighty Evolution."

Regarding the racial hierarchy embedded in the Root-Race scheme (which this passage draws on): Blavatsky's text includes descriptors comparing brain sizes of living racial groups in terms that reflect the scientific racism of the 1880s.
