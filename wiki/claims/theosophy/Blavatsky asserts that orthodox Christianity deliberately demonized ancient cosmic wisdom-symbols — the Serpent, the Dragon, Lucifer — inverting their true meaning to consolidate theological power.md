## Claim Statement

Blavatsky asserts that Christian theology systematically appropriated the cosmic symbols, deities, and philosophical figures of older pagan and esoteric traditions and then deliberately re-labeled them as Satanic or demonic, in order to (a) claim exclusive divine authority, (b) suppress the esoteric wisdom those symbols encoded, and (c) create a manufactured duality between a personal God and a personal Devil upon which the entire edifice of Christian soteriology (Fall, Atonement, Redemption) depends. This inversion affected: the Serpent (universal symbol of wisdom and cyclic eternity → biblical tempter), the Dragon (symbol of cosmic cycles and divine dynasties → Satan), Lucifer (Latin "Light-Bearer," the Morning Star, the Astral Light → the Devil), the Asuras and Titans (divine intelligences, "gods of Secret Wisdom" → fallen rebel-angels), and Prometheus (benefactor of humanity → type of Satan). Blavatsky frames this as not mere misunderstanding but a consciously maintained policy of "transformation of the divine alter ego into the grotesque Satan of their theology."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

On the general inversion:

> "Every bright sun-god of antiquity — a glorious deity by day, and its own opponent and adversary by night, named the Dragon of Wisdom, because it was supposed to contain the germs of night and day — has now been turned into the antithetical shadow of God, and has become Satan on the sole and unsupported authority of despotic human dogma."

On the Church's systematic policy:

> "Christian theology had to be protected and saved... [theologians] have built their God and his Archangels, their Satan and his Angels, along with the Logos and his staff, entirely out of the dramatis personæ of the old heathen Pantheons. They would have been welcome to these, had they not cunningly distorted the original characters, perverted the philosophical meaning, and taking advantage of the ignorance of Christendom... tossed every symbol into the most inextricable confusion."

On the manufactured Devil as theological necessity:

> "The entire Christian scheme rests upon these Jakin and Boaz — the two contrary forces of good and evil, Christ and Satan... Take away from Christianity its main prop of the Fallen Angels, and the Eden Bower vanishes with its Adam and Eve into thin air; and Christ, in the exclusive character of the One God and Saviour, and the victim of Atonement for the Sin of animal-man, becomes forthwith a useless, meaningless myth."

On the Serpent:

> "From all that has been said in this section, one sees clearly that, between the Serpent of Eden and the Devil of Christianity, there is an abyss. Alone the sledge hammer of ancient philosophy can kill this dogma."

On the Dragon:

> "The 'Dragon' is simply the symbol of the cycle and of the 'Sons of Manvantaric Eternity,' who had descended on earth during a certain epoch of its formative period... The 'third part of the stars of heaven' cast down to the earth — refers to the divine Monads... the human Egos destined to perform the whole cycle of incarnations."

On the Church's counter-argument (that pagan parallels were Satanic pre-emptions of Christianity):

> "The 'ruffians' (les drâles) are of course demons whose manager is Satan. Now this is the easiest and the most sublime and simple way of getting out of the difficulty!" [Blavatsky is here sarcastically quoting the Roman Catholic apologist de Mirville's claim that all pagan parallels to Christianity were diabolical plagiarisms by anticipation.]

On Satan as a constructed figure that could not be cursed even by God's own scripture:

> "The God of the Jews, whomsoever he was, forbids cursing Satan. Philo Judæus and Josephus both state that the Law undeviatingly forbid one to curse the adversary... Satan appeared one day to a man who used to curse him daily... 'Consider that God himself would not curse me...'" — Blavatsky uses this Talmudic passage to argue Satan was never a supreme evil in the original tradition.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

- Blavatsky's claim of deliberate ecclesiastical bad faith is polemical and cannot be demonstrated from the source text alone; she provides extensive comparative evidence but the inference of conscious conspiracy (rather than sincere but erroneous interpretation) is her own.
- She acknowledges that some Christian mystics (Eliphas Levi, Clement of Alexandria, Origen) preserved closer readings of the original esoteric meaning — the inversion is blamed primarily on the institutional/exoteric Church, not on esoteric Christianity.
- The thesis that the entire Christian soteriology collapses without the literal Fall/Satan narrative reflects Blavatsky's philosophical stance; many liberal and mystical Christian theologians dispute this framing while remaining Christian.
- This claim is frequently cited in modern occultist, Theosophist, and certain New Age circles as foundational. It is descriptively recorded here; the wiki takes no position on its accuracy.
