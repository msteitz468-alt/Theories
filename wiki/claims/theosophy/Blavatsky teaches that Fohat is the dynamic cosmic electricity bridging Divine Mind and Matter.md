## Claim Statement

Blavatsky asserts that **Fohat** is the universal active principle — "the dynamic energy of Cosmic Ideation" — that mediates between the Absolute's Divine Thought and physical Matter. It is described as the "bridge" by which the Ideas existing in the Divine Thought are impressed on Cosmic Substance as the "laws of Nature," and as "the mysterious link between Mind and Matter, the animating principle electrifying every atom into life." Fohat is the swift messenger of the Dhyan-Chohans: it runs "circular errands," collects the fiery cosmic dust, hardens the atoms, sets the worlds in motion, traces spiral lines through space, and forms the "germs of wheels" (planets) placing them at the six directions of Space with one central wheel. Blavatsky equates Fohat in various aspects with cosmic electricity, the vital force in nature, and the "steed" whose rider is Divine Thought.

The Stanzas state: "Svabhavat sends Fohat to harden the atoms. Each is a part of the web. Reflecting the 'Self-Existent Lord' like a mirror, each becomes in turn a world." In Stanza V: "Fohat is the steed and the thought is the rider. He passes like lightning through the fiery clouds; takes three, and five, and seven strides through the seven regions above, and the seven below. He lifts his voice, and calls the innumerable sparks, and joins them."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "This something, at present unknown to Western speculation, is called by the occultists Fohat. It is the 'bridge' by which the 'Ideas' existing in the 'Divine Thought' are impressed on Cosmic substance as the 'laws of Nature.' Fohat is thus the dynamic energy of Cosmic Ideation… the mysterious link between Mind and Matter, the animating principle electrifying every atom into life."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "THEN SVABHAVAT SENDS FOHAT TO HARDEN THE ATOMS. EACH IS A PART OF THE WEB. REFLECTING THE 'SELF-EXISTENT LORD' LIKE A MIRROR, EACH BECOMES IN TURN A WORLD."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Stanza III, verse 12

> "THE DZYU BECOMES FOHAT, THE SWIFT SON OF THE DIVINE SONS WHOSE SONS ARE THE LIPIKA… FOHAT IS THE STEED AND THE THOUGHT IS THE RIDER. HE PASSES LIKE LIGHTNING THROUGH THE FIERY CLOUDS."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Stanza V, verse 2

> "HOW DOES FOHAT BUILD THEM? HE COLLECTS THE FIERY DUST. HE MAKES BALLS OF FIRE, RUNS THROUGH THEM, AND ROUND THEM, INFUSING LIFE THEREINTO, THEN SETS THEM INTO MOTION."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Stanza VI, verse 4

## Cross-source Connections

- Fohat as the cosmic intelligence-bearing energy that builds worlds from primordial substance parallels the Hermetic concept of the Nous or Logos as the creative Word that orders chaos, attributed to [[Hermes Trismegistus]].
- The Kybalion's Principle of Vibration — that "nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates" — and its treatment of the All as "Infinite Living Mind" whose mental vibrations create all phenomena, parallels Fohat as the dynamic expression of Cosmic Ideation: see [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]].
- Blavatsky's Fohat is cited by later writers as the ur-concept behind what David Icke and others call universal mind-energy: see [[The Biggest Secret (Icke, 1999)]] for one such derivative treatment.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky acknowledges that Fohat is "at present unknown to Western speculation" and can only be described through analogies (electricity, lightning, cosmic breath). She is careful to note that even the highest Dhyan-Chohans do not create ex nihilo but work through Fohat according to pre-existing divine prototypes from the Absolute's "Divine Thought."
