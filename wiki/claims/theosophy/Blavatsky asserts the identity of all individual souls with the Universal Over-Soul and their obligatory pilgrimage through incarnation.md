## Claim Statement

Blavatsky teaches the third foundational doctrine: **the fundamental identity of all individual Souls with the Universal Over-Soul** (Alaya / Anima Mundi), and the **obligatory pilgrimage** of every Soul — a "spark" of the Over-Soul — through the entire Cycle of Incarnation in accordance with Cyclic and Karmic law. No purely spiritual Buddhi (divine Soul) can attain independent conscious existence before the spark has (a) passed through every elemental form of the phenomenal world in a given Manvantara and (b) acquired individuality "first by natural impulse, and then by self-induced and self-devised efforts (checked by its Karma)," ascending through all degrees of intelligence — from mineral and plant up to the holiest Dhyani-Buddha. The Secret Doctrine "admits no privileges or special gifts in man, save those won by his own Ego through personal effort and merit throughout a long series of metempsychoses and reincarnations."

This is dramatised in Stanza VII where the Spark of the Flame journeys through the seven worlds of Maya: "It stops in the first, and is a metal and a stone; it passes into the second and behold — a plant; the plant whirls through seven changes and becomes a sacred animal. From the combined attributes of these, Manu, the Thinker, is formed." The final verse of Stanza VII declares the flame to the spark: "Thou art myself, my image, and my shadow. I have clothed myself in thee."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "The fundamental identity of all Souls with the Universal Over-Soul, the latter being itself an aspect of the Unknown Root; and the obligatory pilgrimage for every Soul — a spark of the former — through the Cycle of Incarnation (or 'Necessity') in accordance with Cyclic and Karmic law."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "The pivotal doctrine of the Esoteric philosophy admits no privileges or special gifts in man, save those won by his own Ego through personal effort and merit throughout a long series of metempsychoses and reincarnations."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "THE SPARK HANGS FROM THE FLAME BY THE FINEST THREAD OF FOHAT. IT JOURNEYS THROUGH THE SEVEN WORLDS OF MAYA… MANU, THE THINKER IS FORMED. WHO FORMS HIM? THE SEVEN LIVES AND THE ONE LIFE."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Stanza VII, verse 5

## Cross-source Connections

The Alaya as "the Over-Soul of Emerson" (Blavatsky's own comparison in the Stanza I Commentary) links this doctrine to Western transcendentalism. The doctrine of souls as sparks of one divine fire is also implicit in [[Hermes Trismegistus]] ("The Kybalion" frames a similar teaching through the Principle of Correspondence — "as above, so below"): see [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky distinguishes the Theosophical understanding of soul-identity from simple pantheism: the individual Ego does not dissolve into the Absolute until the end of the Great Manvantara, and the process of ascent is genuinely earned, not granted. She also notes that the Alaya (Universal Soul) changes "with respect to the lower planes" during active manifestation, though "eternal and changeless in its inner essence" on higher planes.
