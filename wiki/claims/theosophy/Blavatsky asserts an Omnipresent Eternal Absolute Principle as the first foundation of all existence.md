## Claim Statement

Blavatsky asserts that the first and supreme axiom of the Secret Doctrine is an **Omnipresent, Eternal, Boundless, and Immutable PRINCIPLE** — which she calls "BE-NESS" (*Sat* in Sanskrit) rather than "Being" — that antecedes all manifested existence. It is beyond the range and reach of human thought: "unthinkable and unspeakable." It has no attributes and no relation to finite, conditioned Being; it is identified with Parabrahm in Vedantic language and with "Absolute Consciousness" that, paradoxically, appears as unconsciousness to finite minds. Two symbolic aspects are given: (1) **absolute abstract Space** (pure subjectivity) and (2) **the Great Breath** — absolute Abstract Motion representing Unconditioned Consciousness. From this ONE Absolute, Spirit (Cosmic Ideation) and Matter (Mulaprakriti / root-substance) emerge as two complementary facets, neither independently real. A third principle, **Fohat**, bridges them as the dynamic energy of Cosmic Ideation made manifest through the Dhyan-Chohans.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "An Omnipresent, Eternal, Boundless, and Immutable PRINCIPLE upon which all speculation is impossible, since it transcends the power of human conception and could only be dwarfed by any human expression or similitude. It is beyond the range and reach of thought — in the words of Mandukya, 'unthinkable and unspeakable.'"
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "This 'Be-ness' is symbolised in the Secret Doctrine under two aspects. On the one hand, absolute abstract Space, representing bare subjectivity… On the other, absolute Abstract Motion representing Unconditioned Consciousness… This latter aspect of the one Reality is also symbolised by the term 'The Great Breath'."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "Fohat is thus the dynamic energy of Cosmic Ideation; or, regarded from the other side, it is the intelligent medium, the guiding power of all manifestation… the mysterious link between Mind and Matter, the animating principle electrifying every atom into life."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

Blavatsky explicitly equates this Absolute with Space itself ("Space is called in the esoteric symbolism 'the Seven-Skinned Eternal Mother-Father'") and argues that pantheist and Vedantin thinkers — far from being atheists — simply refuse to attribute creation or finite qualities to an Infinite Principle, which is philosophically correct.

## Cross-source Connections

The Kybalion's doctrine of THE ALL as "Infinite Living Mind" that is "UNKNOWABLE" in its essence and yet the substrate of all things is closely parallel; both traditions deny that the Absolute is a personal, anthropomorphic creator: see [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]]. The Hermetic tradition summarised under [[Hermes Trismegistus]] similarly posits an ineffable, unknowable divine substrate ("That which Is") from which all proceeds. Blavatsky explicitly situates her Absolute as the ancient root from which later Hermetic, Vedantic, and Neoplatonic conceptions derive.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky acknowledges that even this description of the Absolute is necessarily imperfect and symbolic: "The Absolute can have no relation to the bounded and the conditioned." She also distinguishes her position from both Christian theology (which personalises Deity) and orthodox materialist science (which denies any metaphysical substrate). She treats her own formulations as the best available approximations rather than definitive statements.
