## Claim Statement

Blavatsky teaches that the early Third Root-Race, though physically constituted, remained without
self-consciousness or intellectual capacity — "mindless" (Amanasa) — because the higher spiritual
principle, **Manas** (mind/intellect), had not yet been awakened in their forms. The "Sons of
Wisdom" or **Manasaputras** (literally "sons of mind") — a class of divine beings, identified with
the Kumâras of Hindu tradition and with the "fallen angels" of exoteric religion — voluntarily
descended and incarnated in the most suitable human vehicles of the Third Race, thereby kindling
true self-conscious intellect in humanity. This is the occult meaning, Blavatsky asserts, of the
myth of Prometheus, the Fall of the Angels, and the Serpent of Genesis offering the "fruit of
knowledge."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Stanza VII (Anthropogenesis) declares:
"The Sons of Wisdom, the Sons of Night, ready for rebirth, came down, they saw the vile forms of
the first Third, 'We can choose,' said the Lords, 'we have wisdom.' Some entered the Chhaya. Some
projected the spark. Some deferred till the Fourth."

Stanza IX: "Seeing which [the sin of the mindless], the Lhas who had not built men, wept, saying:
'The Amanasa have defiled our future abodes. This is Karma. Let us dwell in the others. Let us
teach them better, lest worse should happen.' They did.... Then all became endowed with Manas."

Commentary on Stanza VII explains the sequential refusal: "How did the Manasa, the Sons of Wisdom,
act? They rejected the self-born. They are not ready. They spurned the sweat-born. They are not
quite ready. They would not enter the first egg-born." Only the later Third Race, egg-born and
possessing bones and sufficient physical organization, was deemed suitable: "When the sweat-born
produced the egg-born, the two-fold and the mighty, the powerful with bones, the Lords of Wisdom
said: 'Now shall we create.'"

Blavatsky equates these beings with multiple traditions: the Hindu Kumâras ("Sanandana and the other
sons of Vedhas, who, previously created by him 'without desire or passion, remained chaste, full of
holy wisdom and undesirous of progeny'"); the Prometheus of Greek myth; and the "Serpent" of Genesis
who offered knowledge. "The divine man dwelt in the animal" until the Manasaputras' incarnation made
the animal form a true human being.

The power by which the earlier "Sons of Will and Yoga" created their holy offspring was **Kriyasakti**
— "the mysterious power of thought which enables it to produce external, perceptible, phenomenal
results by its own inherent energy."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky notes that not all Manasaputras fully incarnated: "some entered the Chhaya; some projected
the spark; some deferred till the Fourth." Those who received only a "spark" rather than full
incarnation "remained destitute of knowledge; the spark burned low." This explains — within the
doctrine — why cognitive capacity varies across sub-races and individuals.
