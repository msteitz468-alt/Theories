## Claim Statement

Blavatsky asserts the second foundational principle of the Secret Doctrine: the **Eternity of the Universe in toto** as a boundless plane, perpetually cycling through phases of active manifestation (Manvantara) and dissolution (Pralaya). She calls this "the absolute universality of the law of periodicity, of flux and reflux, ebb and flow," which she regards as the most perfectly universal law observable in nature. The universe is not created ex nihilo by a personal God but is the periodic "outbreathing" of the Great Breath; when the Breath is withdrawn, the universe is re-absorbed and "darkness solitary and alone, broods once more over the face of the deep." The process has neither a first beginning nor a last end: our present universe is one of an infinite series.

The Stanzas of Dzyan open with this principle in action: Stanza I depicts the universe plunged in Pralaya — time not yet existing, Universal Mind not yet manifest, the Dhyan-Chohans absorbed in "Paranishpanna" (absolute perfection / rest) — and Stanza III depicts the re-awakening at the last vibration of the Seventh Eternity.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "The Eternity of the Universe in toto as a boundless plane; periodically 'the playground of numberless Universes incessantly manifesting and disappearing'… The appearance and disappearance of Worlds is like a regular tidal ebb of flux and reflux."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "This second assertion of the Secret Doctrine is the absolute universality of that law of periodicity, of flux and reflux, ebb and flow, which physical science has observed and recorded in all departments of nature. An alternation such as that of Day and Night, Life and Death, Sleeping and Waking, is a fact so common, so perfectly universal and without exception, that it is easy to comprehend that in it we see one of the absolutely fundamental laws of the universe."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Proem

> "The appearance and disappearance of the Universe are pictured as an outbreathing and inbreathing of 'the Great Breath,' which is eternal, and which, being Motion, is one of the three aspects of the Absolute — Abstract Space and Duration being the other two."
> — *The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Stanza I Commentary

Blavatsky assigns precise time-scales to Manvantaric and Pralayic cycles derived from Hindu cosmological arithmetic (the Day of Brahmâ = 4,320,000,000 mortal years; the Great Age = 311,040,000,000,000 years), though she acknowledges these as esoteric figures whose key "cannot be given."

## Cross-source Connections

The Kybalion's Principle of Rhythm — "Everything flows out and in; everything has its tides; all things rise and fall" — is directly parallel and the Kybalion attributes this principle to the ancient Hermetic teaching: see [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]]. The broader Hermetic tradition (see [[Hermes Trismegistus]]) similarly teaches the cyclical nature of cosmic manifestation.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky acknowledges that the Secret Doctrine "deals only with our Planetary System" for the purposes of the Stanzas, noting that "the Teachers say openly that not even the highest Dhyani-Chohans have ever penetrated the mysteries beyond those boundaries that separate the milliards of Solar systems from the 'Central Sun.'"
