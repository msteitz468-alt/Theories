> **Sensitivity note.** The Root-Race scheme assigns attributes to races in a hierarchical sequence.
> The claim is recorded descriptively as Blavatsky's assertion, not as fact or endorsement.

## Claim Statement

Blavatsky teaches that humanity has not evolved upward from animal ancestors (as Darwinian theory holds)
but rather has descended from above — from spiritual, ethereal beings — and is currently in the process
of re-ascending. The seven Root-Races constitute the complete cycle of this evolution, each characterized
by a distinct mode of reproduction, continent, and degree of spiritual or material emphasis.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The Stanzas of Dzyan (Anthropogenesis section) lay out the races sequentially: (1) "the Chhaya," the
astral shadow-men born of the Pitris; (2) the "sweat-born," produced by budding or cellular-style
division from the first; (3) the "egg-born," first androgynes, then sexually separated, inhabiting
Lemuria; (4) the Atlantean Fourth Race, fully physical giants of developed intellect but prone to
sorcery; (5) the current humanity, born from a "holy stock" of the surviving Fourth Race elect. The
sixth and seventh are prophesied but not yet manifest.

Key passages (Vol. II, commentary on Stanzas IV–XII):

- "The First men were Chhayas (1); the second, the 'Sweat-born' (2); the Third, 'Egg-born,' and the
  holy Fathers born by the power of Kriyasakti (3); the Fourth were the children of Padmapani (4)."
- "The first Root-Race was as ethereal as ours is material... the Occultist maintains that the case
  was as stated æons of years before even the evolution of the Lemurian, the first physical man,
  which itself took place 18,000,000 years ago."
- "Our teachings show that... the tendency... is one inherent in every atom. All things had their
  origin in spirit — evolution having originally begun from above and proceeding downwards, instead of
  the reverse, as taught in the Darwinian theory."

Each race is said to be born under the influence of one of the seven Planetary Spirits, and each
passes through seven sub-races before giving way to the next Root-Race.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky herself acknowledges that the first two races leave no fossil record (being ethereal), and
that physical science cannot confirm the existence of Lemurians 18 million years ago. She frames this
as science's limitation rather than her scheme's weakness. She also notes the teaching is selective:
"only forty-nine Slokas out of several hundred are here given."
