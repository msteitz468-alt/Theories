## Claim Statement

Blavatsky asserts that the esoteric or "Mystery" language of antiquity was a universal ideographic
system in which the same symbols — the cross, the serpent, the lotus, the zodiac, sacred animals,
geometrical figures (circle, triangle, cube, pentagon, hexagon), and astronomical motifs — encoded
identical metaphysical truths across every culture. This language has "seven dialects," each
corresponding to one of the seven mysteries of Nature. Properly decoded with the occult key that the
Secret Doctrine claims to restore, these symbols do not reveal naive nature-worship or primitive
superstition, but a sophisticated cosmological and spiritual philosophy: the same doctrine taught
in the Egyptian, Hindu, Chaldean, Greek, and Hebrew mystery traditions. Comparative symbolism,
practised at this esoteric level, is therefore the scientific method of theosophy.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

*The Secret Doctrine*, Vol. I, Book I, Part II (*Evolution of Symbolism*):

- **On the seven-dialect mystery language:** "The great archaic system known from prehistoric ages
  as the sacred Wisdom Science... had, and still has, its universal... language of the Hierophants,
  which has seven 'dialects,' so to speak, each referring, and being specially appropriated, to one
  of the seven mysteries of Nature. Each had its own symbolism. Nature could thus be either read in
  its fullness, or viewed from one of its special aspects." (§ I, line ~4020)

- **On geometry as the oldest symbolism:** "The earliest forms of elementary geometry must have
  certainly been suggested by the observation of the heavenly bodies and their groupings. Hence the
  most archaic symbols in Eastern Esotericism are a circle, a point, a triangle, a plane, a cube,
  a pentacle, and a hexagon, and plane figures with various sides and angles. This shows the knowledge
  and use of geometrical symbology to be as old as the world." (§ II)

- **On the Bible as esoteric:** Blavatsky argues that the "whole cycle of biblical glyphs and numbers
  as suggested by astronomical observations" connects the Hebrew Bible's "secret system" with
  "Indian, Chaldean, and Egyptian symbolism" — the zodiac signs, planetary aspects, and sacred nodes
  carry the same meaning across traditions.

- **On cross-civilisational parallels in cosmogony:** Tracing the Chaos-Theos-Kosmos triad across
  Hindu, Egyptian, Greek, Chaldean, and Hebrew sources (§ IV), Blavatsky argues that "the Aryan,
  Hermetic, Orphic, and Pythagorean cosmogonical doctrines, as well as those of Sanchoniathon and
  Berosus, are all based upon one irrefutable formula" — the primordial duality of Æther (spirit/mind)
  and Chaos (undifferentiated matter), whose union produces the manifest cosmos.

- **On the serpent symbol specifically:** The serpent appears as the Agathodaemon (good spirit) in
  Egyptian cosmogony (Kneph), as Ananta-Sesha (eternity) in Hinduism, and as the "seven-headed
  Serpent of Space" in the Kabbalah — all, Blavatsky argues, encoding the same occult teaching
  about the cyclic nature of manifestation and the Astral Light.

## Cross-source connections

- **[[The Secret Teachings of All Ages (Hall)]]** — Hall inherits this method wholesale: his 1928
  encyclopaedia systematically traces the cross-cultural identity of symbols (the Rosicrucian rose,
  Masonic geometry, the caduceus, the zodiac) using precisely the comparative-symbolism framework
  Blavatsky lays out here.

- **[[Hermes Trismegistus]]** — Blavatsky singles out the Hermetic tradition as a key surviving
  fragment of the universal esoteric language; the Hermetic formula of Æther-Chaos-Logos maps
  exactly onto her Chaos-Theos-Kosmos triad.

- **[[The Forbidden Universe (Picknett & Prince)]]** — argues that Renaissance Hermetists (Ficino,
  Bruno) and later Freemasonry encoded the same prisca-theologia claim in their symbolic systems;
  complements Blavatsky's account of the continuity of the mystery-language.

- **[[Where did civilization and advanced knowledge come from? Models compared across sources]]** —
  Blavatsky's comparative-symbolism method is one of the primary inputs into the "common ancient
  source" model in that synthesis page.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky is polemical in rejecting Max Müller's solar-myth hypothesis and the broader academic
consensus that mythological parallels reflect independent cultural evolution or diffusion rather
than a common esoteric origin. She does not engage with the possibility that symbolic parallels
could arise from universal human cognitive structures (a hypothesis later advanced in comparative
religion and anthropology). Within the source, the claim that the esoteric key "fits all" is
asserted rather than demonstrated by independent methodology; it rests on Blavatsky's authority
as a teacher with access to the Mahatmas.
