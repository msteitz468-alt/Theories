## Claim Statement

Blavatsky teaches that early humanity possessed a working physical "third eye" — an additional sensory
organ at the back of the skull, through which direct spiritual and cosmic perception was achieved.
This organ is identified with the **pineal gland** in the modern brain, which Blavatsky regards as
the atrophied remnant of this once-active faculty. The third eye functioned actively during the Third
Root-Race and in the early Fourth Race; as humanity fell deeper into physical matter, the organ
progressively closed and "petrified." Occult training (Yoga, celibacy, initiation) can partially
re-activate the faculty — which Blavatsky calls "the deva-eye" — in advanced practitioners.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Stanza X, verse 42: "They built great images nine yatis high... they worshipped them. Then the third
eye acted no longer."

Commentary: "There were four-armed human creatures in those early days of the male-females
(hermaphrodites); with one head, yet three eyes. They could see before them and behind them. A Kalpa
later (after the separation of the sexes) men having fallen into matter, their spiritual vision became
dim; and coordinately the third eye commenced to lose its power... The third eye, likewise, getting
gradually PETRIFIED, soon disappeared. The double-faced became the one-faced, and the eye was drawn
deep into the head and is now buried under the hair."

On the pineal gland: "The 'deva-eye' exists no more for the majority of mankind. The third eye is
dead, and acts no longer; but it has left behind a witness to its existence. This witness is now the
PINEAL GLAND."

Blavatsky cites contemporary anatomical science: "In the Hatteria species, a lizard of the order
Lacertilia, recently discovered in New Zealand (a part of ancient Lemuria so called, mark well),
presents this peculiarity in a most extraordinary manner" — the species possessing a clearly vestigial
third eye. She also cites Descartes' identification of the pineal gland as the "Seat of the Soul."

On the connection to celibacy: "When we learn that the 'third eye' was once a physiological organ...
the connection will become clear. Sexual action being closely connected, by interaction, with the
spinal cord and the grey matter of the brain, it is useless to give any longer explanation" of why
chastity is required for Yoga-development. The pineal gland's activity is suppressed by sexual
activity via inductive action from the medulla oblongata.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Blavatsky acknowledges that no fossil proof of a three-eyed human skull will be found, since the
"third eye" was in the back of the head (not the forehead as in popular depictions of the "Cyclops"),
and "the first two races were too ethereal and phantom-like in their constitution... to be called
physical men," leaving no recoverable fossil record. She also concedes that the claim of "three eyes"
is "one more of the points on which Occultists will be charged with superstition."
