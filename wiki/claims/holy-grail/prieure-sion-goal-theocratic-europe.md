**Claim Statement**

Based on their analysis of the "Prieuré documents" and the alleged history of the Prieuré de Sion, the source concludes that Sion's current objectives include the creation of a theocratic United States of Europe — a trans-European confederation under a dynasty descended from Jesus via the Merovingian bloodline. The new priest-king would "rule but not govern" (in accordance with Merovingian tradition); actual governing would be handled by the Prieuré de Sion itself. The dynasty would occupy both a throne of secular power and, conceivably, the throne of Saint Peter.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**

"In many of its documents Sion asserts that the new king — in accordance with Merovingian tradition — would 'rule but not govern'. In other words he would be a priest-king, who functions primarily in a ritual and symbolic capacity; and the actual business of governing would be handled by someone else — conceivably by the Prieuré de Sion."

"[Sion's objectives] would seem to include a theocratic United States of Europe — a trans- or pan-European confederation assembled into a modern empire and ruled by a dynasty descended from Jesus. This dynasty would not only occupy a throne of political or secular power, but quite conceivably, the throne of Saint Peter as well. Under that supreme authority there might then be an interlocking network of kingdoms or principalities, connected by dynastic alliance and intermarriage — a kind of twentieth-century 'feudal system.'"

The source traces this goal back at least to the nineteenth century: "During the nineteenth century the Prieuré de Sion, working through Freemasonry and the Hieron du Val d'Or, attempted to establish a revived and 'updated' Holy Roman Empire — a kind of theocratic United States of Europe, ruled simultaneously by the Habsburgs and by a radically reformed Church. This enterprise was thwarted by the First World War and the fall of Europe's reigning dynasties."

The source acknowledges there are "at least a dozen families in Britain and Europe today with numerous collateral branches who are of Merovingian lineage," including Habsburg-Lorraine, Plantard, Luxembourg, Montpezat, Montesquieu, Sinclair, and various Stuart branches.

**Contexts Within the Source**

The source notes that a lineal descendant of Jesus would not, in modern eyes, be "any more divine, any more intrinsically miraculous, than the rest of us," and that "many people would simply shrug and ask, 'So what?'" The political rather than theological significance of the bloodline revelation is therefore emphasized: such a figure could unify Western Europe on the basis of deep spiritual and emotional appeal, creating "a new and unified political force in international affairs ... stronger than either the Soviet Union or the United States, because it would rest on deep-rooted spiritual and emotional foundations."

**Opposing Information Within the Source**

The source frames this as speculative: "Needless to say, our understanding of those objectives can only be speculative." It presents the goal as an inference from the pattern of Sion's alleged behavior across centuries.
