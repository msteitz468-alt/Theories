**Claim Statement**

The source proposes that after the Crucifixion, Mary Magdalene — carrying at least one child by Jesus — was smuggled to Gaul (southern France), where established Jewish communities provided refuge. The bloodline thus founded perpetuated itself for approximately four centuries through dynastic intermarriages with Jewish, Roman, and Visigoth families, and in the fifth century became allied with the Frankish royal line, producing the Merovingian dynasty.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**

The source states the hypothesis: "After the Crucifixion, perhaps the Magdalene, with at least one child, was smuggled to Gaul where established Jewish communities already existed and where, in consequence, she might have found a refuge."

Medieval traditions the source cites in partial support:
- The church at Rennes-le-Château is consecrated to Mary Magdalene (consecrated 1059, on foundations of a Visigoth structure from the sixth century).
- Medieval traditions maintain the Magdalene landed at Marseilles (southern France), bringing with her the Grail.
- René d'Anjou is described as possessing a cup he claimed had been used at the Wedding at Cana and obtained at Marseilles "where the Magdalene, according to tradition, landed with the Grail." A mysterious inscription on the cup's rim: *Qui bien beurra Dieu voira. Qui beurra tout dune baleine Voira Dieu et la Madeleine* — "He who drinks well will see God. He who quaffs at a single draught will see God and the Magdalene."
- The term "Madeleine" (Magdalene) recurs in the titles of the "Prieuré documents" — e.g. a work attributed to "Madeleine Blancassal" is entitled *Les Descendants mérovingiens et l'énigme du Razès*.

The source notes there were indeed established Jewish communities in southern France by the first century AD, making the scenario logistically plausible.

**Contexts Within the Source**

The source argues this scenario would explain: (1) the extraordinary cult significance of the Magdalene during the Crusades; (2) why the Grail romances associated the Grail with both France and the Magdalene rather than Britain and Joseph of Arimathea; (3) why the church at Rennes-le-Château — at the center of the mystery — is dedicated to her; (4) why the founding legend of Merovee involves a "creature from beyond the sea" impregnating his mother (interpreted as a disguised memory of a bloodline arriving by sea).

**Opposing Information Within the Source**

The source explicitly acknowledges: "Whatever else he discovered, he also discovered a secret ... the scenario of the Magdalene's flight to Gaul rests on inference rather than direct evidence." No primary source attesting to the Magdalene's arrival in Gaul is presented — the traditions cited are medieval legends, not first-century documents.
