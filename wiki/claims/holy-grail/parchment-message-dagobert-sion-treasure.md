**Claim Statement**

One of the two parchments composed by the Abbé Antoine Bigou (Saunière's predecessor at Rennes-le-Château) contains a second hidden message that can be extracted by reading certain raised letters in sequence. This message reads:

*A DAGOBERT II ROI ET A SION EST CE TRESOR ET IL EST LA MORT*

Translated: "To Dagobert II, King, and to Sion belongs this treasure and he is there dead."

The first and longer decoded message reads:
*BERGERE PAS DE TENTATION QUE POUSSIN TENIERS GARDENT LA CLEF PAX DCLXXXI PAR LA CROIX ET CE CHEVAL DE DIEU J'ACHEVE CE DAEMON DE GARDIEN A MIDI POMMES BLEUES*

Translated: "Shepherdess, no temptation. That Poussin, Teniers, hold the key; peace 681. By the cross and this horse of God, I complete/destroy this daemon of the guardian at noon. Blue apples."

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**

The source reports that these decipherments appeared in French works devoted to Rennes-le-Château and in two of the authors' BBC films. The second (shorter) message is described as "patently, even flagrantly obvious" — the raised letters spell it out directly. The longer message required a complex cipher that the source indicates was broken by French Army Cipher Department experts using computers, though the source notes cipher specialists from British Intelligence were skeptical this was possible without a key.

The source notes that the inscription on the Marquise de Blanchefort's tombstone — designed by the same Abbé Bigou — was a perfect anagram of the message containing the references to Poussin and Sion. Saunière later obliterated this tombstone.

**Contexts Within the Source**

The reference to "Sion" in the encoded messages is what first drew the authors toward investigating the Prieuré de Sion. The connection of Dagobert II with Rennes-le-Château is historically plausible: the region was Visigoth territory in Dagobert's era, and documents speak of wealth Dagobert amassed and concealed in the environs of Rennes-le-Château. The message's claim that "to Sion belongs this treasure" is interpreted by the authors as evidence that the Prieuré de Sion had knowledge of — or custodianship over — whatever secret Saunière discovered.

The eight letters "REX MUNDI" also appear distributed across one of the parchments — a term immediately recognizable as a Cathar designation for the material world's ruler.
