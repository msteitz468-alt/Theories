## Claim Statement

Continuing the thread that closed *The Scars of Eden*, Wallis discloses that he is himself an "experiencer": in a 2021 regression session with abduction therapist **Barbara Lamb** he recovered the opening moments of a 1985 close encounter — though, characteristically, only a fragment, with no dramatic content.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He recounts waking from the session "strangely heavy and rather hungry," and describes the recovered memory as "the first few moments of an encounter and then nothing more. No punchline. No examination. No anal probe." He explicitly contrasts himself with Whitley Strieber (whose 1985 encounter he notes was "the very same year" as his own), and aligns himself with "the great majority of experiencers around the world" whose "experience is of things [they] cannot explain." He frames the whole investigation as driven by the question "How do you go back to life as normal when your whole world has just changed?"

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Wallis is candid that the memory is fragmentary and unexplained, presenting it as a personal anomaly that motivated the book rather than as evidence of anything specific.
