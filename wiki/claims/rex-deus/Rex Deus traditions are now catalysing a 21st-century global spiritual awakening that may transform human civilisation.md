## Claim Statement

In the concluding chapter of [[Custodians of Truth (Wallace-Murphy & Hopkins)]], the authors argue that the centuries of Rex Deus transmission — through the Grail sagas, the Tarot, and Freemasonry and its offshoots — are now bearing fruit in a visible and accelerating global spiritual awakening. The accumulated dissatisfaction with Church dogma, scientific materialism, and political violence that characterised the 20th century is, they contend, driving humanity toward the experiential initiatory spirituality that Rex Deus preserved at such cost. The book's conclusion is explicitly normative: the authors call on readers to act on Rex Deus wisdom as a "motor for action in the temporal world" to create a just, fraternal, and peaceful global civilisation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "The dissemination of Rex Deus traditions using the Grail sagas, the tarot, and Freemasonry, and its many offshoots and imitators bore fruit in the esoteric revival that occurred toward the end of the 19th century when the Catholic Church was visibly losing its grip. Thus began a process that is only now approaching its culmination."

> "This new awareness has continued to grow and now inspires an ever-expanding group of spiritually inspired people who, individually and collectively, form a growing pool of consciousness that carries with it potential benefits for all mankind."

> "We can find the answers to these problems in the hidden wisdom preserved by the Rex Deus families and those who followed the tried and trusted initiatory paths of other great faiths. The question is: Have we the wit, the humility, and the courage to apply them?"

The authors also quote French mystic and initiate Frederic Lionel (from a 1997 meeting): "You are writing of matters that are known to very few. You have a sacred duty, you must continue, there is not much time."

## Characterisation of the Spiritual Awakening

The authors describe it as comprising:
- Mass rejection of outdated Church dogma, beginning at the end of the 19th century.
- The social activism of the 1960s and the "consciousness revolution" of the early 1970s.
- Proliferation of access to mystical traditions (Buddhist, Sufi, Kabbalistic, Hindu) in the West; university extension courses, ashrams, Theosophical and Anthroposophical movements.
- The Internet as an accelerating mechanism for spiritual information exchange.
- A common principle across all valid paths: they are experiential, not merely informational; they require humility, patience, and dedicated work; they reveal the "divine spark" within all people.

The authors cite contemporary esoteric schools as genuine continuations of the Rex Deus principle: the Ramtha School of Ancient Wisdom (America) and Beshara, the Sufi centre in Scotland (founded on the teachings of Bulent Rauf).

## Prophetic and Civilisational Scope

The authors frame this as a world-historical threshold:
> "Human consciousness is crossing a threshold as mighty as the one from the Middle Ages to the Renaissance." (M. C. Richards, quoted)

They invoke the "new Aquarian age" as a period in which science will be harnessed to humanity's service, and call for "true fraternity, compassion, and justice" as realities rather than ideals. The chapter closes with poems by Christopher Fry and a quotation from Sathya Sai Baba ("There is only one religion, the religion of Love").

## Discernment Problem Acknowledged

The authors note that the proliferation of New Age publishing and courses presents a "crucial problem… of discernment." They offer a criterion derived from Rex Deus tradition itself: "By their fruits shall ye know them" — valid spiritual paths produce service, love, and harmony in their adherents; they do not offer "instant cure-alls."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

This chapter is explicitly the authors' personal conclusion and advocacy rather than historical reconstruction. They make no claim to neutrality: "Only we, and others like us, can ensure that this will come to pass." The chapter is normative and eschatological in tone.
