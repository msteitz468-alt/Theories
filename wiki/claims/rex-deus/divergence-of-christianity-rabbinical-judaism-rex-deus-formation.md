## Claim Statement

Per [[Custodians of Truth (Wallace-Murphy & Hopkins)]], the Roman destruction of [[Jerusalem]] in 70 CE simultaneously produced three distinct outcomes: (1) the rise of Rabbinical Judaism, reconstructed by Rabbi Yohanen ben Zakkai at Jamnia with messianic nationalism deliberately stripped out; (2) the triumph of Pauline Christianity over the Ebionite/Nazorean movement, which had depended on the Jerusalem community; and (3) the dispersal of the surviving members of the twenty-four ma'amadot and the Desposyni (the family of Jesus), who scattered across Europe and the Middle East as the founding lineages of the [[Rex Deus]] families.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**The foundation of Rabbinical Judaism:**
Rabbi Yohanen ben Zakkai, who had opposed the Zealot revolution and was smuggled out of besieged Jerusalem in a coffin, approached Emperor Vespasian and obtained permission to found a school at Jamnia. The source describes the result: "Judaism was to be stripped of its messianic and intensely nationalistic zeal." The rabbis taught Jews "to experience God in their neighbors" and created a form of worship and ritual acceptable to Romans. The emphasis changed but not the substance — continuing to speak of Jerusalem in the present tense "even though the temple building no longer existed."

**The Pauline Christians' advantage:**
The Ebionites (followers of [[James the Just]]) were scattered and dependent on oral transmission of Jesus's teachings from people who had known him directly. The Pauline Christians were comparatively settled and had written documents circulating since c. 47 CE. The community in Rome, whose theology was most closely aligned with Paul's, ultimately won the battle for doctrinal supremacy. The Gospels (written from c. 70–120 CE) were composed largely under the influence of Paul's framework and under Roman political censorship.

**The dispersal of the ma'amadot and Desposyni:**
The Ebionites and surviving members of the ma'amadot had returned from their wartime refuge at Pella under the leadership of Simeon (cousin of Jesus) to the ruins of Jerusalem, taking up residence near Mount Sion. However, successive emperors (Vespasian, Titus, Domitian, Trajan) ordered the occupying tenth legion to hunt down and execute any Jew claiming Davidic descent. This persecution drove the Rex Deus families to scatter across France, England, Spain, Italy, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East.

The source describes their response: before dispersing, the families used their time in Jerusalem's ruins to "reinforce their Cohenite marital practices in order to preserve their sacred bloodlines" and to transform their beliefs toward survival — discarding "fervent nationalism and anti-Roman stance" while "laying even greater emphasis on behavior and creating an elite within the elite dedicated to the principles of sacred brotherhood founded firmly on the gnostic principles of justice and truth."

**Their outward conformity strategy:**
Per Michael's account and the wider Rex Deus tradition: "Their traditions dictated that they outwardly profess to follow the prevailing religion of the time and culture in which they lived, but that secretly they were bound by oath to follow the 'true way.'" Each family maintained accurate genealogies and was required to marry within the Rex Deus group, preserving the Cohenite bloodlines.

**What Rex Deus represents:**
The body of evidence from the Rex Deus informant Michael and the broader research led the authors to conclude: "The real heroes of history, we found, are the Rex Deus families in both the Christian and Islamic worlds, whose message of true monotheism, tolerance, brotherhood, and respect for nature is as relevant today as it has ever been."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim closes the foundational arc of the book. Chapters 8–19 (Agent B and Agent C territory) trace the Rex Deus families' role in medieval history — the Crusades, the founding of the [[Knights Templar]], the [[Cathars (Albigensians)]], Rosslyn Chapel, and Freemasonry.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

- The source acknowledges that the Desposyni's return to Jerusalem and subsequent dispersal is reconstructed partly from family oral tradition and partly from the testimony of early Church fathers, some of whose works survive only in fragments or quotation
- The precise genealogical records inscribed on the walls of rooms under the Temple are not independently attested
- The authors do not claim to have independently verified the specific family genealogies, only the structural pattern of intermarriage and geographic dispersal
