## Claim Statement

Per [[Custodians of Truth (Wallace-Murphy & Hopkins)]], [[St. Paul]] was a Roman citizen and Herodian kinsman of King Agrippa II who systematically distorted the Torah-observant, Jewish-nationalist, initiatory teachings of [[Jesus of Nazareth]] and [[James the Just]] into a de-nationalized, pro-Roman "savior god" cult. Had the Temple not been destroyed in 70 CE, Paul's version would have been forgotten; the destruction of the Ebionite community created a power vacuum that the Roman-centered Pauline Christians filled. The Church that emerged was the deliberate inversion of what Jesus taught.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The source synthesizes Robert Eisenman's Dead Sea Scrolls scholarship, the Ebionite Kerygmata Petrou, early Church fathers (Irenaeus, Origen, Epiphanius), and the Q document scholarship of Burton Mack.

**Paul's Herodian identity (key to the argument):**
Paul's own epistle (Romans 16:10–11) identifies Herodian as his "relative." As a Herodian, Paul was born a Roman citizen. The source argues this explains his entire trajectory: his role persecuting Jesus's followers as a temple guard agent, his comfortable imprisonment, his extraordinary military escort, his high-level Roman contacts (including Epaphroditus, a senior advisor to Nero), and the fact that the first Christians (at Antioch) were mainly other Herodians. "Paul was getting considerable support from his family."

**The theological inversion:**
The source enumerates what Jesus's actual followers (the Ebionites/Nazoreans) believed vs. what Paul taught:

| James / Nazoreans | Paul / Roman Christians |
|---|---|
| Torah strictly observed (613 commandments) | Torah superseded by faith alone |
| Circumcision required | Circumcision unnecessary |
| No social contact with Gentiles | Mission to all nations |
| Dietary laws strictly kept | Dietary laws abolished |
| Jesus was human — not divine | Jesus as divine "savior-god" |
| Nationalistic, anti-Roman | "Obey lawful authorities" |

**Early Christian sources confirming the betrayal:**
- Ebionite documents: Paul is "an apostate of the Law," "a Spouter of wickedness and lies," "the distorter of the true teachings of Jesus"
- Kerygmata Petrou: his Damascus visions were "dreams and illusions inspired by devils"
- Irenaeus quotes an Ebionite document calling Paul "an apostate of the Law"
- Paul physically assaulted [[James the Just]], throwing him down the temple steps and breaking his legs (Pseudo-Clementine Recognitions; Anabathmoi Jacobou)

**The Q document and Gospel authorship:**
The source notes that Paul's letters (from c. 47 CE) are earlier than any Gospel, and that the canonical Gospels are "in large part doctrinally dependent upon Paul." The Q source (the lost document underlying the Synoptic Gospels) is reconstructed by Burton Mack as depicting Jesus as "a Jewish prophet whose teaching made it possible to live an attainable and righteous life" — with no suggestion of divinity, resurrection, or trinitarian doctrine. The deification of Jesus is attributed to "intrusion from Gentile or heretical sources" that was "staunchly resisted by the original apostles."

**The role of the Temple's destruction:**
Neil Faulkner is quoted: "The defeat of apocalyptic hope and the physical destruction of the Judaeo-Christian sect cleared the way for Pauline Christians to de-nationalise Jesus, cauterise his revolutionary message and repackage him as a 'saviour-god' dispensing opiate for the masses." Paul Johnson is quoted as saying that without the destruction of Jerusalem, Paul's evangelical effort "would have been consigned to a minor footnote of history."
