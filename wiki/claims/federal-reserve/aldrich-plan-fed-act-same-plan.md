## Claim Statement

The Republican "Aldrich Plan" and the Democratic "Federal Reserve Act" were substantially the same central-bank plan, both originating at Jekyll Island and written by the same people. Their staged opposition was a "two-stratagem" device ensuring the bankers would get their central bank no matter which party won.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 2): "The Federal Reserve Act ... was in all its essentials the same plan for a central bank, and was promoted by the same New York banking interests. It was an alternate stratagem which made it impossible for the bankers to lose. No matter who won the election, they would get their central bank."

He notes Aldrich and Vanderlip publicly attacked the Federal Reserve Act as anti-banker — which the source calls "a smoke screen to make people think that the big bankers were afraid of the Act" — while objecting to the very provisions they had championed in the Aldrich Plan. Senator Robert L. Owen openly accused Vanderlip of misrepresentation. Vanderlip's own autobiography is quoted: "The essential points of the Aldrich Plan were all contained in the Federal Reserve Act as it was passed."
