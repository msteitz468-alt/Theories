## Claim Statement

The most important function of a central bank is to finance major wars by mobilizing a nation's money and credit; the Federal Reserve was designed with this purpose, and its creation made large-scale war finance (and thus war itself) possible for the United States.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 8): the Reserve System was set up "to perform the most important function of a central bank, mobilizing a nation's money and credit to finance a major war." He quotes Bernard Baruch (Nye Committee, 1937): "All wars are economic in their origin."

The source argues European nations could not afford WWI without such finance (citing the *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 1887, on Europe's unsustainable debts), and that the Rothschild system of national war loans was the model. It notes the Federal Reserve Banks began operations in 1914 — the same year WWI began — and were designated "fiscal agents of the Government," and quotes Cordell Hull that the war "assisted the putting into effect of the Federal Reserve System ... in the nick of time."

## Contexts Within the Source

Connects the monetary thesis to the book's claim that the Fed brought the US into both World Wars; extended to WWII and the Bretton Woods institutions.
