## Claim Statement

International financiers are motivated by a strategy Griffin calls the "Rothschild Formula": to propel governments into war for the profits war yields. As long as central banking exists, such men face "an irresistible temptation to convert debt into perpetual war and war into perpetual debt."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 11 summary) attributes to "that special breed of international financiers" the traits of "cold objectivity, immunity to patriotism, and indifference to the human condition," from which he derives the formula. He concedes it "most likely has never been consciously phrased as it appears here" but operates through subconscious motivation.

Supporting history: the House of Rothschild as "pioneers in the practice of lending money to governments," profiting "from both sides" of conflicts; Nathan Rothschild's use of advance Waterloo news to acquire "the dominant holding of England's entire debt at but a tiny fraction of its worth." Griffin then "tracks the distinctive footprint of the Rothschild Formula" through WWI (the Lusitania), the Bolshevik Revolution, and the American Civil War.

**Extraction note:** A contested, motive-attributing conspiracy thesis; recorded as the source's assertion, without endorsement.
