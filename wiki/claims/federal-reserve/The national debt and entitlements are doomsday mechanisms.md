## Claim Statement

The U.S. faces self-reinforcing "doomsday mechanisms": a national debt whose interest consumes a growing share of revenue; automatic entitlement spending; an electorate able to vote itself wealth transfers; and, above all, a Federal Reserve money supply created entirely as debt — so that paying off the debt would destroy the money supply, making the debt structurally permanent.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 24 summary, writing in the early 1990s): the federal debt was "5-trillion-dollar"; by 1993 net interest was "$214 billion per year ... about 14% of all federal revenue"; interest "is already consuming more than 57% of all the revenue collected from income taxes." Entitlements were "52% of federal outlays," so "two-thirds of all federal expenses are now entirely automatic."

"The biggest doomsday mechanism of all is the Federal Reserve System. Every cent of our money supply came into being for the purpose of being loaned to someone. Those dollars will disappear when the loans are paid back. If we tried to pay off the national debt, our money supply would be undermined." He also identifies the "point of no return" when "people can vote on issues involving the transfer of wealth to themselves from others — the ballot box becomes a weapon whereby the majority plunders the minority."
