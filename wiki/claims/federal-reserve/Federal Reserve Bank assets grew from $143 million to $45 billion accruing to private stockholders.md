## Claim Statement

The Federal Reserve Banks' assets grew from $143 million when they opened in 1914 to more than $45 billion by 1949 — an enormous profit accruing to the private stockholders of the banks, produced by a non-productive enterprise that merely creates money on the government's credit.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 7): "When the Federal Reserve Banks were set up and began operations on November 16, 1914, their total assets were listed at $143,000,000. On December 23, 1949, testimony before the House Banking and Currency Committee showed that the Federal Reserve Banks listed assets in excess of forty-five billion dollars ... The men who bought stock in those Federal Reserve Banks in 1914 made one of the most fortuitous investments in financial history."

In Ch. 14 he repeats the figure, noting the increase "went entirely to the private stockholders of the banks" since the 1933 Act sent all earnings to the banks, and that the System "did not produce anything ... yet they had this enormous profit, merely by creating money, 95% of it in the form of credit."
