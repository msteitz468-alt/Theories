## Claim Statement

The ultimate sponsors of the Federal Reserve Act were the European Rothschild banking interests, acting in America through their agents — chiefly Kuhn, Loeb & Co. (Paul Warburg, Jacob Schiff) and J. P. Morgan & Co.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins states Paul Warburg's family house M. M. Warburg & Co. "was the chief German representative of the great European banking family, the Rothschilds," and that "Liberal amounts of Rothschild funds had enabled Jacob Schiff to purchase a partnership in Kuhn, Loeb Company." He quotes Colonel Ely Garrison: "The mastermind of both plans [Aldrich and Federal Reserve] was Baron Alfred Rothschild of London."

The source characterizes J. P. Morgan & Co. as having become "the principal international agency" of the Rothschilds in 1869, and quotes Senator Robert L. Owen defining Kuhn, Loeb as "the representatives of the European Rothschilds in New York." The Northern Securities Company (1901) is called "the consolidation of the Rothschild Empire in America."

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the apex of the source's attribution of motive and control; it frames the entire Federal Reserve project as serving international (Rothschild) finance.
