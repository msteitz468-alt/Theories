## Claim Statement

J. P. Morgan & Co. was a concealed Rothschild agency from its origins: the firm grew out of George Peabody & Company, which Nathan Mayer Rothschild secretly financed from 1835; the Morgans "always" directed the firm from London and "agreed to continue the sub rosa relationship" with N.M. Rothschild, the affiliation deliberately hidden behind an "American" facade.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 5): Rothschild summoned [[George Peabody]] and secretly paid for his lavish London entertaining; Peabody's successor Junius S. Morgan "agreed to continue the sub rosa relationship with N.M. Rothschild Company"; "The Morgan firm then and since has always been directed from London." The source quotes George Wheeler that the first suggestion of Pierpont Morgan for a financial role "originated with the London branch of the House of Rothschild, Belmont's employers," and quotes John Moody that "The Rothschilds were content to remain a close ally of Morgan ... Secrecy was more profitable than valor."

Mullins corrects William Guy Carr, who dated the Morgan–Rothschild affiliation to 1899; Mullins says "it went back to 1835." He cites the 1891 founding of the Round Table by Cecil Rhodes and "his banker, Lord Rothschild," with the Morgan group as its U.S. representative.
