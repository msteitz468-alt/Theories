## Claim Statement

Inflation is a hidden tax: by expanding the money supply, the Federal Reserve lowers the value of the dollar, quietly transferring wealth from the public to the federal government and the banks without a visible levy.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 1): since its inception the Fed has presided over "a 1000% inflation which has destroyed 90% of the dollar's purchasing power" — "By 1990, an annual income of $10,000 was required to buy what took only $1,000 in 1914. That incredible loss in value was quietly transferred to the federal government in the form of hidden taxation, and the Federal Reserve System was the mechanism by which it was accomplished."

He repeats the theme throughout: the bailout cost "is passed to the public in the form of a hidden tax called inflation"; the Mandrake Mechanism's money-supply expansion "is the root cause of the hidden tax called inflation." Griffin extends the analysis historically: the First Bank of the U.S. confiscated "42% of everything people had saved" through inflation in five years; the War of 1812 financing fleeced "approximately 66%"; WWI inflation took "fully one-half of all they had saved" within five years.

## Contexts Within the Source

Inflation is Griffin's unifying mechanism connecting money creation, bailouts, and war finance to wealth confiscation.
