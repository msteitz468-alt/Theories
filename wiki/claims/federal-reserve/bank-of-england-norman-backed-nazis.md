## Claim Statement

Montagu Norman, Governor of the Bank of England, deliberately used the Bank's financial power to assist Hitler and the Nazis in gaining and maintaining power.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 7) quotes the financial editor of the London *Daily Herald* (Sept. 30, 1933) on "Mr. Norman's decision to give the Nazis the backing of the Bank (of England)," and Norman's biographer John Hargrave: "It is quite certain that Norman did all he could to assist Hitlerism to gain and maintain political power, operating on the financial plane from his stronghold in Threadneedle Street."

The source describes the Nazi philosopher Alfred Rosenberg being brought to London and introduced to Norman, after which Rosenberg met the Schroder Bank's representative; and an early-1934 gathering in Norman's room of City financiers (Kindersley of Lazard, Charles Hambro, F. C. Tiarks, Sir Josiah Stamp — several also Bank of England directors) at which Norman spoke approvingly of "a new power" that had "established itself" as "a great stabilizing" force in Europe.
