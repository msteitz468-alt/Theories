## Claim Statement

A secret Round Table Group founded in London on February 5, 1891 by Cecil Rhodes, Lord Rothschild, and associates — represented in the U.S. by the Morgan group — evolved after World War I into the Council on Foreign Relations (U.S.) and the Royal Institute of International Affairs (UK), and later spawned the Trilateral Commission and the Bilderberg group, all representing the same financial interests.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 5): "on February 5, 1891, a secret association known as the Round Table Group was formed in London by Cecil Rhodes, his banker, Lord Rothschild, the Rothschild in-law, Lord Rosebery, and Lord Curzon," with the Morgan group as its U.S. representative. He cites [[Carroll Quigley]]'s *Tragedy and Hope* on "The British-American Secret Society" running "from the Morgan Bank in New York to a group of international financiers in London led by Lazard Brothers."

"After World War I, the Round Table became known as the Council on Foreign Relations in the United States, and the Royal Institute of International Affairs in London.... In the 1960s ... subsidiary groups, known as the Trilateral Commission and the Bilderbergers, representing the identical financial interests, began operations," with figures such as Robert Roosa "being members of all three groups."
