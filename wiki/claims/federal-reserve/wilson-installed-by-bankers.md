## Claim Statement

Woodrow Wilson was put into the presidency by Wall Street bankers (chiefly the Kuhn-Loeb / National City Bank circle) on the understanding that he would enact the Federal Reserve Act; his role was essentially to sign the bill the bankers had written.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 4): Wilson "was limited by the fact that he had been put into office by the biggest Wall Street banking house of them all, Paul Warburg's firm of Kuhn, Loeb Co. His campaign for the Presidency had been entirely financed by Cleveland H. Dodge ... Jacob Schiff ... Henry Morgenthau, Sr., Bernard Baruch, and Samuel Untermyer." It states the National City Bank "gave Wilson the Presidency ... after Wilson promised to enact the Federal Reserve Act."

The source recounts Wilson's earlier praise of J. P. Morgan and the Aldrich Plan, his emissary Colonel House's role as conduit to Warburg, and Bernard Baruch's intervention to get Wilson to sign. George Creel is quoted that "Woodrow Wilson was without influence, save for the patronage he possessed" — the work being done by William Jennings Bryan.
