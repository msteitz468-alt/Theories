## Claim Statement

The twelve regional Federal Reserve districts were a deliberate disguise to make the public believe control of money and credit had been removed from Wall Street; in reality, because the nation's money market is in New York, the Federal Reserve Bank of New York dominates the System.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 4) argues Glass's regional system "ignored the fact that the nation's money market was in New York," quoting even Marriner Eccles: "New York is the only money market you have in this country." He cites Ferdinand Lundberg (*America's Sixty Families*): "the Federal Reserve Bank of New York became the fountainhead of the system of twelve regional banks ... The other eleven banks were so many expensive mausoleums erected to salve the local pride and quell the Jacksonian fears of the hinterland."

The source states the System "was to fall into the hands of two men during the first fifteen years," Paul Warburg and Benjamin Strong, both Jekyll Island participants. It also recounts that Warburg originally wanted only four banks and tried for eight, but Glass said twelve were needed to pass Congress.
