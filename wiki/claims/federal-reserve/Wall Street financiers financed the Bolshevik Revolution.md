## Claim Statement

The Bolshevik Revolution was not a spontaneous mass uprising but was planned, financed, and orchestrated largely by financiers in England and the United States (with some German funding), who sought profit and the long-term goal of drawing Russia into a financier-controlled world order.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 13 summary): "The Bolshevik revolution was not a spontaneous uprising of the masses. It was planned, financed, and orchestrated by outsiders.... most of the money and leadership came from financiers in England and the United States. It was a perfect example of the Rothschild formula in action."

Specifics from the source: [[Jacob Schiff]] of Kuhn, Loeb "personally financed Trotsky's trip from New York to Russia"; the American contingent "disguised itself as a Red Cross mission" (William Boyce Thompson, then [[Raymond Robins]]) and obtained "profitable business concessions"; a J. P. Morgan cablegram advised that $1 million had been transferred to Thompson via the National City Bank. Griffin (Ch. 14) frames the motive as profit and power: "These financiers are not pro-Communist nor pro-anything else.... War and threats of war are tools to prod the masses toward the acceptance of [world government]."

**Extraction note:** Contested conspiracy claim; recorded per strict-extraction policy. Griffin's cited authority, [[Wall Street and the Bolshevik Revolution (Sutton)]], documents the financing in detail but attributes it to the **Morgan–Rockefeller / Guaranty Trust** group rather than to Schiff/Kuhn-Loeb, and finds that Schiff actually *opposed* the Bolsheviks. See [[Controversy: Did Jacob Schiff finance the Bolshevik Revolution?]] and [[The Morgan-controlled Guaranty Trust financed the Bolsheviks]].
