## Claim Statement

Herbert Hoover was a London-based mining promoter and Rothschild secret agent installed in the U.S. presidency in 1928 by the "London Connection" bankers (Kuhn Loeb, Warburg, Schroder) in exchange for the Hoover Moratorium on German war debts — intended to fund German rearmament for World War II.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 7): Hoover "had also carried out a number of mining promotions in various parts of the world as a secret agent for the Rothschilds, and had been rewarded with a directorship in one of the principal Rothschild enterprises, the Rio Tinto Mines." Because he had lived abroad and lacked a U.S. address, Hoover listed the New York office of Edgar Rickard (shared with Schroder partner Julius Barnes) during his campaign.

He quotes Congressman McFadden: "If the German international bankers of Wall Street — that is Kuhn Loeb Company, J. & W. Seligman, Paul Warburg, J. Henry Schroder — and their satellites had not had this job waiting to be done, Herbert Hoover would never have been elected President.... In exchange ... Mr. Hoover promised to impose the moratorium of German debts." Mullins adds the moratorium "was necessary so that Germany would have funds for rearming," anticipating WWII. McFadden introduced an impeachment resolution against Hoover (including his appointment of Eugene Meyer), which was tabled.
