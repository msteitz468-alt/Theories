## Claim Statement

All money in the banking system is created out of nothing through the making of loans; more precisely, it is created out of debt. The Federal Reserve converts debt into money (the "Mandrake Mechanism"), so the entire money supply "would vanish into bank vaults and computer chips if all debts were repaid."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 10 summary): "The American dollar has no intrinsic value. It is a classic example of fiat money.... It is true that our money is created out of nothing, but it is more accurate to say that it is based upon debt.... The entire money supply would vanish ... if all debts were repaid. Under the present System, therefore, our leaders cannot allow a serious reduction in either the national or consumer debt. Charging interest on pretended loans is usury, and that has become institutionalized under the Federal Reserve System."

He states the mechanism's "end product ... is artificial expansion of the money supply, which is the root cause of the hidden tax called inflation," producing "the destructive boom-bust cycle." Griffin's account parallels Mullins's quotation of [[Marriner Eccles]] ("We created it ... out of the right to issue credit money").

## Contexts Within the Source

Foundational to Griffin's argument that the Fed is a wealth-confiscation engine; it underlies the bailout, inflation, and "doomsday mechanism" claims.
