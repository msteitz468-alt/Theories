## Claim Statement

The Federal Reserve creates money out of nothing but the government's credit — by purchasing government securities with checks drawn on itself — and can likewise extinguish money by selling them; all money in the system is created by debt, public or private.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Chs. 14, 16) quotes Governor Marriner Eccles extensively:
- "Money is created out of the right to issue credit-money."
- "When you sell bonds on the open market, you extinguish reserves."
- "If there were no debts in our money system, there would be no money."
- (1941, asked how $2 billion in 1933 securities were funded) "We created it ... Out of the right to issue credit money ... there is nothing behind it ... except our Government's credit."
- "All money is created by debt — either private or public debt."

He also quotes economist Benjamin Anderson: when the Reserve Banks buy government securities they "pay for [them] by giving checks on themselves ... and then money exists which did not exist before." Henry Ford is quoted on "the creation of inextinguishable debts."
