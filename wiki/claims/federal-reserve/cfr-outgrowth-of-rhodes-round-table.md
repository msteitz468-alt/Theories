## Claim Statement

The Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) is the American branch of a secret international network founded by Cecil Rhodes (the Round Table), aimed at world domination through control of the world's central banks; it is "even more powerful than the federal government" because its members hold "almost all of the key positions in government."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 13 summary): the Bolshevik financing "group centered mainly around a secret society created by Cecil Rhodes.... Headquartered in England, the Rhodes inner-most directorate was called the Round Table. In other countries, there were established subordinate structures called Round-Table Groups. The Round-Table Group in the United States became known as the Council on Foreign Relations. The CFR, which was initially dominated by J.P. Morgan and later by the Rockefellers, is the most powerful group in America today.... In other words, it is the United States government."

Griffin credits [[Carroll Quigley]]'s *Tragedy and Hope* as documenting that the CFR "is an outgrowth of the secret society formed by Cecil Rhodes" and describing "how an international network of financiers has created a system of financial control able to dominate the political systems of all countries through their central banks. He named names ... His book was suppressed."
