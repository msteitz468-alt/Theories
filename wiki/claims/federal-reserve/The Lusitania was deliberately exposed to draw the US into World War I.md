## Claim Statement

The 1915 sinking of the Lusitania was a deliberately engineered provocation to bring the United States into World War I: the ship was a registered armed auxiliary cruiser carrying contraband munitions, its protective escort was withdrawn by Winston Churchill, German warnings to passengers were suppressed by the U.S. State Department, and the loss was used to inflame American opinion.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 12 summary): the Lusitania "was built to military specifications and was registered with the British Admiralty as an armed auxiliary cruiser. She carried passengers as a cover to conceal her real mission, which was to bring contraband war materials.... This fact was known to Wilson and others in his administration, but they did nothing to stop it." The German embassy's warnings were suppressed except one ad in the *Des Moines Register*.

"As the Lusitania moved into hostile waters, where a German U-boat was known to be operating, First Lord of the Admiralty, Winston Churchill, ordered her destroyer protection to abandon her. This, plus the fact that she had been ordered to travel at reduced speed, made her an easy target." After one torpedo, "a mighty second explosion from within ripped her apart" — sinking her in under eighteen minutes with 195 Americans aboard. William Jennings Bryan reportedly compared the munitions-carrying to "putting women and children in front of an army."
