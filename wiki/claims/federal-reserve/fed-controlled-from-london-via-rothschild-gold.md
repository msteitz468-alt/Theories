## Claim Statement

The Federal Reserve, and through it the United States, is ultimately controlled from the City of London by a small group of London merchant banks tied to the House of Rothschild — whose daily fixing of the gold price in N.M. Rothschild's London office makes them "the final arbiters of the volume of money and the price of money" in countries including the U.S.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins (Ch. 6): "in 1910, the United States was for all practical purposes being ruled from England ... centered in the London Branch of the House of Rothschild." He names five London merchant banks that "actually control the New York banks which own the controlling interest in the Federal Reserve Bank of New York" — Schroder, Morgan Grenfell, Lazard Brothers, N.M. Rothschild, and Brown Shipley.

On gold: "Each day, representatives of four other London banking firms meet in the offices of N.M. Rothschild Company in London to fix the price of gold for that day.... Because of this factor, the London merchant bankers, with their power to set the price of gold each day, become the final arbiters of the volume of money and the price of money in those countries which must bow to their power. Not the least of these is the United States."
