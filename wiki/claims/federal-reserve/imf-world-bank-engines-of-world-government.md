## Claim Statement

The IMF and World Bank, created at Bretton Woods in 1944, were designed not merely to facilitate trade but to eliminate the gold-exchange standard and establish world socialism — the IMF evolving into a world central bank issuing a world fiat currency, and the World Bank transferring wealth via unpayable "loans" to draw nations into a world government within the UN framework.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 5 summary): the "unannounced goals ... were the elimination of the gold-exchange standard ... and the establishment of world socialism." The IMF "acting as a world central bank, would create [a world currency] out of nothing"; the World Bank would transfer money "disguised as loans ... in such a way as to insure the demise of free enterprise."

He names the theoreticians: [[John Maynard Keynes]] (Fabian Socialist) and [[Harry Dexter White]] (U.S. Treasury, "a Member of a Communist espionage ring"), "working together to bring about their mutual goal: world socialism." Ch. 6: "the conscious and deliberate evolution of the IMF/World Bank into a world central bank with the power to issue a world fiat currency ... an important step in an even larger plan to build a true world government."
