## Claim Statement

A 1966 government-sponsored think-tank study, the *Report from Iron Mountain*, concluded that war had been the only reliable means for a government to control its population, and — anticipating world government in which war would be impossible — proposed environmental catastrophe as the most promising substitute "enemy," with predictions designed "to frighten, not to inform."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 24 summary): the report's purpose was "to analyze methods by which a government can perpetuate itself in power.... It was concluded that a suitable substitute for war would require a new enemy which posed a frightful threat to survival. Neither the threat nor the enemy had to be real. They merely had to be believable." The model judged most promising was "the environmental-pollution model," because it could be tied to observable conditions and support "end-of-earth scenarios" where "accuracy in these predictions would not be important."

"While the followers of the current environmental movement are preoccupied with visions of planetary doom, the leaders have an entirely different agenda. It is world government." Griffin cites John Kenneth Galbraith as confirming the report "was totally authentic," against the government's claim that it was a hoax.

**Extraction note:** The *Report from Iron Mountain* is widely held to be a 1967 political satire by Leonard Lewin; its authenticity is disputed. Recorded as the source's assertion, without endorsement.
