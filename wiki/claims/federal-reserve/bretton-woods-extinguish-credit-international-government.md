## Claim Statement

The International Monetary Fund and World Bank (International Bank for Reconstruction and Development), created at Bretton Woods, were designed to extinguish the enormous credit produced by World War II — returning to Europe the gold sent to the US — and to advance an international currency and, with it, international government.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Mullins quotes Governor Marriner Eccles: "We must have this International Monetary Fund. An international currency is synonymous with international government." The source states the IMF and the World Bank (headed by Eugene Meyer Jr.) "were created to extinguish the enormous amount of credit created by the Second World War," paralleling how "after the First World War, we had two great depressions to extinguish the credit produced by the war."

It connects this to the League of Nations' failed gold-standard project, arguing the League failed "because it had not created an international currency" and lacked US credit, "and we had the Great Depression of 1929-31 as the inevitable result." Secretary Vinson is quoted (Bretton Woods Hearings) that the plan rejected credit creation, and Eccles's earlier statements on monetizing the public debt are tied in.

## Contexts Within the Source

The book's concluding internationalist thesis — that central banks, allied through Bretton Woods, propose "to rule unchallenged over all peoples of the world" via inextinguishable debts.
