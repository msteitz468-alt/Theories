## Claim Statement

The Federal Reserve is a banking cartel with a government facade, created at Jekyll Island to serve five objectives: (1) stop the growing competition from newer banks; (2) make the money supply elastic to recapture the industrial loan market; (3) pool reserves so all banks follow the same loan-to-deposit ratios and are protected from currency drains and bank runs; (4) shift the cartel's inevitable losses to the taxpayer; and (5) convince Congress the scheme protects the public.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Griffin (Ch. 1 summary): "What emerged was a cartel agreement with five objectives" (as stated above). He defines a cartel as businesses joining "to reduce competition and thereby increase profitability," and calls the Jekyll Island composition "a classic example of cartel structure," uniting Morgan, Rockefeller, Rothschild, Warburg, and Kuhn-Loeb against "their common enemy ... competition."

Corroborating quotations: Aldrich ("not a bank, but a cooperative union of all the banks of the country"); A. Barton Hepburn of Chase ("it will make all incorporated banks together joint owners of a central dominating power"); Aldrich (1914): "Now we are able to dominate the bank reserves of the entire country." Griffin notes the number of U.S. banks had "more than doubled to over twenty thousand" in a decade, mostly in the South and West — the competition the cartel sought to halt.

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the organizing thesis of the entire book; the Fed's documented failure to stabilize the economy is explained by the claim that stabilization "was never its true objective."
