## Claim Statement

Brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) will progress to read and write electrical signals directly to the brain's neural networks. This will enable players to experience simulated graphics, sounds, and physical sensations directly in their mind's eye, bypassing physical sensory organs (eyes, ears, skin) and manual controllers (controllers, keyboards).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Virk describes this as Stage 7 (Mind Interfaces) in Chapter 4:
> "BCIs, or brain–computer interfaces, as shown by Neuralink and other startups... can already read brain signals and determine intent... in the final stage, the joystick connection is terminated but the monkey is still able to play the game via its own brain signals."

In Chapter 1, he writes:
> "The Great Simulation is a video game that is so real because it is based on incredibly sophisticated models and rendering techniques that are beamed directly into the mind of the players."

He explains that this maps to how dreams function, where the brain generates and experiences full virtual worlds without external sensory input:
> "Pictures and sounds are projected directly into the player's mind's eye... The dream reads the player's intentions directly from the mind." (Ch. 11)
