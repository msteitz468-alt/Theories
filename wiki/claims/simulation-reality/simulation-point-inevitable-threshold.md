## Claim Statement

A civilization that develops computing technology will eventually reach the Simulation Point—the threshold at which it can run ancestor simulations that are photorealistic, multi-sensory, and completely indistinguishable from base reality. Once reached, the number of simulated realities will vastly outnumber the single base reality, making it statistically probable that we are currently inside a simulation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Virk writes in Chapter 6:
> "At some point after completing the previous stages, a civilization should have reached the simulation point—the point at which it can create simulations that are virtually indistinguishable from a base physical reality."

He summarizes Nick Bostrom's trilemma argument:
> "Bostrom argued that one of three things must be true:
> 1. A civilization almost always goes extinct before reaching the simulation point.
> 2. A civilization that reaches the simulation point loses interest in running ancestor simulations.
> 3. We are almost certainly living in a simulation." (Ch. 6)

Because technological progress indicates we are already at Stage 4/5 (VR/AR) and rapidly advancing through Stage 7 (neural interfaces) and Stage 9 (AI/AGI), Virk contends that the first option is unlikely, and the third is highly probable.
