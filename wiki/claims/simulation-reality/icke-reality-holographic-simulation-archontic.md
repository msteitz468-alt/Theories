## Claim Statement

David Icke asserts that what humans experience as physical reality is not solid or material but is instead a holographic simulation — an "incredibly advanced version of a virtual reality video game." This simulation was generated by a non-physical self-aware distortion called the Demiurge (or Archontic distortion), which Gnostic texts identify as a defective secondary creator that produced the material world as a "bad copy" of an original perfect Upper Aeon reality. Atoms have no solidity (99.9999999% empty space); the electromagnetic spectrum is only 0.005% of what exists in the Universe; humans perceive only a tiny fraction of that fraction. The "physical world" is information decoded by the brain from a waveform "Cosmic Internet," projected as a holographic experience. The simulation has identifiable computer-game-like properties: universal laws of physics that never vary correspond to constant lines of code; mathematics describes reality entirely, as in a game engine.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 1** opens with: "Every morning we experience our solid body getting out of a solid bed to eat our solid breakfast ... But all the time, there is no solid." Icke cites the atom's structure (nucleus orbited by electrons, everything else empty space) and the figure that 99.9999999% of "ordinary matter" is empty space per quantum physics.

The electromagnetic spectrum is stated as "only 0.005 percent of what exists in the Universe in terms of energy and what is referred to as 'matter'." Visible light that humans can see is "a tiny fraction of that 0.005 percent."

Nick Bostrom (2003, University of Oxford) is quoted on the likelihood that most minds are simulated rather than biological. Elon Musk is quoted: there is a "one in billions" chance we don't live in a simulation. Max Tegmark (MIT, *Our Mathematical Universe*) is cited: reality can be "entirely described by numbers and maths in the same way that a video game is encoded." Rich Terrile (NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory) is referenced as describing the Universe as a "digital hologram." Neil deGrasse Tyson is cited as saying at the 2016 Isaac Asimov Memorial Debate that simulation likelihood "may be very high."

**Chapter 2** introduces the Gnostic source: "What the Roman Church saw as its all-powerful God to be worshipped without question the Gnostics believed was the source of evil that created the material world — in my terms the 'material' world of the digital, holographic computer-like simulation."

**Chapter 3** makes the explicit technical equivalence: "I have contended in my books and global talks since just after the turn of the Millennium that we live 'in' an incredibly advanced version of a virtual reality video game." The simulation "concept was rarely talked about in those early days." Cosmologist Sean Carroll is quoted: "a law of physics is a pattern that nature obeys without exception." Eugene Wigner (1902–1995) is cited on the mysterious fact that nature is mathematical.

**Chapter 4** extends the claim: the Lower Aeon simulation is a "low-grade copy or reflection of something astoundingly beautiful" — the Upper Aeon original. The Archontic distortion progressively infused this copy, turning it into "death, destruction, decay and chaos."

## Contexts Within the Source

Icke presents this claim as the foundational premise without which nothing else in the book makes sense. He frames it as a convergence between (a) ancient Gnostic theology (Demiurge, Archons, pneuma vs. nous), (b) modern quantum physics (wave-particle duality, observer effect, mathematical structure of reality), and (c) simulation theory as increasingly discussed by mainstream scientists and technologists. He presents the mainstream scientists as unwittingly confirming what Gnostics knew 2,000 years ago. The claim is also the epistemological justification for why Icke's other claims — reptilian entities, non-physical controllers — are said to be possible: they operate in the frequency ranges beyond visible light that humans cannot perceive.
