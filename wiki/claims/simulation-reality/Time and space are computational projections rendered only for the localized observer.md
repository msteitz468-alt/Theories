## Claim Statement

Time and space do not exist as objective, continuous physical realities. Instead, they are coordinates within a computational matrix, rendered dynamically for the active observer. Relativistic effects (like time dilation) and quantum connections (like nonlocality) represent the simulation engine's calculation properties, where space is bypassed and time is adjusted relative to the observer's frame.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Virk notes in Chapter 9:
> "We actually live in a pixelated reality... space and time are not continuous but are made up of tiny, discrete pieces—Planck units—which are the pixels of the simulation."

In Chapter 8, he compares parallel universes to saved game files or multiple threads:
> "Because the simulation engine stores the database of all possible coordinates (the Alternatives Space), multiple parallel timelines can exist simultaneously as different branches in the code. Time is simply the coordinate along which the player moves. The feeling of the passage of time is a subjective projection rendered for the observer as their avatar changes position in the coordinate grid."

He explains that Einstein's theory of relativity (where time slows down as speed increases) represents the simulation's processing limitation:
> "Relativistic time dilation is similar to a computer lagging when it has to perform complex rendering calculations. The speed of light is the maximum processing speed of the processor; as an object approaches this limit, the system slows down the passage of time for that coordinate to keep the calculations consistent." (Ch. 9)
