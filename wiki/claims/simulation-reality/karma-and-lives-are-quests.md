## Claim Statement

The spiritual laws of karma, soul growth, and reincarnation are the operational rules of a multiplayer video game engine. Karma functions as an automated tracking system of player achievements, stats, and debuffs, while reincarnation represents the player "respawning" (beginning a new play session) to attempt uncompleted quests.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Virk explains in Chapter 11:
> "If we think of reincarnation and karma in terms of video games, the parallel is striking... Karma operates like a set of rules that track our actions and determine our starting conditions in the next game."

He compares the soul's journey to persistent player profiles:
> "Reincarnation represents the player starting a new 'life' (respawning) with the same character sheet... Your 'soul' is the player outside the game, and the physical body is the avatar inside the game. The choices you make in each life update the character's stats—what Eastern religions call karma." (Ch. 11)

In Chapter 15, he reiterates that this game engine is designed for spiritual evolution:
> "The Great Simulation is a video game designed for soul growth... The system tracks your score (karma) across multiple sessions (lives) until you resolve all your quests and can log out permanently (achieve liberation or moksha)."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim bridges the gap between Eastern mystical traditions (Buddhism/Hinduism) and computer science. By mapping the abstract, moralistic concept of karma to logical data-tracking structures in databases, Virk seeks to demystify reincarnation for a scientific audience.
