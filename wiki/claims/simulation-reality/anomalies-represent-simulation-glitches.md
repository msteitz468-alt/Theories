## Claim Statement

Paranormal phenomena, near-death experiences (NDEs), telekinesis, and religious visions are interactions with the simulation’s underlying software or its administrators. NDEs represent a temporary disconnect from the game server, allowing the player to return to the control room or lobby, while psychic anomalies are equivalent to inputs that bypass the local physical ruleset.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Virk notes in the Foreword to the Second Edition:
> "Many fields rejected or neglected by modern science, such as religious visions, near-death experiences, psychic phenomena, and even UFOs, can be brought under the framework proposed by Riz Virk... they suggest that the universe is somehow optimizing resources similarly to the way video games optimize computing resources."

In Chapter 10, he compares NDEs to leaving a VR play session:
> "Near-death experiences, in which the player temporarily leaves the simulation and looks back at their physical body (the avatar), represent a temporary logout... The player enters a 'lobby' or 'loading zone' (often described as a tunnel of light), reviews their play session, interacts with guides or counselors (system administrators), and is then sent back into the game."

In Chapter 13, he addresses psychic phenomena:
> "Unexplained phenomena like telekinesis, synchronicities, and precognition represent instances where the player accesses the underlying code or the server directly, bypassing the local physics engine. What we call a 'miracle' is simply an administrator or high-level player executing a cheat code or editing a database variable."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim provides a unified framework for anomalous events. Rather than dismissing them as hallucinations or invoking unprovable spiritual mechanisms, Virk uses simulation architecture to explain how such events are technically possible within a software-based universe.
