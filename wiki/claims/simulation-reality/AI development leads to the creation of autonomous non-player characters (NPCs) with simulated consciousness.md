## Claim Statement

Advancements in Artificial Intelligence (specifically moving toward Artificial General Intelligence) lead to the creation of autonomous non-player characters (NPCs) in the virtual environment. These digital entities possess simulated consciousness, behaving and reacting indistinguishably from biological human players, thereby passing the "Metaverse Turing Test."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Virk notes in Chapter 5:
> "One of the most important stages (which we’ll cover in Chapter 5...) is the development of realistic artificial beings that can fool us into thinking they are real, and perhaps can even fool themselves into thinking they are conscious beings."

In Chapter 15, he expands on this:
> "If we are in a Great Simulation, some of the characters we interact with are not played by real players outside the game; they are NPCs generated by the system itself... AI will reach a level of sophistication where simulated consciousness is indistinguishable from biological consciousness."

He defines the requirement to pass the Metaverse Turing Test:
> "Avatars in a virtual world would need to show humanlike awareness of the three-dimensional space we are in and interact with us directly, not just 'behind a curtain,' as the Turing Test implies." (Ch. 5)
