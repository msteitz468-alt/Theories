## Claim Statement

Gest argues that mid-twentieth-century education, which taught that organized civilization began only around 3000–6000 BCE with hunter-gatherers preceding it, has been overturned by late-twentieth-century archaeology. He cites Çatal Höyük (Turkey, founded 7000–8000 BCE) as an advanced early city with domesticated animals and Mother Goddess worship; megalithic temples on Malta and Gozo (from c. 5000–4500 BCE) built with advanced stone-moving and stone-cutting techniques predating Egyptian pyramid-building by roughly 1,000 years; the Minoan civilization of Crete; and anomalous artifacts (an Egyptian boomerang dated to c. 2500 BCE, and an iron hammerhead found embedded in Texas sandstone) that he says imply technologies existed thousands of years before their conventionally accepted dates.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

- "Traditional education in the mid-twentieth century taught that organized civilization started around 5,000 to 8,000 years ago—about 3000 to 6000 BCE... Immediately prior to this initial period of settlement, our ancestors were supposed to have been little more than hunter-gatherers wandering the plains, of minimum intelligence, and still wielding stone clubs."
- "Excavations at Çatal Höyük, in modern Turkey, show evidence of a very advanced community living in an early form of shallow multistory apartment blocks... founded between 7000 BCE and 8000 BCE, some 4,000 to 5,000 years prior to the development of ancient Egypt."
- On Malta/Gozo: "Even as early as 4500 BCE, they appear to have developed a concept of moving very large stones by maneuvering them on specially carved stone balls... their origins are believed to date from as early as 5000 BCE."
- "an iron hammerhead, complete with the remains of a wooden handle, was discovered in Texas in the 1930s, embedded in a lump of sandstone on a ledge near a waterfall. The implication is that such a hammer must have been made a very long time ago, possibly several thousand years prior to the supposed start of what we are educated to believe was the only Iron Age of civilization."
- "I found a cabinet displaying boomerangs, dated from around 2500 BCE and uncovered in archeological excavations in central Egypt" — used to question the uniqueness of the boomerang to Aboriginal Australia.

## Contexts Within the Source

Gest frames this material as groundwork for arguing that ancient peoples possessed sophisticated astronomical and geometric knowledge well before conventional chronology allows, which he uses to support his later claim that Moses, David, and Solomon could plausibly have drawn on genuinely ancient "sacred geometry" and solar-alignment knowledge in designing the Tabernacle, Ark, and Temple.
