## Claim Statement

Gest reports Dr. David Rohl's "new chronology" (from A Test of Time), which redates biblical figures against Egyptian pharaonic history and concludes that Saul and David were contemporaries of the heretical pharaoh Akhenaten/Amenhotep IV (dated by Rohl to 1022–1007 BCE), rather than of Rameses II as conventional chronology holds. Gest reports Rohl's argument that the Amarna Letters (clay tablets found in 1887 at Tel-el-Amarna) identify the "Habiru" mentioned in correspondence to Pharaoh as the outlawed band led by David during his exile from Saul, and identify a correspondent called "Labayu" ("Lion Man") as Saul — with tablet EA254 referring to Labayu's son being "implicated in activities of the Habiru," which Gest/Rohl read as a reference to Jonathan's collusion with David. Gest further notes the end-of-twentieth-century academic suggestion that some Psalms attributed to David were actually written by, or derived from, Akhenaten's hymns to the Sun god Aten.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

- "Dr. Rohl claims to demonstrate that specific biblical characters can be identified... Moses is established as having Pharaoh Khaneferre Sobekhotep IV (1529 BCE–1508 BCE) as his stepfather in his youth, Dudimose (who became pharaoh in 1448 BCE) as pharaoh at the time of the Exodus, and David and Saul as contemporary with the heretical pharaoh, Akhenaten/Amenhotep IV (pharaoh between 1022 BCE and 1007 BCE)."
- "in the Amarna tablets, reference is made to the Habiru. These are identified as the outlawed followers of David... in a letter from Labayu (Saul) to pharaoh, known simply as tablet EA254, David Rohl notes that Labayu's son has been 'implicated in activities of the Habiru.'"
- "At the end of the twentieth century, there was a well-founded suggestion that David was not the author of significant tracts of these poems [the Psalms], with entire sections being attributed to Akhenaten and his praise for Aten."
- Professor Robert Steven Bianchi's preface to Rohl's book, quoted by Gest: "He [Dr. David Rohl] has been courageous enough to set up a new, revolutionary historical model for critical examination by his colleagues."

## Contexts Within the Source

Gest presents Rohl's chronology as resolving discrepancies of up to 200 years between the "conventional chronology" and biblical internal timelines (e.g., 1 Kings 6:1's 480 years between Exodus and Solomon's fourth regnal year). He uses the Rohl/Akhenaten synchronism to support his broader thesis (see [[Gest argues Moses was raised as an Egyptian prince initiated into priestly ancient wisdom]]) that David, having grown up alongside Egypt's Amarna solar-monotheism controversy, designed the Jerusalem Temple to reflect solar symbolism inherited from that Egyptian religious upheaval.
