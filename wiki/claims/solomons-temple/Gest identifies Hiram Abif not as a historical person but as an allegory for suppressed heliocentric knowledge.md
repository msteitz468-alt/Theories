**Claim Statement**
Gest argues that the "Hiram Abif" of Masonic ritual — supposedly the murdered architect of Solomon's Temple whose death caused the "genuine secrets of Masonry" to be lost and replaced with "substituted secrets" until "time or circumstances shall restore the genuine" — is not a reference to a historical individual, but an allegory for the heliocentric model of the solar system. He argues the heliocentric system was understood in Solomon's time, was condemned as antireligious around 100-150 BCE in favor of the geocentric model (a substitution that persisted for nearly 2,000 years, enforced by episodes such as Galileo's torture and house arrest), and was preserved as a "trade secret" by masons under the name/legend of Hiram Abif until the heliocentric system was formally restored by later astronomy.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
- 2 Chronicles 2:13-14 is quoted describing Huram-Abi as "a man of great skill... trained to work in gold and silver, bronze and iron, stone and wood," which Gest argues describes a skilled craftsman/artisan, not an architect, and therefore cannot be the "Hiram Abif" of Masonic legend on this basis alone.
- Gest surveys prior identifications of Hiram Abif offered by other writers: an allegorical link to the burning of Knights Templar Grand Master Jacques de Molay in 1314; an idea attributed to Plato; a link (attributed to Elias Ashmole) to the beheading of Charles I in 1649; and Robert Lomas and Christopher Knight's (*The Hiram Key*) identification with the loss of Egyptian king-making knowledge following the violent death of Pharaoh Seqenenre Tao II.
- Gest quotes a paraphrased Masonic catechism: "Q. What was it that was lost? A. The genuine secrets of Masonry. Q. How came they to be lost? A. By the untimely death of our Master, Hiram Abif... Master: The substituted secrets have been communicated to me... until time or circumstances shall restore the genuine."
- He glosses "Hiram" as Hebrew for "noble" or "kingly" and "Abif" as Old French for "lost one," and argues this fits the heliocentric system as the "ruling... genuine secret."
- Historical detail is given on the suppression of heliocentricity: Aristotle's endorsement of the geocentric model (citing Pythagorean belief in "a big fire at the center of the universe"); Aristarchus's brief heliocentric revival c. 150 years after Aristotle; condemnation of heliocentricity as antireligious c. 100-150 BCE; and Galileo's torture in Venice and house arrest by Vatican order "just a few decades before" 1717.
- Gest quotes the Masonic second-degree teaching, "The Earth, constantly revolving on its axis in its orbit about the Sun," as evidence heliocentric knowledge was embedded in Masonic ceremony.
- He extends the allegory to Royal Arch/Chapter ceremony's "three principals" (Hiram King of Tyre, Solomon, Hiram Abif), reading Hiram Abif there as representing the heliocentric knowledge underlying the Temple's construction.

**Contexts Within the Source**
Gest presents this as the resolution of a puzzle he had been building toward across the book: what "danger" could have justified deliberately substituting false knowledge in Masonic ritual. He states plainly, "Hiram Abif, I submit, is not a reference to a person."

**Opposing Information Within the Source**
Gest acknowledges without endorsing several alternative identifications from other writers (Jacques de Molay, Plato, Charles I, Seqenenre Tao II) before proposing his own reading in their place.
