**Claim Statement**
Gest argues that the Cambridge Round Church (Church of the Holy Sepulchre, built c. 1130 CE by the Knights Templar in imitation of the Holy Sepulchre in Jerusalem) was deliberately designed with the same solar-calendar function he attributes to Solomon's Temple: its eight surrounding pillars encode the octagon and the Templar/Hospitaller "crux fourchette" (eight-pointed cross) emblem; its clerestory windows are positioned so that a south-facing window casts a dawn beam that moves seasonally around the nave and ambulatory (striking the center of the nave at the summer solstice, the floor-wall junction at the equinox, and traveling up the wall by the winter solstice); and the church's chancel-nave-door axis is oriented toward the summer solstice sunrise, on a bearing Gest measured at just over 45 degrees, coinciding with the Sun's rising in Gemini at the time of construction.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
- Gest describes personally visiting the Round Church repeatedly, using a compass to measure orientation and observing, on the autumn equinox at approximately 12:50 p.m. (adjusted for daylight saving), a beam of sunlight burst through the south-facing clerestory window and strike "the point where the floor of the ambulatory and the wall of the round met, on the north side of the church."
- He assesses the nave's diameter as twelve cubits (six-cubit radius) at the pillar center-line and twenty-four cubits overall, describing these as "symbolic numbers," and states the eight surrounding pillars "easily delineate the other Templar feature, the octagon."
- He connects the church's eight pillars and the Templar/Hospitaller "crux fourchette" (eight-pointed forked cross, symbolizing eight virtues: faith, charity, truth, justice, innocence, humility, sincerity, patience) to a decorative pattern he had earlier identified in the floor pavement of the Sussex Masonic Temple, arguing both encode the same octagram-derived geometry.
- Using celestial simulation software set to the church's construction epoch, Gest reports the chancel-nave-door axis (measured at just over 45 degrees) coincided with the sunrise position at the summer solstice, when the Sun rose "into the constellation of Gemini... a dual sign," which he links to the number 2/duality theme he attributes to Solomon's Temple.
- Gest states he submitted his observations on the Round Church "to the relevant authorities in Cambridge with a suggestion that the church be the subject of further investigations, including perhaps by astro-archeologists."
- He draws a parallel to Chartres Cathedral's solar/lunar alignment (championed, in his account, by St. Bernard, the same patron who championed the Knights Templar) as corroborating evidence that Gothic-era Templar-linked architecture deliberately incorporated solstice alignments.

**Contexts Within the Source**
Gest presents the Round Church findings as unplanned discoveries made during repeated personal visits over years, framed as confirmation that the solar-calendar design principles he attributes to Solomon's Temple were consciously transmitted into later Templar and Gothic architecture.

**Opposing Information Within the Source**
None presented; Gest notes some original markings may have been erased by 15th- and 19th-century modifications to the church (addition of the south aisle, north chapel, and vestry) but treats this as a limitation on further verification, not a contradiction of his thesis.
