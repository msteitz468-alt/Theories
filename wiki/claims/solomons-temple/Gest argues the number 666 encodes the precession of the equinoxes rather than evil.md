## Claim Statement

Gest asserts that the number 666, popularly associated with evil via the Book of Revelation's "number of the beast," is actually an astronomical-symbolic reference to the precession of the equinoxes: the zodiac belt shifts one constellation roughly every 2,160 years, and dropping the zero from 2,160 gives 216, which equals 6×6×6. He further claims that 666 recurs deliberately throughout the Bible in contexts connected to the Sun and to Solomon specifically, most notably as the weight of gold — 666 talents — that came to Solomon "in one year" (1 Kings 10:14), which Gest reads as a second solar symbol (gold) reinforcing the numeric one.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

- "It takes seventy-two years for the effects of precession to advance the belt just one degree. If we take the 2,160 and drop the zero, we have 216, and this is the product of 6 × 6 × 6—i.e., 666."
- "For many years this number, 666, has been associated with evil... This connection with a beast was progressively challenged... It is speculated that the reference to 'the beast' was a code in use at that time, meaning Rome and its emperor."
- "Now the weight of gold that came to Solomon in one year was Six hundred, three score, and six, talents of gold. —1 Kings 10:14. Gold at that time, partly because of its color, also symbolized the Sun. So we have two symbolic references to the Sun in one single passage of the Old Testament; gold and the number 6 × 6 × 6 = 216."
- Gest also cites Ezra 2:3 ("the children of Adonikam' six hundred, sixty and six") as an earlier biblical occurrence of the number found via an electronic word search.
- He notes the Roman numeral system's six letter-values (I, V, X, L, C, D) sum to 666, and that summing integers 1–36 also totals 666.

## Contexts Within the Source

Gest presents this as part of his broader thesis that ancient number symbolism embedded in scripture and Masonic architecture encodes solar/astronomical knowledge rather than moral or supernatural meaning. He notes the conventional Book of Revelation "number of the beast" reading (Revelation 13:18) as the received interpretation before offering his numerological alternative.
