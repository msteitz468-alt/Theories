**Claim Statement**
Gest argues that the Grand Lodge of England was formed specifically in 1717, rather than 1716 or 1718, because certain Freemasons recognized that the Hebrew characters vaw (numeral 4) and daleth (numeral 6), which visually resemble the digits "1" and "7," could be read into the number "1717" as "4646," summing (4+6+4+6) to 20 — the cubit measurement of the cube-shaped holy of holies, the equinox/solstice pillar-shadow length, and half the 40-cubit pillar-to-pillar/portico distance in his reconstruction of Solomon's Temple — and thereby deliberately "enshrined the number twenty of ancient wisdom" in the founding date, in addition to (not instead of) the conventional explanations of administrative consolidation and distancing from the Jacobite rebellion.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
- Gest notes the official explanation ("four lodges existing in London... felt that a single organization should be created") and an alternative explanation from other writers that the founding was motivated by a desire to distance London lodges "from the Jacobite rebellion."
- He observes the first Grand Master was installed on June 24, 1717, St. John the Baptist's Feast Day, "around the time of the summer solstice," which he calls "too coincidental to have been accidental."
- Gest describes encountering a table comparing Hebrew and Western numerals while researching Pythagorean arithmetic, and connects it to a line from the Second Degree Tracing Board ceremony noting that "the operative mason had his attention directed toward certain Hebrew characters" in a room of Solomon's Temple where masons received payment.
- He substitutes vaw (=4) and daleth (=6) for the "1" and "7" digits, giving 1717 → 4646 → 4+6+4+6 = 20.
- He connects 20 cubits to multiple Temple dimensions established in Chapter Thirteen and Fourteen: the holy of holies cube, the pillar-shadow lengths at equinox and solstice, and half of the 40-cubit hall length/pillar spacing, and to the number-2 "duality" symbolism (Heaven and Earth) used throughout the book.
- Appendix 3's chronology restates this: "Certain Freemasons... understood the significance of the number 20 and that certain Hebrew characters could be added together to make that number... they realized that those same Hebrew characters could also represent the year 1717... four lodges established the Grand Lodge of England in 1717... and in so doing enshrined the number twenty of ancient wisdom."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Gest presents this as "an unexpected solution" reached near the completion of his research, explicitly building on rather than replacing the conventional administrative/Jacobite explanations for 1717.

**Opposing Information Within the Source**
Gest himself notes "some documentary evidence shows that the moves to create the single governing entity originated in 1716," treating 1716 as the year of "advance planning" while 1717 was chosen for the numerological/symbolic reasons he proposes.
