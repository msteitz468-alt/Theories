**Claim Statement**
Gest sets out a Pythagorean system in which each number from 1 (monad) to 10 (decad) carries fixed symbolic meaning (e.g., the tetrad/4 as "the most perfect number... symbolic of the deity"; the hexad/6 as "the creation of the world" and "the symbol of marriage"), and argues this system, together with a related Greek/Hebrew gematria technique for converting words into numbers, underlies aspects of Masonic symbolism (e.g., a Masonic handshake allegedly symbolizing the Pythagorean decad) and can be applied to decode hidden numerical meaning in biblical names such as "Jehovah."

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
Gest reproduces, "paraphrased and edited from information provided in *The Secret Teachings of All Ages* by Manley P. Hall," the full Pythagorean numerical system from monad through decad, including characterizations such as "Ogdoad (8)—Sacred symbol of the cube" and "Ennead (9)—...regarded as the number of man because it takes nine months of gestation to receive life." He states: "there is a view that when two Freemasons shake hands they are symbolizing the Pythagorean decad. The decad can be divided into two equal portions of five, and thus two hands coming together, each with five fingers, creates the decad." Citing Hall, Gest demonstrates converting the Hebrew letters for "Jehovah" (Yod-He-Vau-He) into the numerical value 10+5+6+5=26, reducible by Pythagorean digit-summing to 2+6=8 (the ogdoad, symbol of the sacred cube, "also a symbolic representation of the world"). He quotes Hall: "Old Testament words and names, therefore, must be translated back into the early Hebrew characters and New Testament words into the Greek," and states plainly: "the Bible contains a code, a code unlocked by applying the type of process typified by the Pythagorean doctrine."

**Contexts Within the Source**
Gest presents this material as background explaining recurring numerological patterns he identifies elsewhere in the book (e.g., his treatment, at the opening of Chapter Six, of the Temple workforce totals in 1 Kings reducing via Pythagorean digit-summing to the number 6, "the value for harmony").
