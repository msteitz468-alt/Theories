## Claim Statement

Gest argues that the Ark of the Covenant's specified dimensions (1.5 cubits square by 2.5 cubits long) were not arbitrary but deliberately encode the golden proportion (phi) and a series of sacred/cosmological numbers: 1.5÷2.5 = 0.6 (phi) and 2.5÷1.5 = 1.6 (phi again); the square ends (1.5×4=6, symbolizing harmony/the six days of creation); doubling for two ends (6×2=12, months/zodiac signs); multiplying by length (12×2.5=30, lunar phases); and further combinations yielding 8 (the "sacred cube"), 10/1 (unity, the deity), and 15 (degrees of Earth's rotation per hour). He also argues the builders Bezalel and Oholiab, described in Exodus as "cunning" master craftsmen empowered to "teach others," were transmitting esoteric geometric/numerological knowledge rather than merely carpentry skill.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

- "the Ark was a relatively small wooden box, 1.5 cubits square and 2.5 cubits long... These dimensions appear to have been deliberately chosen and have links with sacred geometry, divine proportion, sacred numbers, and the value of phi (1.5 ÷ 2.5 = 0.6—the accepted value of phi; 2.5 ÷ 1.5 = 1.6—phi again)."
- "If we now take the square ends, 1.5+1.5+1.5+1.5=6—a highly revered number meaning harmony... There are two ends to the box, so 6×2=12... Then there are the two ends of the box multiplied by the length—12×2.5=30, which relates to the phases of the Moon."
- Comparing translations of Exodus 35:35: King James version, "the wisdom... of the cunning workman... even of them that do cunning work, and those that devise cunning work," versus New International Version, "He has filled them with skill to do all kinds of work as craftsmen... all of them master craftsmen and designers." Gest concludes: "their tricks of the trade appear to have extended beyond the methods of construction. They included knowledge of the ancient mysteries."

## Contexts Within the Source

Gest frames the Ark's design as continuous with, and evidence for, his broader claim that Moses (educated in Egyptian priestly "ancient wisdom") deliberately embedded geometric and cosmological symbolism into the Ark, the Tabernacle, and — by inheritance through David — Solomon's Temple.
