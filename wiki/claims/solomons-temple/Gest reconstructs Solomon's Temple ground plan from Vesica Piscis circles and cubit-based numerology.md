**Claim Statement**
Gest argues that the builders of Solomon's Temple laid out the ground plan using a sequence of overlapping ten-cubit-radius circles in Vesica Piscis form (five circles for the 60x20-cubit outline, extended to six circles once the ten-cubit portico is added), that the 30-cubit height was set using the "mason's secret square" (a square reduced by half stacked atop a base square), and that each resulting dimension encodes Pythagorean-style numerology (e.g., the radius of 10 "drops the zero" to 1, symbolizing the deity; the diameter of 20 becomes 2, symbolizing the duality of Heaven and Earth; 60 becomes 6, harmony; the cube of the holy of holies, 20x20x20, reduces to 2x2x2=8, the sacred cube).

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**
- 1 Kings 6 is cited for the stated internal dimensions: 60 cubits long, 20 cubits wide, hall 40 cubits, holy of holies 20 cubits, height 30 cubits, and the portico "extended the width of the Temple... and projected ten cubits" (1 Kings 6:3).
- Gest describes a step-by-step reconstruction: setting an east-west axis with two spears aligned to the equinox sunrise, then striking five ten-cubit-radius circles in Vesica Piscis to generate the 60x20-cubit outline, with a sixth circle added for the portico ("the design had been based on an overall length dictated by six circles... God created heaven and earth in six days").
- The 30-cubit height is derived via the "mason's secret square" — one square (20 cubits) plus "half a square on top" (10 cubits) = 30 cubits — described as an alternative to a simpler two-circle vesica method that "seemed just too simple."
- Numerological readings are given throughout: radius 10 → 1 (unity/deity); diameter 20 → 2 (duality, Heaven and Earth); length 60 → 6 (harmony); 40 → 4 (the four elements); the cube 20x20x20 → 2x2x2=8 (Pythagoras's "sacred cube," symbolizing the world/Heaven per Bede).
- The oracle's stated height of 120 cubits (per "the Book of Paralipomenon") is reinterpreted not as a literal superstructure but as the sum of the triangular number series 1 through 15 (=120), tied to the cherubim's 5-cubit wingspans and the number 15's link to the 24 divisions of the Earth's rotation and the mid-point of the lunar month.

**Contexts Within the Source**
Gest presents this as a deliberate exercise in "thinking like" Bronze Age builders using "basic and uncomplicated construction methods" rather than reproducing conventional architectural reconstructions found in "myriad other books." He states his method was to apply the geometric principles about Vesica Piscis and Pythagorean number-symbolism established earlier in the book.

**Opposing Information Within the Source**
Gest notes his own uncertainty between two possible height-construction methods (a simple two-circle vesica approach versus the "mason's secret square") and states he adopted the square method as his "primary choice" without claiming certainty.
