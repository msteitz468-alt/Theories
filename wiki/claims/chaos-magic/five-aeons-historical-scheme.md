## Claim Statement

Carroll proposes that human consciousness has evolved through four historical aeons — Animism/Shamanism, Paganism, Monotheism, and Atheism — and that a fifth Chaoist aeon is now embryonically developing. This fifth aeon, he holds, represents "a return to the consciousness of the first aeon but in a higher form," with Chaoist philosophy and psychic powers again becoming dominant intellectual and social forces.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

*Liber NOX*, "The Millennium" (the primary statement):

> "Humanity has evolved through four major states of consciousness, or aeons, and a fifth is on the horizon. The first aeon comes out of the mists of time. It was an age of animism, shamanism, and magic when the rulers of humans had a firm grasp of the psychic forces."

The section traces the second (pagan), third (monotheistic), and fourth (atheistic) aeons in succession, then states:

> "The evolution of consciousness is cyclic in the form of an upward spiral. The fifth aeon represents a return to the consciousness of the first aeon but in a higher form. Chaoist philosophy will again become a dominant intellectual and moral force."

Carroll offers specific predictions under five headings about how the transition will unfold: "The Death of Spirituality," "The Death of Superstition," "The Death of Identity," "The Death of Belief," and "The Death of Ideology" — describing the dissolution of fixed systems in favor of what he calls emotional, magical, transmutation, psychological, and political "technologies."

*Liber AOM*, "Aeonics" adds the role of the adept:

> "The fifth magical aeon exists only in embryonic form. Its precise manifestation hangs in the balance. The new Chaoist aeon may develop into an Aquarian Age or a time of totalitarian tyranny … As agents of the fifth aeon Illuminati, adepts may occasionally break their meditation to alter the course of history."

Carroll also states that the erection of chaospheres "will tend to hasten the immanentization of the eschaton, the change of aeon."

## Contexts Within the Source

- *Liber NOX*, "Random Belief" — the six "dice options" (Paganism, Monotheism, Atheism, Nihilism, Chaoism, Superstition) form an experiential map of the aeonic belief cycle.
- *Psychonaut*, "New Aeon Magic" — frames the current moment as a rupture demanding a new magical spirituality, corroborating the aeonic narrative.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Carroll openly acknowledges the fifth aeon could fail or be delayed: "Either that or a new dark age." He also notes that cultures at different stages coexist, so aeons do not cleanly supersede one another: "most have never completely freed themselves of the residues of the past."
