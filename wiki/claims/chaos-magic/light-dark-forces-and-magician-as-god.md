## Claim Statement

Carroll holds that chaos gives rise to two irreducible prime forces — a light power and a dark power — and that the magician's highest goal is not to follow either but to master both, thereby becoming "a center of creation and destruction, a living manifestation of the chaos force within the realm of duality, a complete microcosm, a god."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The key passage occurs in *Liber AOM*, "The Work of the Adept":

> "For out of chaos arise the two prime forces of existence, the solve et coagula of existence. The light power and the dark. The light power is the expanding, outgoing, dualizing, increasing expression of chaos, responsible for the new birth, creation, incarnation, and variety. The dark power is the contracting, returning, transcending, withdrawing expression of chaos, responsible for death, dissolution, reabsorption, simplicity, and return to the source."

Carroll further holds that mystics err by following one pole exclusively, while the magician transcends this:

> "The positive and negative paths of magic converge and form a unity which itself diverges from the mystic paths. While mysticism is at the root, concerned with following either the light power or the dark, magic aims to play one off against the other. The magician aims to become a center of creation and destruction, a living manifestation of the chaos force within the realm of duality, a complete microcosm, a god."

The "Alphabet of Desire" in *Liber NOX* corroborates this at the emotional level: the table labeled "Emotional Duality" maps all emotions onto the twin principles of *Coagula* (attraction, coming together) and *Solve* (repulsion, separation), and Carroll writes: "The force which creates is also that which destroys."

## Contexts Within the Source

- *Liber AOM*, "The Work of the Adept" — the primary statement of the light/dark dyad and the magician-as-god thesis.
- *Liber NOX*, "The Alphabet of Desire" — emotional correlates of *solve et coagula* pervade the dual alphabet system.
- *Liber NOX*, "Liber NOX" (opening) — the spirit of "black magic" is framed as requiring acceptance of duality: "Eternal warfare is the price, purpose, and reward of existence."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Carroll acknowledges that the labels "light" and "dark" are morally arbitrary: "Adherents of one will always call the other black. Thus, the expansion into existence can as easily be called the dark ascent into matter, and the withdrawal from existence can be called the return to the light. But this is mere moral philosophy and ultimately devoid of meaning." The claim is thus descriptive of cosmic forces, not a moral prescription.
