## Claim Statement

Carroll holds that the single most valuable magical capacity is not power over external events but
the ability to change oneself at will. The Neophyte syllabus (Liber MMM) establishes metamorphosis
— the willed restructuring of the mind — as the foundation for all subsequent magical work. This
restructuring requires confronting and exploiting dualism: every virtue implies its opposite, and
no one-sided self-improvement is possible. Rather than pursuing a fixed ideal, the chaos magician
aims to achieve "any arbitrary perceptual perspective at will." Habits (thought, word, and deed)
are the anchor of personality; the magician systematically breaks and replaces them to loosen
the ego's grip. The same principle is extended in Liber LUX through liberation techniques
(sacrilege, heresy, iconoclasm, anathemism) and culminates in the Augoeides operation, where
the magician seeks to strip away "excess" to reveal the true will beneath.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"Even a slight ability to change oneself is more valuable than any power over the external
universe. Metamorphosis is an exercise in willed restructuring of the mind." (Liber MMM,
"Metamorphosis")

"All attempts to reorganize the mind involve a duality between conditions as they are and the
preferred condition. Thus, it is impossible to cultivate any virtue like spontaneity, joy… without
involving oneself in more conventionality, sorrow, guilt… High magic recognizes the dualistic
condition but does not care whether life is bittersweet or sweet and sour; rather it seeks to
achieve any arbitrary perceptual perspective at will." (Liber MMM, "Metamorphosis")

"Select any minor habit at random and delete it from your behavior; at the same time, adopt any
new habit at random… By persisting with such simple beginnings, you become capable of virtually
anything." (Liber MMM, "Metamorphosis")

"The personality, a mask of convenience, becomes stuck to the face… The most powerful minds cling
to the fewest fixed principles. The only clear view is from atop the mountain of your dead
selves." (Liber LUX, "Anathemism")

"As reorganization of belief is the key to liberation, so is reorganization of desire the key to
will." (Liber LUX, "Enchantment")

## Contexts Within the Source

Metamorphosis is introduced in Liber MMM as the third pillar of the Neophyte syllabus alongside
mind control and basic magic. Carroll places it between trance training and magical technique to
emphasize that self-transformation underlies all outward magical work. In Liber LUX, liberation
and anathemism extend this teaching to social and philosophical conditioning. The Augoeides
chapter provides the culminating form: a daily invocation aiming at progressive metamorphosis
toward one's "true will."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Carroll cautions that premature or unbalanced self-transformation carries real risk. The Augoeides
section warns that "if any unnecessary or imbalanced scraps of ego become identified with the
genius by mistake, then disaster awaits. The life force flows directly into these complexes and
bloats them into grotesque monsters, one of which is the demon Choronzon." Laughter and
non-attachment are prescribed as antidotes to the "imbalance and possible madness" that trance
work and magical obsession can produce.
