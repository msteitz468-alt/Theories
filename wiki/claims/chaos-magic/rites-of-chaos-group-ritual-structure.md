## Claim Statement

Carroll holds that five rites — the Mass of Chaos, Initiation, Exorcism, Extreme Unction, and
Ordination — constitute a sufficient ritual toolkit for the chaos priest, together covering "most
situations that the priests of chaos may encounter." Each rite is explicitly scalable: "Each may
be performed by a single operator or by an unlimited number of participants and assistants."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

From the "Rites of Chaos" chapter of [[Liber Null & Psychonaut (Carroll)]]:

> "Five rites based on the principles of gnostic new aeon shamanism are presented here to cover
> most situations that the priests of chaos may encounter."

The Mass of Chaos is defined as having six minimum required parts (preparation, statement of
intent, invocation of chaos, invocation of Baphomet, oath, and closing), making it one of the
more formally structured rites in the chaos magic corpus. For initiation, Carroll insists on
four required elements — Ordeal, Empowerment, Induction, and The Unexpected — while rejecting
fixed formulae: "Any order which attempts to initiate by a set formula is displaying a remarkable
lack of perception and imagination."

On exorcism, Carroll presents three competing models (religious, psychiatric, shamanic) and
advocates the shamanic method of personifying and confronting the candidate's demons directly.

On extreme unction, the magician acts as psychopomp, delivering guidance telepathically if
necessary: the essential points are freedom from fear, rejection of illusory visions, and the
choice between reabsorption and separate reincarnation.

## Contexts Within the Source

This material occupies the opening chapters of the *Psychonaut* section and is presented as
the practical liturgical output of the theoretical framework developed in *Liber Null*. The rites
presuppose mastery of [[Gnosis (chaos magic)]] and optionally of [[Chemognosis]]. The Mass of
Chaos functions as the master rite that ordination requires the candidate to be able to perform
for invocation, enchantment, and consecration.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Carroll explicitly excludes rites for the creation of adepts or masters: "No rites are given for
the creation of adepts or masters, for each seeker must devise his own entrance into these grades
and await peer recognition." This places a principled ceiling on formalized ritual: the highest
magical grades cannot be conferred ceremonially.
