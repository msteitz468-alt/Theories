## Claim Statement

Carroll holds that the attachment of consciousness to a desired outcome — which he calls "lust of
result" — is the primary mechanism by which magical workings fail. When a desire is held in
awareness it becomes entangled with ego, anxiety about failure, and an unconscious counter-will
not to succeed (arising from the mind's dual nature). The solution is to encode desire into an
abstract form — a sigil or spell — which is then deliberately forgotten, so that the will can
operate below the threshold of conscious interference. The oft-observed phenomenon that wished-for
outcomes appear only after they have been forgotten is, for Carroll, the empirical proof of this
principle.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"If an attempt is made to focus on some form of desire, the effect is short-circuited by lust of
result. Egotistical identification, fear of failure, and the reciprocal desire not to achieve
desire, arising from our dual nature, destroy the result." (Liber MMM, "The Magical Trances")

"The mere act of wanting is rarely effective, as the will becomes involved in a dialogue with the
mind… Often the wished-for result arises only when it has been forgotten. This last fact is the
key to sigils and most forms of magic spell. Sigils work because they stimulate the will to work
subconsciously, bypassing the mind." (Liber MMM, "Sigils")

"Non-attachment/non-disinterest best describes the magical condition of acting without lust of
result. It is very difficult for humans to decide on something and then to do it purely for its
own sake. Yet it is precisely this ability which is required to execute magical acts." (Liber MMM,
"Metamorphosis")

"Will is not willpower, virility, obstinacy, or hardness. Will is unity of desire." (Liber LUX,
"Enchantment")

## Contexts Within the Source

This thesis appears at the core of three sections: the rationale for meaningless concentration
objects in trance training, the three-stage sigil procedure (construct / lose / charge), and the
discussion of will in enchantment. The metamorphosis section also frames non-attachment as a
practical mental habit to cultivate, partly as an antidote to this tendency.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Carroll does not claim that desire must be entirely absent — only that it must not be active in
conscious awareness during the working. The metamorphosis prescription of "non-attachment /
non-disinterest" insists that one must not lose interest in the desired outcome, only loosen the
grasping quality. The Augoeides invocation section notes that one's "dominant obsessions or
personality are quite arbitrary, and hence more easily banished" — framing the lust-of-result
problem as solvable through sustained self-knowledge.
