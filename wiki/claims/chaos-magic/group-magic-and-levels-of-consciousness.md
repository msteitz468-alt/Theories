## Claim Statement

Carroll holds that properly synchronized group magical work produces synergistic effects exceeding the sum of individual contributions, and that human consciousness operates across five distinct states — unconsciousness, dreaming, robotic, awareness, and gnosis — with gnosis being the uniquely magical state and the robotic level being undervalued even by mystics.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Group magic.** *Psychonaut*, "Group Magical Experiments":

> "The purpose of structuring group activity with ritual is to generate more power than individual efforts might achieve. Synergistic effects will come into play in a properly synchronized working, and the collective power will exceed the sum of individual powers participating."

Carroll details four experimental domains for group work: psychism (especially synchronized telepathy projection), ritual (enchantment, evocation, invocation, divination), trance (hypnotic states directed by an operator), and dream (the "astral sabbat," in which participants arrange to meet in shared dreaming at a known physical location). He notes that a single well-synchronized projector outperforms "a poorly synchronized mob."

**Five levels of consciousness.** *Psychonaut*, "Levels of Consciousness":

> "It is evident that consciousness occurs on a scale of five states, herewith: gnosis, awareness, robotic, dreaming, unconsciousness."

Carroll's account of each level:
- *Unconsciousness*: "allows the body to rest and keeping the organism out of harm's way."
- *Dreaming*: "allows the mind to digest conscious experience … also provides a window into the psychic dimension."
- *Robotic*: automatic behavior; also "the level that inspiration or clairvoyant impressions often intrude into" and the level from which enchantments are partly cast.
- *Awareness*: nonautomatic, intelligent response; "of very little use in performing magic."
- *Gnosis*: "intense consciousness of something … the very font of magical powers and mystic states of consciousness. Magically, gnosis is the state which most easily allows the will and perception to reach out and touch realities beyond the mind."

Carroll explicitly rehabilitates the robotic level against mystic denigration: "Most of the world's geniuses have had some sort of robotic hobby or distraction, which they use to create a vacuum in their awareness into which something useful could manifest."

He concludes that the Kia "should be able to find expression at any level" and that magical training should "open up the neglected dream level … add new programs to [the robotic level] … teach the method of turning awareness on or off at will, and of entering the gnostic level and acting within it."

## Contexts Within the Source

- *Psychonaut*, "New Aeon Magic" — the experimental, technique-first orientation motivates both the group work program and the systematic mapping of consciousness levels.
- *Liber NOX*, "Ecstasy" — the quadriga sexualis section provides the individual gnostic techniques that group work scales up.
- *Liber NOX*, "Gnosis" (implied throughout) — [[Gnosis (chaos magic)]] is the shared operative concept for both individual and group work.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Carroll warns that trance experiments can have dangerous side effects: "the operator's influence over the subject may gradually extend itself to nontrance states as well" and "memory and imagination can become very active in trance states and begin to delude both operator and subject." He recommends trance experiments be "performed infrequently and for objective results only." He also concedes that gnosis can lead mystics into "three common errors" (illusions of unity, self-annihilation, or supreme importance of the object).
