## Claim Statement

Carroll holds that the magician must be able to "act without belief or being able to temporarily
invest belief in anything." No paradigm — whether animist, scientific, or mystical — is granted
permanent allegiance; each is adopted as a working hypothesis when it is operationally useful and
set aside thereafter. The universe "will tend to provide confirmation of any paradigm one chooses
to live in," so paradigm choice is itself a magical act. Carroll presents six paradigms (the
chaoetheric, probability manipulation, morphic field theory, observer-created universe, holographic
universe, and higher dimensionality) as equally valid magical frameworks, none of which he
considers metaphysically final.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

From the "Magical Paradigms" chapter of [[Liber Null & Psychonaut (Carroll)]]:

> "Every system of thought and understanding stems from a number of basic postulates about the
> universe and humanity's relationship to it. These ideas and assumptions go to make up the
> paradigm or dominant worldview through which a culture or an individual interacts with its
> universe."

> "The universe (being the accommodating creature that she is) will tend to provide confirmation
> of any paradigm one chooses to live in. We are, to an extent, in an observer-created universe."

> "To be able to operate in all spheres, the magician must master the art of either acting without
> belief or being able to temporarily invest belief in anything. The magician should be equally at
> home with a crozier, a paintbrush, a test tube, or a wand."

On the six paradigms: "Six alternative magical paradigms appear below, and they are indeed a
strange mixture of sorcery and hyperscience. All of them seem a little crazy from our normal point
of view, but our normal point of view also proves to be pretty strange on close inspection."

## Contexts Within the Source

The "Magical Paradigms" chapter follows "Occult Priestcraft" and "Magical Weapons," and precedes
"Anecdotes" and "Catastrophe Theory and Magic." It functions as the epistemological centerpiece of
*Psychonaut*, grounding the eclectic, anti-dogmatic stance of [[Chaos Magic]] in a coherent
theoretical framework. The six paradigms share two structural features: (a) action at a distance
in space or time, and (b) a microcosm–macrocosm equivalence.

Carroll also notes a strategic argument for keeping magic occult: "Humanity has proved itself
totally incapable of handling even a moderately dangerous substance like plutonium with
responsibility. Imagine what it would do with machine-enhanced sorcery or even simple, reliable
telepathy. It is in the interests of the survival of the species that occultists continue to
ridicule and discredit their own arts in the eyes of orthodox science."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Carroll states a personal preference for the higher dimensionality paradigm while acknowledging
it as merely a preference. He frames all six as approximations: "There are alternatives to
irrationalism when it comes to erecting the theoretical basis of a magical modus operandi."
