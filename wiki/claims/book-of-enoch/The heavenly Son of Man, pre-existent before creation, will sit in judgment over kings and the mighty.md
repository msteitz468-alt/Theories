## Claim Statement

The Book of Enoch's Parables (ch. 37–71) teach that a heavenly being — called both the **Son of Man** and the **Elect One** — exists in the presence of the Lord of Spirits (God) and will descend to execute judgment over all earthly rulers at the end of days. The key doctrines:

### Pre-existence Before Creation

"Even before the sun and the signs were created, before the stars of heaven were made, His name was named before the Lord of spirits" (ch. 48:3). The Son of Man was "hidden" by God before the creation of the world and "preserved… in the presence of His might" (ch. 62:7). His name was spoken before creation; his role was ordained from the beginning.

### Appearance and Identity

Enoch first encounters him alongside the "Head of Days" (the Ancient of Days figure): "One whose face looked ancient. His head was white like wool, and with Him was another being whose countenance had the appearance of a man, and his face was full of graciousness, like one of the holy angels" (ch. 46:1). The interpreting angel identifies this as "the Son of Man who hath righteousness, with whom dwells righteousness, and who reveals all the treasures of that which is hidden, because the Lord of spirits hath chosen him" (ch. 46:3).

He is also called the **Elect One**, seen dwelling "under the wings of the Lord of spirits" (ch. 39:7), chosen before creation and seated on the throne of glory to judge all things.

### Judgment of Kings and the Mighty

The Parables are insistent and detailed on the fate of earthly power before the Son of Man:

- He "shall raise up the kings and the mighty from their seats, and the strong from their thrones and shall loosen the reins of the strong, and break the teeth of the sinners" (ch. 46:4).
- "He shall put down the kings from their thrones and kingdoms because they do not exalt and praise Him" (ch. 46:5).
- On the great day, "all the kings and the mighty and all who possess the earth shall stand up and shall see and recognize that He sits on the throne of His glory" (ch. 62:3).
- The kings beg for mercy but find none: "We have not believed in Him nor glorified the name of the Lord of spirits, but our hope was in the scepter of our kingdom, and in our own glory" (ch. 63:7). They are "driven away" and find no respite.
- Earthly wealth, gold, and silver will not save them: "None shall be saved, either by gold or by silver" (ch. 52:7); the mountains of metals (iron, copper, silver, gold, soft metal, lead) will melt "like wax before a fire in the presence of the Elect One" (ch. 52:6).

### The Elect One Seated on the Throne

The act of the Elect One being placed on the throne of glory by the Lord of Spirits (ch. 45:3, 51:3, 61:8, 62:2) is the defining eschatological moment in the Parables. When he sits, "the spirit of righteousness was poured out on Him, and the word of His mouth slays all the sinners" (ch. 62:2). Angels of punishment have prepared instruments for the kings and powerful (ch. 53:5); after the Elect One's judgment, the righteous and holy enter into rest and dwell with the Son of Man "for ever and ever" (ch. 62:14).

### Enoch as the Son of Man (ch. 70–71)

In the final and most surprising turn of the Parables, Enoch's spirit is transported to heaven of heavens, and an angel announces: "This is the Son of Man who is born to righteousness, and righteousness abides over him, and the righteousness of the Head of Days forsakes him not" (ch. 71:14). Enoch himself is identified with the Son of Man — a development that later Jewish mysticism (particularly 3 Enoch) built into the full Metatron doctrine.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Parables show the kings and mighty requesting mercy but being denied it — not because the Lord of Spirits is capricious, but because they consistently "denied the Lord of spirits" and placed their faith in wealth and man-made gods (ch. 46:7, ch. 63:7). The text presents this judgment as just and long-delayed rather than arbitrary.
