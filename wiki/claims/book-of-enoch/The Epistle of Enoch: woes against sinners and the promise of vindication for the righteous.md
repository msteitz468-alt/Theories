## Claim Statement

The Book of Enoch teaches, through the extended address comprising chapters 94–108, a stark moral dualism: the rich, violent, and deceitful are catalogued in a litany of "woes" that promise their imminent destruction, while the suffering righteous are assured that their deeds are recorded in heaven, that angels intercede on their behalf, and that they will shine as lights and receive eternal vindication. The Epistle concludes with a vision of the place of punishment for sinners, a blessing for those who loved righteousness, and an exhortation for the righteous to copy and preserve Enoch's words faithfully.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**The woe-series against the wealthy and violent (chs. 94–99):** Enoch delivers a sustained series of prophetic woes addressed to distinct categories of sinner:

- Against builders of unrighteousness: "Woe to those who build unrighteousness and oppression and lay deceit as a foundation; for they shall be suddenly overthrown, and they shall have no peace" (ch. 94:6, line 3257).
- Against the rich: "Woe to you, you rich, for you have trusted in your riches, and from your riches shall you depart, because you have not remembered the Most High in the days of your riches…He who hath created you will overthrow you, and for your fall there shall be no compassion, and your Creator will rejoice at your destruction" (ch. 94:8–10, lines 3265–3269).
- Against lying witnesses and the unjust: "Woe to you, lying witnesses, and to those who weigh out injustice, for you shall suddenly perish" (ch. 95:6, line 3301).
- Against persecutors: "Woe to you, sinners, for you persecute the righteous; for you shall be delivered up and persecuted because of injustice, and your yoke shall be heavy on you" (ch. 95:7, line 3303).
- Against those who acquire gold through unrighteousness: "Woe to you who acquire silver and gold in unrighteousness and say: 'We have become rich with riches and have possessions'…your lies shall flow away; for your riches shall not abide but quickly depart from you" (ch. 97:8–10, lines 3349–3357).
- Against those who oppress the righteous and eat blood: "Woe to you, you obstinate of heart, who work wickedness and eat blood…you shall have no peace" (ch. 98:11, line 3391).
- Against those who pervert righteous words: "Woe to you who say the words of the wise are empty" (ch. 98:14, line 3407).
- Against those who write lying words: "Woe to you who write down lying and godless words; for they write down their lies so that men may hear them and act godlessly towards their neighbor. Therefore they shall have no peace but die a sudden death" (ch. 98:15, line 3409).

**The sin is humanity's own creation:** Chapter 98 contains a notable doctrinal statement: "sin has not been sent on the earth, but man of himself has created it, and they that commit it shall fall under a great curse" (ch. 98:4, line 3373). This is a direct claim that evil is not imposed from outside but is a human product — a significant theological assertion within the Enochian corpus.

**All sins are recorded in heaven:** The Epistle insists repeatedly on the heavenly ledger: "your evil deeds are revealed in heaven, and none of your wrong deeds are covered and hidden…all your wrongdoing that you do will be written down every day, until the day of your judgment" (ch. 98:6–8, lines 3377–3381). Sinners cannot plead ignorance; "light and darkness, day and night, see all your sins" (ch. 104:8, line 3643).

**The righteous dead — their vindication assured (chs. 102–104):** The Epistle addresses a specific pastoral concern: the righteous suffer in life and die without visible reward. Sinners mock: "As we die, so die the righteous, and what benefit do they reap for their deeds?" (ch. 102:6, line 3565). The Book of Enoch answers: "I have read the heavenly tablets, and have seen the holy books, and have found written in it and inscribed regarding them: That all goodness and joy and glory are prepared for them, and written down for the spirits of those who have died in righteousness" (ch. 103:1–3, lines 3583–3587). The righteous dead shall "live and rejoice, and your spirits shall not perish, nor shall your memory from before the face of the Great One to all the generations of the world" (ch. 103:4, line 3589). Chapter 104 adds that in heaven "the angels remember you for good before the glory of the Great One. And your names are written before the glory of the Great One…you shall shine as the lights of heaven" (ch. 104:1–2, line 3629).

**The voice of oppressed righteous (ch. 103:9–15):** A sustained lament is given in the first person for the righteous poor who toiled, were oppressed, cried to rulers, and received no justice: "We hoped to be the head and have become the tail…we have been destroyed and have not found any to help us even with a word…They helped those who robbed us and devoured us…and they concealed their murder" (ch. 103:11–15, lines 3607–3619). This is answered by the assurance that their rulers will be held accountable.

**Warning against corrupting Enoch's words (ch. 104:10–13):** "I know this mystery, that sinners will alter and pervert the words of righteousness in many ways, and will speak wicked words, and lie, and practice great deceits, and write books concerning their words. But when they write down all my words truthfully in their languages, and do not change or omit any of my words…then, I know another mystery, that books will be given to the righteous and the wise to produce joy and righteousness and much wisdom" (ch. 104:10–12, lines 3647–3651).

**The miraculous birth of Noah (chs. 106–107):** The Epistle includes a fragment in which Lamech's newborn son has white skin, white hair, eyes "like the rays of the sun," levitates in the midwife's hands, and blesses the Lord — causing his father to fear the child is of angelic origin. Enoch confirms the child is Lamech's son, but discloses that in the generation of Jared "some of the angels of heaven violated the word of the Lord…had sex with women and committed sin with them…and they shall produce on the earth giants not according to the spirit, but according to the flesh." The child is to be named Noah, who "shall be left to you, and he and his sons shall be saved from the destruction" (ch. 106:16, line 3709).

**The place of punishment (ch. 108):** The closing chapter offers Enoch's vision of the sinners' fate: a chaotic desert with blazing fire and "things like shining mountains circling and sweeping back and forth," from which comes the sound of weeping, moaning, and agony. An angel explains this is "where the spirits of sinners and blasphemers, and of those who pervert everything that the Lord hath spoken through the mouth of the prophets" are cast (ch. 108:6, line 3741). In contrast, God promises to bring out in shining light those who loved his holy name and "seat each on the throne of his honor" (ch. 108:12, line 3755).

## Cross-source connections

The Epistle's polemic against wealthy oppressors who go unpunished in this life but face eschatological reckoning is closely paralleled in the material surveyed in [[The Book of Enoch: Watchers, Nephilim, and apocalyptic revelation]]. The warning that scribes will corrupt Enoch's words is notable in relation to broader claims about suppressed or altered ancient texts, a theme discussed in [[Escaping from Eden (Wallis)]] and [[The Gods of Eden (Bramley)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Lumpkin edition's translation includes frequent inline biblical cross-references (James 5, Matthew 24, 2 Thessalonians 1, Revelation 14, etc.) which are editorial overlays, not part of the original text. The Noah birth-fragment (chs. 106–107) is headed in the edition as a "Fragment of the Book of Noah," suggesting it may be a separate source incorporated into 1 Enoch rather than original to it. Chapter 105, very brief, has Jesus-reminiscent language ("I and my son will be united with them for ever in the paths of righteousness") that scholars have flagged as a possible Christian interpolation; it is presented without comment in the Lumpkin text.
