## Claim Statement

The Book of Enoch singles out **Azazel** as the Watcher most responsible for corrupting humanity. He taught:

- How to make **swords, knives, shields, and breastplates**
- **Metallurgy** — working metals of the earth
- **Jewelry and ornamentation** — bracelets and ornaments
- **Cosmetics** — use of antimony, beautifying of eyelids, precious stones and dyes

The text characterises these disclosures as revealing "eternal secrets which were made and kept in heaven, which men were striving to learn" (ch. 9:6). The result was "great impiety" — people turned away from God, committed fornication, "were led astray, and became corrupt in all their ways" (ch. 8:2).

The Lord's verdict, delivered through the archangel Gabriel to the heavenly court, is unambiguous: "The whole earth has been corrupted through the works that were taught by Azazel: to him ascribe ALL SIN" (ch. 10:8, capitalisation as in the source text).

### Binding in the Desert of Dudael (ch. 10, 13)

Enoch delivered the divine sentence to Azazel personally: "Azazel, you shall have no peace: a severe sentence has been passed against you that you should be bound: And you shall not have rest or mercy, because of the unrighteousness which you have taught" (ch. 13:1–2).

The Lord's command to Raphael was to:
1. Bind Azazel hand and foot
2. Cast him into a split in the desert of **Dudael**
3. Fill the hole with rough and jagged rocks
4. Cover him with darkness so he cannot see light
5. Keep him there until the Day of the Great Judgment, when "he shall be hurled into the fire" (ch. 10:4–6)

In the Parables (ch. 54–55), the chains prepared for Azazel's hosts are revisited: angels of punishment are seen preparing iron chains for "the hosts of Azazel" to be thrown into the pit of hell, and the Lord's Elect One will judge Azazel and all his associates on the great day (ch. 55:4).

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Enoch text is unambiguous in its moral condemnation of Azazel's teachings — they are presented as unambiguously evil gifts that caused earth's corruption. No text within the Book of Watchers presents Azazel's disclosures as beneficial or as civilisational progress. The Blavatsky reinterpretation is external to this source.
