# 2 Enoch narrates Enoch's ascent through ten heavens conducted by angels

## Claim Statement

2 Enoch narrates that while Enoch sleeps in his house at age 365, two enormous, shining angels appear at his bedside and tell him that the eternal God has sent them to take him up to heaven. They lift him on their wings and conduct him progressively through ten heavens, each revealing a different cosmic and moral reality. The ascent culminates in Enoch's transfiguration into an angelic being in the presence of God on the tenth heaven.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 1 (opening)**: Two angels of extraordinary appearance — faces shining like the sun, eyes like burning light, fire proceeding from their lips — appear to Enoch and announce: "The eternal God sent us to you, and you shall ascend today with us into heaven."

**Chapter 3**: Angels take Enoch on their wings to the first heaven, where he sees a great cosmic sea and storehouses of snow (Ch. 5) and dew (Ch. 6), guarded by angels.

**Chapter 7**: Second heaven — fallen angelic apostates held in darkness, weeping, awaiting judgment. They are described as those who "took counsel with their own will, and turned away with their prince, who is also held captive in the fifth heaven." Enoch pities them but cannot pray for them.

**Chapter 8–10**: Third heaven — paradise in the south (tree of life, rivers of milk/honey/oil/wine, 300 radiant angels; prepared for the righteous) and a place of torment on the north side (fire, frost, darkness; prepared for the wicked).

**Chapter 11–15**: Fourth heaven — the courses of the sun and moon, angelic armies, Phoenixes and Chalkydri (equated with Seraphim/serpents) attending the sun, six solar gates in east and west marking a 365.25-day solar year.

**Chapter 17**: Brief note on angelic soldiers in the middle heavens, singing continuously.

**Chapter 18**: Fifth heaven — the **Grigori** (Greek transliteration of the Hebrew *Watchers*), giants of human appearance, withered and silent, mourning their brethren who "went down to the place Erman [Mount Hermon], broke through their vows, saw the daughters of men how good they are, and took wives, and fouled the earth with their deeds, producing giants." Enoch urges them to resume service before God; they break their silence and sing.

**Chapter 19**: Sixth heaven — seven bands of archangels governing stars, seasons, rivers, seas, fruits, grass, and all living things; recording the deeds of all human souls.

**Chapter 20–21**: Seventh heaven — Cherubim, Seraphim, thrones; Enoch left alone, afraid; Gabriel lifts him "like a leaf caught up by the wind" before the Lord's face. He glimpses the eighth (*Muzaloth*, the Zodiac), ninth (*Kuchavim*, constellations), and tenth (*Aravoth*) heavens.

**Chapter 22**: On the tenth heaven, Enoch sees the Lord's face "like iron made to glow in fire." God commands Michael to remove Enoch's earthly garments and anoint him with divine ointment: "the appearance of that ointment is more than the great light … and I was transformed into one of his glorious ones." The archangel Pravuil then delivers the heavenly record books to Enoch.

## Cross-source connections

The ten-heaven schema and Enoch's transformation into an angelic scribe anticipate the 3 Enoch identification of Enoch as the angel **Metatron** ("the Lesser YHWH"). The Grigori/Watchers in the fifth heaven directly extend the material of [[The Watchers, their fall, and the forbidden teachings (Book of Enoch)|1 Enoch's Watchers narrative]]. The Phoenixes/Chalkydri attending the sun parallel angel-as-nature-force motifs discussed in [[Escaping from Eden (Wallis)]] and [[The Gods of Eden (Bramley)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

- The scholarly introduction in this edition notes that the 8th, 9th, and 10th heavens are likely later additions to the original 2 Enoch text.
- The Grigori narrative in Ch. 18 is compressed relative to 1 Enoch; only three Watchers are said to have "gone down," whereas 1 Enoch gives a list of 200.
- The Phoenixes/Chalkydri and the Greek planetary names in Ch. 30 (Zeus, Cronus, Aphrodite, etc.) are noted by the editor as possible later insertions, perhaps as late as the 7th century AD.
- 2 Enoch's heaven structure diverges from later Jewish mystical traditions (e.g., the 7-palace Merkabah literature of 3 Enoch).
