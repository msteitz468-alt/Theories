## Claim Statement

Feder argues that pseudo-archaeology is sustained by identifiable motives rather than evidence: money
(books, tours, TV), fame, nationalism, racism, the desire for a more "romantic" past, and religious
or ideological commitment.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 1 Feder enumerates these motives, noting wryly that anyone truly sitting on
world-changing hidden knowledge would have every incentive to publish, not hide, it ("Are you
listening, History Channel?"). He treats the motive analysis as the backdrop to the case studies that
follow.

## What this counters in the wiki

A general counter to the commercial alternative-history corpus; the racism strand specifically
underlies the [[Pre-Columbian contact and the Mound Builder myth assessed (Feder)|Mound Builder myth]]
and the [[The ancient-astronaut fantasy debunked (Feder)|ancient-astronaut]] denial of non-Western
achievement.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Feder concedes some proponents are sincere and that pointing to motive does not by itself refute a
claim — the evidence test (Ch. 2) does.
