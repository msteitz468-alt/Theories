## Claim Statement

Feder argues there is no credible evidence that extraterrestrials visited Earth in the deep past or
gave humanity a technological "head start," and that the ancient-astronaut thesis ultimately **denies
human ancestors credit for their own achievements.**

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Feder traces the idea's modern origin to an audacious 1963 article riffing on the optimism of the
Drake equation, and notes its pop-culture face today (Erich von Däniken; *Ancient Aliens* / Giorgio
Tsoukalos — "I'm not saying it was aliens, but it was aliens"). His verdict is "a decided NO," and he
frames the key question as *why archaeologists are skeptical*: the supposed mysteries dissolve once
real archaeology is applied.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Feder accepts the *premise* that extraterrestrial life may well exist (the Drake equation); his
objection is to the claimed *evidence* of past visitation, not to astrobiology.
