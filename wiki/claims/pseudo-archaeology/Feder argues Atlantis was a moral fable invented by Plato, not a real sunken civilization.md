## Claim Statement

Feder argues Atlantis originates solely in Plato's dialogues (*Timaeus* and *Critias*) as a
**deliberate moral fable** — a cautionary tale of a proud sea-power destroyed by the gods — not a
historical report, and that there is no geological or archaeological evidence for any sunken advanced
continent.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Feder shows the story appears nowhere before Plato, that Plato framed it to make a philosophical point,
and that the modern Atlantis industry largely descends from Ignatius Donnelly's 1882 speculation.
Plate tectonics and ocean-floor mapping leave no room for a lost continent, and no Atlantean artifacts
exist; Mu/Lemuria likewise began as a discarded scientific hypothesis later inflated by occultists.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Feder allows that real events (e.g. the Bronze-Age Thera eruption) may have colored later legend, but
insists that is not the lost super-civilization of Atlantis lore.
