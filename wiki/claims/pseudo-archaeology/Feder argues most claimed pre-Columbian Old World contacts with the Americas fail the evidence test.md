## Claim Statement

Feder argues that the Americas were first peopled by the ancestors of Native Americans (via Beringia),
and that the many claimed *pre-Columbian* Old World "discoverers" — Egyptians, Phoenicians, Israelites,
Welsh, Africans, etc. — fail for lack of credible archaeological evidence. The one well-supported
exception is the Norse at L'Anse aux Meadows.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Feder works through the popular diffusionist claims and shows they rest on misidentified artifacts,
forged inscriptions, or wishful interpretation, contrasting them with the genuine, artifact-backed
Norse case.

## What this counters in the wiki

Counters hyperdiffusionist and lost-civilization framings of how culture spread; relates to
[[Maps of the Ancient Sea Kings (Hapgood, 1966)]] and the synthesis
[[Where did civilization and advanced knowledge come from? Models compared across sources]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Feder accepts contact *can* be demonstrated where real evidence exists (the Norse) — his objection is
evidentiary, not a denial that oceans were ever crossed.
