## Claim Statement

Feder argues that the specific "evidence" Erich von Däniken offered for ancient astronauts collapses
on inspection — the items are misidentified, their context ignored, or in some cases fabricated.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Feder works through von Däniken's stock examples and the prosaic, documented explanations for them
(human-made monuments and artworks read in their own cultural context), noting that von Däniken's
method is to treat any unexplained or unfamiliar detail as proof of aliens — an argument from
ignorance rather than from evidence.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Feder treats von Däniken as the popular archetype; he notes the same fallacies recur across many
ancient-astronaut authors, not von Däniken alone.
