## Claim Statement

Feder argues the supposed "unsolvable mysteries" of ancient monuments — the Egyptian pyramids, the
Nazca lines, Easter Island, Stonehenge — all have well-documented human explanations, and the
"mystery" framing works by underrating ancient engineering.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He notes the pyramids were built by organized human labor with known techniques (an archaeologically
traceable sequence), and that the Nazca lines are technically simple ground drawings makeable with
stakes and cord, meant to be experienced from the ground or as ritual pathways — not "alien landing
strips" (which would make no aerodynamic sense anyway).

## What this counters in the wiki

Counters the "lost super-science / alien help" reading in [[Fingerprints of the Gods (Hancock)]],
[[Chariots of the Gods (von Däniken)]], and the synthesis
[[Where did civilization and advanced knowledge come from? Models compared across sources]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Feder grants that not every construction detail is fully reconstructed, but argues open questions are
ordinary research gaps, not evidence of the paranormal.
