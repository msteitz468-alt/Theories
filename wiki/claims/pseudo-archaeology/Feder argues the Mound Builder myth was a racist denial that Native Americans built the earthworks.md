## Claim Statement

Feder argues the once-popular "Mound Builder" myth — that North America's great earthworks were built
by some vanished superior race (Israelites, Atlanteans, etc.) rather than by Native Americans — was
driven by **racism**, and that archaeology shows the ancestors of Native Americans built them.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Feder traces how 19th-century writers attributed the mounds to anyone *but* Native peoples, and how
this conveniently justified dispossession and removal; he cites the archaeological work (notably
Cyrus Thomas's Bureau of Ethnology survey) that established Native American authorship.

## What this counters in the wiki

A paradigm case of pseudo-archaeology serving an ideological/racist end; the same "they couldn't have
built it" logic recurs in [[The ancient-astronaut fantasy debunked (Feder)|ancient-astronaut claims]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Feder notes genuine scholarly uncertainty existed early on, but argues the myth persisted long after
the evidence settled the question.
