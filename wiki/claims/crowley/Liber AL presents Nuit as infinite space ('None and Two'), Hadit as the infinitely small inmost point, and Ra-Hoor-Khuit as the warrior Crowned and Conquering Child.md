## Claim Statement

Liber AL structures its cosmology around three divine principles: **Nuit**, defined as infinite space ("None and Two"), whose nature is the circumference containing all stars; **Hadit**, the infinitely small, inmost point — the complement of Nuit, present as the centre in every sphere, "the flame that burns in every heart of man, and in the core of every star"; and **Ra-Hoor-Khuit**, the hawk-headed warrior-god and Crowned and Conquering Child, whose enthronement at the Equinox of the Gods marks the current aeon. Each deity speaks one chapter of the three-chapter scripture.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Nuit (Chapter I):**

> "Had! The manifestation of Nuit." (I:1)

> "Every man and every woman is a star." (I:3)

> "I am known to ye by my name Nuit…I am Infinite Space, and the Infinite Stars thereof." (I:22)

> "let men speak not of Thee as One but as None." (I:27)

> "None, breathed the light, faint & faery, of the stars, and two." (I:28)

> "For I am divided for love's sake, for the chance of union." (I:29)

**Hadit (Chapter II):**

> "I, Hadit, am the complement of Nu, my bride. I am not extended, and Khabs is the name of my House." (II:2)

> "In the sphere I am everywhere the centre, as she, the circumference, is nowhere found." (II:3)

> "I am the flame that burns in every heart of man, and in the core of every star. I am Life, and the giver of Life, yet therefore is the knowledge of me the knowledge of death." (II:6)

> "I am the Magician and the Exorcist. I am the axle of the wheel, and the cube in the circle." (II:7)

> "I am perfect, being Not; and my number is nine by the fools; but with the just I am eight, and one in eight: Which is vital, for I am none indeed." (II:15)

**Ra-Hoor-Khuit (Chapter III):**

> "Abrahadabra; the reward of Ra Hoor Khut." (III:1)

> "Now let it be first understood that I am a god of War and of Vengeance." (III:3)

> "I am the warrior Lord of the Forties." (III:46)

> "I am the Hawk-Headed Lord of Silence & of Strength; my nemyss shrouds the night-blue sky." (III:70)

> "I am the Lord of the Double Wand of Power." (III:72)

> "The half of the word of Heru-ra-ha, called Hoor-pa-kraat and Ra-Hoor-Khut." (III:35)

> "The ending of the words is the Word Abrahadabra." (III:75)

## Cross-source connections

The Book of Lies ("The Glow-Worm") confirms and elaborates this triad: "Nuit, Hadit, Ra-Hoor-Khuit, are only to be understood by the Master of the Temple. They are above The Abyss, and contain all contradiction in themselves." This aligns with the Kabbalistic structure also explored in [[The Book of Lies (Crowley)]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Chapter III's martial passages — including injunctions to "Kill and torture; spare not" (III:18) and antinomian verses rejecting other world religions (III:50–54) — are presented by Ra-Hoor-Khuit as the doctrines of the current aeon's war-god. The text itself, at III:16, cautions: "Deem not too eagerly to catch the promises; fear not to undergo the curses. Ye, even ye, know not this meaning all." This internal caveat suggests the text acknowledges limits to its own literal reading.
