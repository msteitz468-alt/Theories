## Claim Statement

The system holds that the thirty Aethyrs (Aires) are concentric planes of spiritual or cosmic reality surrounding the fourfold elemental universe of the Watchtowers. They are numbered from 30 (innermost, most accessible) to 1 (outermost, most exalted), and correspond — though not in the same order — to the ascending Sephiroth of the Qabalistic universe. Each Aethyr is governed by a group of angelic governors whose sigil-names are derived from the Watchtower tablets. Access to each Aethyr is obtained by scrying (typically with a crystal or shew-stone) while intoning the Call of the Thirty Aethyrs with the name of the target Aethyr inserted.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Liber LXXXIV, Section III: "He symbolized the Fourth-Dimensional Universe in two dimensions as a square surrounded by 30 concentric circles (the 30 Aethyrs or Aires) whose radii increase in a geometrical proportion."

Section V opens: "The Thirty Aethyrs or Aires and their divisions and angels are as follows." The text notes that the Aethyrs' total angelic population is 91 — "the numeration of Amen" — and that the letters of the first three Aethyrs (LIL, ARN, ZON) carry the key-sigils of the most exalted Sephiroth: "I is therefore Kether; L, Chokmah and Binah; A, Chesed; N, Geburah; R, Tiphereth..."

The Vision and the Voice's introductory note (from Crowley): "It is to be remarked that the last three aethyrs have ten angels attributed to them, and they therefore represent the ten Sephiroth. Yet these ten form but one, a Malkuth-pendant to the next three, and so on, each set being, as it were, absorbed in the higher."

From the 12th Aethyr (LOE): "The Angel sayeth: ... the plane of the Aethyrs interpenetrateth and surroundeth the universe wherein the Sephiroth are established, and therefore is the order of the Aethyrs not the order of the Tree of Life. And only in a few places do they coincide. But the knowledge of the Aethyrs is deeper than the knowledge of the Sephiroth."

From the 13th Aethyr (ZIM): "And he answers: It is of the essence of him that is called NEMO, combined with the essence of the Aethyr, for from the 1st Aethyr to the 15th Aethyr, there is no vision and no voice, save for him that is called NEMO."

The Introduction (DuQuette) states: "These correspond roughly to the ascending planes of consciousness of the Qabalistic universe and were explored by the magician in the same manner as 'path workings.'"

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Vision and the Voice warns that the 1st through 15th Aethyrs (the higher half) yield genuine vision "only for him that is called NEMO" — an initiatory grade understood as having crossed the Abyss. Below that threshold, the higher Aethyrs are said to deceive: "He shall send them strong delusion, that they should believe a lie ... he that seeketh the vision and the voice therein is led away by dog-faced demons that show no sign of truth." The system thus presents the higher Aethyrs as accessible in principle but practically gated by spiritual attainment.
