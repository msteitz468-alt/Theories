## Claim Statement

The Kybalion holds that the Universe is not simply real or simply illusory — it is both simultaneously, depending on the viewpoint taken. From the Absolute perspective (the standpoint of THE ALL), the Universe is "in the nature of an illusion, a dream, a phantasmagoria." From the Relative perspective (the standpoint of finite beings within the Universe), the Universe "is very real indeed, and must be so considered." The Three Initiates teach that holding only one of these poles is a dangerous error they call "Half-Truth."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The Kybalion states directly:

> "Remember the Divine Paradox, that while the Universe IS NOT, still IT IS. Remember ever the Two Poles of Truth — the Absolute and the Relative. Beware of Half-Truths."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates further clarify:

> "While to THE INFINITE ALL, the Universe, its Laws, its Powers, its Life, its Phenomena, are as things witnessed in the state of Meditation or Dream; yet to all that is Finite, the Universe must be treated as Real, and life, and action, and thought, must be based thereupon, accordingly, although with an ever understanding of the Higher Truth."

The text defines these two poles using a philosophical distinction: "Absolute Truth has been defined as 'Things as the mind of God knows them,' while Relative Truth is 'Things as the highest reason of Man understands them.'"

The Kybalion supports this across traditions: "Philosopher, metaphysician, scientist and theologian all agree upon this idea" — that anything with a beginning and an ending is in some sense unreal.

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter VI is devoted entirely to this paradox, which the Kybalion treats as a practical danger for students who encounter Hermetic Mentalism (the teaching that the Universe is a Mental Creation of THE ALL). The instinctive response — "the Universe is mere illusion" — is described as the defining error of the "half-wise." The chapter insists that the Mental Laws of the Universe are "Iron Laws" binding on all except THE ALL itself, regardless of their mental nature.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion acknowledges the paradox is genuinely unresolvable at the level of finite understanding: "the Universe and its laws, and its phenomena, are just as REAL, so far as Man is concerned, as they would be under the hypotheses of Materialism or Energism." It does not dissolve the tension — it asks the student to hold both poles. It also notes that "not until THE ALL finally withdraws us into itself does the vision [of the illusory nature] actually vanish."
