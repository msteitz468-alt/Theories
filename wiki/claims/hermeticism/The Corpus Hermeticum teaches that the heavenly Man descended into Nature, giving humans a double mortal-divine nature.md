## Claim Statement

The Father-Mind begets a **Man in his own image**; granted authority over creation, the Man looks down,
falls in love with Nature, and descends into a body — so every human is **double**: mortal through the
body and Nature, but immortal and divine through the Man within.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"Mind, the father of all… gave birth to a man like himself whom he loved as his own child. The man was
most fair: he had the father's image" (C.H. I.12); the Man, entering the craftsman's sphere, is loved by
the governors who each give "a share of his own order," then unites with Nature — "because of this, unlike
any other living thing on earth, mankind is twofold — mortal because of the body, immortal because of the
essential man" (I.14–15).

## Cross-source connections

This dual nature and the "fall" of a heavenly Man parallel the Gnostic and theosophical anthropologies the
wiki records — compare Blavatsky's
[[Fallen angels, the Serpent, and Lucifer as misunderstood cosmic forces (Blavatsky)|Manasaputra/"fall into matter" scheme]].

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The descent is told mythically; the same treatise treats the body's pull as the thing salvation must reverse.
