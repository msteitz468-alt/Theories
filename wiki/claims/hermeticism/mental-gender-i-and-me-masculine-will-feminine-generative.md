## Claim Statement

The Kybalion teaches that every human mind contains two distinct principles corresponding to the universal Masculine and Feminine Principles of Gender: the "I" (Masculine, the seat of Will, Being, direction) and the "Me" (Feminine, the seat of generation, imagination, and becoming). These two aspects must work in concert for original mental creation; most people, the text argues, have allowed the Masculine "I" to lie dormant, making them subject to the mental influence of stronger-willed others.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The text presents the duality through introspection: consciousness first yields "I Am," but further examination reveals "an 'I' and a 'Me.'"

- "The 'I' represents the Masculine Principle of Mental Gender—the 'Me' represents the Female Principle. The 'I' represents the Aspect of Being; the 'Me' the Aspect of Becoming."
- The "Me" is "the 'mental womb'... capable of generating mental offspring." It receives impressions and generates thoughts and images.
- The "I" projects energy toward the "Me": "a sense and consciousness of an ability to project an energy from the 'I' to the 'Me'—a process of 'willing' that the mental creation begin and proceed."
- "The Feminine Principle conducts the work of generating new thoughts, concepts, ideas, including the work of the imagination. The Masculine Principle contents itself with the work of the 'Will' in its varied phases."

The Kybalion uses the Principle of Correspondence to link this to cosmic creation: "You will notice that the Principle of Correspondence operates on this plane just as it does upon the great plane upon which the creation of Universes is performed. The two are similar in kind, although vastly different in degree. 'As above, so below; as below, so above.'"

The practical consequence: "the majority of persons really employ the Masculine Principle but little, and are content to live according to the thoughts and ideas instilled into the 'Me' from the 'I' of other minds." Such people are governed by suggestion, hypnotism, and the mental force of stronger-willed individuals.

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is the central thesis of Ch. XIV (Mental Gender) and is presented as the Hermetic resolution to the modern psychological debate over the "dual mind" — a debate the text traces through Thomson J. Hudson's "objective and subjective minds," the "conscious and subconscious minds," etc. The Kybalion claims priority for the Hermetic doctrine: all these modern theories rediscover what the ancient teaching already stated clearly.

The chapter also explains telepathy, hypnotism, suggestion, and personal magnetism as the projection of Masculine Vibratory Will-energy from the "I" of one person onto the "Me" of another.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion acknowledges that it does not "agree with the many modern theories regarding the nature of the two phases of mind, nor does it admit many of the facts claimed for the two respective aspects — some of the said theories and claims being very far-fetched." It aligns with modern dual-mind theories only for purposes of helping students bridge existing knowledge, not as full endorsements.

The claim also implies a risk: the "Me" passively accepts seed-thoughts from external "I"s, and such implanted thoughts "grow and develops, and in time is regarded as the rightful mental offspring of the individual, whereas it is in reality like the cuckoo egg placed in the sparrow's nest, where it destroys the rightful offspring and makes itself at home." The solution is to activate one's own Masculine "I" through Will.
