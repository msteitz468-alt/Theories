## Claim Statement

The Kybalion holds that the Universe can be analyzed across Three Great Planes — Physical, Mental, and Spiritual — but that these are not separate places or ontologically distinct realms. They are degrees on a single scale of vibration (what the text calls "the Fourth Dimension"), running from the lowest vibration of gross Matter to the highest vibration of Spirit. All Planes, including the highest Spiritual Plane and its most advanced inhabitants, exist solely within the Mind of THE ALL and are subject to its Laws.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

On the nature of a Plane: "A 'dimension,' you know, is 'a measure in a straight line.' ... there is another dimension of 'created things'... known to occultists, and to scientists as well... and this new dimension... is the standard used in determining the degrees or 'planes.' This Fourth Dimension may be called 'the Dimension of Vibration.'"

On the unity of the scale: "The atom of matter, the unit of force, the mind of man, and the being of the archangel are all but degrees in one scale, and all fundamentally the same, the difference between solely a matter of degree, and rate of vibration — all are creations of THE ALL, and have their existence solely within the Infinite Mind of THE ALL."

On the highest inhabitants of the Spiritual Plane: "even the highest of these advanced Beings exist merely as creations of, and in, the Mind of THE ALL, and are subject to the Cosmic Processes and Universal Laws. They are still Mortal. We may call them 'gods' if we like, but still they are but the Elder Brethren of the Race."

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter VIII maps this scale in detail. The Great Physical Plane has seven Minor Planes (three of Matter, one of Ethereal Substance, three of Energy). The Great Mental Plane has seven Minor Planes ranging from Mineral Mind to Human Mind, with three interspersed Elemental Planes. The Great Spiritual Plane contains seven Minor Planes inhabited by Masters, Adepts, Angels, Archangels, and beings the Kybalion calls "The Gods" — all under the Law of THE ALL.

The text notes that the highest knowledge of the Spiritual Plane is deliberately restricted: "much of these Inner Teachings is held by the Hermetists as being too sacred, important and even dangerous for general public dissemination."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion consistently warns that the division into three Planes and their sub-divisions is pragmatic, not metaphysical: "The Three Great Planes are not actual divisions of the phenomena of the Universe, but merely arbitrary terms used by the Hermetists in order to aid in the thought and study of the various degrees and forms of universal activity and life." The Planes "shade into each other" and no hard boundary separates adjacent levels.
