## Claim Statement

The Kybalion teaches that the universe is entirely governed by Law, and that "Chance" is not a real force or agent but only a label for causation that the observer has not traced or understood. Every event — no matter how apparently random — is the necessary consequent of a chain of prior causes stretching back indefinitely. Nothing in the phenomenal universe falls outside this causal order.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "Every Cause has its Effect; every Effect has its Cause; everything happens according to Law; Chance is but a name for Law not recognized; there are many planes of causation, but nothing escapes the Law." — THE KYBALION.

> "Nothing escapes the Principle of Cause and Effect, but there are many Planes of Causation, and one may use the laws of the higher to overcome the laws of the lower." — The Kybalion.

The Three Initiates illustrate with the fall of a die: "Each time a die falls, and displays a certain number, it obeys a law as infallible as that which governs the revolution of the planets around the sun. Back of the fall of the die are causes, or chains of causes, running back further than the mind can follow." The point is generalized: "There is always a 'cause' and a 'because' to every event. Nothing ever 'happens' without a cause, or rather a chain of causes."

The text also traces the passage of a speck of soot across the field of vision to the ancient history of the world, demonstrating the infinite regress of causal chains behind even trivial phenomena.

## Contexts Within the Source

Ch. XII develops this claim as the philosophical foundation of the Sixth Hermetic Principle. The Kybalion notes that the Principle of Cause and Effect "underlies all scientific thought, ancient and modern" and has been "accepted as correct by practically all the thinkers of the world worthy of the name." The text deliberately avoids taking a full position on the Free Will vs. Determinism debate, invoking the Principle of Polarity to argue both are Half-Truths. Freedom increases as one approaches the "Centre" (i.e., approaches THE ALL).

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion does not claim that causal chains can be fully traced by human minds — it acknowledges that chains of causation exceed mental comprehension. The text also resists pure determinism: "do not make the mistake of supposing that Man is but a blind automaton." The Hermetic resolution is that "Man may use Law to overcome laws, and that the higher will always prevail against the lower." On higher planes, the adept becomes a Cause rather than an Effect — but is still subject to the Principle at whatever level they operate.

The teaching on reincarnation is assumed in the full account of compensation across lives.
