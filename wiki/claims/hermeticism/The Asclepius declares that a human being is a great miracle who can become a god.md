## Claim Statement

The Asclepius proclaims the exalted status of humanity: **"a human being is a great wonder/miracle"**
(*magnum miraculum est homo*) — a being who "changes his nature into a god's," occupies a middle place
between gods and the lower world, and in mind "is everything, and he is everywhere."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"Because of this, Asclepius, a human being is a great wonder, a living thing to be worshipped and
honored: for he changes his nature into a god's, as if he were a god… He has been put in the happier
place of middle status… He is everything, and he is everywhere" (Asclepius 6).

## Cross-source connections

This is the locus classicus behind the Renaissance "dignity of man," a theme [[The Forbidden Universe (Picknett & Prince)]]
makes central to the Hermetic origins of the scientific revolution; it also undergirds the magical
optimism the wiki records in [[The Secret Teachings of All Ages (Hall)]] and the Kybalion. It pairs with
the [[Man as a great miracle and the art of making gods (Asclepius)|art of making gods]] in the same text.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The dignity is potential, not automatic — the same treatise warns that humans who "despise the part of
him that is human nature" rightly, but those who do not remain bound to the lower world.
