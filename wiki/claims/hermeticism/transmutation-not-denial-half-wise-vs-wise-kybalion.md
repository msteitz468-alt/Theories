## Claim Statement

The Kybalion teaches that upon encountering the Hermetic doctrine that the Universe is a Mental Creation of THE ALL, students divide into two types: the "half-wise," who conclude the Universe is therefore unreal and attempt to live as though its Laws do not apply to them, and the "wise," who hold the two-pole truth and instead use higher Laws against lower Laws through the art of Mental Transmutation. The half-wise are "broken against the rocks." The weapon of the Master is transmutation, not denial.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The chapter's opening Kybalion maxim states this thesis in full:

> "The half-wise, recognizing the comparative unreality of the Universe, imagine that they may defy its Laws — such are vain and presumptuous fools, and they are broken against the rocks and torn asunder by the elements by reason of their folly. The truly wise, knowing the nature of the Universe, use Law against laws; the higher against the lower; and by the Art of Alchemy transmute that which is undesirable into that which is worthy, and thus triumph. Mastery consists not in abnormal dreams, visions and fantastic imaginings or living, but in using the higher forces against the lower — escaping the pains of the lower planes by vibrating on the higher. Transmutation, not presumptuous denial, is the weapon of the Master."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates use a striking image for the half-wise: "like a sleep-walker he stumbles ever around and around in a circle, making no progress, and being forced into an awakening at last by his falling bruised and bleeding over the Natural Laws which he ignored."

They summarize the practical instruction: "Keep your mind ever on the Star, but let your eyes watch over your footsteps, lest you fall into the mire by reason of your upward gaze."

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the ethical and practical conclusion of Chapter VI. The Kybalion frames "half-wisdom" as a specific spiritual hazard distinct from simple ignorance — the half-wise have grasped a genuine truth (the mental nature of the Universe) and been undone by it, whereas the ignorant at least navigate the relative world competently. The truly wise, by contrast, are "not hypnotized by the apparent unreality of things."

The Kybalion links the practical method — using higher Laws against lower — directly to [[Mental Transmutation (Kybalion)]] and to the Principle of Vibration (escaping lower planes "by vibrating on the higher").

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion adds a boundary on mastery: "Nothing but THE ALL can escape Law — and that because THE ALL is LAW itself, from which all Laws emerge." Even the highest Masters and Adepts "must bow to the Law." The injunction to "use higher Laws against lower" does not mean escaping Law entirely — it means navigating within the full hierarchy of Laws rather than pretending the lower ones do not exist.
