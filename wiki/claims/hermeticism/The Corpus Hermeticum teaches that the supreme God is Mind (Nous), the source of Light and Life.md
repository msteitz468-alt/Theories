## Claim Statement

The Hermetica teach that the supreme God is **Mind (Nous)** — at once **Light and Life** — the
unbegotten source from which all reality proceeds and through whom alone it can be known.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Poimandres, the revealer identifies the first principle: "in you which sees and hears is the word of
the lord, but your mind is god the father; they are not divided from one another for their union is
life" (C.H. I.6); "the father of all things was constituted of light and life, and from him the man came
to be." Other treatises restate it — "the energy of god is mind and soul" (C.H. XI) — and insist God,
though invisible, "is seen through the entire cosmos" and grasped only by the mind's eye (C.H. V).

## Cross-source connections

This God-as-Mind is the doctrine the wiki otherwise meets second-hand: the Kybalion's
[[The Principle of Mentalism (Kybalion)]] ("THE ALL is Mind") and Picknett & Prince's
[[The cosmic Mind of God (Forbidden Universe)]] both trace their teaching to these texts.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Copenhaver's introduction stresses the treatises are unsystematic and sometimes inconsistent (monist in
some, dualist in others), so "God is Mind" is a dominant theme rather than a single worked-out system.
