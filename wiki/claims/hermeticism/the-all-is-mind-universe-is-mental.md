## Claim Statement

The Kybalion teaches that the deepest truth about reality is that the underlying Substance of all
things is MIND — specifically, the Infinite Living Mind of [[THE ALL (Kybalion)]]. The entire
perceived universe does not exist independently but is held within, and generated by, the Mind of
THE ALL. This is the First Hermetic Principle and the foundational axiom of the entire Hermetic
system.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "THE ALL is MIND; The Universe is Mental."
> — THE KYBALION

> "THE UNIVERSE, AND ALL IT CONTAINS, IS A MENTAL CREATION OF THE ALL. Verily, indeed, ALL IS MIND!"
> — The Kybalion (Ch. V)

> "The Universe is Mental—held in the Mind of THE ALL."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates explain: this Principle "embodies the truth that 'All is Mind.'" THE ALL —
"the Substantial Reality underlying all the outward manifestations" — is SPIRIT, "AN UNIVERSAL,
INFINITE, LIVING MIND." The universe exists "in the Mind of THE ALL, in which Mind we 'live and
move and have our being.'"

The Kybalion reaches this conclusion by logical exclusion (Ch. V): the Universe is neither THE ALL
itself (immutable, One) nor Nothing (we sense its existence). The only mode by which THE ALL can
create without external materials and without subdividing itself is mental creation, analogous to
how a human mind generates mental images.

## Contexts Within the Source

This axiom is introduced as the first of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]], restated at the opening
of Ch. III (Mental Transmutation), and argued at length in Ch. V (The Mental Universe). It is the
premise from which [[Mental Transmutation (Kybalion)]] derives its cosmic significance: if all is
mental, then mastery of mental transmutation is mastery of the universe itself.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion acknowledges the apparent paradox: if THE ALL is everything, and finite changing
things also exist, how can both be real? It warns against two mistaken conclusions — (1) that
finite things are therefore THE ALL (the "I AM GOD" error), and (2) that they are therefore Nothing.
The resolution is deferred to later lessons but framed as a "Reconciliation" of the paradox, not a
denial of it. The text also insists THE ALL is "above" every law including those of Time and Space —
making it beyond full rational comprehension.
