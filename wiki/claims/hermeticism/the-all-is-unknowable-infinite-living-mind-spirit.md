## Claim Statement

The Kybalion teaches that [[THE ALL (Kybalion)]] — the Substantial Reality underlying all
phenomena — is in its inner essence absolutely unknowable, yet human reason is compelled to
characterize it as Infinite, Eternal, Immutable, and Absolute. Beyond these logical necessities,
it can only be described as "Infinite Living Mind," which the illumined call "SPIRIT." It is
neither mere Matter nor blind mechanical Energy, since neither can account for the Life and Mind
we observe in the universe.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "In its Essence, THE ALL is UNKNOWABLE."
> — THE KYBALION

> "THAT which is the Fundamental Truth—the Substantial Reality—is beyond true naming, but the Wise
> Men call it THE ALL."
> — THE KYBALION

> "'THE ALL' is Infinite Living Mind—the Illumined call it SPIRIT!"
> — The Kybalion, Ch. IV

The Three Initiates reason that Matter cannot be THE ALL because "Matter cannot manifest Life or
Mind." Blind Energy or Force cannot be THE ALL because "nothing can rise higher than its source—
nothing is evolved unless it is involved." What is higher than both Matter and Energy in the
universe? "LIFE AND MIND!" — and the form of Life and Mind that could underlie all existence must
be "INFINITE LIVING MIND as compared to finite Life and Mind." This is what the illumined mean by
"SPIRIT."

The text also rules out theological anthropomorphism: ascribing "the personality, qualities, properties,
characteristics and attributes of themselves" to THE ALL — including "jealousy, susceptibility to
flattery and praise, desire for offerings and worship" — is dismissed as "not worthy of grown men
and women."

## Contexts Within the Source

Ch. IV presents this as the metaphysical foundation for Ch. V's cosmological argument (that the
Universe must be a mental creation of THE ALL). The unknowability of THE ALL is also why the
Kybalion distinguishes Religion (intuitive recognition of THE ALL's existence and one's relationship
to it) from Theology (attempts to ascribe personality and characteristics to it) — the latter being
dismissed as rooted "in the quicksands of ignorance."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion explicitly acknowledges the paradox: if THE ALL is infinite and all things are within
it, yet finite changeable things also exist, how are both true? It states "There is a Reconciliation
of this apparently contradictory state of affairs" but defers the full resolution to later lessons.
The unknowability claim is held simultaneously with the positive assertion that THE ALL is
"Infinite Living Mind" — the latter is offered as the limit of what reason and the Illumined can
say, not as a full definition.
