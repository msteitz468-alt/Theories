## Claim Statement

The Kybalion teaches that what appear to be diametrically opposed things — Heat and Cold, Light and Darkness, Love and Hate, Good and Evil, Spirit and Matter — are not different in kind but only in degree. They are the two poles of a single continuous scale. There is no point on any such scale where one quality "ends" and its opposite "begins"; all such distinctions are relative, not absolute.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The central maxim of [[The Principle of Polarity (Kybalion)]]:

> "Everything is dual; everything has poles; everything has its pair of opposites; like and unlike are the same; opposites are identical in nature, but different in degree; extremes meet; all truths are but half-truths; all paradoxes may be reconciled." — THE KYBALION.

The Three Initiates use the thermometer as their primary physical illustration: there is no absolute "cold" or "heat," only higher and lower degrees of the same vibration. The musical scale (C to C), the color spectrum (red to violet), and the compass directions (East/West, North/South) are all cited as analogies. On the mental plane, Love and Hate are presented as poles of the same emotional continuum: "Beginning at any point of the scale we find 'more love,' or 'less hate,' as we ascend the scale; and 'more hate' or 'less love' as we descend."

The metaphysical extension: Spirit and Matter, THE ALL and The Many, Infinite Mind and finite minds are said to differ only in degree of Mental Manifestation, not in fundamental nature.

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim is the foundational thesis of Ch. X (Polarity) and is presented as the resolution to philosophical paradoxes that have "perplexed the mind of men." The Kybalion frames it as an ancient teaching that modern thought has approached obliquely through sayings such as "there are two sides to everything" and "every truth is half-false." The Principle underlies the Hermetic resolution of the Free Will vs. Determinism controversy: both are described as "Half-Truths — the opposing poles of Truth."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The text acknowledges that while things of the same scale can be transmuted into each other along a polarity continuum, things of *different classes* cannot: "Heat may readily be transmuted into Cold, simply by lowering the vibrations. In the same way Hate and Love are mutually transmutable; so are Fear and Courage. But Fear cannot be transformed into Love, nor can Courage be transmuted into Hate." Transmutation is possible only along the axis of the same quality; cross-category change is not claimed.

Additionally, the claim that "all truths are but half-truths" is stated as itself a "half-truth" by implication — the text acknowledges its own relativism without fully resolving it.
