## Claim Statement

The Kybalion holds that both of the following statements are simultaneously true: (1) All is in THE ALL — the Universe and everything in it exists solely within the Infinite Mind of THE ALL; and (2) THE ALL is in All — THE ALL is immanent in, abiding within, every particle, unit, and combination within the Universe. These are not contradictory but two poles of a single Hermetic truth. Critically, the second proposition does not collapse into pantheist identity: the Universe is not THE ALL, and its constituents are not identical to THE ALL, any more than a character in a novel is identical to the author.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The Kybalion maxim that opens Chapter VII:

> "While All is in THE ALL, it is equally true that THE ALL is in All. To him who truly understands this truth hath come great knowledge."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates illustrate the non-identity aspect through literary analogy: Shakespeare's characters (Othello, Iago, Hamlet) exist within Shakespeare's mind, and yet Shakespeare is immanent in each character — yet no character is Shakespeare. They quote:

> "Man, like Micawber, may exclaim: 'The Spirit of my Creator is inherent within me — and yet I am not HE!'"

They contrast this with what they call the "shocking half-truth" of those who cry "I Am God!" — illustrated as the absurdity of a fictional character declaring itself to be its author. The Kybalion specifically states: "THE ALL is in the earth-worm, and yet the earth-worm is far from being THE ALL."

## Contexts Within the Source

Chapter VII develops this doctrine as the complement to Chapter IV's teaching on Mentalism. The Two-Pole truth of "All in THE ALL / THE ALL in All" is said to rest on the [[The Divine Paradox (Kybalion)|Law of Paradox]], itself an aspect of the Principle of Polarity. The immanence of THE ALL is also the ground of spiritual development: "In the degree that Man realizes the existence of the Indwelling Spirit immanent within his being, so will he rise in the spiritual scale of life."

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Three Initiates explicitly acknowledge that their literary analogy (finite author / finite characters) is "necessarily imperfect and inadequate, for they represent the creation of mental images in finite minds, while the Universe is a creation of Infinite Mind." The difference between finite and infinite creation is "merely a matter of degree — the same Principle is in operation."

On the question of why THE ALL creates, the Kybalion is deliberately agnostic: "THE ALL ACTS BECAUSE IT ACTS." The text treats this as permanently unanswerable from within the Universe, and notes that even Hermes himself, when asked, "pressed his lips tightly together and said not a word."
