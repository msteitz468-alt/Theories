## Claim Statement

In the **Lament**, Hermes prophesies that a time will come when the Egyptians' faithful worship will be
in vain: **divinity will withdraw from earth, Egypt will be abandoned**, foreigners will occupy the land,
reverence will be **outlawed under penalty of law**, and the holy land "will be filled completely with
tombs and corpses."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"A time will come when it will appear that the Egyptians paid respect to divinity… to no purpose… for
divinity will return from earth to heaven, and Egypt will be abandoned… not only will reverence fall into
neglect but… a prohibition under penalty prescribed by law… will be enacted against reverence… Then this
most holy land, seat of shrines and temples, will be filled completely with tombs and corpses"
(Asclepius 24–25).

## Cross-source connections

This is the direct textual source of [[The Forbidden Universe (Picknett & Prince)|Picknett & Prince's]]
[[Lament for Hermes: the scientific revolution was the Hermetic revolution, whose origins were then suppressed|"Lament for Hermes"]],
where the prophesied suppression of Hermetic religion becomes a template for the later erasure of the
Hermetic roots of modern science.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Lament ends with a promise of eventual renewal (the god restoring the world), so it is a lament of
decline-and-restoration, not of final doom; scholars also read it as reflecting real anxieties of late
Roman Egypt.
