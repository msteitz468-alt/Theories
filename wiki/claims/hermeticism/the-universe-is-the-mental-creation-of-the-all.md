## Claim Statement

The Kybalion teaches that the Universe's mode of existence is neither (a) identity with THE ALL,
nor (b) nothingness, nor (c) creation from external material, nor (d) a fragment broken off from
THE ALL. The only logically and metaphysically consistent option is that THE ALL generates the
Universe mentally — in its Infinite Mind — analogously to, but infinitely exceeding, how human
minds create mental images.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "THE ALL can create in no other way except mentally, without either using material (and there is
> none to use), or else reproducing itself (which is also impossible). There is no escape from this
> conclusion of the Reason, which, as we have said, agrees with the highest teachings of the Illumined."
> — The Kybalion, Ch. V

> "THE UNIVERSE, AND ALL IT CONTAINS, IS A MENTAL CREATION OF THE ALL. Verily, indeed, ALL IS MIND!"
> — The Kybalion, Ch. V

> "The Infinite Mind of THE ALL is the womb of Universes."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates lay out three modes of human creation by analogy (Principle of Correspondence):
(1) making from external materials — impossible for THE ALL, as nothing exists outside it; (2)
biological reproduction — impossible, as THE ALL cannot subtract from or multiply itself; (3) mental
creation — the only remaining option, and the one that accounts for how Spirit can "pervade" its
creation without being divided. "Just as you, student, may create a Universe of your own in your
mentality, so does THE ALL create Universes in its own Mentality. But your Universe is the mental
creation of a Finite Mind, whereas that of THE ALL is the creation of an Infinite."

The text also rebuts pantheistic identification: those who conclude from "THE ALL is ALL" that they
personally are THE ALL are gently ridiculed — "The claim of the corpuscle that 'I am Man!' would
be modest in comparison."

## Contexts Within the Source

This argument directly depends on [[THE ALL (Kybalion)]]'s attributes (infinite, immutable,
indivisible) established in Ch. IV, and is the metaphysical grounding for why [[Mental Transmutation (Kybalion)]] has cosmic — not merely psychological — scope. It also introduces the cosmological
gender teaching: THE ALL above Gender, but manifesting Masculine (Will) and Feminine (Nature)
principles in the act of mental creation.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion acknowledges this leaves a "great question" about the exact relationship between the
mutable, finite universe and the immutable, infinite THE ALL — it calls this an "apparently
contradictory state of affairs" and promises a "Reconciliation" in later lessons. The mental
creation analogy is explicitly labeled a partial explanation: "we must either do this or stop
thinking of the matter at all."
