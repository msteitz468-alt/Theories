## Claim Statement

In the Poimandres vision the supreme **Mind** emits a holy **Word (Logos)** and generates a second,
**creator-Mind (demiurge)** who, together with the Word, frames the seven planetary **governors** and
sets the cosmos in motion — a cosmogony that consciously parallels and reinterprets Genesis.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Poimandres shows Hermes the primal Light, then a watery Darkness, then "a holy word" descending on
nature; Mind, "being androgyne, existing as life and light, gave birth by a word to a second mind, a
craftsman," who shaped the governors. Their cycling "course" then brings forth the living kinds (C.H.
I.9–11; "the beasts came to be… and every germinating seed," I.[3]/v.18).

## Cross-source connections

The triad of Mind → Word → creator parallels the Logos theology that Copenhaver's introduction notes was
debated as evidence of pre-Christian Gnosticism; within the wiki it underlies the
[[The cosmic Mind of God (Forbidden Universe)]] reading and the [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)|Kybalion's]]
mentalist cosmology.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The text is allegorical and, by its own framing, addressed "to those whom god himself wishes"; Poimandres
warns the deeper stages "cannot be taught."
