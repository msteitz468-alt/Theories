## Claim Statement

The treatises present themselves as **primordial Egyptian wisdom** revealed by **Hermes Trismegistus**
(the syncretic Thoth-Hermes) to his disciples — a claim of great antiquity that later made the Hermetica
a cornerstone of the *prisca theologia*. Copenhaver's introduction sets them instead in Greco-Roman
Egypt of the first centuries AD.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The dialogues are addressed by "thrice-great" Hermes to Tat, Asclepius, and Ammon and presented as
sacred revelation ("a Holy Book of Hermes Trismegistus addressed to Asclepius"). Copenhaver's
introduction traces the Thoth-Hermes syncretism and dates the Greek treatises to roughly the 1st–3rd
centuries AD, well after the pharaonic age they evoke.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Copenhaver is explicit that the Egyptian "frame" is largely literary; modern scholarship (Reitzenstein,
Festugière) debated how much is genuinely Egyptian versus Greek/Jewish.
