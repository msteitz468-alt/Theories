## Claim Statement

The closing axiom of the Kybalion asserts that all the practical work of the preceding principles — changing vibration, polarizing mental states, neutralizing rhythm, rising to higher planes of causation — ultimately rests on the foundational premise that the Universe is Mental in nature. Because THE ALL is Mind, the only real power that can influence universal phenomena is Mental Power, and all so-called miracles or wonder-workings are reducible to Mental Transmutation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "True Hermetic Transmutation is a Mental Art." — The Kybalion.

> "THE ALL is MIND; The Universe is Mental." — The Kybalion.

The Three Initiates explain: "The Universe being wholly mental, it follows that it may be ruled only by Mentality. And in this truth is to be found an explanation of all the phenomena and manifestations of the various mental powers which are attracting so much attention and study in these earlier years of the Twentieth Century. Back of and under the teachings of the various cults and schools, remains ever constant the principle of the Mental Substance of the Universe."

The law of use is also stated as a closing moral: "The possession of Knowledge, unless accompanied by a manifestation and expression in Action, is like the hoarding of precious metals—a vain and foolish thing. Knowledge, like Wealth, is intended for Use. The Law of Use is Universal, and he who violates it suffers by reason of his conflict with natural forces." — The Kybalion.

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the final thesis of the book (Ch. XV, the closing chapter). It functions as a grand synthesis: all six principles covered in Chapters X–XV (Polarity, Rhythm, Causation, Gender, Mental Gender, the Axioms) are practical elaborations of the foundational Principle of Mentalism. The Hermetic Axioms chapter explicitly loops back to [[The Principle of Mentalism (Kybalion)]] as the master key that explains why Mental Transmutation works at all: if the Universe were not Mental, no amount of mind-work could alter it.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

The Kybalion presents this claim as axiomatic and self-consistent within its system rather than as an empirically verifiable assertion. The text does not engage with materialist philosophical objections. It does however invoke the "Divine Paradox" tradition (addressed in earlier chapters) by acknowledging that the physical world has practical reality for beings operating within it, even while insisting its ultimate substance is Mental.

The caveat on the Law of Use implies that even correct knowledge is useless unless acted upon — the claim is not just metaphysical but carries an ethical/practical imperative.
