## Claim Statement

Heckethorn holds that religion "has had its secret societies from the most ancient times," dating from when the "true religious knowledge… possessed by the first men, began to decay among the general mass of mankind." The ancient **Mysteries** (Egyptian, Eleusinian, Mithraic, Indian, etc.) preserved this knowledge — but even they "were already a degree removed from the first primeval native wisdom," representing "only the type, instead of the archetype."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He defines the lost "genuine knowledge" as "the knowledge of the constitution of the universe and the Eternal Power that had produced, and the laws that maintained it." Book I surveys the Mysteries across civilizations — Mithraic, Brahminical, Egyptian (Serapis, Osiris), Grecian (Eleusinian, Dionysiac, Cabiri), Chinese/Japanese, Mexican/Peruvian (with their human sacrifice), and Scandinavian (the Drottes) — describing initiation ordeals and the secrecy enforced on pain of death.
