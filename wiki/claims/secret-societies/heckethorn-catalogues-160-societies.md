## Claim Statement

Heckethorn's work is a comprehensive historical survey of "upwards of one hundred and sixty secret organisations — religious, political, and social — from the most remote ages down to the present time," treated as documented history rather than as either a benign mystery or a sinister plot.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The title page lists the sweep: "the Mysteries of Ancient India, China, Japan, Egypt, Mexico, Peru, Greece, and Scandinavia, the Cabbalists, Early Christians, Heretics, Assassins, Thugs, Templars, the Vehm and Inquisition, Mystics, Rosicrucians, Illuminati, Freemasons, Skopzi, Camorristi, Carbonari, Nihilists, Fenians" and more. Volume II concentrates on Freemasonry and the 18th–19th-century political secret societies — the **Carbonari** (Italian revolutionary network), the Comuneros, the **Nihilists**, Chinese and Turkish societies, and the Irish agrarian societies (White-Boys, Right-Boys, Defenders). Heckethorn consistently favours prosaic, documented explanations over sensational ones.
