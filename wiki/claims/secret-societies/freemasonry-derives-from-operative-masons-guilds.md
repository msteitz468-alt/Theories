## Claim Statement

Heckethorn argues the "true history of Masonry, without the varnish and tinsel Masonic writers have bedizened it with," is mundane: Freemasonry descends from real **operative stonemasons' guilds** that built medieval cathedrals — not from Solomon's Temple or the ancient Mysteries — though it absorbed assorted "Indian, Egyptian, Jewish, and Christian ideas, terms, and symbols" along the way.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He traces the lineage from ancient builders' corporations (the Greek "Dionysiacs," the Roman "Collegium Murorum") to the medieval building associations that, "originating at Cologne," kept lodges at Strasbourg, Vienna, Cologne, and Zurich; these called themselves Freemasons and had initiation ceremonies. From "the end of the sixteenth century, non-operative masons were admitted… called 'accepted' Masons," including "men distinguished for learning or high position," and so "the work in the lodges became more symbolical than operative." Heckethorn calls Freemasonry "a tree the roots of which spread through so many soils."
