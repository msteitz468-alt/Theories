## Claim Statement

Huxley argues that the cleansed, "Mind at Large" perception accessed under mescaline is the same vision that great artists possess "congenitally" and that lies behind religion's reports of heavens, paradises, and the divine. The visionary experience — whether reached by drugs, contemplation, or innate gift — is thus the common source of genuine sacred art and of humanity's intimations of a transcendent reality, while symbols and artworks can only point toward, never substitute for, it.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Of the artist Huxley writes: "What the rest of us see only under the influence of mescalin, the artist is congenitally equipped to see all the time. His perception is not limited to what is biologically or socially useful. A little of the knowledge belonging to Mind at Large oozes past the reducing valve of brain and ego, into his consciousness." Looking at Vermeer, the Le Nain brothers, and Constable's pine trees — "actually been seen as identical with the Dharma-Body" — he treats their work as renderings of cleansed perception, while insisting "symbols can never be the things they stand for… Art, I suppose, is only for beginners."

On religion: the "various 'other worlds' with which human beings erratically make contact" are "elements in the totality of the awareness belonging to Mind at Large"; the terror of the unprepared before "the burning brightness of unmitigated Reality" he aligns with *The Tibetan Book of the Dead* and the *Mysterium tremendum*. He concludes the experience showed him "the true nature of the challenge and the completely liberating response," and that contemplatives "become conduits through which some beneficent influence can flow out of that other country into a world of darkened selves."

## Contexts Within the Source

Applies the reducing-valve theory ([[Huxley: the brain is a "reducing valve" that filters Mind at Large]]) and the cleansed perception ([[Huxley: mescaline cleanses perception, revealing the "is-ness" of things]]) to aesthetics and religion. It connects *The Doors of Perception* to Huxley's [[The perennial philosophy|perennial philosophy]] and to the wiki's shamanism/entheogen material on visionary states (e.g. Hancock's *Supernatural*, Grof's holotropic states).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Huxley distinguishes the chemically opened "way of Mary" (contemplation) from the "way of Martha" (action and charity) and concedes mescaline "shuts the door" on the latter — the visionary source, by itself, is incomplete and even dangerous without being integrated into ethical life. He also stresses that art is a "second-hand" emblem, not the reality.
