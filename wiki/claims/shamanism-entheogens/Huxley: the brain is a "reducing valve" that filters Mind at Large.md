## Claim Statement

Huxley proposes (following C. D. Broad and Bergson) that the function of the brain, nervous system, and sense organs is **eliminative, not productive** — a "reducing valve" that protects us from being overwhelmed by a vastly larger field of awareness he calls **"Mind at Large,"** admitting only the "measly trickle" of consciousness needed for biological survival. Mescaline (like certain spiritual exercises, hypnosis, or spontaneous gifts) impairs the valve, letting more of Mind at Large seep through.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Huxley quotes Broad's Bergson-derived suggestion "that the function of the brain and nervous system and sense organs is in the main eliminative and not productive. Each person is at each moment capable of … perceiving everything that is happening everywhere in the universe. The function of the brain … is to protect us from being overwhelmed and confused by this mass of largely useless and irrelevant knowledge, by shutting out most of what we should otherwise perceive."

He draws the conclusion: "each one of us is potentially Mind at Large. But in so far as we are animals, our business is at all costs to survive. To make biological survival possible, Mind at Large has to be funneled through the reducing valve of the brain and nervous system. What comes out at the other end is a measly trickle of the kind of consciousness which will help us to stay alive." Some people are "born with a kind of by-pass that circumvents the reducing valve"; in others "temporary by-passes may be acquired … through hypnosis, or by means of drugs." Language itself "petrifies" the reduced awareness, so that we "take [our] concepts for data, [our] words for actual things."

## Contexts Within the Source

The theoretical core that explains the perceptual transformation Huxley reports — the cleansed perception of [[Huxley: mescaline cleanses perception, revealing the "is-ness" of things]] and the artist's privileged sight in [[Huxley: the visionary experience is the wellspring of art and religion]]. It is the experiential bridge to his [[The perennial philosophy|perennialist]] conviction that a larger reality lies behind ordinary awareness, and anticipates later "filter"/"transmission" theories of mind (cf. the wiki's [[Post-materialist science]] cluster).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Huxley presents the reducing-valve theory as a "type of theory" worth taking seriously rather than a proven mechanism, hedged with "the suggestion is that…"; he also offers a tentative biochemical story (mescaline lowering the brain's glucose supply) as the physical correlate, conceding "too few cases have been observed, and therefore a comprehensive answer cannot yet be given."
