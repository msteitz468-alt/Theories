## Claim Statement

When America dropped an atomic weapon on Hiroshima, no member of the U.S. Congress had had any idea the bomb was being developed.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "When the world learned that America had dropped an atomic weapon on Hiroshima, no one was more surprised than the U.S. Congress, none of whose members had had any idea it was being developed."

Vice President Harry Truman was equally stunned when he became president on April 12, 1945; Vannevar Bush and Henry L. Stimson relayed the information to him.
