## Claim Statement

Shermer argues that modern skepticism is not "know-nothing" pyrrhonism (total doubt) but a positive,
science-based method: gathering data to test natural explanations for natural phenomena and arriving
at provisional conclusions proportional to the evidence. A skeptic is someone who says, when
confronted with an extraordinary claim, "That's nice — prove it." The method accepts no claim as
final and is self-correcting by design.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 1** frames this through Shermer's personal odyssey as a professional cyclist who spent a
decade testing alternative health and fitness claims (colonics, iridology, pyramid power, Rolfing,
megavitamin therapy, chiropractic, the Electro-Acuscope) and found that "nothing increased
performance, alleviated pain, or enhanced well-being other than long hours in the saddle, dedication
to a consistent training schedule, and a balanced diet." The personal experiment converts him to
skepticism on Loveland Pass, August 6, 1983.

**The definition:** "A skeptic is one who questions the validity of a particular claim by calling for
evidence to prove or disprove it." Not closed-minded, not cynical — from Missouri: "show me."

**The flaw in pure skepticism:** "If you are skeptical about everything, you must be skeptical of
your own skepticism." The position collapses under its own logic. Shermer's resolution: navigate
between "know nothing" skepticism and "anything goes" credulity by applying the methods of science.

**Hume's Maxim** is the ultimate fallback tool: "A wise man proportions his belief to the evidence."
When confronted by an apparently supernatural claim with no immediate natural explanation, weigh the
miracle of the claim against the miracle of its falsehood being greater — "and always reject the
greater miracle."

**Science as the vehicle:** Science is defined as "a set of methods designed to describe and
interpret observed or inferred phenomena, past or present, and aimed at building a testable body of
knowledge open to rejection or confirmation." Its key elements: induction, deduction, observation,
verification. A fact is "a conclusion confirmed to such an extent that it would be reasonable to
offer temporary agreement." Crucially, "all facts in science are provisional and subject to challenge."

**The Hundredth Monkey as exercise:** Shermer walks through the debunking of Lyall Watson's
Hundredth Monkey phenomenon — showing Watson admitted he "improvised details" from "bits of folklore"
and that only 36 of 59 monkeys (not 100) acquired the behavior over 10 years, not suddenly. "Not
only is there no evidence to support this extraordinary claim, there is not even a real phenomenon
to explain."

**Chapter 2** extends the argument: science's greatest strength is self-correction. Cold fusion was
exposed by lack of external verification. The key practical question is not "does the theory behind
astrology make sense?" but "Does astrology do what astrologers claim it can do?" Answer: no.

## What this counters in the wiki

This establishes the methodological baseline against which most alternative claims in the wiki are
assessed. It most directly counters the epistemological assumptions underlying sources that treat
personal testimony, channeling, or unfalsifiable frameworks as evidence:

- [[Behold a Pale Horse (Cooper, 1991)]] and [[Everything You Need to Know But Have Never Been Told (Icke, 2018)]] — both rest heavily on rumor, anecdote, and unfalsifiable conspiracy frameworks that
  Shermer's demand for external validation would reject at the first step.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Shermer acknowledges that science is culturally embedded and theory-laden (Ch. 2, Pirsig's Paradox).
He resolves the paradox by arguing science is still meaningfully progressive in the cumulative,
non-moralistic sense: "useful features are retained and nonuseful features are abandoned, based on
the rejection or confirmation of testable knowledge." He explicitly accepts that there is no
"Archimedean point" outside culture from which to verify absolute Truth.
