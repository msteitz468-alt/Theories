## Claim Statement

Shermer argues that the tendency to force complex, continuous phenomena into binary or discrete categories is one of the fundamental errors of human cognition — and the error that connects creationism, Holocaust denial, racism, and many other belief systems in the wiki.

His primary vehicle is Alfred Kinsey's foundational insight from Sexual Behavior in the Human Male (1948): "Males do not represent two discrete populations, heterosexual and homosexual. The world is not to be divided into sheep and goats… Only the human mind invents categories and tries to force facts into separate pigeonholes. The living world is a continuum in each and every one of its aspects."

Kinsey's point was taxonomic: naturalists who described species were describing unique individuals, not fixed types. Shermer extends this to race (via Luca Cavalli-Sforza's population genetics), sexuality, morality, and belief.

On race specifically: Cavalli-Sforza's History and Geography of Human Genes (50 years of population genetics, 1,032 pages) concludes that "from a scientific point of view, the concept of race has failed to obtain any consensus; none is likely, given the gradual variation in existence." Traditional racial categories (based on skin color, hair, facial traits) are "superficial differences" under "recent evolution mostly under the effect of climate and perhaps sexual selection." More importantly, **within-group genetic variability is greater than between-group variability** — if a nuclear war killed all humans except one band of Australian Aborigines, 85% of Homo sapiens' genetic variability would survive.

The argument connects to Holocaust denial through the Pioneer Fund: the same network that funds *The Bell Curve* (Herrnstein and Murray 1994) and Philippe Rushton's racial hierarchy research also funded Roger Pearson, who worked with Willis Carto (founder of IHR, the Holocaust denial organization). Shermer maps these financial and social connections explicitly.

On morality and belief: Kinsey's observation that "prescriptions are merely public confessions of prescriptionists" applies equally to belief systems. "What is right for one individual may be wrong for the next." Moral and legal codes are statistical generalizations applied to infinitely variable individuals.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

From Kinsey (quoted by Shermer, Chapter 15): "Only the human mind invents categories and tries to force facts into separate pigeonholes. The living world is a continuum in each and every one of its aspects. The sooner we learn this concerning human sexual behavior the sooner we shall reach a sound understanding of the realities of sex."

From Cavalli-Sforza et al. (quoted by Shermer, Chapter 15): "The difference between groups is therefore small when compared with that within the major groups, or even within a single population."

Shermer on race categories: "Checking a box on a form for race — 'Caucasian,' 'Hispanic,' 'African-American,' 'Native American,' or 'Asian-American' — is untenable and ridiculous." Out of Africa theory means everyone in America should simply check "African-American."

On the Pioneer Fund connection: Pioneer Fund (est. 1937) funded Rushton (who claims blacks have lower intelligence, earlier maturation, larger genitalia), Shockley ($179,000 for heritability-of-IQ research), and Pearson's *Mankind Quarterly*. Pearson worked with Carto for three months editing Western Destiny; Carto founded IHR (Institute for Historical Review). The network that funded racial pseudoscience is the same network that funded Holocaust denial pseudohistory.

## What this counters in the wiki

- Sources in the wiki that treat racial, cultural, or belief categories as fixed and binary (e.g., rigid in-group/out-group framings in [[Behold a Pale Horse (Cooper, 1991)]] or [[Everything You Need to Know But Have Never Been Told (Icke, 2018)]]) are subject to the continuum critique.
- The Pioneer Fund → IHR connection ties racial pseudoscience to Holocaust denial, showing that these are not separate eccentric claims but nodes in a connected network.
- See [[Why people believe weird things (Shermer's thesis)]] for the general argument about why category errors and motivated reasoning are epistemically dangerous.

## Opposing or Limiting Notes Within the Source

Shermer acknowledges that races exist as statistical clusters even if the concept lacks biological precision: "Don't we all know a black person or a white person when we see one? Sure, agree the authors [Cavalli-Sforza]." The point is not that clusters don't exist but that the boundaries are fuzzy sets, the within-group variation dwarfs between-group variation, and the moral and policy conclusions drawn from racial categories are unjustified by the underlying biology.
