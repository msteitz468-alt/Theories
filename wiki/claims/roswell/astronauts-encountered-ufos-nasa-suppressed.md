## Claim Statement

Berlitz and Moore claim that U.S. astronauts "frequently encounter[ed]" UFOs in space — at a far higher rate than sightings on Earth — and that NASA systematically censored, altered, de-emphasized, or ignored these reports, ordering the military-duty astronauts to silence. They present this as evidence that UFOs are of extraterrestrial origin and the object of ongoing official secrecy, paralleling the Roswell cover-up.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Chapter 1 asserts that "astronauts frequently encounter [UFOs] in space" and that "if we consider reports that the majority of space ventures have encountered UFOs then the percentage of encounters is vastly greater in space than in the skies of earth," which "would seem definitely to indicate that UFOs are of extraterrestrial origin."

The authors' chief source is "mathematician, physicist, and author Maurice Chatelain, a designer of the Apollo spacecraft and former chief of the NASA Communications for the Apollo lunar missions," who, drawing on "inside sources" from his 1960s NASA work, claimed such encounters "have generally been censored, altered, de-emphasized, or simply ignored by NASA." Because the astronauts "were on military duty," Chatelain said, "they could simply be ordered not to discuss aspects of their UFO encounters." He is quoted: "All Apollo or Gemini flights were followed, both at a distance and sometimes . . . quite closely, by space vehicles of extraterrestrial origin. Every time it occurred, the astronauts informed Mission Control, who then ordered absolute silence."

The chapter also cites an Anglia TV–linked source alleging NASA changed the Apollo 11 *Eagle* landing site because the first was "crawling" with "somebody else's space hardware," quoting an allegedly "deleted" exchange involving Buzz Aldrin and Mission Control, plus rumored astronaut/cosmonaut observations of "ruins, constructions, and 'pyramids' on the moon."

## Contexts Within the Source

The astronaut material opens the book's argument that UFO sightings "increas[e] in direct proportion to our scientific and technological development," establishing a pattern of governmental UFO secrecy that the authors then apply to Roswell.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The authors themselves note that "several of the astronauts have flatly denied experiencing UFO sightings in space," and that "NASA is reported to have dismissed one of its employees for allegedly selling 'falsified tape recordings'" of such conversations — leaving the claims resting on Chatelain's "inside accounts" and unnamed intermediaries.
