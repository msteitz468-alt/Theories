## Claim Statement

Berlitz and Moore argue that a genuine extraterrestrial craft crashed near Roswell in July 1947 and that the Army Air Force recovered its debris — material so unlike anything terrestrial that the recovering intelligence officer, Major Jesse Marcel, called it "nothing made on this earth."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The original July 8, 1947 press release from the 509th Bomb Group stated the intelligence office "was fortunate enough to gain possession of a disc," recovered by Marcel from a ranch after rancher Mac Brazel notified Sheriff Wilcox — reported worldwide ("RAAF Captures Flying Saucer," *Roswell Daily Record*; AP; the London *Times*). The authors describe "a great quantity of highly unusual wreckage, much of it metallic," extraordinarily tough, bearing "columns of hieroglyphic-like writing… on a wooden-like substance (that was not wood)." They place it amid a documented New Mexico UFO wave and the region's atomic/rocket research (Los Alamos, White Sands, the 509th).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The account rests heavily on decades-later recollections, and the authors themselves note the wall of silence and some witnesses possibly "latching on" for publicity. Rival explanations (covered elsewhere in the wiki) include the official Project Mogul balloon and Annie Jacobsen's Soviet-craft theory.
