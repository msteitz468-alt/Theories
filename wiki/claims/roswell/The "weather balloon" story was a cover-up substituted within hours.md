## Claim Statement

Berlitz and Moore argue the famous "weather balloon" explanation was a hastily improvised cover-up: within hours of the "captured disc" announcement, a news blackout descended and the recovered object was re-described as a downed balloon, with the real debris (and bodies) spirited away.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

They report Brig. Gen. **Roger Ramey**, alerted by Deputy Air Force Chief Lt. Gen. **Hoyt Vandenberg**, rebuked Col. Blanchard for the press release and ordered the debris flown via Fort Worth to Wright-Patterson; Ramey then went on the radio to call it "the remains of a downed weather balloon" — adding the qualification "there is no such gadget [as a flying disc] known to the Army… at least not at this level." The authors cite the *Washington Post*'s pointed reference to a "news blackout," the teletype interruption to KOAT Albuquerque ("DO NOT TRANSMIT… STOP COMMUNICATION IMMEDIATELY"), and reporter Johnny McBoyle being told to "forget about it… It never happened."

## Contexts Within the Source

The cover-up half of the Roswell case; "Holes in the Cover-up" (Ch. 6) catalogues the inconsistencies.
