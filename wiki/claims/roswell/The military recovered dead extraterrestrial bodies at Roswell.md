## Claim Statement

Berlitz and Moore claim that, in addition to the debris field, witnesses reported a second site holding the bodies of the craft's dead crew — "the remains of half a dozen or so humanoid creatures, pale in skin coloring, about four feet tall, and dressed in a kind of one-piece jump-suit uniform."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The authors attribute these accounts to "ranchers, soldiers, a civil engineer, a group of student archaeologists, and law enforcement officers" who observed two distinct sites "apparently connected with the same crash." Chapter 5 ("Descriptions of the Aliens") assembles the witness descriptions of the small humanoid bodies. The bodies, like the debris, were said to have been removed under heavy security and flown out.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The bodies claim is the most contested element, resting entirely on second- and third-hand witness recollection gathered decades later; no physical evidence is presented. (Jacobsen's competing account reinterprets such "bodies" as surgically altered child aviators — see Related.)
