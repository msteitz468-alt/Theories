## Claim Statement

Area 51's first customer was the Atomic Energy Commission, not the CIA. The AEC used the site from 1951 for radical research on aircraft and pilot-related projects without oversight or ethical controls—four years before the CIA identified it for the U-2.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "Never before disclosed is the fact that Area 51's first customer was not the CIA but the Atomic Energy Commission. Beginning in 1951, the Atomic Energy Commission used its parallel system of secret-keeping to conduct radical and controversial research, development, and engineering not just on aircraft but also on pilot-related projects—entirely without oversight or ethical controls."

Jacobsen notes the AEC normally had no jurisdiction over aircraft, which she presents as a deliberate shell-game aspect of black-world operations.

## Contexts Within the Source

Presented as correcting the common black-world account that Area 51 opened in 1955 solely for the U-2. Jacobsen states no one had thought to look at the AEC to solve the riddle of Area 51 for more than sixty years.
