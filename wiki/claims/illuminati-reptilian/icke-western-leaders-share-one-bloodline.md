## Claim Statement

Icke asserts that the apparent leaders of the Western world — European royalty and aristocracy, and the US presidents and "Eastern Establishment" — are **not independent figures but offshoots of one interbreeding [[Babylonian Brotherhood (Icke)|Brotherhood]] bloodline**, deliberately placed in power and removed if they defy the Agenda.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Icke states "Nineteen presidents of the United States have also been related to Edward III" (the monarch who founded the Order of the Garter), and that "Prince Charles can trace three thousand lines of descent ... from Edward III." He describes the American "Eastern Establishment" — "the American bloodlines who run the United States according to the Agenda decided in the British Isles and France" — and traces the **Kennedy, Bouvier, Auchincloss, Fitzgerald** and related lines into Scottish/Irish royalty, the Black Nobility, and Merovingian France, calling JFK's marriage "another arranged marriage by the Eastern Establishment."

He frames leadership as conditional on obedience: "It doesn't matter who you are, if you don't do as you are told, you are removed in the most appropriate way, bloodline or not. The Agenda is far greater than any individual or family" — his explanation for the (claimed ritual) removals of JFK and Diana.

## Contexts Within the Source

This genealogical claim underpins the whole Brotherhood thesis: that elections and dynasties are facade over a single hereditary network.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The "related to Edward III" statistic reflects the ordinary mathematics of medieval descent (vast numbers of people descend from such a figure); Icke presents the shared ancestry as evidence of a controlling design rather than a demographic commonplace.
