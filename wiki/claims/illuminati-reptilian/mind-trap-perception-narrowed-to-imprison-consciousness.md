## Claim Statement

Icke argues "the trap" is fundamentally a **trap of perception**: a "deeply evil (inverted) force" — the "'Gods' of the Matrix," operating across the "3-D and 4-D" with "the same goal" — deliberately narrows human awareness to the five senses and the survival-level "reality bubble," imprisoning the infinite consciousness we actually are. Liberation comes by expanding perception beyond the manufactured limits.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Icke describes humanity confined to "myopic perception," with "the cell door slam[ming] shut on wisdom" whenever the intellect rules over expanded consciousness. He frames the suppression of "Forbidden Knowledge" (Ch. 6) and the manipulation of belief as the means by which the Matrix's controllers keep the "All-Seeing 'I'" (true self-awareness) shut down — the perceptual machinery that his later book reframes as the [[The simulation is a soul trap run by Yaldabaoth to entrap the Divine Spark|soul trap]].
