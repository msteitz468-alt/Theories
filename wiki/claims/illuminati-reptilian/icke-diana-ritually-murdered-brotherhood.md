## Claim Statement

Icke asserts that Diana, Princess of Wales, was **deliberately killed in a staged car crash** organised by agencies under the control of the [[Babylonian Brotherhood (Icke)]] — a **ritual blood sacrifice** of Diana as a living symbol of the Moon goddess Diana, performed at an ancient sacrificial site.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The chapter opens: "Agencies under the control of this spider's web of interconnecting families and interests were responsible for the murder of Diana, Princess of Wales. Of that I have no doubt." Icke argues a multi-agency cover-up is feasible by analogy to the JFK assassination ("For Lee Harvey Oswald in Dealey Plaza ... read **Henri Paul** in the Pont de L'Alma Tunnel"), casting driver Henri Paul as the "scapegoat" or "patsy" (noting Prozac traces in his blood).

He frames the death as long-planned ritual: Diana, "named after the ancient Moon goddess," was driven past the Luxor obelisk at the Place de la Concorde (where Marie Antoinette was beheaded), then to the Pont de l'Alma — the "Bridge of the Moon Goddess" — where "the car struck the **13th pillar** ... Now she was left to die on the very site of an ancient underground sacrificial chamber to the goddess Diana and bled to death according to Satanic ritual." He relays [[Arizona Wilder]]'s claim that **Guy de Rothschild** (pseudonym "Dr Barrington") said he was in the tunnel "to steal Diana's soul," and that the gold flame "shrine" above the tunnel is the Brotherhood's signature, matching JFK's grave.

## Contexts Within the Source

Presented as the culmination of the book's thesis: Diana's Spencer bloodline, her symbolic name, and the sacred geography are woven into a claim of centuries-planned ritual murder. Icke invites readers to "look at the evidence and judge for yourself."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Icke acknowledges that "many people dismiss the idea that Diana was assassinated because it would be impossible to ... cover up the evidence," and answers the objection rather than conceding it; he presents no forensic or official finding supporting a deliberate killing.
