## Claim Statement

Icke asserts that the Pont de l'Alma in Paris sits on an **ancient Merovingian underground chamber used for human sacrifice to the goddess Diana**, located on a major ley-line "vortex" — making it, in his reading, the predestined ritual location for Diana's death.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"The Merovingians established the city of Paris on a major vortex point and used an underground chamber there for their rituals, including human sacrifice to the goddess Diana ... The location of the ancient underground chamber in Paris where the Merovingians worshipped and sacrificed to the goddess Diana is now called the Pont and Place de l'Alma and it is still, by design, an underground chamber. On the site today is a road tunnel."

The etymological core: "*Alma* comes from **Al-Mah, a Middle Eastern name for ... the Moon goddess**. So the Pont de l'Alma translates as 'Bridge of the Moon Goddess' ... It can also translate as 'Bridge of the Soul,' and a 'Maiden' of the goddess Diana was called an Al-mah." He links Meroveus, founder of the Merovingian dynasty, to "the Pagan goddess cult of Diana," and notes Notre Dame was "built on a site dedicated to the goddess Diana." He adds the crossroads symbolism of Hecate (an aspect of Diana) and the claim that 31 August is Hecate's reverse-symbolism day of sacrifice.

## Contexts Within the Source

This sacred-geography argument is what licenses Icke's reading of the crash as ritual rather than accident; it ties the Templar/Merovingian/Diana-worship material to the modern tunnel.
