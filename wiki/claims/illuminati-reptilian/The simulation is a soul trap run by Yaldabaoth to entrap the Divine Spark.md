## Claim Statement

Icke argues the holographic simulation was created by demonic, "Astral" entities — fronted by the Gnostic false-creator **Yaldabaoth** (the Demiurge) — specifically as a **"soul trap"** to capture and recycle human consciousness, the "Divine Spark." Reincarnation, sold as a chance to "learn lessons," is on his account part of the trap: a mechanism that keeps the Spark imprisoned in the simulation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Icke writes that "the demonic created the simulation to" entrap, and that even after physical death the captured consciousness "is still in the simulation"; "the game is to so program [the] Divine Spark" that it re-enters. He critiques the popular belief that "reincarnation is necessary to 'learn lessons'" as the bait of the trap. He draws explicitly on Gnostic texts (the Nag Hammadi cosmology of Yaldabaoth and the Archons).
