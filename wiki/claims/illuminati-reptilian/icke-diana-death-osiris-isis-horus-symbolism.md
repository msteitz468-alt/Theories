## Claim Statement

Beyond merely asserting that Diana was murdered, Icke offers a detailed **occult-symbolic decoding** of her death, reading it as a deliberate re-enactment of ancient Egyptian and Greek goddess ritual. The crash, he argues, was staged so that **Diana embodied Isis, Dodi Fayed embodied Osiris, and Diana's alleged unborn foetus embodied Horus** — the Osiris-Isis-Horus "trinity" the Brotherhood is obsessed with — while the killing simultaneously enacted the worship of **Hecate**, the "negative" aspect of the Moon-goddess Diana, at her sacred crossroads.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Chapter 19 ("The Goddess and the King") layers the symbolism explicitly. Icke identifies Diana with a single recurring "female energy" worshipped under many names: "Diana was one of the greatest goddesses of the ancient world ... Barati, Britannia, Isis, Artemis, Aphrodite ... also, Ninkharsag. Diana was known as a Moon goddess."

He invokes the **Diana–Luna–Hecate triad**: "one of the ancient 'triads' or 'trinities' was that of Diana, Luna and Hecate ... Diana on Earth, Luna in Heaven, and Hecate in Hell," with Hecate "one of the premier deities of Satanism." Crossroads are "the sacred places of Diana-Hecate," and the Pont de l'Alma site is "a maze of crossroads." He claims Hecate's calendar day of sacrifice, "under the Satanic law of reverse symbolism," is **August 31st** — the date of the crash.

He then reads the route as ritual: the car "passed the 3,200 year-old Egyptian obelisk brought to Paris from Luxor with its tip ... coloured bright gold symbolising the penis of Osiris," near where Marie Antoinette was beheaded ("another symbolic blood sacrifice of the female energy"), before striking "the 13th pillar." On the **Osiris-Isis-Horus** layer: "The Brotherhood are obsessed with the symbolism in ancient Egypt of the Osiris-Horus-Isis trinity (Nimrod-Tammuz-Semiramis) ... In Paris, part of this ritual was played out again ... inside the vehicle were Diana (another name for Isis) and the Egyptian, Dodi Fayed, representing Osiris ... Her foetus symbolised Horus, the Sun King. In ancient Egypt, they said that Osiris had to die before Horus could come in." Icke says [[Arizona Wilder]] confirmed it: "Diana was Isis, Dodi Fayed was Osiris, and the child was Horus ... the birth of an age — the Age of Horus which begins by the year 2,000 ... the New World Order, the Age of Horus." He also relays Wilder's account of a prior royal ritual using "a golden penis (Osiris symbolism)" of "reptilian shape and size."

Funeral details are folded in as confirming symbolism: burial on an island ("classic symbolism" of Diana worship), the lead coffin (lead = Saturn = "Satan"), the lilies of Lilith/Hecate, and the Althorp black swans ("Hecate kills Diana," as in *Swan Lake*).

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the esoteric core of Icke's Diana case: where the companion claims assert *that* she was killed and *where*, this claim supplies the *why* and the ritual grammar — a mythological template (Osiris dies so Horus is born) onto which he maps the crash, the passengers, and the alleged pregnancy. He concedes it "will sound amazing to most people," but argues it "makes perfect sense" given the Brotherhood's "unbelievable obsession" with ritual.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Icke's evidence for the keystone element — the pregnancy that makes Diana's foetus "Horus" — is hearsay ("I have heard from many sources that Diana was pregnant ... Diana may not even have realized she was pregnant"); he offers no medical confirmation and frames the symbolic reading as interpretation rather than established fact.
