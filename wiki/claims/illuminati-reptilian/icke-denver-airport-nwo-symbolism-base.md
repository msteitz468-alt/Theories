## Claim Statement

Icke asserts that **Denver International Airport** is a Brotherhood landmark whose architecture, dedication stone, and murals encode the coming **New World Order**, and that it conceals a deep **underground reptilian-human base** — naming Denver as the planned headquarters of the NWO's "Western sector" of the United States.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 1 Icke cites researcher Alex Christopher and describes a personal visit: landing there in August 1996 he "felt a very strange energy, very weird and unpleasant." He reads the building as Masonic: "The airport ... is full of Masonic symbols. There are also **gargoyles**, the winged-reptile figures ... Gargoyles are symbols of the reptilians and that is why you will find them at Denver Airport. The **capstone or dedication stone** ... is marked with the classic compass symbol of the Freemasons and it stands in part of the terminal called **The Great Hall**, another Freemasonic term."

He describes the **murals** as "a grotesque mural full of malevolent symbolism, including **three caskets with dead females** in them: a Jewish girl, a Native American and a black woman. Another girl is holding a Mayan tablet that tells of the destruction of civilisation. A huge character, described as a 'green Darth Vader' ... stands over a destroyed city with a sword in his hand and women are walking along a road holding **dead babies**. All the children of the world are depicted ... handing [weapons] to a figure of a German boy with an iron fist and an anvil."

He frames the location geopolitically: "Denver is apparently scheduled to be the headquarters of the **Western sector** of the United States under the fascist global state called the New World Order ... Atlanta is said to be the centre for the Eastern sector," and notes the Queen of England "under another name, has been buying up land" in Colorado. On the **underground base**, citing Phil Schneider ("the son of a German U-boat commander" who "later died in very suspicious circumstances ... meant to appear like 'suicide'"): "Denver Airport was connected to a deep underground base that went down at least eight levels ... a 4.5 square mile underground city and a vast base," with "large numbers of human slaves, many of them children, working there under the control of the reptilians" — linking it to the tunnel network connecting Area 51 and Dulce.

## Contexts Within the Source

Placed in the opening chapter as a tangible "look around you" exhibit foreshadowing the whole thesis: that the Brotherhood telegraphs its planned New World Order through architecture and symbolism. It ties the airport's reptilian gargoyles back to the "reptilian aristocracy" motif and forward to the royal-bloodline and Diana material.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Icke attributes the underground-base and mural readings to third parties (Alex Christopher, Phil Schneider) and his own impressions ("apparently," "reputed to be," "alleged underground reptilian base"), presenting no documentary or official confirmation within the text.
