## Claim Statement

In the run-up to Italy's April 1948 national elections, Dulles and Defense Secretary James Forrestal raised millions of dollars to tilt the vote to the U.S.-backed Christian Democrats and defeat the Italian Communist Party. Cash was handed to Italian agents at Rome's Hassler Hotel; the Christian Democrats won. Talbot says this established a "proprietary" CIA relationship with Italian politics that persisted into the 1960s.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Talbot reports that in March 1948 Angleton flew from Rome to warn Dulles the Communists might win, prompting the funding drive; on election night Dulles watched returns at Forrestal's home. Later, under director Dulles, the CIA paid the Christian Democratic Party ~60 million lire/month (about $100,000), collected in a suitcase by party secretary Sereno Freato. CIA agent F. Mark Wyatt described serving as a 1948 "bagman" delivering cash at the Hassler.
