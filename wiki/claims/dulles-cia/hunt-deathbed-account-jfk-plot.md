## Claim Statement

Talbot recounts that ex-CIA officer and Watergate figure [[E. Howard Hunt]], near death in 2003–2007, told his son Saint John Hunt that he had been approached in 1963 about an "off-the-board" CIA operation to assassinate President Kennedy — referred to by the plotters as "the big event." Hunt named [[William King Harvey]] (who allegedly hired the shooters and moved the weapons, possibly Corsican gangsters) and CIA "hit man" [[David Sánchez Morales]], and said Vice President Johnson had "signed off."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Hunt left video interviews, audiotapes, handwritten notes, and the memoir *American Spy*; his son published *Bond of Secrecy*. Talbot says only *Rolling Stone* and a few outlets covered it. Morales reportedly told his attorney Robert Walton in 1973, "We took care of that son of a bitch, didn't we?" House Assassinations Committee investigator Dan Hardway is quoted: "We considered Harvey to be one of our prime suspects from the very start." CIA officer F. Mark Wyatt is said to have suspected Harvey was in Dallas in November 1963.

## Contexts Within the Source

Talbot argues Hunt's account is "carefully parsed" and self-protective: Hunt named Harvey and Morales but, "loyal to the end," would not name [[Allen Dulles]] — whom Talbot contends was the only figure with the stature to assure LBJ the plot had high-level backing. The plot is rooted in the anti-Castro/ZR-RIFLE milieu (Harvey, Morales, David Atlee Phillips, Cord Meyer).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Talbot himself flags the weaknesses: he says it is "simply not credible" that LBJ dealt directly with someone of Harvey's rank, and that Hunt downplayed his own role and "couched much of his narrative in an oddly speculative manner."
