## Claim Statement

According to former Justice Department Nazi-hunter John Loftus, cited by Talbot, the Dulles–Nixon alliance began in late 1945 when naval officer Richard Nixon, handling war paperwork, came across Nazi documents showing how the Dulles brothers had laundered Nazi funds. Loftus alleged Dulles "told him to keep quiet about what he had seen and, in return, arranged to finance the young man's first congressional campaign against Jerry Voorhis."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Talbot reports the public alliance was forged on the 1947 *Queen Mary* Marshall Plan junket (the Herter Committee), where Dulles and Christian Herter "took the young congressman under their wing." He notes Dulles and his oil/banking clients had reason to target Voorhis, a New Dealer who pushed to nationalize the regional Federal Reserve Banks and attacked Standard Oil and the [[Bank for International Settlements]]. Loftus called Nixon "Allen Dulles's mouthpiece in Congress."

## Contexts Within the Source

The claim is part of Talbot's argument that the Hiss–Chambers and Harry Dexter White cases let the Dulles circle turn the table on New Dealers who might have prosecuted *them* for wartime collaboration. At the August 1948 Roosevelt Hotel meeting, the Dulles brothers, Herter, and Dillon coached Nixon to pursue Hiss while keeping the spotlight off Foster Dulles.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Talbot relies on Loftus "citing confidential intelligence sources" for the financing claim — i.e., it is presented as an allegation, not documented in the same way as the public Marshall Plan partnership.
