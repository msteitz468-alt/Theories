## Claim Statement

Dulles used Nazi gold he had helped track down at the war's end to finance early Cold War covert operations. Talbot states at least $2 million channeled into the National Committee for a Free Europe — a CIA front that funded anti-Communist émigrés and Radio Free Europe — "came from the Nazi gold that Dulles had helped track down."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "In the early years of the Cold War, the Nazi treasure looted from Jewish families and German-occupied nations would become a key source of funding for Dulles's secret operations." (Ch. 7)

The National Committee for a Free Europe (June 1949) had a board including Eisenhower, Cecil B. DeMille, and Henry Luce.

## Contexts Within the Source

This connects to Dulles's wartime opposition to Project Safehaven (which sought to confiscate hidden Nazi assets) — Talbot argues Dulles preferred to use the loot for anti-Soviet operations rather than return it to victims' families.
