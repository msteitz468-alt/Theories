## Claim Statement

When President Kennedy endorsed Italy's *apertura a sinistra* ("opening to the left") during his July 1963 visit, the CIA's Rome operatives — newly led by [[William King Harvey]] and overseen by [[James Jesus Angleton]] — worked to subvert it, even as Harvey allegedly pushed Italian intelligence (SIFAR) to carry out false-flag bombings of Christian Democratic targets to be blamed on the left.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Talbot, citing CIA officer F. Mark Wyatt, says Harvey urged SIFAR's Colonel Renzo Rocca to use "action squads" for bombings, and proposed recruiting Mafia hit men to kill Italian Communist officials; when Wyatt objected, "Harvey pulled a gun on Wyatt." The CIA partnered with Italy on Operation Gladio, the secret NATO "stay-behind army." The 1964 de Lorenzo coup threat forced the Socialists to weaken the coalition. Wyatt later said he "always wondered what Bill Harvey was doing in Dallas in November 1963."
