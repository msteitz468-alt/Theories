## Claim Statement

After the war Dulles and his protégé [[James Jesus Angleton]] recruited or sheltered Nazi and fascist figures as anti-Communist intelligence assets, running a Rome safe house (Via Archimede) for fugitives connected to Operation Sunrise and helping build West German intelligence out of the [[Gehlen Organization]] (former Wehrmacht eastern-front intelligence under Reinhard Gehlen). More than 1,600 Nazi scientists were resettled under Operation Paperclip.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Talbot recounts Angleton extracting SS colonel [[Eugen Dollmann]] from a Milan asylum and a Rome prison; sheltering exterminator Walter Rauff (developer of the gas vans, ~250,000 victims) who later advised Pinochet's DINA; and keeping in touch with commando Otto Skorzeny's arms-and-ratline network in Spain. U.S. Army Nazi-hunter Bill Gowen's investigation of these ratlines was, Talbot says, sabotaged: Gowen was framed via a Drew Pearson story planted by Angleton's deputy Raymond Rocca, and his files were stolen in Frankfurt.

## Contexts Within the Source

This is presented as the operational logic that protected [[Karl Wolff]] and as continuous with Dulles's wartime preference for salvaging "moderate" Nazis against the USSR. Talbot notes Dulles himself, in *The Craft of Intelligence* (1963), denounced the Soviets' "massive recruitment" of Nazis — which Talbot calls gall, given Dulles's own role.
