## Claim Statement

Talbot argues the Dulles brothers regarded democracy as "an enterprise that had to be carefully managed by the right men, not simply left to elected officials," and were loyal above all to a Wall Street "power elite" (C. Wright Mills's term) whose interests they conflated with the national interest.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Talbot quotes Allen Dulles on the eve of his failed 1938 congressional run: "Democracy works only if the so-called intelligent people make it work. You can't sit back and let democracy run itself." He cites John Foster Dulles telling corporate clients to defy New Deal law ("Do not comply… Resist the law with all your might") and complaining that "business people… have had to find ways for getting through and around stupid political barriers." Richard Helms is quoted: "Dulles felt most comfortable running things on his own with a minimum of supervision from above." Talbot leans heavily on Mills's *The Power Elite* (1956) and its account of Wall Street lawyer-bankers as the "invisible elite."

## Contexts Within the Source

This is Talbot's framing for why Dulles repeatedly subverted presidents (FDR, Truman, Eisenhower, Kennedy) and underpins the deep-state thesis [[The Devil's Chessboard argues a deep state removed President Kennedy]].
