## Claim Statement

In August 1953 the CIA, with British MI6, overthrew the democratically elected government of Prime Minister [[Mohammad Mossadegh]] — who had nationalized the Anglo-Iranian Oil Company — and restored the autocratic rule of Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi. Dulles considered it one of the two greatest triumphs of his CIA career (with Guatemala 1954). See [[Event: 1953 Iranian coup d'état (Operation Ajax)]].

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Talbot says [[Kermit Roosevelt Jr.]] ran the operation on the ground, hiring mercenaries and bribing officers; CIA-financed mobs took the streets of Tehran on August 19; loyalist officers such as General Mahmoud Afshartous were kidnapped and murdered. Dulles flew to Rome's Hotel Excelsior, arriving the same day as the exiled Shah, to "stiffen the shah's spine." Dulles had personal stakes: through Sullivan & Cromwell he had ties to oil clients, served on the board of J. Henry Schroder (financial agent for Anglo-Iranian), and had earlier negotiated the lucrative Overseas Consultants Inc. deal that Mossadegh's coalition killed.

After the coup Iran's oil was denationalized, with 40% going to U.S. producers; the Shah's SAVAK secret police, with CIA help, tracked down over 4,000 Tudeh Party members between 1953 and 1957, torturing and executing many. U.S. press, Talbot says, was steered to avoid the word "coup."

## Contexts Within the Source

Talbot argues the "Cold War" framing was a pretext; the real conflict was "between imperialism and nationalism." Mossadegh was "a fervent nationalist, not a secret Communist."
