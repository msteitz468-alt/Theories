## Claim Statement

By the end of his presidency Eisenhower concluded that he had delegated far too much to Dulles and could not control him; he told CIA reformers he would leave the next president a "legacy of ashes." Talbot argues Dulles "undermined or betrayed every president he served in high office."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

After the May 1960 U-2 shootdown — which Dulles had assured Eisenhower could not happen — Talbot says Ike told aides he never wanted to see Dulles again, and confided to Gordon Gray, "I've tried. I cannot change Allen Dulles." When Dulles brushed off reform proposals, insisting the CIA without him would be "a body floating in thin air," the president "bitterly remarked" he would leave a "legacy of ashes" (the phrase later titling Tim Weiner's CIA history). Air Force colonel L. Fletcher Prouty is cited suspecting the U-2 incident was engineered by the Dulles "inner elite" to wreck the Paris peace summit.

## Contexts Within the Source

Talbot frames this against the Prologue's claim that Eisenhower felt Dulles had "robbed him of his place in history as a peacemaker." It sets up the transition to Kennedy, who would be the first president to fire Dulles.
