## Claim Statement

Dulles (with [[Frank Wisner]]'s Office of Policy Coordination) deliberately fed Stalin the false story that his former wartime asset [[Noel Field]] was the hub of a U.S. spy ring inside the Eastern bloc — an operation Dulles personally code-named Splinter Factor — knowing it would aggravate Stalin's paranoia and ignite purges. Talbot says the plot "succeeded beyond the OPC's wildest dreams": hundreds of thousands were arrested across Eastern Europe, many tortured and executed.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Talbot states a CIA double agent in Polish security, Józef Światło, spread the disinformation. Field, his wife Herta, brother Hermann, and foster daughter Erica Wallach all vanished behind the Iron Curtain; interrogators repeatedly demanded, "How do you know Allen Dulles?" In Czechoslovakia ~170,000 party members were seized over the fictitious plot. Wisner reported: "The comrades are merrily sticking knives in each others' backs and doing our dirty work for us." Wallach survived five years in the Vorkuta gulag.

> Wallach (1993): "Anything that destabilized the situation in Eastern Europe was good for U.S. interests… By fanning the flames, you could turn the people against communism. The strategy is completely understandable."

## Contexts Within the Source

Talbot uses the Field affair to illustrate Dulles's willingness to use old acquaintances "as pawns" — the chapter title "Little Mice" comes from Dulles's own remark about watching mice in a trap, "breaking their little necks."
