## Claim Statement

Marrs's central distinction: the **Bavarian Illuminati** (the cohesive German order founded 1776, suppressed by 1787) "no longer exists," but **Illuminism** — the doctrine that a self-enlightened elite may rule others through secrecy and "the end justifies the means" — is "very much alive" and has spread worldwide. He argues one must separate "the old Bavarian organization, which is now defunct, and the theology of Illuminism."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "Any reference to the Illuminati must distinguish between the old Bavarian organization, which is now defunct, and the theology of Illuminism, which is very much alive." (Introduction)

In the Conclusions he writes that Illuminism is "simply another 'ism,'… a philosophy, a mindset," and that figures like Clinton or Cheney need not carry "a membership card" — "both practice Illuminism." He defines the doctrine as the belief that the illuminated "alone had developed a personal enlightenment not accessible to the rest of humankind," with "the desire to control others, to operate in secrecy, and adhere to the idea that the end justifies the means."
