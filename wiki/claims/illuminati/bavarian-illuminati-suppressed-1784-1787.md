## Claim Statement

The Bavarian Illuminati's rapid growth and infiltration of Freemasonry provoked alarm; after defectors and rivals (Rosicrucians, a Berlin Masonic lodge, three Marianum Academy professors) warned that the order undermined Christianity and the state and condoned poisoning and suicide, Bavarian Elector Karl Theodor issued edicts banning all secret societies beginning June 22, 1784. Combined with Knigge's 1784 resignation, this brought the order's swift decline; subsequent raids (seized papers at Zwack's house and Sandersdorf Castle) exposed its documents.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Marrs reports the 1783 Rosicrucian pamphlet *The Illuminati Unmasked*, the defectors' depositions, and Karl Theodor's escalating edicts. Confiscated correspondence (including Weishaupt's own letters about deception and an attempted abortion of his sister-in-law's child) was published by the government, damaging the order's reputation. "The existence of the Bavarian Illuminati was drawing to an end."
