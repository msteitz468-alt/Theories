## Claim Statement

Marrs relays the widely repeated claim that the Illuminati was financially tied to — and by some accounts created by — the Rothschild banking dynasty. Mayer Amschel Rothschild was the financial agent of the Illuminati-connected royal William IX of Hesse-Kassel; the Rothschild fortune was reportedly launched with funds (paid by Britain for Hessian soldiers to fight the American colonists) that William entrusted to Mayer and that were diverted to establish his son Nathan in London, where he "gained control over the Bank of England." Mayer's five sons became the central bankers of Frankfurt, London, Paris, Vienna, and Naples.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Marrs cites biographers Wilson and Ferguson on the diverted Hesse-Kassel money and the Rothschilds' "proximity to politicians," and conspiracy writers (Silver Bear, Livingston, Carr) who claim Weishaupt was "commissioned" by Mayer Rothschild and the "thirteen bloodlines" in 1773 to build the order. He also links Mayer's bookkeeper Seligmann Geisenheimer and the Thurn und Taxis postal family (privy to intercepted mail) to the network.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Marrs presents the "Rothschild created the Illuminati" version as the view of specific conspiracy writers ("Many conspiracy writers link…"), distinct from the documented banking facts, and Masonic historian Mackey doubts the order's anti-Christian aims. He repeatedly stresses the conspiracy is not a Jewish one.
