## Claim Statement

Marrs describes Weishaupt's organizational design as a compartmentalized pyramid that guaranteed his total control: each member knew only his recruiter and his own subordinates, so the order's hierarchy stayed hidden even from its members. It used coded names and a graded degree system (the Nursery, the Masonry, and the Mysteries classes) modeled on the Jesuits and Freemasonry, with built-in surveillance — recruiters ("Insinuators") and spies ("Scrutators") — and required members to write detailed intelligence reports on everyone they met, including family.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Marrs quotes Weishaupt to Zwack: "Immediately under me, I have two [subordinates] into whom I infuse my whole spirit; these two in turn correspond with two others, and so on… I will inflame a thousand men into action." Novices signed a "Silentio" nondisclosure and kept notebooks recording others' "weaknesses, prejudices, actions, financial status, interests, and passions." Only a few ever reached the top Mysteries grades (Magus, and King — reserved for Weishaupt), where "the real objectives of the Order: rule of the world… after the destruction of all existing governments and religions" were revealed.

## Contexts Within the Source

Marrs notes the cell structure was later mirrored by "communist cells… and today's terrorists," and that the intimate-disclosure practice "was later adopted by the Skull & Bones Society."
