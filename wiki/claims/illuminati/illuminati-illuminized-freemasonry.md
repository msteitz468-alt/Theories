## Claim Statement

Marrs argues that the Illuminati spread by infiltrating Freemasonry, producing "Illuminized Freemasonry" — a blending of Masonic structure with Illuminati doctrine and older Cabalistic/Templar lore. Aided by the fusion with the Masonic "Order of Strict Observance" (formalized around the 1782 Congress of Wilhelmsbad), Illuminism penetrated the lodges so that, he says, by 1788 "nearly all Masonic lodges in Europe… had been infiltrated."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Marrs cites Masonic authors on Freemasonry's inner/outer duality — Manly P. Hall's "fraternity within a fraternity… the one visible and the other invisible," and the rule that only initiates "beyond thirty-three-degree status are educated in the group's true goals." He notes the 1826 disappearance of defector Captain William Morgan and the resulting Anti-Masonic Party (the first U.S. third party), and a Massachusetts committee's finding of "an intimate connection between the high orders of Masonry and French Illuminism." He stresses American and European Freemasonry diverged in the 19th century.
