## Claim Statement

Marrs presents the operational ethics of Illuminism as deliberate deception of both the public and the order's own lower ranks, the use of espionage and blackmail to keep members in line, and the maxim that "the end sanctifies the means." Recruits were enlisted as "dupes as well as adepts," led toward "a goal entirely unknown to them," and superiors were to keep their "real purpose… impenetrable to one's inferiors."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Marrs quotes a 1785 deposition by three defecting academics listing the principles taught: "**The end sanctifies the means**… calumnies, poisonings, assassinations, perjuries, treasons, rebellions"; that a member "must be free from all religion"; and obedience to the Superiors above all sovereigns. From confiscated notes: "Devote yourselves to the art of deception… spying on others and perceiving their innermost thoughts." Weishaupt: "We cannot use people as they are, but begin by making them over," and "The great strength of our Order lies in its concealment." Marrs links this ethic to the slogan **Ordo ab Chao** ("Order out of Chaos") and the Hegelian "problem, reaction, solution" dialectic.
