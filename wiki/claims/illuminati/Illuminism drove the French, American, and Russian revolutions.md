## Claim Statement

Marrs argues that Illuminism — surviving inside Freemasonry after the Bavarian order's suppression — was a driving force behind the modern revolutionary tradition: the French and American Revolutions, the Russian Revolution and communism, and movements from anarchism and socialism to (in his framing) Nazism. He cites Librarian of Congress James H. Billington that "the modern revolutionary tradition" stems from "Bavarian Illuminism."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Marrs notes Founding Fathers Washington and Jefferson wrote of their awareness of the Illuminati; William McIlhany's list of Illuminati-created movements (Marxian and utopian socialism, anarchism, Pan-Slavism, nationalisms, Fabian Socialism, "Leninist Bolshevism"); the 1829 New York meeting where "Fanny" Wright reportedly promoted a coming "communist" movement to foment war; and the claim (via Carr) that an "Illuminized Freemasonry" World Revolutionary Movement engineered the 1914 assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand to trigger WWI. He frames the post-WWII Cold War as the elite "pitting a socialist East against a capitalist West."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Marrs flags that several revolution claims rest on circular Internet sourcing, and he explicitly debunks the related Pike "three world wars" letter as a hoax — see [[The Albert Pike "three world wars" letter is a debunked hoax]].
