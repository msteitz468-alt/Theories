## Claim Statement

Marrs reports — and then debunks — the widely circulated claim that 33rd-degree Mason Albert Pike wrote an 1871 letter to Giuseppe Mazzini predicting three world wars engineered by the Illuminati (against the Czars/for communism; exploiting fascism vs. Zionism to create Israel; and setting Islam against Zionism). He records that diligent researchers could find no proof the letter exists.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Marrs follows Terry Melanson tracing the Pike quotes through Canadian author William Guy Carr back to an 1892 French book, *The Devil in the 19th Century* by "Dr. Bataille" — actually Gabriel Jogand-Pagès — which alleged a "Palladian Society" of Pike and Mazzini but contained **no mention of world wars**, and whose author admitted in 1897 that "his story was a hoax." Marrs quotes Melanson: "perhaps you're disappointed to have found nothing about a prediction of three world wars… What it truly represents is the scurrilous fantasies, and militant anti-Catholicism of its author."
