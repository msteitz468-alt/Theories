## Claim Statement

Donald Hoffman asserts that **symmetries we perceive entail nothing about the structure of objective reality**. The claim is backed by the **[[Invention of Symmetry Theorem]]** (Hoffman conjectured, Chetan Prakash proved), which shows constructively that perceptions and actions can possess a symmetry — translation, rotation, mirror, or Lorentz — in a world that has no symmetry whatever.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 7** introduces the theorem to rebut the vision scientist **Zygmunt Pizlo**, who argued that because we see animal bodies as mirror-symmetric, mirror symmetry must be a genuine feature of objective reality ("seeing a mirror-symmetrical object as mirror-symmetrical is not possible unless..."). Hoffman recasts this as the precise claim "any symmetry in our perceptions entails a corresponding symmetry in objective reality" — and the Invention of Symmetry Theorem proves it false.

Hoffman's positive explanation for why we perceive symmetry: **data compression and error correction**. Their algorithms typically involve symmetries; e.g. predicting an apple's appearance after a small movement needs only five numbers (a rotation and translation) rather than millions per view. The theorem applies to any perceptual system, biological or machine — "we can build a robot that sees the symmetries we see. But this grants us no insight into the structure of the world."

## Contexts Within the Source

The claim is a companion to the [[Fitness-Beats-Truth (FBT) Theorem]] and supports the broader proposal that [[Spacetime and physical objects are a data-compression and error-correcting code for fitness]]. It also undercuts Pizlo's further evolutionary argument that successful planning and coordination require veridical perception (Hoffman: online games like Grand Theft Auto show plans guided by a "fake world," not veridical perception).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Pizlo's realist position is quoted directly and at length as the target. Hoffman treats it as refuted by the theorem rather than presenting any internal tension in his own claim.
