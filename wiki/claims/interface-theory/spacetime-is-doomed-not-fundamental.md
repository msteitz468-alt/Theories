## Claim Statement

Donald Hoffman asserts that spacetime is **not fundamental** in physical reality — and that its physical objects are doomed with it. A new theory is required "in which spacetime, objects, their properties, and their fiction of cause and effect, sprout from a more primordial ground." He presents this as a near-consensus among physicists that *converges* with the prediction of the [[Interface Theory of Perception (ITP)]] that spacetime is merely a species-specific interface, not objective reality.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 6** marshals the physics:
- **Wheeler's delayed-choice experiment** (a variation on the double-slit), extended to cosmic scale with the Twin Quasar QSO 0957+561: "our choice today determines billions of years of history." Wheeler's slogan "**It from bit**" — information, not matter, is fundamental.
- The **holographic principle** (Jacob Bekenstein and Stephen Hawking; pioneered by Leonard Susskind and Gerard 't Hooft): the information in a region of space is proportional to the *area* of its bounding surface, not its volume; "the three-dimensional world of ordinary experience... is a hologram." Spacetime has a smallest length (the Planck length) and is pixelated.
- **Black-hole complementarity** (Susskind) and the **AMPS firewall paradox** (Almheiri, Marolf, Polchinski, Sully) — both show there is no single observer-independent spacetime containing inside and outside a horizon.
- **Hawking–Hertog top-down cosmology**: "histories of the universe... depend on what is being observed," even for the big bang.

Chapter 7 opens with the quotations Hoffman repeatedly cites: **Nima Arkani-Hamed** ("Almost all of us believe... that spacetime is doomed, and has to be replaced by some more primitive building blocks"), David Gross, Edward Witten, and Nathan Seiberg ("I am almost certain that space and time are illusions").

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the chapter where ITP "trespasses into the turf of physics." Hoffman's argument is not that physics *proves* ITP, but that ITP's bold prediction (spacetime not fundamental) "has not [been ruled out]. Instead, it has remarkable support." He explicitly notes the survey is "far from exhaustive" and omits realist interpretations (Bohm, Everett).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hoffman acknowledges the jury is "still out" on whether realism or locality is false, and that some physicists pursue interpretations preserving objective spacetime. The claim sets up chapter 7's positive proposal that spacetime is instead a [[Spacetime and physical objects are a data-compression and error-correcting code for fitness]].
