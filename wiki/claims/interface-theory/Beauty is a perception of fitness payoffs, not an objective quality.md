## Claim Statement

Donald Hoffman asserts that beauty is not a quality in objects but "the intelligent verdict of a complex but mostly unconscious computation" — an estimate of **reproductive potential** (the likelihood that a person could raise healthy offspring). Beauty is therefore a perception of [[Fitness payoff function|fitness payoffs]], which vary "from species to species, person to person, and even time to time." This is offered as a concrete, evidence-backed warm-up for the book's general thesis that perception reports fitness, not objective reality.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 2** combines David Hume ("beauty... exists merely in the mind which contemplates them") with Darwin to argue beauty tracks fitness. Evidence from Hoffman's own lab on cues in the human eye:
- **Limbal rings** (the dark annulus between iris and sclera): prominent rings signal a clear, disease-free cornea (health) and youth (they thin with age — Darren Peshek's measurements); observers prefer faces with limbal rings, even upside down.
- **Iris size**: larger irises signal youth/fertility; men prefer larger irises in women under fifty (Negar Sammaknejad's experiments). Reproductive *value* peaks at ~age 20, predicting (and confirming) that men over 20 prefer younger women while teen males prefer slightly older women — across cultures.
- **Pupil dilation, scleral color (bluish = youthful), and highlights** (a tear-film signal of youth/health) similarly modulate attraction.
- The non-human cases — male **jewel beetles** courting beer "stubbies," moose mounting bronze statues — show that even full embodied contact yields fitness-tracking perception, not truth (a foretaste of "supernormal stimuli," ch. 9).

Hoffman also covers genes "lying" about fitness (hammer orchids deceiving thynnid wasps; Photuris fireflies luring and eating Photinus males), inclusive fitness (W. D. Hamilton; Belding's ground-squirrel alarm calls), and parental-investment theory explaining why "men court and women choose."

## Contexts Within the Source

Explicitly a warm-up: "our study of beauty is just the background we need to grapple with our central question: Do we perceive reality as it is?... spacetime and physical objects, like beauty, reside in the eye of the beholder."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hoffman pre-empts misreading: the claim that genes shape perceptions of beauty "does not justify sexism, patriarchy, or oppression" any more than genes influencing cancer justify cancer; and exaptation means we do not have sex only to procreate. No internal contradiction to the claim is presented.
