## Claim Statement

Vallée proposes that human action rests on imagination and belief rather than objective observation, so that controlling imagination shapes collective destiny 'provided the source of this control is not identifiable by the public' — and that it is demonstrably possible to make populations believe in supernatural races via a few carefully engineered, culturally tailored scenes.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He states it is possible 'to make large sections of any population believe in the existence of supernatural races ... by exposing them to a few carefully engineered scenes,' but stops short of claiming the UFO phenomenon is such a system or naming any controller.

## Contexts Within the Source

His central conjecture (the 'functioning lie'); he stresses it holds regardless of whether the objects are physical.

## Contrasting Interpretation (Wallis)

Paul Wallis reads many of the same phenomena Vallée treats as belief-shaping "engineered scenes" (Marian/Star-Child apparitions, anomalous conceptions) as **literal** contact with non-human "Sky People" — see [[A worldwide "Star Children" pattern links ancient figures to modern abduction reports]] from [[The Scars of Eden (Wallis)]]. Where Vallée brackets the question of physical reality, Wallis affirms it.

## Kindred Theorist (Keel)

John Keel reached a closely parallel conclusion independently: his [[Keel's ultraterrestrials shape themselves to human belief|ultraterrestrials]] are belief-driven and self-escalating — "the phenomenon adjusts its manifestations to support that belief and thereby escalate it." Keel and Vallée are the twin founders of the interdimensional/control-system school. See [[The Mothman Prophecies (Keel)]].
