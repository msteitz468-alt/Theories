## Claim Statement

Keel's central thesis: the UFO/paranormal phenomena are not extraterrestrial spacecraft but **ultraterrestrials** — intelligences that have always coexisted with humanity, operating "outside the limits of our space-time continuum" as "a state of energy," and manifesting in whatever form a given era's beliefs supply. The phenomenon is belief-driven and self-reinforcing: it adjusts its manifestations to confirm and escalate whatever the percipient already believes.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "Once you have established a belief, the phenomenon adjusts its manifestations to support that belief and thereby escalate it. If you believe in the devil he will surely come striding down your road one rainy night and ask to use your phone. If you believe that flying saucers are astronauts from another planet they will begin landing and collecting rocks from your garden."

Keel notes that ancient peoples read the same beings as gods, demons, or fairies, while moderns "have shaped a new mythology based upon the belief in spacemen carved in our own image" (citing Xenophanes). He invokes "tulpas" (thought-forms), recurring "window areas," and Charles Fort, and explicitly rejects von Däniken's ancient-astronaut literalism. He concedes the alternative possibility that the entities "do not exist at all" and are "aberrations of the human mind."
