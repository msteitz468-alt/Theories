## Claim Statement

Vallée reproduces (via Jerome Cardan's *De Subtilitate*) Facius Cardan's account of seven men in shining silken garments who appeared to him in 1491, said they were 'men composed, as it were, of air, and subject to birth and death,' might live up to 300 years, and disagreed among themselves about whether God created the world from eternity or 'from moment to moment.'

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He quotes the 1491 record at length, highlighting the sylph's remark that God 'created [the universe] from moment to moment' as antedating quantum theory by four centuries.

## Contexts Within the Source

Offered as a Renaissance instance of the recurring 'aerial people' contact, linked to Paracelsus's theories of the Elementals.
