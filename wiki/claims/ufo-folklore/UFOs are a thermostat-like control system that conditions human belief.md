## Claim Statement

Vallée's central hypothesis: UFOs are "not necessarily caused by extraterrestrial visitors, nor the result of misidentifications and hoaxes," but constitute a **control system** for human consciousness — functioning "not unlike that of a thermostat in a house." A thermostat needs two opposite principles (heating and cooling) to regulate; likewise the phenomenon oscillates between rational/absurd and friendly/hostile elements, and "the variable being controlled" is **human belief** and "the relationship between our consciousness and physical reality."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "I propose to examine the hypothesis that UFOs may constitute a control system… UFOs may serve to stabilize the relationship between man's consciousness needs and the evolving complexities of the world." (Introduction)

> "I propose the hypothesis that there is a control system for human consciousness." (Ch. 9)

Vallée says that in every case he studied in depth he found "as many rational elements as… absurd ones, and many… friendly and many that seemed hostile" — proof, he argues, that "I am working on the wrong level," the level of the thermostat's reference setting. He insists this does not mean a literal jailer or a redefinition of God: "mythology rules at a level of our social reality over which normal political and intellectual action has no real power," operating on century-long timescales and through symbols (meta-logical, "but not metaphysical").
