## Claim Statement

Vallée documents that witnesses near landed craft are frequently 'paralyzed' — immobilized while remaining conscious — as with Maurice Masse at Valensole (1965) and Georges Gatay's eight-man crew at Nouâtre (1954); he links this to the fairy-faith motif of being struck immobile and to the relativity of time 'in Magonia.'

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He clarifies the word 'paralysis' is loose — Masse stayed conscious with normal heartbeat but could not move, then suffered weeks of compulsive drowsiness — and parallels the Hill abduction's account.

## Contexts Within the Source

A 'little-known characteristic of close-proximity cases' he uses to tie modern reports to abduction folklore.
