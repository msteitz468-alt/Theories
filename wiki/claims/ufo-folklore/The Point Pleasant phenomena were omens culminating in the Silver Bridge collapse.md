## Claim Statement

Keel frames the year of Mothman sightings, UFO lights, Men in Black, and contactee messages as a build-up of omens and premonitions that climaxed in the collapse of the **Silver Bridge** across the Ohio River on December 15, 1967, which killed 46 people — the "prophecies" of the title.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Keel reports receiving (and tracking) prophetic warnings of an impending catastrophe — vague predictions of disaster, power failures, and "something awful" around the Christmas season — through contactees and anomalous phone communications, which in hindsight seemed to point at the bridge. His own framing anecdote ("Beelzebub Visits West Virginia") shows how locals retrospectively read a mundane event (Keel, lost in the rain) as a demonic omen because two people he had wakened died in the collapse three weeks later. Keel is careful to note prosaic facts too — e.g., a reported "flash of light" before the bridge fell was later explained as snapping power cables — even as he argues the wider pattern resists mundane explanation.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Keel repeatedly cautions that retrospective omen-reading creates folklore (his own "legend" being the prime example), and supplies mundane explanations for individual details; he presents the prophetic pattern as suggestive rather than proven.
