## Claim Statement

Vallée presents the Spanish **UMMO** affair — a long-running campaign of letters, documents, and a photographed disk purportedly from beings of the planet "UMMO" — as an example of a "sophisticated group… already exploiting" the contactee belief patterns: a deliberate, intelligently constructed **disinformation** operation. He cites it as evidence that "direct genuine contact would ruin the experiment," whereas "deliberately misleading contact, as in UMMO," fits the control-system model.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Vallée lists UMMO among the four elements forcing a new framework — "the sophisticated groups which are already exploiting these patterns, such as the UMMO organization in Spain." In the control-system chapter he uses UMMO as the type-case of contact that is intentionally false, designed to "project an image just beyond the belief structure of the target society" while remaining undetectable to science, church, and state.

## Contexts Within the Source

UMMO illustrates the meta-logical mechanism of [[UFOs are a thermostat-like control system that conditions human belief]] and foreshadows Vallée's later work on UFO deception. He warns "the stage is set for another UMMO," with rumors of imminent government disclosure of extraterrestrial contact.
