## Claim Statement

Vallée argues that the sheer implied frequency of UFO landings is too large to be explained
by random extraterrestrial survey visits — so the appearances must be **staged or
selected**, not chance encounters. Starting from ~2,000 reported close encounters, he scales
up for unreported hours, the one-in-ten reporting rate, and uneven population distribution to
reach an estimate that, if UFOs were spacecraft randomly surveying Earth, they "must have
landed here no fewer than three million times in two decades."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

> "Either the UFOs select their witnesses, or they are something entirely different from
> space vehicles. In either case, their appearances are staged!" (Ch. 1)

His chain of multipliers: landings peak ~10:30 p.m. and fall off as potential witnesses go to
bed (×15 if people stayed out); only ~1 case in 10 is reported (×10 → ~300,000); most landings
occur in unpopulated areas (a further ×10) → on the order of three million events. He concludes
"the theory of random visitation does not explain it."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Vallée presents the multipliers as deliberately "conservative" estimates rather than exact
measurements, and frames the conclusion as a problem "any theory has yet to explain" rather
than a disproof of physical reality.
