## Claim Statement

Vallée argues that UFO research organizations and occult/contactee sects are routinely
**infiltrated, watched, and exploited by intelligence agencies** — and possibly by a private
group of "Manipulators" — who use them as covers for their own designs and as sources of
low-cost field information. The contactees are, in intelligence parlance, "useful idiots" and
"puppets."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Through "[[Major Murphy (Vallée's intelligence source)]]," Vallée presents UFOs as a
counterespionage problem and lists tradecraft he says operates inside UFO groups: the "vacuum
cleaner" (an agent who painlessly extracts everything you know), the "little aunt" (a long-term
casual informant), and "cut-outs." He notes NICAP's board included a former CIA director
(Roscoe Hillenkoetter — see [[Roscoe Hillenkoetter (CIA/NICAP)]]) and "at least three well-known
Intelligence operatives," and quotes the declassified 1953 **CIA/USAF (Robertson) Panel**
recommending that civilian saucer groups "should be watched because of their great influence on
mass thinking" and their "possible use… for subversive purposes."

Murphy's counsel: "work entirely outside of the organized UFO groups; they are infiltrated by
the same official agencies they are trying to influence." On occult bodies: "I never said they
were wrong. I said they were infiltrated." Vallée notes occultists "from John Dee and Casanova
to Aleister Crowley… have often doubled as spies."

## Contexts Within the Source

This underpins both stratagem hypotheses —
[[The Martian Conspiracy (Vallée's first stratagem hypothesis)]] (deception apparatus) and
[[The Esoteric Intervention (Vallée's second stratagem hypothesis)]] (occult cover) — and the
profile of the [[Order of Melchizedek (contactee-occult network)]] and AMORC's
[[Raymond Bernard]]. Vallée says he "resigned from the supposedly 'scientific' UFO
organizations" as a result.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Vallée distinguishes infiltration/exploitation from a single orchestrated conspiracy: "I am not
suggesting that secret agents are going around the world giving these thousands of people
individual suggestions to see UFOs." He concedes most UFO-group leaders are "independent and
sincere," and that "manipulation" may be mere "exploitation."
