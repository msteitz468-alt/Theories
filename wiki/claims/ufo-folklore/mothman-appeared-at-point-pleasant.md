## Claim Statement

Keel documents a wave of sightings, beginning November 1966, of a large winged humanoid with glowing red eyes around Point Pleasant — the creature the press dubbed "Mothman." Witnesses reported a ~7-foot gray man-shaped being with a ~10-foot wingspan that rose vertically and silently and whose red eyes caused eye-burn.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The first prominent sighting was November 15, 1966, by Roger and Linda Scarberry and Steve and Mary Mallette, driving through the **TNT area** (an abandoned WWII explosives dump of concrete "igloos") in a 1957 Chevrolet. Keel also recounts Connie Carpenter's daylight encounter near the Mason County Golf Course (Nov. 27, 1966): a >7-foot figure whose wings "unfolded from its back," rising "straight up like a helicopter, silently," its red eyes leaving her with conjunctivitis lasting over two weeks. Keel files Mothman alongside a global catalogue of winged men, impossibly large birds, and red-eyed "demons" (the Thunderbird/Piasa, the Garuda, a 1905 California "airship-bird"), arguing all are interrelated manifestations.
