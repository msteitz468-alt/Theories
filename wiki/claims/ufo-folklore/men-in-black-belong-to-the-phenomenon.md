## Claim Statement

Keel reports that UFO witnesses were repeatedly visited by strange "Men in Black" — dark-complexioned, vaguely "Oriental," long-fingered men in ill-fitting black suits who interrogated them and pressured them to stop discussing sightings. He argues these figures are not (merely) government agents but are themselves part of the UFO phenomenon: "residents of this planet associated with the UFOs."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Examples: two near-identical short, dark men who appeared in Mary Hyre's office asking about flying-saucer activity and what she'd do "if someone ordered you to stop"; the bizarre "Jack Brown," who couldn't operate his own tape recorder and spoke only when looked at directly; and "Major Richard French," whose suit and shoe-soles looked brand-new and unwalked-upon. Keel notes the descriptions matched those contactees gave of UFO "pilots" — pointed or Oriental features, dusky (non-Negroid) skin, unusually long fingers. He reproduces a genuine 1967 USAF memo warning that impostors were impersonating officers to suppress UFO reports — but treats the official explanation as insufficient to the pattern's strangeness.
