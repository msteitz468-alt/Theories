## Claim Statement

Coleman asserts that the Committee of 300 — "the Olympians" — believes itself charged "by divine right" to implement a concrete, itemized program he sets out as **"21 goals,"** which together amount to a deliberate blueprint for a One World Government / New World Order achieved by destroying nations, religion, industry, and population.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Coleman lists the goals, which he says he "first enumerated in 1969" and which are "well on their way to being achieved." In paraphrase, the program includes: (1) a One World Government with a unified church and monetary system; (2) destruction of national identity and pride; (3) destruction of religion, "more especially the Christian religion"; (4) total mind control via Brzezinski's "technotronics"; (5) ending industrialization and nuclear power in a "post-industrial zero-growth society"; (6) legalization of drugs and pornography; (7) depopulation of large cities "according to the trial run carried out by the Pol Pot regime"; (8) suppression of scientific development (especially fusion); (9) causing the death of "3 billion people by the year 2000" — the **Global 2000** plan; (10) demoralizing workers and the young via mass unemployment, rock music and drugs (the Tavistock-commissioned "Aquarian Conspiracy"); (11) crisis management via **FEMA**; (12) promoting cults and Tavistock-created "rock" groups; (16) collapse of the world's economies and "total political chaos"; (17) control of all U.S. foreign and domestic policy; (18) support for supranational bodies (UN, IMF, BIS, World Court); (19) subverting all governments from within; (20) running a worldwide terrorist apparatus; and (21) taking control of, and "utterly... destroying," American education.

## Contexts Within the Source

Coleman frames the list as the operative agenda flowing from his thesis that the Committee is the supreme hidden world government, and ties its economic core to Malthus and von Hayek (the latter, he says, "long... under the control of David Rockefeller"). Several goals cross-reference the book's other claims — Tavistock psychological warfare, the zero-growth Club of Rome doctrine, and the Global 2000 depopulation plan.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Coleman presents the goals as assertion grounded in his intelligence career rather than documented Committee minutes; he supplies no sourced charter and concedes elsewhere that bodies he approached "never heard of" his sources.
