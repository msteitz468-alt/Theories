## Claim Statement

Coleman claims the Committee of 300 enforces its agenda by threatening, removing, or assassinating national leaders who pursue industrial development against its "zero-growth" decree — presenting several political killings as Committee punishments rather than the work of their ostensible perpetrators.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His flagship case is Italian Prime Minister **Aldo Moro**, kidnapped and murdered by the Red Brigades in 1978; Coleman asserts (citing testimony by Moro aide Guerzoni) that Henry **Kissinger** had earlier threatened Moro in a U.S. hotel room for his "nation-building" policies, and that the killing was Committee enforcement. He makes parallel claims about Pakistan's **Ali Bhutto** and General **Zia ul Haq** — punished, he says, for a nuclear-energy/industrialization program "in contravention of [the Committee's] orders delivered by Kissinger" — and about the kidnapping of General Dozier, alleging Kissinger acted not as U.S. policy but as "the modern-day Illuminati."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

These are Coleman's interpretations layered onto real events (the Red Brigades' Moro kidnapping; Bhutto's execution; Zia's 1988 plane crash); he provides assertion and second-hand testimony rather than documentary proof of Committee direction.
