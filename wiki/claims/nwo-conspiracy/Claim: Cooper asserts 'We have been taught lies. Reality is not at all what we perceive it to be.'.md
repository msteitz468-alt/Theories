**Claim Statement**

Cooper asserts as a foundational premise that the American public (and by extension humanity) has been systematically deceived to the point that their perception of reality is entirely false. "We have been taught lies. Reality is not at all what we perceive it to be."

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**

From the Preface, stated twice for emphasis:

> "We have been taught lies. Reality is not at all what we perceive it to be."

> "We cannot survive any longer by hanging on to the falsehoods of the past. Reality must be discerned at all costs if we are to be a part of the future. Truth must prevail in all instances, no matter who it hurts or helps, if we are to continue to live upon this earth."

The book's epigraph, quoted from a writer named "Delamer Duverus," extends the metaphor:

> "One basic truth can be used as a foundation for a mountain of lies, and if we dig down deep enough in the mountain of lies, and bring out that truth, to set it on top of the mountain of lies; the entire mountain of lies will crumble under the weight of that one truth."

**Contexts Within the Source**

Cooper presents this claim as the motivation for the entire book — not an opinion but a conviction established through his military service and personal investigation. The claim functions as an epistemological reset instruction to the reader: whatever you believed before reading this book, treat it as suspect.

The Foreword, written by an unnamed associate, characterizes Cooper as someone who addresses the gap between "fiction and reality" in a society that "spoon-feeds you deception."
