## Claim Statement

David Icke asserts that political correctness was deliberately engineered by the "Rothschild-connected Frankfurt School" in the 1920s as a social control mechanism. Its purpose is not to protect minorities or reduce discrimination but to get the target population to silence and censor itself — so that the elite network does not have to do so openly. The PC movement embodies the current phase of what Icke calls the "Postage Stamp Consensus" — the elite-managed range of acceptable opinion — and functions as "psychological warfare" by using fear and social intimidation to silence dissent. Those who call themselves "progressive" are described as the primary enforcers of this conformity programme, despite presenting themselves as anti-Establishment. George Soros is named as a primary funder of these progressive organisations.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 8** introduces the Frankfurt School context: "The Rothschild-connected Frankfurt School, established by fellow Zionists in the 1920s, is among the most infamous of social engineering operations. It was first based ... in the Rothschild home city of Frankfurt, Germany, and then also located in Switzerland and the United States." Its listed goals: "Creation of racism offences; continual change to create confusion; teaching of sex to young children (it now happens as young as four); huge immigration to destroy national identity; promotion of excessive drinking; emptying of churches; a legal system with bias against victims of crime; dependency on the state or state benefits; control and dumbing down of the media; encouraging the breakdown of the family."

**Chapter 10** defines the mechanism: "Political correctness is an El-lite tool for getting the target population to silence and censor itself so they don't have to." The definition of "psychological warfare" is cited: "The use of activities that cause fear and anxiety in the people you want to influence without hurting them physically."

The distinction between "progressive" and "liberal" is made central: the dictionary definition of liberal ("favouring maximum individual freedom") is contrasted with how self-described progressives actually behave ("seeking to destroy" freedom of action and personal belief). "Progressives are the new Establishment and crucial to the social engineering networks which instigated political correctness."

George Orwell's "Newspeak" from *Nineteen Eighty-Four* is quoted as a description of political correctness: "a controlled language [used] as a tool to limit freedom of thought and concepts that pose a threat to the regime."

Canadian government's "Carrot Rewards" app (awarding discount points for "good behaviour") and China's social credit system are cited as early implementations of PC-style behaviour engineering through reward rather than punishment.

**Chapter 11**: Icke's personal experience with German venue cancellations (including the Maritim Hotel, Berlin, which cancelled after signing a contract for October 2017) is documented as a case study in Zionist-pressure censorship operating within the PC framework. Jan Rathje of the Amadeu Antonio Stiftung and journalist Ben Knight are named and criticised for accusing Icke of anti-Semitism without evidence. Germany's "Volksverhetzung" (incitement) law is described as deliberately vague to enable broad censorship.

## Contexts Within the Source

Icke distinguishes his critique of "progressives" from the genuine political left: "My use of the term 'progressive' from hereon does not refer to those of the Left and Centre who are genuinely liberal ... but to those who call themselves progressive while being the very antipathy of liberal." He frames the entire PC movement as one phase in a rotating system of control — the Postage Stamp Consensus — which adjusts to whatever ideological form is most effective at each historical moment (fascism, communism, now "progressivism").
