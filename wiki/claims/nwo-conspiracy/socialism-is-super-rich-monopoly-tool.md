**Claim Statement**  
State socialism is not a genuine grassroots movement of the working class rising up against exploiting capitalists, but a top-down control mechanism promoted and financed by the super-rich (the Insiders) to centralize all economic and political power in government, thereby creating a captive market and eliminating all free-enterprise competition.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *None Dare Call It Conspiracy* (Chapter Two):
* **Billionaires Promoting Socialism:** Gary Allen argues that while it is widely believed that billionaires would oppose socialism because it threatens their wealth, they actually promote it to secure total monopolies. He explains: "Socialism is a philosophy which conspirators exploit, but in which only the naive believe... If one wants power, real power, the lessons of history say, 'Get into the government business.' Become a politician and work for political power or, better yet, get some politicians to front for you."
* **Marx's Codification of Illuminist Aims:** Allen asserts that Karl Marx did not write the *Communist Manifesto* in a vacuum; he was hired by the "League of Just Men" (an extension of the Bavarian Illuminati) to update and codify their revolutionary plans. The manifesto's call for state control of property, family, and religion was designed to build an all-powerful dictatorship.
* **Harvard Professors Promoting the Trend:** Allen quotes Harvard economist John Kenneth Galbraith's article "Richard Nixon and the Great Socialist Revival," in which Galbraith observes that Nixon's economic policies were pushing the country into state socialism. He also quotes Arthur Schlesinger's statement that "liberalism grows constantly more liberal, and by the same token, conservatism grows constantly less conservative."

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Allen presents this claim to explain why international financiers consistently fund socialist and collectivist schemes. He argues that under a free-market system, the rich must compete and are subject to market forces, whereas under a socialist state, they can use the coercive power of the government to protect their fortunes and completely dominate the population.
