## Claim Statement

Webster argues the Knights Templar absorbed Cabalistic and Eastern occult doctrine and, after their suppression, became a transmission channel passing this subversive current into the higher degrees of Freemasonry.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

She traces a "Templar succession" surviving into 18th-century "German Templarism," arguing that the Rose-Croix and "Scots Masonry" degrees became "simply a veil for Templarism." She speculates that the documents underpinning Baron von Hundt's Templar revival "may have been… the work of Voltaire, aided probably by a Jew capable of forging Syriac manuscripts," and assigns Frederick the Great a "preponderating role" in the Templar revival. The Templar line, on her account, carries the occult/Cabalistic doctrine of the preceding chapters into modern Masonry.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Webster offers competing "theories" of the Templar succession and explicitly declines to "decide which… is correct," presenting parts of the chain as inference.
