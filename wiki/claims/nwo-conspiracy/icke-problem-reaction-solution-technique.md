## Claim Statement

David Icke asserts that the elite's primary technique for manipulating the population into accepting expansions of control and deletions of freedom is a three-step process he calls "Problem-Reaction-Solution" (also described as the Hegelian Dialectic in prior Icke works, though not named as such here). The elite covertly creates or enables a problem (terrorism, financial crisis, disease, war); ensures the public reacts with fear and demands something be done; then presents the pre-planned "solution" — which invariably consists of exactly the power transfers and freedom deletions the elite intended to achieve all along. This technique is paired with the "Totalitarian Tiptoe" — the graduated, incremental implementation of the agenda in steps too small to trigger mass resistance.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

**Chapter 8** describes the technique as a "perpetual motion programming system" of perception: "Archontic Reptilians and their hybrid El-ite have created a fantastic global structure of mind control and perception manipulation that operates from the shadows." 9/11 is cited as a paradigm case: "after the attacks of 9/11 and the fake official explanation it was easy to win support for the invasion of Afghanistan and the 'war on [of] terror'."

**Chapter 12** names the technique explicitly: "Problem-Reaction-Solution and the Totalitarian Tiptoe. There are other mind games to confuse and manipulate the collective perception of events, but these are the twin pillars of what is a global psychiatric program targeting the human psyche." The section is titled "Before Your Very Eyes" and Icke argues that once you know the technique, all world events become transparent.

War as Problem-Reaction-Solution: "The same terrorism orchestrated by the same networks that then say our freedoms have to be deleted to save us from terrorists they control, arm and fund."

Climate as Problem-Reaction-Solution: "A climate change progressive said on CNN that to save the world 'we must change everything'" — framed as the population campaigning for its own enslavement.

**Chapter 14** (terrorism): "We are going to protect your freedoms by taking them away – Problem-Reaction-Solution." Post-attack responses enumerated: Germany debates deploying troops on streets; France forms National Guard; Britain's police press for all officers to be armed; Theresa May calls for internet regulation.

**Chapter 15** (climate): The manufactured climate problem generates the reaction of public fear; the pre-planned solution is Agenda 21/2030 centralisation.

Edward Bernays' *Propaganda* (1928) is quoted as an early explicit statement of engineered consent: "We are governed, our minds are molded, our tastes formed, our ideas suggested, largely by men we have never heard of."

## Contexts Within the Source

Icke presents Problem-Reaction-Solution as a universal key that, once understood, decodes virtually every major political event of the modern era. He argues that people cannot see it because they are too close to the events ("seeing only twigs, not the forest"). The Totalitarian Tiptoe paired with it means each individual step is too small to resist, but the cumulative direction is unmistakable when viewed across decades.
