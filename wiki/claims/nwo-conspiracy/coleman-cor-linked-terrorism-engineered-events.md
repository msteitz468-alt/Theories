## Claim Statement

Coleman asserts that the Club of Rome maintains connections to **terrorist organizations** and **engineers political events** — that the upheavals of the 20th century "did not just happen by themselves" but "were planned according to a well laid out blueprint."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Chapter 1 opens with the thesis that "the many tragic and explosive events of the 20th century ... were planned according to a well laid out blueprint" by secret societies "controlled by the [[Committee of 300 (Coleman)]]." In Chapter 4 Coleman claims "COR is believed to have maintained some connections with terrorist organizations like the Red Brigades," implemented "through arch-Socialist Bettino Craxi, a former leading player in the COR" with alleged "contacts in the Bader-Meinhoff gang." Craxi, he says, "repeatedly tried to break the resolve of the Italian government not to negotiate with the Red Brigades for the release of kidnapped U.S. General Dozier."

Coleman further claims the COR predicted/engineered the political returns of Craxi and François Mitterrand ("returned to power in France — courtesy of the Club of Rome"), citing his own newsletter *World in Review*, and that COR predictions "later proved to be 100 percent accurate."

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim grounds Coleman's "nothing happens by accident" worldview, casting terrorism and elections alike as COR/Committee-of-300 instruments — and foreshadowing the warning that "Jacobin-style mobs will be employed" in America.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The links are hedged ("is believed to have maintained some connections") and rest on Coleman's own reporting; no corroborating evidence ties the Club of Rome to the Red Brigades.
