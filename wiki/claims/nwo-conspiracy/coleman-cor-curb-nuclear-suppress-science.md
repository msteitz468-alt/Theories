## Claim Statement

Coleman asserts that the Club of Rome deliberately **suppresses scientific and technological development — especially cheap nuclear energy** — in order to keep the population in artificial scarcity and dependence, as a tool of deindustrialization and depopulation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"The Club of Rome ... is strongly anti-national and favors the suppression of scientific development in the United States, Britain and Europe, and latterly, Japan" (Ch. 4). Chapter 14 ("Curbing Nuclear Energy") frames opposition to nuclear power as a COR strategy: abundant cheap energy would raise living standards and population, contrary to the [[Post-Industrial Zero-Growth]] goal, so it must be blocked. The *Global 2000* premise he cites — "cutting off technology and keeping food in short supply" — is the same logic applied to energy and science generally.

Coleman treats the environmental movement as the public cover for this scarcity program (the deceptive "German Marshall Fund" funding, the anti-industry rhetoric).

## Contexts Within the Source

This claim supplies the mechanism: by capping energy and science, the COR allegedly manufactures the shortages that justify and enforce population reduction.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Coleman presents mainstream environmental and anti-nuclear positions as a deliberate scarcity conspiracy; he offers interpretation rather than documentary proof of intent.
