## Claim Statement

Coleman's central thesis: the world is secretly governed by an apex body of roughly 300 powerful individuals — which he says calls itself "**the Olympians**" — that operates "in open view" through an interlocking web of organizations and is driving an "open conspiracy against God and man" to impose a "One World Government–New World Order."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Coleman traces the Committee's roots to the British East India Company and casts its members as inheritors of "Illuminism… the Cult of Dionysius, the Cult of Isis, Catharism, Bogomilism," believing themselves "equal in power and stature to the legendary gods of Olympus." He claims its agents are hidden in plain sight (naming, e.g., Queen Elizabeth II as its head and the U.S. President as a member) and lists "21 goals," including a One World Government, the end of nation-states and industrialization (the "post-industrial zero-growth society"), population reduction (tied to "Global 2000"), the spread of drugs and counterculture, and a worldwide terrorist apparatus. Its instruments include the Round Table, the RIIA, NATO, the Club of Rome, the Tavistock Institute, the German Marshall Fund, and the Venetian Black Nobility.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Coleman concedes he can produce no verifiable membership list and that bodies he approached "had never heard of" his sources; he frames the case as intelligence-derived exposé.
