**Claim Statement**  
The Insiders utilize a dual-force pincer strategy of "pressure from above" (coercive government regulations and executive decrees passed by Establishment conspirators) and "pressure from below" (radical street violence and activist groups demanding socialist reforms) to crush the middle class and compel the expansion of state power.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *None Dare Call It Conspiracy* (Chapter Seven):
* **Jan Kozak's Czecho-Slovakia Tactic:** Allen cites the Communist historian Jan Kozak, who documented how the Reds captured Czechoslovakia by utilizing a pincer strategy of "pressure from above" and "pressure from below."
* **Establishment-Mob Cooperation:** Allen asserts that the street rioters and radical groups are not fighting the Establishment, but are actively funded and directed by it. He writes: "The pressure from above comes from secret, ostensibly respectable Comrades in the government and Establishment, forming, with the radicalized mobs in the streets below, a giant pincer around middle-class society."
* **Middle Class Squeezed:** The street mobs create civil disruption and demand solutions. In response, the Establishment figures in government enact new regulations and taxes that centralize power. The middle class, which pays the taxes and obeys the law, is squeezed and gradually socialized.

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Allen presents this claim to warn readers against falling for radical street movements. He argues that by recognizing that both the street agitators and the government regulators are playing for the same team (the Insiders), citizens can resist the false choices offered by political actors and prevent the planned socialization of the United States.
