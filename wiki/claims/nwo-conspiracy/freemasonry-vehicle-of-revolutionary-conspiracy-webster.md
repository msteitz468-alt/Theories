## Claim Statement

Webster argues that Freemasonry — particularly its Continental, higher-degree, and later **Grand Orient** forms — was infiltrated and used as an organizing vehicle for the subversive tradition, carrying occult/Cabalistic doctrine and serving revolutionary aims.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In the "Grand Lodge Era" she suggests early Jewish influence on English Masonry via "the Jews of Amsterdam," the Rabbi Templo who "designed the coat-of-arms later adopted by Grand Lodge," and Cromwell as a possible "channel." She reads the Rose-Croix/"Scots Masonry" degrees as veils for Templarism, and elsewhere notes "the affinity between the Druses and Freemasons of the Grand Orient." Throughout she relies on what she presents as Masons' own admissions, arguing the Craft was "utilized for a subversive purpose."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Webster states at the outset that she does not "enquire into the secrets of Freemasonry" as such, only "the manner in which these systems have been utilized for a subversive purpose" — distinguishing (in principle) ordinary Masonry from the subversive use she alleges.
