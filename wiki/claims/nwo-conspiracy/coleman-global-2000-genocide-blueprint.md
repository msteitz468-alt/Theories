## Claim Statement

Coleman asserts that the *Global 2000 Report* is **"a blueprint for global genocide"** — a plan endorsed by the Club of Rome calling for "the death of 2 billion people by the year 2010" (the source of the report's title, in his reading), to be achieved by withholding technology, food, and development from the Third World.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Coleman: "The December 5, 1980 follow-up to the original COR meeting in Washington D.C., endorsed and accepted the COR's Global 2000 Report — a blueprint for global genocide. The report called for the death of 2 billion people by the year 2010 (hence the title)." He claims the second conference "also adopted the policy of euthanasia" for the elderly.

In Chapter 15 he states the report's "underlying premise": "we must act now to keep consumption as low as possible by cutting off technology and keeping food in short supply. We must be prepared to let Third World populations starve, so that their country's raw materials ... are available for the rulers of the world." He links it to Gen. Maxwell Taylor and Robert McNamara's "Draper Fund Population Crisis Committee," and claims "the role played by the Club of Rome in formulating a policy of genocide ... was carried out by the Pol Pot regime in Cambodia" as "an experiment," enforceable elsewhere via NATO troops and IMF debt.

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the apex of Coleman's thesis — depopulation as the ultimate goal that deindustrialization, scarcity, and environmentalism all serve.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Coleman presents the real *Global 2000 Report to the President* (a 1980 US environmental study) as a confessed extermination plan; the document itself contains no such call. His attribution of the Cambodian genocide to the Club of Rome is offered as assertion, without documentary support.
