## Claim Statement

Coleman asserts that the **Pol Pot regime's genocide in Cambodia** was a deliberate Club of Rome / Socialist International **experiment** in depopulation and de-urbanization — a trial run for what is planned for America.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

At the close of Chapter 3, Coleman writes that the Socialist International "has long championed the idea of breaking up large cities and moving the population into smaller, more manageable ... towns and the countryside," and that "The first experiment in this regard was carried out by the Pol Pot regime in Cambodia, with the knowledge of Thomas Enders, a high-ranking U.S. State Department official."

In Chapter 9 he restates it as Club of Rome policy: "we know perhaps less well, the role-played by the Club of Rome in formulating a policy of genocide, which was carried out by the Pol Pot regime in Cambodia. That plot was hatched and set in motion in Cambodia as an experiment. And don't think that the same thing couldn't happen in America; it can and it will."

## Contexts Within the Source

The Cambodia example anchors Coleman's depopulation thesis — that the COR "treats overpopulation as the enemy" and aims to cull billions — by presenting an actual completed atrocity as a controlled test of the doctrine, with the explicit warning that the United States is the next target.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The causal attribution rests on Coleman's assertion alone; he cites only "the knowledge of Thomas Enders" and offers no documentary link tying the Khmer Rouge to the Club of Rome.
