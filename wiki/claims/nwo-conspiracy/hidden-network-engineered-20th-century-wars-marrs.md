## Claim Statement

Marrs argues the elite network used its control of money and policy to engineer or exploit the 20th century's great wars and economic upheavals for profit and consolidation of power — the "fingerprints" by which the conspiracy can be detected.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Part II runs through: the founding of the **Federal Reserve** (1913) as the financiers' instrument; "**Trading with the Enemy**" (financing both sides); the bankrolling of the **Rise of the Nazi Cult** and the **Russian Revolution / Rise of Communism**; **World War I and II** as engineered or exploited conflicts; **Korea, Vietnam**, and the **Persian Gulf**; the contention that **JFK opposed the globalists**; and the *Report from Iron Mountain* as evidence of war treated as a tool of social control. The recurring claim is that the same names finance and profit from opposing sides.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Marrs presents this as a pattern inferred from financial and documentary records; he relays contested items (e.g. the *Report from Iron Mountain*, whose authenticity is disputed) within his broader "connect the documented dots" framing.
