## Claim Statement

In her chapter "The Jewish Cabalists," Webster asserts that Jewish influence in modern revolutions has been "twofold — financial and occult": that Jews, while not necessarily *creating* upheavals, have backed and exploited them as financiers and as carriers of the Cabalistic occult doctrine running through the secret societies. This is the antisemitic core of the book, recorded here strictly as Webster frames it — attributed and flagged, not endorsed, amplified, or rebutted.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Webster writes that "in modern revolutions the part played by the Jews cannot be ignored, and the influence they have exercised will be seen… to have been twofold — financial and occult," casting them historically as "sorcerers and usurers" and later as "magicians and loan-mongers… behind the scenes of revolution." She illustrates with the Cromwell-era Marranos/Crypto-Jews (e.g. Carvajal as a "political intelligencer"), claiming a hidden Jewish "secret service."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Webster wraps the thesis in claims of impartiality: she cites the Jewish writer Bernard Lazare to argue the point is not mere "anti-Semitism," states she will indicate the Jewish role "only where it is to be clearly detected," relies (she says) "principally on the writings of the Jews themselves," and explicitly concedes that "the revolutionary spirit has always existed independently of the Jews" and that it would be "absurd" to ascribe earlier revolts (Catiline, the Gracchi, Wat Tyler, the jacqueries) to them. These self-exonerating caveats are her own framing and do not negate the chapter's antisemitic thrust.
