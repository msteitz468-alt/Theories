## Claim Statement

Webster concludes that the same secret-society network that drove the French Revolution continued unbroken through the 19th century into the revolutions of 1848, the Paris Commune, the First International, and ultimately Bolshevism — making modern communism the latest expression of the ancient subversive tradition.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

She marshals admissions she attributes to the revolutionaries themselves: that "Bakunin was a disciple of Weishaupt" (Malon) and that Bakunin's secret society had "a direct affiliation" with "the secret societies of 1795" (Kropotkin); that "Communism was handed down in the dark through the secret societies" of the 19th century (Malon); and that Freemasons (via Crémieux, Lamartine) "boasted of their share" in 1848. She argues "the organization of this later outbreak by the secret societies is not a matter of surmise, but a fact admitted by all well-informed historians and by the members of the secret societies themselves," extending the same role to the Commune and the First International.
