## Claim Statement

Marrs argues the secret tradition was carried through the medieval era by a connected set of "elder secret societies": the **Knights Templar** (and the Assassins they encountered), who became Europe's first international **bankers and builders**, were guardians of a hidden bloodline secret tied to the **Priory of Sion** and the **Merovingian** dynasty — the Holy-Blood-Holy-Grail synthesis.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Part IV moves through the Templars, the Assassins, "Templar Bankers and Builders," the **Cathars** and the Albigensian Crusade, "The Templars' Demise," the **Priory of Sion**, and the **Merovingians**, concluding with "A Far-Reaching Web." Marrs treats the Templars' suppression as an attempt to destroy custodians of a dangerous secret that nonetheless survived through allied societies.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Marrs relays this chain largely from *The Holy Blood and the Holy Grail* and related works; the Priory/Merovingian links are among the book's most contested, sourced rather than independently proven.
