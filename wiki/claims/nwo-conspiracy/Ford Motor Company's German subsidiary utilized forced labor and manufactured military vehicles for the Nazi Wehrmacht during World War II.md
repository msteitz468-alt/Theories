**Claim Statement**  
Ford Motor Company's German subsidiary (Ford Motor A.G.) actively supported the Nazi war effort by manufacturing military transport vehicles for the Wehrmacht and utilizing forced labor (including prisoners of war and concentration camp inmates) in its Cologne plant during World War II.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *Wall Street and the Rise of Hitler* (Chapter Six):
* **Ford's Cartel Merger with I.G. Farben:** In 1928, Henry Ford merged his German assets with those of the I.G. Farben chemical cartel. Carl Bosch of I.G. Farben became head of Ford Motor A.G. in Germany, and Edsel Ford joined the board of American I.G. Farben in the United States.
* **Henry Ford's Nazi Decoration:** Henry Ford was decorated by the Nazi regime in 1938, receiving the Grand Cross of the German Eagle on his 75th birthday, in recognition of his early financial and ideological support of the National Socialist movement.
* **Wartime Vehicle Manufacture:** Ford's German subsidiary manufactured over 100,000 military trucks and tracked vehicles for the Wehrmacht during the war.
* **Utilization of Forced Labor:** The Cologne Ford plant utilized forced labor from 1941 to 1945 to maintain high production levels under the Nazi war economy.
* **Post-War Tax Write-offs:** Sutton notes that after the war, Ford Motor Company filed claims with the U.S. government for war damages to its German facilities, effectively receiving tax write-offs for plants that had manufactured weapons for the enemy.

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Sutton presents this claim to illustrate the collaboration between prominent American industrialists (members of the "power elite") and totalitarian regimes. He highlights that while Henry Ford publicly claimed to oppose war and international finance, his family's corporate operations directly bankrolled and supplied the Nazi military machine.
