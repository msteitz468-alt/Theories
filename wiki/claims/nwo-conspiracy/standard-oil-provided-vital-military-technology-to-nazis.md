**Claim Statement**  
Standard Oil of New Jersey (controlled by the Rockefeller interests) transferred vital synthetic gasoline (coal hydrogenation) and synthetic rubber (Buna) patents and manufacturing data to the German chemical cartel I.G. Farben. This technical assistance enabled the Nazi Wehrmacht to achieve self-sufficiency in fuels and rubber, which made the motorized invasions of Europe possible.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *Wall Street and the Rise of Hitler* (Chapter Four):
* **Technology Transfer for Synthetic Fuel:** Sutton shows that before WWII, Germany depended almost entirely on imported petroleum. Through Standard Oil's transfer of hydrogenation patents, Germany was able to convert soft coal into high-grade synthetic aviation gasoline. By 1944, synthetic fuel accounted for 85 percent (5.5 million tons) of Germany's total oil production.
* **Buna Synthetic Rubber:** Standard Oil entered a cartel agreement with I.G. Farben in 1929. Standard Oil gave Farben the patents for Buna rubber while withholding them from the U.S. government and military, leaving the U.S. without synthetic rubber capacity when WWII broke out.
* **Tetraethyl Lead for the Luftwaffe:** In 1938, Standard Oil's joint venture (Ethyl Gasoline Corporation) loaned the German air force 500 tons of tetraethyl lead (an essential fuel additive) and transferred the technical blueprints to manufacture it domestically, enabling the Luftwaffe to fly combat missions.
* **DAPAG Subsidiaries:** Standard Oil's German subsidiary, Deutsche-Amerikanische Petroleum A.G. (DAPAG), reinvested its profits directly in Germany and placed Karl Lindemann, its director, on Himmler's S.S. Circle of Friends.

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Sutton presents Standard Oil's cooperation as a clear example of corporate interests putting profit and global cartel agreements above national loyalty. He highlights that Standard Oil executives, like Walter Teagle (who served on the board of American I.G.), actively assisted Germany's rearmament while knowing that the Wehrmacht was preparing for war.
