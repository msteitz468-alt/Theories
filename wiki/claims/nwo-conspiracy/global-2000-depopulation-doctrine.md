## Claim Statement

Coleman asserts that the Committee of 300 has decreed a deliberate program of mass depopulation — operationalized as the U.S. government's **"Global 2000 Report"** — to cull billions of people it calls "useless eaters" and reduce world population to a "manageable level," in service of its "zero-growth" anti-industrial agenda.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Coleman writes that "the Committee of 300 long ago decreed that there shall be a smaller — much smaller — and better world," and that "the myriads of useless eaters consuming scarce natural resources were to be culled." As the ninth of his "21 goals," he says the Committee aims to cause "the death of 3 billion people by the year 2000... by means of limited wars in the advanced countries, and by means of starvation and diseases in Third World countries." He claims the Committee "commissioned Cyrus Vance to write a paper on... how best to bring about such genocide," produced as the **"Global 2000 Report,"** which he says was "accepted and approved for action by President Carter" and Secretary of State Edwin Muskie, mandating that "the population of the United States is to be reduced by 100 million by the year 2050." Elsewhere he restates the goal as eliminating "at least 4 billion 'useless eaters'... by the year 2050 by means of limited wars, organized epidemics of fatal rapid-acting diseases and starvation," with food, water and energy kept "at subsistence levels for the non-elite," until world population "reaches a manageable level of 1 billion." He grounds the doctrine in Malthus and points to the Pol Pot regime — whose plans he says "were drawn up here in the United States by one of the Club of Rome's research foundations" — as the "trial run" for urban depopulation.

## Contexts Within the Source

Coleman presents Global 2000 as the lethal endpoint of the "post-industrial zero-growth society": ending industry collapses the resource base that supports population, so culling "must be practiced." He treats it as already underway ("'culling' is going on today, using the methods mandated in the 'Global 2000 Report'").

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Coleman builds the depopulation thesis from his own reading of a real government study (the Carter-era Global 2000 Report) plus assertion, without documentary proof that the Committee directed it as genocide; he offers no verifiable evidence of the claimed casualty quotas.
