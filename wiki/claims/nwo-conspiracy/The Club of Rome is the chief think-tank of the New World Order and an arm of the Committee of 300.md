## Claim Statement

Coleman asserts that the Club of Rome is **"the chief 'think tank' for the New World Order,"** a secret society set up "on the orders of the [[Committee of 300 (Coleman)]]" to serve as the Committee's "international foreign policy arm" — its "executor and overseer" — playing "a vital role in all U.S. government planning, internal and external."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The book's framing statement: "The Club of Rome (COR) is the chief 'think tank' for the New World Order ... Set up on the orders of the Committee of 300, its existence was denied until the silver anniversary celebrations of its founding held in Rome 25 years later." Coleman claims he first exposed it "back in 1970, [when] only a handful of people in intelligence knew of the existence of this, the most powerful secret society in the hands of the Committee of 300."

He stresses the deceptive name ("has nothing to do with the Vatican or the Catholic Church"), names an American chapter (Samuel Huntington, Cyrus Vance, Milton Friedman, Paul Volcker, Gerald Ford, et al.), and says it is funded by the German Marshall Fund. As "the executor and overseer of the Committee's foreign policy decisions," it allegedly directs events down to coercing nations via NATO and the IMF.

## Contexts Within the Source

This is the organizing claim from which all others follow: every subsequent allegation (deindustrialization, depopulation, terrorism) is presented as COR carrying out the Committee of 300's agenda.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Coleman's evidence is largely his own assertion and "minutes ... secretly recorded by an intelligence officer"; he offers no independent documentation of the Committee of 300's orders.
