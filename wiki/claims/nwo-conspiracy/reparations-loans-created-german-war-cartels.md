**Claim Statement**  
Wall Street reparations loans issued under the Dawes Plan (1924) and Young Plan (1928)—which were engineered by Morgan representatives and U.S. central bankers—were utilized to finance and consolidate the giant German industrial cartels, specifically I.G. Farben and Vereinigte Stahlwerke, which later funded Adolf Hitler's rise and manufactured the bulk of Germany's military materials during World War II.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *Wall Street and the Rise of Hitler* (Chapter One):
* **Dawes and Young Plans as Morgan Products:** Antony Sutton argues that the reparations plans were engineered by international bankers for their own profit. The Dawes Plan was "largely a J. P. Morgan production," and the U.S. delegations to these committees were J.P. Morgan delegations (including GE's Owen D. Young and J.P. Morgan himself) using the seal of the United States.
* **Funding the Cartels:** Sutton details that the three largest loans floated on Wall Street in the 1920s went directly to General Electric's German affiliate (A.E.G.), the steel cartel Vereinigte Stahlwerke (United Steelworks, managed by Dillon Read), and the chemical cartel I.G. Farben (managed by National City Co.).
* **Reparations Circular Financing:** Germany paid 36 billion marks in reparations to the Allies, but borrowed 33 billion marks abroad (primarily in the U.S.) during the same period, meaning the reparations were effectively funded by foreign buyers of German bonds issued by Wall Street firms (led by Dillon Read, Harris Forbes, and National City Company), who reaped huge commissions.
* **Explosives and War Materials Monopoly:** Built with this Wall Street capital, I.G. Farben and Vereinigte Stahlwerke cooperated to produce 95 percent of all German explosives in 1937–38 on the eve of World War II.

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Sutton presents this claim to show that the industrial base of the Nazi war machine was not built by Hitler, but was established in the 1920s by international bankers. He argues that this circular loan system represents a planned effort by corporate socialists to establish a controlled global cartel system.
