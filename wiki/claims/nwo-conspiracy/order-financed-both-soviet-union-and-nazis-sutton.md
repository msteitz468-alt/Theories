## Claim Statement

Sutton's flagship historical application of the dialectic: Wall Street Bonesmen financed and encouraged **both** the creation of the Soviet Union (the "thesis") **and** the rise of National Socialism in Germany (the "antithesis"), then guided the resulting Communism-vs-Nazism conflict to control its outcome.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He states that "Skull and Bones members were in the dominant decision-making positions — Bush, Harriman, Stimson, Lovett, and so on — all Bonesmen, and instrumental in guiding the conflict through use of 'right' and 'left.' They financed and encouraged the growths of both philosophies and controlled the outcome to a significant extent." Dedicated memoranda lay out "Thesis: The Order Creates the Soviet Union" and "Antithesis: Financing the Nazis," extending the documentary case of his earlier Wall Street trilogy.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Sutton presents the financing as documented through corporate and banking records, and frames the motive as Hegelian power-management and profit, not ethnic or partisan ideology.
