**Claim Statement**  
The Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) and its British counterpart, the Royal Institute of International Affairs (RIIA), were established as front organizations for the secret Round Table groups (founded by Cecil Rhodes and Lord Alfred Milner) to manipulate U.S. and British foreign policies and guide both nations toward a centralized world government controlled by international financiers.

**Source Attribution and Direct Evidence**  
According to *None Dare Call It Conspiracy* (Chapter Five):
* **Cecil Rhodes' Secret Society:** Gary Allen details how Cecil Rhodes left his fortune to establish a secret society modeled on the Jesuit order to expand British rule and bring the world under a single governing body. The society was run behind the scenes by Lord Alfred Milner and backed by the Rothschild banking house.
* **Formalization in Paris (1919):** Following World War I, representatives of the Round Table met at the Paris Peace Conference and drew up plans to expand their network. Lionel Curtis led the establishment of front organizations: the Royal Institute of International Affairs (RIIA) in London and the Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) in New York (formally founded in 1921 with "Colonel" Edward Mandell House playing a key role).
* **Quigley's Account:** Allen quotes Carroll Quigley's *Tragedy and Hope* describing the CFR's origin: "In New York it was known as the Council on Foreign Relations, and was a front for J.P. Morgan and Company in association with the very small American Round Table Group."
* **Infiltration of Administrations:** Allen asserts that the CFR has controlled the State Department and presidential administrations for decades. He claims that members of the CFR dominated the founding of the United Nations and notes that President Richard Nixon (a CFR member himself) appointed over 100 CFR members to high-level positions in his administration.

**Contexts Within the Source**  
Allen presents the CFR as the visible "midwife" of the Insiders' conspiracy in the United States. He highlights the organization's secrecy, noting that the mass media (many of whose executives are CFR members) deliberately avoids reporting on its meetings and influence, ensuring that the public remains unaware of the planned path toward a world superstate.
