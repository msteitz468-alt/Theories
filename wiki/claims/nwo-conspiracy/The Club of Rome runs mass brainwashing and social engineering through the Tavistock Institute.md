## Claim Statement

Coleman claims the Club of Rome carries out **mass brainwashing and social engineering through the Tavistock Institute of Human Relations** — "the mother of all brainwashing institutions" — which trained and "certified" COR founder Aurelio Peccei and, with allied bodies, engineered the counter-culture "Aquarian Conspiracy" to soften populations for the New World Order.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

In Chapter 7 Coleman says the COR sent Peccei "to England for training at the Tavistock Institute of Human Relations, the mother of all brainwashing institutions in the world." Chapter 8 describes Tavistock "under the direction and control of Major General" John Rawlings Reese (with Bertrand Russell and the Huxleys) "certif[ying] Peccei as 'qualified'" and dispatching him to NATO to recruit COR and Bilderberg members.

Coleman attributes engineered cultural decay to the same source: the Beatles, "Mick Jagger, Keith Richard and all manner of decadent, depraved amoral reprobates are the creation of the Tavistock Institute under the aegis of the COR," with "Made for television movies of street gangs" and a flood of "'New Age' literature" deployed against Japan. He names "Tavistock Institute, the Institute of Social Relations and Stanford Research Center" as the "official organs" of the "Aquarian Conspiracy," "where applied social psychiatry played a pivotal role in forming and guiding NATO." He summarizes COR member Willis Harmon's paper on reshaping the "image of man," and claims that when JFK "rejected the advice of the Tavistock Institute, the CFR, Rand Institute and Stanford ... That made Kennedy a man marked for elimination."

## Contexts Within the Source

Tavistock is presented as the COR's practical instrument for engineering consent — converting the abstract "post-industrial zero-growth" doctrine into mass psychological conditioning via music, "New Age" culture, drugs, and social-psychiatry programs run through NATO.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The links are Coleman's own attributions, asserted from his decades of "World in Review" reporting rather than documented institutional records; the causal chains (e.g. rock music "created" by Tavistock) are not evidenced beyond his statement.
