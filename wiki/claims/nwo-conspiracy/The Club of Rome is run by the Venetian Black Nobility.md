## Claim Statement

Coleman asserts that the Club of Rome "consists of the oldest members of the so-called **Black Nobility** of Europe" — the ancient Venetian and Genoese families — together with European royalty, who control it as part of the [[Committee of 300 (Coleman)]].

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"The Club of Rome consists of the oldest members of the so-called Black Nobility of Europe, descendants of the ancient families, who owned, controlled and ran Genoa and Venice in the 12th century. They are called 'Black Nobility' because of their use of dirty tricks, murder, terrorism, unethical behavior, and worship of Satan." Coleman calls them "the richest and most ancient of all European families, their wealth far surpassing that of the Rockefellers," and "part of the Committee of 300."

He names the **Guelph dynasty** as one of the oldest lines and asserts "Queen Elizabeth II ... is a Black Guelph — her great grandmother Victoria descended from that family." He ties specific COR-linked figures to these families (e.g. Richard Gardner's marriage into "the Luccatti family, one of the most powerful of the Venetian Black Nobility").

## Contexts Within the Source

The Black Nobility supplies, in Coleman's account, both the bloodline pedigree and the ruthless methods ("dirty tricks, sedition, terror-tactics") of the COR.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

The claim rests on Coleman's genealogical assertions; he provides no membership documentation. The framing overlaps with David Icke's reuse of "Black Nobility" — see [[House of Windsor (Icke)]] — though Coleman does not add Icke's reptilian element.
