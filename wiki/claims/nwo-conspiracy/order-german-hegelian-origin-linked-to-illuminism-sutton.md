## Claim Statement

Sutton argues The Order is German in origin — a chapter ("Chapter 322") of an older German secret society brought to Yale in 1832 — carrying a Hegelian/Illuminist philosophical lineage, and he raises directly the question "Is The Order also Illuminati?"

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He treats Skull & Bones as "a chapter of a German organization" and reads its very name, symbol (the death's-head, "the symbol of the Nazi Death Head Division"), and "Chapter 322" designation as marks of this German-Hegelian descent. His closing memoranda examine "The Secret Cult of The Order" — its ritual and "satanic aspects" — and pose the explicit question of an Illuminati connection, situating Bones within the same statist-philosophical current he blames for "both Naziism and Marxism."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Sutton frames the German/Illuminati origin as inference from the society's structure, symbolism, and philosophy rather than a documented charter, and poses the Illuminati link as a question.

## Contexts Within the Source

Connects Sutton's documentary case to the wider Illuminati literature: the same society is presented as a possible Illuminati incarnation in [[The modern Illuminati operates through bloodline families and elite societies toward a New World Order]] (Marrs) and [[Claim: Cooper asserts Skull and Bones and Scroll and Key are Chapter 322 of the Illuminati and their executive members form the JASON Society|Cooper's Chapter 322 / JASON Society claim]], and the German-Hegelian "managed conflict" lineage parallels Webster's [[The Bavarian Illuminati coordinated the strands and engineered the French Revolution (Webster)]].
