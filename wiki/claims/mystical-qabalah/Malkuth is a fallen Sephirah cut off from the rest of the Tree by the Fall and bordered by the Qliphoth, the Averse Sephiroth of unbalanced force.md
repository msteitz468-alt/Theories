## Claim Statement
Fortune states that Malkuth, the Kingdom, differs from the other Sephiroth in that it is "not part of any equilibrated triangle, but is said to be the receptacle of the influences of all the others," and that it "is a fallen Sephirah, for it was cut off from the rest of the Tree by the Fall," with "the coils of the Stooping Dragon arising from the World of Shells" separating it from its brethren. The Sphere of Malkuth "abuts upon the Hells of the Averse Sephiroth, the Qliphoth, or evil demons" — emanations of unbalanced force that arose during periods when the Sephiroth were not in equilibrium, called "the Kings of Edom" or "the Kings of Unbalanced Force."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 7, section 31: "There remains for our final consideration the Sephirah Malkuth, the Kingdom of Earth. This Sephirah differs from the others in several respects. Firstly, it is not part of any equilibrated triangle, but is said to be the receptacle of the influences of all the others. Secondly, it is a fallen Sephirah, for it was cut off from the rest of the Tree by the Fall... The Sphere of Malkuth abuts upon the Hells of the Averse Sephiroth, the Qliphoth, or evil demons." Section 33: "The Qliphoth (singular Qliphah, an immodest woman or harlot) are the Evil or Averse Sephiroth, each an emanation of unbalanced force from its corresponding Sphere upon the Holy Tree... referred to as the Kings of Unbalanced Force, the Kings of Edom."

## Contexts Within the Source
This claim closes Fortune's Chapter 7 preliminary survey of the whole Tree, deferring full treatment of the Qliphoth to a later chapter while establishing enough context to place Malkuth correctly, and comparing the Tree as a whole to Crowley's image of "a card-index file, in which each symbol is an envelope."
