## Claim Statement
Fortune rejects the idea of God "fabricating creation stage by stage" in favor of emanation: "each Sephirah were a pool which, being filled, overflowed into a lower pool." Borrowing Mathers' image, "hidden in an acorn is an oak with its acorns, and hidden in each of these is an oak with its acorns" — so each Sephirah "contains the Potentiality of all that come after it," with Kether containing the other nine and Chokmah containing the eight after it, and so on. In any given Sephirah only one aspect of manifestation is unfolded; later aspects remain latent and earlier ones are present only "by reflection." The order of overflow is traced on the Tree by the Lightning Flash (or Flaming Sword).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 6, section 12: "The Qabalah does not conceive of God as fabricating creation stage by stage, but thinks of the different phases of manifestation as evolving one from another, as if each Sephirah were a pool which, being filled, overflowed into a lower pool... hidden in an acorn is an oak with its acorns... So each Sephirah contains the Potentiality of all that come after it in the scale of downflowing manifestation. Kether contains the rest of the Sephiroth, nine in number." Section 14: "Let us conceive of Kether, then, as a fountain which fills its basin, and the overflow therefrom feeds another fountain, which in its turn fills its basin and overflows."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune contrasts this emanationist model favorably with exoteric Judaism's (and its Christian heir's) concept of a "celestial craftsman" making each part of creation separately, arguing the emanation doctrine anticipated modern evolutionary science.
