## Claim Statement
Fortune holds that the deities of all pagan pantheons can be classified into the ten "pigeon-holes" of the Ten Holy Sephiroth using astrology as "the one universal language, for all people see the same Planets": Space to Kether, the Zodiac to Chokmah, the seven planets to the next seven Sephiroth, and Earth to Malkuth. Saturn-gods (e.g. Kronos) go to Binah, Jupiter to Chesed, war/destructive gods to Geburah, love goddesses to Netzach, wisdom-initiator gods to Hod, sacrificed/redeemer gods to Tiphareth, corn goddesses to Malkuth, and lunar goddesses to Yesod; the Supernal triangle (Kether, Chokmah, Binah) always corresponds to the "Old Gods," the predecessors recognized by a given pantheon's current faith.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XII: "we sort out the gods and goddesses of all the pagan pantheons into the ten pigeon-holes of the Ten Holy Sephiroth, relying chiefly upon their astrological associations to guide us... Space is referred to Kether, the Zodiac to Chokmah, the seven planets to the next seven Sephiroth, and Earth to Malkuth... any god who has an analogy with Saturn will be referred to Binah... The Supernal triangle of Kether, Chokmah, and Binah always refers to the Old Gods... Thus Rhea and Kronos would be referred to Binah and Chokmah, and Jupiter to Chesed. All the corn goddesses refer to Malkuth, and all the lunar goddesses to Yesod. The war gods and destructive gods... refer to Geburah, and the goddesses of love to Netzach. The initiator gods of wisdom are referred to Hod, and the sacrificed gods and redeemers to Tiphareth."

## Contexts Within the Source
She frames this within an argument that primitive personification of natural forces as gods was not "crude" but a genuine, if pre-scientific, monistic cosmology, and urges sympathetic rather than dismissive study of pagan and non-Christian religion (citing Alexandra David-Néel and W. B. Seabrook), quoting Richard Payne Knight's observation of "the remarkable concurrence of the allegories, symbols, and titles of ancient mythology in favour of the mystic system of emanations."
