## Claim Statement
Following MacGregor Mathers' fourfold classification (Practical, Dogmatic, Literal, and Unwritten Qabalah), Fortune defines the Unwritten Qabalah as "a correct knowledge of the manner in which the symbol-systems are arranged on the Tree of Life." She explains the Tree has twenty-two Paths (not the "Thirty-two Mystical Paths" the Rabbis speak of, since the Ten Sephiroth themselves were counted as the first ten Paths as a deliberate "blind" for the uninitiated), and that the twenty-two Hebrew letters, twenty-two Tarot trumps, twelve zodiac signs, seven planets, and three of the four elements (Earth being excluded, since we ourselves are in the Element of Earth) are all correctly assigned only to these twenty-two Paths.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 4, section 9: "The Unwritten Qabalah, which consists of a correct knowledge of the manner in which the symbol-systems are arranged on the Tree of Life." Section 12: "We find, if we count them, that there are twenty-two, not thirty-two Paths upon the Tree; but for their purposes the Rabbis treated the Ten Sephiroth themselves as Paths, thus misleading the uninitiated." Section 13: "Upon the physical plane we are ourselves in the Element of Earth, therefore that symbol does not appear upon the Paths which lead into the Unseen. Remove this, and we are left with twenty-two symbols, which fit accurately... with the Tarot trumps."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune presents this as a deliberately concealed "blind" of the ancient Rabbis, now disclosed because, in her judgment, withholding it serves only to preserve the prestige of a priesthood rather than protect any genuinely dangerous knowledge.
