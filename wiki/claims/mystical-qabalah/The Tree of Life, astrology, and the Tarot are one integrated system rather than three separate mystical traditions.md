## Claim Statement
Fortune declares that "the Tree of Life, astrology, and the Tarot are not three mystical systems, but three aspects of one and the same system, and each is unintelligible without the others." Astrology is only philosophically coherent when interpreted through the Tree's Four Worlds (e.g. Saturn functions as Divine Mother Binah in Atziluth but very differently in Assiah), and the Tarot's four suits/court cards/aces-through-tens map directly onto the Four Worlds and the Ten Sephiroth, following Crowley's system in "777" rather than Waite's noncommittal one.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter X: "The Tree of Life, astrology, and the Tarot are not three mystical systems, but three aspects of one and the same system... It is only when we study astrology on the basis of the Tree that we have a philosophical system... Astrology is so elusive because the uninitiated astrologer works on one plane only; but the initiated astrologer, with the Tree as his ground-plan, interprets on the four planes of the Four Worlds." She likens working without this key to "the foolhardy person who has a pharmacopoeia of patent medicines," and states, "the suit of Wands is assigned to Atziluth and Fire. The suit of Cups to Briah and Water. The suit of Swords to Yetzirah and Air. The suit of Pentacles or Coins to Assiah and Earth," with the four aces assigned to Kether, the four twos to Chokmah, and so on down to the tens assigned to Malkuth.

## Contexts Within the Source
She favors Crowley's "777" attribution system over Waite's because "the correspondences work out without discrepancies, a thing they do in none of the other systems," and notes the court cards (King/Prince/Princess in the esoteric Tarot) correspond respectively to Yod, Vau, and He final of Tetragrammaton.
