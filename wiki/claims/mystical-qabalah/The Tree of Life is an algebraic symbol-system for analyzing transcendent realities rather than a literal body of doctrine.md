## Claim Statement
Fortune explains that the Qabalist "goes to work in exactly the opposite way to the student of natural science; the latter builds up synthetic concepts; the former analyses abstract concepts." The Tree of Life functions "exactly the same principle as algebra": symbols stand in for unknown transcendent quantities, and by working out their relations to known quantities the mind comes to understand what was originally unthinkable, without the mind ever grasping the Absolute directly.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 3, section 2: "The student of the Qabalah goes to work in exactly the opposite way to the student of natural science; the latter builds up synthetic concepts; the former analyses abstract concepts." Section 7: "It is exactly the same principle as algebra. Let X represent the unknown quantity, let Y represent the half of X, and let Z represent something we know... we may in the end be able to express Y in terms of Z, and then we shall begin to understand X." Section 9: "The initiate... uses the symbol as a means of guiding thought out into the Unseen and Incomprehensible."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune distinguishes this "algebraic" use of symbolism, employed by initiates, from the ordinary devotional use of symbols such as the Cross or phallic emblems, which merely concentrate the mind and stir associated feelings without functioning as instruments of calculation.
