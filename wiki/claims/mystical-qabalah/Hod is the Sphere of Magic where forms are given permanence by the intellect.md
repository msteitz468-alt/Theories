## Claim Statement
Fortune states that "Hod is especially the Sphere of Magic, because it is the sphere of the formulation of forms, and is therefore the sphere in which the magician actually works, for it is his mind that formulates the forms, and his will that makes the link with the natural forces of the Sphere of Netzach that ensoul them"; without Netzach's force, Hod's forms cannot be ensouled, and without Hod's formulating intellect, Netzach's forces remain blind.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXIII, §17: "The functioning triangle of the Lower Triad consists of Hod, Netzach, and Yesod. Hod and Netzach, as we have noted before, are respectively Form and Force on the astral plane... Hod is especially the Sphere of Magic, because it is the sphere of the formulation of forms, and is therefore the sphere in which the magician actually works... Be it noted, however, that without the contacts of Netzach, the force aspect of the astral, there could be no ensouling." §43 sums up: "in Hod we have the Sphere of formal magic as distinguished from simple mind power. The forms that are formulated thereon by the magician initiating the forces of nature are the Beni Elohim, or Sons of the Gods."

## Contexts Within the Source
Developed through the extended discussion of the "functioning triangle" of Hod, Netzach, and Yesod, and Fortune's account of how primitive man's imagination builds and ensouls god-forms, culminating in her account of the Mass as an operation of this kind.
