## Claim Statement
Fortune reads the Tarot symbolism of Hod's four Eights — "Abandoned Success" (Cups), "The Lord of Shortened Force" (Swords), "Prudence" (Pentacles), all summed up under "Swiftness" (Wands) — as expressing that "it is through inhibitions and refrainings on the lower planes that the dynamic energy of the highest plane is rendered available," and that sublimation, distinct from repression or eradication, is the key to the powers of Hod.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXIII, §21-24: "'Abandoned Success,' the inhibition of the instinctive reaction which would give satisfaction—in other words, sublimation—is the key to the powers of Hod. But remember that sublimation is not the same thing as either repression or eradication... We see, then, that it is through inhibitions and refrainings on the lower planes that the dynamic energy of the highest plane is rendered available. It is in the Sphere of Hod that the rational mind imposes these inhibitions on the dynamic animal nature of the soul... This power of direction and control is only obtained by the sacrifice of fluidity, and Hod is therefore aptly said to be the reflection of Binah through Chesed."

## Contexts Within the Source
Given as the interpretive key to the four Eights of the Tarot assigned to Hod, following her account of Hod as "the mean of the Primordial" and preceding the detailed treatment of the God-name, Archangel Michael, and the Order of Angels of Hod.
