## Claim Statement
Fortune explains that Kether "differentiates into Chokmah and Binah before it achieves phenomenal existence," and these two are called by the Qabalists "Abba, the Supernal Father, and Ama, the Supernal Mother." Chokmah is "an active male potency" and Binah "a passive female potency," standing at the heads of the two side pillars of the Tree (Chokmah on the Pillar of Mercy, Binah on the Pillar of Severity), with Kether's Middle Pillar being the "Column of Equilibrium." Binah is also called the Great Sea and Shabbathai, the Sphere of Saturn; she is two-aspected as Ama, the Dark Sterile Mother, and Aima, the Bright Fertile Mother. Considered as a pair, Chokmah and Binah represent Force and Form.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 7, section 8: "Kether differentiates into Chokmah and Binah before it achieves phenomenal existence, and these two are called by the Qabalists, Abba, the Supernal Father, and Ama, the Supernal Mother. Binah is also called the Great Sea, and Shabbathai, the Sphere of Saturn." Section 10: "We have, then, Kether differentiating into an active male potency, Chokmah, and a passive female potency, Binah." Section 12: Binah "is two-aspected, and these aspects are distinguished as Ama, the Dark Sterile Mother, and Aima, the Bright Fertile Mother." Section 14: "we see Chokmah and Binah as Force and Form, the two units of manifestation."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune presents this differentiation as "the root concept of our cosmogony," invoking Blavatsky's principle that "there can be no manifestation without differentiation into the Pairs of Opposites," a pattern she says will recur "again and again under innumerable aspects" throughout the book.
