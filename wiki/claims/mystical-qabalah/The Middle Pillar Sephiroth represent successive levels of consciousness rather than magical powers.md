## Claim Statement
Fortune teaches that the Sephiroth on the Middle Pillar (Malkuth, Yesod, Tiphareth, Daath, Kether) represent levels of consciousness and the planes on which consciousness operates, whereas the paired Sephiroth on the two side Pillars carry the magical powers. Consciousness descends plane by plane and manifests only when the polarizing (side-Pillar) Sephiroth reach equilibrium.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter VIII: "The Sephiroth on the Middle Pillar may be taken as representing levels of consciousness and the planes on which they operate. Malkuth is sensory consciousness; Yesod is astral psychism; Tiphareth is illuminated consciousness, the highest aspect of the personality with which the individuality has coalesced... it is here, too, that we die to the lower self in order that we may rise in the higher self." She continues: "the modes of consciousness are assigned to the Equilibrating Sephiroth upon the Middle Pillar, but the magical powers are assigned to the opposing Sephiroth, each at the end of the beam of the balance of the pairs of opposites."

## Contexts Within the Source
She links Tiphareth's illuminated consciousness to why "the Messiahs and Saviours of the world are assigned to Tiphareth," and distinguishes the "Way of Initiation" (following the Serpent of Wisdom, i.e. the full Lightning Flash) from the swifter "Way of Illumination" via the Path of the Arrow (Malkuth to Yesod to Tiphareth) reserved to the mystic rather than the occultist.
