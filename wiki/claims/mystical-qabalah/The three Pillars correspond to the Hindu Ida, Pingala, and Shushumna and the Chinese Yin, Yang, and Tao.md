## Claim Statement
Fortune argues that the Tree's three-Pillar structure is independently corroborated by two other metaphysical systems: the three channels of Prana in Yoga (Ida, Pingala, Shushumna) and the Yin/Yang/Tao of Chinese philosophy, and that "by the agreement of witnesses truth is established" when three great metaphysical systems concur. She takes the Central Pillar to represent consciousness and the two side Pillars the positive and negative factors of manifestation, noting that Kundalini's rise through the Shushumna and the Western magical "Rising on the Planes" up the Central Pillar (via the Path of the Arrow, Malkuth-Yesod-Tiphareth) are parallel operations.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XI: "The first and most obvious division of the Tree is into the three Pillars, and this immediately reminds us of the three channels of Prana described by the yogis, Ida, Pingala, and Shushumna; and the two principles, the Yin and the Yang of Chinese philosophy, and the Tao, or Way, which is the equilibrium between them. By the agreement of witnesses truth is established... we may conclude that we are dealing with established principles." She concludes the chapter: "the Pillar of Severity, with Binah at its head, is... the Pingala of the Hindus and the Yang of the Chinese; the Pillar of Mercy, with Chokmah at its head, is the Ida of the Hindus and the Yin of the Chinese; and the Pillar of Equilibrium is Shushumna and Tao."

## Contexts Within the Source
She uses this correspondence to organize the whole chapter's survey of the Tree: the Pillar of Severity (Binah, Geburah, Hod = Saturn, Mars, Mercury) as "form"-Sephiroth, the Pillar of Mercy (Chokmah, Chesed, Netzach = Zodiac, Jupiter, Venus) as "force"-Sephiroth, synthesized respectively in Daath/Tiphareth and in the Netzach-Hod-Yesod triad of "the magical and astral side of things."
