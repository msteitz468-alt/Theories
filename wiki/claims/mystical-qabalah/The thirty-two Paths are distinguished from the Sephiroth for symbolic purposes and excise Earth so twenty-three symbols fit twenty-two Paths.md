## Claim Statement
Fortune explains that although the Sepher Yetzirah technically calls both the Ten Sephiroth and their connecting lines "Paths" (thirty-two in all), practical Qabalistic working reserves the term "Paths" for the twenty-two connecting lines only, so they can be equated with the twenty-two letters of the Hebrew alphabet. Since the symbol-set to be distributed across the Paths (twelve zodiac signs, seven planets, four elements) totals twenty-three, the element of Earth is omitted — because human consciousness is already "in the element of Earth" and Malkuth supplies all the earth needed — leaving exactly twenty-two correspondences for twenty-two Paths.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter X: "it is usual in practical working to consider the lines between the Sephiroth only as the Paths, and the Sephiroth themselves as Spheres upon the Tree. This is one of the many tricks and blinds to be found in the Qabalistic system... there are twelve signs in the Zodiac, seven planets, and four elements, making in all twenty-three symbols. How are these to be arranged on Twenty-two Paths? Here is another Qabalistic blind... Our consciousness being in the element of Earth, we do not need the symbol of earth in our calculations... Malkuth is all the earth we need for practical purposes."

## Contexts Within the Source
She adds that the twenty-two trumps of the Tarot major arcana form a third set of Path-symbols, and that together with the four colour scales these three symbol-sets plus their innumerable correspondences constitute the whole of Qabalistic "major symbolism."
