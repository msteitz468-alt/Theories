## Claim Statement
Fortune explains that the symbolic animals, plants, precious stones, drugs, and perfumes traditionally assigned to each Sephirah and Path serve two functions: they are keys correlating the gods of different pantheons to the Sephiroth, and — more practically — they act as landmarks confirming or discrediting visions experienced in spirit-vision travel, since an out-of-place symbol signals "confusion of plane" and an unreliable vision.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XIII: "Each Sephirah and Path has assigned to it symbolic animals, plants, and precious stones. It is necessary that the student should know these for two reasons: firstly, they give some very important keys to the relations of the gods of the different pantheons to the Sephiroth; and secondly, they form part of the symbolism of the Astral Paths and serve as landmarks when travelling in the spirit-vision. For instance, if one saw a horse (Mars) or a jackal (Luna) in the sphere of Netzach (Venus), one would know that there was confusion of plane and the vision was not reliable." On stones: "a blood-red ruby for the fiery Martian forces of Geburah; an emerald for the Green Ray nature forces of Netzach."

## Contexts Within the Source
She grounds the symbolism's validity by appeal to Jung's theory of the myth-making faculty and to the poet "A. E." (whom she claims as a fellow student "nurtured on the mystical Qabalah"), arguing the racial imagination's animal-god associations are not arbitrary but penetrate "far more deeply into their secret causes" than science has yet recognized; she also notes certain drugs (aphrodisiacs to Netzach, abortifacients to Yesod-Hecate, analgesics to Chesed, irritants to Geburah) fall under the same principle of correspondence, studied seriously by initiate-physicians such as Paracelsus.
