## Claim Statement
Fortune states that "each symbol upon the Tree represents a cosmic force or factor. When the mind concentrates upon it, it comes into touch with that force" — a "channel in consciousness" is made between the individual mind and a factor in the world-soul, "and through this channel the waters of the ocean pour into the lagoon." She holds this produces "a tremendous access of energy to the individual soul," which is the source of magical power, but warns that the Higher Self must rule this influx or the result is "unbalanced force," likened to "the rule of the Kings of Edom."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 3, section 16: "Each symbol upon the Tree represents a cosmic force or factor. When the mind concentrates upon it, it comes into touch with that force... The aspirant who uses the Tree as his meditation-symbol establishes point by point the union between his soul and the world-soul. This results in a tremendous access of energy to the individual soul; it is this which endows it with magical powers." Section 17: "The Higher Self must dominate its universe or there will be unbalanced force... Then do we have the rule of the Kings of Edom, whose kingdoms are unbalanced force."

## Contexts Within the Source
This claim closes Chapter 3's account of method, linking the algebraic/correspondence use of the Tree to concrete magical results and to the moral requirement of a governing Higher Self.
