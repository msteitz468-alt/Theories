## Claim Statement
Fortune argues that although Negative Existence is "outside the range of our realisation," we are not outside the range of its influence: "all that we know as existing has its roots in this Negative Existence," so "we cannot know it directly" yet "have experience of it at one remove," just as "we are ignorant of the nature of electricity yet are able to turn it to good account in our lives." She adds that symbols such as "Ain Soph Aur, the Limitless Light" or the adept's remark "God is pressure" can convey a genuine realization to the mind even though, analyzed as mere words, "there was nothing in them."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 5, section 11: "Although we cannot know its nature, we know its effects, in the same way as we are ignorant of the nature of electricity yet are able to turn it to good account in our lives, and from our experience of its effects we are able to come to certain conclusions concerning some at least of the qualities it must possess." The same section recounts an adept's statement, "God is pressure," and Fortune's realization that it conveyed "an image... to the mind" though the words themselves held "nothing."

## Contexts Within the Source
This claim explains why the Qabalistic method uses symbols and seed-sentences rather than direct definition: transcendent ideas cannot be grasped by reason, only "impregnated" into the mind to gestate below the threshold of consciousness until understanding is born.
