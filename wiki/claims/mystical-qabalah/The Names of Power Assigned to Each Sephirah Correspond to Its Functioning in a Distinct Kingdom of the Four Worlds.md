## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that a Sephirah's God-Name represents its most spiritual form of functioning in Atziluth (the highest of the Four Kingdoms); its Archangelic Name represents its functioning in Briah, the Kingdom of the higher mind; its Order of Angels corresponds to Yetzirah, the Astral Plane; and its Mundane Chakra represents the force's functioning in Assiah, the Material Plane.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"SECTION 6. The Names of Power assigned to each Sephira - The God-name represents the most spiritual form of the force and is conceived of as representing the functioning of that force in the Kingdom of Atziluth, the highest of the... Kingdoms of the Qabalists" (Ch. XIV.12). "The Archangelic Names represent the functioning of a force in Briah, the Kingdom of the higher mind, are the archetypal ideas" (Ch. XIV.13). "Angelic choirs correspond to the Kingdom of... the Astral Plane, and the Mundane Chakras are representatives of each force in the Kingdom of Assiah - the Material Plane" (Ch. XIV.14).

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune later applies this scheme in her invocatory practice: "It is a maxim of White Occultism that every operation should commence with the invocation of the God-name of the Sphere in which the operation is to take place. This ensures that the operation shall be in harmony with cosmic law" (Ch. XV.11). She distinguishes the "spiritual experience" (her preferred term for the Sephiroth) from Crowley's "magical power," a term she reserves for the Twenty-two Paths (Ch. XIV.15).
