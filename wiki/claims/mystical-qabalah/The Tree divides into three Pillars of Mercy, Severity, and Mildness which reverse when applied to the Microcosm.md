## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that the primary conformation of the Tree of Life is into three vertical Pillars: the Right-hand Pillar of Mercy (Chokmah, Chesed, Netzach), the Left-hand Pillar of Severity (Binah, Geburah, Hod), and the Middle Pillar of Mildness or Equilibrium. When the Tree is used to represent the Macrocosm these sides are read as given, but when used to represent the Microcosm (the human being) one must mentally "back into" the diagram, reversing right and left so the Middle Pillar equates with the spine.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter VIII: "The primary conformation of the Tree is into three Pillars... called the Right-hand Pillar of Mercy, the Left-hand Pillar of Severity, and the Middle Pillar of Mildness or Equilibrium." She adds that when using the Tree to represent "our own being, we, as it were, back into it, so that the Middle Pillar equates with the spine, and the Pillar that contains Binah, Geburah, and Hod with the right side... These three Pillars can also be equated with the Shushumna, Ida, and Pingala of the Yoga system."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune warns this reversal is "very important to remember," criticizing A. E. Waite for reversing the usual (objective) presentation of the Tree in his frontispiece without noting the distinction. She treats the Pillar of Severity as negative/feminine and the Pillar of Mercy as positive/masculine.
