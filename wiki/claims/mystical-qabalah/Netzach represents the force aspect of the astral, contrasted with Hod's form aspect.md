## Claim Statement
Fortune states that Netzach, the Sphere of Venus, is "best understood by contrasting it with Hod, the Sphere of Mercury," representing force and form on a lower arc: Netzach is the instincts and the emotions they give rise to, while Hod is the concrete mind; in Netzach force is still relatively free-moving and fluidic, while in Hod it takes on definite, if tenuous, permanent form; "All mind is group-mind in Netzach, but in Hod the human mind has its beginnings."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXII, §1: "Netzach, the Sphere of Venus, is best understood by contrasting it with Hod, the Sphere of Mercury, these two representing force and form on a lower arc... Netzach represents the instincts and the emotions they give rise to, and Hod represents the concrete mind... In Netzach a particular form of force represents itself as a type of beings, flowing backwards and forwards over the boundaries of manifestation in an exceedingly elusive manner... In Hod, however, individualisation into units has taken place, and there is continuity of existence."

## Contexts Within the Source
This is the opening structural framework of the Netzach chapter, establishing the force/form pairing that Fortune returns to repeatedly through the rest of the four lower Sephiroth (Netzach/Hod, and later the "functioning triangle" of Hod-Netzach-Yesod).
