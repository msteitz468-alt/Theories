## Claim Statement
Fortune distinguishes "positive evil," a force moving against the current of evolution, from "negative evil," which is "simply the opposition of an inertia which has not yet been overcome, or of a momentum which has not yet been neutralised" (e.g. the natural conservatism of a mature mind versus the natural iconoclasm of youth). She rejects dualism as a heresy, holding that "we cannot deal with evil by cutting it off and destroying it, but only by absorbing and harmonising it," and that equilibrium is a dynamic "stability of strain," not the static grip of a clamp.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXVI, §9-10: "Positive evil is a force which is moving against the current of evolution; negative evil is simply the opposition of an inertia which has not yet been overcome... The natural conservatism of a mature mind is regarded as evil by the would-be reformer; the natural iconoclasm of youth is regarded as evil by the administrator." §14-15: "The instructed and enlightened expositors of all religions regard dualism as a heresy... We cannot deal with evil by cutting it off and destroying it, but only by absorbing and harmonising it." §18: "The equilibrium of the universe may best be likened to the swing of a pendulum rather than the grip of a clamp... it is a stability, not of inertia, but of strain."

## Contexts Within the Source
Follows Fortune's account of the origin of each Qliphah as an emanation of unbalanced force, and precedes the Conclusion; she illustrates the pendulum image with Geburah/Gedulah (Mars/Jupiter) swinging between "the Burning and Destroying Forces" and "the Permitters of Destruction."
