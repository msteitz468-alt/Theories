## Claim Statement
Fortune describes the Otz Chiim as "a set of ten circles arranged in a certain pattern, and connected among themselves by lines" — the circles being the Ten Holy Sephiroth ("Ten Holy Emanations," each a phase of evolution and objective), and the connecting lines being the twenty-two Paths (phases of subjective consciousness, "the Paths or grades... by which the soul unfolds its realisation of the cosmos"). The Sephiroth are arranged "in three vertical columns," with Kether at the apex of the topmost triangle.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 6, section 3: "It is represented... as a set of ten circles arranged in a certain pattern, and connected among themselves by lines. The circles are the Ten Holy Sephiroth, and the connecting lines are the Paths, twenty-two in number." Section 4: "Each Sephirah... is a phase of evolution, and in the language of the Rabbis they are called the Ten Holy Emanations. The Paths between them are phases of subjective consciousness... The Sephiroth are objective; the Paths are subjective." Section 6: "the circles which represent the Sephiroth are arranged in three vertical columns."

## Contexts Within the Source
This is Fortune's opening definition of the Tree's overall structure in Chapter 6, preparatory to detailed study of the individual Sephiroth and Paths in later chapters, framed as the "modern," "Alchemical Qabalah" built by generations of initiates rather than mere historical curiosity.
