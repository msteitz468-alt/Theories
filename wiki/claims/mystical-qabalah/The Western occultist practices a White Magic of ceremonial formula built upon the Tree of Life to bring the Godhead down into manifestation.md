## Claim Statement
Fortune holds that the Western occultist cultivates "a White Magic," "the application of occult powers to spiritual ends," selecting and concentrating forces by means of formulae "based upon the Qabalistic Tree of Life." Whatever pantheon or god-form he works with — Egyptian, Greek, Nordic, Druidic — the operation is strictly related to the Paths of the Tree, so that a grade attained in one system (e.g., the Sephirah Netzach) gives access to the equivalent powers (Isis, Aphrodite, Freya, Keridwen) in every other traditional system.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 2, section 8: "He does not try to escape from matter into spirit... he wants to bring the Godhead down into manhood... This is the root-motive for the acquisition of occult powers upon the Right-hand Path... and explains why initiates do not abandon all for the mystic Divine Union, but cultivate a White Magic." Section 10: "It is by means of formula that the occultist selects and concentrates the forces he wishes to work with. These formulae are based upon the Qabalistic Tree of Life... If he possesses the grade which corresponds to the Sephirah Netzach, he can work with the manifestation of the force of that aspect of the Godhead... In the Egyptian system it will be the Isis of Nature; in the Greek, Aphrodite; in the Nordic, Freya; in the Druidic, Keridwen."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune presents the Qabalah as the "best groundwork" prior to experimenting with pagan systems, because it is "essentially monotheistic," treating all potencies as messengers of a single God rather than independent fellow-workers — a principle she considers necessary for training students of the Arcane forces.
