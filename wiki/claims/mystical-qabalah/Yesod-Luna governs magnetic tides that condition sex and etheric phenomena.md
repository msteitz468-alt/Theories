## Claim Statement
Fortune teaches that the Moon and Earth share one etheric double, with the Moon as the positive pole, so that etheric activity everywhere waxes and wanes with the twenty-eight-day lunar cycle; that these lunar tides govern the reproductive processes of plants and animals (including the human female sexual cycle) and that practical occult work is best avoided during the dark of the Moon, when "unorganised forces have a tendency to rise up and give trouble" and "the Dragon of the Qliphoth raises his multiple heads."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXIV, §16-19: "The Moon and the Earth, according to the occult theory, share one etheric double... in etheric matters the Moon is the Positive Pole of the battery, and the Earth the negative one... it is the light of the Moon which is the stimulative factor in these etheric activities, and as Earth and Moon share one etheric double, all etheric activities are at their most active when the Moon is at its full. Likewise, during the dark of the Moon, etheric energy is at its lowest, and unorganised forces have a tendency to rise up and give trouble. The Dragon of the Qliphoth raises his multiple heads." §29 links this to the generative organs assigned to Yesod: "all the more important aspects of sex are etheric and magnetic."

## Contexts Within the Source
Placed within Fortune's discussion of the Moon-goddess symbolism of Yesod (Diana, Isis, Hecate, Thoth) as the key to why the generative organs, though physical, are assigned to an etheric Sephirah.
