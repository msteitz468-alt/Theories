## Claim Statement
Fortune explains that each Sephirah has "a fourfold nature," a point of contact with each of the Four Worlds — Atziluth (Archetypal/Emanations), Briah (Creation/Thrones), Yetzirah (Formation/Angels), and Assiah (Action/Matter). In Atziluth each Sephirah manifests through one of the Ten Holy Names of God; in Briah through one of the Ten Mighty Archangels; in Yetzirah through an Angelic Host or Choir; and in Assiah — properly the Lower Astral and Etheric background of matter rather than gross matter itself — through a Mundane Chakra. Thus each Sephirah consists of a Mundane Chakra, an angelic host, an Archangelic Throne, and a special aspect of Deity, behind all of which stands God as He is, hidden behind the Negative Veils of Existence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 4, section 14: "But a Sephirah cannot be understood upon a single plane; it has a fourfold nature. The Qabalists express this by saying that there are four worlds: Atziluth... Briah... Yetzirah... Assiah." Sections 15–19 detail the Ten Holy Names, the Ten Mighty Archangels, the Angelic Hosts, and the Ten Mundane Chakras respectively, concluding: "each Sephirah will therefore consist, firstly, of its Mundane Chakra; secondly, of an angelic host of beings... thirdly, an Arch-angelic Consciousness, or Throne; and fourthly, a special aspect of the Deity."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune presents this fourfold schema as foundational background needed before the individual Sephiroth can be studied in detail in later chapters, and links the Sephiroth/Paths distinction to a macrocosmic/microcosmic one: the Sephiroth as successive Divine Emanations, the Paths as stages of unfolding human realization.
