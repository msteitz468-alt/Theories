## Claim Statement
Fortune holds that "no operation is completed until the process has been expressed in terms of Malkuth, or, in other words, has issued forth in action on the physical plane"; force not properly "earthed" in this way is left "lying around" and causes disorder ("the general Psychic upheaval and run of bad luck") in magical work — hence the necessity of ritual action on the physical plane, and of channels of manifestation such as the magnetism of living substances, talismans, and the operator's own body.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXV, §33: "It should be recognised, however, that no operation is completed until the process has been expressed in terms of Malkuth... If this is not done, the force that has been generated is not properly 'earthed,' and it is this loose force left lying around that causes the trouble in magical experiments." §57: "Every magical operation must come through to Malkuth before it can be reckoned to have attained completion, for only in Malkuth is the force finally locked home into form. Therefore all magical work is better carried out in the form of a ritual performed on the physical plane... than simply as a form of meditation operating upon the astral plane only."

## Contexts Within the Source
Developed through Fortune's discussion of talismans, ectoplasm, the "assumption of the god-forms," and mediumship as methods of transition from Yesod to Malkuth, and applied to her example of healing, where she argues for invoking spiritual power through natural channels (the physician) rather than bypassing Malkuth entirely.
