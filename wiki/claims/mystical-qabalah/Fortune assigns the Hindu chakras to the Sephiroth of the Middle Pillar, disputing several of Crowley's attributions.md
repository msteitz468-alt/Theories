## Claim Statement
Fortune correlates the Yoga chakras with the Middle Pillar Sephiroth — Malkuth with the Muladhara (four-petalled) Lotus at the perineum, Yesod with the Svadisthana (six-petalled) Lotus at the generative organs, Tiphareth with the Manipura and Anahata Chakras (solar plexus/breast), Daath uniting the Vishuddha (larynx) and Ajna (nose/pineal) Chakras, and Kether with the Sahasrara (thousand-petalled) Lotus above the head — but disputes Crowley's attribution of Muladhara and Svadisthana to Yesod and Hod respectively, preferring General J. F. C. Fuller's scheme, which she says avoids contradiction and better matches the principle that levels of consciousness belong to the Central Pillar.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XI: "General Fuller assigns the Muladhara Lotus to Malkuth, pointing out that its four petals correspond with the four elements... I consider that... the attribution of Fuller, who refers the Muladhara Lotus to Malkuth, is to be preferred to that of Crowley, who in column CXVIII refers it to Yesod, thus contradicting himself" (since Crowley elsewhere assigns the phallus to Yesod). She adds: "Crowley, though assigning Yesod to the phallus... assigns the Svadisthana Lotus to Hod, Mercury. It is difficult to understand this attribution... I consider it better to adhere to the principle of referring the levels of consciousness to the Central Pillar." On Tiphareth: "by universal consent, represents the solar plexus and breast; it therefore seems reasonable to attribute to it the Manipura and Anahata Chakras, as Crowley does."

## Contexts Within the Source
She stresses correlation between the Western fourfold and Eastern sevenfold systems is imperfect and that "it is better to look for first principles rather than obtain a tidy pattern of arrangement which does violence to the correspondences," naming Crowley and General Fuller as the only writers she knows who have attempted the correlation at all.
