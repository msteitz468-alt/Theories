## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that Chokmah and Binah constitute the first Pair of Opposites — primordial Maleness and Femaleness, Force and Form — with Chokmah as pure, boundless, out-flowing dynamism ("a channel for the passage of force") and Binah as the stabilising, form-giving principle that organizes that force into stable structure; each is incomprehensible without reference to the other, and their union is the archetype of sex, generation, and all polarity in the universe.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"In Binah and Chokmah we have the archetypal Positive and Negative; the primordial Maleness and Femaleness, established while 'countenance beheld not countenance'... It is from these primary Pairs of Opposites that the Pillars of the Universe spring" (Ch. XVI.5). "Chokmah is not an organising Sephirah, but it is the Great Stimulator of the Universe. It is from Chokmah that Binah, the Third Sephirah, receives its influx of emanation, and Binah is the first of the organising, stabilising Sephiroth" (Ch. XVI.4). "Binah represents the female potency of the universe, even as Chokmah represents the male... Force and Form" (Ch. XVII.2). "Chokmah supplies the energy, and Binah supplies the machine" (Ch. XVII.27). "Chokmah without Binah, and Binah without Chokmah, are incomprehensible, for the pair are the functional unit, and not either of them separately" (Ch. XVII.59).

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune illustrates the pair with the analogy of petrol and an engine: "Chokmah explodes like petrol; Binah is the combustion-chamber" (Ch. XVII.26). She insists the symbolism (lingam/phallus for Chokmah, yoni for Binah) is "primarily a dynamic or positive symbol" and not merely sexual, extending to "dynamic force... wherever we see it" (Ch. XVI.10-11), and that this polarity underlies the alternating rhythms of "our individual lives, in our physiological rhythms, and in the history of the rise and fall of nations" (Ch. XVI.7-8).
