## Claim Statement
Fortune identifies three planes of unmanifestation behind Kether: Ain (Negativity), Ain Soph (the Limitless), and Ain Soph Aur (the Limitless Light) — "out of this last that Kether is concentrated." She quotes Mathers: "The limitless ocean of negative light does not proceed from a centre, for it is centreless, but it concentrates a centre, which is the number One of the manifested Sephiroth, Kether, the Crown." Kether is equated with Ehieh, "I am," the Self-Existing One, Pure Being, and is summed up in the seed-sentence: "Kether is the Malkuth of the Unmanifest."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 5, section 9: "The Qabalists recognise four planes of manifestation, and three planes of unmanifestation, or Negative Existence. The first of these is called AIN, Negativity; the second, AIN SOPH, the Limitless; the third, AIN SOPH AUR, the Limitless Light. It is out of this last that Kether is concentrated." Section 5: Kether's "name is Ehieh, translated in the Authorised Version of the Bible as 'I am,' or, more explicitly, the Self-Existing One, Pure Being." Section 7: "Kether is the Malkuth of the Unmanifest."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune presents Kether as the Qabalist's practical starting-point for analysis — the first point "within the reach of finite consciousness" — from which the three Veils are traced backward, rather than working forward from an unreachable Absolute.
