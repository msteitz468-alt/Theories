## Claim Statement
Fortune describes Daath, "Knowledge," as "a curious and mysterious third" Sephirah-like point "never represented in the glyph of the Tree," "said to be formed out of the conjunction of Chokmah and Binah and... situated astride the Abyss." She cites Crowley's view that Daath occupies "another dimension to the other Sephiroth," forming "the apex of a pyramid of which Kether, Chokmah, and Binah form the three basal angles." Unlike the other stations of the Tree, Daath has no assigned Deity-name, angelic host, or mundane symbol. Fortune's own gloss: "Daath presents the idea of realisation and consciousness."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 7, section 3: "But pendent to these two latter Sephiroth is a curious and mysterious third, which is never represented in the glyph of the Tree; this is the invisible Sephirah, Daath, Knowledge, and it is said to be formed out of the conjunction of Chokmah and Binah and is situated astride the Abyss. Crowley tells us that Daath is in another dimension to the other Sephiroth, and forms the apex of a pyramid of which Kether, Chokmah, and Binah form the three basal angles. To me, Daath presents the idea of realisation and consciousness." Section 15: Daath "never appears on the Tree, and to which no Deity-name or angelic host is assigned and which has no mundane symbol in planet or element."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune elaborates Daath at length via quotations from the Book of Concealed Mystery and the Greater Holy Assembly (in Mathers' translations), linking it to Yesod, Tiphareth, and the Middle Pillar as "the secret of both generation and regeneration."
