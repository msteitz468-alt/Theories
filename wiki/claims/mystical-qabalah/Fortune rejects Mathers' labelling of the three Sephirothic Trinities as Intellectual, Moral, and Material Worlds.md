## Claim Statement
Fortune explicitly disputes MacGregor Mathers' terminology for the Three Trinities of the Tree — Intellectual World (Kether, Chokmah, Binah), Moral World (Chesed, Geburah, Tiphareth), Material World (Netzach, Hod, Yesod) — agreeing only with "Moral World," which she equates with her own term "Ethical Triangle," and rejecting "Material World" for Netzach-Hod-Yesod since that term properly belongs to Malkuth alone; she proposes "Astral, or Magical World" instead.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter VIII: "Mathers calls the First Trinity of Kether, Chokmah, and Binah the Intellectual World... To my way of thinking, this terminology is misleading... With the use of the term Moral World for Chesed, Geburah, and Tiphareth I agree; it is identical with my term, Ethical Triangle; but with the term Material World for the Trinity of Netzach, Hod, and Yesod I emphatically disagree, for this term belongs exclusively to Malkuth. These three Sephiroth are not material but astral... I propose the term Astral, or Magical World."

## Contexts Within the Source
She also separately lists the Seven Palaces grouping (Three Supernals as First Palace, Yesod and Malkuth sharing the Seventh Palace) and the threefold Arik Anpin (Vast Countenance) / Zauir Anpin (Lesser Countenance, Microprosopos) / Bride-of-Microprosopos classification, and the Three Mother Letters (Aleph/Air, Mem/Water, Shin/Fire) presiding over triads of Sephiroth, as further non-competing classificatory systems that reveal "functional affiliations among the Sephiroth."
