## Claim Statement
Fortune teaches that as part of the "magical aspect" under which each Sephirah is studied, every Sephirah is assigned a virtue (its ideal quality, "the gift which it brings to evolution") and a vice (the result of that quality's overplus), and that these virtues and vices are derived directly from the character of the planet correlated with that Sephirah, opening a whole approach to astrology through ethical/psychological correspondence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XIII: "Each Sephirah has assigned to it a virtue, which represents its ideal aspect, the gift which it brings to evolution; and a vice which is the result of the overplus of its qualities. For instance, Geburah, Mars, has for its virtues energy and courage, and for its vices cruelty and destructiveness. The student of astrology will at once recognise that the virtues and vices attributed to the various Sephiroth are derived from the characteristics of the planets associated with them, and will find that in this correspondence a whole new line of approach to astrology is opened up."

## Contexts Within the Source
This virtue/vice pairing is presented as the third of three aspects under which a Sephirah must be studied — alongside its "spiritual experience" (the profound realisation constituting the initiatory grade of that Sephirah) and its correlated part of the body, which "must not be taken too literally" but understood as symbolizing a factor in consciousness (e.g. Geburah as "the strong right arm" really signifies "the dynamic will, the executive capacity, the destruction of the effete and unbalanced").
