## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that a systematic study of the individual Sephiroth requires classifying the symbols attached to each one under ten fixed headings: (1) the Title, given in Hebrew and English; (2) the Magical Image and associated Symbols; (3) the Situation on the Tree; (4) the Yetziratic Text from the Sepher Yetzirah; (5) Descriptive Titles from Rabbinical literature; (6) the Names of Power — God-Name (Atziluth), Archangel (Briah), Order of Angels (Yetzirah), and Mundane Chakra (Assiah); (7) the Virtue and Vice of that grade; (8) the Correspondence in Microcosm (in man); (9) the Four Suits of the Tarot; and (10) the Flashing Colours.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"It will be seen that the symbols assigned to each Sephirah are classified in regular order under certain headings. To understand the significance attached to these different sections... it is necessary to explain the method of classification in detail" (Ch. XIV.5). Fortune then details Sections 1 through 10 (Ch. XIV.6-21), explicitly naming: "SECTION 1. The Title," "SECTION 2. The Magical Image and the Symbols," "SECTION 3. The Situation on the Tree," "SECTION 4. The Yetziratic Text," "SECTION 5. Descriptive Titles," "SECTION 6. The Names of Power," "SECTION 7. The Virtues and Vices," "SECTION 8. Correspondence in Microcosm," "SECTION 9. The Four Suits of the Tarot Pack," and "SECTION 10. The Flashing Colours."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune frames these Tables of Correspondences as merely "a selection of the principal symbols and ideas associated with each Sephirah," with "no claim whatsoever to comprehensiveness" (Ch. XIV.2), drawn chiefly from Crowley's "777" and the Mathers manuscripts, themselves traced to Dr Dee, Sir Edward Kelly, Cornelius Agrippa, Raymond Lully, and Pietro de Abano among earlier writers, and to Knorr von Rosenroth, Wynn Westcott, Éliphas Lévi, Mrs Atwood, Mme Blavatsky, and others among the moderns (Ch. XIV.3-4).
