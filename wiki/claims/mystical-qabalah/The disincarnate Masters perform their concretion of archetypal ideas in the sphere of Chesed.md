## Claim Statement
Fortune teaches that the Masters — a class of beings distinguished from living adepts, and who alone properly bear the title because they are "free from the wheel of birth and death" — perceive telepathically the archetypal ideas conceived by the "Logoidal consciousness" of the Logos and, by realising their practical application, produce concrete images that bring these ideas down to the first plane of form, Briah; this work is performed in the sphere of Chesed.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
She writes: "A very important part of the work of the Masters is the concretion of the abstract ideas conceived by the Logoidal consciousness... it is taught in the esoteric traditions that the Masters, disincarnate consciousnesses disciplined by form but now formless, in their meditations upon the Godhead are able to perceive telepathically these archetypal ideas in the mind of God... produce concrete images in their own consciousness which serve to bring the abstract archetypal ideas down to the first of the planes of form, called by the Qabalists, Briah. This, then, is the work that the Masters perform in their special sphere, the sphere of the organising, upbuilding, constructive Chesed." She distinguishes this from the "Dark Masters," who perform a corresponding work in Geburah, and notes the point of contact between the Masters and their human disciples is the Sephirah Hod.

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune is careful to qualify the term "Master," insisting the grade of Adeptus Exemptus (assigned to Chesed) may be held by incarnate persons but that the title "Master" strictly applies only to those free of karma and the Wheel of reincarnation. She warns that mystics who lack "the keys of Chesed" become hallucinated by seeing the Masters reflected into Yesod, "the kingdom of phantasms and hallucinations," mistaking anthropomorphic images for the Masters' true, formless mode of contact.
