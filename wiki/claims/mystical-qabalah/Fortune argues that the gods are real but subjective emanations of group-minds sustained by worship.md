## Claim Statement
Fortune argues that "the gods are the creations of the created," made by the adoration of their worshippers rather than being emanations of Eheieh, the One and Eternal; that they are emanations of the group-minds of races, formed out of "mind-stuff" (Akasha, or the reflecting ether) that is free-moving on the astral plane; and that although imaginary in form, the forces they embody and channel are real and powerful.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXI, §13-16: "The gods are the creations of the created. They are made by the adoration of their worshippers... The gods are emanations of the group-minds of races; they are not emanations of Eheieh, the One and Eternal. Nevertheless, they are immensely powerful, because by means of their influence over the imaginations of their worshippers they link the microcosm with the macrocosm." She locates the "mind-stuff" (called Akasha by Blavatsky, the reflecting ether by Éliphas Lévi) as free-moving on the astral plane, with "Netzach represents the force aspect, and Hod the form aspect of this Akasha." §18: "as consciousness began to develop in the creatures of the Creator, it exercised its function... upon the astral mind-stuff... consequently, 'the thought of man made Gods to love and honour.'"

## Contexts Within the Source
Presented as the resolution to what Fortune calls the "Dweller on the Threshold" question — whether one believes in the gods — arguing for a middle position in which the gods are neither wholly subjective illusions nor independent persons, but real forces clothed in forms built by human worship.
