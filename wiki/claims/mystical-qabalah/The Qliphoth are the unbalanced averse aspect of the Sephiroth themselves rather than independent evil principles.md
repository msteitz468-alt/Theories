## Claim Statement
Fortune states that the Qliphoth "are not independent principles or factors in the cosmic scheme, but the unbalanced and destructive aspect of the Holy Stations themselves"; "there are, in fact, not two Trees, but one Tree, a Qliphah being the reverse of a coin of which the obverse is a Sephirah." Each Qliphah arose as an emanation of unbalanced force during a temporary, inevitable phase of disequilibrium in the evolution of its corresponding Sephirah (e.g. the uncompensated severity of Geburah, prior to the emanation of Tiphareth, formed the Qliphah of Geburah).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXVI, §6: "The Qliphoth are aptly termed the evil and averse Sephiroth, for they are not independent principles or factors in the cosmic scheme, but the unbalanced and destructive aspect of the Holy Stations themselves. There are, in fact, not two Trees, but one Tree, a Qliphah being the reverse of a coin of which the obverse is a Sephirah." §20-23: "each Qliphah arose primarily as an emanation of unbalanced force in the course of the evolution of the corresponding Sephirah... An overplus of the necessary energy of Mars (Geburah)... would be certain to occur during the period prior to the emanation of Tiphareth, the Redeemer... Now this uncompensated severity of Geburah gave us the jealous God of the Old Testament... It forms the Qliphah of Geburah."

## Contexts Within the Source
Introduces the chapter's justification for studying the Qliphoth despite their danger (citing Abramelin's system, which evokes both angelic and demoniac forces under controlled conditions), and grounds Fortune's broader claim that whoever works with a Sephirah's positive aspect must be equipped to deal with its negative, averse aspect.
