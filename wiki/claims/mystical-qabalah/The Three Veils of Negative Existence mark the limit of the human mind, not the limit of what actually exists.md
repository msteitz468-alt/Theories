## Claim Statement
Fortune explains that the Qabalists "draw a veil at a certain point in manifestation, not because there is nothing there, but because the mind, as such, must stop there." The Veils of Negative Existence are "philosophical conventions" corresponding "to human limitations, not to cosmic conditions," and different minds draw the veil at different points — the ignorant man at "an old man with a long white beard," the scientist at "the ether," the philosopher at "the Absolute," and the initiate furthest of all because he thinks in symbols.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 5, section 3: "For the purposes of their system, therefore, they draw a veil at a certain point in manifestation, not because there is nothing there, but because the mind, as such, must stop there... we can understand the nature of the cosmos if we are content to accept the Veils as philosophical conventions, and realise that they correspond to human limitations, not to cosmic conditions." The same section lists how "different minds go back different distances": the ignorant man, the scientist, the philosopher, and the initiate.

## Contexts Within the Source
This claim opens Fortune's treatment of Negative Existence and justifies the Qabalistic method of starting analysis from Kether (the first knowable point) and working backward toward the Veils, rather than starting from the unknowable Absolute and trying to reason forward.
