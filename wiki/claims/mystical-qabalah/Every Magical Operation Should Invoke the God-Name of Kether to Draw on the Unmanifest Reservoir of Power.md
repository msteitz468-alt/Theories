## Claim Statement
Fortune teaches that all magical operations must find their final unification in Eheieh, the God-Name of Kether in Atziluth, because Kether alone is the unlimited source of energy; drawing power from any lower, specialised sphere of nature instead "robs Peter to pay Paul" and forces the magician to pay in suffering, whereas power drawn from the Unmanifest through Kether adds to the resources of the universe without such reaction.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"Every operation, therefore, must have its final unification or resolution in Eheieh, the God-name of Kether in Atziluth" (Ch. XV.11). "In Kether alone is the pure source of all energy. The operations of the magician that aim at the concentrating of energy... must always start with Kether, because here we touch the upwelling force arising from the Great Unmanifest, the reservoir of limitless power" (Ch. XV.12). "If we draw power from any specialised sphere of nature, we are, as it were, robbing Peter to pay Paul... it has been held that the magician pays in suffering for what he wins by magical means... but if it starts in the Kether of Atziluth, he is drawing unmanifest force into manifestation... provided he keeps the forces in equilibrium, there need be no untoward reaction and no payment in suffering" (Ch. XV.12).

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune contrasts the adept who begins operations in the Kether of Atziluth, whom "Nature is with... instead of against," with the "uninstructed" occultist whose astral images become "like foreign bodies in the organism of the Celestial Man," provoking Nature to fight the magician "tooth and nail" (Ch. XV.14). She notes the Three Supernals (Kether, Chokmah, Binah) are beyond brain consciousness but remain "the essential basis of all magical calculations" (Ch. XV.13).
