## Claim Statement
In closing, Fortune states that "all that remains to us of ceremonial in the West is in the hands of the Church, the Masons, and the producers of cabaret," each effectual after its kind — the Church evoking love of God, Masonry love of man, and cabaret love of women — and that ceremonial, though "pure superstition" if viewed as a means of invoking the spirit of God, is "pure psychology" as a means of evoking the spirit of man, a lost art in the West worth reviving through the twin powers of concentration and visual imagination.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXVII, §5-7: "All that remains to us of ceremonial in the West is in the hands of the Church, the Masons, and the producers of cabaret. All three are effectual after their kind: the Church evoking love of God; Masonry evoking love of man; and cabaret evoking love of women. Viewed as a means of invoking the spirit of God, ceremonial is pure superstition; but viewed as a means of evoking the spirit of man, it is pure psychology, and that is how I view it. It is a lost art in the West, but an art that is well worth reviving... its practical application depends... upon the development of certain powers in the mind by careful and prolonged training, of which the first is the power of concentration, and the second the power of visual imagination."

## Contexts Within the Source
Closes the book, following Fortune's remark "The little done, the undone vast" and her announcement that the Twenty-two Paths and the "Spheres of the gods" upon the Tree remain for future study; she notes in passing her own Fraternity of the Inner Light's Guild of the Master Jesus as an instance of working ceremonial "with Power."
