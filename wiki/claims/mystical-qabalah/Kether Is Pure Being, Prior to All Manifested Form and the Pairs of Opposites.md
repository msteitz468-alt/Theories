## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that Kether, the First Sephirah, is a state of pure being without form, activity, or division into opposites — "a latency only one degree removed from non-existence" — and that the pairs of opposites (which characterize all manifested existence) only arise once Chokmah and Binah are emanated from it.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"In Kether there is no form but only pure being, whatever that may be. It is, one might say, a latency only one degree removed from non-existence... With manifested existence there come into being the pairs of opposites; but in Kether there is no division into the pairs of opposites, which must wait for their manifestation till Chokmah and Binah are emanated" (Ch. XV.2). "Kether, then, is the One, and existed before there was any reflection of itself to serve it for an image in consciousness and set up polarity... this state of existing substance must have been utterly inert, pure being without activity, until the activity began which emanated Chokmah" (Ch. XV.3).

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune warns that "we must not draw the Veils of Negative Existence in front of Kether or we shall condemn ourselves to a perpetual unresolved duality" (Ch. XV.4), and describes her own experience of "Rising on the Planes," in which Kether "appeared as a blinding white light, in which all thought went completely blank" (Ch. XV.1). She states "we cannot define Kether; we can only indicate it" (Ch. XV.4).
