## Claim Statement
Fortune teaches that "the universe is really a thought-form projected from the mind of God," and the Tree is "the symbolic representation of the raw material of the Divine consciousness and of the processes whereby the universe came into being" — but that the Tree "applies not only to the Macrocosm but to the Microcosm which, as all occultists realise, is a replica in miniature." This identity of pattern between man and cosmos is, in her account, the philosophical basis of divination, since a man's soul is subconsciously connected to the World-soul "like a lagoon connected with the sea by a submerged channel."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 3, section 14: "The universe is really a thought-form projected from the mind of God. The Qabalistic Tree might be likened to a dream-picture arising from the subconsciousness of God." Section 15: "But the Tree applies not only to the Macrocosm but to the Microcosm which, as all occultists realise, is a replica in miniature. It is for this reason that divination is possible... A man's soul is like a lagoon connected with the sea by a submerged channel."

## Contexts Within the Source
This claim grounds Fortune's later exposition of the System of Correspondences, and explains why she treats meditation upon a single symbol on the Tree as capable of yielding both cosmological and psychological insight simultaneously.
