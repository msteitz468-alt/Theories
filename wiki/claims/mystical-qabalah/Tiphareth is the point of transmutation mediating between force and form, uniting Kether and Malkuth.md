## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that Tiphareth has three defining keys: it is "the centre of equilibrium of the whole Tree," it is "Kether on a lower arc and Yesod on a higher arc," and it is "the point of transmutation between the planes of force and the planes of form" — functioning as a "two-way switch" that receives "the influxes of the Emanations" from the five higher Sephiroth and causes that influence "to flow into all the reservoirs of the blessings" of the four denser Sephiroth below.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
She writes: "There are three important keys to the nature of Tiphareth. Firstly, it is the centre of equilibrium of the whole Tree, being in the middle of the Central Pillar; secondly, it is Kether on a lower arc and Yesod on a higher arc; thirdly, it is the point of transmutation between the planes of force and the planes of form." She elaborates via the Yetziratic Text's description of Tiphareth as "the Mediating Intelligence": "we must consider it both as receiving the 'influxes of the Emanations' and as 'causing that influence to flow into all the reservoirs of the blessings.' ... If looked at from the side of form, it is force; if looked at from the side of force, it is form." She adds: "Macrocosmically... Tiphareth is the equilibrium of Chesed and Geburah; microcosmically... it is the point where the types of consciousness characteristic of Kether and Yesod are brought to a focus. Hod and Netzach equally find their synthesis in Tiphareth."

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune situates this mediating function within the doctrine of the "Second Triad" (Chesed-Geburah-Tiphareth) as the evolving individuality or "Higher Self, the Holy Guardian Angel," overshadowing the "Third Triad" of personality below, with Malkuth as physical vehicle. She notes that "exoteric religion goes no farther up the Tree than Tiphareth," since it lacks understanding of the mysteries of Kether, Chokmah, Binah, Geburah, Gedulah, and Daath.
