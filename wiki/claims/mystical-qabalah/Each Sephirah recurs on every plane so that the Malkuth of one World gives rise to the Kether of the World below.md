## Claim Statement
Fortune holds that the Ten Sephiroth are not confined to a single arc of manifestation but reappear complete in each of the Four Worlds. Just as Ain Soph Aur concentrated into the point Kether and emanated down to Malkuth, so the Malkuth of Atziluth gives rise to the Kether of Briah, the Malkuth of Briah to the Kether of Yetzirah, and the Malkuth of Yetzirah to the Kether of Assiah, whose own Malkuth, in its lowest aspect, abuts upon the Qliphoth.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter IX: "the Ten Holy Sephiroth are considered as appearing in each Qabalistic World upon another arc or level of manifestation; so that just as Ain Soph Aur... concentrated a point, which was Kether, and the emanations worked down through increasing grades of density to Malkuth, so the Malkuth of Atziluth is conceived of as giving rise to the Kether of Briah, and so on consecutively down the planes... and the Malkuth of Assiah, in its lowest aspect, abutting upon the Qliphoth."

## Contexts Within the Source
She specifies Atziluth as "the natural sphere of the Sephiroth as such," the World of Emanations where God acts directly; in Briah He acts through Archangels, in Yetzirah through Angelic Orders, and in Assiah through the "Mundane Chakras" — the planets, elements, and zodiac signs.
