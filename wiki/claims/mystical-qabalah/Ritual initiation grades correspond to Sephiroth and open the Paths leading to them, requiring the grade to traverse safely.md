## Claim Statement
Fortune states that the ritual initiations of the Greater Mysteries of the Western Esoteric Tradition are based on the Tree, with each grade corresponding to a Sephirah (worked from Malkuth upward) and conferring both the powers of that Sephirah's sphere and access to the Paths leading to it — so an initiate becomes, for example, "Lord of the Thirty-second Path" upon taking the Yesod grade. Without holding the corresponding grade, one is unknown to the Paths' guardians, who will be "inimical rather than helpful," and one lacks any means of countenance-checking visions encountered while astrally projecting along a Path.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter X: "The ritual initiations of the Greater Mysteries of the Western Esoteric Tradition are based upon the principles of the Tree of Life. Each grade corresponds to a Sephirah and confers... the powers of that sphere of nature. Likewise it opens up the Paths leading to that Sephirah, so that the initiate is said to be Lord of the Thirty-second Path when he has taken the initiation that corresponds to Yesod." And: "unless one has received the grade, one will be unknown to guardians of the Paths, and they will be inimical rather than helpful... there are plenty of beings in the lower sphere who are only too ready to take advantage of presumptuous ignorance."

## Contexts Within the Source
She allows that sincere meditation alone (without formal ritual initiation) can eventually win a Path's guardian's welcome — "he will then literally have initiated himself" — though this self-initiation process is described as slower than ceremonial initiation, "sure enough if persevered with by a suitable person."
