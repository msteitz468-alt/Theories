## Claim Statement
Fortune teaches that the Mysteries of the Crucifixion, assigned to Tiphareth as the sphere of the Sacrificed God, do not concern "the voluntary loss of something dear" but rather "the translation of force from one form to another" — a releasing of energy locked in static form into free circulation, by which incarnated gods are sacrificed so that the emotional force set free may redeem and re-equilibrate their Kingdom.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
She writes: "We shall not understand Tiphareth aright unless we have some concept of the real meaning of sacrifice, which is very different to the popular one, which conceives of it as the voluntary loss of something dear. Sacrifice is the translation of force from one form to another... Energy may be locked up in form, and therefore static; or it may be free from its bondage to form and in circulation. When we make a sacrifice of any sort, we take a static form of energy, and by breaking up the form that imprisons it, put it into free circulation in the cosmos." Of the incarnated gods she writes: "To this end are the incarnated gods sacrificed, dying for the people, in order that the tremendous emotional force set free by this act may compensate the unbalanced force of the Kingdom and thus redeem it or bring it into equilibrium." She notes all sacrificed gods of the pantheons (she names Osiris and Dionysos alongside the Christian Christ) are assigned to Tiphareth for this reason.

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune connects this doctrine to the two Magical Images of Tiphareth as Child and Sacrificed God, and to its two Spiritual Experiences, the Vision of the Harmony of Things and the Mysteries of the Crucifixion — stating it is "the only Sphere on the Tree" assigned more than one Spiritual Experience. She links the doctrine to her broader teaching that "at the Fall the four lower Sephiroth became detached from Tiphareth," so that redemption consists of the Redeemer in Tiphareth "for ever striving to redeem His Kingdom by re-uniting it to the Supernals across the gulf made by the Fall."
