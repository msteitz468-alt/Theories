## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that while the Three Supernals and the Chesed-Geburah-Tiphareth triangle are each functional triangles of latent and potent force, the lowest functional unit of the Tree — Netzach, Hod, Yesod, and Malkuth — is not a triangle but a quaternary, and that this quaternary "is said by the Qabalists to have been affected by the Fall," with the head of Leviathan rising out of the Abyss to a point between Yesod and Tiphareth.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXI, §4: "The lowest functional unit on the Tree of Life consists... not of a triangle, but of a quaternary, and this quaternary is said by the Qabalists to have been affected by the Fall, the head of Leviathan rising out of the Abyss to a point between Yesod and Tiphareth. Beyond this it was not permitted to go, and the six higher Sephiroth retained their innocency." She adds that the four lower Sephiroth "belong to the planes of form, wherein force is no longer free-moving, but 'cabined, cribbed, confined'; only to be freed by works of destruction."

## Contexts Within the Source
This claim opens Chapter XXI as the structural premise for treating Netzach, Hod, Yesod, and Malkuth together as "the planes of form," distinct from the six higher, unfallen Sephiroth, and frames Tiphareth's equilibrium as giving rise, on the descent, to increasing cohesion culminating in Malkuth.
