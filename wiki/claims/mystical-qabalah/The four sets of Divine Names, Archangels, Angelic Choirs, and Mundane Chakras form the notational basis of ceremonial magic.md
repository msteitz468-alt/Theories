## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that to each Sephirah in each of the Four Worlds is assigned a distinct name of power — a Divine Name in Atziluth, an Archangel in Briah, a choir of Angels in Yetzirah, and a Mundane Chakra in Assiah — and that this fourfold system of correspondence is the basis of ceremonial magic, talismanic magic, and the Tarot system of divination, alongside Crowley's four colour scales (King, Queen, Emperor, Empress) presided over by the four letters of Tetragrammaton (Yod, He, Vau, He).

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter IX: "In the Archetypal World of Atziluth there are assigned to the Ten Sephiroth ten forms of the Divine Name... In the world of Briah it is held that the mighty Archangels carry out the mandates of God... In Yetzirah it is the choirs of angels... In Assiah, as we have already noted, certain natural centres of force are given similar correspondences... We have, then, in these four sets of symbols a complete system of notation for expressing the mode of function of any given power at any given level, and this system of notation is the basis of ceremonial magic with its Names of Power, and also of talismanic magic and the Tarot system of divination."

## Contexts Within the Source
