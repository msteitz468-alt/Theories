## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that the Sephiroth denote states of being or types of functioning force rather than discrete things or places, so that any phenomenon exhibiting a given quality — whether an atom, a static institution, or a personal experience — can be classified to its corresponding Sephirah regardless of its literal subject matter.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
"It must be remembered that the Sephiroth are states, not places. Wherever there is a state of pure, unconditioned being, without parts or activities, it is referred to Kether... wherever we see pure energy functioning, we know that the underlying force is that of Chokmah" (Ch. XVII.4). "The stability thus achieved, which, be it noted, is a condition and not a thing in itself, is what Qabalists call Binah... For instance, the atom, being for all practical purposes the stable unit of the physical plane, is a manifestation of the Binah type of force. All social organisations on which the dead hand of unprogressiveness weighs heavily, such as the Chinese civilisation before the revolution, or our older universities, are said to be under the influence of Binah" (Ch. XVII.7).

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune connects this classificatory method to the subconscious mind's natural sorting process: "This is the method of the subconscious mind, which it pursues automatically; the occultist trains his conscious mind in the use of the same method" (Ch. XVII.4), and notes that initiatory schools require students to memorize the correspondences by heart rather than rely on reference tables, so that "the complex phenomena of objective existence sort themselves out in our understanding" (Ch. XVII.20).
