## Claim Statement
Fortune asserts that Chesed, being "the first Sephirah of Microprosopos, or the manifested universe, represents the formulation of the archetypal idea, the concretion of the abstract," and that "all the creative work of the world is done thus, by minds working in terms of Chesed."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
In Chapter XVIII she writes: "Chesed, being the first Sephirah of Microprosopos, or the manifested universe, represents the formulation of the archetypal idea, the concretion of the abstract. When the abstract principle that forms the root of some new activity is formulating in our minds, we are operating in the sphere of Chesed." She illustrates this with the example of an explorer who, looking over a newly discovered country, mentally maps out the wharfs, town, and railway before any brick is laid — "he is functioning in the sphere of Chesed whether he knows it or not." By contrast, minds that "cannot function above the level of Malkuth" and "think in terms of detail, lacking any synthetic principle" never reach this sphere.

## Contexts Within the Source
Fortune frames this doctrine in relation to Chesed's position on the Tree: it is Chokmah "upon a lower arc," emanated by Binah and emanating Geburah, and represents the point where "the type of activity comes within the range of human consciousness" for the first time as one descends the Tree from the Three Supernals. She also links it to the grade of Adeptus Exemptus and the work of the disincarnate Masters, who perceive archetypal ideas telepathically and translate them toward manifestation.
