## Claim Statement
Fortune directly criticizes Crowley's "777" for assigning pagan gods to the Paths as well as to the Sephiroth, calling it "a mistake and leads to confusion." She maintains that only the Sephiroth represent natural forces (objective), while the Paths represent states of consciousness (subjective); consequently the Paths should be worked only under the Holy Names of their Sephirothic attributions and the appropriate elemental rite/Tattva, not through the "inextricable tangle" of assorted deity correspondences Crowley assigns to them.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XII: "In the system he gives in his book of correspondences, '777,' Crowley assigns the gods to the Paths as well as to the Sephiroth. This, in my opinion, is a mistake and leads to confusion. It is the Sephiroth alone that represent natural forces; the Paths are states of consciousness. The Sephiroth are objective and the Paths are subjective... The Paths themselves, in my opinion, should be regarded as under the direct presidency of the Holy Names governing their Sephirothic attributions only, and should not be confused with other pantheons." She gives the Seventeenth Path (Tiphareth-Binah, assigned to Air by the Sepher Yetzirah) as an example, preferring the Air rite and Tattva over Crowley's "inextricable tangle" of Castor and Pollux, Janus, Apollo, and Mercury for that Path.

## Contexts Within the Source
She notes the working glyph used by initiates represents Sephiroth and Paths in two different Colour Scales specifically to preserve this objective/subjective distinction, and concludes "The Sephiroth should be interpreted macrocosmically, and the Paths microcosmically."
