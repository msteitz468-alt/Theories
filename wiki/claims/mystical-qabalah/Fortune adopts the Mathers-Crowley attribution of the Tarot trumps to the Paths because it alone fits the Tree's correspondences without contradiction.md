## Claim Statement
Fortune surveys three modern authorities on the Tarot's attribution to the Paths — Papus, A. E. Waite, and MacGregor Mathers' Golden Dawn manuscripts (published without authorization by [[Aleister Crowley]]) — and finds "all three are different." She concludes that because "Papus... does violence to many of the correspondences when placed upon the Tree, the final test of all systems, and as the Mathers-Crowley system fits admirably," the latter must be "the correct traditional order," and states she will adhere to it throughout the book. She also states she will use Crowley's published material generally to supplement points on which Mathers, Wynn Westcott, and Waite are silent.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter 4, section 12: "All three are different... Papus disagrees with both these writers in his method, but as his system does violence to many of the correspondences when placed upon the Tree, the final test of all systems, and as the Mathers-Crowley system fits admirably, I think we may justly conclude that the latter is the correct traditional order, and I propose to adhere to it in these pages." Section 10: "In these pages it is the system given by Crowley of which I shall avail myself to supplement the points upon which MacGregor Mathers, Wynn Westcott, and A. E. Waite... are silent."

## Contexts Within the Source
This claim appears amid Fortune's discussion of whether publishing the "Unwritten Qabalah" breaches any initiatory oath; she argues that correspondence-fit with the whole Tree is the empirical test by which the correct system can be recognized among competing published versions.
