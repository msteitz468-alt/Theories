## Claim Statement
Fortune identifies Yesod as the sphere of the "Ether of the Wise" — the Akasha or Astral Light, akin to but not identical with ectoplasm or the physicist's ether — which purifies and corrects the "representations" designed in Hod before they are bodied forth in Malkuth; it is "the receptacle of the emanations" of all the other Sephiroth and, considered by itself, "the Treasure House of Images," the Sphere of Maya (Illusion), corresponding in the microcosm to the psychologists' Unconscious.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence
Chapter XXIV, §4: "Yesod is the sphere of that peculiar substance, partaking of the nature of both mind and matter, which is called the Ether of the Wise, the Akasha, or the Astral Light." §13: "Netzach and Hod equilibriated in Yesod, which is conceived as receiving their emanations... it may aptly be called, in the words of Qabalists, the 'receptacle of the emanations'; and it is from Yesod that Malkuth receives the influx of the Divine forces." §14: "he enters Yesod, the Treasure House of Images, the sphere of Maya, Illusion... and corresponds in the microcosm to the Unconscious of the psychologists, filled with ancient and forgotten things."

## Contexts Within the Source
Presented as resolving the "two apparently incongruous sets of symbols" of Yesod (strength versus fluidic Moon-symbolism), and as the basis for Fortune's claim that "spirit works through mind, and mind works through the Ether," so that all magic and "miraculous" phenomena operate by manipulating the Ether within the limits of its nature.
