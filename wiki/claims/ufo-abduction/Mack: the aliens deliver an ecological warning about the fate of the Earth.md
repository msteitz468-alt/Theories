## Claim Statement

Mack reports that the information transmitted to experiencers is "concerned primarily with the fate of the earth in the wake of human destructiveness." Abductees are shown apocalyptic scenes — the planet ravaged by nuclear war, suffocated by pollution and toxic clouds, even cracked open — followed by "triage" visions of who will survive. The aliens appear genuinely puzzled by human destructiveness and seem to intervene to change human consciousness and humanity's relationship to the planet.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"The information that abductees receive is concerned primarily with the fate of the earth in the wake of human destructiveness. Scenes are shown of the planet wasted by nuclear war and especially of the earth's environment devastated by pollution and toxic clouds. Sara and Arthur, for example, were shown great black clouds or 'blobs' suffocating the earth's living systems." In Catherine's case "an entire conference room setting was displayed for her benefit … to create the right conditions for communicating with her about the state of the earth's living systems."

Speaking from the alien viewpoint, Paul said: "We don't understand why you choose destruction," and "An organism that gets to be at such a degree of destruction should flip back and learn upon itself." Sara reframed "environment" as "the entire context of life itself … the creative, life-affirming place of unconditional love. 'You are your environment,' she said." In Appendix A Mack notes the phenomenon "is occurring in the context of a planetary ecological crisis," corroborated by the Zulu healer Credo Mutwa and Zimbabwean schoolchildren who "spoke spontaneously … about the failure of our species to take proper care of the earth."

## Contexts Within the Source

The "reception of information" pillar of the synthesis and the bridge to transformation: the warning motivates the experiencers' changed lives in [[Mack: abduction is ultimately a spiritually transformative ego death and expansion of consciousness]]. Mack, who had long studied the psychology of the nuclear threat, treats it as one of the phenomenon's most significant features.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Mack concedes "It is not clear what to make of these images," that outsiders "may [view them] metaphorically" even where experiencers hold them "as literal and concrete," and — most pointedly — that "my focus upon growth and transformation might reflect a bias of mine," since clients "may know of my interest in such aspects of human psychology."
