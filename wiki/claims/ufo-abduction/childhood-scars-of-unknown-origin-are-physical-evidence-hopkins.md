## Claim Statement

Hopkins argues that unexplained scars — neat, deep, painless incisions whose origin neither the subject nor their parents can recall, often dating to early childhood — are **physical evidence** that the body bears of a hidden abduction. Where the rest of his evidence rests on hypnotically recovered memory, these "scars of unknown origin" (later popularized as "scoop marks") are tangible marks left when the abductors took skin, blood, or tissue samples.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Hopkins reports that several subjects bore scars "from incisions made years earlier when the subjects had been children," and that under hypnosis witnesses "describe in almost exactly the same words the equipment used to make these incisions." Three of the seven core subjects "were taken as seven-year-old children, and incisions were made in their bodies."

The pattern is built from specific cases. **Philip Osborne** had "a perfectly straight three-inch 'scar of unknown origin' at his hip whose origin no one could remember"; under hypnosis he recalled a "robot arm" that cut him "very cleanly, and something taken out, and then just put right back together." **Virginia Horton** bore a scar on her leg ("The only mark on my body," she said) from a childhood incident and later returned from a French family picnic with a blouse stained with "blood of unknown origin." A further subject, "Mary," carried a "still visible scar" from "a bloody, painless incision on the back of her calf." Hopkins stresses the wounds were deep yet barely bled and healed cleanly, implying a "healing technology," and that even the parents' memories of the "accidents" seem "tampered with."

## Contexts Within the Source

The physical-evidence counterpart to [["Missing time" marks a hidden UFO abduction recoverable by hypnotic regression]] and to the pattern of [[UFO abductions follow a consistent pattern amounting to a systematic research program]]: the recurring "sample-taking" (skin, blood, hair, nasal probes) is read as the purpose of the abductions, with the scar as its residue.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hopkins concedes the scars' link to abduction is itself established through hypnotic regression of subjects with no conscious memory of the wound's cause, and that "Mary's" case remains "in a kind of investigative limbo." He acknowledges the book lacks the "central" physical proof (no captured craft), so the scars function as corroborative rather than conclusive evidence.
