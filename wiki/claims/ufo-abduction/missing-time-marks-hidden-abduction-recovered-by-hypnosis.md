## Claim Statement

Hopkins argues that an unexplained gap of "missing time" (usually an hour or two) after a UFO encounter signals that the witness was abducted and the memory blocked — and that **regressive hypnosis** is "the most efficient method of unlocking the forgotten period" and recovering "the often harrowing account of what actually happened."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His composite case: a couple (and baby) on a night highway see a silent, erratically moving light; their car's electrical system fails; the object departs; "they are bewildered but relieved that things are normal again. What they are not aware of is that it is now two hours later" — those two hours spent aboard a landed UFO undergoing examination, "blocked from their conscious memories." Hopkins builds on the **Barney and Betty Hill** case (1961, recovered via hypnosis, published 1965) as the prototype, and works with psychologists such as Dr. Aphrodite Clamar; some witnesses were polygraph-tested.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hopkins concedes the reliance on hypnotically recovered memory, the absence of a "captured UFO" as final proof, and uncertainty over "what part of the experience may be purely psychic."
