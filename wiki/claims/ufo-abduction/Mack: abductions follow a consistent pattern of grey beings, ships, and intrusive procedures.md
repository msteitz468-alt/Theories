## Claim Statement

Mack reports a remarkably consistent abduction sequence across unconnected experiencers: an onset of unexplained light, hum, or vibration and paralysis; being "floated" through walls, windows, or the roof of a car along a beam of light; transfer into a metallic saucer- or cigar-shaped craft; and examination by small "grey" humanoids — three to four feet tall, with pear-shaped heads and huge black eyes — directed by a slightly taller "doctor" or leader, communicating telepathically. He treats this density of agreement, including details absent from the media, as itself a key form of evidence.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"What struck me initially … was the consistency of the stories told by individuals who had not been in communication with each other." After onset, "the abductee is commonly 'floated' … down the hall, through the wall or windows … experiencing only a slight vibratory sensation." The most common entity: "the small 'grays,' humanoid beings three to four feet in height … large, pear-shaped heads that protrude in the back, long arms with three or four long fingers … By far the most prominent features are huge, black eyes." The leader is "slightly taller … clearly in charge of the procedures," and "communication between the aliens and humans is experienced as telepathic, mind to mind."

In the synthesis (ch. 16) he restates the sequence as "so densely consistent as to defy conventional psychiatric explanations" — onset shift in consciousness, transport by a beam, the cold white-walled craft with "computer-like consoles," gazing/mind-scanning, and the medical-like procedures under the "doctor."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Mack acknowledges "small variations in what is experienced," cautions that he cannot prove media accounts contributed nothing to the consistency (though "abductees avoid media accounts … and are uniquely distressed by them"), and notes that his criterion for taking a report seriously is that it "was felt to be real by the experiencer" — "This does not mean that I have concluded that what the experiencer reports happened literally in our physical world."
