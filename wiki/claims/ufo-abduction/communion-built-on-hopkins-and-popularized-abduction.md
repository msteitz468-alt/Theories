## Claim Statement

Strieber's case is directly downstream of Budd Hopkins's abduction research: it was Hopkins (whose *Missing Time* had just appeared) who helped him recognize and investigate his experience and connected him to a sympathetic psychiatrist — and *Communion*, a runaway bestseller, in turn carried the abduction phenomenon and its imagery to a mass audience.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Strieber recounts that "the day before I met Budd Hopkins I almost jumped out a window," and credits Hopkins and psychiatrist Dr. Donald Klein with the "open-mindedness" that helped him "learn to live with uncertainty." He was startled to read Hopkins's description of the "archetypal abduction experience" and find his own memories matched it (the depression in the woods, the recalled "animal"). He notes the corroborating community of experiencers Hopkins had catalogued, and frames his book as adding a first-person voice to that investigative work.
