## Claim Statement

Hopkins argues the abduction accounts — though drawn from "a random cross-section of professions, ages, and social backgrounds" — share such consistent detail that they "constitute some kind of systematic 'research' program, with the human species as subject": people are involuntarily "borrowed," examined, sampled, and returned with memories erased.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The recurring elements include medical-style examination aboard the craft, deliberate sample-taking, and **incisions leaving scars** — "several abductees bear scars on their bodies from incisions made years earlier when the subjects had been children," and Hopkins reports witnesses "under hypnosis describe in almost exactly the same words the equipment used to make these incisions." He notes abductions often begin in childhood and recur, and that while the accounts contain "almost nothing… deliberately hurtful or malevolent," the overall pattern leaves him "thoroughly alarmed." He cites a corroborating multi-witness 1975 Catskill encounter (with Ted Bloecher).

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hopkins acknowledges the purposes are unknown ("we can only guess") and that the consistency rests on hypnotically recovered testimony rather than physical proof of the abductors.

## Contexts Within the Source

The substantive finding behind [["Missing time" marks a hidden UFO abduction recoverable by hypnotic regression]]; its "sample-taking/hybridization" implications anticipate the abduction-and-hybridization theme later taken up by Paul Wallis (*The Scars of Eden*) and the wider abduction literature (Strieber, Mack).
