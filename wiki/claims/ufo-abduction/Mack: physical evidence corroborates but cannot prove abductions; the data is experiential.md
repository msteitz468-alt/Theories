## Claim Statement

Mack holds that the abduction phenomenon's physical evidence — UFO sightings, burned earth, lesions, scoop marks, "implants," missing pregnancies, reported absences — is real but "subtle, elusive, and difficult to prove," sufficient only to corroborate the experiencers' accounts, never to constitute scientific proof. The primary data, he argues, is therefore experiential: rigorously evaluated subjective report, demanding a "science of subjective experience."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

"From my perspective, the physical evidence is important to corroborate the experiencers' reports. But if taken out of this context, the physical phenomena are rarely sufficiently robust to stand in their own right." Were he to publish photographs of skin lesions, dermatologists could rightly object "that I could not prove that they were directly related to the abduction experiences." Every category of evidence "is also … subtle, elusive, and difficult to prove."

"As personal reports are our principal source of knowledge of abductions, we must be especially rigorous in evaluating their authenticity, affective intensity, and consistency." He calls for "the development and application of a science of subjective experience," and grounds his confidence in the recovered emotion: "It is hard to imagine how the psyche could generate so intense a level of emotion without some kind of exposure to an extraordinary experience as the template for that emotion." His summary: "there is physical evidence but not proof."

## Contexts Within the Source

The evidentiary qualifier that conditions the entire book — the reason Mack insists ([[Mack: the alien abduction phenomenon is real and cannot be explained psychiatrically]]) on experiential rather than physical proof, and the answer to critics of his method ([[Mack works with experiencers as co-investigators using modified hypnosis and breathwork]]). It follows from the ontology of [[Mack: abduction is a "daimonic" reality that breaches the materialist worldview]], where direct external measurement "is not possible."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

This claim *is* Mack's concession: he was "faulted frequently by critics … for not dealing … more fully with the physical evidence," and acknowledges the whole edifice rests on hypnotically aided subjective report, with "more research … needed" on memory and hypnosis accuracy. Skeptics in the wider wiki treat the absence of robust physical proof as decisive against the phenomenon.
