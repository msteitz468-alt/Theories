## Claim Statement

Hopkins describes UFO abductions as constituting "an epidemic" — far more numerous than the relatively few investigated cases — because the memory-blocking, the fear of ridicule, and confusion about where to report leave the great majority of incidents invisible.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He cites the Bloecher–Webb **HUMCAT** catalogue (nearly three hundred probable abductees), plus uncatalogued cases investigated by Dr. James Harder and Dr. Leo Sprinkel (APRO), bringing estimates to "roughly five hundred" individual abductees — and stresses this is a floor, since "roughly seventy or eighty percent of these events go unreported," and many abductions involve more than one person. He explains the invisibility mechanism with his composite case: most witnesses never report even the sighting, and those who do are usually filed away by police rather than referred to investigators like the Center for UFO Studies.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Hopkins is explicit that "we have no idea how many such kidnappings may already have taken place," presenting the "epidemic" as an inference from catalogued cases and reporting rates, not a measured count.
