## Claim Statement

Good argues that official UFO secrecy is not a uniquely American affair but a worldwide one: governments across Britain, Europe, Australia, Canada, China, the USSR, and the USA have secretly taken UFOs seriously — devoting intelligence resources to them — while publicly denying any such interest. He holds this is demonstrable from the documentary record, not mere speculation.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Against Chapman Pincher's claim that UFOs are "entirely mythical" and the British government's 1979 assurance (Lord Strabolgi) that "there is no cover-up and no security ban," Good marshals FOIA-released records — e.g. General George C. Marshall's 1942 memo to President Roosevelt on the Los Angeles air raid; the 1946 State Department telegram on the Swedish "ghost rockets"; Churchill's 1952 minute ("What does all this stuff about flying saucers amount to?… Let me have a report"). He organizes the book by country to show the same pattern of official concern-plus-denial recurring globally, and cites the Canadian Wilbert Smith memo calling UFOs "the most highly classified subject in the United States Government, rating higher even than the H-bomb."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Good concedes 70–90% of sightings are misidentifications/hoaxes, stresses that an unexplained UFO "does not necessarily imply an extraterrestrial spacecraft," and reproduces the official denials in full before contesting them.
