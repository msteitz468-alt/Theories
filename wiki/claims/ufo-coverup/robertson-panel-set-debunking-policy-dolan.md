## Claim Statement

Dolan presents the **CIA-sponsored Robertson Panel** of January 1953 as the institutional pivot at which the U.S. adopted a deliberate policy of *managing public perception* of UFOs — keeping the subject's profile low, debunking sightings, and monitoring civilian UFO groups — rather than openly investigating it.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He traces the policy to the CIA's Office of Scientific Intelligence: H. Marshall Chadwell "strongly suggested the conclusions later arrived at by the Robertson Panel: to keep the profile of UFOs as low as possible while the real investigation and activity could continue. At all cost, mass hysteria and panic must be avoided." Dolan situates the Panel at the close of the 1952 crisis — after analysts like Fournet had concluded the objects showed intelligent control — as the mechanism that converted internal alarm into public debunking, and notes its recommendations bore on civilian organizations such as NICAP.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Dolan presents the Panel from the documentary record (CIA/OSI papers and participants' accounts); the inference that it institutionalized concealment is his reading of that record.
