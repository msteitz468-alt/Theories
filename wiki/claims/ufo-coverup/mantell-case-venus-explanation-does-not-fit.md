## Claim Statement

Keyhoe presents the death of Captain Thomas Mantell — a National Guard pilot who crashed and died in January 1948 while pursuing a large object near Godman Field, Kentucky — as a flagship case showing both a real, anomalous craft and Air Force obfuscation. He argues the official "Venus" explanation cannot account for what Mantell and Godman tower officers described.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Keyhoe lists the problems with the official account: "the obvious determination to fit some explanation, no matter how farfetched, to the Mantell sighting," and "the impossibility that Venus — a tiny point of light, seen only with difficulty — was the tremendous metallic object described by Mantell and seen by Godman Field officers." He notes Project "Saucer" "gave out a hint, but they've never released the transcript" of Mantell's radio report to the tower — treating the withheld transcript as part of the concealment.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Keyhoe acknowledges the case was officially explained (as Venus, and later as a Skyhook balloon in other accounts) and that the full radio transcript was unavailable to him; his case rests on witness descriptions versus the official explanation.
