## Claim Statement

Dolan argues the U.S. Air Force's later official explanations of the 1947 Roswell incident — that the debris was a **Project Mogul** balloon train and that "alien body" reports were misremembered 1950s **crash-test dummies** — do not fit the evidence, and reads them as further cover-up rather than resolution.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His point-by-point objections include: the Air Force never interviewed still-living 1947 witnesses; the dummy tests post-dated 1947 and no dummies fell near the crash site (a fact even an Air Force project officer affirmed); Mogul's "glue-treated balsa-wood sticks," rubber, and tinfoil do not match the tough, durable wreckage described by Marcel, Brazel, Blanchard, and others; a Mogul balloon was too small to cause the widespread debris field and ground scarring; the reports never explain why the army detained and interrogated rancher Mac Brazel for a week (corroborated by family, neighbors, and the base provost marshal); and other Mogul crashes drew no comparable security response.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Dolan lays out the Air Force's Mogul case (e.g., debris "consistent with" Mogul Flight #4; Brazel's June-vs-July timing) before rebutting it, treating the official explanation seriously rather than ignoring it.
