## Claim Statement

Dolan documents that during the intense 1952 wave, the Air Force's and Navy's own analysts concluded that UFOs moved under intelligent control and were probably extraterrestrial — a conclusion that was circulating at high levels just before the policy clamp-down of early 1953.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He recounts Air Force Maj. **Dewey Fournet**'s study of the "top fifty" reports, which "concluded that the movements of UFOs did show intelligent control and indicated they were probably extraterrestrial"; Fournet planned a press conference to "conclude that alien spacecraft were observing the Earth," but the report "wasn't official… because it was too hot." The U.S. **Navy**'s photographic lab judged the Tremonton, Utah film to show "unknown objects under intelligent control," with "an impressive amount of technical support." Dolan quotes an October 1952 FBI memo: "some military officials are seriously considering the possibility of interplanetary ships."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Dolan notes these conclusions were never officially adopted — "no one was willing to stick his neck out" — and that air intelligence officially still favored "optical illusions or atmospherical phenomena."
