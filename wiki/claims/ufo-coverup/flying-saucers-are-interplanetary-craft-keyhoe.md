## Claim Statement

Keyhoe's founding conclusion, as published in *True* (January 1950): "(1) The earth has been observed periodically by visitors from another planet. (2) This observation has increased markedly in the past two years." He argues the saucers are real interplanetary craft and that the only competing explanation — a secret terrestrial craft — would require "an almost incredible leap in technical progress" that the Air Force denies.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

Keyhoe reaches the interplanetary conclusion after a six-month investigation of ~100 sighting reports, drawing on pilots, "rocket experts, astronomers, [and] Air Force officials." He cites an Air Force ("Project Saucer") document's own analysis tying the visitors' arrival to atomic testing: "Since the acts of mankind most easily observed from a distance are A-bomb explosions, we should expect some relation… between the time of the A-bomb explosions [and] the time at which the space ships are seen," and notes an Air Force report stating "space travel outside the solar system is almost a certainty." He stresses the post-1949 rise in "authentic reports, which Air Force denials cannot disprove."

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Keyhoe presents the interplanetary thesis as the best of limited options rather than proof, explicitly naming the secret-earthly-craft alternative, and acknowledges *True* hedged by publishing "alternate conclusions."
