## Claim Statement

Keyhoe argues that the U.S. Air Force privately knows flying saucers are real (and likely interplanetary) but hides this behind "a curtain of Air Force secrecy" — issuing contradictory denials, attributing sightings to "mistakes or… hysteria," and possibly planting hoaxes "to hide the real answer." He reads the secrecy partly as a deliberate policy of cautiously preparing the public against panic.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

He cites his editor's belief that one explanation "was deliberately planted when they found we were checking"; that "even the Air Force statements and the Project 'Saucer' report contradict each other"; and that after *True*'s article the Pentagon, "apparently fearing a panic," dismissed reports — yet days later released a Project "Saucer" file conceding "it will never be possible to say with certainty that any individual did not see a space ship." He recounts intelligence officers quizzing airline pilots for hours and an officer privately admitting "they did" have "a good idea of what the saucers are."

## Contexts Within the Source

The founding articulation of the UFO cover-up thesis. It is the 1950 origin point of the documented history later assembled in [[The U.S. national security state ran a sustained UFO cover-up]] (Dolan), and concerns the same Project Sign/"Saucer" apparatus Hynek describes from the inside.
