## Claim Statement

Good argues that senior military and intelligence figures have privately acknowledged the reality of UFOs and of a cover-up, even as their governments publicly deny both — a gap between "official culture" and what insiders know.

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

His highest-ranking witness is **Admiral of the Fleet Lord Hill-Norton**, former Chief of the Defence Staff (1971–73), who "personally affirmed" to Good "that there has been a cover-up," pressed the Ministry of Defence in correspondence with the Defence Secretary, and (in the House of Lords UFO Group) demanded to know who decided that pre-1962 sighting reports were "of no defence interest." Good also cites first CIA director **Roscoe Hillenkoetter**'s public statement that "through official secrecy and ridicule, many citizens are led to believe the unknown flying objects are nonsense," and Captain Ruppelt's account (to Major Keyhoe) that General Vandenberg rejected the extraterrestrial "Estimate of the Situation" because "it would cause a stampede."
