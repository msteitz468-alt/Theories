## Claim Statement

Good presents the **Majestic 12 (MJ-12)** documents — describing a top-secret committee of twelve scientists and officials, allegedly created by President Truman in 1947 to manage recovered UFOs and the cover-up — and concludes that "the group did indeed exist, and the document seems authentic enough."

## Source Attribution and Direct Evidence

The alleged members he discusses include **Dr. Vannevar Bush** (wartime science chief, plausible head of the "small group"), first CIA director **Rear-Admiral Roscoe Hillenkoetter**, first DCI **Rear-Admiral Sidney Souers**, Defense Secretary James **Forrestal**, and General Hoyt **Vandenberg**. Good notes the CIA was established "on exactly the same day as MJ-12 – 18 September 1947," ties the group to Wilbert Smith's "rating higher even than the H-bomb" memo, and cites a 1980 Air Force Office of Special Investigations (AFOSI) document referencing "Project Aquarius" and restricted access to "mj twelve." He acknowledges, however, that all alleged members were deceased and his questions to a former CIA director and two ex-presidents went unanswered.

## Opposing Information Within the Source

Good's endorsement is qualified ("seems authentic enough"), and he records the absence of any official confirmation — the MJ-12 documents' authenticity has remained contested.
