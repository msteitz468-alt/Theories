## Phenomenon Overview

The Federal Reserve System is the central banking system of the United States, established on December 23, 1913, with the enactment of the Federal Reserve Act. It was created following a series of financial panics (most notably the Panic of 1907) to provide a stable and flexible monetary and financial system. Academically, it is framed as a quasi-governmental institution designed to manage inflation, maximize employment, and stabilize the banking sector. Within alternative history and conspiracy literature, however, the Federal Reserve is analyzed as a privately controlled banking cartel designed to establish a monopoly over credit, generate national debt, and serve the interests of international financiers.

* **Eustace Mullins (*Secrets of the Federal Reserve*):**  
  Asserts that the Federal Reserve was created by a secret syndicate of international bankers acting on behalf of the Rothschild financial house in London. Mullins argues that the 1910 secret meeting at Jekyll Island was organized by Senator Nelson Aldrich and Paul Warburg (a Rothschild agent) to draft the legislation under the guise of "reform." He contends that the Federal Reserve Bank of New York is privately owned by a small group of member banks that are ultimately controlled by European financial houses. According to Mullins, the primary function of the Fed is to convert national wealth into private profit, generate interest-bearing national debt, and finance major global wars. ### The Jekyll Island Banking Cartel & Debt Machine

* **G. Edward Griffin (*The Creature from Jekyll Island*):**  
  Frames the Federal Reserve as a classic corporate cartel formed in secret at Jekyll Island to eliminate free-market competition among major banks. According to Griffin, the Fed's primary purpose is to protect its member banks from failures, establish a monopoly on the creation of currency, and cartelize the banking industry under the seal of the federal government. He explains the Fed's operation via the "Mandrake Mechanism," through which the Fed creates money out of nothing by purchasing government debt, leading to systemic inflation that acts as a hidden tax on the public. He also shows that the central banking and industrial cartels built up by the Fed's credit expansion subsequently financed and assisted the rise of National Socialism in Germany.
