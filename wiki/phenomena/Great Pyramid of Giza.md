## Phenomenon Overview

The Great Pyramid of Giza (also known as the Pyramid of Khufu or Cheops) is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids on the Giza Plateau in Egypt. Constructing the monument involved an estimated 2.3 million stone blocks, weighing between 2 to 50 tons each. Academically, Egyptologists attribute the pyramid's construction to the Fourth Dynasty pharaoh Khufu (Cheops) around 2560 BC as a monumental tomb. However, its architectural precision, astronomical alignments, and mathematical proportions have made it a central focal point for alternative historical, technological, and metaphysical theories.
