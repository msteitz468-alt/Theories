## Identity and Nomenclature

The powerful New York corporate law firm (48 Wall Street) where John Foster Dulles was managing partner and Allen Dulles a senior partner.

## Primary Textual Appearances

Talbot describes the firm at the center of an international network rebuilding Germany after WWI, structuring U.S. investment into German cartels like IG Farben and Krupp, and representing oil clients (Standard Oil) and the [[Bank for International Settlements]].

## Source-Specific Portraits

For Talbot the firm embodies the merger of Wall Street and U.S. foreign policy: its clients' interests recur as motives across Dulles's career (the Nazi-collaboration cases, the Iran coup, the targeting of Jerry Voorhis).
