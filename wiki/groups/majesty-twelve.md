## Identity and Nomenclature

Cooper asserts that Majesty Twelve (also written MAJESTY TWELVE) is "the secret group charged with oversight and control of the Alien Question." He states it has been known by different names across administrations to conceal its continuity:

- Under Eisenhower and Kennedy: the 5412 Committee, or more correctly, the Special Group
- Under Johnson: the 303 Committee
- Under Nixon, Ford, and Carter: the 40 Committee
- Under Reagan: the PI-40 Committee

Cooper states that "over all those years only the name changed."

## Origins

Cooper claims that after the Roswell crash recovery and subsequent alien contacts, President Eisenhower and Nelson Rockefeller began planning the secret structure for alien-task supervision. According to Cooper, "The idea for MJ-12 was thus born." He writes that the study panel created by Truman was not itself called MJ-12, but that MJ-12 emerged only four years after that initial study.

Cooper also cites a "Top Secret/MAJIC" presidential briefing document by Majesty Twelve, which he states he saw while in Naval Intelligence. He further references a document titled "Operation Majority" (Final Release) as his source.

## Membership

Cooper names the original nineteen members as:

- Nelson Rockefeller
- Director of Central Intelligence Allen Welsh Dulles
- Secretary of State John Foster Dulles
- Secretary of Defense Charles E. Wilson
- Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Admiral Arthur W. Radford
- Director of the FBI J. Edgar Hoover
- Six men from the executive committee of the Council on Foreign Relations known as the "Wise Men"
- Six men from the executive committee of the JASON Group
- Dr. Edward Teller

Cooper names the most important of the Wise Men as John McCloy, Robert Lovett, Averell Harriman, Charles Bohlen, George Kennan, and Dean Acheson. He also names Gordon Dean, George Bush, and Zbigniew Brzezinski as later members of Majesty Twelve.

## Voting Rules

Cooper states: "The first rule of Majesty Twelve was that no order could be given and no action could be taken without a majority vote of twelve in favor, thus Majority Twelve. Orders issued by Majesty Twelve became known as Majority Twelve directives."

## Study Group and the Country Club

Cooper describes a 35-member Council on Foreign Relations secret study group convened under Majesty Twelve's direction. The study group met initially at Quantico Marine Base (the "Quantico meetings") beginning in 1954, then at a second phase called "Quantico II." Nelson Rockefeller built a retreat in Maryland — accessible only by air and known by the code name "the Country Club" — for Majesty Twelve and the study committee to meet away from public scrutiny.

The study group was chaired by Gordon Dean. Dr. Henry Kissinger served as study director for the second 18-month phase beginning in November 1955; he subsequently wrote the publicly released study results as *Nuclear Weapons and Foreign Policy* (1957), published for the CFR by Harper & Brothers.

Cooper states a major finding was that the public could not be told about the alien presence because it would lead to economic collapse, collapse of religious structures, and national panic. A corollary finding was that if the public could not be told, Congress could not be told, and funding would have to come from outside the government — from the military budget and CIA nonappropriated funds.

## Alien Treaty and Subsequent Events

Cooper claims that by 1955 it became obvious that the aliens had broken the treaty they had signed with the Eisenhower administration: mutilated humans and animals were being found, and the aliens were not submitting a complete list of abductees. Cooper also states the aliens told Majesty Twelve they had been manipulating human populations through secret societies, witchcraft, magic, the occult, and religion.

After combat engagements with alien craft showed that U.S. weapons were ineffective, Cooper asserts that Majesty Twelve decided to continue friendly relations rather than risk open conflict.

## Kennedy and the Drug Operation

Cooper claims that at some point President Kennedy discovered that Majesty Twelve was running a drug operation through the CIA. Kennedy issued an ultimatum in 1963, assuring Majesty Twelve that if they did not clean up the drug problem he would, and that he intended to reveal the alien presence to the American people within the following year.

Cooper asserts that "President Kennedy's assassination was ordered by the Policy Committee [of the Bilderberg Group] and the order was carried out by agents in Dallas." He states that Majesty Twelve subsequently had a "different agenda" from that of the elected government.

## Control Over Presidents

Cooper asserts that "Majesty Twelve has presented most new Presidents with a picture of a alien threat from outer space and the need for a united world government to defend the Earth." He also states that "Presidents were told only what Majesty Twelve and the intelligence community wanted them to know."

## Primary Textual Appearances

Majesty Twelve is the central subject of Chapter Twelve ("The Secret Government") and appears throughout Cooper's book. Cooper cites Top Secret/MAJIC documents he claims to have seen while serving in Naval Intelligence, as well as his own earlier paper "Operation Majority."
