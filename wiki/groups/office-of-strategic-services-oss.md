## Identity and Nomenclature

The Office of Strategic Services, founded by William "Wild Bill" Donovan, was the United States' WWII intelligence agency and the institutional ancestor of the [[Central Intelligence Agency]].

## Primary Textual Appearances

Talbot's Part I centers on Dulles's OSS Bern station (23 Herrengasse), from which he ran agents (including Carl Jung as "Agent 488" and [[Mary Bancroft]]) and conducted unauthorized peace contacts with the Nazis.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Talbot frames the OSS as the seedbed of the Dulles network — many later CIA leaders (Helms, Wisner, Kim Roosevelt, Angleton) were OSS veterans who gathered around Dulles after the war.
