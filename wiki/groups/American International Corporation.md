## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

Per Sutton, the American International Corporation (AIC) — controlled by the J. P. Morgan firm and headquartered at 120 Broadway — was "the vehicle for this pro-Bolshevik activity." Sutton notes that AIC's views on the Bolshevik regime "were requested by Secretary of State Robert Lansing only a few weeks after the revolution began," and that AIC's executive secretary, William Franklin Sands, "could barely restrain his enthusiasm for the Bolshevik cause."

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Owned Amsinck & Co., whose senior partner Von Pavenstedt was "the chief German espionage paymaster in the U.S." (a thread Sutton traces in connection with Trotsky's funding).
- Sands intervened with the State Department on behalf of [[John Reed]].
- The executive heart of General Electric and the Guggenheims (also interested in AIC) were at 120 Broadway.

## Source-Specific Portrait

Sutton presents AIC as the Morgan-controlled corporate instrument at the center of the 120 Broadway network.
