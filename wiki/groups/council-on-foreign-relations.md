## Identity and Nomenclature

The New York-based private membership organization of bankers, lawyers, officials, and media figures. Allen Dulles was elected its president in 1945; John Foster Dulles and David Rockefeller were leading figures.

## Primary Textual Appearances

Talbot calls the CFR a key arena where the "power elite" forged consensus; he says the Guatemala coup was set in motion after a CFR study group urged action against Arbenz — "If CFR was the power elite's brain, the CIA was its black-gloved fist."

## Source-Specific Portraits

The CFR is, in Talbot's framing, an institutional face of the "deep state" that backed Dulles and remained suspicious of Kennedy.
