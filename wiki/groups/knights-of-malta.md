## Identity and Overview

Cooper describes the Knights of Malta as "a world organization with its threads weaving through business, banking, politics, the CIA, other intelligence organizations, P2, religion, education, law, military, think tanks, foundations, the United States Information Agency, the United Nations, and numerous other organizations." He states they are "not the oldest but are one of the oldest branches of the Order of the Quest in existence."

## Governance and Oath

Cooper states that the world head of the Knights of Malta is elected for a life term, with the approval of the Pope. He writes that the Knights of Malta have their own Constitution and are sworn to work toward the establishment of a New World Order with the Pope at its head. Cooper further asserts that Knights of Malta members are "also powerful members of the CFR and the Trilateral Commission."

Cooper emphasizes a conflict of interest: "Membership in the Knights of Malta entails obedience to one's superior in THE ORDER and ultimately to the Pope. Therefore, a U.S. ambassador who is also a member of the Knights of Malta faces a conflict of interest."

## Diplomatic Privileges

Cooper claims that "the Knights of Malta ALL have diplomatic immunity. They can ship goods across borders without paying duty or undergoing customs check."

## Membership Base

Cooper states the total membership is approximately 10,000, and that "nearly half of the 10,000 members belong to Europe's oldest and most powerful families." He identifies this as the mechanism that "cements the alliance between the Vatican and the 'Black Nobility.'"

## Named Members (Per Cooper)

Cooper names the following individuals as Knights of Malta or Dames of Malta:

- **William Casey** — Director of the CIA, head of Reagan's presidential campaign, and former head of the Export-Import Bank under Nixon. Cooper states Casey arranged financing for the Kama River truck factory in the Soviet Union and asserts "I believe Casey was murdered."
- **John McCone** — President Kennedy's envoy and Director of the CIA during the early 1960s. Cooper states he was also a Knight of Malta.
- **William Wilson** — The first U.S. Ambassador to the Vatican. Cooper asserts his appointment was "probably illegal and, for a fact, was highly unethical" because Wilson's allegiance was sworn to the Pope. Cooper notes Wilson took an unauthorized trip to Libya to meet with Libyan officials when travel there was banned.
- **Frank Shakespeare** — Replaced Wilson as U.S. Ambassador to the Vatican; also described as a Knight of Malta.
- **Clare Booth Luce** — Described as a Dame of the Knights of Malta and director of the Pope John Paul II Center for Prayer and Study for Peace.
- **J. Peter Grace** — Of W.R. Grace Company; described as head of the Knights of Malta in the United States and director of the Pope John Paul II Center.
- **Thomas Melledy** — Appointed by President Bush as U.S. Ambassador to the Vatican; described as a Knight of Malta.

Cooper also notes that President Reagan spoke at the annual Knights of Malta dinner.

Cooper further states that the U.S. Treasurer of the Knights of Malta was named in congressional hearings related to the 1930s plot (an apparent reference to the Business Plot allegations). He cites an article by Leslie Geld connecting various ties to the Order.

## Connection to P2 and the Vatican

Cooper writes that "P2 is directly connected to the Vatican, the Knights of Malta" and other organizations. He cites the infiltration of the Vatican by Freemasons as context for how all these organizations interconnect.

## Primary Textual Appearances

The Knights of Malta are discussed extensively in Chapter Two ("Secret Societies and the New World Order") of Cooper's book, particularly in the context of the Vatican's role in the New World Order and the interlocking membership of intelligence and diplomatic officials.
