**Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)**

Sitchin takes the biblical *Nefilim* (Genesis 6, "the sons of the gods" who took human wives) literally as **"Those Who Were Cast Down" — i.e. those who descended to Earth from the heavens** — rejecting the conventional gloss "giants." He identifies them with the Sumerian/Akkadian gods (the **DIN.GIR**) and with the **Anunnaki** — which he parses as *AN.UNNAK.KI*, "those who from Heaven to Earth came." In his reading the Anunnaki are specifically the working rank-and-file of the Nefilim stationed on Earth.

**Source-Specific Portrait**

Per Sitchin the Nefilim are **flesh-and-blood astronauts** from [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]] — biological humanoids, anthropomorphic "in all other respects," whose winged depictions are decorative attachments to flight-gear rather than natural wings. He reads the statue of "Inanna/Ishtar with a vase" (Mari) as a goddess in an **aeronaut's helmet, earphones, goggles and instrument pack**, and the SHU.GAR.RA helmet as "that which makes go far into universe." Their "immortality" is the product of the Twelfth Planet's vast (3,600-Earth-year) orbital "year." They arrived to mine gold ([[The Nefilim came to Earth about 445,000 years ago to mine gold]]), later created man to do their labour after the [[Event: The Mutiny of the Anunnaki]], and ultimately are "crowded off" the Earth by the multiplying humans they made.

This portrait is **distinct from later reworkings**: Sitchin's Nefilim are not reptilian or shape-shifting. Contrast [[Anunnaki (Icke)]].
