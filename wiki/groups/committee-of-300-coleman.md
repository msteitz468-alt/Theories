**Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)**

The Committee of 300 is John Coleman's signature concept — defined at length in his 1992 book [[Conspirators' Hierarchy: The Story of the Committee of 300 (Coleman)]] and reapplied in *The Club of Rome* (2008): the supreme **hidden world-government body** (which Coleman says calls itself "the Olympians") that he claims sits atop all the secret societies and controls global events, with roots in the British East India Company. In *The Club of Rome* it is the entity on whose "orders" the Club of Rome was set up, "the most powerful controlling body in the world." See the foundational claim [[The Committee of 300 ("the Olympians") is the supreme hidden world government]].

**Source-Specific Portrait**

Coleman presents the Committee of 300 as the apex of a pyramid of occult secret societies ("Luciferianism, Black Magic and Voodoo"), drawing in the [[Black Nobility (Coleman)]], the Wall Street and City of London financial powers, the Illuminati, and the Fabian/Socialist networks. The [[Club of Rome (Coleman account)]] functions as its "international foreign policy arm" and "executor." Coleman attributes to it the long-range plan to dissolve the nation-state, deindustrialize the West, and cull the world's population — the "occult theocracy future planned for the world."
