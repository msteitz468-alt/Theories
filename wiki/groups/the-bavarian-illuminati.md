## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

The secret society founded by [[Adam Weishaupt]] at Ingolstadt on May 1, 1776 — first "The Order of Perfectibilists," renamed the Order of the Bavarian Illuminati in 1778. "Illuminati" means "the enlightened ones." Marrs treats it as the first modern public coalescence of a much older esoteric tradition.

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Origins/Germany: founding, Weishaupt and Zwack ("Cato"), the recruitment of Knigge, fusion with the Strict Observance Masons at the 1782 Congress of Wilhelmsbad, growth to ~3,000 members, and suppression by Karl Theodor's 1784 edict.
- Methodology: the pyramidal cell structure, the graded degrees (Nursery, Masonry, Mysteries), and the espionage/deception ethic.

## Source-Specific Portrait

Marrs presents the order as drawing its structure from the Jesuits and Freemasonry, its symbols (Owl of Minerva, the dot-in-circle, the pyramid) from the ancient Mysteries, and its goal — abolition of monarchy and state religion — as either genuine Enlightenment idealism or a cover for elite world control, depending on the source. Though suppressed, its *doctrine* (Illuminism) is held to survive worldwide.
