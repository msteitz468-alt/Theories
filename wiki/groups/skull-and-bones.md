## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

The secretive Yale University order founded by William Russell in 1832, said to have been "brought from Germany." Marrs raises it in the Introduction — "Could it be that Skull and Bones is a modern incarnation of the infamous Bavarian Illuminati?" — citing its core of ~25 "liberal Eastern Establishment" families (Harriman, Rockefeller, Whitney, Bundy, Bush, etc.) and reputed initiation rites (lying naked in a coffin while disclosing intimate sexual histories).

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Introduction and Modern Illuminati: as an elite, German-derived society practicing the intimate-disclosure ritual Marrs traces to the Illuminati Novice's confessional reports.

## Source-Specific Portrait

Presented as one of the modern channels of Illuminism among the bloodline/elite societies (alongside the CFR, Bilderberg, Bohemian Grove, Knights of Malta). A New Orleans Mardi Gras "Mystick Krewe of Comus" is alleged by one whistleblower to have been a Skull and Bones chapter.

## Antony Sutton's Account (America's Secret Establishment)

Sutton treats Skull & Bones as a genuinely documented secret society (working from a leaked membership list) rather than a symbol — "Chapter 322," a chapter of a German order, guided by Hegelian philosophy. On his account The Order manipulates society through the dialectic of *managed conflict*, financing both the Soviet Union and the Nazis, and importing the Prussian education system to "dumb down" America. Crucially, Sutton frames this as statist-philosophical and class power, **not** an ethnic conspiracy — a deliberate contrast with Webster.
