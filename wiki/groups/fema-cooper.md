## Overview

Cooper devotes an entire chapter (Chapter Six, "H.R. 4079 and FEMA") to what he describes as FEMA's role as a "tool that can be used to establish the Police State." The chapter consists primarily of a transcript of an audiotape Cooper dictated in early 1990, recorded by an associate named Richard Murray.

## Origins

Cooper claims that in 1978, Harvard professor Samuel P. Huntington drafted Presidential Memorandum 32 for President Jimmy Carter, which led to the creation of FEMA in 1979. Cooper notes that Huntington also wrote "the 'Seminal Peace'" for the Trilateral Commission in the mid-1970s, recommending that "democracy and economic development be discarded as outdated ideas."

Cooper states that Huntington's ideas were rewritten into National Security Decision Directive 47, enacted by President Reagan on July 22, 1982, which he says "laid the groundwork for the secret government's options to institute a police state" under the title "Emergency Mobilization Preparedness."

## Mechanism for Takeover

Cooper asserts that all that is needed for FEMA to implement total control is for the President to declare a national emergency of any type. He lists the Executive Orders that would then take effect:

- **EO 10995** — takeover of all communications media
- **EO 10997** — takeover of all electric power, petroleum, gas, fuels, and minerals
- **EO 10988** — takeover of food resources and farms
- **EO 10999** — takeover of all modes of transportation, control of highways and seaports
- **EO 11000** — mobilization of all civilians into work brigades under government supervision
- **EO 11001** — government takeover of all health, education, and welfare functions
- **EO 11002** — Postmaster General to operate national registration of all persons
- **EO 11003** — government takeover of airports and aircraft
- **EO 11004** — Housing and Finance Authority to relocate communities and designate areas to be abandoned
- **EO 11005** — government takeover of railroads, inland waterways, and public storage facilities

Cooper states that all of the above were combined under Nixon into a single large executive order, and that this combined authority was signed into law by President Carter on July 20, 1979, as Executive Order 11490.

## Transfer of Presidential Power to FEMA

Cooper asserts that President Bush issued an executive order on March 24 delegating to the Director of FEMA "powers which were vested in the President by the Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act of 1988." Cooper states this meant the FEMA Director, not the President, would have direct responsibility for General Federal Assistance, Federal Emergency Assistance, Hazard Mitigation, Individual and Family Grant Programs, and "the power to direct other federal agencies to assist in an emergency." He emphasizes: "AND THAT'S THE KEY. All other federal agencies will come under FEMA."

## REX-84A

Cooper references the April 1984 test of FEMA capabilities, called REX-84A, which he states "was designed to test the readiness of the U.S. civilian and military agencies to respond to a serious national security crisis."

## H.R. 4079 and the Drug Emergency Pretext

Cooper warns that if H.R. 4079 were passed, the President could declare a national emergency over the drug situation, which would trigger FEMA's emergency powers regardless of duration — "whether it's for one year, five years, five minutes or forever, it doesn't make any difference." He connects this to Oliver North's statement during the Iran-Contra hearings that they had been "prepared to suspend the Constitution."

## FEMA Leadership

Cooper identifies Maj. Gen. Calvin Franklin as the head of FEMA at the time of writing and notes he had been the Commanding General of the District of Columbia National Guard. Cooper argues the National Guard is unsuited to drug interdiction but effective at controlling mass civil disorders.

Cooper also states that a member named Buster Horton was part of FEMA and "a member of the interdepartmental unit which is empowered in the event of a national security emergency to become the unelected national government, a sort of FEMA secret government."

## FEMA Advisory Board

Cooper states the FEMA Advisory Board is "dominated by Prof. Samuel P. Huntington." He notes that Huntington was a Harvard professor and the author of key documents that led to FEMA's creation, as well as the anti-democratic "Seminal Peace" paper for the Trilateral Commission.

## Concentration Camp Program

Cooper references a "concentration camp program of the Department of Defense" and relates FEMA's emergency powers to emergency detention capabilities already built into the system.

## Primary Textual Appearances

FEMA is the subject of Chapter Six in Cooper's book. The chapter presents itself as an urgent public warning about the constitutional dangers of FEMA's emergency powers framework.
