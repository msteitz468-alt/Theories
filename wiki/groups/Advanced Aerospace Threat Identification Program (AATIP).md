## Identity and Nomenclature

AATIP (Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program) was the classified Pentagon program that succeeded AAWSAP under the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence (OUSD(I)). It was directed by Luis Elizondo from approximately 2009 through his resignation on October 4, 2017. The program operated from Pentagon office 3C503A. The name "AATIP" and its predecessor "AAWSAP" were not publicly known until the December 2017 New York Times story.

Jay Stratton coined the term "UAP" (unidentified aerial phenomena) as a deliberate replacement for "UFO" to reduce institutional stigma within DoD analytical culture.

## Primary Textual Appearances

AATIP is the central institutional subject of the entire memoir. Its analytical work produced: the Five Observables framework; case files on the Nimitz (2004) and Roosevelt (2014–2015) encounters; documentation of international UAP cases including Colares (1977–1978); biological effects case histories; and an interface with classified Legacy Program holdings and contractor-held materials.

## Source-Specific Portrait

Elizondo portrays AATIP as operating with legitimacy and rigor within normal DoD intelligence structures — SCIF facilities, formal clearance protocols, structured briefing processes — while facing persistent institutional resistance from officials who feared the stigma associated with the UAP topic. The program was starved of resources and support despite producing significant findings, until Elizondo concluded that resignation and public disclosure was the only path to action.
