## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

Mullins characterizes Kuhn, Loeb & Co. as the New York banking house where [[Paul Warburg]] was a partner at $500,000 a year, and as the American representative of the European Rothschild interests. The source states that "Liberal amounts of Rothschild funds had enabled Jacob Schiff to purchase a partnership in Kuhn, Loeb Company," and quotes Senator Robert L. Owen defining the firm as "the representatives of the European Rothschilds in New York."

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Financed (with Cleveland H. Dodge's National City Bank, Schiff, Morgenthau, Baruch, Untermyer) Woodrow Wilson's campaign.
- Allied with J. P. Morgan interests via the Northern Securities Company (1901), which the source calls "the consolidation of the Rothschild Empire in America."
- Placed numerous partners and employees in the WWI Treasury (Jerome Hanauer as Assistant Secretary for Liberty Loans; McAdoo; Gilbert and Leffingwell).
- Owned, per the source, most US and Mexican railway mileage.

## Source-Specific Portrait

The firm is presented as the institutional vehicle through which Rothschild influence and Paul Warburg's authorship of the Federal Reserve Act were exercised, and as a principal beneficiary of WWI war finance.
