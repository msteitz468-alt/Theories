## Overview from Source

Cooper discusses the Rockefeller family as a major power center in his account of the secret government. He names Nelson Rockefeller as the most significant individual family member in connection with the alien question and Majesty Twelve.

## Power and Limitations

Cooper asserts that "The Rockefeller family was, is and always will be the benefactor of both organizations" — meaning the Council on Foreign Relations and the Trilateral Commission. However, he carefully states that "Rockefeller, though powerful, is not in control in this country or anywhere else." The evidence he offers for this limitation is that "Rockefeller had to put out feelers at a Bilderberg Group meeting in 1972 about forming a private group of trilateral leaders" — meaning Rockefeller required approval from the Bilderberg Group before founding the Trilateral Commission. "The Bilderberg Group gave the nod and Rockefeller's man Zbigniew Brzezinski gathered up a membership and organized the Trilateral Commission in 1972."

## The Trilateral Commission

Cooper states that the Trilateral Commission was the idea of its founder, "American banking magnate David Rockefeller," and that "the real reason for its formation was the decline of the Council on Foreign Relation's power as a result of the people's dissatisfaction with the Vietnam War." He describes it as "entering two horses in the same race" to double the chances of winning — with real power still residing in the CFR.

## Nelson Rockefeller and Eisenhower

Cooper states that "it was Nelson's uncle Winthrop Aldrich who had been crucial in convincing Eisenhower to run for President." He writes: "The whole Rockefeller family and with them, the Rockefeller empire, had solidly backed Ike. Eisenhower belonged heart and soul to the Council on Foreign Relations and the Rockefeller family."

Within one week of Eisenhower's election, Cooper asserts, Eisenhower had appointed Nelson Rockefeller chairman of a Presidential Advisory Committee on Government Organization. Rockefeller was "responsible for planning the reorganization of the government, something he had dreamed of for many years." When Congress approved a new Cabinet position in April 1953, Nelson was named Undersecretary to Oveta Culp Hobby.

Cooper writes that "Asking Rockefeller for help with the alien problem was to be the biggest mistake Eisenhower ever made for the future of the United States and maybe for humanity."

## Membership in Majesty Twelve

Cooper names Nelson Rockefeller as the first-named member of Majesty Twelve. He also states that Nelson Rockefeller was given important posts in connection with alien-task oversight: one directly and solely reporting to the President, and another as "the head" of a coordinating group whose members included a representative of the Department of Defense and others.

Nelson Rockefeller is described as "a frequent visitor" to the Quantico II study group meetings that were convened beginning in November 1955. He is also listed by name in the study group's member roster under that session: "David Rockefeller" appears in the published list of CFR study group members.

Cooper further states that "Rockefeller built a retreat somewhere in Maryland for Majesty Twelve and the study committee" — a location reachable only by air, known by the code name "the Country Club."

## Rockefeller Commission

Cooper notes that "President Ford organized the Rockefeller Commission" to investigate the intelligence community after Watergate. He states that Nelson Rockefeller, "who headed the commission investigating the intelligence community, [had helped] build the Majesty Twelve power structure" — and therefore "uncovered only enough to keep the hounds at bay."

## Rockefeller-Funded Center

Cooper mentions that "The Center for the Study for Democratic Institutions recently completed a proposed constitution for the 'Newstates of America'" and that "The Center is Rockefeller funded." He notes this proposed constitution mentioned "national emergency" 134 times and lacked a Bill of Rights.

## Connection to the Kennedy Assassination

Cooper asserts that "the man who was in charge of the Secret Service at the time of the assassination became the man in charge of security for the Rockefeller family upon his retirement" — implying a direct link between the Secret Service agent responsible for Kennedy's death and subsequent Rockefeller employment.

## Primary Textual Appearances

The Rockefeller family appears throughout Chapter Two and Chapter Twelve of Cooper's book. Nelson Rockefeller is most prominently featured in the context of Majesty Twelve and alien-task oversight.
