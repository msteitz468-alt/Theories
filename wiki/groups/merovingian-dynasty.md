**Identity and Nomenclature**

The dynasty takes its name from Merovee (Merovech/Meroveus), a semi-legendary king whose name echoes both the French word for "mother" (*mère*) and the French/Latin words for "sea" (*mer*/*mare*). Unlike other dynasties, the Merovingians did not arise through violent displacement of a predecessor — they were already the rightful, acknowledged kings of the Franks. Something about one of them was so special that he conferred his name on the whole dynasty.

**Source-Specific Portraits**

**Merovee's birth legend**: According to both the leading Frankish chronicler and subsequent tradition, Merovee was born of two fathers: his mother was pregnant by her husband King Clodio when she went swimming in the ocean, where she was seduced/impregnated by a sea creature described as *bestea Neptuni Quinotauri similis* ("a beast of Neptune resembling a Quinotaur"). The source interprets this as a symbolic encoding of a real historical fact — possibly the fusion of a bloodline arriving "from beyond the sea" with the Frankish royal line.

**The sacred/magical attributes of Merovingian kings**: Called "sorcerer kings" and "thaumaturge kings." Allegedly could heal by laying on of hands; the tassels of their robes were said to have miraculous curative powers. They reportedly bore a distinctive birthmark — a red cross either over the heart or between the shoulder blades (an "anticipation of the Templar blazon"). They were loath to cut their hair (like Samson); their power was said to reside in their hair, and when Childeric III was deposed in 754, his hair was ritually shorn at the pope's express command.

**Dagobert II's assassination and erasure**: The last effective Merovingian king was assassinated in 679 with alleged Church complicity, then systematically erased from official records for centuries. The Carolingians who supplanted them were paradoxically obsessed with claiming Merovingian pedigree to legitimize themselves — intelligible only if the Merovingian blood was regarded as uniquely sacred.

**The pact with the Church**: The source argues that the Church's binding pact with Clovis (c.496) — by which the Church committed to the Merovingian dynasty in perpetuity — makes sense only if the dynasty was regarded as uniquely sacred: "a pact with Jesus's lineal descendants would be the obvious pact for a church founded in his name."

**Post-Carolingian survival**: The bloodline allegedly survived through Prince Guillem de Gellone (Count of Razès, near Rennes-le-Château), and through subsequent dynastic intermarriages eventually connecting to the house of Lorraine and Godfroi de Bouillon.

**Contemporary claimed descendants** (per the "Prieuré documents" and the source's analysis): Habsburg-Lorraine (present titular Dukes of Lorraine and Kings of Jerusalem), Plantard, Luxembourg, Montpezat, Montesquieu, Sinclair (Britain), and various Stuart branches. The Devonshire family is also mentioned.
