**Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)**

The "Black Nobility" are, per Coleman, "the oldest members" of European aristocracy — "descendants of the ancient families, who owned, controlled and ran Genoa and Venice in the 12th century." He says they are "called 'Black Nobility' because of their use of dirty tricks, murder, terrorism, unethical behavior, and worship of Satan — 'black' deeds."

**Source-Specific Portrait**

Coleman casts the Venetian Black Nobility as "the richest and most ancient of all European families," wealthier than the Rockefellers, and a core component of the [[Committee of 300 (Coleman)]]. He identifies the **Guelph dynasty** as one of the oldest such lines and claims "Queen Elizabeth II ... is a Black Guelph," descended through Queen Victoria. The [[Club of Rome (Coleman account)]], he says, is staffed at its core by these families (and the German Marshall Fund is "closely allied" with them). He ties named figures (e.g. the Luccatti family, into which Richard Gardner married) to Black Nobility "dirty tricks and terrorism."

This overlaps with David Icke's "Black Nobility" usage — compare [[House of Windsor (Icke)]] and [[Claim: Icke asserts the House of Windsor is a reptilian Black Nobility bloodline]] (Icke adds a reptilian dimension Coleman does not).
