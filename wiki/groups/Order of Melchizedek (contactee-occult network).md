## Overview from Source

The Order of Melchizedek is the loose international occult/contactee current that Vallée traces
as a recurring thread through *Messengers of Deception*. He first encountered it as graffiti in
the Paris subway — "THE LORD IS AN EXTRATERRESTRIAL WHO HAS RETURNED IN A FLYING SAUCER. Order
of Melchizedek" — and pursued it on the advice of [[Major Murphy (Vallée's intelligence source)]],
who told him to "follow that track seriously." Melchizedek (the biblical "priest of the most
high God" who blessed Abraham with bread and wine, Genesis 14) serves as "a symbol and a
rallying point for saucer contactees." Vallée joined several of its branches as a participant-
observer.

## Branches and Figures from Source

- **Paris** — Ivan and Cyna, who run the Order for "Israel, Africa, and Europe, as well as India
  and China"; their leaflets announce that money, religion, and military service are abolished by
  the extraterrestrial "Lord," and predict flying saucers over Paris. Ivan also founded the
  "Front for Christian Liberation," "Jesus Revolution," and a "Christian Socialist Party."
- **San Francisco** — Dr. [[Grace Hooper Pettipher]], who lectured at the Hyatt on Union Square
  ("Be Your Own Psychiatrist"), preaching Atlantis, reincarnation, and "cosmo-voyance."
- **American esoteric root** — Hiram Erastus Butler's Esoteric Fraternity at Applegate,
  California (founded 1889), and Enoch Penn's 1926 writings: four degrees of initiation,
  mandatory sexual abstinence, control of "the Mob" through psychology, and a plan for the
  "144,000 sons of God" to "take the dominion under the whole heaven." By 1971 only two aged
  members survived.
- **Modern revivals** — the STELLE community in Illinois; eleven students initiated in a Menlo
  Park hot tub via Rabbi Zalman Schachter; and Jim Hurtak's "Keys of Enoch" / "Sons of Light,"
  who invited Vallée to become a "consciousness reprogrammer."

The Book of Urantia (see [[Urantia movement]]) also makes the Melchizedeks a roaming inter-
planetary order, reinforcing the link between the name and UFO belief.
