## Overview from Source

"Rex Deus" is the name Wallace-Murphy & Hopkins give to a claimed network of "elect families"
descended from the Davidic and Hasmonean royal lines and from the **twenty-four *ma'amadot*** (the
hereditary high-priestly families of the Temple of Jerusalem). Per the source, after the Temple's
destruction these families scattered across Europe and the Islamic world but were bound by sacred oath
to keep their genealogies, marry within the group (after the manner of the Cohenite priesthood), and
secretly preserve "the true way" while outwardly professing the prevailing religion. Their claimed
heraldic colors are green and gold. The book argues they are wrongly branded heretics and in truth
deserve the title "Custodians of Truth." This is the umbrella concept of the source.

## Description from Source

The hypothesis (built from the informant "Michael," a claimed descendant of Hugues de Payen) is
offered as the hidden key to medieval enigmas — e.g. why the [[Knights Templar]] were founded only
after Baldwin II took the throne of Jerusalem and "knew where to dig" under the Temple Mount, and why
certain families wove tight marital/political alliances across Europe. The source presents Rex Deus as
continuous from biblical Israel, through the Templars and the [[Cathars (Albigensians)]], to the
St. Clairs of Roslin and the foundation of Freemasonry. It is explicitly framed as a continuation of
*[[The Holy Blood and the Holy Grail (Baigent, Leigh, Lincoln 1982)]]* and overlaps the
[[Prieuré de Sion]] / Merovingian bloodline literature.

## Strict-extraction note

Rex Deus is a speculative bloodline thesis; the wiki records it as the authors' claim, attributed and
not adjudicated.
