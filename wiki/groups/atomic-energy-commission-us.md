## Identity and Nomenclature

Jacobsen identifies the Atomic Energy Commission as a civilian federal agency that regulated nuclear weapons information in a system separate from presidential executive orders. It was formerly known as the Manhattan Project.

## Primary Textual Appearances

- **Prologue**: Atomic Energy Act of 1946; "born classified"; seventy thousand nuclear bombs; first controller of Area 51; withheld records from President Clinton's Advisory Committee on Human Radiation Experiments.
- **Chapter 1**: Area 51's first customer (from 1951); pilot-related projects without oversight; trucks at Gate 385 parking lot.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Portrayed as possessing "terrifying and unprecedented power" over secrets, including authority to classify private research as "born classified" and to deny even the president need-to-know access.
