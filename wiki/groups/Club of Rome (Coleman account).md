**Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)**

Per Coleman the Club of Rome's name "was chosen to deceive the unwary," having "nothing to do with the Vatican or the Catholic Church" (nor with Rome, Italy). He calls it "the most powerful secret society in the hands of the [[Committee of 300 (Coleman)]]" and "an important international foreign policy arm" — "the executor and overseer of the Committee's foreign policy decisions." It is funded, he says, by the **German Marshall Fund** (another deceptively named entity).

**Source-Specific Portrait**

Coleman says the COR "consists of the oldest members of the so-called [[Black Nobility (Coleman)|Black Nobility]] of Europe," together with European royalty, and that its central objective is "the dissolution of the United States as an industrial and agricultural power." He names an American chapter including Samuel Huntington ("chief planner destroying nations"), Cyrus Vance, Milton Friedman, Paul Volcker, Gerald Ford, and others, and identifies **Aurelio Peccei** as founder and **Willis Harmon** as its "high priest." Its policy, he writes, is "fewer and fewer people, consuming less and less," enforced through a "Post-Industrial Zero-Growth" program and the *Global 2000 Report*.
