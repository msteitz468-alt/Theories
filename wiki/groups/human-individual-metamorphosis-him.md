## Overview from Source

Human Individual Metamorphosis (H.I.M.) is a 1970s American UFO contactee group Vallée studied
as a participant-observer; he attended its recruiting meeting at Stanford's Bechtel
International Center on August 13, 1975. The group was led by "**the Two**" — a man and a woman
who claimed to have incarnated from "a level above the human level, an actual, physical level
out in space" to lead Earth's "graduating class." Vallée presents H.I.M. as the clearest case
of a cult acquiring "very significant power over apparently 'rational' people" with startling
speed. The group is a documented precursor of the **Heaven's Gate** cult (the 2008 foreword
notes the 1997 mass suicide and recalls H.I.M.'s slogan, "It only costs your life").

## Doctrine and Methods from Source

The Two taught that a follower could "convert that physical vehicle into a different form" able
to "leave this atmosphere and go out into… space," overcoming "disease or decay or death." They
promised a public proof: the Two would be killed and would rise "three and a half days later,"
then be taken up by a UFO. (Vallée notes that, "as I write this two years later," the
resurrection "has still not taken place.") Recruitment was by mailed literature; the doctrine
was explicitly to be accepted irrationally — "you are capable of picking up this information
without solving it on a rational, conscious level." The butterfly/caterpillar metamorphosis was
the central image; joining meant abandoning job, family, and possessions ("It only costs your
life, you know…"). When an audience member asked how they knew they were not "deceived by
demonic forces," the answer was that "the potency of the information speaks to you."

## Leaders Identified in the Source

In November 1975 the Two were identified as **Marshall Herff Applewhite** (43, born Spur,
Texas; musician and opera singer, son of a Presbyterian minister) and **Bonnie Lu Nettles** (48,
a nurse who met him in Houston while he was "recovering from a mental breakdown"). A former
member told Vallée: "These two people are dangerous. It is not hypnosis. It is thought
transplant." Vallée notes ex-members would "go home and, for several weeks, simply stare at the
walls."
