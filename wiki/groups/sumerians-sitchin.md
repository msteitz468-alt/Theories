**Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)**

Sitchin treats the Sumerians as humanity's first high civilization, appearing in southern Mesopotamia. He cites their own claim that their knowledge and "kingship" were "lowered from Heaven" by the gods — i.e. given to them by the [[Nefilim (Sitchin)]].

**Source-Specific Portrait**

Chapters 2 and 4 catalogue Sumerian "firsts" — the sexagesimal mathematics, writing, the wheel, kilns and brick, medicine, law codes, schools, banking and the silver shekel, deep-water seafaring, and a complete astronomy including planets invisible to the naked eye. Sitchin's argument is that this civilization **flowered suddenly, with no precursor**, ~6,000 years ago, and that its sophistication (e.g. astronomical knowledge of Uranus, Neptune, Pluto and a "Twelfth Planet") is inexplicable except as **knowledge transmitted by the gods**. He reads the Sumerian texts not as myth but as historical records of the Nefilim and their dealings with man.
