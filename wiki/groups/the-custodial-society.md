## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

Bramley coins "Custodial society" (or "Custodians") to label the extraterrestrial society — or "succession of societies" — that he argues has owned and held custody of Earth since prehistory. He chooses a relationship-based label rather than naming a home planet, and stresses they are physically humanlike, racially diverse, and "very similar to human beings behaviorally," running "the entire gamut from saints to sinners," with the "brutal and despotic element" dominant in Earth's affairs.

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Ch. 4 (The Gods of Eden): the Custodial creation and exploitation of *Homo sapiens*.
- Throughout: as the hidden top of the Brotherhood network, behind UFOs, plagues, religions, and wars.

## Source-Specific Portrait

The Custodians are equated with the Sumerian/Biblical "gods" (Ea/Enki, Enlil, Anu, Jehovah), with the UFO phenomenon across history, and with Charles Fort's notion that "we're property." They allegedly keep humanity disunited, spiritually ignorant, and consumed by survival — see [[Humanity is a slave race owned by an extraterrestrial Custodial society]].
