**Identity and Nomenclature**

Publicly founded 1118 when nine knights, led by Hugues de Payen, were established in quarters on the Temple Mount in Jerusalem. The source argues the Templars were active from at least 1114 and were created by, and functioned as the armed wing of, the Ordre de Sion.

**Source-Specific Portraits**

The source's treatment of the Templars in Part One (Chapters 3–4) focuses on orthodox history alongside the anomalies pointing toward a hidden side.

**The founding mystery**: The source argues it is "frankly absurd" that nine knights could have protected pilgrimage routes (as the official founding story claims) while simultaneously undertaking extensive excavations under the Temple Mount — the real purpose of their first years. The source proposes they were searching for something specific, probably documents or physical artifacts relating to the Jesus bloodline or to the Jerusalem Temple's contents.

**Rapid rise and unexplained wealth**: The Templars rose from obscurity to wealth and power with a speed the source finds inexplicable without the backing of the Ordre de Sion. Saint Bernard (André de Montbard's nephew) championed them publicly, wrote their Rule (essentially the Cistercian Rule), and secured their formal recognition at the Council of Troyes in 1128.

**The mysterious "head" (Baphomet / Caput LVIII)**: The Templars were accused at their trial of worshipping a severed head they called "Baphomet." The Inquisition records confirm that among goods seized at the time of the Templars' arrest was "a large head of silver gilt ... the image of a woman, which Guillaume [de Gisors] on May 11th [1308] presented before the Inquisition. The head carried a label, 'CAPUT LVIIIm'." The Dossiers secrets state: "in 1307, Guillaume de Gisors received the golden head, Caput LVIII F[a], from the Order of the Temple." This connects the head to the Grand Master of the Prieuré de Sion.

**The suppression (1307)**: On Friday, October 13, 1307, Philippe IV of France ordered the simultaneous arrest of all Templars in France. The Templars apparently received advance warning — the Order's vast treasure disappeared before the arrests, and the Order's archives were never recovered. The source speculates that the Prieuré de Sion may have orchestrated or colluded in the suppression, as the formal Sion-Templar separation had occurred in 1188.

**The accurate Grand Masters list**: The Dossiers secrets contains a list of Templar Grand Masters that the source, after exhaustive research, concludes is more accurate than any other known list — suggesting it derives from "inside information" rather than public records.
