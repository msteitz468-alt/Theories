## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

Mullins presents the National Monetary Commission as the body created (per the source, its "real purpose") by the Aldrich-Vreeland Act of 1908, headed by [[Nelson Aldrich]], ostensibly to study financial practices before drafting reform legislation. The source argues it was a device to channel and neutralize public indignation after the Panic of 1907 while the bankers prepared their own plan.

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Spent nearly two years and more than $300,000 touring Europe.
- Per the source, only two members (Aldrich and A. Piatt Andrew, a Special Assistant) actually worked on the plan; the others "had a two year joyride around Europe."
- The plan it advanced — drafted at [[Event: Jekyll Island drafting meeting (November 1910)]] — was presented to Congress as the "Aldrich Plan."

## Source-Specific Portrait

Presented as the official cover under which Paul Warburg's central-bank plan was developed and laundered into legislation.
