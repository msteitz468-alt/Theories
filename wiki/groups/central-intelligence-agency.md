## Identity and Nomenclature

The Central Intelligence Agency is described as choosing Area 51 in 1955 for spy-plane testing and bringing the US Air Force as a partner.

## Primary Textual Appearances

- **Prologue**: U-2 declassified 1998; A-12 Oxcart declassified 2007; name "Area 51" redacted in declassified memos.
- **Chapter 1**: Richard Bissell and Herbert Miller selected the site; coordinated cover stories with NACA and Navy.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Associated with black operations, peacetime espionage, and decades of secrecy at Groom Lake. Two early Groom Lake projects were declassified but the base name remained redacted.

## Source-Specific Portrait — Talbot, *The Devil's Chessboard* (2015)

Talbot traces the agency from its 1947 founding (which Dulles helped frame and Truman later regretted) to its transformation under DCI Allen Dulles into "a vast kingdom, the most powerful and least supervised agency in government." In this account the CIA recruited Nazis, ran coups (Iran 1953, Guatemala 1954), conducted assassination programs and MKULTRA, operated with "virtually no congressional oversight," subverted allied democracies (Italy), and — in the book's thesis — was complicit in the Kennedy assassination and Warren Commission cover-up. Truman's December 1963 op-ed warned the CIA had become "a symbol of sinister and mysterious foreign intrigue."
