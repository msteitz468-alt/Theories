## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

Bramley's name for the secret Brotherhood whose logo was the snake/serpent. Originally founded (he claims) by the god **Ea** ([[Enki (Sitchin)]]) to spread spiritual knowledge and free humanity, it was defeated and "corrupted" by rival Custodians into "the world's most effective tool for preserving mankind's status as a spiritually ignorant creature of toil." Bramley presents it as the parent of a vast network of secret societies, mystery schools, and religions.

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Ch. 5: its founding by Ea and corruption.
- Ch. 6–8: the Egyptian Mystery Schools, monotheism, the mason guilds, and the apron symbol.
- Later chapters: offshoots include Freemasonry, the Rosicrucians, the knightly orders, and (per Bramley) the engines of the Reformation, the banks, and the modern revolutions.

## Source-Specific Portrait

The Brotherhood's secrecy is, for Bramley, what lets the Custodians "hide at the top… behind veils of myth" while breeding the conflicts that keep humanity enslaved — see [[The Custodians breed conflict between humans through the Machiavellian third-party technique]]. He insists genuine humanitarians persisted within it throughout history.
