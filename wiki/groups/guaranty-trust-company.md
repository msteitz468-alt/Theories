## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

Per Sutton, Guaranty Trust Company of New York was "the Morgan-controlled Guaranty Trust" — the central Wall Street institution in his account of financial support for the Bolsheviks. Sutton documents "a continuing working relationship between the Guaranty Trust Company and the Bolsheviks" before, during, and after the revolution.

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Worked with [[Olof Aschberg]] (Morgan's tsarist-era agent in Russia, then revolutionary financial intermediary, then head of Ruskombank).
- [[Max May]], a Guaranty Trust vice president, became director of the foreign department of Ruskombank (1923), which appointed Guaranty Trust its U.S. agent.
- Backed the Soviet Bureau in New York (1919) and, via directors (Thomas Lamont, Harry Payne Whitney), the American-Russian syndicate (1918) and John Reed.
- Sutton notes Guaranty Trust's involvement (per the WWI espionage chapter) and its address near [[120 Broadway and the alliance of bankers and revolution|120 Broadway]] (Guaranty Securities Co. at 120; Guaranty Trust next door at 140).

## Source-Specific Portrait

Sutton treats Guaranty Trust as the institutional spine of the "alliance of bankers and revolution," controlled by the J. P. Morgan interests.
