## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

Mullins characterizes J. P. Morgan & Co. as having become "the principal international agency" of the Rothschilds in 1869, when (per the source) J. P. Morgan and Anthony Drexel concluded an agreement in London with N. M. Rothschild & Company to act as their agent. The source lists the linked Morgan houses (Drexel & Co.; Morgan, Grenfell & Co. of London; Morgan Harjes & Co. of Paris) as transacting Rothschild business under "a less known name."

## Primary Textual Appearances

- Henry P. Davison (senior partner) and Charles D. Norton (President of Morgan's First National Bank) were part of the Jekyll Island group.
- [[Benjamin Strong]] is presented as a Morgan lieutenant who became first Governor of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York.
- The first Liberty Loan of $400 million, per the source, went to J. P. Morgan & Co. to repay a British loan.
- The source repeats accusations against the firm's founder (sale of condemned Hall carbines to the Union Army) drawn from Gustavus Myers.

## Source-Specific Portrait

Presented, alongside [[Kuhn, Loeb & Co.]], as one of the two banking alliances that controlled US money and credit and stood to benefit from the Federal Reserve and WWI finance.
