## Identity and Nomenclature

Skinwalker Ranch is a remote Utah property purchased by Robert Bigelow, named for Navajo folklore about shape-shifting entities. Bigelow and BAASS established it as a primary field research site for AAWSAP, staffing it with live-in investigators who documented anomalous phenomena continuously.

## Primary Textual Appearances

In Chapter 4, Elizondo describes AAWSAP briefings that included Skinwalker Ranch case material as part of the SCIF-based evidence review. In Chapter 6, he describes the "hitchhiker effect" — coined by Jay Stratton — being documented at the ranch: anomalous events followed investigators home after time on the property. He also describes dogs being "vaporized" by a blue orb on the ranch property — one of the most extreme biological effects incidents in the AAWSAP case files. Blue orbs are described as the most dangerous UAP subtype.
