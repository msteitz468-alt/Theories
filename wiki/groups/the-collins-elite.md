## Identity and Nomenclature

The Collins Elite is Elizondo's name for an informal network of religiously motivated individuals within the US government and intelligence community who believe that UAP are demonic entities and that acknowledging or engaging with them publicly would cause theological and social catastrophe. The group operates through informal networks rather than formal organizational structure, making it difficult to identify with precision.

## Source-Specific Portrait

In Chapter 4, Elizondo introduces the Collins Elite as one of three internal factions regarding UAP policy: the threat-response faction, the scientific-engagement faction, and the Collins Elite. In Chapter 8, Devon Woods (DIA, former CIA) explicitly tells Elizondo that UAP are "demonic" — expressing the Collins Elite viewpoint directly. Elizondo credits the Collins Elite with contributing to the institutional resistance AATIP encountered in getting findings accepted and programs supported.

The Collins Elite represents the religious obstruction layer of the UAP coverup distinct from the national security secrecy layer (military/contractor compartmentalization) and the resource protection layer (Legacy Program contractors).
