## Identity and Nomenclature

Project Stargate was the US government's classified remote viewing research program, funded initially by the CIA and later by the DIA, running from approximately 1972 to 1995. Hal Puthoff served as a key scientific contractor, conducting foundational remote viewing research at SRI International. The program operated under multiple code names over its lifespan.

## Primary Textual Appearances

In Chapter 3, Elizondo describes his own exposure to remote viewing through Army program work with Gene Lessman before his AATIP recruitment. In Chapter 10 and 14, Puthoff's Stargate role is cited to explain how remote viewers — who share the enlarged caudate-putamen brain feature documented by Garry Nolan in UAP experiencers — became part of the same neurological research thread that AATIP pursued.
