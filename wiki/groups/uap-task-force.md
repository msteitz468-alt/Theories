## Identity and Nomenclature

The UAP Task Force (UAPTF) was established by the DoD in 2020 — the first officially acknowledged government UAP investigation body since Project Blue Book. Jay Stratton (former AATIP co-founder) served as its first director. David Grusch served as the NRO and NGA representative to the UAPTF.

## Primary Textual Appearances

In Chapter 23, Elizondo describes the UAPTF as a direct successor to AATIP's mission, created through Congressional pressure following the December 2017 New York Times disclosures and years of lobbying by Elizondo, Mellon, and others. By the May 2022 public Congressional hearing, the UAPTF had compiled 144 cases; this number grew to over 400 following the hearing. Grusch testified in 2023 that the UAPTF was refused access to a broader crash retrieval program maintained by Legacy Program contractors.

The UAPTF was succeeded by AARO (All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office) in July 2022.
