## Identity and Nomenclature

Cooper asserts that the Bilderberg Group is "the most powerful secret organization in the world," organized in 1952 and named after the hotel where its first known public meeting took place in 1954. Cooper states that the group is also known as "the Bilderbergers," and that its headquarters office is in The Hague in Switzerland.

## Origins and Founding

Cooper claims the Bilderberg Group was organized by Prince Bernhard of the Netherlands, whom he describes as the leader of the "Black Families" and a person who can claim descent from the Roman emperors and from the House of David. According to Cooper, Prince Bernhard used CIA assistance to bring "the hidden ruling body of the Illuminati into public knowledge as the Bilderberg Group."

Cooper states the group held its first public meeting in 1954 at the Bilderberg Hotel, from which the name derives. He also writes that an earlier, less visible meeting occurred in 1952.

## Structure and Membership

Cooper describes the internal structure as follows: the heart of the group consists of 39 total members of the Illuminati, divided into three committees of thirteen members each. These 39 members are drawn from the Freemasons, the Vatican, and the Black Nobility. The 39-member core answers to a 13-member Policy Committee, which in turn answers to a Round Table of Nine.

Cooper claims the three committees work year-round in offices in Switzerland and determine who is invited to annual meetings and what policies will be discussed.

Cooper asserts that the members are "the most powerful financiers, industrialists, statesmen and intellectuals, who get together each year for a private conference on world affairs," and that the meetings are "notorious for the cloak of secrecy they are held under."

## Alleged Goals and Activities

Cooper asserts that the Bilderberg Group is "directing the 'quiet war' that is being waged against us" and that its ultimate goal is "a one-world totalitarian socialist government and economic system."

Cooper states that "every proposal or plan that has ever been discussed at an annual meeting of the Bilderberg Group has come to pass usually within one or two years following the meeting."

Cooper also claims that certain very secret meetings are held aboard submarines under the oceans, involving the Policy Committee and Soviet representatives, at which the agenda includes the secret space program known as "Alternative 3." He cites a BBC-TV documentary program called "Science Report" as having revealed these same facts, which was subsequently retracted as fiction — though Cooper notes the program had never aired fiction before.

## Connection to Other Entities

Cooper asserts that the Trilateral Commission was formed only after David Rockefeller obtained approval at a Bilderberg Group meeting in 1972. He states that President Kennedy's assassination was ordered by the Bilderberg Policy Committee after Kennedy announced he intended to reveal the alien presence to the public. Cooper also identifies the Bilderberg Group as the international supervisory body for covert operations that were domestically supervised by the Council on Foreign Relations executive committee.

Cooper notes that Prince Bernhard, as the Bilderberg Group's organizer, holds the power to veto the Vatican's choice of any Pope because his family, the Hapsburgs, are descended from the Roman emperors.

## Primary Textual Appearances

The Bilderberg Group appears throughout Chapter Two ("Secret Societies and the New World Order") and Chapter Twelve ("The Secret Government") of Cooper's book. Cooper cites it repeatedly as the apex public-facing structure of the global Illuminati conspiracy.
