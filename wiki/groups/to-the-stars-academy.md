## Identity and Nomenclature

To The Stars Academy of Arts and Science (TTSA) was co-founded by musician Tom DeLonge as a public benefit corporation aimed at UAP research and disclosure. After Elizondo's Pentagon resignation in October 2017, he joined TTSA alongside Hal Puthoff, Steve Justice (former Lockheed Skunk Works Advanced Development Programs director), Jim Semivan, and Christopher Mellon.

## Primary Textual Appearances

Elizondo describes joining TTSA in Chapter 21 and his experience through Chapter 22. TTSA provided the organizational platform through which the December 2017 New York Times UAP stories were coordinated. The organization produced two seasons of the History Channel show *Unidentified*, which aired in 2019 and 2020. TTSA experienced significant financial difficulties; Elizondo and his family lived in an RV while his TTSA salary was cut in half. He was fired from TTSA just before Christmas 2020.

Elizondo is careful not to blame Tom DeLonge directly for the financial difficulties, crediting DeLonge's role in the disclosure movement while acknowledging the professional and personal costs the TTSA experience imposed on his family.
