## Overview from Source

Cooper references the Department of Defense in several distinct contexts throughout his book. The source does not contain a dedicated discussion of the DoD's structure or history; rather, Cooper mentions it instrumentally in connection with the alien coverup, emergency planning, and what he calls a concentration camp program.

## Concentration Camp Program

Cooper references a legal complaint filed on April 20, 1976, entitled "Complaint Against the Concentration Camp Program of the Dept of Defense," filed in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Texas, Houston Division (civil action number 76-H-667, before Judge Carl Bue). He also cites a document that "updated reported on the concentration camp program of the Department of Defense of the United States." Cooper presents this in the context of a broader claim that the DoD has developed detention infrastructure for use against American citizens.

## Alien-Program Budget

Cooper asserts that the secret alien-related program funding "was allocated [to the Department of Defense] as a Top Secret item in the Army" budget so that the name Majesty Twelve would not appear in any appropriations document. He writes that "the power of the [alien program] was to be found through this office" passing through Majesty Twelve and out to contractors.

## Emergency Powers Role

Cooper describes the DoD's role in a declared national emergency scenario, stating that it "would be responsible for the expropriation of industry; direction of services and national production system; control of censorship; and communication expropriation of non-industrial facilities." This is presented as part of the broader framework of Executive Order 11490, which Cooper claims consolidated all emergency powers under FEMA's director.

## Majesty Twelve Membership

Cooper states that one of the original members of Majesty Twelve was "a representative of the Department of Defense," sitting alongside Nelson Rockefeller on the coordinating group that reported to the President.

## Primary Textual Appearances

The Department of Defense is referenced in Chapter Six (H.R. 4079 and FEMA), Chapter Twelve (The Secret Government), and in the lawsuit documentation included in the book. It appears as an institutional vehicle rather than an independent subject of analysis in Cooper's text.
