## Description from Source

As self-described in *The First Global Revolution*, the Club of Rome is a "flexible and informal" international group concerned with "the interactions within the tangle of contemporary problems," for which it coined the term the [[The world problematique and resolutique|world problematique]]. Founded in 1968 (the authors note its 1972 report *The Limits to Growth* sold "some ten million copies in over thirty languages" and gave the Club "worldwide visibility or, as some would say, notoriety"). It has since published eighteen-plus reports (e.g. *Mankind at the Turning Point*). The book's authors are its co-founder/president **Alexander King** and secretary-general **Bertrand Schneider**; its founding president was [[Aurelio Peccei]].

This is the organization's account of itself. For the conspiratorial counter-portrait, see [[Club of Rome (Coleman account)]].
