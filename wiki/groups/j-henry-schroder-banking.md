## Identity and Nomenclature (from the source)

Per Mullins, J. Henry Schroder is one of the seventeen London merchant banks of the Accepting Houses Committee (ranked #2 by capitalization), each approved by the Bank of England. Like the Warburgs, the von Schroders "began their banking operations in Hamburg, Germany"; Baron Bruno von Schroder established the London branch in 1900, joined by Frank Cyril Tiarks (a director of the Bank of England, 1912–1945). The source presents Schroder as a key node of the [[The London Connection]].

## Primary Textual Appearances

- WWI: Schroder partners (Julius Barnes, Prentiss Gray, G. A. Zabriskie) ran the U.S. Food Administration's grain, shipping, and sugar operations under Herbert Hoover, per the source — "the London Connection controlled all food in the United States."
- The Hitler financing: Hitler "was invited to a meeting at the Schroder Bank in Berlin on January 4, 1933" where his debts were covered and his chancellorship enabled. The Dulles brothers (Sullivan & Cromwell, which "represented the Schroder Bank") were present; [[Allen Dulles]] "later became a director of J. Henry Schroder Company." The Great Soviet Encyclopaedia is quoted: "The banking house Schroder Bros. (it was Hitler's banker)."

## Source-Specific Portrait

Presented as the institutional link between the City of London, the Bank of England, U.S. food control in WWI, Herbert Hoover, and the financing of Hitler.
