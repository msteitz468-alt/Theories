## Identity and Nomenclature

The postwar intelligence network built from Reinhard Gehlen's Wehrmacht eastern-front intelligence staff, taken over and funded by U.S. intelligence and later folded into West Germany's BND.

## Primary Textual Appearances

Talbot says Dulles helped "build the West German intelligence system out of the poisoned remains of the Third Reich"; the Org operated for a time out of Camp King, where it delivered prisoners for CIA Operation Artichoke interrogations. Bill Harvey worked closely with Gehlen in Berlin.

## Source-Specific Portraits

Part of Talbot's argument that the CIA's anti-Soviet drive led it to recruit Nazis wholesale — the same logic that protected Karl Wolff and Eugen Dollmann.
