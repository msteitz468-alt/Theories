## Biographical Details from Source

The Harvard lecturer and clinical psychologist who, after a 1960 magic-mushroom trip in Mexico, launched the psilocybin/LSD research project that became the public face of the psychedelic movement. With Richard Alpert he ran the Concord Prison study and the "Good Friday" experiment; he was converted to LSD by Michael Hollingshead's "mayonnaise jar." Lee and Shlain note his "Leary" personality test "was used by the CIA," that his group was monitored by the CIA's Office of Security, and that he gave LSD to Mary Pinchot Meyer (ex-wife of CIA official Cord Meyer). They quote his later quip that "the LSD movement was started by the CIA."
