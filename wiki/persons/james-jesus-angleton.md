## Biographical Details from Source

Dulles's protégé and "ghostly knight." A gaunt, chain-smoking Yale poet who ran OSS X-2 counterintelligence in postwar Rome, where Talbot says he operated a Via Archimede safe house for Nazi fugitives connected to Operation Sunrise. He rose to become the CIA's all-powerful counterintelligence chief, worked to block Italy's "opening to the left," and was forced to retire in 1975. He carried Dulles's ashes at his funeral.

## Primary Source Appearances

Talbot: Chs. 5, 17; Epilogue.

In the Epilogue, the dying Angleton tells journalist Joseph Trento that the CIA's founders "were liars… The better you lied and the more you betrayed, the more likely you would be promoted," and that he had been on "a satanic quest"; of Dulles, Helms, and Wisner, "you were in a room full of people that you had to believe would deservedly end up in hell."
