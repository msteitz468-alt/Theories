## Biographical Details from Source

Per Griffin, Carroll Quigley was "a professor of history at Georgetown University" whose book *Tragedy and Hope* "revealed that the Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) is an outgrowth of the secret society formed by Cecil Rhodes." Griffin states Quigley "wrote the history of how an international network of financiers has created a system of financial control able to dominate the political systems of all countries through their central banks. He named names and provided meticulous documentation. His book was suppressed."

Griffin cites Quigley as a primary authority for the Round Table / CFR thesis — notably because Quigley wrote as an insider sympathetic to the network rather than as a critic.

## Beyond the network thesis (Quigley's own analytical frameworks)

Conspiracy authors cite Quigley almost entirely for the Round Table/CFR passages, but the bulk of *Tragedy and Hope* is his own philosophy of history and political analysis. He is the author of the [[Quigley's life cycle of civilizations|seven-stage life cycle of civilizations]] (the "instrument of expansion becomes an institution" mechanism) and the [[Weapons systems and political forms|weapons thesis]] (cheap "amateur" weapons favor democracy; expensive "specialist" weapons favor authoritarianism). His outlook for the future gives the book its title — see [[Professionalized weapons and the nuclear stalemate portend a decline of democracy]].
