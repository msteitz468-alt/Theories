## Biographical Details from Source

Mircea Eliade (1907–1986) was a Romanian historian of religions (long at the University of Chicago) whose *Shamanism: Archaic Techniques of Ecstasy* (1951; English 1964) became the foundational comparative study of shamanism. Eliade defines shamanism narrowly as a **technique of ecstasy** — the shaman as the specialist of the soul who, in trance, ascends to the sky or descends to the underworld — and documents its initiation patterns, cosmology (the axis mundi and three cosmic zones), and worldwide distribution across Siberia, Central Asia, the Americas, and beyond. His work is the scholarly backbone behind the modern shamanism material (e.g. Graham Hancock's *Supernatural*).
