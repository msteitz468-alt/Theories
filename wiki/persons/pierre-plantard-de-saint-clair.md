**Biographical Details from Source**

Pierre Plantard de Saint-Clair is identified in the source as the person most prominently associated with the modern Prieuré de Sion and the "Prieuré documents" deposited in the Bibliothèque Nationale, Paris (known as the Dossiers secrets). The source describes these documents as having been assembled or organized largely through Plantard's efforts, beginning in the 1950s–1960s. The Dossiers secrets include genealogies, lists of Grand Masters, and documents purporting to trace the Merovingian bloodline to the present day via Plantard's own family (the "Saint-Clair" connection invoking both the Sinclair family of Scotland and Rosslyn Chapel).

Plantard is described in the "Prieuré documents" and related materials as secretary-general of the Prieuré de Sion and as a claimant to Merovingian descent. His family crest is depicted in the source. In 1956, the Prieuré de Sion was formally registered in France under the Law of Associations (July 5, 1956) — with Plantard listed as secretary-general.

The source notes that Plantard was one of the individuals who fed the authors additional information during their research, though his exact role and reliability are treated with appropriate caution.

**Primary Source Appearances**

Plantard appears in Part Two as a figure associated with the modern Prieuré de Sion; his name and claims recur throughout the "Prieuré documents" analyzed by the authors.
