## Biographical Details from Source

The source describes Merovee (also spelled Merovech or Meroveus) as "a semi-supernatural figure worthy of classical myth" from whom the Merovingian dynasty took its name. His historical reality, the authors note, is "eclipsed by legend."

**Name and origin**

According to the source, Merovee's very name bears witness to his miraculous origin: it echoes the French word for "mother" (*mère*) as well as both the French and Latin words for "sea" (*mer*, *mare*).

**The legend of dual birth**

According to both "the leading Frankish chronicler and to subsequent tradition," Merovee was said to be "born of two fathers." His mother was already pregnant by her husband, King Clodio, when she supposedly went swimming in the ocean. There, according to the tradition, she was "seduced and/or raped by an unidentified marine creature from beyond the sea — *bestea Neptuni Quinotauri similis*" — a "beast of Neptune similar to a Quinotaur." The creature allegedly impregnated her a second time. The source notes: "when Merovee was born, there allegedly flowed in his veins a commingling of two different bloods — the blood of a Frankish ruler and of a mysterious aquatic creature."

The source observes that such legends "are quite common … in later European tradition as well. Usually they are not entirely imaginary, but symbolic or allegorical, masking some concrete historical fact behind their fabulous facade." In the case of Merovee the dual-birth legend might, the authors suggest, indicate an intermarriage of some sort — "a pedigree transmitted through the mother, as in Judaism, for instance, or a mingling of dynastic lines whereby the Franks became allied by blood with someone else; quite possibly with a source from 'beyond the sea.'"

**Supernatural powers and dynasty**

By virtue of his dual blood, the source states, Merovee was said to have been "endowed with an impressive array of superhuman powers." The Merovingian dynasty as a whole continued to be "mantled in an aura of magic, sorcery and the supernatural." Merovingian monarchs were called "the sorcerer kings" or "thaumaturge kings" and were said capable of healing by laying on of hands, clairvoyant communication with animals, and protection by an arcane spell. They bore a distinctive birthmark — "a red cross, either over the heart … or between the shoulder blades."

**Historical record**

The source identifies at least two historical figures named Merovee. One was "a Sicambrian chieftain, alive in 417, who fought under the Romans and died in 438." In 448 the son of this first Merovee, bearing the same name, "was proclaimed king of the Franks at Tournai and reigned until his death ten years later. He may have been the first official king of the Franks as united people."

**Connection to the book's central thesis**

The source states that according to the "Prieuré documents," the Merovingians were descended, via Arcadia, from the Tribe of Benjamin of ancient Israel. The source also states that the "Dossiers secrets" list "Merovee, King" in connection with the bloodline, indicating that he is the fountainhead of the lineage the source traces ultimately to Jesus.
