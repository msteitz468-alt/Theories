## Biographical Details from Source

Lehrich describes John Michell as "an old Etonian with a penchant for UFO research, archaeoastronomy, and numerology," who became convinced that UFO sightings pointed to something other than what most believers assumed. With the 1967 publication of Michell's *The View over Atlantis*, Lehrich states that ley hunting divided into two camps: those seeking "lines of force" of possibly Atlantean or extraterrestrial origin, and those preferring archaeological reconstruction.

## Primary Source Appearances

Following Tony Wedd's earlier suggestion that UFOs use leys as navigational beacons, Michell proposed — in explicit reaction against Erik von Däniken's *Chariots of the Gods?* — that ancient civilizations (Atlanteans) were fully capable of building monumental structures such as Stonehenge, Avebury, and the Great Pyramid, but that their civilization collapsed catastrophically (drawing on a modified version of Immanuel Velikovsky's catastrophism), leaving later, inferior imitations. Lehrich states Michell held that leys represent real underground currents in the earth's magnetic field, tapped by standing stones acting as "acupuncture needles into the currents of the earth," providing the Atlanteans (and, in Michell's account, present-day UFOs) with clean power for flight and communication.

Lehrich reads Michell's Atlantis as "a kind of illud tempus... a time of wonders and understanding, of peace and decency, when ecological harmony led to comfortable and spiritual living," closely paralleling both Ægypt and Eliade's concept of reactualization: Michell "dreams of a reactualization of this magical time" through better understanding of space and place.
