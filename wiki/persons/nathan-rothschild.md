## Biographical Details from Source

Per Griffin, Nathan Rothschild was the London-based brother of the five-brother House of Rothschild network. The source recounts the legend that "when an exhausted Rothschild courier delivered the first news of the Battle of Waterloo, Nathan was able to deceive the London bond traders into a selling panic, and that allowed him to acquire the dominant holding of England's entire debt at but a tiny fraction of its worth." Griffin presents this episode as the archetype of the [[The Rothschild Formula]] — profiting from advance intelligence and from both sides of conflict.

The source also names Nathan Rothschild as the financier (with the Bank of England) behind [[Cecil Rhodes]]'s diamond and gold monopoly in South Africa.
