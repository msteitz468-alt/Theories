## Biographical Details from Source

In *Hidden History*, Martin Luther King Jr. is presented as a political figure who had "become
especially dangerous to the establishment" by extending his vision beyond racial equality to
challenge the class structure itself and by openly opposing the Vietnam War. His planned Poor
People's March on Washington, per Jeffries, "would have been a far more memorable historical event
if he had been alive to lead it."

Per Jeffries, King was assassinated on April 4, 1968, in Memphis, Tennessee, and James Earl Ray
— characterized as a "lifelong petty criminal" with no known marksmanship skills and no record of
physical violence — was designated as the lone gunman. Jeffries presents Ray as a patsy, notes
that the FBI under J. Edgar Hoover had written a 1964 letter to King that "appeared to be a
thinly veiled instruction to take his own life," and argues the Bureau had far stronger motive to
kill King than Ray did. King's widow Coretta Scott King and her son Dexter King worked to help
Ray obtain a new trial before his 1998 death.
