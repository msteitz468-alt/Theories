## Biographical Details from Source

Per Griffin, Cecil Rhodes made one of the great fortunes of the era — financed by Nathan Rothschild and the Bank of England — establishing a monopoly over South African diamond output and much of its gold. The source states Rhodes "formed a secret society which included many of the top leaders of British government," whose "elitist goal was nothing less than world domination and the establishment of a modern feudalist society controlled by themselves through the world's central banks."

Griffin (drawing on Carroll Quigley) describes the structure: the inner directorate was the **Round Table**, with subordinate **Round-Table Groups** in other countries; the American branch became the [[Council on Foreign Relations and the Round Table network|Council on Foreign Relations]]. The source ties figures such as Raymond Robins (a "disciple of Cecil Rhodes") to this network's activity in revolutionary Russia.
