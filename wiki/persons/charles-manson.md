## Biographical Details from Source

Leader of the "Family," a commune of mostly young women he formed in San Francisco's Haight-Ashbury during the 1967 "summer of love" after his release from federal prison. O'Neill documents Manson's near-daily contact with parole officer [[Roger Smith (parole officer)]] and the [[Haight-Ashbury Free Medical Clinic]], his use of LSD to "reprogram" followers ("negate your ego," turning them into "computers"), and the remarkable legal leniency he enjoyed. Convicted (with followers) of conspiracy in the 1969 Tate–LaBianca murders; died in prison November 2017. O'Neill stresses he considers Manson genuinely evil and is "not a Manson apologist."

## Source-Specific Portrait

O'Neill's brief 2000 phone interviews with Manson were cryptic ("I barely know myself"; claims the "United States Navy" funded the murders; "I'm Vincent Bugliosi's godfather"), mediated by associates "Pin Cushion" (Roger Dale Smith) and "Gray Wolf" (Craig Hammond), and ended with a warning to never make contact again.
