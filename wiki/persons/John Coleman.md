## Biographical Details from Source

Author who presents himself as a former professional (British) intelligence officer who, while serving "in the field of political science," gained access to classified documents revealing the existence of the Committee of 300. Emigrating to the United States in 1969, he says he devoted himself from 1969–70 onward to exposing the body and its constituent organizations through monographs, cassette tapes, and books. *Conspirators' Hierarchy* (1992) is his defining work; *The Club of Rome* (2008) extends it.

## Primary Source Appearances

Author/narrator of *Conspirators' Hierarchy: The Story of the Committee of 300* and *The Club of Rome*.
