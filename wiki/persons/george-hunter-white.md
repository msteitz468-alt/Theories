## Biographical Details from Source

The tough, hard-drinking narcotics officer (on loan from the Federal Narcotics Bureau) who ran the CIA's safehouse experiments for Sidney Gottlieb from 1953 to ~1966. Posing as an artist and seaman, White dosed unwitting people with LSD in his Greenwich Village and San Francisco safehouses, and in **Operation Midnight Climax** used prostitutes to spike men's drinks while he watched through two-way mirrors. By day a drug-buster, by night a CIA doser, he coined the code word "Stormy" for LSD.

## Primary Source Appearances

*Acid Dreams*, Part One (Midnight Climax).

## Source-Specific Portrait

Lee and Shlain quote his retirement letter to Gottlieb: "Where else could a red-blooded American boy lie, kill, cheat, steal, rape, and pillage with the sanction and blessing of the All-Highest?"
