## Biographical Details from Source

Cooper introduces Dr. Guillermo Mendoza as a botanist brought in to treat "EBE" (Extraterrestrial Biological Entity), the live alien Cooper claims was recovered wandering the desert after the 1949 Roswell crash. According to Cooper, in late 1951 EBE became ill, and because EBE's "system was chlorophyll-based" — processing food into energy and excreting waste "much the same as plants" — several specialists, "medical doctors, botanists, and entomologists," were called in. Among them, "A botanist, Dr. Guillermo Mendoza, was brought in to try and help him recover."

Cooper states that "Dr. Mendoza worked to save EBE until June 2, 1952, when EBE died," and that "Dr. Mendoza became the expert on at least this type alien … biology." Cooper further asserts that "The movie *E.T.* is the thinly disguised story of EBE."

The source provides no other biographical detail about Mendoza — no nationality, affiliation, or background beyond his role as the botanist assigned to the captive EBE.

## Primary Source Appearances

Mendoza appears solely within Cooper's account of the captured EBE and the early-1950s government alien-contact programs (the "Yellow Book," Project Grudge, Project SIGMA, and the founding of the NSA).
