**Biographical Details from Source**

Aurelio Peccei is identified by Coleman as the **founder of the Club of Rome** (and pictured on the book's cover alongside Alexander King and Samuel Huntington). Coleman treats Peccei as a mouthpiece of the [[Committee of 300 (Coleman)]]'s depopulation agenda, quoting a statement he attributes to Peccei at a March 1982 COR meeting in Paris:

> "Men are like insects. They proliferate too much ... It is about time to put on trial the concept of nation states [as] obstacles to world culture. Christianity makes proud men; a mercantile society ... creates nothing but dead culture ..."

Coleman reads this as evidence that Peccei and the Club of Rome are "the successors of the French and Bolshevik Revolutions," seeking "fewer and fewer people, consuming less and less."
