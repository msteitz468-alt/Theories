**Biographical Details from Source**

Duke of Lower Lorraine. Led one of the four principal Christian armies of the First Crusade. According to the source, Godfroi was unique among the crusading commanders in renouncing his fiefs and selling all his goods before departing — implying he knew in advance he would not return. He was allegedly tutored by Peter the Hermit, whom the source connects to the band of Calabrian monks at Orval.

When Jerusalem fell in 1099, an anonymous conclave — including "a certain bishop from Calabria" — selected Godfroi as ruler over competing claimants. He declined the title of King of Jerusalem out of "uncharacteristic modesty," accepting instead "Defender of the Holy Sepulchre" — king in everything but name.

He is said by the "Prieuré documents" to have founded the Ordre de Sion in 1090 (or 1099) and to have built the Abbey of Notre Dame du Mont de Sion at his express command. Godfroi is identified in the Grail romances (via his grandfather Lohengrin, Knight of the Swan, and great-grandfather Parzival) as a scion of the "Grail family." The source argues that if the Merovingian bloodline hypothesis is correct, Godfroi's capture of Jerusalem in 1099 would have meant he was literally reclaiming his own ancestral heritage.

He died in 1100, whereupon his brother Baudouin I accepted the royal title Godfroi had declined.

**Primary Source Appearances**

Introduced in Part Two as the founder of the Ordre de Sion; his lineage and the Grail connections are analyzed in Part Three. Central to the argument that the Prieuré de Sion was created to protect and eventually restore the Merovingian/Jesus bloodline.
