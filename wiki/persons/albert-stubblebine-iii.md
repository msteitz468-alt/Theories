## Biographical Details from Source

The U.S. Army's chief of intelligence in the early 1980s, commanding 16,000 soldiers, signals/photographic intelligence, and covert spying units. In Ronson's telling, Stubblebine became convinced that psychic abilities should be standard intelligence tools — he repeatedly (and unsuccessfully) tried to **walk through the wall** of his Arlington office, practiced spoon-bending, and pitched **psychic healing** to skeptical Special Forces commanders at Fort Bragg. He championed the recruitment of psychic spies and the broader paranormal-warfare agenda. (Often rumored to be Lee Marvin's identical twin, which he encouraged because "mystique can be beneficial to a career in intelligence.")
