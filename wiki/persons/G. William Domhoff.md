## Biographical Details from Source

G. William ("Bill") Domhoff is Distinguished Professor Emeritus at the University of California, Santa Cruz, and the leading American "power-structure" sociologist. *Who Rules America?* — first published in 1967 and now in its eighth edition (2022) — is his signature work, applying the institutional, class-based approach pioneered by C. Wright Mills (*The Power Elite*, 1956) and the network methods of sociologist Michael Mann. He is the author or co-author of some 16 other books on power and diversity, including *The Higher Circles* (1970), *The Powers That Be* (1979), *The Power Elite and the State* (1990), *Diversity in the Power Elite* (with Richard Zweigenhaft), and *The Corporate Rich and the Power Elite in the Twentieth Century* (2020).

Domhoff's central argument is that the United States is dominated by a "corporate community" and an allied "social upper class" who together furnish a "power elite" that shapes government policy through visible, legally documented networks — emphatically *not* through secret conspiracy. He is therefore the wiki's sober academic counterpoint to the New-World-Order / international-banker tradition (Quigley, Mullins, Allen, Marrs, Coleman), and an ally of the analytical counterweights (Hofstadter, Barkun, Olmsted).
