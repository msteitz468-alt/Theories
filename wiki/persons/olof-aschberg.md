## Biographical Details from Source

Per Sutton, Olof Aschberg owned the Nya Banken in Stockholm, through which part of the German funds for Lenin's return were transferred. Sutton documents "a continuing working relationship between Bolshevik banker Olof Aschberg and the Morgan-controlled Guaranty Trust Company in New York before, during, and after the Russian Revolution." In tsarist times "Aschberg was the Morgan agent in Russia and negotiator for Russian loans in the United States"; in 1917 he was "financial intermediary for the revolutionaries"; and after the revolution he "became head of Ruskombank, the first Soviet international bank" (1923), which "promptly appointed Guaranty Trust Company its U.S. agent."
