## Biographical Details from Source

Per Mullins (Ch. 5, "The House of Rothschild"), George Peabody (1795–1869) began in business in Georgetown, D.C. in 1814 (Peabody, Riggs & Co., "dealing in wholesale dry goods, and operating the Georgetown Slave Market"), moved to Baltimore, and in 1835 established George Peabody & Company in London. The source states that soon after arriving, Peabody "was surprised to be summoned to an audience with the gruff Baron Nathan Mayer Rothschild," who — disliked by London aristocracy — proposed that Peabody be established as a lavish host with Rothschild secretly paying all the bills. Peabody accepted, becoming "the most popular host in London."

Per Mullins, Peabody's American agent was Junius S. Morgan (father of J. P. Morgan), whom he made a partner in 1854; the firm passed to the Morgans and became, ultimately, J. P. Morgan & Company. The Bank of England saved Peabody's firm with a £1 million advance during the Panic of 1857, enabling it to buy distressed securities and profit while ~900 other firms failed.

## Primary Source Appearances

Mullins *Secrets*: Ch. 5. Presents Peabody as the origin point of the secret Morgan–Rothschild affiliation (dating it to 1835).
