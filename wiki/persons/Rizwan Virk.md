## Biographical Details from Source

The following is drawn exclusively from the "About the Author" section and the foreword/introduction of *The Simulation Hypothesis*.

The book describes Rizwan ("Riz") Virk as "a successful entrepreneur, investor, video game industry pioneer, scholar, teacher, and indie film producer."

**Education**
Riz studied computer science at MIT and management at the Stanford Graduate School of Business. His PhD research is at the School for the Future of Innovation in Society (SFIS) at Arizona State University (ASU). He is also described as a research associate and faculty associate at ASU's Center for Science and the Imagination (CSI) and the Fulton Schools of Engineering.

**Startup and investment activity**
Riz was the founder of Play Labs @ MIT, a startup accelerator hosted on campus at the MIT Game Lab. He is described as a venture partner at Griffin Gaming Partners, characterized as "the largest video game venture capital fund in the world." The source lists his roles as cofounder, investor, and adviser to startups including Gameview Studios (sold to DeNA), CambridgeDocs (sold to EMC), Tapjoy, Funzio (sold to GREE), Pocket Gems, Discord, Telltale Games, Service Metrics, and Theta Labs. These companies produced "enterprise software, blockchains, and video games played by millions, including Tap Fish and games based on Penny Dreadful, Grimm, Star Trek, The Walking Dead, and Game of Thrones."

**Film production**
The source states that Riz "has produced many indie films and series, including *Thrive: What on Earth Will It Take?*, *Knights of Badassdom*, the CW's *The Outpost*, *Sirius*, and adaptations of the works of Philip K. Dick and Ursula K. Le Guin."

**Books**
The source identifies him as the bestselling author of *Zen Entrepreneurship*, *The Simulated Multiverse*, *Wisdom of a Yogi*, *Startup Myths & Models*, and *Treasure Hunt*, in addition to *The Simulation Hypothesis* itself.

**Media and public presence**
His articles have been featured in TechCrunch, VentureBeat, Scientific American, Digital Trends, CNN, The Telegraph, and NBC News. He has appeared on shows ranging from Coast to Coast AM to The Joe Rogan Experience and has spoken at Google, the Conscious Life Expo, and MIT. The source also notes that he taught what he describes as the first accredited university-level simulation theory course in the US: "[[Simulation Theory (Icke's Framework)|Simulation Theory]]: Sci Fi, Technology, Religion and Philosophy."

**Personal background in the book's narrative**
The foreword recounts that Virk was a computer science student at MIT in the 1990s, where he studied AI and game-playing algorithms. He later moved to Silicon Valley during the mobile gaming revolution, designing games including Tap Fish (which reached more than thirty million downloads). He visited Arthur C. Clarke's office in Sri Lanka, which inspired him to begin writing an article on the simulation hypothesis that "went viral." A subsequent health crisis gave him the sustained time to complete the full manuscript. The first edition was published on March 31, 2019 — the twentieth anniversary of the release of *The Matrix*. A Google talk on the book also went viral, and foreign publishers began seeking translation rights. The book's second edition was written in 2024.

**Location**
The source states Riz "splits his time between California, Massachusetts, and Arizona."
