## Biographical Details from Source

Ronald Hutton is the historian who contributed the foreword to the 2022 edition of
*[[Liber Null & Psychonaut (Carroll)]]*. In it he situates [[Chaos Magic]] within the long history of
Western magical traditions — tracing the survival of magical technique from preagricultural animism
and shamanism, through ancient Egyptian, Zoroastrian, Tantric, and Taoist magic, the Neoplatonic
Hermetics/Gnosticism/Kabbala of the early centuries AD, the Renaissance revival, and the 19th-century
Golden Dawn synthesis (which he credits largely to MacGregor and possibly Moina Mathers) — and
argues that chaos magic distills "the techniques, rather than the dogma" of all these into a
practical contemporary art.
