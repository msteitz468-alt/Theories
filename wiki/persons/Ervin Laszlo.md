## Biographical Details from Source

Ervin Laszlo is a Hungarian-born systems philosopher and theorist of consciousness who developed the idea of the **Akashic field** (or "A-field") — a fundamental, cosmos-wide information field that records and conveys the experience of all things, recovering the ancient Sanskrit notion of *Akasha* in the language of contemporary physics and systems science. In *Science and the Akashic Field* (2004) he lays out the scientific case — the "puzzles of coherence" across physics, cosmology, biology, and consciousness, resolved by an in-formation field rooted in the quantum vacuum — and in *The Akashic Experience* (2009) he gathers first-person testimonies that he argues are evidence of that field, both part of an emerging post-materialist scientific paradigm.
