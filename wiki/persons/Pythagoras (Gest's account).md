**Biographical Details from Source**
Per Kevin L. Gest's account in Chapter Five, Pythagoras was born (traditionally) at Sidon in Phoenicia around 580 BCE, though Gest also reproduces William Preston's 1795 *Illustrations of Masonry*, which places his birth/life at Samos "in the reign of Tarquin, the last king of the Romans." According to tradition as related by Gest, Pythagoras first became an initiate of the Eleusinian mysteries in Greece, then travelled to Egypt where he was admitted to the Mysteries of Isis by the priests at Thebes, was initiated into the Mysteries of Adonis, passed into Mesopotamia to receive the Mysteries of the Chaldeans, and finally studied under the Brahmin priestly caste in "Hindustan" (India). He returned to the Mediterranean and founded a school at Crotona in southern Italy, where he initiated disciples into secret knowledge gathered from his travels, dividing them into esoteric and exoteric classes. Gest states Pythagoras coined the term "philosopher" ("one who is attempting to find out"). Preston's account (quoted at length by Gest) adds that Pythagoras was the son of a sculptor, was educated under Therecydes of Syrus, studied the laws of Minos at Crete and Lycurgus at Sparta, faked his own death by hiding in a cave to enhance his reputation, taught the transmigration of souls (metempsychosis, said to be borrowed from the Brahmins), held that the sun was at rest in the center of the cosmos with the earth and planets moving around it, and required a five-year probation of silence from initiates. Preston's text states plainly: "Pythagoras was regularly initiated into masonry."

**Primary Source Appearances**
Chapter Two: credited (via a Babylonian clay tablet predating him) as having "packaged" but not originated the Pythagorean theorem, since the Babylonians already understood the underlying relationship.
Chapter Four: identified as the founder of a Greek mystery school ("the Pythagoreans") whose adepts used the pentagram as a symbol of recognition, discoverable by cutting an apple crosswise to reveal a pentagram pattern in the core.
Chapter Five ("Pythagoras, Mystery Schools, and Freemasonry"): the chapter's central figure. Gest reproduces a system of Pythagorean number symbolism (monad through decad) paraphrased from Manley P. Hall's *The Secret Teachings of All Ages*, and reproduces extended passages from William Preston's 1795 *Illustrations of Masonry* asserting Pythagoras's direct initiation into masonry and describing the transmission of his doctrine through the Druids.
