## Biographical Details from Source

Jay Stratton is credited by Elizondo as the person who recruited him into AATIP in early 2009, along with a colleague Elizondo calls "Rosemary Caine." Stratton co-founded AATIP within the OUSD(I) structure. He used the operational callsign "JAY" and personally investigated the USS Nimitz Tic Tac case.

Stratton coined the term "UAP" (unidentified aerial phenomena) as a deliberate replacement for "UFO" to reduce institutional stigma and make the subject analytically tractable within DoD analytical culture (Chapter 19). He also coined the term "hitchhiker effect" to describe the phenomenon of UAP-related anomalies following witnesses home after an encounter (Chapter 6).

Stratton developed OPLAN Interloper with Elizondo — a proposal to use a nuclear-capable asset as bait to draw UAP to a controlled engagement zone. The Joint Staff rejected the proposal.

After Elizondo's resignation, Stratton became the first director of the UAP Task Force (UAPTF), established in 2020 (Chapter 23). He sent Jay an email from the Pentagon when Pentagon public affairs told him they planned to deny Elizondo's AATIP role — Stratton told them that would be wrong and untrue.
