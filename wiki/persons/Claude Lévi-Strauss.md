## Biographical Details from Source

Claude Lévi-Strauss (1908–2009) is treated in *The Occult Mind* as the presiding theoretical
authority for [[Christopher I. Lehrich]]'s method. Lehrich invokes Lévi-Strauss as the anthropologist
whose name "by itself grants sufficient legitimacy" to the study of magic, and builds his own
comparative approach on Lévi-Strauss's structuralism — especially the four-volume *Mythologiques*, its
musical metaphors ("Overture" to "Finale"), and the mythographic method of analyzing myth as
transformations of a shared structure.

Lehrich reads Lévi-Strauss's structural method as itself close to a "magical" mode of comparison, and
uses it as the lens through which he analyzes tarot (see [[The Occult Mind: Magic in Theory and Practice (Lehrich)]],
ch. 5) and the general problem of comparison in the study of magic.
