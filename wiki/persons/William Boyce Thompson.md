## Biographical Details from Source

Per Sutton, William Boyce Thompson was "a director of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, a large stockholder in the Rockefeller-controlled Chase Bank, and a financial associate of the Guggenheims and the Morgans." Sutton states (substantiated by a cablegram) that Thompson "contributed $1 million to the Bolshevik Revolution for propaganda purposes." Thompson led and personally funded the American Red Cross Mission to Russia (1917) — "a private venture" through which "British policy was diverted towards the Lenin-Trotsky regime by the personal intervention of Thompson with Lloyd George in December 1917."

Griffin's account (citing the same J. P. Morgan cablegram transferring $1 million to Thompson via the National City Bank) presents Thompson as head of the Red Cross Mission before [[Raymond Robins]].
