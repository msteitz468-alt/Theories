## Biographical Details from Source

The astronomer who served as scientific (astronomical) consultant to the U.S. Air Force's UFO projects — Sign (1947), Grudge (1949), and Blue Book (1952–1969) — based at Wright-Patterson. As he recounts in his own *The UFO Experience* (1972), he began as a confident debunker ("the laughter of science") and gradually concluded that a residue of credible reports constitutes a real, unexplained phenomenon. He authored the standard UFO-report classification, coining the **Close Encounter** scale. In Vallée's account he also invited Vallée into UFO research (to apply computer science to Blue Book statistics) and, with Vallée, used the term "Invisible College" for the informal scientific network studying the phenomenon.

## Primary Source Appearances

Author/narrator of *The UFO Experience* (1972). Also a central figure in Vallée's *The Invisible College* (1975).
