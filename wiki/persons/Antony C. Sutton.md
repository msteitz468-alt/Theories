## Biographical Details from Source

Sutton is the author of *Wall Street and the Bolshevik Revolution*. Within the text he is identified (and in Griffin's citation) as a former Research Fellow at the Hoover Institution on War, Revolution and Peace and Professor of Economics at California State University, Los Angeles. His method is documentary, relying on U.S. State Department Decimal File records, British intelligence papers, and corporate director lists.

Distinctively among the authors in this cluster, Sutton frames the financiers' motive as profit and monopoly power ("an alliance of statists: statist revolutionaries and statist financiers") rather than ethnicity or ideology, and explicitly rejects the "Jewish-conspiracy theory" of the revolution.

In *America's Secret Establishment* (1983), Sutton turns the same documentary method on Yale's Skull & Bones, arguing The Order manipulates society via the Hegelian dialectic of managed conflict — and (consistent with his rejection of ethnic-conspiracy framing) attributing this to statist philosophy and class power, not race.

## Primary Source Appearances

Author throughout *Wall Street and the Bolshevik Revolution* and *America's Secret Establishment*. Also cited by [[G. Edward Griffin]] in *The Creature from Jekyll Island*.
