## Biographical Details from Source

Claude Vorilhon is a young French journalist and racing enthusiast from Clermont-Ferrand who, on
December 13, 1973, claimed to encounter a small, glowing humanoid that descended in a craft in
the old volcano country near his home. Over half a dozen morning meetings the being — whose suit
bore a symbol combining the swastika and the Star of David — renamed him "**Raël**" and dictated
commentaries on the Bible, published in 1974 as *The Message Given by the Extraterrestrials*. The
2008 foreword notes the Raëlian movement Vorilhon founded "has flourished internationally and
burst into prominence… with claims of human cloning."

## Doctrine Reported in the Source

Vorilhon's message: humanity was created by scientists of an advanced space civilization (the
**Elohim**), who split into camps led by Jahweh and Satan; we live in the "Last Days of an Age."
To progress, mankind must make drastic political changes — Vallée quotes the instruction to
"eliminate elections and votes," create "a world government and a new monetary system," adopt "a
single language," and end military service, after which the Elohim will return. In a later book,
*Beings From Outer Space Took Me to their Planet*, Vorilhon describes being given six robot
girls on the Elohim planet who "submitted to all my desires."

Vallée notes the contradictions Vorilhon never resolves (why pick a prophet from "the country of
democracy" only to abolish democracy; why use a terrestrial swastika/Star-of-David insignia) and
treats him as a case where contactee revelation brings a dangerously "simple" answer — comparing
the appeal to Hitler's "final solution." He quotes Vorilhon's own confession: "In the light of
these revelations, everything is cleared up and appears simple."
