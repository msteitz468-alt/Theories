## Biographical Details from Source

Born 1748 in Ingolstadt to Jewish parents (his father possibly a Cabalist rabbi), raised by his godfather Baron von Ickstatt and educated by the Jesuits, against whom he rebelled, converting to Protestantism. As a professor of canon law at the University of Ingolstadt he founded the Order of the Bavarian Illuminati on May 1, 1776, taking the code name "Spartacus." Influenced by a Gnostic-Manichean merchant called Kolmer and steeped in the ancient mysteries, he built a compartmentalized order modeled on the Jesuits and Freemasonry. Marrs records his quarrels with [[Adolph Knigge]], his authoritarian secrecy ("the great strength of our Order lies in its concealment"), and a scandalous personal life. After the 1784 ban he fled Bavaria and lived out his life in exile.
