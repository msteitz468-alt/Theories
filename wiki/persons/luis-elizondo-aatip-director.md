## Biographical Details from Source

Luis Elizondo was born to a Cuban father who fled Cuba after the revolution and a mother of Native American (Cherokee) heritage. His father's story of escaping Castro's Cuba shaped his worldview about authoritarian information suppression. His mother died of cancer during his AATIP tenure.

Elizondo served in the US Army in counterintelligence and special operations, working with classified programs including Grey Fox (Special Mission Unit) and Project Stargate remote viewing (with Gene Lessman). He eventually transitioned to the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence (OUSD(I)).

In early 2009, he was recruited into AATIP by Jay Stratton and a colleague he calls "Rosemary Caine." He became the program's director and ran it from Pentagon office 3C503A until his resignation on October 4, 2017. He obtained what he describes as "superuser" clearance — access to all classification levels — enabling him to review the full spectrum of UAP-related programs.

He resigned in protest over the DoD's failure to take the UAP threat seriously, delivering a resignation letter to SECDEF James Mattis. He immediately joined To The Stars Academy (TTSA) with Tom DeLonge, Hal Puthoff, and Steve Justice. After the December 2017 New York Times revelations, he became a public face of UAP disclosure advocacy. He was fired from TTSA just before Christmas 2020. He went on to consult with members of Congress on UAP legislation through 2023. His wife Jennifer suffered a traumatic brain injury after being struck by a car, and the family experienced significant financial hardship during the post-resignation period, including living in an RV.

## Primary Source Appearances

*Imminent* is Elizondo's first-person memoir. He is the narrator throughout. His personal credibility rests on his documented government service, the DoD's initial (later retracted) confirmation of his AATIP role, and on independent corroboration by Christopher Mellon, Jay Stratton, and Brad Byers (SECDEF's office).
