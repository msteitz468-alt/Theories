## Biographical Details from Source

Harry Reid served as US Senator from Nevada and as Senate Majority Leader. He is credited by Elizondo as the primary congressional champion of UAP research funding: Reid, along with Senators Ted Stevens (Alaska) and Daniel Inouye (Hawaii), secured the $22 million appropriation that funded AAWSAP through the Defense Intelligence Agency.

Reid subsequently requested that AATIP be placed under Special Access Program protection, a request documented in official records (cited in the appendix with a URL to the DIA FOIA reading room). He is quoted in the appendix: "After looking into this, I came to the conclusion that there were reports . . . that there were actual materials that the government and the private sector had in their possession. It is extremely important that information about the discovery of physical materials or retrieved craft come out."

Reid died after President Biden signed the NDAA FY2023 UAP legislation in December 2022 — described by Elizondo as a moment of personal significance, Reid having lived to see the legislative culmination of the effort he initiated. Senator Schumer invoked Reid's legacy when sponsoring the 2023 UAP Disclosure Act.
