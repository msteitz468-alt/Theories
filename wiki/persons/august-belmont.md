## Biographical Details from Source

Per Mullins and Griffin, August Belmont came to the United States in 1837 as the financial agent of the Rothschilds. Mullins states he was born "August Schoenberg" and "changed his name to Belmont when he came to the United States as the representative of the Rothschilds," because "it was extremely advantageous to them to have an American representative who was not known as a Rothschild agent" — amid "a considerable anti-Rothschild movement" in Europe and America.

Griffin's account agrees: Belmont "funneled vast amounts of capital into American investments, often without anyone knowing whose money he was spending," and "when his affiliation became commonly known ... he was replaced by J.P. Morgan."
