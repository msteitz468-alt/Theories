## Biographical Details from Source

Mullins presents Colonel House as Wilson's "unofficial" intermediary, recommended to Wilson by Paul Warburg, who functioned as the channel between the White House and the New York bankers. The source quotes *The Intimate Papers of Colonel House* to document House's repeated meetings with Warburg, J. P. Morgan Jr., and McAdoo while the currency bill was being prepared (entries from December 1912 through November 1913), and states it was House who gave Warburg's name to Wilson as a candidate for the first Federal Reserve Board.

Mullins quotes House's memoir that he and Wilson knew they had created in the Federal Reserve "an instrument more powerful than the Supreme Court," and (citing Rabbi Wise) characterizes House as effectively "our unofficial President during the Wilson years," representing the US at Versailles.
