## Biographical Details from Source

Austin Osman Spare is named in *Liber Null & Psychonaut* as a primary influence on
[[Chaos Magic]]. Carroll describes him as the "sometime pupil" of [[Aleister Crowley]] who
"discovered forms of practical magic that dispensed with virtually all supposedly ancient symbols
and ideas and used the contents of the personal subconscious instead," forming the basis of what
"Spare called the Zos Kia Cultus."

The source draws on Spare-derived techniques — notably **sigil magic** (encoding desire into an
abstract glyph and firing it into the subconscious past the censorship of conscious "lust of
result") and **the Death Posture** — which Carroll systematizes within his chaos-magic syllabi. The
terms **Kia** and **Zos** in the book's metaphysics derive from Spare's vocabulary.
