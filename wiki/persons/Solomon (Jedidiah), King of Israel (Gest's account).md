**Biographical Details from Source**
In Kevin L. Gest's *The Secrets of Solomon's Temple*, the king known to biblical tradition as Solomon, son of David and Bathsheba, was named Jedidiah at birth (2 Samuel 12:24-25), with "Solomon" attributed to him only after his death (citing Dr. David Rohl). Gest argues the name "Solomon" derives from "Sol Amon" — Sol (Sun) and Amon (the hidden Sun, i.e. the Moon) — making the Jerusalem Temple originally the "Temple of Sol and Amon," dedicated to Sun and Moon worship. Gest portrays Solomon/Jedidiah as having built high places for the gods Chemosh and Molech for his foreign wives (1 Kings 11:7-8), implicating him in the Moloch cult and its associated child-sacrifice rites described in Chapter Twelve, and as having presided over a Temple dedication ceremony (2 Chronicles 7) that Gest speculates may itself have involved such sacrifice, alongside a large-scale animal sacrifice of 42,000 animals. Gest also attributes to Solomon/Jedidiah "educated" wisdom in astronomy, geometry, and botany rather than innate insight, reflected in his reputed knowledge of "plant life... animals and birds, reptiles and fish" (1 Kings 4).

**Primary Source Appearances**
- Chapter Twelve ("Who Was Solomon? What Was Moloch?") establishes the Jedidiah/Sol Amon identification and the Moloch-worship connection.
- Chapter Thirteen ("Revealing the Design of Solomon's Temple") treats Solomon/Jedidiah as the commissioning builder (distinct from David as architect and Huram-Abi as craftsman) of the Temple reconstructed via Vesica Piscis geometry.
- Chapter Sixteen ("The Closing") restates the Jedidiah identification as the resolution of Gest's inquiry: "It was called Solomon's Temple after the death of the builder: Jedidiah, son of David."
