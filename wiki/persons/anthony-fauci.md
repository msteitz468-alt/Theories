## Biographical Details from Source

**Dr. Anthony Fauci** is the central subject of *[[Faucian Bargain (The Most Powerful and Dangerous Bureaucrat in American History)]]*. The book identifies him as director of the **National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID)**, the lead public face of the U.S. COVID-19 response under Presidents Trump and Biden, "the highest-paid employee in the entire U.S. federal government" (citing Forbes), and previously "the nation's leading infectious disease expert" during the 1980s HIV/AIDS era. The book is named for him but stresses he is presented as a representative figure, "the symptom" of an administrative state, not its mastermind.

> Note (strict-extraction): this page records only how the **source** portrays Fauci. *Faucian Bargain* is an adversarial polemic; its characterizations are contested and are catalogued here as the source's claims, not as adjudicated fact.

## Portrayal in the Source

The book casts Fauci as an unelected bureaucrat who accumulated unprecedented, unaccountable power over American life and used shifting, media-driven, and at times admittedly dishonest guidance. Specific charges it levels:

- **Self-contradiction** on virtually every COVID issue — risk level, masks, asymptomatic spread, the Imperial College/IHME models, and herd immunity (see [[Fauci repeatedly contradicted himself on COVID-19]]).
- An **unexplained reversal** from his February 28, 2020 *NEJM* "akin to severe seasonal influenza" comparison to his March 11 "10 times more lethal than the flu" testimony (see [[Fauci's February-to-March 2020 reversal on COVID severity was never explained]]).
- Admitting he **lied about masks** early on to preserve supply.
- Becoming a **de facto president** when Trump deferred to him (see [[Fauci became an unaccountable de facto president, and Trump's failure to fire him cost his reelection]]).
- His NIAID's **funding of gain-of-function research at the Wuhan Institute of Virology** (see [[Fauci's NIAID funded gain-of-function research at the Wuhan Institute of Virology, and the lab-leak origin was prematurely dismissed]]).
- Publicly **legitimizing [[Andrew Cuomo]]** despite the New York nursing-home scandal.
- Serving as the cultural **"high priest"** of what the authors call the [[The Branch Covidian Cult]].

The book concedes Fauci "has said things the science agrees with" at times, and offers "only two possible explanations" for his conduct — deliberate manipulation or incompetence — calling "both... bad."
