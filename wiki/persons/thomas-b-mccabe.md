## Biographical Details from Source

Mullins (Ch. 16) presents McCabe — former President of the Scott Paper Company (which the source repeatedly calls "the Toilet-Paper Trust") — as the man who replaced [[Marriner Eccles]] as Chairman of the Federal Reserve Board after Eccles clashed with the Truman–Snyder approach to the war-bond debt. Per the source (citing Drew Pearson), McCabe agreed to go along with the Truman–Snyder plan to wipe out the $160 billion in war bonds held by citizens and outside banks through inflation.

The source charges that as head of the Foreign Liquidation Commission (1945) McCabe disposed of $12 billion in surplus Army goods, much of it "on the black markets of the world," and recounts Senate hearings alleging black-market dealings under his supervision — including the sale of donated American blood plasma and of narcotics from first-aid kits — yet his appointment as Federal Reserve Chairman was confirmed. Mullins notes McCabe resigned in March 1951 and was replaced by William McChesney Martin, whose father had been a close associate of Paul Warburg in the [[American Acceptance Council]].
