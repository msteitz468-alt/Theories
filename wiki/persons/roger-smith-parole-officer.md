## Biographical Details from Source

Charles Manson's San Francisco parole officer and "one of the most vital figures" in O'Neill's investigation, because he watched the Family form. Smith (no relation to David E. Smith) was simultaneously a criminology/drug researcher — an expert on "gangs, collective behavior, violence, and drugs" — who ran the **Amphetamine Research Project (ARP)** out of the Haight-Ashbury Free Medical Clinic, funded by the NIMH (later acknowledged as a CIA LSD-research front). O'Neill documents that Smith "acted more as Manson's guardian" than a parole officer: he could have revoked Manson's parole at any time but did not, took custody of Manson's infant son, and submitted concealed leniency pleas for jailed Family members. His ARP files were reportedly stolen just after Manson's arrest.

## Source-Specific Portrait

In a 1969 *Life* essay Smith called Manson "the most hostile parolee I've ever come across," yet in official documents reported Manson making "excellent progress" — a disparity O'Neill reads as Smith sweeping Manson's hostility "under the rug."
