## Biographical Details from Source

Giordano Bruno (1548–1600) is the central figure of *The Forbidden Universe*'s Part One (Chapter 2,
"The Hermetic Messiah"). Following Frances Yates, Picknett & Prince present Bruno not as a
proto-scientific martyr for heliocentrism in the modern sense, but as a **Hermetic magus** whose
championing of an infinite universe and a moving Earth was driven by his religious devotion to the
[[Hermes Trismegistus|Hermetic]] tradition — which he sought to restore as a universal reformed
religion (the authors discuss his "Giordanisti" project). He was burned at the stake by the Roman
Inquisition in 1600.

The source argues Bruno's cosmology and mission make him a pivotal link between the Renaissance
Hermetic revival and the new astronomy, and a key exhibit for its thesis that the occult, not pure
rationalism, lay at the root of the Scientific Revolution. (Detailed claims are extracted on the
chapter pages.)
