**Biographical Details from Source**

Nancy Lea Honeycutt (later McMoneagle) is described by Campbell in the acknowledgements as "not only a fellow traveler but also the primary enabler of Monroe's success." She was the stepdaughter of Robert Monroe.

She joined the research team at Whistlefield Research Laboratories after graduating from college, making her participation contemporaneous with Campbell's and Dennis Mennerich's early years of research in the early 1970s. Campbell describes her initial contribution: "Nancy Lea, who had joined our research team after her graduation from college, orchestrated and administered all the necessary arrangements" for the first large experimental seminar. She also provided practical technical assistance: "with the help of Bill Yost and Bob's stepdaughter, Nancy Lea Honeycutt, we had the equipment installed, checked out, and ready to go."

Campbell describes her full integration into the research team: "Nancy Lea had joined the research effort with Dennis and me after her graduation from college and soon became an integral part of the team, collecting evidence, testing concepts, participating in singular as well as joint explorations — even soldering wires on occasion." As Campbell and Mennerich reduced their availability due to family obligations, Nancy Lea took on increasing responsibility: "She began to carry more and more of the workload as Dennis and I reached and passed our limits of available time."

She became a full partner in NPMR exploration: Campbell states that Nancy Lea "became a full partner in our explorations of nonphysical reality (as had her sister, Penny Honeycutt, a few years before)." Campbell describes joint out-of-body explorations with her, including remote viewing experiments: "Nancy Lea and I shared equally astonishing joint experiences. We tried other things as well. We read three and four digit numbers written on a blackboard next to the control room. Somebody would write a random number and we would read it while our bodies lay asleep."

Ultimately, Nancy Lea took over organizational leadership: "Nancy Lea took over the seminar operations and after a few years of successfully building and managing the business, she became the director of The Monroe Institute of Applied Sciences." In the acknowledgements, Campbell also thanks her "for aiding and corroborating the accuracy of my memories of the early years at Whistlefield."

**Note on Name**

The source text uses "Nancy Lea Honeycutt" and "Nancy Lea McMoneagle" (the latter in the acknowledgements section). The dedication page of *My Big TOE* includes "To Dennis & Nancy" and "To Dennis & Nancy Lea," suggesting close association with both Mennerich and Monroe's extended family.

**Primary Source Appearances**

Nancy Lea appears throughout Book 1, Section 1 (the autobiographical chapters) of *My Big TOE*, including the narrative of the Tuckahoe Motel experiment, the NPMR joint explorations, and the institutional development of the Monroe Institute.
