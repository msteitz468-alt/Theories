**Biographical Details from Source**

Enlil ("Lord of the Airspace") is, per Sitchin, the **legal firstborn** of [[Anu (Sitchin)]] by his consort Antu, and the second-most-powerful god of the Sumerian pantheon — outranking his half-brother and rival [[Enki (Sitchin)]] in succession though arriving on Earth later. Sitchin places his arrival ~400,000 years ago, when Enlil **established Nippur as the mission's "command post" / Mission Control Center**. He fathers a "rightful heir" (Ninurta) by his half-sister Ninhursag. In the later narrative Enlil turns against mankind: "disenchanted" by humanity's proliferation and noise, he **seeks its demise**, and it is Enlil who imposes the gods' oath to conceal the coming Deluge from the Earthlings so that mankind should perish — the act Enki then subverts.

**Primary Source Appearances**

Chapters 3–4 (pantheon and rank), 9–10 (Nippur, the cities of the gods), 11–13 (his opposition to man and his role before the Deluge).
