**Biographical Details from Source**

Dagobert II was the last effective Merovingian king of Austrasia. He was married to a Visigoth princess, and Rennes-le-Château (then part of Visigoth territory) was reportedly a sort of royal treasury during his reign; documents speak of great wealth he amassed and concealed in the environs of Rennes-le-Château. This connection is encoded in the parchments Saunière discovered: "To Dagobert II, King, and to Sion belongs this treasure and he is there dead."

He was assassinated on December 23, 679, while sleeping under a tree near Stenay (then Sathanacum), stabbed through the eye by a lance wielded by a servant in the pay of Pépin d'Héristal. The source alleges the assassination was endorsed or abetted by the Roman Church.

Subsequently, Dagobert II was systematically excluded from official Frankish histories for centuries. The source argues this erasure was deliberate — the Carolingians and the Church needed to bury the evidence of their participation in the murder. He was eventually canonized as a saint, his feast day December 23. The source finds his canonization suspicious given the alleged Church complicity in his death.

The name "Dagobert" and references to Stenay recur throughout the "Prieuré documents," which the source reads as evidence that the Prieuré de Sion was founded partly to avenge Dagobert and preserve his descendants' bloodline.
