**Biographical Details from Source**
Joseph H. Peterson is the editor of the 2001 Weiser Books critical edition of
*[[The Lesser Key of Solomon (Peterson)|The Lesser Key of Solomon]]* (*Lemegeton Clavicula Salomonis*). Working directly from
British Library manuscripts (studying them firsthand and preparing microfilm copies), he collated the
text principally from Sloane 3825, with variants noted from Sloane 2731, Sloane 3648, and Harley 6483.
His Introduction reconstructs the textual history of the compilation — dating the assembly of the five
books to the seventeenth century, tracing the Ars Notoria as the oldest component (a medieval work),
and identifying Johann Weyer's *Pseudomonarchia Daemonum* (1577) as a direct ancestor of the Goetia's
spirit list, which he reprints as an appendix.
