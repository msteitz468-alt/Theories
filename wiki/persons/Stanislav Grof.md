## Biographical Details from Source

Stanislav Grof (b. 1931) is a Czech-born psychiatrist, a founder of **transpersonal psychology**, and one of the most important researchers of non-ordinary ("**holotropic**") states of consciousness. From thousands of psychedelic (LSD) therapy sessions and, later, his drug-free **holotropic breathwork** method (developed with Christina Grof), he mapped a "cartography of the psyche" extending beyond the biographical to **perinatal** (birth-related) and **transpersonal** realms. The book *Stanislav Grof, LSD Pioneer* by Brigitte Grof presents his life and work. He is repeatedly cited elsewhere in the wiki as an influence on John Mack's abduction method and on Graham Hancock.

## Primary Source Appearances
