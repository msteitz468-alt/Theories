## Biographical Details from Source

George Adamski is presented in *Messengers of Deception* as the archetypal early UFO contactee,
famous for his claimed November 20, 1952 desert meeting with a "Venusian" with long blond hair.
Vallée treats him as a "false professor… meetings with Venusian spacemen" example of the
"apparently harmless hoaxes" within the larger deception. He contrasts Adamski's unwitnessed
encounter with the more substantive (though still doubtful) case of [[Jacques Bordas]].

## Connections Reported in the Source

Vallée reports, "according to some of my own informants," that Adamski "had prewar connections
with American fascist leader [[William Dudley Pelley]]," who may have put fellow contactee
[[George Hunt Williamson]] in touch with Adamski; Williamson is said to have witnessed Adamski's
1952 desert contact. Vallée also reports that Adamski "gave credit to four U.S. government
scientists" (from the Point Loma Naval Electronics Laboratory and a Pasadena facility) for
launching his career, that his major foreign supporter was a former British Army intelligence
officer, and that on his Australia tour he allegedly traveled "with a passport bearing special
privileges" — details Vallée cites as consistent with the "Martian Conspiracy" hypothesis that
some contactees were set up to propagate space-brother messages.

## Strict-extraction note

These connections are reported by Vallée from unnamed informants and as supporting a hypothesis
he labels speculative; they are recorded here as his account, not as established fact.
