## Biographical Details from Source

Mullins is the author of *A Study of the Federal Reserve and its Secrets* and its expanded edition *Secrets of the Federal Reserve: The London Connection*. In the Preface to the former he states that in the fall of 1949 he went to the Library of Congress to gather material for a newspaper article on the Federal Reserve Board of Governors, and that the inquiry expanded into a research project of nineteen months. He found, he writes, that "there existed not one narrative account of the origins and activities of this powerful organization," and assembled his information piecemeal from periodicals, bankers' memoirs, and congressional hearings. The Preface is signed "E. MULLINS, Jan. 12, 1952."

The Foreword to the later edition gives the commissioning story: the research was undertaken at the request of the poet **Ezra Pound**, then held without trial as a political prisoner at St. Elizabeth's Hospital, who paid Mullins ten dollars a week to investigate the Federal Reserve. Mullins states Pound had broadcast from Italy urging Americans not to enter World War II and that Franklin D. Roosevelt "personally ordered Pound's indictment."

The text is written in the first person and advances a single sustained thesis (see [[A Study of the Federal Reserve and its Secrets (Mullins)]]).
