## Biographical Details from Source

The source introduces Baudouin I as king of Jerusalem at the time the Knights Templar was founded. His elder brother, Godfroi de Bouillon, had captured the Holy City nineteen years before. According to the source's account — drawing on Guillaume de Tyre — when Hugues de Payen and eight companions "unsolicited, presented himself … at the palace of Baudouin I … Baudouin seems to have received them most cordially, as did the Patriarch of Jerusalem." The king placed "an entire wing of the royal palace at the knights' disposal." This palace was said to be built on the foundations of the ancient Temple of Solomon, from which the Order derived its name.

**Relationship with the Prieuré de Sion**

A "Prieuré document" cited by the source — the "Cercle d'Ulysse" pamphlet — states that "In March 1177 Baudouin was compelled, at Saint Leonard d'Acre, to negotiate and prepare the constitution of the Order of the Temple, under the directives of the Prieuré de Sion." The source does not elaborate on this particular claim but quotes it as part of the document's account of the relationship between Sion and the Temple.

**Brother of Godfroi de Bouillon**

The source identifies Baudouin I explicitly as the younger brother of Godfroi de Bouillon. The source's wider argument traces Godfroi — and by extension Baudouin — to the Merovingian bloodline and, according to the authors' central hypothesis, to the lineage descended from Jesus.
