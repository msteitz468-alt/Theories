## Biographical Details from Source

John Archibald Wheeler (1911–2008), described by the book as "widely regarded as the greatest theoretical physicist of modern times" after Einstein. Wheeler coined the terms "black hole" and "wormhole," studied under both Niels Bohr and Einstein, worked with Bohr and Enrico Fermi on atomic bomb theory, and collaborated with Hawking. He is cited as the physicist who takes the observer effect to its most radical conclusion — the "participatory anthropic principle" (PAP).

Per the authors, Wheeler's "delayed choice" thought-experiment (later confirmed experimentally by Vincent Jacques's French team in 2006) demonstrated that an observer's present decision about how to detect a photon determines how it behaved in the past — up to billions of years ago in the cosmic version. Wheeler concluded: "our very act of measurement not only revealed the nature of the photon's history on its way to us, but in some sense determined that history. The past history of the universe has no more validity than is assigned by the measurements we make — now!"

From this, Wheeler formulated the participatory anthropic principle: "observership is the mechanism of genesis." The universe requires conscious observers to exist; without them, its wave function never collapses. He declared: "We are participators in bringing into being not only the near and here but the far away and long ago." The book presents this as an independent scientific rediscovery of the Hermetic teaching that the cosmos is the thought of God, and notes Wheeler's own explicit connection of his work to Leibniz (himself a closet Hermeticist whose hero was [[Giordano Bruno]]).

Wheeler considered the multiverse "unscientific speculation" carrying "too much metaphysical baggage." Despite his PAP theory being radically pro-consciousness, he was fiercely opposed to parapsychology and tried to expel the Parapsychological Association from the AAAS — the authors note the irony that "his own ideas seem even weirder than the most incontinent claims of the paranormalists."
