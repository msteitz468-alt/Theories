## Biographical Details from Source

The source mentions Arnaud-Amaury only briefly and without naming him directly: he appears as "the pope's representative" in the account of the Albigensian Crusade. In the town of Béziers in 1209, according to the source, at least 15,000 men, women and children were slaughtered, many of them in the sanctuary of the church itself. "When an officer inquired of the pope's representative how he might distinguish heretics from true believers, the reply was, 'Kill them all. God will recognise His own.'" The source notes this quotation "though widely reported, may be apocryphal. Nevertheless, it typifies the fanatical zeal and bloodlust with which the atrocities were perpetrated." The same papal representative subsequently wrote to Innocent III in Rome, "announcing proudly that 'neither age nor sex nor status was spared.'"

The source also notes that the Abbot of Cîteaux (a position held by Arnaud-Amaury at this period) was among the crusade's leading figures — a detail referenced in passing in the context of the Cistercian abbey and its relationship to the Templar order more broadly.

The source references the Abbot of Cîteaux at page 50.

## Primary Source Appearances
