## Biographical Details from Source

Jacques Bordas (born July 20, 1911) is, in Vallée's account, "one of the most remarkable European
contactees." A sickly, obese boy in Barcelona, he claimed that in August 1923, aged twelve, he
saw small triangular craft land on his terrace; a white-clad being his own size told him "we
have taken you under our protection… Now that we have adopted you, we will never forsake you,"
and gave him a dark candy. Over the next four years Bordas became physically strong, developed a
passion for mountains and science, became an expert climber (first Spaniard to climb Green
Needle, 1934), and a meteorologist. Vallée met him in Barcelona in 1975 through Spanish
researcher Antonio Ribera.

## Reported Encounters in the Source

After surviving an execution during the Spanish Civil War (his would-be killers "ran away in
abject terror"), Bordas claimed a third contact in 1951 at Casteil in the Pyrenees: a tall,
blond, blue-eyed "Stranger From Above" who bought milk and bread from him daily, paid in raw
gold from the Cadi River (verified by a jeweler in Perpignan), produced an impossibly detailed
map of Canigou mountain, and delivered a political message that "another species evolving now…
will replace [man]" and "will change everything, the social structure, religion." A 1971 phone
call promised Bordas a further "mutation." The being is said to have been unphotographable and to
have erased the memory of the village mayor, Mr. Nou.

## Vallée's Assessment

Vallée found Bordas "open-minded" and self-critical, unlike the fanatical contactees — Bordas
declined to frame his visitors as good-vs-evil "Luciferian forces," calling them "simply forces
of a different type, which we are not able to comprehend." Vallée treats him as a man "whose life
had been bent out of shape at an early age by the apparent intervention of another life form,"
and compares his "biological transformation" to that promised by [[Human Individual Metamorphosis (H.I.M.)]].
Local investigation (mayor Nou, restaurateur Cases) neither confirmed nor refuted the story.
