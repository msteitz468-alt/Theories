## Description from Source

Len Filppu is a real, named individual who contacted [[D. W. Pasulka]] after reading her earlier UFO research. He is a former atheist whose series of anomalous experiences — beginning with a 1967 teenage UFO sighting in Brockport, New York — gradually transformed his worldview and led him to describe himself as "spiritual but not religious." Pasulka features him across Chapters 9 and 10 of *Encounters* as a paradigm case of conversion from secular materialism to a sense of cosmically embedded meaning.

**Background**: Filppu grew up in the post-war "American eyes skyward" culture of the 1950s. He studied journalism and went on to work in media, politics (he served as traveling state press secretary for the 1976 Jimmy Carter presidential campaign and worked for Representative Andrew Maguire in Congress), and New York film production (unit publicist on *Fear No Evil*, 1979).

**The 1967 Brockport sighting**: At age sixteen, on an overnight outdoor sleep-out with friends, Filppu witnessed a large, silent, orange spherical object with trailing flames and a small white orb following behind it. The sighting lasted roughly thirty seconds and was confirmed by more than thirty independent witnesses in nearby towns, two Brockport police officers, two Monroe County sheriff's deputies, and reported in the *Rochester Democrat and Chronicle*. NICAP (National Investigations Committee on Aerial Phenomena) confirmed it as a genuine unidentified aerial object and ruled out a bolide. Filppu describes the moment as the beginning of a permanent shift in his worldview, including a loss of trust in institutional authorities.

**The Fulton Sheen vision (1979)**: After a period of hard drinking in New York City, Filppu underwent what he calls a "dark night of the soul" and silently called out to the universe for help. He then received a clear, full-color waking inner vision of Archbishop Fulton J. Sheen's face, which rose slowly upward and out of view. The next morning he read in the *New York Times* that Sheen had died in his private chapel the previous night. Filppu credits the synchronicity with triggering a permanent change in his sense of reality.

**Vivid dream career**: Filppu reports a series of distinctive, colorful, lucid dreams that he distinguishes from ordinary dreaming. One involved a deceased friend, John, appearing at his bedside to ask him to relay a message to another man in a recovery group. When Filppu attended the meeting the next morning, the man with the "odd hat" was present and confirmed he had been waiting to hear from John.

**Self-description**: Filppu calls his ongoing spiritual journey "Len's Magical Mystery Tour, with a tip of the hat to the Beatles." He describes himself as content to "live with the mystery" while remaining committed to sharing his experiences so scholars and journalists will investigate with "more vigor, open-mindedness, and honesty."

Pasulka notes that Filppu made initial contact by sending her an essay published in *Busted Halo*, a Catholic online periodical, about his Fulton Sheen encounter, prefaced by the question: "Do you think there is a UFO connection here?" He attended the Archives of the Impossible conference at Rice University, where Pasulka first met him in person.
