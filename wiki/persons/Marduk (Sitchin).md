**Biographical Details from Source**

Marduk (MAR.DUK, "son of the pure mound") is, per Sitchin, the firstborn of [[Enki (Sitchin)]] by his wife Damkina, who rose to become Babylon's supreme national god. Sitchin's key move is to read the *Enuma Elish* (the Babylonian "Epic of Creation") on **two registers at once**: Marduk is both a god and the **invading planet** — the body Sitchin identifies with [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]] — that collides with the watery planet Tiamat. As that planet, "Marduk" arrives from outer space, passes Neptune, Uranus, Saturn and Jupiter (acquiring satellites — the "winds"), and its satellites smash Tiamat in two, creating Earth and the asteroid belt ([[Earth and the asteroid belt are the remains of a planet (Tiamat) shattered in a celestial collision]]). Sitchin treats the elevation of "Marduk" as the victor as a later Babylonian political overlay onto an older Sumerian tradition in which the home planet itself (Nibiru) is the actor.

**Primary Source Appearances**

Chapters 6–7 ("The Twelfth Planet"; "The Epic of Creation") — the celestial battle and the dismemberment of Tiamat.
