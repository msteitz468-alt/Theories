## Biographical Details from Source

Per Griffin, Raymond Robins chaired the Progressive Party convention in Chicago in 1912 and "later became head of the American Red Cross Mission in Russia after the Bolshevik revolution." Although he "represented Wall Street interests, he was a disciple of [[Cecil Rhodes]] and was anti-capitalist in his beliefs," and "held great influence over Lenin." The source presents the Red Cross Mission as a cover for Wall Street financiers seeking commercial concessions from the new Soviet government, financing "all factions of the revolutionary movement to be sure of gaining influence with whatever group should come out on top."
