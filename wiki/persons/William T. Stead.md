## Biographical Details from Source

Per Quigley, William T. Stead (1849–1912) was "England's most sensational journalist," an "ardent social reformer and imperialist" who brought the Ruskinite Oxford and Cambridge disciples into association with Cecil Rhodes. On February 5, 1891, "Rhodes and Stead organized a secret society of which Rhodes had been dreaming for sixteen years," with Rhodes as leader and Stead, Brett (Lord Esher), and Milner forming the executive committee. Stead also recruited members in Australia and New Zealand through his magazine *The Review of Reviews*. Quigley notes Stead introduced Flora Shaw to Rhodes in 1889.
