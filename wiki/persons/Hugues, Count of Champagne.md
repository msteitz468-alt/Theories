## Biographical Details from Source

According to the source, Hugues de Payen, the founding knight of the Knights Templar, was a nobleman from Champagne and a vassal of the Count of Champagne. The source emphasises the significance of the Count of Champagne (Hugues) in several ways:

The Bishop of Chartres addressed a letter to the Count of Champagne in 1114 — prior to the official founding of the Temple in 1118 — referring to a "militia of Christ" and a journey to Jerusalem. At least three of the nine founding knights of the Temple, including Hugues de Payen, appear to have had family ties to each other and were all vassals of the same lord: the Count of Champagne. In 1115 the Count of Champagne donated the land on which Saint Bernard built the famous Abbey of Clairvaux. One of the nine founding knights, André de Montbard, was Saint Bernard's uncle.

Most strikingly, the source states that the Count of Champagne himself became a Templar in 1124, "pledging obedience to his own vassal" (Hugues de Payen, whom he had effectively patronised into the Grand Mastership). The source describes this as one of several "singular coincidences" suggesting a premeditated and concealed design behind the founding of the Order.

The "Dossiers secrets" cited by the source list the founders of the Temple as "Hugues de Payen, Bisol de St. Omer and Hugues, Comte de Champagne, along with certain members of the Ordre de Sion." This makes the Count of Champagne a named co-founder — a detail the authors note they had not seen before encountering the Dossiers secrets. The source states that the Council of Troyes, at which the Templars were officially recognised in January 1128, was convened "at Troyes — court of the count of Champagne."

The source describes Hugues, Count of Champagne, André de Montbard (Saint Bernard's uncle) and Hugues de Payen as constituting "a vital link" — three individuals who "constitute a vital link. They are like markers breaking the surface of history, indicating the dim configurations of some elaborate, concealed design."
