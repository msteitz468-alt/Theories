## Biographical Details from Source

A Vietnam veteran who, after immersing himself in California's late-1970s New Age / human-potential scene, authored the *First Earth Battalion Operations Manual* — a vision of the soldier as a peaceful "warrior monk." Ronson presents Channon as the well-meaning idealist at the root of the whole story: he proposed battlefield uniforms with "ginseng regulators" and loudspeakers emitting "indigenous music and words of peace," and entering hostile areas carrying baby lambs. Offered command of a real First Earth Battalion at Fort Knox (1979), he declined — preferring his ideas to "float out there and take root wherever fate decreed," assimilated into the army "without anyone really knowing." He later did the same motivational work for corporations from his Hawaiian estate.

## Source-Specific Portrait

Ronson notes that although Channon was "filled with the kindest of intentions and thoughts of peace," he was "the inspiration behind a really quite bizarre form of torture undertaken by US forces in Iraq" — an irony central to the book.
