## Biographical Details from Source

Joseph Campbell (1904–1987) was the American comparative mythologist whose *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* (1949) introduced the **monomyth** — the claim that the hero myths of all cultures are local variations of one universal narrative pattern, the **hero's journey** of departure, initiation, and return. Drawing on Jung's archetypes, Freud, and a vast comparative survey of world mythology and religion, Campbell reads myth as a symbolic language of the psyche and of humanity's relationship to the cosmos. (Not to be confused with Thomas Campbell, author of *My Big TOE*.)
