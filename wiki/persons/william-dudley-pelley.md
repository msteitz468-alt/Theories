## Biographical Details from Source

William Dudley Pelley (died 1965) appears in *Messengers of Deception* as the figure linking
the early UFO contactee scene to American fascism. Vallée identifies him as the leader of the
**Silver Shirts**, "an American Nazi group which began its activities about 1932," whose
membership "overlapped strongly with Guy Ballard's 'I Am' movement." Pelley ran as a "Christian
Party" candidate in Indiana in 1936, opposed Roosevelt, and was interned for sedition in 1942
for eight years.

## Reported Activities in the Source

After the war Pelley founded the occult group **Soulcraft** (Noblesville, Indiana), published a
racist magazine called *Valor*, and wrote *Star Guests* (1950), "a compilation of automatic
writing reminiscent of the Seth Material." Vallée reports that around 1950 [[George Hunt Williamson]]
worked at Soulcraft Publications, and that Pelley "may have put Williamson in touch with"
[[George Adamski]] (possibly through their shared interest in the "I Am" cult). Vallée uses this
lineage — Pelley → Williamson/Adamski — to argue that several men who "have been influential in
shaping the UFO myth in the United States" had connections to extremist and racist organizations.

## Strict-extraction note

Pelley's links to the contactee scene are presented by Vallée partly on the basis of unnamed
informants and as evidence for his thesis about the political undertow of UFO belief; they are
recorded here as Vallée's account.
