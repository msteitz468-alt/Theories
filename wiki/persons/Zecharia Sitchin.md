## Biographical Details from Source

Zecharia Sitchin (1920–2010) was the author of *The Earth Chronicles*, a series advancing the thesis that humanity's gods were the **Nefilim/Anunnaki** — flesh-and-blood astronauts from a twelfth planet of the solar system, **Nibiru**, on a 3,600-year orbit — who came to Earth to mine gold and genetically fashioned modern humans as a workforce. Writing as a self-taught reader of Sumerian and other ancient Near-Eastern texts, Sitchin reinterprets Sumerian, Akkadian, Babylonian, Egyptian, biblical, and (in *The Lost Realms*) Mesoamerican sources as literal-historical records of these visitors.

The series runs: *The Twelfth Planet* (1976), *The Stairway to Heaven* (1980), *The Wars of Gods and Men* (1985), *The Lost Realms* (1990), *When Time Began* (1993), *The Cosmic Code* (1998), and *The End of Days* (2007), plus companion volumes. His readings are rejected by mainstream Assyriology, astronomy, and biology; per the wiki's strict-extraction policy his claims are catalogued **as presented**, without endorsement. Sitchin is the upstream source for much later conspiracist literature — note the distinct **reptilian** reframing in [[Anunnaki (Icke)]], which Sitchin's biological-humanoid account does not support.
