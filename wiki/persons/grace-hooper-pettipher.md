## Biographical Details from Source

Dr. Grace Hooper Pettipher is described in *Messengers of Deception* as "International Lecturer
and Representative" of the San Francisco branch of the [[Order of Melchizedek (contactee-occult network)]],
holding weekly meetings in the basement of the Hyatt on Union Square. Vallée and his wife Janine
attended (paying three dollars each) a lecture titled "Be Your Own Psychiatrist." He estimates
she "might be seventy," with "the energy of an adolescent girl" and a commanding stage presence.

## Teachings Reported in the Source

Pettipher preached reincarnation ("We've all met before, near the Mediterranean… People remember
meeting in Greece, in Atlantis"), claimed to have seen Atlantis materialize on Fifth Avenue in
New York, and distinguished ordinary "clairvoyance" from a higher "cosmo-voyance" of "the vision
and hearing of God." She taught "seven times seven aethers that form the garments of your soul,"
demanded that disciples renounce emotional and sexual involvement, and told Vallée's group that
"we even have some who've come from outside this planet tonight." She recounted a UFO sighting
and a Glastonbury vision of beings at a window.

Vallée uses her as his prime example of deliberately "absurd and confusing" mystical jargon that
"lulls the mind of the hearer into a semihypnotic state until it is ready to receive the real
message" — "she was using absurdity and confusion in the skilled way of a brainwashing expert."
