## Biographical Details from Source

British journalist (former East Africa correspondent for *The Economist*) turned author, whose *Fingerprints of the Gods* (1995) became the foundational popular text of modern "alternative history." Hancock argues for a forgotten advanced human civilization destroyed in a prehistoric cataclysm. He is careful to distinguish his thesis from the extraterrestrial claims of von Däniken and Sitchin: his "gods" are human survivors of a lost culture, not aliens.

His work spans two strands now represented in the wiki. The **lost-civilization** strand runs from *Fingerprints of the Gods* (1995) to its sequel *Magicians of the Gods* (2015), which re-grounds the cataclysm in the Younger Dryas comet impact and the evidence of Göbekli Tepe. The **consciousness / shamanism** strand is *Supernatural* (2005), which argues that altered states of consciousness — often induced by psychoactive plants — gave rise to symbolic culture, religion, and humanity's recurring encounters with non-human "teachers."

## Primary Source Appearances

Author/narrator throughout *Fingerprints of the Gods*, *Magicians of the Gods*, and *Supernatural*.
