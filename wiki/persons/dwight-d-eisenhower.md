## Biographical Details from Source

The president who gave the Dulles brothers (Allen at CIA, Foster at State) wide latitude and "a license to kill," presiding over the Iran and Guatemala coups and nuclear brinksmanship — "America's most covert president," per historian Blanche Wiesen Cook. Talbot argues Eisenhower came to regret his loss of control over Dulles, especially after the U-2 affair, and warned he would leave a "legacy of ashes."
