## Biographical Details from Source

Griffin presents Keynes as one of the two guiding theoreticians of the [[Event: Bretton Woods Conference (1944)]], identifying him as "the well-known Fabian Socialist from England." Per the source, the Fabians "agreed with Communists as to the goal of socialism but disagreed over tactics," favoring "gradualism and the transformation of society through legislation." Griffin pairs Keynes with [[Harry Dexter White]] as "a Fabian Socialist and a Communist, working together to bring about their mutual goal: world socialism" through the IMF/World Bank.

Griffin elsewhere cites Keynes on the boom-bust cycle and on bank reserves ("reserves must be used to the hilt"), and Mullins references "Lord Keynes" as a popularizer of managed currency.
