## Biographical Details from Source

**Keith Thomas** is an English social and intellectual historian of the early modern period. As identified in the front matter of *Religion and the Decline of Magic*, he was President of Corpus Christi College, Oxford and a former President of the British Academy, previously Professor of Modern History and Fellow of St John's College. He has written extensively on the social and intellectual history of the early modern period and is general editor of the Past Masters series (Oxford University Press) and of Oxford Studies in Social History (Clarendon Press).

*Religion and the Decline of Magic* (1971), his first book, won one of the two Wolfson Literary Awards for History in 1972. He is also the author of *Man and the Natural World: Changing Attitudes in England 1500–1800* and editor of *The Oxford Book of Work* (1999). He was knighted in 1988 for services to the study of history.
