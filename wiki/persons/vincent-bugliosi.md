## Biographical Details from Source

The lead prosecutor in the 1970–71 Tate–LaBianca trial and author of *Helter Skelter* (1974), "the most popular true-crime book ever." In *Chaos*, Bugliosi is the architect and chief defender of the official race-war motive. O'Neill portrays him as a "master orator" who, in a six-hour 2006 interview, threatened "a hundred-million-dollar libel lawsuit" and, per other reporters, had a history of intimidation. O'Neill says he found notes in Bugliosi's own handwriting suggesting a key witness lied under oath; co-prosecutor Stephen Kay called the notes potentially verdict-overturning. Bugliosi died in 2015.

## Primary Source Appearances

*Chaos*, Prologue and throughout (esp. Ch. 4, "The Holes in Helter Skelter").
