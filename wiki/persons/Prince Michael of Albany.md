## Biographical Overview from Source

Per [[Custodians of Truth (Wallace-Murphy & Hopkins)]], "Michael" is a middle-aged Englishman who approached [[Tim Wallace-Murphy]] after a talk on Rosslyn Chapel in 1994. He introduced himself by first name only and claimed descent from the family of Hugues de Payen ([[Hugues de Payen (Templar founder)]]), founder and first Grand Master of the Knights Templar. He subsequently demonstrated this lineage to Wallace-Murphy's satisfaction.

Michael identified himself as a member of a "widespread group of elect families" whose traditions — passed down through either the eldest child or the child of highest spiritual awareness — identify them as descendants of the Davidic and Hasmonean royal families and of the twenty-four families that were the hereditary high priests of the Temple of Jerusalem at the time of Jesus. Initiates into this tradition were made to swear: "May my heart be torn out or my throat be cut" if they disclosed the secret to any outsider.

The publication of [[The Holy Blood and the Holy Grail (Baigent, Leigh, Lincoln 1982)]] in 1982, Michael told Wallace-Murphy, released him from this oath of secrecy, as enough of the story had already entered the public domain.

### Contents of Michael's Account

Michael's disclosures formed the basis of the "Rex Deus hypothesis" that Wallace-Murphy and Hopkins developed in their earlier book *Rex Deus* (2000) and expanded in *Custodians of Truth* (2005). His account included:
- The existence of two Temple boarding schools for boys and girls
- The ritual impregnation of female pupils by the high priests, including the conception of Jesus by the high priest Gabriel and Mary
- The genealogies of the Rex Deus families originally inscribed on walls under the Temple
- Rex Deus connections with important families in Byzantium
- The heraldic colors of the group: green and gold
- The 4th-century reburial of the body of Jesus on the Temple Mount

After Wallace-Murphy and Hopkins published *Rex Deus*, they received a letter from a separate correspondent (unnamed in the source) indicating their account was "incomplete" and offering the remainder of the story. This additional source is distinct from Michael and is not named in *Custodians of Truth*.

**Note:** The source does not confirm whether "Michael" is Prince Michael of Albany specifically; this page reflects the identification as used in the Rex Deus literature but the source refers to him only as "Michael."
