## Biographical Overview from Source

Per [[Custodians of Truth (Wallace-Murphy & Hopkins)]], the historical Moses was almost certainly Egyptian-born and a full member of the Egyptian royal family — a conclusion the authors reach by synthesizing scholars including Sigmund Freud, Ahmed Osman, Robert Feather, Manetho, Philo Judaeus, Flavius Josephus, and Justin Martyr.

### Egyptian Origins

The authors note that the name Moses derives from the Egyptian *Mos*, meaning simply "child," and that Freud demonstrated the birth narrative of Moses (hidden in a reed bed and found by a princess) was copied from earlier myths of Sargon and Horus. Freud concluded that Moses' "humble origins" story was a later fabrication to disguise his royal Egyptian identity. Robert Feather is quoted: "Moses was not only born and raised as an Egyptian, but was, in fact, a Prince of Egypt — a son of the Royal House of Pharaoh."

### Identification with Akhenaten

The source presents Ahmed Osman's identification of Moses with the pharaoh [[Akhenaten]] as the most plausible hypothesis, following the earlier suggestion of Dr. Karl Abraham. In this reading:
- Moses/Akhenaten founded Atenism — a strict monotheism abolishing the Amun pantheon
- His expulsion/flight from Egypt following the counter-revolution of Horemheb constitutes the Exodus
- The religion he transmitted to the Israelites was Atenism effectively unchanged

### Transmission of Gnosis

The authors argue that Moses, as an Egyptian initiate, transmitted the hereditary priestly gnosis of Egypt — ultimately derived from the tradition of [[Tep Zepi (the First Time)]] — to the Levitic priesthood. The Levitic hereditary priestly clan is described as an extension of the Atenist priesthood, continuing the transmission of sacred wisdom in the traditional manner. The 613 strictures of the Torah represent the legalistic crystallization of this originally initiatory, gnostic heritage.
