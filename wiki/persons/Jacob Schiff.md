## Biographical Details from Source

Per Griffin, Jacob Schiff was head of the New York investment firm [[Kuhn, Loeb & Co.]], "one of the principal backers of the Bolshevik revolution" who "personally financed Trotsky's trip from New York to Russia," "a major contributor to Woodrow Wilson's presidential campaign and an advocate for passage of the Federal Reserve Act." Griffin also links Schiff and Kuhn, Loeb to "rumored ties" behind the assassination of Tsar Alexander II (via the People's Will).

In Mullins's account, Schiff is the partner whose Rothschild-funded entry into Kuhn, Loeb established the firm as the Rothschilds' New York representative, and a financier of railroads ("financial irrigator"). Both sources place him among the New York bankers who funded Wilson and promoted the Federal Reserve Act.

## Disputed: Schiff and the Bolsheviks

Griffin and Mullins name Schiff as a financier of Trotsky and the Bolsheviks. [[Antony C. Sutton]], working from State Department records, disputes this — finding that Schiff "was in fact against support of the Bolshevik regime" and attributing the documented financing to the Morgan-Rockefeller group instead. See [[Controversy: Did Jacob Schiff finance the Bolshevik Revolution?]].
