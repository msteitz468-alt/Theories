## Biographical Details from Source

Mullins presents Eugene Meyer Jr. as the second of the three WWI "emperors," son of a partner in the international banking house Lazard Frères, and brother-in-law of George Blumenthal of J. P. Morgan & Co. (agents of the Rothschilds). Per the source he and Baruch jointly purchased the Alaska-Juneau gold mine in 1915.

As Chairman of the War Finance Corporation, Mullins alleges, Meyer "used his position to sell himself ... two billion dollars worth of United States bonds." The source cites a 1931 Senate investigation (House Report 1635, 68th Congress) into Meyer's fitness for the Federal Reserve Board, alleging that under his direction $24 million in Liberty Bonds were printed in duplicate, $10 billion in WWI bonds were surreptitiously destroyed, and Treasury books were altered nightly during the investigation — yet his appointment was upheld.

The source traces his career through the Federal Farm Loan Board, the Federal Reserve Board (1930), the Reconstruction Finance Corporation (1932), and the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (1946), tying him to the marshals' seizure of the Joint Stock Land Banks and to the international gold trade.
