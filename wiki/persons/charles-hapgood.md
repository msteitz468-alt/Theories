## Biographical Details from Source

American academic (Keene College, New Hampshire) whose work underpins two of Hancock's central arguments. In *Maps of the Ancient Sea Kings* he argued that maps such as the **Piri Reis** map preserve accurate prehistoric surveys — including the ice-free coast of Antarctica — "passed down from people to people," originating with "a hitherto unknown" civilization. In *Earth's Shifting Crust* (1958, foreword by Albert Einstein) he proposed **earth-crust displacement**, the mechanism Hancock uses to locate the lost civilization on a once-temperate Antarctica.

## Primary Source Appearances

Cited extensively throughout *Fingerprints of the Gods* (Parts I and VIII), including the 1960 USAF letter to Hapgood evaluating the Piri Reis map.
