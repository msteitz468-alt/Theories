**Biographical Details from Source**

Cooper provides an extended autobiography in the Introduction to *Behold a Pale Horse*. Key facts as stated:

- Born May 6, 1943. Father: USAF Lt. Col. (Ret.) Milton V. "Jack" Cooper, a career command pilot. Mother: Dovie Nell (Woodside) Cooper. Siblings: twins Ronnie and Connie (two years younger).
- Grew up in a military family stationed overseas extensively, including the Azores and Japan. Graduated 1961 from Yamato High School, Japan.
- Enlisted in the Air Force 1961. Served with the 4245th Bomb Wing (Strategic Air Command) at Sheppard AFB, working on B-52 bombers, KC-135s, and Minuteman missiles. Held Secret clearance. Honorable discharge 1965.
- Enlisted in the U.S. Navy December 1965. Attended Naval Training Center San Diego. Served aboard submarine USS Tiru (SS-416) at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. Subsequently served aboard USS Tombigbee (AOG-11), a gasoline tanker. Advanced to second-class petty officer (quartermaster striker).
- Lost his leg in an unspecified incident; brother Ronnie visited him in the hospital in 1976.
- Wife Annie is Chinese. Daughter Dorothy mentioned prominently.
- Cooper states he was labeled in various disinformation efforts as "a notorious, radical, right-wing extremist," a racist, a Communist, and an alcoholic — all of which he denies.

**Key Formative Experience (per source)**

While aboard USS Tiru during a Pacific transit (~1966), Cooper claims to have witnessed a large disk-shaped craft repeatedly emerge from and re-enter the ocean. He was interrogated by an ONI commander at Pearl Harbor and forced to sign classified non-disclosure documents. He presents this as the beginning of his conviction that the U.S. government suppresses UFO reality. See [[Claim: Cooper witnessed large disk craft emerge from ocean from USS Tiru c.1966]].

**Cooper's Creed (from source)**

Cooper states his four-point creed:
1. Belief in God and Jesus Christ as saviour
2. The U.S. Constitution, without interpretation, as written
3. The family unit and his own family specifically
4. "Any man without principles that he is ready and willing to die for at any given moment is already dead"
