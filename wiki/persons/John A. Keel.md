## Biographical Details from Source

American journalist and paranormal investigator, author of *The Mothman Prophecies* (1975). Keel spent 1966–67 investigating the Point Pleasant events first-hand, working closely with local reporter [[Mary Hyre]]. He positions himself as pursuing "the source of the phenomenon itself" rather than its manifestations, "divorced… from all the popular frames of reference." A friend of zoologist Ivan T. Sanderson (from whom he borrows the term "OINTs" — Other Intelligences), Keel coined and championed the **ultraterrestrial** model and is, with Jacques Vallée, a founder of the interdimensional/control-system school. He drew on Charles Fort's catalogues of anomalies ("you can measure a circle by beginning anywhere").

## Primary Source Appearances

First-person narrator throughout *The Mothman Prophecies*; he becomes a character in his own story (the "Beelzebub" anecdote; targeted by Men in Black phenomena; receiving prophetic phone calls).
