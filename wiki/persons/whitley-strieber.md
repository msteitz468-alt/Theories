## Biographical Details from Source

American novelist (horror/thriller author of *The Wolfen* and *The Hunger*) who, in *Communion* (1987), recounts his own apparent abduction by "the visitors," beginning with the December 26, 1985 encounter at his upstate New York cabin. A self-described former skeptic ("I was with the skeptics"), Strieber became the most famous abduction experiencer of his era. He worked with [[Budd Hopkins]] and psychiatrist Dr. Donald Klein, passed a polygraph, and tested in the normal psychological range — yet remained pointedly agnostic about what the visitors are. Paul Wallis later invoked him as "the poster-boy for close encounters of the traumatic kind."
