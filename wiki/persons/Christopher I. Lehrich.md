## Biographical Details from Source

Christopher I. Lehrich is the author of *The Occult Mind: Magic in Theory and Practice* (Cornell
University Press, 2007). Within that work he presents himself as a scholar seeking to open a
theoretical, comparative approach to the study of occult, magical, and esoteric traditions — a field
he argues "modern academe does not recognize" as a discipline in its own right. He is also the author
of prior work on Renaissance magic (notably a study of the occult philosophy of Cornelius Agrippa,
referenced in *The Occult Mind*).

Lehrich writes from within religious studies and the history of ideas, drawing heavily on
structuralism ([[Claude Lévi-Strauss]]), deconstruction ([[Jacques Derrida]]), and the comparative
study of religion. His stated aim is not to "bash" the occult or its great scholarly interpreters, but
to treat magical thought as a serious intellectual system worthy of rigorous theoretical analysis.
