## Biographical Details from Source

Griffin presents Nicholas Biddle as head of the Second Bank of the United States, "a formidable adversary" who "wielded great political power" because "many members of Congress were indebted to him for business favors." Per the source, after President [[Andrew Jackson]] vetoed the Bank's charter renewal and removed federal deposits, Biddle "counterattacked by contracting credit and calling in loans," deliberately triggering "a national panic-depression" to frighten voters and blame Jackson. Griffin quotes him: "All other banks and all the merchants may break, but the Bank of the United States shall not break."

When the strategy "leaked out, it backfired"; Biddle was called before a Congressional investigative committee, Jackson's censure was rescinded, and the Bank's charter expired in 1836.
