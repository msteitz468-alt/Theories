## Biographical Details from Source

**Donald D. Hoffman** is a cognitive scientist and the author of *[[The Case Against Reality (Why Evolution Hid the Truth from Our Eyes)]]* (2019). The biographical details below are those the book itself supplies.

- As a senior at **UCLA** (1977–78 academic year), working toward a B.A. in Quantitative Psychology, he was admitted to a graduate AI class taught by Professor Edward Carterette, where he first encountered the work of **David Marr** and found it "electrifying."
- He worked his way through UCLA as a "cold warrior" at **Hughes Aircraft**, writing flight simulators and cockpit displays for fighter jets (e.g. the F-14) in the machine code of the AN/UYK-30 microprocessor. He graduated in June 1978.
- He entered **MIT** in fall 1979 as the graduate student of David Marr (in the Psychology Department and the Artificial Intelligence Laboratory). Marr died of leukemia in November 1980, aged 35, fourteen months into Hoffman's studies. Whitman Richards became Hoffman's advisor after Marr's death.
- He completed his **PhD in 1983** and joined the Department of Cognitive Sciences at **UC Irvine** that fall.
- By **1986** he had come to doubt Marr's claim that we evolved "to see a true description of what is there."
- He was a member of the **Helmholtz Club**, a private group of neuroscientists, cognitive scientists, and philosophers (including Francis Crick) that met at UC Irvine to pursue a scientific theory of consciousness. He first met split-brain surgeon Joseph Bogen there in 1995, and exchanged a series of letters with **Francis Crick** in 1994 about perception and reality.
- He ran a perception lab at UC Irvine whose graduate students (Darren Peshek, Negar Sammaknejad, Justin Mark, Brian Marion, and others) produced much of the empirical work cited in the book.

## Intellectual Contribution

Hoffman is the originator of the **[[Interface Theory of Perception (ITP)]]** and **[[Conscious Realism]]**. He conjectured the **[[Fitness-Beats-Truth (FBT) Theorem]]** and the **[[Invention of Symmetry Theorem]]**, both proved by the mathematician **Chetan Prakash**. With Manish Singh and Prakash he published "The Interface Theory of Perception" (2015, *Psychonomic Bulletin & Review*). His research was supported in part by the Federico and Elvia Faggin Foundation.

## Primary Source Appearances

The entire argument of *The Case Against Reality* is Hoffman's, told largely in the first person, including his 1994 correspondence with Crick (chapter 3) and his lab's experiments on the perception of beauty (chapter 2).
