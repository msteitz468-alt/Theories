## Biographical Details from Source

**Andrew Cuomo** is identified in *[[Faucian Bargain (The Most Powerful and Dangerous Bureaucrat in American History)]]* as the Democratic Governor of New York during the COVID-19 pandemic and the brother of CNN host Chris Cuomo. Chapter 8 ("Andrew Cuomo, the First Bishop of Faucism") makes him the book's case study of a [[Anthony Fauci]] "proxy."

> Note (strict-extraction): this records the source's adversarial portrayal only.

## Portrayal in the Source

- Wrote a book, *American Crisis: Leadership Lessons from the COVID-19 Pandemic* (2020), and won an Emmy for his daily briefings, which the authors call "propaganda."
- Central to the **New York nursing-home death scandal**: the book alleges his policy sent COVID patients into nursing homes, and that his aide **Melissa DeRosa** admitted the death toll there had been underreported by more than 6,000 to limit political fallout.
- Said by the book to have received Fauci's public blessing (Fauci's July 20, 2020 statement that New York "did it correctly") despite the scandal, with Fauci later declining to comment on it (see [[Fauci publicly legitimized Andrew Cuomo despite the New York nursing-home death scandal]]).
- The authors note a long Fauci–Cuomo-family relationship (Fauci's daily calls to Chris Cuomo during his illness).
