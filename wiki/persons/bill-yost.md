**Biographical Details from Source**

Bill Yost was Thomas Campbell's supervisor (boss) at a civilian position within a military organization, circa 1972. Campbell describes him as "a super person... smart — no frills, no BS, no hidden agenda, no tact — an engineer through and through. Bright, honest, and straightforward." Campbell identifies this as "the personality type that I related to most easily."

It was Yost who directly introduced Campbell to the work of Robert Monroe: "One day Mr. Yost came by my desk and tossed a book at me. I caught it in mid air and read the title. *Journeys Out Of The Body*, by Robert A. Monroe." Yost's instruction was simply: "Read it, and tell me what you think." Yost subsequently organized a group visit to Monroe's estate (Whistlefield) with approximately twelve colleagues from work, including Campbell. On that visit Yost expressed genuine curiosity about Monroe's claims: "'Me too,' said Yost with a twinkle in his eye — 'me too!'"

Yost participated in the early research at Whistlefield Research Laboratories. Campbell describes his contribution to the team's role: "Bill Yost contributed his engineering insights, management skills, encouragement, and support." He assisted in the construction of equipment: for the first large-scale experimental seminar at the Tuckahoe Motel, "with the help of Bill Yost and Bob's stepdaughter, Nancy Lea Honeycutt, we had the equipment installed, checked out, and ready to go late in the afternoon of the last day." Specifically, "Bill Yost brought in an exceptionally sensitive high input-impedance voltmeter" for the instrumented research setup.

**Primary Source Appearances**

Bill Yost appears in Book 1, Section 1 of *My Big TOE* — the autobiographical narrative that recounts the origins of Campbell's research. He is the catalyst figure who introduced Campbell to Monroe and provided practical engineering support in the early research years.
