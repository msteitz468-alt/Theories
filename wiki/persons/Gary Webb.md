## Description from Source

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], Gary Webb was an award-winning investigative journalist whose 1996 "Dark Alliance" series for the San Jose Mercury News alleged that the CIA had knowingly allowed Nicaraguan Contra drug traffickers to sell crack cocaine in Los Angeles, with profits funding the Contras. The mainstream press attacked the series aggressively, and Webb was forced to resign from the Mercury News. The CIA's own 1998 inspector general report subsequently confirmed a 1982–1995 Memorandum of Understanding between the CIA and the Justice Department permitting them to ignore drug trafficking by CIA assets.

Webb was found dead on December 10, 2004, from two gunshot wounds to the face. The Sacramento County coroner ruled suicide. Jeffries alleges he was murdered; see [[Jeffries alleges investigative journalist Gary Webb was murdered, not a suicide]]. Webb's own assessment of his career: "The truth was that, in all those years, I hadn't written anything important enough to suppress."
