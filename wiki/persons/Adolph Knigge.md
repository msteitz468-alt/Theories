## Biographical Details from Source

A nobleman and leading Strict Observance Freemason, initiated into the Illuminati by Weishaupt in 1780. Marrs credits Knigge with giving the order a "genius for organization": he worked out the higher grades, fused the Illuminati with Freemasonry, and recruited established professional men and nobility, swelling membership to thousands. He founded the ruling "Areopagites" council. After constant quarrels with the dictatorial Weishaupt (whom he accused of being "a latent Jesuit"), Knigge resigned in 1784. He is also remembered for his later book on manners (*Über den Umgang mit Menschen*).
