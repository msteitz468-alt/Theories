## Biographical Details from Source

Dean Radin, described as "leading American parapsychologist," senior researcher at the Institute of Noetic Sciences (founded by Apollo astronaut Edgar Mitchell). Per the book, Radin expanded Dick Bierman's accidental 1995 discovery — that Random Event Generators (REGs) became non-random during the Ajax Champions League final — into systematic research.

Beginning in 1996 Radin and colleagues monitored REGs during mass-attention events (the Oscars, Super Bowl, Atlanta Olympics). The results led to the Global Consciousness Project (created 1998, headed by Roger Nelson of Princeton), a permanent worldwide network of 65 REGs continuously churning out data, which is then analysed for correlations with major global events. Radin's analysis of the 11 September 2001 data showed it as "the day above all" in terms of REG deviation from randomness, and is quoted: "in metaphorical terms, our bell rang more loudly on this day than any other day in 2001." His book *Entangled Minds: Extrasensory Experience in a Quantum Reality* (2006) develops the thesis that psi phenomena may be explained by quantum nonlocality.

The book cites Radin alongside Brian Josephson (Nobel laureate, Cambridge) as credible scientists who take the consciousness-matter interface seriously and seek quantum explanations.
