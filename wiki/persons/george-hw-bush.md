## Biographical Details from Source

Cooper discusses George H.W. Bush extensively throughout the book, making a series of serious allegations about his membership in secret societies and his alleged participation in criminal operations.

## Membership in Secret Orders

Cooper asserts: "George Bush is a member of the Order." He states that Bush's father "was also a member who helped finance Hitler." Cooper writes that members of the Order "take an oath that absolves them from any allegiance to any nation or king or government or constitution," and that "George Bush is not a loyal citizen of the United States but instead is loyal only to the destruction of the United States and to the formation of the New World Order. According to the oath Bush took when he was initiated into Skull & Bones, his oath of office as President of the United States of America means nothing."

Cooper further states that Bush "was a member of the Trilateral Commission and only resigned as an expediency to get elected" and that "Bush is still a member of the Order and the CFR."

Cooper also asserts that Bush is "New World Order. George Bush is a member of the CFR."

## Skull & Bones Initiation

Cooper references Bush's Skull & Bones initiation in a passage about secret-society rituals, writing: "George Bush, when he was initiated into the Skull & Bones, did not lie naked in a coffin with a ribbon tied around his genitalia and yell out the details of all his sexual experiences because it was fun. He had much to gain by accepting initiation into the Order."

## Drug Trafficking Allegation

Cooper makes one of his most serious allegations in Chapter Twelve. He states that when CIA planners needed a way to smuggle drugs from South America into the United States, they "approached" someone to help: "His name is George Bush, who at the time was the president and CEO of the offshore division of Zapata Oil, based in Texas." Cooper asserts that offshore oil platforms could receive drugs from fishing boats and transfer them to shore through normal supply logistics, bypassing customs.

Cooper states: "George Bush agreed to help, and organized the operation in conjunction with the CIA. The plan worked better than anyone had dreamed. It has since expanded worldwide." He writes: "It must always be remembered that George Bush began the sale of drugs to our children. The CIA now controls most of the world's illegal drug markets."

## Membership in Majesty Twelve

Cooper lists "George Bush" as one of the members of Majesty Twelve over the years, alongside Gordon Dean and Zbigniew Brzezinski.

## FEMA and the National Security State

Cooper describes President Bush's March 24 executive order delegating broad powers to the FEMA director, writing that "in reality the revision delegates to the FEMA Director, direct responsibility for a large number of items which were earlier only the President's prerogative." Cooper presents this as part of the construction of the secret government's emergency powers framework.

He also states that Bush "was a member of FEMA's Advisory Board until he was appointed by Bush to become the superior to FEMA in the chain of command of the National [Security Council]" — referring to a specific official in connection with the FEMA structure.

## New World Order Meeting at Malta

Cooper references a meeting aboard a ship at Malta attended by "Bush, Gorbachev, and the ten regional heads of the New World Order," at which Lord Maitreya was allegedly present as a witness.

## Appointment of Knights of Malta

Cooper notes that "President Bush appointed Knight of Malta Thomas Melledy to the post of U.S. Ambassador to the Vatican."

## Primary Textual Appearances

Bush appears throughout Cooper's book, but most substantially in Chapter Two (secret society memberships) and Chapter Twelve (the drug operation and Majesty Twelve). Cooper identifies him as a central operative of the secret government.
