# Wouter J. Hanegraaff (b. 1961)

## Biographical Details from Source

Wouter J. Hanegraaff writes as Professor of History of Hermetic Philosophy and Related Currents at the University of Amsterdam — the first full chair in the academic study of Western esotericism — and as a leading figure in establishing the field as a rigorous, empirical, historically-grounded discipline distinct from both the "religionist" approach and from the esoteric currents it studies. In *Western Esotericism: A Guide for the Perplexed* (2013) he presents the field to newcomers: what "Western esotericism" is, how the category was constructed, how it can be studied, and what its principal worldviews, forms of knowledge, and practices are.

## Positions Advanced in the Source

- That "Western esotericism" is best understood historically as the domain of knowledge that was **rejected and polemically excluded** by mainstream religion, science, and philosophy — the "waste-basket of rejected knowledge" of Western culture.
- That the field must be studied by **empirical, historical method**, not by religionist assumptions of a hidden universal truth (the "grand polemical narrative" and its mirror-image apologetics both distort it).
- A survey of the field's **worldviews, epistemologies (gnosis / reason / faith), and practices**, and of how **"modernization" / disenchantment** reshaped esotericism after the Enlightenment.
