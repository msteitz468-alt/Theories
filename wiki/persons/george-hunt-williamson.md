## Biographical Details from Source

George Hunt Williamson (real name given by Vallée as Michel d'Obrenovic) is one of the
"seminal" early UFO contactees in Vallée's account of the contactee-to-extremist lineage
(Chapter 10). Around 1950 he is said to have worked for [[William Dudley Pelley]] at Soulcraft
Publications in Noblesville, Indiana, before moving to California, where he witnessed
[[George Adamski]]'s claimed 1952 desert contact with a Venusian. Vallée notes Williamson
"assured me that he had seen the craft in flight" in connection with the Bordas case.

## Reported Activities in the Source

With **John McCoy**, Williamson co-authored *UFOs Confidential*, which Vallée says "introduced
into the picture the idea that the Jewish Banker conspiracy was involved in the UFO problem" —
McCoy operated the Essene Press; the Stanford brothers (Ray and Rex) were associates in the same
Texas milieu. Vallée cites Williamson and McCoy's claim that "the force behind the 'International
Bankers' who constitute the 'Hidden Empire' stems from Communist Russia" as an example of
contactee conspiracy doctrine echoing antisemitic myths like the *Protocols of the Elders of
Zion*.

## Strict-extraction note

Williamson's biography and affiliations are recorded as Vallée reports them, including from
unnamed informants; the antisemitic "International Bankers" doctrine is documented as a *belief
of the contactees Vallée studied*, which he explicitly criticizes, not as fact.
