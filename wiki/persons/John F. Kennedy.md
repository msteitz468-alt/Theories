## Biographical Details from Source

In Talbot's account, the president whose "duel" with Allen Dulles defines the book. As senator, Kennedy broke with Cold War orthodoxy, criticizing Western imperialism (Vietnam, Algeria); as president he refused to escalate the Bay of Pigs, fired Dulles, intended to "splinter the CIA," and pursued peace initiatives. Talbot argues elite and national-security hostility to Kennedy culminated in his assassination — the book's central thesis.
