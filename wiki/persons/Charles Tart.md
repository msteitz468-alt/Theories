## Biographical Details from Source

Parapsychologist and a long-time scientific associate of Robert Monroe, Tart wrote the introduction to *Journeys Out of the Body* and the foreword to Ronald Russell's biography. In *The Journey of Robert Monroe* he is presented as the researcher who first studied Monroe in the laboratory (early 1960s) and who frames Monroe's significance: he praises Monroe as "unique among the small number of people who have written about repeated OBEs" for recognizing how his own mind "tries to interpret his experiences, to force them into familiar patterns," and working hard to "tell it like it is." It was a conversation with Tart (about Andrew Neher's research on driving EEG responses with acoustic stimuli) that, Russell notes, likely seeded Monroe's interest in using sound to drive brainwaves — the germ of Hemi-Sync.

## Source-Specific Portrait

Tart's foreword makes a sweeping claim for the OBE's importance (see [[Tart argues out-of-body experiences underlie the world's religious traditions]]) and offers a working definition of the OBE as "an altered state of consciousness in which the subject feels that his mind or self-awareness is separated from his physical body."
