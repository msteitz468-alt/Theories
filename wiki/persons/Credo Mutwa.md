## Biographical Details from Source

According to Icke, Credo Mutwa is a Zulu shaman ("sangoma"). Icke writes that in February 1998, in Johannesburg, South Africa, he spent nearly five hours in conversation with Mutwa.

## Primary Source Appearances

Icke describes two separate encounters or citations of Mutwa in the text:

**1. February 1998 interview (Chapter on reptilian infiltration, ~p. 34 of the source)**
Icke claims he met Mutwa in Johannesburg in February 1998 and talked with him for nearly five hours. Icke states that Mutwa discussed "the global manipulation and his belief that an extraterrestrial race is behind the global Brotherhood of monarchs, politicians, bankers and media owners." Icke notes that the Zulu word for their people means "People from the Stars," citing Mutwa as his source for the claim that the Zulu believe themselves "a royal race that originates beyond this planet."

**2. Freemasonic ritual comparison (~p. 297 of the source)**
Icke reports that Mutwa told him that "compared with the Freemason rituals he had seen in the United States and the United Kingdom, the voodoo and black magic of Africa was put in the shade."
