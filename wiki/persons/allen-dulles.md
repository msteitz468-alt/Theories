## Biographical Details from Source

Per Mullins (Ch. 7), Allen W. Dulles and his brother John Foster Dulles, of the New York law firm Sullivan & Cromwell — which "represented the Schroder Bank" — were present at the January 4, 1933 meeting at the Schroder Bank in Berlin at which German industrialists and bankers covered Hitler's debts and enabled his assumption of power. Mullins states Allen Dulles "later became a director of J. Henry Schroder Company," and that the brothers' biographers prefer "to pretend [the meeting] never happened." John Foster Dulles would become Eisenhower's Secretary of State; Allen Dulles "headed the Central Intelligence Agency for many years."

## Biographical Details from Source — Talbot, *The Devil's Chessboard* (2015)

Talbot makes Dulles the central figure of an investigative history of the CIA and the American "deep state." The portrait: a Wall Street lawyer ([[Sullivan & Cromwell]]) emotionally "impenetrable" (his wife called him "The Shark"), who as OSS Bern chief negotiated with the SS in defiance of FDR (see [[Dulles pursued a separate peace with the SS in defiance of FDR's unconditional-surrender policy]]), buried Holocaust intelligence, protected Nazi war criminals, and helped build postwar U.S. intelligence. As DCI (1953–1961) under Eisenhower he ran the Iran and Guatemala coups, the MKULTRA program, and assassination operations, with "virtually no congressional oversight." Talbot argues he "undermined or betrayed every president he served," was fired by Kennedy after the Bay of Pigs, and — in the book's thesis — was complicit in JFK's assassination and steered the Warren Commission. He died in 1969; [[James Jesus Angleton]] carried his ashes.
