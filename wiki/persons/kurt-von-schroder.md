## Biographical Details from Source

According to Antony Sutton's research:
* **The Cologne Meeting (1933):** Baron Kurt von Schröder was a prominent German private banker and partner in the international J. Henry Schroder Banking Company (which had branches in New York and London). On January 4, 1933, Schröder hosted a secret meeting at his home in Cologne between Adolf Hitler and Franz von Papen. At this meeting, they reached an agreement to form a coalition government, which directly paved the way for Hitler to become Chancellor on January 30, 1933.
* **Financial Conduit for the S.S.:** Immediately after the Nazi takeover, Schröder's political and financial influence grew. He became the German representative at the Bank for International Settlements (B.I.S.) and head of the private bankers group advising the German Reichsbank. Heinrich Himmler appointed Schröder an S.S. Senior Group Leader (Brigadeführer). Schröder served as the central financial conduit for the Keppler Circle, managing a special "S" account at the Stein Bank in Cologne where I.T.T., I.G. Farben, and other multinational corporations deposited annual contributions that Himmler used to fund S.S. operations.
* **Legal and Corporate Interlocks:** Schröder's banking firm operated across national borders during World War II. The German end of the J. Henry Schroder bank loans was handled by Schröder's law firm Albert & Westrick (run by attorney Gerhard Westrick), while the New York end of the Schroder bank's transactions was handled by the law firm Sullivan & Cromwell, led by John Foster Dulles and Allen Dulles.
