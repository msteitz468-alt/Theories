## Description from Source

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], Vince Foster was a close friend and colleague of both Bill and Hillary Clinton from their Arkansas days, who served as Deputy White House Counsel in the early Clinton administration. His body was found in Fort Marcy Park in northern Virginia on July 20, 1993, with an official ruling of suicide by gunshot.

Jeffries presents Foster's death as one of the most thoroughly documented entries in the "Clinton Body Count" and argues that the physical evidence overwhelmingly points to murder and a subsequent cover-up involving the White House, the FBI, and special prosecutors. See [[Jeffries alleges Vince Foster was murdered and the suicide ruling was a White House cover-up]].

Clinton aide Web Hubbell is quoted in the source as having said: "Don't believe a word you hear. It was not suicide. It couldn't have been." (*Esquire*, November 1993).
