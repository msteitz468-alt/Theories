## Biographical Details from Source

Stanford neurophysiologist and author of the textbook *Languages of the Brain*. Talbot credits him with pioneering 1940s work on the limbic system and, as a young neurosurgeon, with courageously refusing to perform the then-fashionable prefrontal lobotomy. Working from Karl Lashley's finding that memory could not be localized or surgically removed from rats' brains, Pribram concluded that memory and perception are **distributed** throughout the brain on holographic principles — the brain storing and reading information as interference patterns / frequencies. He arrived at the holographic idea independently of Bohm, and the two later corresponded.
