## Biographical Details from Source

Mullins presents Frank Vanderlip — President of the National City Bank of New York, "the most powerful bank in this country, representing the Rockefeller oil interests" — as one of the Jekyll Island group. The source treats Vanderlip's own later accounts as confessions: it quotes his *Saturday Evening Post* article (1935) admitting he was "as secretive, indeed, as furtive, as any conspirator," and his autobiography *From Farmboy to Financier*: "Our secret expedition to Jekyl Island was the occasion of the actual conception of what eventually became the Federal Reserve System. The essential points of the Aldrich Plan were all contained in the Federal Reserve Act as it was passed."

Mullins also recounts Vanderlip's public role campaigning against the Federal Reserve Act (which the source frames as a deliberate smokescreen to make people think bankers feared it), and his later proposal to replace the Board with a "Federal Monetary Authority."
