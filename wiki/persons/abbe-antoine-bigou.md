## Biographical Details from Source

The source identifies Antoine Bigou as Bérenger Saunière's predecessor as curé of Rennes-le-Château, having served approximately a century before Saunière (c.1774–1790). Bigou also served as personal chaplain to the noble Blanchefort family.

## Composition of the Parchments

When Saunière discovered four parchments hidden in a hollow Visigoth column in 1891, the source states that "two remaining documents had apparently been composed in the 1780s by one of Saunière's predecessors as curé of Rennes-le-Château, the Abbé Antoine Bigou."

The source speculates that these parchments — which appeared to be "pious Latin texts, excerpts from the New Testament" — were in fact encoded documents concealing a cryptic message, and that the apparent errors in the texts were "contrived precisely" to allow the message to be decoded.

## The Tombstone of Marie de Blanchefort

Bigou designed and installed both the headstone and the flagstone marking the grave of "Marie, Marquise d'Hautpoul de Blanchefort" in the churchyard at Rennes-le-Château. The source states that the headstone's inscription "which included a number of deliberate errors in spacing and spelling was a perfect anagram for the message concealed in the parchments referring to Poussin and Teniers." Saunière subsequently defaced and erased this tombstone inscription, apparently to prevent others from reading it.

## Confessor to Marie de Blanchefort

The source suggests in passing — through a rhetorical question — that Bigou may have been the custodian of a long-held secret connected to the Blanchefort family: "Was the pope then privy to some secret entrusted to the custody of his family — a secret which remained in the Blanchefort family until the eighteenth century, when the Abbé Antoine Bigou, curé of Rennes-le-Château and confessor to Marie de Blanchefort, composed the parchments found by Saunière?"

## Flight to Spain

The source states that Bigou fled to Spain on the eve of the French Revolution, where "shortly after, he died." The authors speculate that as a result, the Prieuré de Sion "for a time at any rate, did not know precisely where the parchments were," since Bigou "had concealed, and possibly composed, the coded parchments on the very eve of the Revolution and then fled."

## The Date January 17th

The source notes that January 17th appears on the tombstone of Marie de Blanchefort designed by Bigou, and that this date is the same as Saunière's fatal stroke and the feast day of Saint Sulpice. The source treats Bigou's use of this date as one of the recurring and suspicious coincidences throughout the story.

## Primary Source Appearances

The source's index lists: "Bigou, Abbé Antoine, 25, 28, 428." He appears primarily in Chapter 1 (the parchments and the tombstone) and is referenced again in Chapter 4 in connection with the Templar immunity at Bézou.
