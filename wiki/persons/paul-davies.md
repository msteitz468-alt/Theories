## Biographical Details from Source

Paul Davies (b. 1946), described by the book as "that rare animal, both a highly regarded academic and a successful popular science writer." Per the authors, Davies is "the most high-profile scientific advocate of the design idea." His key works cited include *God and the New Physics* (1983), *The Mind of God* (1992), *The Cosmic Blueprint* (1988), and *The Goldilocks Enigma* (2006).

The book quotes Davies's statement from *The Mind of God*: "Through my scientific work I have come to believe more and more strongly that the physical universe is put together with an ingenuity so astonishing that I cannot accept it merely as a brute fact … I have come to the point of view that mind — i.e. conscious awareness of the world — is not a meaningless and accidental quirk of nature, but an absolutely fundamental facet of reality."

Davies coined the term "designer universe" for the interpretation of the anthropic principle and identified the kind of designer it implies as fitting the Hermetic Demiurge rather than the omnipotent biblical God. He described the vacuum energy fine-tuning as "the biggest fix in the universe." He also explored the simulation argument (after Nick Bostrom), concluding that accepting the multiverse logically requires accepting that our universe is probably simulated — which, the authors note, is itself a form of design.

Davies is also cited in Chapter 11 for his *Cosmic Blueprint* argument that random mutations "would tend to downgrade, rather than enhance, the complex and intricate adaptedness of an organism," and in Chapter 12 for pointing out that physicists are "quite happy to explore ideas of backward causation" but "it is only when the end state involves life and mind that most scientists take fright."
