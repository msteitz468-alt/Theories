## Biographical Details from Source

Raymond Bernard, of Villeneuve-St.-Georges near Paris, headed the European branch of the
**Ancient and Mystical Order of the Rosy Cross (AMORC)**, reporting to the worldwide "Imperator"
at the order's San Jose, California headquarters. Vallée presents Bernard's own book as his prime
exhibit of a contactee who claims to have met one of the "Manipulators" in person — making his
"Adventures of a Grand Master" central to Chapter 8.

## The "Maha" Encounter Reported in the Source

In 1967 Bernard was contacted by a "superior being" calling himself **Maha**, with "extraordinary
eyes" that "reflected an entire universe." At their first meeting (Carlton Hotel, Amsterdam)
Bernard found himself standing without willing it, perceiving nothing "except Maha's eyes." Maha
said he belonged to "the High Council" (also called "the A"), and described what Vallée calls the
**Control System**: a planetary body "unconcerned by minor philosophical differences between
capitalism and communism," working toward "the abolition of borders, and the death of
nationalism," and a coming reversal of "the value of money." A second episode in Vienna involved
a ritual with twelve white-robed men, a marble floor of "black and white triangles," inaudible
speech, a rising vibration, and loss of consciousness — which Vallée reads as hypnotic control
and post-hypnotic suggestion.

Maha instructed Bernard to **publish** his encounters so the symbols and message would spread
through "books, movies, conversations." He claimed the tradition came "from another galaxy" via
Atlantis, that its "pure race" survives, and that the group awaits "the coming of the Kingdom of
Graal… when the knights of the Earth meet the celestial knights of the macrocosm" — i.e., open
UFO contact. Vallée doubts the "High Council" literally exists but treats Bernard as a sincere
initiate "completely at the mercy of those whom they regard as Unknown Superiors."
