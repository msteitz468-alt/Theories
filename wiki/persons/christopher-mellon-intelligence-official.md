## Biographical Details from Source

Christopher Mellon is described by Elizondo as a crucial ally in the UAP disclosure effort. He served as Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and as minority staff director of the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence — credentials that gave him deep access to both executive and legislative branch classified information. He wrote the foreword to *Imminent*.

Mellon became aware of UAP through his intelligence oversight work and met Elizondo at the Pentagon, where Elizondo gave him a three-hour classified briefing (Chapter 18). Mellon subsequently became a key congressional liaison, leveraging his Senate relationships to advance UAP legislation. He joined To The Stars Academy (TTSA) as a national security advisor alongside Elizondo, Puthoff, and Steve Justice, before eventually departing TTSA as the organization encountered financial difficulties.

In his foreword, Mellon specifically identifies two critical national security failures: (1) NORAD and Strategic Command were not being informed of UAP incursions in US controlled airspace, and (2) the institutional stigma around UAP was preventing serious military analysis of a genuine threat.

## Primary Source Appearances

Mellon authors the foreword and is referenced in Chapters 18 and 22. His institutional credentials are presented as independent corroboration of Elizondo's account.
