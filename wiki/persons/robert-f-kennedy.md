## Biographical Details from Source

In *Hidden History*, Robert Kennedy appears as a political figure whose anti-establishment
credentials made him a target of the same forces Jeffries alleges were behind his brother's
assassination. Per Jeffries, RFK's presidential campaign "captured the imaginations of young people"
after LBJ declined to seek reelection, and RFK was winning the 1968 California primary when he
was shot at the Ambassador Hotel in Los Angeles on June 5, 1968.

Jeffries argues that RFK privately doubted the Warren Commission's findings but maintained public
support for them for family reasons; author David Talbot's research is cited showing that behind
the scenes RFK was actively working to find the truth, and former campaign worker Richard Lubic
quotes RFK saying he would reopen the investigation "subject to me getting elected." RFK's press
spokesman Frank Mankiewicz claimed Kennedy had answered "yes" when asked at a campaign appearance
whether he would reopen the investigation.

Jeffries presents RFK as the most serious political threat Nixon feared — Nixon instructed aides
to try to get the Secret Service to expose Kennedy's womanizing, removed Secret Service protection
from him after the 1972 election, and obsessively surveilled him. The book treats RFK's
assassination as a political conspiracy paralleling his brother's.
