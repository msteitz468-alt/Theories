## Biographical Details from Source

The Israeli psychic, brought to the U.S. by parapsychologist Andrija Puharich in 1971 and tested in laboratories, whom Vallée uses as a key example of a UFO "percipient." Geller traces his abilities (metal-bending, apparent psychokinesis and dematerializations) to exposure to a beam of light from the sky, believes himself invested with a "mission" from a cosmic source, and is regarded by some followers as a possible Messiah. Vallée compares him to the anonymous abducted "engineer" and to the French Dr. X.

## Primary Source Appearances

Introduction; Chapter Eight ("Who Are You, Mr. Geller?"); Chapter Nine.

## Source-Specific Portrait

Vallée treats Geller's experiences as real to him and his effects as partly lab-reproducible, but explicitly **rejects** the interpretation (pushed by Puharich) that Geller channels a benign extraterrestrial intelligence or is a Messiah — reading him instead as a manifestation of the belief-conditioning control system.
