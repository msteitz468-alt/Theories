## Biographical Details from Source

Per Sutton, John Reed — author of *Ten Days That Shook the World* and "the American member of the Third International executive committee" — "was financed and supported by Eugene Boissevain, a private New York banker, and ... was employed by Harry Payne Whitney's Metropolitan magazine." Whitney "was at that time a director of Guaranty Trust." Sutton presents Reed as an "Establishment revolutionary" who had "consistent support for his activities, even to the extent of intervention with the State Department from William Franklin Sands, executive secretary of [[American International Corporation]]."
