## Biographical Details from Source

Allen Dulles's older brother and the dominant sibling. As managing partner of [[Sullivan & Cromwell]], Talbot says Foster structured U.S. investment in German cartels (IG Farben, Krupp) and was slow to break with Hitler. As Eisenhower's Secretary of State he authored the "Policy of Boldness" (nuclear "massive retaliation" / brinksmanship), pushed nuclear "first use" in crises (Korea, Dien Bien Phu, Quemoy-Matsu, Berlin), and resisted détente with the post-Stalin USSR. Churchill dubbed him "Dull, Duller, Dulles." He died of cancer in May 1959.
