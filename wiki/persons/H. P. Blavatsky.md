## Biographical Details from Source

Helena Petrovna Blavatsky (1831–1891) was a Russian-born occultist, author, and co-founder (1875) of
the Theosophical Society. In *The Secret Doctrine* she presents herself not as the originator of its
teachings but as the "humble recorder" transmitting an ancient esoteric "wisdom-religion" preserved by
hidden Masters (Mahatmas), built around the archaic Stanzas of Dzyan. Her earlier major work was
*Isis Unveiled* (1877). She writes that the reader who is not a Theosophist is "invited to regard all
that which follows as a fairy tale" — while insisting the doctrine is older and more philosophical
than the science of her day.
