## Biographical Details from Source

Cooper discusses James Forrestal as the first major opponent of the secrecy surrounding the alien question and as one of the earliest casualties of what Cooper calls the cover-up.

## Opposition to Secrecy

Cooper states: "Secretary of Defense James Forrestal objected to the secrecy. He was a very idealistic and religious man. He believed that the public should be told." Cooper adds that "James Forrestal was also one of the first known abductees."

When Forrestal "began to talk to leaders of the opposition party and leaders of the Congress about the alien problem," Cooper states "he was asked to resign by Truman." Cooper says Forrestal "expressed his fears to many people" and "rightfully believed that he was being watched." This was interpreted by those unaware of the alien situation as paranoia.

## Commitment and Death

Cooper states that Forrestal "later was said to have suffered a mental breakdown" and "was ordered to the mental ward of Bethesda Naval Hospital." Cooper asserts the Administration had no authority to commit him but the order was carried out anyway, because "it was feared that Forrestal would begin to talk again. He had to be isolated and discredited. His family and friends were denied permission to visit."

Cooper claims that on May 21, 1949, Forrestal's brother notified authorities he intended to remove James from Bethesda on May 22. Cooper then asserts: "Sometime in the early morning of May 22, 1949, agents of the CIA tied a sheet around James Forrestal's neck, fastened the other end to a fixture in his room, then threw James Forrestal out the window. The sheet tore and he plummeted to his death."

Cooper claims that "James Forrestal's secret diaries were confiscated by the CIA and were kept in the White House for many years. Due to public demand the diaries were eventually rewritten and published in a sanitized version. The real diary information was later furnished by the CIA in book form to an agent who published the material as fiction." Cooper names that agent as Whitley Strieber and the book as *Majestic*.

Cooper concludes: "James Forrestal became one of the first victims of the cover-up."

## Connection to EBE

Cooper situates Forrestal's death immediately before his account of the alien survivor (EBE) found wandering in the desert — the same passage in which Vannevar Bush names the entity. This sequencing frames Forrestal as the first prominent figure killed because of his knowledge of the alien presence.

## Reference in Cooper's Earlier Work

Cooper also references Forrestal in passing in connection with a list of Secretaries whose communications were monitored: "Secretaries Marshall, Forrestal, Patterson, and the director of the State Department Policy Planning Staff."

## Primary Textual Appearances

Forrestal appears primarily in Chapter Twelve ("The Secret Government") of Cooper's book, in the narrative of the early cover-up period under Truman.
