## Biographical Details from Source

American expatriate in wartime Zurich who became Dulles's mistress, courier, and confidante, and a patient/interlocutor of Carl Jung (whom Dulles ran as OSS "Agent 488"). Her journals are a key source for Talbot's portrait of Dulles's coldness — she recorded his "little mice… breaking their little necks" remark and his boast of besting Napoleon's count of lovers "by one." She befriended Dulles's wife [[Clover Dulles]].
