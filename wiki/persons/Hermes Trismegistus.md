## Biographical Details from Source

In *The Kybalion*, Hermes Trismegistus is the legendary "Master of Masters" of ancient Egypt — "the
father of the Occult Wisdom; the founder of Astrology; the discoverer of Alchemy." The source says
"the details of his life story are lost to history," dates his sojourn to "the early days of the
oldest dynasties of Egypt—long before the days of Moses," and reports that "the best authorities
regard him as a contemporary of Abraham," with some Jewish traditions claiming "Abraham acquired a
portion of his mystic knowledge from Hermes himself."

According to the source, tradition holds he "lived three hundred years in the flesh"; after his
passing the Egyptians deified him as **Thoth**, "the Scribe of the Gods," and the Greeks made him
"Hermes, the god of Wisdom." His title *Trismegistus* ("the thrice-great") made his name "synonymous
with the 'Fount of Wisdom.'" The Kybalion derives the modern word "hermetic" (meaning "secret" /
"sealed") from his followers' principle of secrecy, and presents its own teaching as descending from
him "from lip to ear" across the centuries, veiled in the terms of alchemy and astrology.
