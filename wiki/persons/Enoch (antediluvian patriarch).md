## Biographical Details from Source

Enoch is the antediluvian patriarch ("the seventh from Adam") to whom the Book of Enoch is attributed.
In the narrative he is a righteous scribe who "walked with God," is shown visions of the cosmos and the
coming judgment, ascends through the heavens, and acts as **intercessor** — petitioned by the fallen
Watchers to plead their case before God, which is denied. He is presented as the recipient and
recorder of secret heavenly knowledge (the calendar, the names and roles of angels, the future of the
world). Per the framing material, the Epistle of Jude quotes him directly ("Enoch, the seventh from
Adam, prophesied").
