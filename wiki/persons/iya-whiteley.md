## Person Summary from Source

Dr. Iya Whiteley is a British space psychologist and cognitive engineer, born in Latvia, who works at a university in London. She advised the UK Space Agency as Chair of its Space Environment Working Group. Pasulka describes her as a champion skydiver, scuba diver, and pilot who researches extreme environments from personal experience as well as scientific study.

Whiteley first contacted Pasulka in 2020 by email, identifying herself as "Space Psychologist — researcher at a university in London. Developing communication means with unknown phenomena." She had been in South America doing related fieldwork when the COVID-19 pandemic closed borders, and her subsequent emails arrived from different countries as she navigated a return to England.

**Professional contributions documented in *Encounters*:**

- Developed the **Psy-Matrix**, a systematic framework assessing over thirty-six categories of psychological challenge for astronauts on long-duration missions.
- Led the European Space Agency study on psychological preparation, monitoring, and prevention for Moon and Mars exploration missions.
- Co-invented voice-analysis technology for early detection of astronaut fatigue.
- Led projects to develop **EPSILON** (Embedded Psychological Support Integrated for LONg-duration missions).
- Contributed to a culture shift in aviation: her work helped eliminate the stigma associated with pilot reports of anomalous phenomena.
- Currently helps pilots safely process anomalous phenomena as a personal focus.

**Project: Earth Language / AI language bridge (Chapter 2):**

Whiteley is developing a program to decode cross-species communication using AI. She proposes that insects, frogs, birds, and whales share acoustic patterns conforming to the Fibonacci sequence / golden ratio, and that these patterns constitute an "Earth language" that predates and underlies human language. Her hypothesis is that babies possess synesthetic perceptual abilities — cross-sensory integration — that can be cultivated through systematic exposure to mapped sound-image pairings. She plans to use AI to identify innate codes among animal calls, NASA's solar-system sound databases, and Earth's magnetic field sounds, then use 2D/3D grapheme projections to train infant neural pathways for cross-species and potentially cross-entity communication.

Her vision is explicitly oriented toward contact with nonhuman intelligence: "I have been developing these ways so we can encode and decode communication and prepare our children's brains and cognition for connection and communication with other species and beings."

Pasulka compares Whiteley to great navigators — forging an impossible path that becomes possible only because she makes it. Whiteley's work is also connected to indigenous knowledge systems (Australian Aboriginal Fibonacci traditions, Tyson Yunkaporta) and to [[The organic network / noösphere (Pasulka)]].
