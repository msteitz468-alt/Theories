## Biographical Details from Source

**Anders Tegnell** is identified in *[[Faucian Bargain (The Most Powerful and Dangerous Bureaucrat in American History)]]* (chapter 6) as the unelected bureaucrat who largely ran **Sweden's** COVID-19 response — the country the authors present as "the greatest control group in the history of Western civilization" for having avoided mandatory lockdowns and masks.

## Portrayal in the Source

The book presents Tegnell favorably as a contrast to [[Anthony Fauci]]: he "has pursued real science and actual data, while balancing the broader concerns about preserving his country's way of life." However, the authors are careful that their argument is **not** "replacing a Fauci with a Tegnell," because they hold that concentrating such power in any one unelected person — "whether vain or empathetic" — is "anathema to liberty." Tegnell is thus invoked to support [[Sweden's no-lockdown approach was a "control group" indicating lockdowns were ineffective]], not as a model bureaucrat.
