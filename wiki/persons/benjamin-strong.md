## Biographical Details from Source

Mullins presents Benjamin Strong as one of the original [[Event: Jekyll Island drafting meeting (November 1910)]] group and the man who, with Paul Warburg, controlled the Federal Reserve System during its first fifteen years. Per the source he rose to prominence during the Panic of 1907 as "an able lieutenant of J. P. Morgan" and President of the Bankers Trust Co. He served as the first Governor of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York from its opening in 1914 until his death in 1928.

The source (quoting Ferdinand Lundberg and Paul Einzig) states that under Strong the Reserve System was "brought into interlocking relations with the Bank of England and the Bank of France," with Strong working in close cooperation with Montagu Norman of the Bank of England. Mullins ties Strong to the 1925 $200 million gold credit to Britain and to the [[Event: Federal Reserve–European central bank conference (1927)]].

Mullins notes Strong "died a few weeks after his return from Europe, without appearing before the Committee" investigating the secret meetings with European central bankers — drawing a parallel to J. P. Morgan Sr.'s death shortly after the Pujo hearings.
