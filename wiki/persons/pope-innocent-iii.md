## Biographical Details from Source

The source references Pope Innocent III primarily in connection with two acts: the launching of the Albigensian Crusade against the Cathars, and an early admonishment of the Knights Templar.

## The Albigensian Crusade

The source states that on January 14, 1208, one of the papal legates to the Languedoc, Pierre de Castelnau, was murdered. Though the source notes "the crime seems to have been committed by anticlerical rebels with no Cathar affiliations whatever," Rome blamed the Cathars. "At once Pope Innocent III ordered a Crusade."

The source describes the resulting military campaign as devastating: in the town of Béziers alone, at least 15,000 men, women, and children were slaughtered. A papal representative is said to have written to Innocent III announcing that "neither age nor sex nor status was spared." Dominic Guzman, characterised by the source as "a Spanish fanatic named Dominic Guzman," was aided in his activities by the Crusade, and in 1216 — still during Innocent's pontificate — created the Dominican Order, which in 1233 spawned the Holy Inquisition.

## The Knights Templar

The source notes that as early as 1208, Pope Innocent III had "admonished the Templars for un-Christian behaviour, and referred explicitly to necromancy." This is presented in the context of the mystique of occult power surrounding the Templars even during their existence.
