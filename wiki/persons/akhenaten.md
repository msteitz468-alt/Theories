## Biographical Overview from Source

Per [[Custodians of Truth (Wallace-Murphy & Hopkins)]], Akhenaten (the pharaoh born Amenhotep IV) is the most likely candidate for the historical figure behind the biblical Moses, following the identification first proposed by Dr. Karl Abraham and developed most prominently by the scholar Ahmed Osman. Sigmund Freud, in *Moses and Monotheism*, preferred to identify Moses with an official in Akhenaten's entourage named Tuthmosis; the authors lean toward Osman's more direct identification of Akhenaten himself as Moses.

### Egyptian Monotheism and the Exodus

Akhenaten is described as the grandson of Yuya (identified in the source as the patriarch Joseph) and therefore of direct Israelite-Egyptian lineage. He abolished the pantheon of Egyptian gods — most critically the cult of Amun centered on Karnak — and replaced them with exclusive worship of Aten, the solar disk, in what the source describes as "the heresy of monotheism." His new capital, Amarna (Aketaten, "the Holy Land"), was abandoned after his deposition.

The authors argue that the disappearance of Akhenaten and the entire population of Amarna from Egyptian records corresponds to the biblical Exodus. His Atenist priesthood, scribes, nobles, artisans, and foreign residents formed the "mixed multitude" described in Exodus 12:38, who fled to Canaan under the banner of Yahweh (argued to be linguistically derived from Aten).

### Parallels Between Atenism and Judaism

The source enumerates parallels Wallace-Murphy and Hopkins present as proof that early Judaism is a direct evolution of Atenism:

- The Shema prayer ("Hear O Israel, the Lord thy God is One God") is argued to be an almost exact translation of an Atenist prayer
- The Ten Commandments mirror passages from the Egyptian Book of the Dead
- Psalm 104 parallels Akhenaten's Hymn to the Aten almost verbatim
- The Ark of the Covenant parallels the ritual transport of the Aten in ceremonies at Amarna
- The Levitic hereditary priesthood mirrors the hereditary Atenist priestly caste
- The term "Holy Land" used at Amarna prefigures its Judaic usage

The treasure the Israelites carried out of Egypt is identified as the personal wealth of Akhenaten/Moses and the royal treasure of Amarna.
