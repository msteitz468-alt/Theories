**Biographical Details from Source**

Chuck Zukowski is the protagonist of *The 37th Parallel*. Per Mezrich he is an engineer "by training" who worked at a midlevel microchip firm in Colorado Springs while serving for eight years as a **reserve sheriff's deputy** in El Paso County and pursuing UFO and animal-mutilation investigation as an obsessive avocation. He carried a .40 Glock and a self-designed equipment vest (rubber gloves, sample bags, two cameras, a $400 Trifield EMF reader, a metal-detecting wand). He scrupulously kept his law-enforcement and UFO identities separate, never investigating mutilations in uniform.

Over roughly two decades he built "an inch-thick" case file on hundreds of incidents, became a **MUFON field investigator** (trained from the MUFON Field Manual), and turned himself, per the book, into "one of the most knowledgeable [mutilation] investigators in the world." His family — wife **Tammy** and three children — was woven into his fieldwork via RV "vacations" routed through sites like Roswell, Meteor Crater and White Sands. He worked closely with his sister [[Debbie Ziegelmeyer]] (Missouri MUFON) and outdoorsman friend Joe Fex. The book states he **lost his reserve-deputy position** after publicly investigating the Glenda/Rush mutilation case.

His signature contribution is the **37th-parallel "UFO Highway" hypothesis** and the recovery and SEM analysis of a metallic fragment from the Roswell debris site.

**Primary Source Appearances**
