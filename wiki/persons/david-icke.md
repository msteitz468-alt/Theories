**Biographical Details from Source**

Icke identifies himself in the "Life beyond the bubble" preface as someone who underwent a personal transformation in "the early 1990s" that eliminated his "concern for what other people might think of him" — enabling him to write about subjects he acknowledges the mainstream considers insane. He cites his willingness to go wherever evidence leads regardless of social consequences as the defining quality of his research approach.

He describes himself as having left the concern for ridicule behind after "hard and extreme experience." (In wider context known but not detailed in this source: Icke famously announced on the BBC in 1991 that he was "the son of God," triggering mass public ridicule, which he subsequently reinterpreted as a spiritual awakening.)

The preface is dated "January 1st 2001, Ryde, Isle of Wight."

**Methodological Position (from source)**

Icke explicitly states he is not asking readers to believe anything: "Please remember that what you read here is simply information. It is not compulsory to accept it and the last thing I am trying to do is persuade you to believe anything. What you believe is your business, not mine."

He presents himself as synthesising: ancient texts and mythology; Zechariah Sitchin's Sumerian translations; eyewitness accounts from mind-control survivors; testimony from Illuminati insiders (especially Phillip Eugene de Rothschild and anonymous others); Credo Mutwa's Zulu tradition; and his own research across prior books (*The Biggest Secret*, *...And The Truth Shall Set You Free*, etc.).

He acknowledges his books are not self-contained ("Readers of my previous books will see information they already know fused with the latest knowledge") but aims to make each book self-sufficient for new readers.

**Additional Claims from 2018 Book**

The 2018 book *Everything You Need to Know But Have Never Been Told* substantially updates and reframes Icke's earlier thesis. The autobiographical introduction ("On The Road To 'Now'") provides an extended self-portrait: born Leicester, England, 29 April 1952; grew up on a council estate; professional goalkeeper with Coventry City cut short by rheumatoid arthritis at 21; BBC sports presenter; British Green Party spokesperson (Isle of Wight). He recounts the 1990 encounter with psychic Betty Shine as his first deliberate contact with unseen forces, and a Peruvian hilltop "download" experience as the trigger for his public work. His ayahuasca experience in Brazil is identified as the trigger for his simulation-theory phase.
