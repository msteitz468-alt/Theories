## Biographical Details from Source

Griffin presents Andrew Jackson as the president who destroyed the Second Bank of the United States (the nation's third central bank). Per the source, Jackson vetoed the Bank's early charter-renewal bill in 1832, calling the Bank a "monster," and made it the central campaign issue, winning re-election by a large margin. He then removed federal deposits and placed them in regional banks. Griffin quotes him: "I am ready with the screws to draw every tooth and then the stumps."

The source treats Jackson (with Martin Van Buren) as a leader of the new Democrat Party formed around sound-money, anti-Bank principles inherited from the Jeffersonians.
