## Biographical Details from Source

Rupert Sheldrake is a Cambridge-trained biologist (a former Fellow of Clare College and research fellow of the Royal Society) best known for his hypothesis of **morphic resonance** — that nature has an inherent collective memory, and that the "laws" of nature are better understood as evolving habits. In *Science Set Free* (2012; UK title *The Science Delusion*) he argues that institutional science has hardened into a materialist belief system resting on ten unexamined dogmas, and that questioning them opens new scientific possibilities, including the reality of telepathy and the extended mind.
