## Biographical Details from Source

Per Sutton, Max May was a vice president of the Morgan-controlled [[Guaranty Trust Company]] who, when the Soviets formed their first international bank (Ruskombank) in 1923, "became director and chief of the Ruskombank foreign department." Sutton presents this as "the first really concrete signal that previous political and financial support was paying off": a senior officer of a leading Wall Street bank taking a directorship in the first Soviet international bank, which then appointed Guaranty Trust its U.S. agent.
