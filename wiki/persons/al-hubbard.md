## Biographical Details from Source

A former OSS officer and uranium millionaire who became the first major apostle of LSD as a visionary/transcendental drug. Lee and Shlain stress the irony of a career intelligence man (he ran a covert pre–Pearl Harbor arms-smuggling operation for the OSS, later pardoned by Truman) devoting himself to turning on as many influential people as possible after his first acid trip in 1951. He connected with Humphry Osmond and Aldous Huxley and epitomizes the "interface" between the CIA's covert LSD history and the grassroots movement.
