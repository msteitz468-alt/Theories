## Biographical Details from Source

Per Griffin, Montagu Norman was head of the Bank of England during the first decades of the Federal Reserve, who "frequently traveled to the U.S. to meet in secret with [[Benjamin Strong]], head of the Federal Reserve." Griffin states Strong agreed to use the Federal Reserve "to unofficially help Great Britain meet its financial obligations," with "billions of dollars" flowing from the U.S., and that "it was that outflow that set the stage for America's great depression of the 1930's." He recounts Norman telling William Wentworth aboard ship in 1929, in confidence, that there would soon be a "shake-out" in U.S. markets — months before the crash.

In Mullins's account, Norman is the Bank of England Governor who arranged the 1925 sterling/gold credit with Strong and conferred with Andrew Mellon in February 1929 just before the Fed reversed to a high-rate policy.
