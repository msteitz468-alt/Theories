**Biographical Details from Source**

In Sitchin's reading Anu is the supreme ruler of [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]], reigning from a celestial court "of great splendor" on the home planet rather than on Earth. He is the father of the two principal half-brothers and rivals on Earth, [[Enlil (Sitchin)]] (the legal firstborn, by Anu's consort Antu) and [[Enki (Sitchin)]]. Royal legitimacy among the gods, and human kingship "lowered from Heaven," derive from Anu — Sitchin notes that even later rulers such as Hammurabi traced their authority to Anu. The Sumerian sign-syllable AN means "Heaven," and Anu's periodic state visits to Earth (with his consort Antu) are recorded in temple ritual texts that Sitchin reads as the ceremonial sighting of the approaching planet.

**Primary Source Appearances**

Chapter 8 ("Kingship of Heaven") and the discussion of the pantheon's hierarchy (Ch. 3–4). Anu sits atop the rank-numbering of the gods (the "60" of the highest divine numerical rank).
