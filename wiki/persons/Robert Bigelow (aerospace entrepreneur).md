## Biographical Details from Source

Robert Bigelow is a Nevada-based aerospace entrepreneur described by Elizondo as the primary contractor through whom AAWSAP's $22 million was channeled. Bigelow founded Bigelow Aerospace Advanced Space Studies (BAASS) specifically to execute the AAWSAP contract — conducting UAP fieldwork, case investigations, and scientific analysis on behalf of the DIA.

Bigelow purchased Skinwalker Ranch in Utah, which became a major field investigation site for AATIP/AAWSAP. The ranch was the location of the "hitchhiker effect" case where dogs were reportedly vaporized by a blue orb.

Elizondo first met Bigelow in the context of a dinner in Brazil (Chapter 2) attended also by General Paulo Roberto Uchôa and Hal Puthoff, during which the Colares UAP incidents were discussed. Bigelow's resources and institutional access enabled AAWSAP to operate with a scope that a purely government-funded program could not have achieved.
