## Biographical Details from Source

The British novelist (*Brave New World*) who, after first taking mescaline under Humphry Osmond's supervision in 1953, became an influential theorist of the psychedelic experience (*The Doors of Perception*, *Heaven and Hell*). In *Acid Dreams* he advises Leary's Harvard group and argues for an **elitist** strategy — offering psychedelics to "the talented, the well-born, the intelligent rich, and others in positions of influence" — in contrast to Allen Ginsberg's egalitarian crusade. He links the covert and grassroots strands (Hubbard sought him out; he steered Hollingshead to Leary).
