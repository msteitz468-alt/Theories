## Biographical Details from Source

Cooper discusses William Casey in two distinct contexts: as a Knight of Malta and as an operative of the secret government, and in connection with the Iran-Contra affair and the effort to circumvent congressional oversight of covert operations.

## Knight of Malta and Government Roles

Cooper states: "William Casey was the Director of the CIA. He was a member of the CFR. Casey was a Knight of Malta. He was the head of Ronald Reagan's political campaign. He was head of the Securities and Exchange Commission. During the Nixon administration he was head of the Export-Import Bank."

## Kama River Truck Factory

Cooper asserts that "Casey arranged financing for the Kama River truck factory in the Soviet Union with 90% of the funds guaranteed or furnished by the U.S. taxpayer. This factory built military truck and tank engines for the Soviet Army. It was, and may still be, the largest factory in the world and could produce more heavy trucks than all U.S. factories together." Cooper states he believes this arrangement was a deliberate act of technology transfer to the enemy.

## Death

Cooper states: "I believe Casey was murdered." He does not elaborate further in the passages available.

## Iran-Contra Connection

Cooper references Casey in the context of the Iran-Contra affair and the effort to strip away the congressional oversight role in covert operations. He writes that "William Casey, with the help of Vice President Bush, Anne Armstrong and others, drafted legislation" aimed at emasculating congressional oversight "so that Bush, Casey, North and others could carry out their plans."

Cooper also states that when "problems arose, Director of Central Intelligence William Casey took" action to manage them through the drug operation described elsewhere in the book.

## Primary Textual Appearances

Casey appears in Chapter Two ("Secret Societies and the New World Order"), in connection with the Knights of Malta discussion, and at several other points in the book in connection with the Iran-Contra affair and the drug trafficking operation.
