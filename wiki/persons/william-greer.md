## Biographical Details from Source

Cooper identifies William Greer as the Secret Service agent who drove President Kennedy's limousine in the Dallas motorcade on November 22, 1963, and makes the explicit claim that Greer was the actual shooter who killed Kennedy.

## The Assassination Claim

Cooper writes: "President John F. Kennedy was murdered by the Secret Service agent who drove his car in the motorcade and the act is plainly visible in the Zapruder film. WATCH THE DRIVER AND NOT KENNEDY WHEN YOU VIEW THE FILM."

He further states: "All of the witnesses who were close enough to the car to see William Greer shoot Kennedy were themselves all murdered within two years of the event." Cooper calculates that "the odds against the first 18 to die within two years of the assassination were calculated at one hundred thousand trillion to one."

## Source of the Claim

Cooper states he first encountered this claim in "Top Secret documents" read while in Naval Intelligence, which "stated that the act was plainly visible in a film withheld from the public." He writes that he spent 16 years looking for such a film. In December 1988, during a phone conversation with John Lear, he described what he had read; Lear then showed him a video titled *Dallas Revisited*, which Cooper states confirmed his interpretation.

Cooper states he was later given a copy of the video and showed it "whenever I spoke to a group of people." He identifies Lars Hansson as the originator of that version of the Zapruder film.

## The Greer-Grodin Controversy

Cooper describes a dispute with Bob Grodin, whom he identifies as a government operative. Cooper states that Grodin "blatantly lied to the committee [the House Select Committee on Assassinations in 1976] and told them that the driver, William Greer, never took his hands off the wheel" — and that a film showing Greer with both hands on the wheel was fabricated. Cooper writes: "Grodin knows that Greer shot Kennedy because he is part of the cover-up."

Cooper states that Grodin subsequently appeared on television "stating that Greer killed Kennedy!" — which Cooper interprets as an attempt to co-opt and discredit the claim through an unreliable source.

## Orders

Cooper asserts that Greer acted "on orders of the Illuminati" and states: "You cannot welcome the New World Order if you have faith in your government. You WILL have faith in your government if you learn that Greer killed Kennedy on orders of the Illuminati and that it had nothing to do with the legal, Constitutional government."

## Post-Assassination Connection

Cooper implies a direct post-assassination connection between the Secret Service and the Rockefeller family, writing: "Did you know that the man who was in charge of the Secret Service at the time of the assassination became the man in charge of security for the Rockefeller family upon his retirement?"

## Primary Textual Appearances

Greer is discussed at length in Chapter Twelve ("The Secret Government") of Cooper's book, in the section concerning the Kennedy assassination and the film evidence Cooper claims to have seen.
