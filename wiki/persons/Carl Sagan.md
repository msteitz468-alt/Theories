## Biographical Details from Source

Carl Sagan (1934–1996) was an astronomer and planetary scientist and one of the 20th century's
foremost science communicators. In *The Demon-Haunted World* he writes as a working scientist and
skeptic defending evidence-based reasoning against pseudoscience and superstition, drawing on his own
experience fielding public questions about science, UFOs, and the paranormal. He presents himself as
combining hard-nosed skepticism with a sense of wonder, and as concerned that scientific illiteracy
endangers democracy.
