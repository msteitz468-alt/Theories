**Biographical Details from Source**

Born in the village of Montazels, a few miles from Rennes-le-Château. Received his first appointment as parish priest at Rennes-le-Château on June 1st, 1885, aged thirty-three. Described as "robust, handsome, energetic and ... highly intelligent." He had seemed destined for a promising clerical career in seminary school, but at some point incurred the displeasure of his superiors. He employed as housekeeper an eighteen-year-old peasant girl, Marie Denarnaud, who was to be his lifelong companion and confidante.

Between 1885 and 1891 his income averaged the equivalent of £6 sterling per year. He hunted, fished, read voraciously, perfected his Latin, and began studying Greek and Hebrew. He was a close friend of the Abbé Henri Boudet, curé of Rennes-les-Bains.

In 1891 he embarked on modest restoration of the village church and discovered four parchments in a hollow Visigoth column. He was immediately dispatched to Paris by his bishop to present the parchments to Abbé Bieil (Director General of the Seminary of Saint-Sulpice) and Émile Hoffet. In Paris, he was introduced to Hoffet's distinguished esoteric circle, which included Stéphane Mallarmé, Maurice Maeterlinck, Claude Debussy, and the opera singer Emma Calvé — whom he may have taken as a lover.

Beginning in 1896, Saunière began to spend millions of unexplained francs. Among his notable guests: Emma Calvé, the French Secretary of State for Culture, and Archduke Johann von Habsburg. He defied his bishop's demands for an accounting, was suspended by a local tribunal, and was exonerated by the Vatican — suggesting the Vatican had its own interest in the affair.

He was found in suspiciously good health on January 12, 1917; on the same date Marie Denarnaud ordered a coffin for him. On January 17 he suffered a stroke. A priest called to hear his confession refused to administer the last rites and reportedly "never smiled again." Saunière died unabsolved on January 22, 1917. His body was placed in an armchair on the Tour Magdala, and mourners filed past plucking tassels from his robe in an unexplained ceremony. He had transferred all his wealth to Marie Denarnaud before his death and was declared penniless.
