## Biographical Details from Source

The intelligence officer of the 509th Bomb Group at Roswell Army Air Field who, in July 1947, recovered the crash debris from Mac Brazel's ranch and is the central first-hand witness of *The Roswell Incident*. Named in the original 1947 press release as having recovered "the disc," Marcel described the wreckage as metallic, extraordinarily tough, bearing "hieroglyphic-like" markings, and "nothing made on this earth," and was ordered to fly it to higher headquarters. His later testimony to the authors is foundational to the modern Roswell case.

## Primary Source Appearances

Central witness throughout *The Roswell Incident* (Chs. 2–3).
