## Biographical Details from Source

Edward Kelley was the scryer who worked with [[John Dee (1527–1608-9)]] to receive the Enochian
revelations. Per *Success Magick*, their experiments began in 1582, with "the most fruitful period
occurring in 1584": Dee called to the angels while Kelley "saw visions and heard the angels'
instructions," which Dee recorded — yielding the angelical language and alphabet, the Calls (or Keys),
and the names and duties of celestial entities, often revealed "one letter at a time."

Brand stresses that the historical record shows Dee and Kelley as "pious, obstinate, angry, deceptive,
confused, contradictory" rather than serene sages, and recounts the episode in which Kelley, wanting
to quit, returned from upstairs with a "reformed" Watchtower Table and a relayed angelic instruction
that the two men swap wives — the origin of the central dispute over which Table is authentic (see
[[Which Enochian Watchtower Table is authentic — the original or Kelley's reformed Table]]). Brand
argues the magic works regardless, even if Kelley "duped poor Dee," since he "might have unconsciously
channelled something genuine."
