## Biographical Details from Source

Per Griffin, John D. Rockefeller "made his initial fortune in oil but soon gravitated into banking and finance." His entry was "not welcomed by Morgan, and they became fierce competitors," until "they decided to minimize their competition by entering into joint ventures" and "worked together to create a national banking cartel called the Federal Reserve System." Griffin notes Rockefeller's dime-giving publicity stunts and large-scale philanthropy as image management.

The source identifies the Rockefeller group (with [[Frank Vanderlip]]'s National City Bank representing William Rockefeller) as one of the two American financial orbits — Morgan and Rockefeller — represented at Jekyll Island, and states the Rockefellers later came to dominate the [[Council on Foreign Relations and the Round Table network|Council on Foreign Relations]] and the drive for world government. Nelson Aldrich's daughter married John D. Rockefeller Jr.
