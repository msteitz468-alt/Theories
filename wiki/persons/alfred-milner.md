## Biographical Details from Source

Per Quigley, Alfred (later Lord) Milner was a devoted disciple of John Ruskin at Oxford and, with Cecil Rhodes and W. T. Stead, a founding figure of the secret society organized on February 5, 1891 — sitting on its executive committee. As High Commissioner of South Africa (1897–1905), Milner recruited a group of young Oxford men ("Milner's Kindergarten") who "became the dominant influence in British imperial and foreign affairs up to 1939."

Quigley states that in 1909–1913 this group "organized semisecret groups, known as Round Table Groups, in the chief British dependencies and the United States," coordinated through the quarterly *The Round Table* (founded 1910, financed by Sir Abe Bailey). Milner was the chief Rhodes Trustee. Quigley calls the power of "this Rhodes-Milner group in British imperial affairs and in foreign policy since 1889 ... hardly [able] to be exaggerated."
