## Biographical Details from Source

The source describes Marie Denarnaud as an eighteen-year-old peasant girl whom Saunière employed as housekeeper and servant when he arrived at Rennes-le-Château in 1885. She became his "lifelong companion and confidante," sharing his life and secrets for thirty-two years.

**Role in the Saunière affair**

The source records that before Saunière's death, "he had apparently transferred the whole of his wealth to Marie Denarnaud … Or perhaps most of that wealth had been in Marie's name from the very beginning." After Saunière's death, the reading of his will revealed him to be "utterly penniless."

Marie Denarnaud had ordered a coffin for Saunière on 12 January 1917 — five days before his stroke — a date on which, the source notes, his parishioners declared he had seemed "in enviable health for a man of his age." This detail the source treats as one of several suspicious circumstances surrounding Saunière's final days.

The source also notes that Marie Denarnaud "burning her inheritance of banknotes" was one of the anomalies that could not be explained by the hypothesis of simple treasure: "And Marie herself had promised to divulge a 'secret' which conferred not merely wealth but 'power' as well."

**Role according to a later "Prieuré publication"**

A more recent "Prieuré" publication cited by the source claims that Marie Denarnaud "was in fact Boudet's agent. It was through her that Boudet supposedly transmitted instructions to Saunière. And it was to her that all money was made payable." On this account, the Abbé Henri Boudet (curé of Rennes-les-Bains and Saunière's best friend) directed operations at Rennes-le-Château, and Denarnaud was his conduit to Saunière.

The source states that "there is reason to conclude that the bulk of Saunière's revenue was paid to him by Boudet, through the intermediary Marie Denarnaud, Saunière's housekeeper."

Saunière died without divulging his secret. "So did his housekeeper, Marie Denarnaud."
