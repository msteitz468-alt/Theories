## Biographical Details from Source

The claimed author and narrator of the tablets: an immortal Atlantean priest-king born in the city of Keor on the island of Undal, son of Thotme, taught by the Dweller, made free of the Halls of Amenti and granted the "Key of Life" so he could "taste not of death except as thou will it." After Atlantis sank he led survivors to Egypt (Khem), built the Great Pyramid, ruled ~16,000 years, then withdrew into Amenti, his soul incarnating among men — finally as "Hermes, the thrice-born." He presents himself as a teacher who leaves the tablets as "Keys" for those who come after.
