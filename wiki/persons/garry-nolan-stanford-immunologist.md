## Biographical Details from Source

Dr. Garry Nolan is a professor of pathology at Stanford University School of Medicine described by Elizondo as the scientist who conducted the most significant biological research associated with AATIP's work. Nolan brought both neurological imaging expertise and materials science capabilities (through collaboration with Jacques Vallée) to the UAP problem.

Two major research contributions attributed to Nolan in *Imminent*:

1. **Caudate-putamen neurological study**: Using MRI brain imaging of 105 individuals with reported UAP encounters, Nolan found that all 105 shared an enlarged and hyperconnected caudate-putamen — a brain region associated with pattern recognition, intuition, and rapid information integration. All subjects were described as high-IQ. The same anatomical feature was found in government remote viewers (Project Stargate) and virtually all AATIP team members.

2. **Council Bluffs material isotope analysis**: In collaboration with Dr. Jacques Vallée, Nolan analyzed a sample of molten metal recovered in Council Bluffs, Iowa, in 1977 and found isotope ratios of magnesium and iron inconsistent with any natural or known industrial process, a fractal internal matrix, and anomalous antenna-like electromagnetic properties.
