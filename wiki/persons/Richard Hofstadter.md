## Biographical Details from Source

Richard Hofstadter (1916–1970) was an American historian, a leading mid-century intellectual historian
at Columbia University. In *The Paranoid Style in American Politics* — revised from his 1963 Herbert
Spencer Lecture at Oxford and published in *Harper's* in 1964 — he writes as an "Americanist" diagnosing
a recurring rhetorical mode in U.S. politics. He is careful to say he borrows the clinical word
"paranoid" only as a metaphor for a style of expression, not to label anyone mentally ill.
