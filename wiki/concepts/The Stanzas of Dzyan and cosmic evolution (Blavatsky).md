## Definition from Source

Blavatsky presents the **Book of Dzyan** as an archaic manuscript of extraordinary antiquity, written in the Senzar language, of which only fragments are here rendered for the first time into a European language. The seven Stanzas that form the foundation of Volume I trace the abstract formula of cosmic evolution — from the state of the ONE ALL in Pralaya through the differentiation into septenary hierarchies down to the appearance of sentient Man.

### The Seven Stanzas: Summary

**Stanza I — The Night of Brahmâ (Pralaya)**
Before manifestation, the "Eternal Parent (Space) wrapped in her ever-invisible Robes slumbers." Time is not; Universal Mind is not; the Dhyan-Chohans are absorbed into Paranishpanna. The ONE FORM of Existence stretches "boundless, infinite, causeless, in dreamless sleep."

**Stanza II — Cosmic Pre-Dawn**
The Builders — the Luminous Sons of Manvantaric Dawn — rest in the "Bliss of Non-Being." The hour has not yet struck; the Ray has not yet flashed into the Germ; the Matripadma (Mother Lotus) has not yet swollen. The Universe remains "concealed in the Divine Thought and the Divine Bosom."

**Stanza III — Re-Awakening and the World-Egg**
The last vibration of the Seventh Eternity thrills through Infinitude; the Mother swells, expanding from within without like the bud of the Lotus. Darkness radiates Light; one solitary Ray drops into the Mother-Deep, shoots through the Virgin Egg, causing the Eternal Egg to thrill and drop the non-eternal (periodical) Germ — the World-Egg. Father-Mother spin a web from Spirit to Matter, fastened to spirit above and to shadowy Matter below. This web is the Universe "spun out of the two substances made in One, which is Svabhavat." Then Svabhavat sends **Fohat** to harden the atoms, and each atom, reflecting the Self-Existent Lord like a mirror, becomes in turn a world.

**Stanza IV — The Septenary Hierarchy of Divine Powers**
From the Effulgency of Light spring the re-awakened energies — the Essences, the Flames, the Builders. These are the Dhyan-Chohans, organised as the "Army of the Voice." The second seven are the Lipika, the cosmic scribes of Karmic record. This stage corresponds to the Hindu "Creation of the Gods" and is the differentiation of the Germ into the hierarchy of conscious Divine Powers who are the active manifestations of the One Supreme Energy.

**Stanza V — Fohat and World-Formation**
The Primordial Seven produce from their circumgyrating breaths the fiery whirlwind. Dzyu becomes **Fohat** — the Swift Son, steed of Divine Thought — who runs circular errands, calls the innumerable Sparks, forms germs of wheels, places them in the six directions of Space with one at the centre, and traces spiral lines uniting the sixth to the seventh. From one Light, seven Lights; from each of the seven, seven times seven.

**Stanza VI — Formation of the Solar System and Planetary Chain**
Fohat produces the seven Laya Centres and seats the Universe on eternal foundations. The progressive manifestation of the seven cosmic elements unfolds: one manifested then six concealed, and so on, until "seven small wheels revolving, one giving birth to the other." Fohat collects the fiery dust, makes balls of fire, sets them in motion — some hot, some cold, some moist, some dry — thus acting "from one twilight to the other during seven eternities." At the Fourth stage, one third of the creative beings refuse to create their images; the rest obey. The "first war" and the "curse" of involuntary birth into matter follow.

**Stanza VII — The Descent of Life to Man**
Sentient formless life begins. The One Ray multiplies; life precedes form and survives the last atom of form. The Spark hangs from the Flame by the finest thread of Fohat and journeys through the seven worlds of Maya — stopping first as metal and stone, passing into plant, whirling through seven changes to become "a sacred animal," from whose combined attributes Manu (the Thinker) is formed. The thread between the Silent Watcher and his shadow grows ever stronger, until the Builders don their first clothing and descend on radiant Earth to reign over men — who are themselves.

## Key Terms

| Term | Blavatsky's meaning |
|---|---|
| Pralaya | Cosmic dissolution / Night of Brahmâ |
| Manvantara | Cycle of active manifestation |
| Dhyan-Chohans | Hierarchy of celestial intelligences / Builders |
| Fohat | Dynamic cosmic electricity; the bridge between Cosmic Mind and Matter |
| Svabhavat | The "Plastic Essence" filling the Universe; the Buddhistic concrete absolute |
| Lipika | Cosmic scribes who record karma and set the Ring "Pass Not" |
| Alaya | Universal Soul / Anima Mundi |
| Laya Centres | Neutral, zero-points where spirit transitions to matter |

## Cross-source Connections

- The Hermetic axiom "As above, so below" (attributed to [[Hermes Trismegistus]]) is implicit throughout: every macrocosmic process is mirrored in the microcosm of Man.
- The Kybalion's Principle of Vibration — that everything is in motion — parallels the Great Breath as the first stirring of manifestation: see [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]].
- Blavatsky's cosmogenesis is the claimed ancient source for the perennial philosophy outlined broadly in [[The Secret Teachings of All Ages (Hall)]].
