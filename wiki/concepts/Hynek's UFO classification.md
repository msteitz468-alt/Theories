## Definition / Characterization from Source

J. Allen Hynek's systematic taxonomy of UFO reports, organized by the observational circumstances rather than by any assumed cause. Distant sightings fall into three classes — **Nocturnal Lights** (lights in the night sky), **Daylight Discs** (daytime objects), and **Radar-Visual** (sightings confirmed simultaneously by radar and eyewitness). Sightings at close range (roughly within 500 feet) he groups as **Close Encounters**, in three grades:

- **Close Encounter of the First Kind (CE-I)** — a UFO observed at close range with no physical interaction or trace.
- **Close Encounter of the Second Kind (CE-II)** — a close encounter leaving measurable physical effects (scorched or depressed ground, affected vehicles/animals, physiological effects on witnesses).
- **Close Encounter of the Third Kind (CE-III)** — a close encounter in which animate occupants or "entities" are reported.

Hynek pairs the classification with two evaluative scales: a **Strangeness Rating** (how far a report departs from the ordinary / resists prosaic explanation) and a witness **Probability (credibility) Rating** (the reliability and number of witnesses) — a report being significant only when both are high.
