# The Tree of Life (Otz Chiim)

## Definition from Source

In *The Mystical Qabalah*, [[Dion Fortune (Violet Mary Firth, 1890–1946)]] presents the **Tree of Life** (Hebrew *Otz Chiim*) as the central glyph of the Qabalah and "the ground-plan of the Western Esoteric Tradition." It is a composite symbol of ten **Sephiroth** (emanations) joined by connecting **Paths**, arranged in three **Pillars** and reflecting the **Four Worlds**. Fortune calls it "the best meditation-symbol we possess because it is the most comprehensive" — a mystical filing-cabinet or "mind-machine" onto which all the phenomena of the universe, the gods, and the levels of consciousness can be classified and inter-related.

This is the umbrella concept for Fortune's system. See the individual emanations and structural components:

- Emanations: [[Kether (the First Sephirah)]], [[Chokmah (the Second Sephirah)]], [[Binah (the Third Sephirah)]], [[Chesed (the Fourth Sephirah)]], [[Geburah (the Fifth Sephirah)]], [[Tiphareth (the Sixth Sephirah)]], [[Netzach (the Seventh Sephirah)]], [[Hod (the Eighth Sephirah)]], [[Yesod (the Ninth Sephirah)]], [[Malkuth (the Tenth Sephirah)]]
- Structure: [[The Three Supernals]], [[The Four Worlds of the Qabalah]], [[The Paths upon the Tree of Life]], [[The Negative Veils of Existence (Ain, Ain Soph, Ain Soph Aur)]], [[The Qliphoth (the averse Sephiroth)]]
