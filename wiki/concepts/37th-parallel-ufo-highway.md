**Definition (from the source)**

The "**37th parallel**" or "**UFO Highway**" (also "Paranormal Highway") is [[Chuck Zukowski]]'s name for a roughly east–west corridor of latitude near 37° N across the United States along which, he argues, an outsized share of UFO sightings, animal mutilations, abduction reports, and other "high strangeness" occur — and which also threads through major military bases and Native American sacred sites.

**Use in the source**

Zukowski noticed the pattern after observing that earthquake and incident coordinates he was tracking kept landing near 37° N (e.g. the Sangre de Cristo fault event at 37.0412° N). He concedes the line is **not exact** — many anomalies sit nearer the 36th or 38th — so he frames it as a band "between the 38th and 36th parallels." Sites the book plots on or near it include: Joplin Spook Lights (37.09), the Mantell Incident (37.77), Aztec NM (36.83), Taos, Scammon KS (37.28), Area 51 (37.62), NORAD/Cheyenne Mountain, Fort Knox (37.91), Mammoth Cave, Zion, Monument Valley, Chaco Canyon, and the Bristlecone Pine Forest. (Roswell, on the 33rd, is rationalized as lying on the flight path from Kenneth Arnold's Mount Rainier sighting.)

His co-investigator [[Debbie Ziegelmeyer]] cautions that "some of this has got to be coincidence ... related to the reporting mechanisms," but agrees the correlation exceeds what she would expect by chance. By the book's end Zukowski reframes the corridor not as a "highway" but as **a runway** — see [[The 37th parallel is 'a runway, not a highway']].
