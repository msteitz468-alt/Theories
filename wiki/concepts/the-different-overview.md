## Definition / Characterization from Source

Robert Monroe's term in *Ultimate Journey* for the radically altered "recognition of reality" he arrived at through decades of out-of-body experience — a "clear way of perceiving" that reframes physical life, death, and human identity. It rests on an epistemology of **"Knowns" versus "Beliefs"**: a *Known* is something verified through direct personal experience, whereas "belief" is "a currently popular label for anything that cannot be fully understood or identified." The goal is to convert fear-producing **Unknowns** into Knowns, since "fear is the great barrier to human growth" and "Unknowns create fears."

The foundational Known of the Different Overview is **survival of physical death** — "not just hoping, having faith, or believing, but knowing." Monroe insists the Different Overview "can be at most only a belief until you begin to test it for validity within your own ongoing experience," and that it must be built one small verified Known at a time "until you are free."
