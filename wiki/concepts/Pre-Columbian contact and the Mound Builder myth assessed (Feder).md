## Definition from Source

Feder assesses two linked claims about the peopling of the Americas: (1) that the Americas were
"discovered" before Columbus by various Old World peoples (Egyptians, Phoenicians, Israelites,
Welsh, Vikings, etc.), and (2) the **Mound Builder myth** — the long-popular belief that the great
earthworks of North America were built by some vanished superior race rather than by the ancestors of
Native Americans.

## Description from Source

Feder grants the one well-evidenced pre-Columbian contact (the Norse at L'Anse aux Meadows) but shows
most claimed transoceanic contacts fail for lack of credible artifacts, while the Mound Builder myth
was driven by **racism** — a refusal to credit Native Americans with their own monumental achievements,
conveniently justifying dispossession. It is a recurring lesson of the book that pseudo-archaeology
often serves ideological ends. See the umbrella [[Pseudo-archaeology and how to detect it (Feder)]].
