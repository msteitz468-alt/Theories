## Definition from Source

Zeland draws a fundamental distinction between desire and intention as two entirely different relationships to a goal. Desire is the concentration of attention on the goal as something lacking; intention is the concentration of attention on movement toward the goal (inner intention) or on how the goal realizes itself (outer intention).

"Realization is brought about not by desire but by one's attitude to that which is desired." (Ch. VII)
"It is not desire that is fulfilled so much as intention." (Ch. VII)

## Desire (from source)

- Concentration of attention on the goal itself
- "Desire always creates excess potential. Desire is potential by definition." (Ch. VII)
- Creates [[Excess Potential]] by framing the goal as something not yet possessed and urgently wanted
- Triggers [[Law of Balance]] counter-forces
- The three types of desire recognized (Ch. III):
  1. Pure intention (desire without importance) → fulfilled
  2. Inactive tormenting desire → wastes energy
  3. Desire dependent on an object → creates violent excess potential
- "Visualisation of your goals is the work of desire which will not bring you a single step closer to your goal." (Ch. VIII)

## Intention (from source)

- "Intention = resoluteness in the decision to have and act" (Ch. VII)
- "Intention unites desire and action" (Ch. VII)
- "Intention neither believes nor desires; it simply does." (Ch. VII)
- Does not create excess potential when purified of importance
- Three forms (in ascending power):
  1. Desire alone (no real power)
  2. [[Inner Intention]] — decision to act; concentration on process
  3. [[Outer Intention]] — decision to have; concentration on how goal realizes itself

## The Substitution (from source)

"The secret to fulfilling one's desires lies in the understanding that desire must be replaced by intention, total resoluteness in the decision to have and act." (Ch. VII)

The practical method for this substitution involves two steps:
1. Accept the possibility of failure at the outset — eliminates the stake that makes the goal feel like a desire
2. Reduce the importance of the goal — brings it within the [[Comfort Zone (Transurfing)]]
