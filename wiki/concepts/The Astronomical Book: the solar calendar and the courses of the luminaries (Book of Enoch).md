# The Astronomical Book: the solar calendar and the courses of the luminaries (Book of Enoch)

The Astronomical Book (1 Enoch chapters 72–82) is among the oldest strata of the Enochian corpus. It purports to be a revelation given to [[Enoch (antediluvian patriarch)]] by the angel Uriel, who serves as "guide" and "leader" of the luminaries. The section is also called the Book of the Courses of the Luminaries of Heaven. Its primary concern is the correct ordering of time — the paths of the sun and moon, the regulation of winds, and the defense of a 364-day solar year as the divinely ordained calendar.

## Core doctrines from the source

### The role of Uriel
The Book of Enoch teaches that Uriel, described as "the holy angel" and "the archangel, who is their leader," was appointed by the Lord of glory to have dominion over all the luminaries of heaven. He personally escorted Enoch through a comprehensive tour of celestial mechanics, showing him "all their laws exactly as they are" and the ordering of years "to eternity, until the new creation is accomplished." Enoch records that Uriel revealed "the signs and the times and the years and the days."

### The six doors of the sun
The text describes six eastern doors and six western doors through which the sun rises and sets over the course of the year. The sun spends approximately thirty mornings in each door, producing the observed lengthening and shortening of days. Door six (midsummer) produces the longest day — twelve parts of light to six of night — while door one (midwinter) reverses this, giving twelve parts of night to six of day. Equinoxes occur when the sun occupies the middle doors, producing equal nine-part days and nights.

### The 364-day solar calendar
A central polemic of the Astronomical Book is the declaration that the true year contains exactly 364 days. The text states: "the year is accurately completed in conformity with their world-stations…the sun, which rise from the doors through which the sun rises and sets 30 days" and "the year is exactly as to its days three hundred and sixty-four." Four additional "intercalary" days — positioned at the solstices and equinoxes — are accounted for by the leaders of the four heads of the year but are asserted not to appear in ordinary human calendars, causing widespread error. The Book of Enoch warns that humans "shall be at fault and not count them in the whole number of days."

### The moon and its falling behind
The Astronomical Book also tracks the moon's phases in meticulous detail — describing the waxing and waning in fourteenth parts, the 29- and 30-day months, and the moon's phases relative to the sun's position in the six doors. A key mathematical claim: in five years the sun has an excess of thirty days over the moon, and in eight years the moon falls behind by eighty days. The text insists that "the sun and the stars bring in all the years exactly" while the moon accumulates a deficit.

### The wind system and geography
Chapters 76–77 describe twelve doors at the ends of the earth from which twelve categories of winds proceed, four bringing blessing and prosperity, eight bringing destruction. The text also describes seven mountains, seven rivers, and seven islands visible to Enoch from his elevated vantage point. The north is divided into three zones: the habitation of men, seas and abysses, and the Garden of Righteousness.

### Consequences for sinners who err on the calendar
Chapter 80 delivers a prophecy that in "the days of the sinners" cosmic order will break down: years shall be shortened, harvests delayed, the moon will alter her customs, and the stars "shall transgress their customary order." The sinners will be confused and "take them to be gods." Chapter 82 closes with a blessing on the righteous — those who count the calendar correctly — and a warning that leaders of the stars and the four annual divisions have been appointed precisely so the year's reckoning cannot err.

### Transmission to Methuselah
Enoch addresses Methuselah throughout the Astronomical Book, instructing him to preserve these revelations in books "for the generations of the world." The seven holy angels return Enoch to earth for one year to complete his instructions before taking him away.
