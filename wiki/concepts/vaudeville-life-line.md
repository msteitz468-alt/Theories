## Definition from Source

The vaudeville life line is a specific type of [[Life Lines|life line]] characterized by the absence of complex problems. Zeland introduces it in Ch. VII in the context of the "Game According to Your Rules" section as a target life line accessible through intention.

"Vaudeville life line = no complex problems." (Ch. VII)
"To shift to this life line all that is required is the intention to imagine to yourself that it is so." (Ch. VII)

## Key Properties (from source)

The source describes the vaudeville life line through the technique of imagining a problematic situation as a trivial matter — as if it were a mere trifle in a light comedic play (vaudeville). By holding this image, the person intends themselves onto a life line where the situation genuinely has no great significance.

The technique illustrates the principle that the [[Alternatives Space]] contains life lines to match any parameters of thought energy — including a life line where one's current problem is simply not serious.
