## Concept Definition

**The Speakers** are a group of physical and non-physical personalities who speak to humanity throughout history. Their primary role is to preserve and transmit eternal, inner knowledge regarding the nature of reality, the soul, and multidimensional consciousness, ensuring that this wisdom is never fully forgotten.

They communicate through direct speech, trance mediumship, dreams, and sudden intuitive insights. Their teachings form the esoteric core of major world religions, although these messages are frequently distorted by subsequent dogmatic and institutional developments.

## Historical and Anthropological Context

According to Seth:
* **Pre-literate Traditions:** The Speakers existed in early tribes in Africa and Australia. They memorized vast bodies of cosmological and psychic wisdom and spread it orally across generations.
* **Pre-Christian Europe:** Prior to the rise of Christianity, the Speakers travelled throughout northern Europe, maintaining and spreading the core secrets of consciousness before they were written down.
* **Historical Manuscripts:** During periods when the knowledge was suppressed, the Speakers often operated secretly, copying and burying manuscripts to preserve the data for future generations.
* **The Speakers' Voice:** Jane Roberts describes the Speakers as a collective presence. She frames them as "voices, intuitions, flashes of insights and messages" through which the universe addresses each individual personally. 

## Key Teachings of the Speakers

The Speakers' primary message is to direct humanity to look within the self to find spiritual truth, rather than relying on external organizations, hierarchies, or holy books. They emphasize:
* The direct, intimate connection of every individual to All-That-Is.
* The illusory nature of physical matter and time.
* The absolute, eternal safety and validity of the soul.
