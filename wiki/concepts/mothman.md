## Definition / Characterization from Source

The winged creature reported around Point Pleasant, West Virginia, in 1966–67. Witnesses describe a roughly seven-foot, broad, gray, man-shaped being with a ~10-foot wingspan and, above all, large round **glowing red eyes** that fixed on percipients "with hypnotic effect." It rose vertically "straight up like a helicopter, silently," its wings not flapping. The first prominent sighting was November 15, 1966, by two young couples (the Scarberrys and Mallettes) in the **TNT area** — an abandoned WWII explosives dump of concrete "igloos" outside town. Several witnesses suffered conjunctivitis / "eyeburn" (Keel attributes it to actinic/UV rays) after the encounters.

Keel sets Mothman within a worldwide tradition of winged beings, giant birds, and red-eyed "demons" (the Thunderbird/Piasa, the Garuda, 1905 "airship-birds," etc.), treating it not as a single biological animal but as one more manifestation of the [[Ultraterrestrials|ultraterrestrial]] phenomenon.
