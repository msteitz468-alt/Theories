# Gnostic demons (Bane)

## Definition from Source

According to Bane's *Encyclopedia of Demons in World Religions and Cultures*, Gnostic demonology arises from the diverse second-century C.E. sectarian movements (most prominently the Basilidians of Alexandria and the Ophites) that viewed the material world as the flawed creation of an inferior or evil deity — the Demiurge — rather than the true transcendent God. Gnostic demonic figures appear in Greek magical papyri, Coptic gospel texts (such as the *Gospel of the Egyptians*), and later alchemy and Hermetic literature.

The organizing principle in Gnostic demonology is cosmic hierarchy: below the "unknown father" (the true God) stand the Demiurge and his seven elohim (creative angels), who shaped the visible universe and matter. These demiurgic figures — sometimes called Archons — are associated with ignorance, pride, and material entrapment rather than outright malice; yet in the Ophite tradition especially, the chief Archon Ialdabaoth is depicted as actively deceptive and malevolent. A key Gnostic motif Bane records is that some entities classified as "demons" by other traditions appear in Gnostic sources as cosmic administrators of the lower planes, neither fully evil nor fully good.

Notable structural features Bane identifies: the existence of named Archons whose names and attributes partially overlap with Kabbalistic and Enochian demonology; the figure of Barbelo as a female Archon and one of the eight Arch She-Demons; and the repeated appearance of Abrasax/Abraxas, the supreme deity of the Basilidians, who accumulated both divine and demonic attributes in later traditions.

## Notable Entities

**Ialdabaoth** — In alchemy, Gnostic, Hebrew, and Phoenician mythology, Ialdabaoth is one of the seven elohim who created the visible universe, the demiurges directly below the "unknown father." His name means "artisan" in ancient Greek and "child of chaos" in Hebrew. A Fallen Angel formerly of the Order of Thrones and a supreme Archon, he is said to have the head of a lion. He created the Lower World, the physical aspects of the universe, and humanity's physical nature, along with seven further elohim in his own image. Ignorant, proud, and vengeful, he resides in the darkness of matter. According to the Ophites, he was born the child of Sophia, Christ's sister.

**Abrasax** — The title given to the supreme deity worshipped by the second-century Gnostic Basilidians of Alexandria; possibly borrowed from Persian mythology. His numerous descriptions include a human torso with the head of a rooster and snakes for legs; a hydralikelike creature; a king with clawed dragonlike feet; a man with a lion's head and scorpions for feet. He commands 365 different heavens, one for each day of the year, and is lord of 365 virtues. His holy number is 365. After his demonization he was associated with the dual nature of Satan, and his name was linked to the word "Abracadabra." He appears in Greek magical papyri and Gnostic texts such as the Gospel of the Egyptians.

**Barbelo** — Listed in Gnostic texts as one of the eight Arch She-Demons; her worshippers were called Barbelgnostics. She is variously identified with Athena, Sophia, Noria, Achamoth, and the Light-Maiden of the Pistis Sophia. In the Gnostic schema she represents a complex female emanation — at once a heavenly wisdom figure and a demonized archonic power.

**Glykon** — In Gnostic-Mithraic demonology, Glykon ("the sweet one") is believed to be an incarnation of an archonic power. He is named as one of the Dukes of Hell in this mixed tradition and is summoned to discover treasure locations, foretell past, present, and future, and obtain love for a woman.

**Hutriel** — According to Gnostic lore, Hutriel ("rod of God") is one of the seven Angels of Punishment. In the Theurgia Goetia he is ranked as supervisor of the fifth division of Hell, described as looking like an ass, and characterized as hostile by nature.

**Archon Daimonion** — A Gnostic-influenced designation (Greek: "daimonic ruler") recorded by Bane as a variant of the names Baal and Achor; represents the class of Archontic powers that govern the lower material planes, acting as cosmic administrators rather than purely destructive demons.
