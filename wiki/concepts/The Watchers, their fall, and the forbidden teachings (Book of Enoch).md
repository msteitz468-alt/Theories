## Definition and Characterisation from Source

The Book of Enoch narrates that a class of divine beings called the **Watchers** (also termed "the sons of heaven" and "the holy ones") were appointed to observe and guard humanity. Two hundred of these beings, led by **Semjaza** (also spelled Semyaza, Samlazaz), descended to earth in the days of Jared, landing on **Mount Hermon** — a mountain the text says took its name from the oath ("hermon" = curse/oath) the Watchers swore together to carry out their plan (ch. 6). They swore a binding mutual oath so that no individual could back out and bear the penalty alone.

The central transgression was that the Watchers "left the high heaven, the holy eternal place" (ch. 15:3), took human women as wives, "defiled themselves with them," and begot children. The text frames this as an abandonment of their spiritual nature: "Though you were holy, spiritual, living the eternal life, you have defiled yourselves with the blood of women" (ch. 15:4). The Lord's reply to their intercession through Enoch is that spiritual beings were never given wives because heaven was their proper dwelling.

### The Forbidden Teachings (ch. 7–8, ch. 69)

The Watchers did not merely take wives — they transmitted esoteric and technical knowledge that humanity was not meant to possess:

- **Azazel**: metallurgy (swords, knives, shields, breastplates), working metals of the earth, bracelets and ornaments, use of antimony, beautifying of eyelids (cosmetics), precious stones and dyes. The text declares of Azazel: "to him ascribe ALL SIN" (ch. 10:8) and states he "taught all unrighteousness on earth and revealed the eternal secrets which were made and kept in heaven" (ch. 9:6).
- **Semjaza**: casting of spells and root-cuttings
- **Armaros**: counter-spells (release from spells)
- **Baraqijal**: astrology
- **Kokabel**: knowledge of the constellations / portents
- **Ezeqeel**: knowledge of the clouds
- **Araqiel**: signs of the earth
- **Shamsiel**: signs of the sun
- **Sariel**: course of the moon
- **Gadreel** (ch. 69): showed the children of men "all the blows of death" — the shield, coat of mail, sword — and is said to have led Eve astray
- **Penemue** (ch. 69): taught the bitter and the sweet; taught mankind writing with ink and paper — the text regards literacy itself as a corrupting gift
- **Kasdeja** (ch. 69): showed wicked smitings of spirits and demons, smitings of the embryo in the womb, and the bites of the serpent

The cumulative message is that human suffering, warfare, sorcery, and spiritual corruption all trace back to knowledge the Watchers disclosed from heaven's secret store.

### Judgment and Binding

After the heavenly archangels (Michael, Uriel, Raphael, Gabriel) observed the devastation and cried to heaven, the Lord issued decrees:

- **Raphael** was commanded to bind **Azazel** hand and foot, cast him into a split in the desert of **Dudael**, cover him with rough stones and darkness, and keep him there until the Day of Judgment when he shall be hurled into fire (ch. 10:4–6).
- **Gabriel** was commanded to destroy the Nephilim offspring by turning them against each other in battle (ch. 10:9).
- **Michael** was commanded to bind **Semjaza** and his associates "under the hills of the earth" for seventy generations, then consign them to the abyss of fire forever (ch. 10:11–13).

Enoch himself was summoned to deliver the verdict to the condemned Watchers. His intercession — he wrote their petition and prayed at the waters of Dan — failed. The divine response (ch. 14–16) is direct: "Your petition will not be granted. From here on you shall not ascend into heaven again for all eternity, and you will be bound on earth for all eternity." The Watchers, who should have interceded for men, instead required men to intercede for them — an ironic inversion of their purpose.

The text adds that the spirits of the dead Nephilim — being neither fully angelic nor fully human — became evil spirits on earth, afflicting and oppressing the living until the Final Judgment (ch. 15:8–12, ch. 16:1).
