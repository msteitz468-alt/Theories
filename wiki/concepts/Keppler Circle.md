## Definition / Characterization from Source

The **Keppler Circle** (later expanded and renamed **Heinrich Himmler's S.S. Circle of Friends** or *Freundeskreis Himmler*) was a group of prominent German industrialists, bankers, and representatives of multinational corporations who provided direct financial subsidies, political backing, and industrial assistance to the Nazi S.S.

According to *Wall Street and the Rise of Hitler*:
* **Origins:** The group was originally formed in 1932 by Wilhelm Keppler (Hitler's financial expert) at Hitler's request, to serve as a bridge between the Nazi Party and Germany's business elite. Following the Nazi seizure of power in 1933, the circle expanded under the guidance of Heinrich Himmler.
* **Wall Street and Multinational Representation:** Sutton highlights that representatives of Wall Street-connected multinationals were heavily represented in the Circle. Key members included:
  * **Kurt von Schröder:** Senior partner in the J. Henry Schroder Banking Company and S.S. Senior Group Leader, who acted as the primary treasurer and financial conduit for the Circle.
  * **Hermann Schmitz:** Director of I.G. Farben.
  * **Karl Lindemann:** Director of the Standard Oil subsidiary Deutsche-Amerikanische Petroleum A.G. (DAPAG).
  * **Emil Meyer:** Director of the I.T.T. subsidiary Standard Elektrizitäts-Gesellschaft.
* **Financial Subsidies to the S.S.:** The members of the Circle deposited hundreds of thousands of Reichsmarks annually into a special "S" account managed by Kurt von Schröder at the Stein Bank in Cologne. These funds were placed directly at Heinrich Himmler's personal disposal to finance S.S. operations, research projects (including occult expeditions), and activities. In exchange, the participating corporations received political protection, lucrative state contracts, and favorable treatment under the Nazi regime.
