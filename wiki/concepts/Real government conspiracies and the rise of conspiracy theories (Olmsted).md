## Definition from Source

The umbrella thesis of *Real Enemies*: American conspiracy theories acquired their plausibility and
power because the U.S. government *itself* repeatedly conspired, lied, and covered up across the
twentieth century. Olmsted argues that "the most dangerous conspiracies" were often the government's
own, and that proven official deception — together with the government's own *conspiracy theories
about its enemies* — corroded democratic trust and seeded the modern conspiracy culture. Emblematic
case: **Operation Northwoods** (the 1962 Joint Chiefs plan to stage terrorist attacks on Americans
and blame Castro).
