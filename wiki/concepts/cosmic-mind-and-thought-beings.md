## Definition / Characterization from Source

In Mark Booth's account, ultimate reality is a **cosmic mind**, and everything else is its emanation. Gods, angels, and spirits are "emanations from the great cosmic mind — **Thought-Beings**." These are not metaphors but quasi-independent living intelligences (Booth compares them to Tibetan *tulpas* and to Paracelsus's "aquastor," beings formed by concentrated imagination that can become "visible, even tangible"). At the lowest rung of the hierarchy, "these emanations… interweave so tightly that they create the appearance of solid matter."

A corollary Booth stresses is that in antiquity the relationship of mind and person was the reverse of ours: "if today we naturally think of people thinking, in ancient times they thought of Thoughts peopling" — the great Thought-Beings "expressed themselves through people." This is the philosophy academics call **idealism**: the cosmic mind and its ideas are "the higher reality," physical objects "mere shadows or reflections."
