**Definition (from the source)**

The *shar* is, in Sitchin's reading, the **orbital period of [[The Twelfth Planet (Nibiru)]] — 3,600 Earth-years** — and the key that unlocks the "impossible" reign-lengths of the Sumerian and Babylonian king lists. Sitchin argues the single Sumerian term *shar* simultaneously denotes (a) the number 3,600 (written as a large circle), (b) "a perfect circle / completed cycle," and (c) the epithet "supreme ruler" for the planet — and that this triple identity of *planet / orbit / 3,600* "could not be a mere coincidence."

**Use in the source**

- One *shar* = 3,600 Earth-years = one orbit of the Twelfth Planet around the Sun.
- The Nefilim measured time on Earth in *shars*; what reads as "years" in the king lists (and as the *ma*/"period" of the labour texts) are really these orbital cycles.
- Berossus and the Sumerian king list W-B/144 give the ten pre-Diluvial rulers a combined reign of **120 shars = 432,000 years**, "reaching to the time of the Deluge."
- The Anunnaki are said to have laboured **40 shars (144,000 years)** before mutinying; with the landing ~450,000 years ago, this places the creation of man ~300,000 years ago.
- Because the planet's "year" is so long, the Nefilim's lifespans appear "immortal" to short-lived humans.
