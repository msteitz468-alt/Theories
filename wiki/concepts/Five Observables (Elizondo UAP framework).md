## Definition from Source

The Five Observables is an analytical framework developed by Luis Elizondo within AATIP to provide an objective, intelligence-community-based methodology for distinguishing genuine UAP from misidentified conventional aircraft, balloons, atmospheric phenomena, or adversary technology. An object that exhibits one or more of the Five Observables exceeds the performance envelope of any known human technology and warrants classification as a genuine UAP warranting further investigation.

## The Five Observables

1. **Hypersonic velocity** — Sustained flight at Mach 17 or greater at sea level, without sonic boom, without thermal bloom, without detectable exhaust or propulsion signature
2. **Instantaneous acceleration** — Transition from hypersonic velocity to hover or to right-angle turn instantaneously, without the structural forces that would destroy any known aircraft or biological occupant
3. **Low observability** — No radar cross-section, no infrared signature, no acoustic signature, no communications emissions; the object is effectively undetectable by all standard sensor modalities until it chooses otherwise
4. **Transmedium travel** — Seamless transition between air, water, and space without structural reconfiguration, speed reduction, or observable cavitation effects
5. **Positive lift** (antigravity) — Sustained flight without conventional wings, rotors, thrust vectoring, or any visible propulsion system

**Sixth unofficial observable**: **Biological effects** — physical harm to human witnesses including radiation burns, neurological changes, cancer, cardiac damage, implants, and altered brain anatomy. Not formally codified to protect the program from further institutional ridicule.

## Conceptual Significance

Elizondo designed the framework to be usable by any trained intelligence analyst without prior UAP background. The five criteria are each grounded in measurable physical or engineering limits — they define what known human technology cannot do. Any object exhibiting even one criterion is, by definition, operating beyond the frontier of current human aerospace capability.

The framework was later adopted informally by the UAP Task Force and informed the analytical methodology of AARO.

Hal Puthoff's warp bubble theory (Chapter 16) provides a unified physical mechanism that explains all Six Observables simultaneously.
