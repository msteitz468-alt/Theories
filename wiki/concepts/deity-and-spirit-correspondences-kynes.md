## Definition from Source

Chapter Five of Kynes' reference work covers **deities and other beings** as one entire axis of the
correspondence web. The organizing premise is that herbs, stones, animals, runes, and other
correspondences can serve as "meaningful touchstones to connect with" a deity or spirit — extending
practical work beyond candles and figurines. Kynes writes that the deities "are more than myths handed
down from the ancients; they are alive and immanent in the natural world as well as in our lives."

The chapter is divided into three broad subsections:

1. **Goddesses** — individual goddesses from Celtic, Greek, Roman, Egyptian, Norse, Hindu, Aztec, and
   other traditions, each with a multi-correspondence profile.
2. **Gods** — parallel treatment of male deities across the same pantheons.
3. **Magical Beings and Spirits** — entities Kynes placed in a separate section because her sources
   split between calling them gods and spirits: the Fates (Greek), the Norns (Norse), the Lares and
   Penates (Roman), as well as fairy types, elemental beings (Sylphs, Undines, Salamanders, Gnomes),
   dryads, mermaids, brownies, pixies, gnomes, elves, and the four archangels (Auriel, Gabriel,
   Michael, Raphael).

Each entry gives: a brief description and cultural origin; planetary/solar associations; zodiac
signs; sabbats; element; direction; color; number; tarot card; runes; Ogham; trees, herbs, and
miscellaneous plants; gemstones and metals; animals and birds; and a list of "Issues, Intentions &
Powers" for ritual use.

## Representative Correspondences

The following examples convey the breadth of the domain. Each entry is descriptive of Pagan/Wiccan
traditional attribution, not an endorsed claim.

**Goddesses**

- **Aphrodite** (Greek, love/beauty) → Water element; Friday; number 1; copper; sea pearl and scallop;
  apple, myrtle, rose; dove, swan; intentions include love, attraction, fertility, beauty, sensuality.
- **Artemis** (Greek, animals/fertility) → Moon; Earth element; cedar, oak, willow; mugwort; bear,
  deer, wolf; intentions include animals, independence, healing, magic (moon), protection of girls.
- **Brigid** (Celtic–Ireland, healing/smithing/poetry) → Sun and Neptune; Fire element; South direction;
  blue; Imbolc celebration; rowan, oak; dandelion; intentions include creativity, healing, inspiration,
  fertility, prophecy, witches/witchcraft.
- **Cerridwen** (Celtic–Wales, fertility/knowledge) → Moon; New and Waning phases; white; number 5;
  beech, oak, willow; honeysuckle; cat, pig, wolf; intentions include wisdom, transformation,
  prophecy, shape-shifting (shamanic), crone magic.
- **Hecate** (Greek, witchcraft/crossroads) → deeply connected with the Waning Moon and Samhain;
  associated with the Cailleach Bheur (Celtic winter crone) via shared attributes of death, wisdom,
  and reversal magic.
- **Isis** (Egyptian, magic/healing) → Moon, Earth, Neptune; extensive tree list (oak, olive, palm);
  lotus; intentions include the afterlife, healing, magic (general and moon), protection, rebirth.
- **Kali** (Hindu) → Saturn; black and blue; tiger; intentions include transformation, destruction,
  karma, power, liberation.
- **Lakshmi** (Hindu/Buddhist/Jain) → Friday; basil, lotus; elephant, owl; intentions include
  prosperity, beauty, abundance, spiritual growth, wishes.

**Gods**

- **Apollo** (Greek, medicine/music/prophecy) → Sun; Gemini; summer; number 1; laurel, olive, oak;
  heliotrope, sunflower; crow, raven, swan; amber, sapphire; intentions include healing, prophecy,
  creativity, truth, solar energy, transformation.
- **Anubis** (Egyptian, the dead) → Mercury; Scorpio; jackal, dog; intentions include the afterlife,
  divination, justice, protection of the dead, truth.
- **Odin** (Norse) → Mercury; Wednesday; Virgo; raven; intentions include wisdom, magic (rune),
  shamanic work, sacrifice for knowledge.
- **Thoth** (Egyptian, wisdom/time/magic) → Mercury and Moon; Monday; Air element; ibis, baboon;
  intentions include communication, justice, magic (moon), the mind, prophecy, truth, wisdom — making
  him a strong resonance point for [[Hermes Trismegistus]], to whom Thoth was syncretized in the
  Hermetic tradition (see also [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]]).
- **Zeus** (Greek) → Jupiter; Thursday; Air element; numbers 4 and 13; oak, olive; eagle, peacock;
  intentions include authority, leadership, justice, weather, marriage.

**Magical Beings and Spirits**

- **Fairies** → Moon; Saturday; Air and Earth elements; green and yellow; number 9; hawthorn, elder,
  oak, rowan (fairy trees); many flowers (bluebell, foxglove, primrose, thyme); emerald, jade, peridot
  attract; iron averts; intentions include enchantment, the otherworld, creativity, transformation.
- **The Fates / The Norns** → Full, Waxing, and Waning moon phases; flax; Tarot (Wheel of Fortune,
  World); intentions include destiny, divination, beginnings, endings, magic (crone), past-life memory.
- **Salamanders** (fire elementals) → Fire element; red; Tarot (Wands); intentions include action,
  control, transformation.
- **Sylphs** (air elementals) → Air element; yellow; Tarot (Swords); intentions include action,
  communication, creativity, the mind.
- **Undines** (water elementals) → Water element; blue; Tarot (Cups); intentions include divination,
  emotions, intuition.
- **Gnomes** (earth elementals) → Earth element; green; Tarot (Pentacles); intentions include
  grounding, fertility, knowledge, prosperity, secrets.
- **Archangel Raphael** → Mercury and Sun; Air and Water; East; dawn; Wednesday; numbers 1, 6, 13;
  intentions include healing, guidance, harmony, travel, intuition.
- **Archangel Michael** → Mercury and Sun; Air and Fire; South; noon; Fire element; numbers 11;
  intentions include protection, battle, justice, strength, truth.
