## Concept Definition

**7 Hz Body Resonance** (also called **cardio-sympathetic resonance** or **micro-motion**) is a physiological standing wave resonance feedback loop induced in the human body during deep meditation. It operates at approximately 7 to 7.8 Hz, entraining the brain's motor cortex and locking the individual's electromagnetic field into phase with the Earth's Schumann resonance.

## Physiological Mechanics

In the Appendix ("Stresses and the Body"), Bentov outlines the biomedical loop that generates this resonance:
1. **Heart-Aorta Recoil:** During deep meditation, breathing becomes quiet and the heart's pumping becomes regular. As the heart ejects blood, the recoil of the aorta creates a rhythmic, physical micro-motion or vibration in the body (approx 7 Hz).
2. **Standing Wave Formation:** This micro-motion travels down the body, reflecting back from the hips and limbs, forming a standing wave. This wave causes the skull to oscillate up and down by a fraction of a millimeter.
3. **Brain Entrainment:** The oscillation of the skull acts as an acoustic and electromagnetic stimulator on the brain's sensory-motor cortex. This induces a rhythmic, circular wave of current that stimulates the brain's pleasure centers, producing a state of deep ecstasy.
4. **Schumann Resonance Lock:** The 7.5 Hz frequency matches the planet's electrostatic resonant field (the Schumann resonance at approx 7.8 Hz). The resonating body acts as an emitter, sending a coherent signal through the planet's electrostatic field, entraining others and locking the individual into planetary consciousness.

## Psychological Effects

This resonance acts as a biological trigger that quietens the nervous system and allows the psyche (observer) to detach from physical sensory input, enabling out-of-body experiences (OBEs), telepathy, and the reception of intuitive knowledge.
