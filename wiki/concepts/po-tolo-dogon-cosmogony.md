## Definition / Characterization from Source

**Po Tolo** (referred to by anthropologists as the **Digitaria star**, after the botanical name for the tiny *po* seed) is the central focal point of the secret cosmological system of the Dogon tribe of Mali. 

According to Robert Temple's synthesis of Dogon astronomical lore:
* **Identification with Sirius B:** The Dogon identify *Po Tolo* as a tiny companion star that revolves around the bright star Sirius A. Astrometrically, this companion corresponds exactly to the white dwarf star **Sirius B**.
* **Super-density and Weight:** Long before modern astronomers developed telescopes or the theory of degenerate matter, the Dogon described *Po Tolo* as "the smallest and heaviest of all stars." They assert that it is composed of a super-dense metal called *sagala* (which they claim is heavier than all the iron on Earth combined) and that a single palm-full of its matter would weigh as much as several hundred pack animals.
* **Orbital Period:** The Dogon calculate the orbital cycle of *Po Tolo* around Sirius as exactly fifty years, which matches the modern astronomical measurement of Sirius B's 50-year orbit. To celebrate this orbital cycle, the Dogon host their sacred *Sigui* festival every sixty years (broken down into 20-year periods, mirroring the cycles of Jupiter and Saturn).
* **The Root of Creation:** In Dogon theology, *Po Tolo* is considered the "egg of the world" and the starting-point of all creation. Its axial rotation and orbital movement are believed to project and uphold all physical matter in space.
