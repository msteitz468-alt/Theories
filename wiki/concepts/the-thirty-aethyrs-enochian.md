## Definition from Source

The Aethyrs are a series of thirty Enochian realms or spiritual "levels" named in the angelic
revelations to [[John Dee (1527–1608/9)]] and [[Edward Kelley]]. In *Success Magick* they supply the
powers behind the later rituals: Brand's Rituals of the First Aethyrs (20–38) and Final Aethyrs
(39–49) each work by speaking the name of a given Aethyr three times (e.g. "Lil, Lil, Lil" for the
Aethyr LIL).

## Description from Source

Brand treats each Aethyr as a reservoir of a particular quality of power that the practitioner tunes
into through its name and sigil, then offers to humanity. He notes that historically the Aethyrs have
been explored as visionary realms (the tradition [[Aleister Crowley]] famously worked through in *The
Vision and the Voice*), but the Gallery of Magick approach uses them in a stripped-down, results-
oriented way rather than as a scrying journey. The Aethyr workings make up the bulk of the book's
[[The forty-nine rituals of Success Magick|49 rituals]].
