# Netzach (the Seventh Sephirah)

## Definition from Source
Netzach, meaning Victory, is the seventh Sephirah, situated at the foot of the Pillar of Mercy. Fortune gives its attributions (Chapter XXII) as: God-Name, Jehovah Tzabaoth, "The Lord of Hosts"; Archangel, Haniel; Order of Angels, the Elohim ("Gods"); Mundane Chakra, Nogah (Venus); Magical Image, "a beautiful naked woman"; Spiritual Experience, "Vision of beauty triumphant"; Virtue, Unselfishness; Vice, Unchastity and Lust; correspondence in the microcosm, the loins, hips, and legs; symbols, the Lamp and Girdle, and the Rose; Tarot cards, the four Sevens (Valour, Illusory success, Unstable effort, Success unfulfilled). Its additional title is Firmness.

Netzach represents the "refulgent splendour" into which the White Light of Tiphareth is broken as by a prism into many-rayed manifestation — force still relatively free-moving, bound only into fluidic and ever-shifting shapes. It is contrasted throughout with Hod: Netzach is force, instinct, and emotion; Hod is form and intellect. "All mind is group-mind in Netzach, but in Hod the human mind has its beginnings." Netzach is the Sphere of Venus and of polarity in the broadest sense (not merely sex), and its contacts are made not by philosophy or image-making psychism but by "feeling with," through dance, sound, colour, and the arts.
