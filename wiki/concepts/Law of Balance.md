## Definition from Source

The law of balance is presented as the primary law governing the alternatives space. When a person creates [[Excess Potential]] by attributing too much importance to something, balanced forces arise to eliminate that potential — typically by working counter to the person's aims in that area.

"The law of balance is primary; all difficulties linked to balanced forces eliminating excess potential." (Ch. IV)

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Primary status**: described as the most fundamental operative law in the Transurfing model, superseding other considerations
- **Non-personal**: balanced forces do not punish intentionally; they simply act to restore equilibrium where imbalance has been created
- **Operates through opposition**: balanced forces typically manifest as circumstances that seem to work against the person's goal — not as punishment but as mechanical correction
- **Triggered by importance**: balanced forces are activated specifically by the creation of excess potential through importance, not by other causes

## Examples from Source

- **Perfection**: a person who makes themselves seem highly superior to others in a relationship or social context generates excess potential; balanced forces then work to bring them down
- **Guilt**: guilt is described as pure excess potential; once created it reliably produces circumstances involving punishment
- **Atlantis**: the source asserts that Atlantis was destroyed by balanced forces after its inhabitants perfected the use of [[Outer Intention]] — the excess potential of that mastery triggered catastrophic balancing (Ch. VII)
- **Money**: focusing on money as the goal rather than as an attribute generates excess potential around money; balanced forces then work to prevent its accumulation

## Working With the Law (from source)

The source does not present the law of balance as something to overcome but as a constraint to work within:
- Reduce importance around goals to avoid triggering balanced forces
- Use [[Pure Intention]] — acting without excess importance — to pursue goals without creating counter-forces
- "Do not try to overcome obstacles; reduce their level of importance" (Ch. IV)
