## Definition from Source

The "Martian Conspiracy" is the first of two speculative hypotheses Vallée offers in Chapter 11
of *Messengers of Deception* to explain how a human group could be behind the UFO phenomenon. It
proposes that a **high-level international military deception apparatus** — of the kind that
actually existed in World War II — may be simulating or exploiting an extraterrestrial threat,
perhaps to prevent a third world war by uniting humanity against an outside menace.

The name comes from British Intelligence's WWII codename "Martians" (the spy network informing
Allied command), not the planet Mars.

## Description from Source

Vallée draws on Anthony Cave Brown's *A Bodyguard of Lies* to describe Churchill's **London
Controlling Section (LCS)**, headed by Colonel John Bevan ("Controller of Deception"), and its
collaborators — including Satanism expert Dennis Wheatley and computer pioneer **Alan Turing**,
whose "engines" were long claimed to be mere thought-experiments but were actually built and
operated. Such a group, Vallée argues, demonstrably faked invasions, phantom armies, and
inflatable tanks to deceive Hitler, and "the institutions created to carry them out are still in
existence."

He notes precedents in fiction (Bernard Newman's 1948 *The Flying Saucer*, Koestler's *Twilight
Bar*, Lewin's *Report from Iron Mountain*) for uniting the world against a faked space threat,
and connects it to "Major Murphy's" theory that 1946 Swedish "ghost rockets" and German wartime
secret-weapon research could have seeded a postwar deception. The hypothesis would explain
military silence, Project Blue Book's "bungled" public-relations role, NICAP's intelligence-
linked board, and the manufactured ridicule that keeps scientists away.

## Limits Vallée notes

Vallée presents this only as a hypothesis and ultimately finds it insufficient — it cannot
account for all the reports, and he returns to his own [[The control system (Vallée's functioning lie)]]
view. See the companion [[The Esoteric Intervention (Vallée's second stratagem hypothesis)]].
