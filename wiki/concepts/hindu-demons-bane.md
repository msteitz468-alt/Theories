## Definition from Source

According to [[Theresa Bane]]'s *Encyclopedia of Demons in World Religions and Cultures*, Hindu demonology is one of the oldest and most elaborate demonic traditions in the world, drawing from the Vedas, the Puranas, the Mahabharata, and the Ramayana. The principal demonic classes are the **Asuras** (the primordial adversaries of the gods, born of Kasyapa and cast out of Heaven), the **Rakshasas** (vampiric, shape-shifting flesh-eaters created to guard the ocean's elixir of immortality), the **Daityas** (giant offspring of the goddess Diti who oppose all sacrifice to the gods), and the **Danavas** (an aerial sub-class of the Asuras, born of the goddess Danu). These categories overlap in the sources Bane cites, and individual demons are often simultaneously classified under more than one heading. Bane notes that several Hindu entities began as gods and were progressively demoted — a pattern she documents across many traditions — and that the Vedic and Puranic layers of the mythology frequently assign different attributes to the same being.

The Hindu framework as Bane presents it is markedly cosmological: demons possess entire realms beneath the ocean, challenge the sovereignty of the gods atop Mount Sumeru, and their defeat requires divine avatars and divine weapons. Sexual and creative evil is equally prominent: Kali drinks blood to prevent new demons from spawning from each drop spilled in battle, and the Rakshasas are vampiric by nature.

## Notable Demons

**Ahi** — A demonic dragon god who brings drought, egotism, and ignorance; he controls and withholds rainfall and has dominion over the sun. Described as resembling a snake, a spider, and a man with a snake rising from each shoulder.

**Abhiyoga** — In Hindu and Buddhist mythology, the demon of darkness and rain; the name translates as "prosecution."

**Arundhati** — The Hindu personification of the morning star (identified with Lucifer-Venus by ancient Greeks); animates dormant things in the void and is represented by the sign of a coiled serpent.

**Asuras** — A primal class of fallen god-demons, children of Kasyapa, cast out of Heaven; very strong, possessing dominion over secret wisdom, they constantly plot against the god Indra and live in a city beneath the ocean ruled by their king, Rahula.

**Daityas** — Gigantic demonic spirits born of the goddess Diti and Kasyapa, under the command of the dragon-serpent Vritra; they oppose sacrifice to the gods. During Krita Yuga they overpowered the gods and scattered them across the cosmos until Indra, armed with the demon-slaying weapon Vajra, drove them into Patala, a realm deep beneath the ocean.

**Danavas** — An aerial sub-race of the Asuras born of the goddess Danu; immortal and ranked beneath the ocean's dominion.

**Daivers** — A species of demonic djinn in Hindu mythology who have both material and spiritual bodies; they number 330,000,000 and live in the realm Daiver Logum under their king Daivuntren.

**Hidimba** — An Asuras, Pisacha, or Rakshasa (sources vary) from the Mahabharata; lived in a bone-strewn cave in the forest and used his beautiful sister to lure travelers before he was slain by the hero Bhima.

**Hiranyaksha** ("Goldeneye") — An Asuras and king of the world in Vedic mythology; he preyed upon all animals and people and dragged the earth to the bottom of the ocean, until Vishnu took the form of a sow and slew him after a battle lasting one thousand years.

**Hiranyakashipu** — Both an Asuras and a Rakshasa; born at sunset, this demon is arrogant, cruel, and materialistic; his name means "one who is wrapped in gold." He and his brother Hiranyaksha are one of the three incarnations of the cursed gatekeepers Jaya and Vijaya.

**Kaitabha** — An Asuras born from the ear wax of Vishnu; with his brother Madhu he intended to kill Brahma while he slept. Their bodies, after being slain, disintegrated into twelve pieces used to sculpt the earth's tectonic plates.

**Kali** — The vampiric goddess of Change and Destruction in Hinduism; she drinks blood through an exceptionally long tongue and wears a necklace of human skulls and a belt of severed arms. She became a blood-drinker out of necessity while fighting the demon Raktavija, whose every drop of blood spawned a thousand new demons.

**Kumbhakarna** — A Rakshasa said to stand 420,000 meters tall; because he misspoke a divine boon he sleeps for six months and wakes for only a single day, during which he is ravenously hungry. Sliced-off pieces of him continue to fight in battle.

**Madhu** — An Asuras, the demon of darkness, born from the ear wax of Vishnu alongside his brother Kaitabha; ranked as a king, he is the father of Lavanasura.

**Mahasura** — "Great demon not-god," an Asuras from Hindu demonology said to be very strong; often referred to as the Satan of Hinduism.

**Mahisha** ("The buffalo demon") — An Asuras, demon of death; born the son of a buffalo-demon union, he could shape-shift into a buffalo, elephant, human, or lion, and create a million copies of himself. He received a boon from Brahma that no male could kill him; he was ultimately slain by the goddess Durga.

**Rahu** — An Asuras and prince of the Daityas in Vedic demonology; he disguised himself as a god and drank the nectar of immortality before being beheaded by Vishnu. His head became the constellation Draco, and he causes lunar and solar eclipses by riding a chariot in eternal pursuit of the sun and moon.

**Raktavija** ("Blood seed") — A general Asuras who commanded an army of elephants, horses, and men; extremely difficult to kill because whenever a drop of his blood hit the ground, a duplicate of him was formed. Eventually slain by Kali, who drank his blood as it was shed.

**Rakshasas** — A vampiric race created by Brahma to protect the ocean's elixir of immortality; part human, part tiger (most commonly), with fangs and shape-shifting ability. Males lurk in treetops and attack infants and pregnant women; female Rakshasas, called rakshasis, lure men by appearing as beautiful women.
