## Definition / Characterization from Source

**The Insiders** is the term used by Gary Allen and Larry Abraham to describe a self-perpetuating, highly organized, and immensely wealthy clique of international financiers and political elites at the apex of the global conspiratorial network.

According to *None Dare Call It Conspiracy*:
* **Composition:** The group consists of the world's most powerful international bankers (such as the Rockefeller, Rothschild, Schiff, Warburg, and Morgan dynasties) and their chosen political agents, corporate partners, and academic mouthpieces. Unlike street-level radicals, they possess top prep school, Ivy League, and Oxford educations, and operate from penthouses and executive boardrooms in Manhattan, London, and Paris.
* **Goals:** The primary objective of the Insiders is to establish a centralized world system of financial and political control in private hands, capable of dominating the government of each nation and the economy of the world as a whole. This includes establishing a world superstate (or "new world order") where individual choices are tracked and controlled within narrow parameters.
* **Mechanisms of Power:**
  * **Central Banking Cartels:** The Insiders utilize private central banks (like the Federal Reserve and the Bank of England) to control money supplies, create national debt, and engineer economic cycles (inflation and depressions) to consolidate wealth and control.
  * **Fostering Collectivism:** They finance and exploit state socialism and Communism to eliminate free-enterprise competitors, nationalize resources, and establish global monopolies.
  * **Front Organizations:** They operate through concentric circles of influence, using front groups such as the Council on Foreign Relations (CFR), the Round Table groups, and the Bilderberg Group to direct national foreign policies.
  * **Double-Sided Warfare:** They orchestrate and finance both sides of geopolitical conflicts to create artificial enemies, justify military spending, and accelerate the centralization of national governments.
