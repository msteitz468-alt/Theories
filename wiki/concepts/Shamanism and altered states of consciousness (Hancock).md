## Definition / Characterization from Source

The organizing framework of *Supernatural*: that human beings across all cultures and epochs have entered **altered states of consciousness** — through hallucinogenic plants (iboga, ayahuasca, psilocybin, DMT), as well as fasting, drumming, pain, and trance — and that in those states they reliably encounter a structured visionary world, including geometric "entoptic" patterns and seemingly autonomous intelligent beings. Hancock adopts archaeologist David Lewis-Williams's **neuropsychological model**, in which Upper Paleolithic cave art is the record of such trance visions, passing through three deepening stages.

For Hancock this is the common root of shamanism, religion, fairy lore, and modern alien-abduction and DMT-entity reports — all, he argues, culturally varied descriptions of the same encounter with the "ancient teachers of mankind," and possibly with an objectively real parallel reality rather than mere brain chemistry.
