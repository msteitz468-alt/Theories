# Mineral correspondences (Kynes)

Part of the [[Magical correspondences (Kynes)]] system compiled by [[Sandra Kynes]].

See also sibling pages: [[Plant correspondences (Kynes)]] · [[Animal correspondences (Kynes)]] · [[Magical intentions and powers (Kynes)]] · [[Deity and spirit correspondences (Kynes)]] · [[Astrological and time correspondences (Kynes)]] · [[Elemental, color, and number correspondences (Kynes)]]

## Definition from Source

Chapter Three of [[Llewellyn's Complete Book of Correspondences (Kynes)]] covers the Mineral Kingdom, organized into three sections: **Gemstones and Minerals**, **Metals and Alloys**, and **From the Sea** (shells, coral, pearl, abalone, etc.). Kynes frames minerals as products of the natural world's most powerful creative and destructive forces, giving them an inherently dynamic magical charge. She distinguishes two broad energetic poles: **heavier metals and dense minerals** aid in grounding during magical work, while **transparent crystals** embody light, clairvoyance, and wisdom.

Importantly, Kynes argues that **perfection and beauty are not the most important attributes** — an ordinary or even fragmentary crystal carries as much power as a flawless gem; what matters is the energy a practitioner perceives. Each entry is mapped across the same multi-axis schema used throughout the book: zodiac signs, planetary rulers, lunar phases, elemental associations, energy polarity (yin/yang), chakras, tarot cards, directions, deities, and "Issues, Intentions & Powers." Many gemstones receive sub-entries for varieties (e.g., Fire Agate, Blue Lace Agate, Snowflake Obsidian), each with differentiated associations.

The correspondence logic here mirrors [[The Principle of Correspondence (Kybalion)]] — material properties (color, density, transparency, elemental origin) are read as signatures of higher-plane qualities — applied to minerals in a practical reference format for Pagan/Wiccan ritual work.

## Organizational Notes

Sub-varieties within a gemstone family receive their own entries with distinct correspondences — the system is granular enough to distinguish, for example, the grounding/protective energy of Black Agate from the luck and consciousness-expanding qualities of base Agate. Metals are mapped to planetary rulers in the classical alchemical tradition (gold/Sun, silver/Moon, copper/Venus, iron/Mars, lead/Saturn, tin/Jupiter), making this section a practical bridge between Pagan correspondence magic and older Hermetic and alchemical frameworks also discussed in [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]] and [[Hermes Trismegistus]].
