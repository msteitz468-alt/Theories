## Definition from Source

The Divine Paradox is the Kybalion's name for the foundational tension between two simultaneously true perspectives on the Universe: from the Absolute viewpoint (the standpoint of THE ALL), the Universe is unreal — a mental creation, a dream or meditation; from the Relative viewpoint (the standpoint of finite beings within the Universe), the Universe is very real and its Laws are binding. The Kybalion treats this not as a contradiction to be dissolved but as a two-poled truth to be held consciously at all times.

The Three Initiates describe it as "an aspect of the Principle of Polarity which manifests when THE ALL begins to Create." The key maxim encapsulating the paradox is:

> "While the Universe IS NOT, still IT IS."

The practical danger the Kybalion emphasizes is "half-wisdom" — grasping only one pole. The half-wise who seize only the Absolute pole (treating the Universe as mere illusion) become like sleep-walkers and are "broken against the rocks." The wise hold both poles simultaneously and use higher Laws against lower Laws rather than denying the lower outright. The weapon of the Master is "Transmutation, not presumptuous denial."
