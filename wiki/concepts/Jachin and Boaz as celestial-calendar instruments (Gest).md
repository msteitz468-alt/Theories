**Definition/Characterization from the Source**
In Kevin L. Gest's *The Secrets of Solomon's Temple*, the two freestanding bronze pillars of Solomon's Temple — named Jachin (south) and Boaz (north) per 1 Kings 7:21 — are treated not as decorative furnishings, incense burners, or structural supports, but as a deliberately engineered pair of gnomons/calendrical markers. Gest argues their height, base height, capital dimensions, and spacing were chosen so that their midday shadows recorded the solstices and equinox, and so that their numeric dimensions (measured in cubits) encoded lunar-cycle constants (27.5, 28, 29.5, 18.6 years) and solar-cycle geometry. He connects the pillars' shadow-marking function to the Masonic Junior Warden's ceremonial duty of "marking the Sun at its meridian," and to the phrase "admitted on the square," which he links (via Robert Lomas) to the solstice-shadow geometry at Rosslyn Chapel's latitude.
