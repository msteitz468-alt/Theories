## Definition from Source

Blavatsky presents these three propositions as the bedrock on which the entire Secret Doctrine rests (Proem, Vol. I). They must be grasped before the Stanzas of Dzyan can be understood.

### Proposition (a) — The Absolute

An **Omnipresent, Eternal, Boundless, and Immutable PRINCIPLE** on which all speculation is impossible, since it transcends the power of human conception. It is "beyond the range and reach of thought — unthinkable and unspeakable" (Mandukya Upanishad). Blavatsky calls this "BE-NESS" rather than Being — Sanskrit *Sat* — and describes it as "the rootless root of all that was, is, or ever shall be," devoid of all attributes and without any relation to manifested, finite Being. It is symbolised under two aspects: (1) **absolute abstract Space**, the one thing no human mind can exclude from any conception; and (2) **absolute Abstract Motion** (the Great Breath), representing Unconditioned Consciousness. From this Absolute, Spirit (Cosmic Ideation) and Matter (Mulaprakriti) are not independent realities but two facets of the same Absolute. A third principle, **Fohat**, is the dynamic "bridge" by which the Divine Ideas are impressed on Cosmic Substance — the animating link between Mind and Matter, made manifest through the Dhyan-Chohans.

### Proposition (b) — The Law of Periodicity

**The Eternity of the Universe in toto** as a boundless plane, "periodically the playground of numberless Universes incessantly manifesting and disappearing." Blavatsky asserts the absolute universality of the law of periodicity — flux and reflux, ebb and flow — observable in every department of nature: Day and Night, Life and Death, Sleeping and Waking. Cosmically this expresses as the alternation of Manvantara (active manifestation) and Pralaya (rest and dissolution). Each universe is an "outbreathing" of the Great Breath; its withdrawal is an "inbreathing." The process has no first beginning and will have no final end; our present universe is one of an infinite series.

### Proposition (c) — The Identity of All Souls and the Pilgrimage

**The fundamental identity of all individual Souls with the Universal Over-Soul** (Alaya / Anima Mundi), and the **obligatory pilgrimage** of every Soul — a spark of the Over-Soul — through the Cycle of Incarnation (or "Necessity") in accordance with Cyclic and Karmic law. No spiritual Buddhi (divine Soul) can have independent conscious existence before it has (a) passed through every elemental form of the phenomenal world of that Manvantara, and (b) acquired individuality by "self-induced and self-devised efforts" ascending from mineral and plant up to the highest Dhyani-Buddha. The esoteric philosophy "admits no privileges or special gifts in man, save those won by his own Ego through personal effort and merit throughout a long series of metempsychoses and reincarnations."
