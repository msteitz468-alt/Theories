## Definition from Source

INSPEC is Monroe's acronym for "Intelligent Species" — non-physical beings of a fundamentally different order from the post-human helper entities found in the inner rings around Earth. Monroe coined the term in *Far Journeys* as a label for the beings who have been guiding and navigating his OBE explorations, which he traces back to the very beginning of his OBE activity in 1958.

Monroe's key characterization: INSPECs "had knowledge and technology far beyond that drawn from the human experience" and appeared "independent of the recycling rings." Their mode of communication is pure NVC (non-verbal communication / thought-ball exchange); they use words only when making contact with humans through a "tight, narrow-banded tuning."

By the end of *Far Journeys*, Monroe discovers that what he had imagined as a category of external beings is actually a single unified INSPEC — "just one" — which is itself the fully merged I-There cluster of many past-human souls, each of whom has completed all incarnations and merged into a collective whole. "There are many others around us," the merged INSPEC entity tells Monroe, indicating many such unified wholes exist.

## Key Attributes as Described by Monroe

- Predate human physical life by millions of years ("by your time measurement, that is correct")
- Number "more than the current in-physical human population, perhaps one hundred times as many" — yet this is "a very few" relative to the INSPEC totality
- Were not the creators of Earth, time-space, or the human process: "That is not our department"
- Their concern is the "output and quality" of the human learning system — "fine-tuning" the energy flow
- Possess technology capable of: removing human energy bodies from physical bodies; operating vacated human bodies via vocal cords; changing body temperature remotely; providing healing treatments via light/disc devices; creating realistic simulations indistinguishable from reality; traveling instantly across vast ranges of space-time
- Have been perceived throughout human history as gods, angels, the devil, miracle workers, saints
- Are distinct from physical spacecraft Monroe observes near Earth (which are of physical universe origin)
- Communicate with Monroe using his personal identifier "Ashaneen" and address him as "Mister Monroe" in a friendly, sometimes gently humorous manner

## Source Context

Monroe first uses the term in Chapter 8 (Contact Point) after years of working with these beings without naming them. He notes the label "presumes humans are not quite so" (intelligent). The INSPEC confirms the label is adequate "at this point, as well as any other." In Chapter 9, Monroe discovers the deeper structure: INSPEC is not a category of beings but the identity reached when all parts of an I-There cluster have merged. The INSPEC communicates: "You are still incomplete. There are parts of you yet to be transformed… That is why we remain at this point, to reach back and gather additional and remaining parts of us until we are complete."
