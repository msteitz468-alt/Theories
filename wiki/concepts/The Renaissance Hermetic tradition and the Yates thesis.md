## Definition from Source

The umbrella argument of *Giordano Bruno and the Hermetic Tradition*. Yates contends that the Renaissance
mistakenly believed the **[[The Corpus Hermeticum|Corpus Hermeticum]]** to be the work of a primordial
Egyptian sage (**[[Hermes Trismegistus]]**) older than Plato and Moses, and that this dating gave the
Hermetic texts immense authority — fuelling a revival of **magic, prisca theologia, and a new dignified
image of man as operator** that runs from **Ficino** and **Pico** through **Agrippa** to **Giordano
Bruno**, and that this magical-Hermetic worldview was a significant **stimulus toward the scientific
revolution** (the "Yates thesis"). The tradition was thrown into crisis by **Casaubon's 1614 redating**
of the Hermetica to the early Christian era.
