# The Paths upon the Tree of Life

## Definition from Source
Fortune defines the Paths (Chapter X) as the lines connecting the Ten Sephiroth. Although the Sepher Yetzirah technically numbers thirty-two Paths (counting the Sephiroth themselves as Paths too), practical working restricts the term to the twenty-two connecting lines, equated with the twenty-two letters of the Hebrew alphabet. Symbol-sets assigned to the Paths are: the twenty-two Hebrew letters; the signs of the Zodiac, planets, and elements (twenty-three symbols reduced to twenty-two by omitting Earth, since Malkuth already covers it); and the twenty-two trumps/major arcana of the Tarot, per Crowley's "777" attribution rather than Waite's. Each Path "represents the equilibrium of the two Sephiroth it connects" and must be studied in light of both.

Fortune insists (Chapter XII) that the Sephiroth are objective — representing natural forces — while the Paths are subjective, representing states of consciousness; she considers it a mistake (made by Crowley) to assign pagan gods to the Paths, since doing so confuses practical working. Instead the Paths should be worked "under the direct presidency of the Holy Names governing their Sephirothic attributions only," using the appropriate elemental rite and Tattva rather than an "inextricable tangle" of deity associations — e.g. the Seventeenth Path (Tiphareth–Binah), assigned to Air, should be worked with the Air element rite rather than via Castor and Pollux, Janus, Apollo, or Mercury.

Practically (Chapter XIII), each Path has associated Magical Images, geometrical forms, animals, plants, precious stones, drugs, perfumes, and magical weapons, which serve both as intellectual keys to correlating pantheons and gods with the Tree, and as landmarks for checking the validity of visions encountered when "travelling in the spirit-vision" — an out-of-place symbol (e.g. a horse or jackal appearing in the sphere of Netzach) signals "confusion of plane" and an unreliable vision. Each of the Thirty-two Paths also carries one of the cryptic texts of the Sepher Yetzirah, used in meditation.
