## Definition / Characterization from Source

Carroll Quigley's philosophy of history, set out in the opening chapters of *Tragedy and Hope* (and developed at length in his *The Evolution of Civilizations*). A civilization passes through **seven stages**: (1) Mixture, (2) Gestation, (3) Expansion, (4) Age of Conflict, (5) Universal Empire, (6) Decay, (7) Invasion. Expansion proceeds along four lines (population, geographic area, production, knowledge) and produces a **core/periphery** structure in which the core enters conflict before the periphery.

The engine of the cycle is what Quigley calls the transformation of an **"instrument of expansion" into an "institution"**: a social arrangement that drives growth eventually "petrifies into a structure of vested interests" that throttles it, opening the Age of Conflict. A civilization either collapses toward a Universal Empire, decay, and invasion — or (uniquely, in the West) reorganizes by circumventing the petrified institution with a new instrument.
