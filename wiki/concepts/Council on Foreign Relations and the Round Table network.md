## Definition from Source

Griffin presents the Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) as the American branch of an international financier network originating with [[Cecil Rhodes]]. Per the source, Rhodes "formed a secret society" whose "innermost directorate was called the Round Table," with "subordinate structures called Round-Table Groups" in other countries; "the Round-Table Group in the United States became known as the Council on Foreign Relations."

Griffin asserts the CFR — "initially dominated by J.P. Morgan and later by the Rockefellers — is the most powerful group in America today. It is even more powerful than the federal government, because almost all of the key positions in government are held by its members. In other words, it is the United States government."

## Characterization in the Source

- Griffin credits Carroll Quigley's *Tragedy and Hope* as documenting that the CFR is "an outgrowth of the secret society formed by Cecil Rhodes" and describing "how an international network of financiers has created a system of financial control able to dominate the political systems of all countries through their central banks."
- The network's goal, per Griffin, is "world dominion and the establishment of a modern feudalist society controlled by the world's central banks" — pursued through the [[The IMF and World Bank are engines for world government and world socialism|IMF/World Bank]] and the United Nations.
