## Definition from Source

Dispenza's signature meditation practice in which the meditator slows brain waves to alpha/theta, then sequentially rests attention in each of eight "energy centers" of the body, opens focus to the space surrounding each center, and sustains elevated emotion (love, gratitude, joy) while mentally "blessing" the center for the greatest good. The goal is to restore coherent energy flow through the body's autonomic nervous system, reverse disease caused by incoherent stress-state energy, and eventually evolve creative energy from the survival-oriented lower centers to the higher, more selfless centers.

## Description from Source

Dispenza describes the body as organized around eight energy centers (corresponding roughly to the Hindu chakra system, which he acknowledges). Each center has its own: frequency/consciousness, glands, hormones, chemistry, and a plexus of neurons he calls a "mini-brain" — and therefore its own "mind." When the meditator places focused attention on a center, energy moves to it; when the meditator then opens focus to the space around the center, this shifts brain waves from beta to alpha/theta, bypasses the analytical mind, and gives the meditator access to the subconscious operating system (the autonomic nervous system). Dispenza claims that combining elevated emotion with this open-focus attention produces a coherent electromagnetic field around each center, which then "gives the proper instructions to matter" and allows the body to heal.

**The eight centers and their primary associations (as Dispenza describes them):**

1. **First center** — perineum/pelvic floor; sex organs, bladder, lower bowel; reproductive hormones (estrogen, progesterone, testosterone); inferior mesenteric nerve plexus; associated with creative energy and sexual identity.
2. **Second center** — below navel; ovaries, uterus, colon, pancreas, lower back; digestion, blood sugar, social relationships; superior mesenteric nerve plexus; associated with safety, holding on/letting go.
3. **Third center** — pit of gut/solar plexus; stomach, small intestine, liver, adrenal glands, kidneys; adrenaline, cortisol; celiac plexus; associated with will, power, self-importance, competition.
4. **Fourth center** — center of chest; heart, lungs, thymus gland; growth hormone, oxytocin, and 1,400 immune-stimulating chemicals; heart plexus; the "seat of the soul," associated with love, compassion, gratitude, selflessness. (Transition from selfish to selfless nature.)
5. **Fifth center** — center of throat; thyroid, parathyroid, salivary glands; T3 and T4 thyroid hormones; thyroid plexus; associated with speaking one's truth and expressing love.
6. **Sixth center** — center of the brain toward the back of the head; pineal gland (which Dispenza calls "the first eye"); serotonin, melatonin; associated with higher-dimensional perception, lucidity, and access to nonlinear reality. (Linked to [[The pineal gland as a transducer (Dispenza)]].)
7. **Seventh center** — center of the head; pituitary gland (the "master gland"); governs a downward hormonal cascade to all other glands; highest expression of divinity and harmony with all things.
8. **Eighth center** — approximately 16 inches above the head; no physical anatomy; the Egyptian "Ka"; represents connection to the cosmos and the quantum field; activated with gratitude as "the ultimate state of receivership"; the doorway for insights, epiphanies, and creative downloads from the unified field.

Dispenza teaches that in modern stress-driven life, energy tends to get stuck in the lower three centers — especially in the first (excessive sexuality), second (guilt, shame, victimhood), and third (control, anger, competitiveness) — preventing it from flowing upward to activate the higher centers. The Blessing of the Energy Centers meditation is designed to free stuck energy and restore its upward flow.

He claims workshop participants who perform this meditation show measurable before/after improvements in energy-center balance and body energy fields, as measured by a gas discharge visualization (GDV) device — a piece of equipment he notes "is not approved by the scientific community in the United States" but is acknowledged in Russia. Community testimonials include claimed healings of chronic bladder infections, prostate problems, Crohn's disease, ovarian tumors, arrhythmias, thyroid conditions, brain tumors, and cancer — framed as results of epigenetic changes produced by the shift from incoherent to coherent energy in the relevant centers.
