## Definition from Source

"New Aeon Magic" is Carroll's term for a program of practical and philosophical magical renewal set out in the *Psychonaut* volume of [[Liber Null & Psychonaut (Carroll)]]. It is positioned as the successor to both orthodox religion and materialist science, claiming that "the next great advance that humanity will make will be into the psychic domain."

Carroll identifies two pillars of the new aeon's most important magical work:

1. **Experimental technique over symbolism** — "an experimental investigation into the actual techniques as opposed to the mere symbolism of magic." Carroll holds that magical methods are "remarkably uniform throughout history and across cultures" and that it is time to "unearth them and make them work."

2. **A spirituality of magic** — developing "its own flavor, its own worldview, and its own philosophy," distinct from religion and from the superstition that the world is wholly material.

Carroll frames New Aeon Magic as recuperating power that is shifting from gross physical to psychic levels: "A profound change has occurred, and the magic force is now manifesting with increasing strength on the psychic levels." Earlier gross phenomena (levitation, fire-walking) are receding; telepathy, clairvoyance, and astral travel are described as increasingly accessible.

**Political and social alignment.** Carroll aligns New Aeon Magic explicitly against: oppressive psychiatry and medicine; politics as "some people's perverse desire to dominate others"; ideology ("the main products of ideological solutions are repression and corpses"); religion; and the superstition of pure materialism. It is committed to "individual freedom and consciousness" and to concern for "all other life forms on this planet," which Carroll calls "chaotic good."

**Psychological anarchy** — "The chaotic aspect of new aeon magic is psychological anarchy. It is a species of operation mindfuck applied to ourselves as much as the world. The aim is to produce inspiration and enlightenment through disordering our belief structures. Humor, random belief, counter-information, and disinformation are its techniques."

Carroll also identifies the origin of the arts not in medieval or pagan civilization but in animistic and shamanistic cultures: "Before history began, humanity knew a strange and terrible power which has gradually slipped from its grasp."
