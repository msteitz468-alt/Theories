## Definition / Characterization from Source

In *Limits to Growth*, **overshoot** means "to go too far, to go beyond limits accidentally — without intention." The authors hold it has three necessary-and-sufficient causes "at any scale from personal to planetary": (1) **growth**, acceleration, rapid change; (2) some **limit** or barrier beyond which the system cannot safely go; and (3) a **delay or error** in perceiving and responding to that limit. Their claim is that humanity's population and material economy have overshot the Earth's carrying capacity.

They measure the human demand on the planet as the **ecological footprint** (Wackernagel's metric — the land needed to supply resources consumed and absorb wastes), reporting that since the late 1980s humanity has used more each year than the planet can regenerate. Crucially, the relevant limits are limits to **throughput** — the continuous flows of energy and materials drawn from planetary **sources** (mineral deposits, aquifers, soils, fisheries, forests) and dumped into planetary **sinks** (the atmosphere, water bodies, landfills) — not direct limits on the number of people or factories.
