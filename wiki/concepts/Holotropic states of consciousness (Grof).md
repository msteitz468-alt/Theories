## Definition / Characterization from Source

Stanislav Grof's term ("holotropic" = "moving toward wholeness") for a distinct class of **non-ordinary states of consciousness** — induced by psychedelics (notably LSD), by his drug-free **holotropic breathwork** (accelerated breathing, evocative music, focused bodywork), or arising spontaneously — that have therapeutic and transformative potential rather than being mere pathology. From thousands of sessions Grof drew an expanded **"cartography of the psyche"** with three broad domains: the **biographical** (personal/postnatal memory), the **perinatal** (organized around the trauma of birth into four **Basic Perinatal Matrices**, BPMs), and the **transpersonal** (experiences beyond the individual ego — ancestral, collective, archetypal, past-life, cosmic).

The framework grounds **transpersonal psychology**, treats birth and death–rebirth as central psychospiritual themes, and connects to shamanic initiation, mysticism, and the wiki's broader consciousness material; Grof influenced John Mack's abduction work and Graham Hancock.
