## Definition / Characterization from Source

The catastrophist mechanism at the heart of *Magicians of the Gods*: the hypothesis (developed by scientists Richard Firestone, James Kennett, Allen West and others, and championed by Hancock with collaborator Randall Carlson) that a large comet, fragmenting in the inner solar system, struck the Earth — especially the North American ice cap — around **12,800 years ago**, igniting wildfires, collapsing ice sheets, and driving the abrupt ~1,200-year cold reversal known as the **Younger Dryas**. A second pulse of melting/flooding around 11,600 years ago closed the episode.

Hancock uses the impact to supply *Fingerprints'* missing cause: it is the cataclysm that destroyed [[The lost civilization hypothesis (Hancock)|the lost civilization]], the event remembered in worldwide flood myths, and (he argues) the date memorialized on Göbekli Tepe's Pillar 43. He warns that surviving fragments in the **Taurid meteor stream** could return.
