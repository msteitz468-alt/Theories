## Definition and Characterization from the Source
In [[Transcendental Magic (Lévi)]], the Kabalah is presented as the surviving key to a "primitive book" of hieroglyphs once possessed by "the sages of the earliest epoch of the world," resting on the single dogma of Magic — "the visible is for us the proportional measure of the invisible." It structures reality through the ten Sephiroth (KETHER, CHOKMAH, BINAH, CHESED, GEBURAH, TIPHERETH, NETSAH, HOD, JESOD, MALKUTH), the Sacred Tetragram, and the twenty-two figures of the Tarot, together forming "the thirty-two paths of absolute science." Lévi treats the Kabalah as the historical source of true astrology (Chapter XVII) and as underlying the final synthetic principle of the entire Doctrine, Analogy (Chapter XXII): "Analogy is the final word of science and the first word of faith."
