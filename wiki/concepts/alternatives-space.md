## Definition from Source

The alternatives space is described as a material information structure containing all possible life scenarios that ever have existed or could exist. Zeland writes: "The alternatives space is a gigantic information field containing scripts and scenery for all possible events." Every point within this field contains both a script (sequence of events) and scenery (physical conditions) for a corresponding possible reality.

The field exists independently of human consciousness as a given structure; it is not created by thought but accessed and "lit up" by it. The alternatives space is also referred to as the **information field** or **energy matrix** (Ch. VI), which serves as the template for all physical possibilities.

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Infinite**: contains all scenarios that ever have existed or could exist
- **Pre-existing**: not created by human thought; thought energy merely selects which sectors become physically manifest
- **Structured**: organised into sectors with coherent parameters; adjacent sectors share similar scripts and scenery
- **Inert**: "The material realisation of the alternatives space is inert, like resin" — physical transitions between sectors are gradual, not instantaneous (except in dreams)
- **Accessed by subconscious**: "The subconscious directly connects to the alternatives space and the information it contains" (Ch. VII)
- **Dream travel**: the soul travels through the alternatives space during dreaming
