## Definition from Source

The umbrella framework of the Book of Enoch: an apocalyptic revelation, attributed to the patriarch
[[Enoch (antediluvian patriarch)|Enoch]], whose narrative spine is the descent of the **Watchers** —
angels who came down on Mount Hermon, took human wives, and fathered the giant **Nephilim** — and the
forbidden knowledge they taught humanity, which corrupted the Earth and provoked the judgment of the
Flood. Around this core the work gathers cosmic visions, a solar calendar, symbolic histories of the
world, and (in the Parables) a heavenly **Son of Man**.

Its parts (each on its own page): the
[[The Watchers, their fall, and the forbidden teachings (Book of Enoch)|Watchers and their forbidden teachings]];
the [[The Nephilim: giant offspring of the Watchers and human women (Book of Enoch)|Nephilim]]; the
[[The Son of Man and the Lord of Spirits (Book of Enoch's Parables)|Son of Man of the Parables]]; the
[[The Astronomical Book: the solar calendar and the courses of the luminaries (Book of Enoch)|Astronomical Book]];

2 Enoch, [[2 Enoch: the ascent through the ten heavens and the secrets of creation|Enoch's heavenly ascent]]
and [[The miraculous birth of Melchizedek (2 Enoch)|Melchizedek]].

As a **primary root** it underlies the wiki's Watchers/Nephilim material — [[The Gods of Eden (Bramley)]],
the Wallis "Eden" books, [[The Twelfth Planet (Sitchin)]], [[Demonology across world religions (Bane)]],
and Blavatsky's [[Fallen angels, the Serpent, and Lucifer as misunderstood cosmic forces (Blavatsky)|fallen-angels re-reading]].
