## Definition from Source

Carroll posits the aether as an intermediate realm between [[Chaos (Carroll's metaphysical principle)]]
and ordinary matter, and between [[Kia (Carroll)]] and the mind. It is "a realm of half-formed
substance" that is "dualistic matter but of a very tenuous, probabilistic nature." Carroll writes:
"It consists of all the possibilities which chaos throws out which have not yet become solid
realities. It is the 'medium' by which the 'nonexistent' chaos translates itself into 'real'
effects. It forms a sort of backdrop out of which real events and real thoughts materialize."

Because aetheric events are "only partially evolved into dualistic existence, they may not have a
precise location in space or time. They may not have a precise mass or energy either, and so do
not necessarily affect the physical plane." Carroll draws a parallel to quantum physics: "Even the
physical sciences have begun to blunder into the aetheric with their discoveries of quantum
indeterminacy and virtual processes in subatomic matter."

The aether's normal function is as a "Kia-thought intermediary," but "its properties are so
infinitely mutable that almost anything can be accomplished with it. Thought gives it shape, and
Kia gives it power." Magic works by manipulating the aether: "As centers of Kia or chaos
ourselves, we can sometimes call very unlikely coincidences or unexpected events into existence
by manipulating the aether."

The mutability of the aether explains the diversity of magical systems worldwide. Because it can
be shaped by any symbolic or belief system, "almost any [system] can be used." Yet when
"stripped of local symbolism and terminology, all systems show a remarkable uniformity of method."

In divination, ripples through the aetheric plane (caused by events, especially mental events)
can be intercepted: "Some of the various densities of the aether have only a partial or
probabilistic differentiation into existence and are somewhat indeterminate in space and time" —
the basis for events at a distance and prescience.

Entities created through evocation consist of "a portion of Kia, or the life force, attached to
some aetheric matter." These may be mobile in the aether or bound to talismans, places, or objects.
