## Definition from Source

"The Manipulators" is the name Vallée gives, in *Messengers of Deception*, to "the hypothetical
agents who might cause the UFO contacts and engineer their effects." The book's organizing
question is their "role, their identity, their designs": "Who could they be? Alien beings…?
Psychic entities…? Automata…? But perhaps we are looking far away for something which is right
under our nose: could they simply be human?"

## Description from Source

Vallée proposes that "there is a machinery of mass manipulation behind the UFO phenomenon" aimed
"at social and political goals," using contactees and occult sects as instruments. He suspects
"the manipulators may be human beings with a plan for social control," exercising "incredible
spiritual blackmail" by harnessing "the fear of cosmic forces and the emotions surrounding UFO
contact." Such weapons are "less detectible than tanks and aircraft" and "a more lasting form of
control over the lives of men."

He explores two candidate identities — the
[[The Martian Conspiracy (Vallée's first stratagem hypothesis)]] (a military deception apparatus)
and the [[The Esoteric Intervention (Vallée's second stratagem hypothesis)]] (an occult group
wielding "psychotronic" technology) — but finds neither fully adequate and returns to his
[[The control system (Vallée's functioning lie)]] view, in which the *belief*, not necessarily a
single cabal, is what is being manipulated.

## Vallée's caution

Vallée repeatedly flags this as unproven: "I confess I do not have any proof that a single group
of men is responsible," and "What I have called 'manipulation' may be simple exploitation." His
closing wish is "to know more… about the image of humanity such Manipulators harbor in their own
minds — and in their hearts. Assuming, of course, that they do have hearts."
