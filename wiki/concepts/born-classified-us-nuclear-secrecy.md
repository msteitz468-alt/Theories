## Definition from Source

Jacobsen states the concept "born classified" came from the Atomic Energy Act of 1946. Under this system, the Atomic Energy Commission (formerly the Manhattan Project) could classify nuclear weapons information separately from presidential executive orders.

Restricted Data could originate outside government through "the thinking and research of private parties"—meaning a private company conducting AEC research could produce work that was classified from inception, potentially beyond even the president's need-to-know.

Donald Woodbridge, an AEC classifications officer quoted in the source, said born classified "give[s] the professional classificationist unanswerable authority."
