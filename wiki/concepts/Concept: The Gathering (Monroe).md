## Definition from Source

"The Gathering" is Monroe's term for what the INSPEC describes as an assembly of non-physical beings — manifested from "other nearby energy systems" — gathered near Earth to observe an imminent rare event: the conflux of multiple intense energy fields arriving simultaneously in Earth's time-space. The INSPEC states this occurs approximately once every 87 million years (though this frequency is not guaranteed; there are random elements). Monroe observes the gathering from a vantage point between Earth and the Moon in Chapter 12 (Hearsay Evidence).

The gathering includes both:
1. Non-physical beings from other energy systems (appearing as glowing forms of various densities)
2. Physical spacecraft from other civilizations within the physical universe ("their origins are of your physical universe but not necessarily of your time reference")
3. The final-process humans in the outermost ring around Earth — Last-Timers on their final incarnation — who also radiate expectancy

## What the Gathering Observes

The imminent event is the "possible birth of a new energy" from the human learning system. Monroe is told the outcome possibilities (all framed by the INSPEC as still unresolved at the time of Monroe's observation):

**If the opportunity is taken**: "human consciousness a rare potential to emerge rapidly into a unified intelligent energy system that will range far beyond your time-space illusion, creating, constructing, teaching as only a human-trained graduate energy is able to do."

**If the opportunity is missed**: "Humans will retreat as the dominant species on earth until they no longer survive as active consciousness, eventually in any form."

The INSPEC uses the Chinese symbol for "crisis" (danger + opportunity) to frame the event in human terms.

## Source Context

The chapter title "Hearsay Evidence" signals Monroe's own epistemic positioning: he received this information via OBE perception filtered through NVC communication with the INSPEC, and acknowledges this is not direct physical observation. He does not specify when in human calendar time the event occurs or what its physical manifestation will be. The INSPEC states it is "long past" the point where the event might not occur — it will happen — but the outcome is genuinely uncertain.

Monroe's action in the preceding chapters (helping BB make contact with a specific individual in a green-pyramid building — which Monroe later realizes is his own Monroe Institute property) is described as "one of the minute random factors that may make it so."
