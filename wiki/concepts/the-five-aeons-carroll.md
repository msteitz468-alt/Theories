## Definition from Source

Carroll proposes that humanity has evolved through four major "states of consciousness," or aeons, with a fifth now emerging. As Carroll writes in the section "The Millennium": "Humanity has evolved through four major states of consciousness, or aeons, and a fifth is on the horizon."

The four historical aeons, in order, are:

1. **The Animistic/Shamanistic Aeon** — the oldest, arising "from the mists of time," characterized by animism, shamanism, and direct magical power used for survival. Carroll holds that "the rulers of humans had a firm grasp of the psychic forces." Its residue persists in witchcraft and sorcery traditions and in some surviving aboriginal cultures.

2. **The Pagan Aeon** — arose with settled agriculture and city life. As humans moved away from nature, direct psychic knowledge degraded into "gods, spirits, and superstition." The pagan aeon began approximately six thousand years ago.

3. **The Monotheistic Aeon** — arose within pagan civilization and supplanted it. Carroll identifies Zoroastrianism, Judaism, Christianity, Islam, and Buddhism as its forms. "In the monotheistic aeon, people worshipped a singular, idealized form of themselves." This aeon began roughly 2,500–3,000 years ago.

4. **The Atheistic Aeon** — arose within Western monotheistic cultures and is still spreading. Carroll describes it as containing "the radical and positive notions that the universe can be understood and manipulated by careful observation of the behavior of material things." It began "several hundred years ago."

5. **The Fifth (Chaoist) Aeon** — embryonic and contested. Carroll holds that it represents "a return to the consciousness of the first aeon but in a higher form," with [[Chaos Magic]] and Chaoist philosophy becoming dominant. In *Liber AOM*'s "Aeonics" section, Carroll writes: "The fifth magical aeon exists only in embryonic form. Its precise manifestation hangs in the balance. The new Chaoist aeon may develop into an Aquarian Age or a time of totalitarian tyranny."

Carroll describes evolution through the aeons as "cyclic in the form of an upward spiral." He also notes that the time between aeon transitions shortens as history progresses.

The "Random Belief" section of *Liber NOX* makes the aeonic schema experiential: the six "dice options" (Paganism, Monotheism, Atheism, Nihilism, Chaoism, Superstition) correspond to stages within the belief cycle that "may help to make clear the aeonic mechanism which creates the various psychic millennia of past and future history."
