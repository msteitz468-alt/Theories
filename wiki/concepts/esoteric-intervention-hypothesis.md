## Definition from Source

The "Esoteric Intervention" is the second speculative hypothesis Vallée offers in Chapter 11 of
*Messengers of Deception*. It proposes that **an occult group has mastered a "psychotronic"
technology** — a means of manipulating physical reality, projecting mentally controlled images,
extending consciousness out of the body, or creating local distortions of spacetime — and is
using it to produce UFO effects and steer human belief.

## Description from Source

Vallée notes that many reported UFO phenomena (poltergeist effects, levitation, psychic control,
healing, out-of-body experiences) are "quite familiar to those who know the occult literature"
(Eliphas Lévi, Papus/Encausse, Reichenbach, Flammarion). He observes documented overlaps between
occult orders (the Rosicrucians, Crowley's O.T.O., offshoots of the Golden Dawn) and UFO
research, and recalls that Nazi Germany ran research institutes "working along these lines" while
the USSR and US pursued "psychotronics." Such a group, he speculates, "could operate
internationally, independently of local governments… and could produce many of the effects UFOs
seem capable of manifesting."

## Limits Vallée notes

Vallée judges this hypothesis weaker than the [[The Martian Conspiracy (Vallée's first stratagem hypothesis)]]:
it assumes manipulators "much more clever than is typical of the human race," and it "is a
conspiracy theory, and suffers from all the weaknesses of such theories." He warns that most
contactee conspiracy claims (e.g., George Hunt Williamson's "International Bankers") echo
antisemitic myths like the *Protocols of the Elders of Zion*. He prefers a *variant*: some group
using occult cults as a front, as "Major Murphy" suggested. The encounter of AMORC's
[[Raymond Bernard]] with the manipulator "Maha" and the "High Council" is his prime exhibit of
what such an "intervention" might look like.
