# Chesed (the Fourth Sephirah)

## Definition from Source
Fortune gives Chesed's title as "Chesed, Mercy" (Hebrew: Cheth, Samech, Daleth), with the additional titles Gedulah, Love, and Majesty. Its Magical Image is "a mighty crowned and throned king." It is situated in the centre of the Pillar of Mercy, immediately below Chokmah, and is the first Sephirah of Microprosopos (Adam Kadmon), coming into being just below the Abyss.

Its Yetziratic Text calls it "the Cohesive or Receptive Intelligence because it contains all the Holy Powers, and from it emanate all the spiritual virtues with the most exalted essences," which "emanate one from another by virtue of the Primordial Emanation, the Highest Crown, Kether."

Attributions as given: God-Name, El. Archangel, Tzadkiel. Order of Angels, Chasmalim (Brilliant Ones). Mundane Chakra, Tzedek, Jupiter. Spiritual Experience, Vision of Love. Virtue, Obedience. Vice, Bigotry, Hypocrisy, Gluttony, Tyranny. Correspondence in the Microcosm, the left arm. Symbols: the solid figure (tetrahedron), the pyramid, the equal-armed cross, the orb, wand, sceptre, crook. Tarot cards, the four Fours (Perfected Work, Pleasure, Rest from Strife, Earthly Power). Colours: violet (Atziluth), blue (Briah), deep purple (Yetzirah), deep azure flecked yellow (Assiah).

Fortune describes Chesed as the loving father, protector and preserver, continuing the organising, upbuilding (anabolic) work of Chokmah and balancing with mercy the severity of Geburah, which it emanates. It is the sphere of formulation of the archetypal idea — the "concretion of the abstract" — and the sphere in which disincarnate Masters (grade of Adeptus Exemptus) work to bring archetypal ideas from the Logoidal consciousness down toward manifestation (Chapter XVIII).
