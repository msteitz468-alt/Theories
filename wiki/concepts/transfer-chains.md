## Definition from Source

A transfer chain is the consecutive series of stages that must be traversed in the [[Alternatives Space]] to reach a goal sector that lies far from one's current [[Life Lines|life line]]. Each link in the chain represents a separate stage; stages cannot be skipped because they are sequentially dependent.

"This consecutive path towards a goal in the alternatives space is called a transfer chain. Every link in the chain represents a separate stage." (Ch. VIII)

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Sequential dependency**: "it is impossible to move onto any given stage without having completed the one immediately before it" — just as one cannot study for a master's degree before finishing an undergraduate degree
- **Each link = homogeneous sectors**: "Each link in a transfer chain is made up of interconnected and relatively homogeneous sectors of the alternatives space"
- **Visualisation applies to current link only**: the key rule is to "practice visualising the process of moving towards your goal at your current stage only" — visualising a distant future link produces frustration and triggers [[Excess Potential]]
- **Signs relate to current link**: [[Guiding Signs]] only relate to the current link in the transfer chain, not to the distant final goal (Ch. VIII)
- **Cannot shortcut**: trying to visualise the process of moving along a part of the path not yet relevant "could end up getting you lost" (Ch. VIII)

## Visualisation in Transfer Chains (from source)

"Visualisation in the Transurfing method: mentally picturing the realization process of your current link in the transfer chain." (Ch. VIII Summary)

The slide (positive visualisation) of the end goal is still useful — it expands the comfort zone and attunes thought energy to the target life line — but the active visualisation of process must focus on the current stage.

"The goal should be held in the mind in the form of a slide which helps widen the limits of the comfort zone and attune the frequency of your thought energy to the target lifeline. This is the sole function of third type visualisation." (Ch. VIII)
