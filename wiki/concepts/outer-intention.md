## Definition from Source

Outer intention is the force by which a person selects life lines in the [[Alternatives Space]] rather than achieving goals through direct personal effort. Zeland describes it as the most powerful intentional force available to humans, and as the power that makes Transurfing possible.

"Outer intention = extending intention to external world; works to choose life lines in the alternatives space." (Ch. VII)
"Outer intention is the power that makes Transurfing possible." (Ch. VII Summary)
"Outer intention is resoluteness in the decision to have." (Ch. VII Summary)
"Outer intention: Circumstances are such that... / It turns out that..." (the characteristic mental posture)

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Chooses rather than achieves**: "Inner intention achieves the goal, outer intention chooses it" — outer intention does not push through obstacles but selects the sector of the alternatives space where the goal already exists
- **Concentration on how goal realizes itself**: "Outer intention = concentration of attention on how goal realizes itself" (Ch. VII) — attention is placed on the realization process, not on the goal or on personal effort
- **Simply allows**: "Outer intention simply allows the goal to realize itself" (Ch. VII)
- **Born of improvisation**: outer intention arises spontaneously, not through forced will; "Outer intention arises not as a result of will but as a consequence of harmony between the heart and mind" (Ch. VII)
- **Operates by moving between sectors**: "It is not the object itself that moves, but its point of realization in the alternatives space... Outer intention makes it possible to shift the point of material realization from one sector to another" (Ch. VII)
- **Requires wakefulness**: "To work with outer intention you have to wake up" — awareness (the Guardian role) is a prerequisite
- **Atrophied in modern humans**: the ability to direct outer intention has atrophied, suppressed by pendulums (Ch. VII)

## Three Conditions (from source)

Conditions for mastering outer intention, stated in Chs. VII and IX:
1. **Awareness** — the Guardian's detached observation
2. **Reducing importance** — eliminating excess potential around the goal
3. **Letting go of the goal** — accepting the possibility of failure at the outset

"The feeling of clarity without words, knowledge without faith, confidence without hesitation comes from the unity that exists between heart and mind." (Ch. VIII, Widening Your Comfort Zone)

## Historical Assertions (from source)

- Egyptian pyramids were built using outer intention (Ch. VII)
- Atlantis was destroyed by balanced forces after its inhabitants perfected outer intention (Ch. VII)
- The Holy Grail, philosopher's stone, and related quests are described as historical attempts to harness outer intention (Ch. VII)
- Telekinesis is asserted to be a unique capacity for outer intention (Ch. VII)

## Negative Outer Intention (from source)

The source notes that outer intention acts just as reliably in the negative direction: "Heart and mind united in negative expectation → easily realized" (Ch. VII Summary). [[Slides (Transurfing)]] are the primary mechanism by which outer intention operates to the person's detriment without their conscious direction.
