## Definition / Characterization from Source

In Manly P. Hall's usage, the **secret doctrine** is a single body of transcendental knowledge — a "Universal Wisdom" concerning "the relation of manifested things to their invisible ultimate cause" — that has existed "from the time of the earliest civilizations" and been "concealed within the rituals, symbols, and allegories of religions and philosophies." Hall holds that the Mystery schools were its guardians, that the great philosophers of antiquity were its initiates, and that it was transmitted in an unbroken esoteric current through the Egyptian, Greek/Pythagorean, Hermetic, Qabbalistic, Rosicrucian, and Masonic traditions.

Because the doctrine was, by design, "never revealed to the profane" and "never committed plainly to writing," Hall says it survives chiefly in symbol and allegory — and that recovering it requires reading those ciphers rather than literal texts.
