## Definition / Characterization from Source

Joseph Campbell's term (adapted from Joyce) for the single, universal narrative pattern he argues underlies the hero myths of every culture: the **hero's journey** of **Departure → Initiation → Return**. A hero ventures from the ordinary world into a region of supernatural wonder, encounters fabulous forces and wins a decisive victory, and returns with a boon to benefit fellow humans. Campbell breaks this into recurring stages — the Call to Adventure, the crossing of the first threshold, the road of trials, the meeting with the goddess, atonement with the father, apotheosis, the ultimate boon, and the return — and reads the figures and ordeals as symbolic projections of the psyche (drawing on Jung and Freud).

For Campbell the monomyth is more than a literary template: it is myth's enduring function — a rite of passage that reconciles the individual with the cosmos and channels psychological and spiritual transformation.
