## Definition from Source

The Principle of Mentalism is the first and foundational of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]], stated
in the Kybalion's central maxim:

> "THE ALL is MIND; The Universe is Mental."
> — THE KYBALION

Per the Three Initiates, this Principle "embodies the truth that 'All is Mind.'" It holds that
THE ALL — "the Substantial Reality underlying all the outward manifestations and appearances which
we know under the terms of 'The Material Universe'; the 'Phenomena of Life'; 'Matter'; 'Energy'"
— is SPIRIT, described as "AN UNIVERSAL, INFINITE, LIVING MIND." The entire phenomenal universe is
accordingly "simply a Mental Creation of THE ALL, subject to the Laws of Created Things," existing
within the Mind of THE ALL, "in which Mind we 'live and move and have our being.'"

The Kybalion treats this Principle as the "Master-Key" of the whole Hermetic philosophy: "He who
grasps the truth of the Mental Nature of the Universe is well advanced on The Path to Mastery."
Without it, "Mastery is impossible, and the student knocks in vain at the many doors of The Temple."
It also explains why all mental and psychic phenomena — "Energy," "Power," and "Matter" — are
subordinate to Mind.
