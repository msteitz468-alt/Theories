## Definition from Source

**Heart coherence** is the physiological state in which the heart beats in a consistent, rhythmic,
orderly manner — the opposite of the incoherent, jagged rhythms produced by stress emotions such as
resentment, anger, and frustration. Dispenza, drawing on HeartMath Institute (HMI) research, teaches
that cultivating elevated emotions (gratitude, appreciation, compassion, love, joy) causes the heart
to enter this coherent state, which then entrains the brain and autonomic nervous system toward
health, creativity, and greater intuition.

**Heart intelligence** is defined by HMI (as quoted by Dispenza) as: "The flow of awareness and
insight that we experience once the mind and emotions are brought into balance and coherence through
a self-initiated process. This form of intelligence is experienced as direct, intuitive knowing that
manifests in thought and emotions that are beneficial for ourselves and others."

## Description from Source

Dispenza teaches that the heart is not merely a pump but an autorhythmic sensory organ with its own
nervous system — the "intrinsic cardiac nervous system" or "heart-brain" — containing approximately
40,000 neurons, a discovery he attributes to J. Andrew Armour (1991) that gave rise to the field of
neurocardiology. Ninety percent of the nerve fibers connecting heart and brain ascend from heart to
brain (afferent pathways), meaning the heart continuously sends signals that modify activity in the
brain's cognitive and emotional centers.

Key claims Dispenza makes about the heart's electromagnetic properties:

- The heart produces the **strongest magnetic field in the body** — 5,000 times greater in strength
  than the brain's field.
- This field can be measured **8 to 10 feet** from the body using a magnetometer.
- HRV (heart rate variability) encodes emotional state information in the intervals between
  heartbeats; with approximately 75% accuracy, science can predict what someone is feeling from
  beat-to-beat analysis alone.
- When the heart is in a coherent state, brain waves entrain to approximately **0.10 Hz**, described
  as a state of optimum performance and access to deeper intuition.
- Sustained heart-centered emotions are said to trigger "at least 1,400 biochemical changes" that
  promote growth and repair; incoherence triggers approximately 1,200 chemical changes linked to
  stress.

Dispenza reports that in his partnership with HMI, workshop students sustaining elevated emotions
for 15–20 minutes per day over four days showed significant increases in immunoglobulin A (IgA),
indicating improved immune function. He presents HRV charts from individual students sustaining
coherence for 38–75 minutes continuously, including one spontaneous episode of "super coherence"
outside of formal meditation.

The practice is taught via a **Heart Coherence Meditation** based on HMI's Heart Lock-In Technique:
attention placed on the heart center, slow breathing, cultivation of elevated emotions, and
broadcasting that energy beyond the body paired with intention.

## Strict-extraction note

Coherence and HRV research by HeartMath Institute is real and peer-reviewed; however, specific
quantitative claims (5,000× magnetic field strength, 1,400 biochemical changes, IgA from elevated
emotions) are Dispenza's framing of that research and may differ from what the original studies
concluded. All healing and gene-expression benefits are recorded as Dispenza's teaching, not
established medical fact.
