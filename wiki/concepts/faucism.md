## Definition from Source

**"Faucism"** is the coinage in *[[Faucian Bargain (The Most Powerful and Dangerous Bureaucrat in American History)]]* for the governing arrangement the book attacks: rule of public life by an unelected, unaccountable health bureaucracy — epitomized by [[Anthony Fauci]] — in which "trust the experts" and "trust the science" displace constitutional self-government. The authors pair it with **"Covidstan"** (their term for the media-and-bureaucracy regime of pandemic messaging).

## Key Points

- Faucism is presented as a species of unaccountable **administrative state**, not a one-man conspiracy: Fauci is "the symptom," and "if Fauci weren't there to step to the microphone, it would've just been someone else."
- Its hallmarks, per the book: indefinite **lockdowns** and **mask mandates** justified by shifting expert pronouncements; suppression or marginalization of dissenting experts; and a feedback loop between Fauci and a media that "feeds off of Fauci."
- Diagnosed against Dwight Eisenhower's 1961 farewell warning that "public policy could itself become the captive of a scientific-technological elite."
- The book's prescribed remedy is constitutional: reasserting that "we the people," not "them the experts," govern — including a proposed "9/11-style tribunal" for accountability.
