Vallée's deductions from the Five Facts: (1) the behavior of nonhuman visitors, or of a superior race coexisting with us, would not necessarily appear purposeful to humans; (2) since the nature of time remains a puzzle, no theory of possible visitors can ignore our ignorance of it; (3) the whole mystery contains all the elements of a myth usable for political or sociological purposes, evidenced by the link between report content and the progress of human technology.
