# The Three Supernals

## Definition from Source
The Three Supernals are Kether (Crown), Chokmah (Wisdom), and Binah (Understanding), the first three Sephiroth, together with their hidden "fourth," Daath (Knowledge). Fortune describes the Lightning Flash proceeding "from Kether outwards and downwards to the right to reach Chokmah, and then turn[ing] on a level course to the left" to establish Binah, forming "a triangular figure upon the glyph... called the Triangle of the Three Supernals, or the First Trinity," which "is separated from the rest of the Sephiroth by the Abyss, which normal human consciousness cannot cross." "Here are the roots of existence, hidden from our eyes."

Kether "differentiates into Chokmah and Binah before it achieves phenomenal existence." Chokmah and Binah are called **Abba**, the Supernal Father (active male potency), and **Ama**, the Supernal Mother (passive female potency), standing at the heads of the Pillar of Mercy and the Pillar of Severity respectively, with Kether's Middle Pillar being the Column of Equilibrium (Mildness). Considered as a pair, Chokmah and Binah are Force and Form. Binah is also called the Great Sea and Shabbathai, the Sphere of Saturn, and is two-aspected as Ama (Dark Sterile Mother) and Aima (Bright Fertile Mother). Kether is titled Arik Anpin, the Vast Countenance, the White Head, the Head Which Is Not; its magical symbol (per Crowley) is "an ancient bearded king seen in profile," of whom "In him is all right side" (the left, turned to the Unmanifest, is unseen). Kether crystallizes from Ain Soph Aur, the Limitless Light; it is "the Crown," resting above the head, and so represents "the raw material of consciousness" rather than consciousness itself.

**Daath**, the invisible fourth, is "formed out of the conjunction of Chokmah and Binah and is situated astride the Abyss." It has no Deity-name, angelic host, or mundane symbol, and never appears drawn upon the Tree. Crowley is cited as placing Daath in "another dimension," forming "the apex of a pyramid" whose base is Kether, Chokmah, and Binah. Fortune: "To me, Daath presents the idea of realisation and consciousness." Daath, Kether, and Tiphareth lie on the Middle Pillar, which "equates with the spine in man," and Daath sits where the Path of the Arrow (the way consciousness rises) crosses the Abyss — "the secret of both generation and regeneration."
