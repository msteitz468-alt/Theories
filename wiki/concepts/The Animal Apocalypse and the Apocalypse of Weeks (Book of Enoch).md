# The Animal Apocalypse and the Apocalypse of Weeks (Book of Enoch)

The Book of Dream Visions (1 Enoch chapters 83–90) and the Apocalypse of Weeks embedded within the Epistle of Enoch (chapters 91–93) are two major apocalyptic compositions within 1 Enoch. Both were revealed to [[Enoch (antediluvian patriarch)]] as prophetic visions spanning all of history from creation to final judgment. They differ in method: the Animal Apocalypse encodes history in an extended beast allegory (animals represent nations, individuals, and cosmic figures), while the Apocalypse of Weeks divides all history into ten "weeks" of moral-historical eras culminating in cosmic renewal.

## The Animal Apocalypse (chapters 85–90)

### Symbolic framework
The Book of Enoch narrates that Enoch received two great dream visions. The first (ch. 83–84) was a catastrophic vision of heaven collapsing and earth sinking into an abyss — interpreted by his grandfather Mahalalel as foretelling the Flood — after which Enoch prayed for a remnant to survive.

The second and larger vision (chs. 85–90), called the Animal Apocalypse, recasts the entire history of the world using animal imagery. The editor's note in the Lumpkin edition identifies the key symbolic equivalents: bulls/oxen for the patriarchal line, wolves for the Egyptians, the white bull for Abraham (and later for the anticipated messianic figure), a sheep as Jacob/Israel, a ram as military deliverers, eagles as the Roman Empire, and wild asses as neighboring peoples.

### Pre-flood and flood sequence (chs. 85–89)
The vision opens before human marriage with a white bull (Adam or the primordial human) and a heifer; a black and a red bull follow (Cain and Abel). Fallen stars descend from heaven, take on animal form, and mate with the cattle — producing elephants, camels, and asses (the Nephilim and associated corruption). Four angelic figures (white men) descend: one binds the first fallen star and casts it into a narrow abyss, another gathers the fallen stars and hurls them into an abyss of the earth. A fourth instructs a white bull — who "became a man" — to build a great vessel. The flood follows: all other cattle perish while the vessel rides out the waters. When the waters recede, the man-bull and three companions emerge; from them descend lions, wolves, foxes, the full range of beasts, and among them a white bull who fathers a wild ass and another white bull (the patriarchal lineage continuing through Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac).

### Israel in Egypt and the Exodus (ch. 89)
A black wild boar (Esau/Edom) and a white sheep (Jacob/Israel) follow. Twelve sheep are born; one is given to the asses (Joseph sold into Egypt) and then to the wolves (Egypt). The Lord of the sheep descends, calls a sheep (Moses) that escaped from the wolves to lead the others, sends it back as emissary alongside another (Aaron), and ultimately the Lord parts a sea, drowning the wolves in pursuit. The sheep receive water and grass in the wilderness, ascend a rock, and the Lord reveals Himself in majesty. The sheep build a house and tower (the Tabernacle/Temple) for the Lord.

### The 70 Shepherds
A defining feature of the Animal Apocalypse is the appointment of seventy shepherds (angelic overseers) to govern Israel during the period of foreign domination. The Book of Enoch teaches that the Lord of the sheep, displeased by Israel's repeated apostasy, deliberately handed the sheep to lions, tigers, wolves, and hyenas. He appointed a recording angel to secretly tally every excess killing the shepherds committed beyond what was commanded — for use as testimony against them in the final judgment. The text specifies twelve final shepherds who destroy even more than their predecessors; the record is sealed and laid before the Lord.

### The great horn and final judgment (ch. 90)
Birds of prey — eagles, vultures, ravens, kites — devour the sheep during the period of the shepherds. Eventually lambs with open eyes appear (variously interpreted as the Essenes or early followers of Jesus), and a great horn grows on one sheep (Judas Maccabee or, in Christian reading, Jesus). The enemies attempt to break the horn but cannot. The Lord of the sheep descends in wrath, strikes the earth with his staff, and the earth splits open, swallowing all the beasts and birds. A throne is established, books are opened, the fallen stars and the seventy shepherds are judged and cast into a fiery abyss. The blinded sheep (those who remained faithless) are also judged and burned. The old house is folded up and a new, greater house — "greater and loftier than that first" — is set in its place. All surviving and transformed creatures bow before the sheep. Finally, a white bull with great black horns is born; all generations become white bulls, and "the first among them became a lamb, and that lamb became a great animal" — the messianic figure who completes the restoration.

## The Apocalypse of Weeks (chapters 91–93)

### Structure and disorder of transmission
The Apocalypse of Weeks divides all history into ten "weeks," each representing a distinct moral-historical era. The editor notes (and the translation reflects) that the codex was not kept in sequence: the ten-week list is given in chapter 93, but its explanatory expansions appear scattered across chapters 91–92. The reconstructed sequence runs from Enoch's own time (Week 1) through the Eternal Judgment and a new heaven (Week 10) and then into unlimited weeks of goodness.

### The Ten Weeks
The Book of Enoch teaches the following schema:

- **Week 1** — Enoch's own time: righteousness and judgment endure; Enoch is born seventh in this week.
- **Week 2** — Great wickedness arises and deceit springs up; the first end (the Flood) occurs; one man (Noah) is saved.
- **Week 3** — A man is elected as "the plant of righteous judgment" (Abraham); his posterity becomes the plant of righteousness forever.
- **Week 4** — Visions of the holy and righteous are seen; a law is given for all generations; an enclosure is made (interpreted as Moses and the covenant).
- **Week 5** — "The house of glory and dominion" is built forever (Solomon's Temple).
- **Week 6** — All who live in it are blinded; hearts forsake wisdom; a man ascends (widely interpreted as Jesus or a prophet); at its close the house of dominion is burned and the chosen root is dispersed.
- **Week 7** — An apostate generation arises with many apostate deeds; at its end the elect righteous are chosen and given sevenfold instruction concerning all creation.
- **Week 8** — A sword is given for righteous judgment on wrongdoers; sinners are handed to the righteous; at its end houses are acquired and a House is built for the Great King in glory forever.
- **Week 9** — The righteous judgment is revealed to the whole world; all deeds of the impious vanish; all people turn to the Path of Uprightness.
- **Week 10** — In the seventh part, the Watchers receive their Eternal Judgment; the First Heaven vanishes; a New Heaven appears; all heavenly powers shine with light sevenfold brighter.
- **After Week 10** — Many weeks without number, forever, in goodness and righteousness; sin is never again mentioned.

### Moral framework of chapters 91–92
Flanking the ten-week list, Enoch exhorts his assembled sons to love righteousness and walk in it. He warns that violence will increase on the earth and a "great chastisement shall come from heaven" when blasphemy and violence are complete. The righteous shall arise from sleep and receive wisdom; sinners shall be cut off by the sword.
