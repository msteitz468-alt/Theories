## Concept Definition

The **Toroidal Universe Model** is Itzhak Bentov's model of the cosmos. He describes the universe as a closed-loop system shaped like a self-reflecting torus (a hollow ovoid or smoke ring) that constantly turns itself inside out. 

In this model, matter is projected outward from a central core, flows across the outer surface of the torus, and is eventually drawn back into the core through a central hole, where it is recycled.

## Characteristics of the Model

* **The Smoke Ring Metaphor:** The universe behaves like a rotating smoke ring. Matter flows along lines of force, moving out of the center and returning. 
* **The Central Core (White Hole/Black Hole):** The center of the torus acts as both a source (white hole) and a sink (black hole). Matter is ejected as a high-energy jet, cools and aggregates as it travels along the outer boundaries, and is pulled back into the central core to be re-energized and re-emitted.
* **Closed-Loop Recycling:** Space and time are curved within this closed loop. There is no beginning or end, but rather a continuous cycle of creation, experience, and destruction.
* **Interacting Holographic Fields:** The entire toroidal structure is a giant hologram. All dimensions, evolutionary bands, and kingdoms of consciousness coexist within this closed loop, constantly overlapping and communicating telepathically.

## Cosmological Implication

Because the universe is a closed loop, instant communication (nonlocality) is possible. The "void" or "null point" at the center of the torus connects all parts of the loop simultaneously, allowing information to bypass spatial distance entirely.
