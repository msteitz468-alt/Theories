## Definition from Source

Pasulka argues that [[Contact with nonhuman intelligence (Pasulka)|UFO contact experiences]] are
intrinsically religious and mystical in character, and that the most accurate interpretive framework
for them is *gnosis* — experiential, embodied knowledge as opposed to abstract information. She draws
on the scholarly definition of esotericism: "knowledge which is specialized or advanced in nature,
available only to a narrow circle of 'enlightened,' 'initiated,' or highly educated people," but
argues that experiencers like Jose access this knowledge *spontaneously*, outside any formal
institution or school.

## Description from Source

### Gnosis, esotericism, and the UFO

Pasulka defines gnosis through a concrete analogy: the difference between reading the ingredients on
a chocolate-bar wrapper (informational knowledge) and actually tasting the bar (gnostic, experiential
knowledge). UFO contact, she argues, is more like tasting — a direct apprehension that cannot be
reduced to propositional claims about space vehicles or anthropomorphized aliens.

She identifies three scholarly "models" of esoteric traditions present in the book's subjects:
(1) an enchanted worldview with ancient roots; (2) occult currents that emerged after the
Enlightenment as alternatives to religion and rational science; (3) a universal "inner" spiritual
dimension of religion. Experiencers like Jose inhabit all three simultaneously.

### Spontaneous esotericism

Pasulka coins the phrase "spontaneous esotericism" to describe how Jose — and many other
experiencers — arrive at gnostic frameworks for their contact without institutional initiation. Jose
interprets his UFO encounters as "spectral messages and gifts," numinous rather than mechanical.
He locates them within a larger worldview of "ángeles y demonios" — angels and demons — and spiritual
warfare, drawing on Charismatic Christianity, the Samurai ethics of *Bushido*, and the philosophy of
[[Pierre Teilhard de Chardin]]. Pasulka argues this is the norm, not the exception: "Many
experiencers see the UFO event as natural and they view the public hysteria around it as unnecessary
and even harmful."

### Hynek, Vallée, and Rosicrucianism

Pasulka anchors the connection between ufology and esotericism in the documented practices of
[[J. Allen Hynek]] and [[Jacques Vallée]], both of whom engaged in Rosicrucianism — a tradition
linking esoteric science with the pursuit of angelic or etheric beings. Vallée's private library,
which Pasulka visited in San Francisco, contained entire bookcases devoted to the history of angels
and fallen angels; Hynek's annotated Rosicrucian texts (bequeathed to Vallée) referenced "etheric or
cosmic entities" imparting a "language that will bridge science and spirituality." Pasulka reads her
own meeting with Vallée — in which he steered conversation not to UFO hardware but to histories of
angels and the scholarship of Carmelite nuns on Satan — as an implicit argument that the tradition
already has the right categories for the phenomena.

Scholar Aaron French is cited as identifying Hynek's ufology as a form of esotericism grounded in
Rudolf Steiner's anthroposophy and Max Heindel's Rosicrucian writings, which argued the twentieth
century would be a time when humans would perceive a "spiritual science" by cultivating meditative
thinking.

### Gnosis and the download

For Simone (a pseudonymous venture capitalist and AI researcher), the gnostic tradition is not a
metaphor but a technical framework. She is described as "initiated into a gnostic tradition" in both
Western and Asian forms, and explains that mystical protocols (prayer, meditation, math, chant)
"occupy the rational mind" to enable direct access to the intelligence that she and other
technologists call [[The download (spontaneous cognition, Pasulka)|the download]]. She connects
Plato's Dialectic — asking and answering questions to achieve a state of gnosis — to the structure of
modern AI language models: "LLMs such as ChatGPT are preparing our minds for inquiry and for asking
questions … This is like the human seeking initiation. The knowledge. The 'upgrade.'"
