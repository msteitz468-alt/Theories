# Kether (the First Sephirah)

## Definition from Source
Fortune's Table of Correspondences for Kether (Ch. XV): Title, Kether, the Crown (Hebrew: Kaph, Tau, Resh). Magical Image: an ancient bearded king seen in profile. Situation on the Tree: at the head of the Pillar of Equilibrium, in the Supernal Triangle. Yetziratic Text: "The First Path is called the Admirable or Hidden Intelligence because it is the Light giving the power of comprehension of the First Principle, which hath no beginning. And it is the Primal Glory, because no created being can attain to its essence." Titles given to Kether: Existence of Existences, Concealed of the Concealed, Ancient of Ancients, Ancient of Days, the Primordial Point, the Point within the Circle, the Most High, the Vast Countenance, the White Head, the Head which is not, Macroprosopos, Amen, Lux Occulta, Lux Interna, He. God-Name: Eheieh. Archangel: Metatron. Order of Angels: Holy living creatures, Chaioth ha Qadesh. Mundane Chakra: Rashith ha Gilgalim, Primum Mobile, First Swirlings. Spiritual Experience: Union with God. Virtue: Attainment, Completion of the Great Work. Vice: none given. Correspondence in Microcosm: the Cranium, the Sah, Yechidah, the Divine Spark, the Thousand-petalled Lotus. Symbols: the point, the crown, the swastika. Tarot Cards: the four Aces. Colours: Brilliance (Atziluth); pure white brilliance (Briah, Yetzirah); white flecked gold (Assiah).

Fortune describes Kether as "the primal crystallisation into manifestation of that which was hitherto unmanifest," pure being with "no form but only pure being," existing prior to the pairs of opposites, which only appear with the emanation of Chokmah and Binah (Ch. XV.2-3). She states Kether is "the abyss whence all arose, and back into which it will fall at the end of its epoch" (Ch. XV.7), and that it is "existence of another type" that appears to us as non-existence only because it conforms to none of our usual criteria of existence. The magical image, the ancient bearded king, is seen only in profile — "he is all right side" — because one aspect of Kether must ever remain hidden, "like the hidden side of the moon" (Ch. XV.18). Its archangel Metatron, the "Prince of Countenances," is said by tradition to have been the teacher of Moses (Ch. XV.20). The Spiritual Experience of Union with God is said to be irrecoverable while in incarnation: "when we aspire to the Vision of Kether in Atziluth we must be prepared to enter into the Light and come not forth again" (Ch. XV.25).
