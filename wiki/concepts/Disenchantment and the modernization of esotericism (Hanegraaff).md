# Disenchantment and the modernization of esotericism (Hanegraaff)

## Definition from Source

In *Western Esotericism: A Guide for the Perplexed* (Chapter 7, "Modernization"),
[[Wouter J. Hanegraaff (b. 1961)]] argues that Western esotericism has **no unchanging "Tradition"**: it is
continuously reshaped by the historical process Max Weber called **disenchantment** — the rise to social
dominance of *instrumental causality* and a mechanistic, de-animated picture of the world. Rather than being
destroyed by this process, esotericism **modernized**: it negotiated a series of compromises between the older
worldview of **correspondences** and **occult causality** and the newer regime of instrumental causality.

Hanegraaff traces several forms this modernization took: nineteenth-century **occultism** adapting esotericism to
a disenchanted, post-Enlightenment and scientific world; the absorption of **evolutionism**, which transformed
esoteric conceptions of spiritual history into progressive/developmental schemes; the twin movements of the
**psychologization of the sacred and the sacralization of psychology**; and, under the separation of church and
state, the emergence of a **"religious supermarket"** of individualized esoteric spiritualities. On this view
esotericism is not a survival of pre-modern tradition but a fully modern phenomenon.
