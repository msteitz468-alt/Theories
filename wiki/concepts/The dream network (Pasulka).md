## Definition from Source

The "dream network" is Pasulka's framing for a recurring pattern among UFO experiencers: a set of distinctive, ultra-vivid dreams that are qualitatively different from ordinary sleep and that function as a medium for receiving information, transmitting messages across individuals, and sometimes coordinating collaborative activity. Pasulka argues that these dreams constitute a shared experiential stratum — a network — linking experiencers to each other and to a wider information field.

## Description from Source

Pasulka introduces the concept in Chapter 10 through Len Filppu's "vivid dream" accounts and then broadens it with comparative material from Tibetan Buddhist dream yoga, Australian Aboriginal Dreamtime (Alcheringa), and Western esoteric initiation theory.

Key characteristics Pasulka attributes to dreams in this network:

- **Lucidity**: dreamers report being aware they are dreaming or believe they are fully awake.
- **Full color, high resolution**: experiencers consistently distinguish these dreams from ordinary "jumbled" dream life by their visual richness and memorability.
- **Precognitive content**: dreams contain information later verified as true (e.g., John's message pointing to the "man with the odd hat").
- **Cross-individual messaging**: dreamers receive instructions to deliver messages to specific people they could not have known about through normal channels.
- **Collaborative meetings**: scientists and technopreneurs in Pasulka's circle report convening in dream states to discuss problems and continuing those conversations during waking hours.
- **Unsolicited arrival**: vivid dreams "came of their own accord," not through deliberate practice, placing them closer to the [[The download (spontaneous cognition, Pasulka)|download]] than to trained meditation.

Pasulka connects the dream network to the [[The organic network - noösphere (Pasulka)|organic network / noösphere]], suggesting both are manifestations of the same underlying information field that UFO contact appears to activate. She draws on Tibetan Lama Lhanang Rinpoche, who describes how initiates "can go anywhere in the universe and learn things from dreams, and bring this back," and on Australian Aboriginal accounts of "dream meetings" where initiates share lore across distance. These traditions all share the idea that certain dreaming is not psyche-internal but involves access to a genuinely external domain.

Within the Western esoteric frame, Pasulka cites Rudolf Steiner's account of initiation, in which "ordinary people" begin to experience a merging of dream and waking reality and to perceive "astral beings" as part of the initiated life. Pasulka suggests that people who undergo [[Contact with nonhuman intelligence (Pasulka)|UFO contact]] undergo an analogous initiation without seeking it.
