# Malkuth (the Tenth Sephirah)

## Definition from Source
Malkuth, the Kingdom, is the tenth Sephirah, at the base of the Pillar of Equilibrium. Fortune gives its attributions (Chapter XXV) as: additional titles, the Gate, the Gate of Death, the Gate of the Shadow of Death, the Gate of Tears, the Gate of Justice, the Gate of Prayer, the Gate of the Daughter of the Mighty Ones, the Gate of the Garden of Eden, the Inferior Mother, Malkah (the Queen), Kallah (the Bride), the Virgin; God-Name, Adonai Malekh or Adonai ha Aretz; Archangel, Sandalphon (the "Dark Angel"); Choir of Angels, the Ashim ("Souls of Fire"); Mundane Chakra, Cholem ha Yesodoth ("Sphere of the Elements"); Magical Image, "a young woman, crowned and throned"; Spiritual Experience, "Vision of the Holy Guardian Angel"; Virtue, Discrimination; Vice, Avarice and Inertia; correspondence in the microcosm, the feet and the anus; symbols, the Altar of the double cube, the Equal-armed cross, the magic circle, the triangle of art; Tarot cards, the four Tens (Oppression, Perfected Success, Ruin, Wealth).

Malkuth alone participates in no functional triangle on the Tree, yet is said to receive the emanations of all other Sephiroth and represents the end-result and "nadir of evolution" through which all life must pass on the outgoing arc before the return. It is divided into four quarters assigned to the four Elements (Earth, Air, Fire, Water). Malkuth is Binah "upon a lower arc," the "Inferior Mother" as against Binah the "Superior Mother." Below Malkuth lie the Qliphothic spheres, into which "cosmic excreta" (the effete and disorganized) are cast.
