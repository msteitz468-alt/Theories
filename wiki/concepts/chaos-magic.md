## Definition from Source

Chaos Magic is the magical paradigm systematized by Peter J. Carroll in
*[[Liber Null & Psychonaut (Carroll)]]*. It treats magic as "an intensely practical, personal,
experimental art" and keeps "the techniques, rather than the dogma" of the historical traditions
(shamanism, the Golden Dawn, [[Aleister Crowley]]'s A∴A∴, [[Austin Osman Spare]]'s Zos Kia Cultus).
Its two central premises: "altered states of consciousness are the key to unlocking one's magical
abilities," and those abilities "can be developed without any symbolic system except reality
itself." Carroll calls it "a tradition with no past" — every generation rewrites magic in its own
symbolism, "only the underlying practical techniques really matter."

## Grades (from "The Quest")

The chaos tradition has "no formal hierarchy" but a "division of activity depending on ability":
**Neophyte** (disciplines the will against one's own mind — *Liber MMM*), **Initiate** (perfects a
particular form of magic — *Liber LUX/NOX*), **Adept** (perfection in all aspects — *Liber AOM*), and
**Master**. Its stated purpose is "to help determine in what form the as yet embryonic fifth aeon
will manifest."
