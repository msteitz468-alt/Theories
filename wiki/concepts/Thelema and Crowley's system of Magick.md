## Definition from Source

The umbrella framework of **Aleister Crowley's** ([[Aleister Crowley]]) magico-religious system.
**Thelema** (Greek for "Will") is the spiritual philosophy founded on the revelation Crowley received in
Cairo in 1904 — *[[The Book of the Law (Crowley)|The Book of the Law]]* — whose central Law is **"Do
what thou wilt shall be the whole of the Law; Love is the law, love under will."** Around it Crowley
built a systematic, quasi-scientific account of **Magick** ("the Science and Art of causing Change to
occur in conformity with Will") set out in *[[Magick in Theory and Practice (Crowley)|Magick in Theory
and Practice]]*, and a practical revival of the Elizabethan **Enochian** system in
*[[The Practice of Enochian Magick (Crowley)|The Practice of Enochian Magick]]*.
