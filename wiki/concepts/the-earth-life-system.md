## Definition / Characterization from Source

Robert Monroe's term for physical biological existence on Earth conceived as a single, self-regulating energy system organized around one "prime directive: **survive!**" In *Ultimate Journey* he describes it as "an exquisitely self-adjusting, autotuning, self-regenerating organization of energy" whose underlying logic is **predation** — a food chain in which "every participant is a predator," driven by competition for "food, water, oxygen, warmth, and sunlight," and in which "honor, ethics, empathy, and the like are nonexistent." Its basic pattern is change; "stasis is entropy… entropy is death." **Fear** — the possibility of non-survival — is the system's central tool.

Into this system, Monroe says, an "unusual spark" appeared in one life-form: the **Human Mind**, a "mind-consciousness foreign to the system itself." Though saddled with the same survival drives, it expresses traits "completely incompatible with the Earth Life System" — empathy for one's own and other species, and a curiosity about what lies beyond. Monroe concludes "human beings are more than mere animals living and dying in the Earth Life System."
