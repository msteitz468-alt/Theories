## Definition from Source

An induced transition is a shift to a negative life line caused by responding emotionally to a pendulum's prod. When a person reacts to a pendulum's provocations — with fear, anger, indignation, or engagement — their thought energy attunes to the pendulum's frequency, and they are carried to a life line corresponding to that frequency.

"Responding to pendulum's prod induces shift to corresponding life line." (Ch. V)

## Mechanism (from source)

The pendulum broadcasts a probe or "prod" designed to elicit a specific emotional reaction. If the person responds — whether in agreement, opposition, or fear — they are energetically entrained with the pendulum's oscillation. The result is a gradual shift to a life line where the pendulum's script plays out fully.

The transition is described as gradual and consistent: "The transition does not take place straight away. It is a gradual and consistent process which lasts for as long as the slide remains in the person's mind." (This phrasing applies to negative slides specifically but the principle extends to induced transitions generally.)

## Role of Mass Media (from source)

Ch. V ("The Induced Transition") specifically addresses the role of mass media in inducing transitions at scale. Negative information broadcast through mass media creates a "pendulum vortex" — an energetic resonance that, when a person absorbs it, pulls them into corresponding negative life lines.

"Pendulum Vortex: mass media negative info creates energetic resonance pulling person in." (Ch. V)

## Prevention (from source)

- "Do not absorb negative information" (Ch. V epigraph)
- "Do not allow" = intentionally ignore, not strive to avoid — actively ignoring rather than anxiously avoiding
- The principle "do not allow" is distinguished from "do not engage": avoidance with effort feeds the pendulum; calm non-engagement does not
