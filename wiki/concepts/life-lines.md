## Definition from Source

Life lines are sectors within the [[Alternatives Space]] that share uniform script and scenery parameters. Zeland describes destiny as flowing along a single life line until a shift occurs. Each life line has its own characteristic quality, atmosphere, and sequence of events.

"The destiny of a person flows along a life line in the alternatives space. When the parameters of a person's thought energy change, they shift from one life line to another." Life lines are not static destinations but flowing trajectories through related sectors.

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Uniform within a line**: script and scenery parameters are internally consistent along a given life line
- **Cannot be changed from within**: "You cannot change the script within a given life line; you can only choose a different alternative" (Ch. I)
- **Shift triggered by thought energy change**: when the parameters of a person's thought energy change (in frequency/quality), they shift to a different life line with matching parameters
- **Wave of fortune**: an accumulation of favourable life lines; each individual has their own (Ch. III)
- **Vaudeville life line**: a specific positive life line with no complex problems, available through intention
- **Adjacent sectors**: sectors adjacent to one's current life line contain similar scripts and scenery; distant sectors are less accessible directly

## Transition Mechanisms

The source identifies several ways life line shifts occur:

- Positive shifts: through [[Wave of Fortune]], embracing joyful energy of first good luck, using [[Slides (Transurfing)]], [[Pure Intention]], [[Outer Intention]]
- Negative shifts: through [[Excess Potential]] (balanced forces push person to a matching negative line), [[Induced Transition]] (responding to a pendulum's prod), negative [[Thought Energy (Transurfing)]] attuned to feared outcomes
