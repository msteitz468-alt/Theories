## Definition from Source

A **conscious agent** is the fundamental entity of [[Conscious Realism]] and the formal unit Donald Hoffman uses to make a science of consciousness precise (chapter 10 and the appendix of *[[The Case Against Reality (Why Evolution Hid the Truth from Our Eyes)]]*).

Informally, a conscious agent perceives, decides, and acts. It has a menu of possible **experiences** and a menu of possible **actions**, and a counter of how many experiences it has had. Each action is "a bet on future experiences."

### Formal definition (appendix)

A conscious agent C is a seven-tuple **C = (X, G, W, P, D, A, T)** where:
- **X** = possible conscious experiences (a measurable space)
- **G** = possible actions (a measurable space)
- **W** = the world (a measurable space)
- **P: W × X → X** = the **perception** kernel (how the world's state influences perception)
- **D: X × G → G** = the **decision** kernel (how perception influences choice of action)
- **A: G × W → W** = the **action** kernel (how action influences the world)
- **T** = a totally ordered set (a counter that increments with each decision)

P, D, and A are **Markovian kernels** (conditional changes — the minimal structure for probabilistic prediction, hence for testability). The perceive–decide–act loop is the **PDA loop**.

## Key Points

- **Conscious Agent Thesis**: "every aspect of consciousness can be modeled by conscious agents" — deliberately bold and falsifiable, analogous to the Church–Turing thesis for computation. Hoffman stresses the math is a *model*, not the territory: it no more creates consciousness than a weather model creates blizzards.
- Networks of conscious agents are **computationally universal** (can perform any cognitive/perceptual task) and offer an alternative to neural networks.
- Agents **combine** into more complex agents *ad infinitum* (Markovian kernels compose); a higher agent's decisions may correspond to Kahneman's effortful "System 2," the agents in its instantiation to automatic "System 1."
- There is a **bottom**: "one-bit" agents (N = 2, two experiences and two actions), whose dynamics can be analyzed completely; their compression into discrete spacetime may correspond to the Planck scale.
- A conscious agent **cannot fully experience itself** (any self-description adds experiences, in a vicious loop of incompleteness), so it must remain partly unconscious to itself — Hoffman's reframing of "unconscious processes."
- Two interacting agents compress into the conformal geometric algebra **G(2,4)** (signature (1,3)), a proposed bridge from agent dynamics to a spacetime interface.
