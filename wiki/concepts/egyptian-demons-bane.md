# Egyptian demons (Bane)

## Definition from Source

Bane characterizes ancient Egyptian demonology as a tradition where the boundary between god, demon, and protective spirit was highly fluid. Many Egyptian demonic figures were gods who had been cast down or had taken on adversarial roles; others were liminal beings who could be both malevolent and invoked for protection depending on context. The cosmology is organized around the daily passage of the sun god Ra across the sky — demonic forces primarily threaten Ra during the night hours when he travels below the horizon, and by extension threaten the souls of the dead in the underworld.

A distinctive feature Bane highlights is the Egyptian development of elaborate ritual counter-measures: magical papyri, burial spells, effigies, and annual rites (such as the Banishing of Apep ceremony) were all deployed against named demonic forces. Egyptian demonology also gave rise to magical traditions later absorbed into Hermetic and Gnostic streams — the Egyptian god Thoth, for example, was demonized in medieval Europe into the gambling-demon "Theutus," while the Hermetic texts attributed to him connect Egyptian wisdom to later Western occult traditions.

## Notable Demons

**Aldinach** — A lesser Egyptian demon appearing in the form of a woman; known to cause earthquakes, hailstorms, floods, and all forms of natural disaster, as well as to sink ships.

**Amelouith** — The demon of the Egyptian magicians; together with the demon Ephippas, Amelouith raised a column of purple substance out of the Red Sea. According to the Testament of Solomon and Exodus tradition, it was this demon who hardened Pharaoh's heart when Moses demanded the Israelites' freedom.

**Amentet** — An Egyptian demon whose name translates to "the mountain of the underworld." The name was commonly applied to cemeteries located in the desert along the western bank of the Nile.

**Apep (Apophis)** — The Lord of Darkness in ancient Egyptian religion; a fallen god who personified chaos and all that is evil, served the god Set, and commanded the demons Nak and Sebau. Depicted as a monstrous serpent, crocodile, or dragon, described as sixteen yards long with a head made of flint. Apep attacks Ra every night to prevent the sun's daily passage, hypnotizes souls in the underworld, and causes earthquakes and thunderstorms through the power he releases in battle. He is immortal and regenerates his damage daily. The *Book of Overthrowing Apep* was a ritual manual for destroying him through effigy.

**Babi** — From the Old Kingdom Books of the Dead and the Book of Going Forth by Day; the demonic god of darkness, described as the first-born son of Osiris and Isis with the appearance of a baboon. He controls darkness, is the very symbol of aggression and virility, and devours human entrails and unworthy souls. His penis is the bolt on the doors to heaven and the mast on the Underworld ferry. During the Weighing of the Heart ceremony, the deceased used spells specifically to ward him off.

**Cherti (Kherty)** — The ferryman of the dead in ancient Egyptian mythology, described in the Pyramid Texts as a man with the head of a ram. A demonic god who ferried souls to the underworld.

**Neqa'el** — A demonic god originating in both Egyptian and Greek demonology; once worshipped by assassins in ancient Egypt. He preys on anyone who harms a cat and only fights when necessary, torturing victims for a month before finally killing them. Bane notes that it was his saliva that spawned the sun god Ra.

**Sphandor** — One of the thousands of Egyptian demons named in the Testament of Solomon as having been worshipped as gods. According to the text, Sphandor breaks and bruises bones in the neck, sucks out their marrow, and paralyzes the nerves in the hands and weakens the shoulders.

**Theutus** — A misinterpretation, in Cornelius Agrippa's demonology, of the Egyptian god Thoth; demonized as the demon of cards, dice, and gambling, and the inventor of all things evil and wicked. He was condemned in medieval Europe because he taught humans numbers, which was associated with gambling. He belongs to a class of demons called the Vessels of Iniquity.
