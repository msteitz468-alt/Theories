## Definition from Source

Dispenza contrasts two models of reality in Chapter 11 of *Becoming Supernatural*:

**Space-time** is the three-dimensional, Newtonian-physical world experienced through the five senses. It is a dimension of matter, particles, people, objects, and places, where *space* is infinite. In space-time, consciousness moves from one point to another by moving a body through space, which creates the linear experience of time. Because all things occupy a fixed position (locality), space-time is a realm of separation, duality, and polarity, governed by predictable Newtonian laws.

**Time-space** is the inverse, nonphysical quantum world — a "five-dimensional multiverse" Dispenza identifies with the unified field. In time-space, *time* is infinite and eternal; there is no linear past or future, only an eternal present moment. Because everything is happening "right now," this realm contains infinite possible spaces, dimensions, and realities. It is a domain of oneness, nonlocality, and wholeness, governed by quantum laws. Entering it requires becoming "no body, no one, no thing, no where, in no time" — dissolving sensory-based identity so that consciousness merges with the unified field.

Dispenza teaches that the "bridge" between the two realms is the speed of light: matter traveling faster than light dematerializes into energy and crosses into time-space. He cites Alain Aspect's 1980s Bell-test entanglement experiments as evidence that a nonlocal, unified field connects all matter beyond three-dimensional space and time, and invokes physicist David Bohm's "implicate order" (unified field) vs. "explicit order" (material realm) framework. He also proposes that the familiar collapse of space creates time, while in time-space the "collapse of time" creates access to new spaces or dimensions.

Meditation practice — specifically becoming fully present and relinquishing attention to body, environment, and linear time — is the method Dispenza prescribes for entering time-space. Once there, Dispenza teaches, practitioners can access unlimited unknown possibilities and accelerate manifestation because they are no longer "matter trying to influence matter."
