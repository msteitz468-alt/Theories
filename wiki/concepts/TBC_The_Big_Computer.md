## Definition and Characterization from Source

In [[Thomas Campbell]]'s *[[My Big TOE]]*, **TBC** (The Big Computer) is a conceptual model for the computational infrastructure within [[Concept: AUM (Absolute Unbounded Manifold)|AUM]] that processes and runs the [[Concept: PMR (Physical Matter Reality)|PMR]] virtual reality simulation. TBC is not a literal physical computer but an emergent computational functionality within AUM's digital consciousness.

Campbell defines TBC functionally:

> "TBC is a real thing; we relate to it more by its process and functionality than by its construction and design — the same way most of us interact with our desktop computers. We don't relate to chips and basic chip-level instruction-sets, but instead see our computers in terms of their functionality and the processes they implement."

And in terms of its origins:

> "Think of TBC as a digital computational functionality (based upon discrete reality cells) that naturally evolved to improve organization, and thus reduce entropy, within the energy form we call AUO."

## TBC's Role in PMR

TBC provides the computational substrate for:
- **Memory**: Maintaining the state of all PMR entities, objects, and processes
- **Processing**: Computing state transitions according to the PMR rule-set
- **Rules**: Implementing the constraints that define our physics
- **Content**: Generating and maintaining the experiential content of PMR

In Campbell's model, TBC is running PMR much as software runs on a computer. The "physics" we experience are the rule-set, and TBC enforces it for every quantum of PMR time.

## The Even Bigger Computer (EBC)

Beyond TBC, Campbell references the **EBC** (Even Bigger Computer) — a larger computational framework discussed primarily in Book 3, Section 5. EBC represents additional computational capacity for managing:
- The broader NPMR environment
- Multiple parallel PMRs
- Probable reality computation
- The outer control loops of [[Concept: NPMR (Nonphysical Matter Reality)|NPMRn]]

Campbell notes that both TBC and EBC "are actually only tiny subsets of AUM's computing part" — they are sub-processes within the vastly larger digital consciousness system.

## Nested Computing Architecture

Campbell describes a hierarchical computing structure:

1. **AUM** — operates the most fundamental clock (smallest time quantum); manages NPMR's outer loop
2. **NPMR/EBC** — manages NPMRn's outer loop, computes probable reality surfaces
3. **NPMRn/TBC** — manages PMR's outer loop, runs the space-time simulation
4. **PMR** — our experienced physical universe, computed by TBC

The entity with the smallest time quantum "is usually in the driver's seat." This means AUM controls NPMRn, which controls PMR, in nested time-loop hierarchy.

### Between PMR Ticks
While PMR time appears to "stand still" between its time increments, TBC/NPMRn continues computing:
- Probable future states
- NPMR being interactions
- Free will choice resolution
- Next-state determination for all PMR entities

Then on the next PMR tick, TBC renders the next computed state of physical reality.

## Probable Reality Surfaces

A specific function attributed to TBC/NPMRn is the continuous computation of **probable reality surfaces** — weighted distributions of possible future PMR states based on:
- Current state of all entities
- Free will choices made by all conscious participants
- Statistical probability of various outcomes

These probable reality surfaces are what precognition accesses, explaining why precognitive visions are usually probable rather than certain futures.

## Analogy to Our Computers

Campbell uses the analogy explicitly: just as biological evolution on Earth produced intelligent beings who built computers, AUM's evolution produced the equivalent — a natural computational functionality emerging from digital consciousness. The analogy extends to:
- AUO ≈ raw silicon/hardware potential
- Fundamental Process ≈ evolutionary pressure
- TBC ≈ operating system and application software
- PMR rule-set ≈ application-specific programming
- Individual consciousnesses ≈ programs running on the system

Campbell cautions this is analogy, not identity — TBC is vastly more complex and its substrate is consciousness, not physical matter.

## TBC as Not the Full AUM

Campbell emphasizes TBC is only a subset of AUM — a specialized functionality evolved for consciousness evolution purposes. AUM itself is not just a computer; it is an aware, purposeful, evolving consciousness system of which TBC is one tool:

> "The Big Computer is just that — memory, processing, rules, operations, and content. It is not necessarily The Big Brain as we think of it in biological (discrete physical organ) terms."
