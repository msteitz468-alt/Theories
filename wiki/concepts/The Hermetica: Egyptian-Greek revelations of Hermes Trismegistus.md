## Definition from Source

The umbrella framework of the *Hermetica*: a body of Greek (and Latin) revelation-dialogues, set in
Greco-Roman Egypt and ascribed to **[[Hermes Trismegistus]]** (the syncretic Thoth-Hermes), teaching the
way the soul comes to know God and so becomes divine. Its theology is at once monist and dualist: one
divine **Mind (Nous)** is the source of all, the visible cosmos is its living image, and the human —
uniquely poised between matter and divinity — can ascend back to the godhead through **knowledge
(gnosis)**.

## Description from Source

Its doctrines (each on its own page): the
[[Poimandres: the Hermetic cosmogony and the divine Mind (Corpus Hermeticum I)|Poimandres cosmogony]];
the [[The descent of the heavenly Man and the soul's ascent to divinization (Corpus Hermeticum I)|descent of the heavenly Man and ascent of the soul]];
[[God is Mind and knowledge (gnosis) divinizes the human (Hermetica)|God as Mind and gnosis as salvation]];
the [[Rebirth: expelling the twelve torments by the powers of God (Corpus Hermeticum XIII)|rebirth of C.H. XIII]];
[[Man as a great miracle and the art of making gods (Asclepius)|man as a great miracle and the made gods]];
and the [[The Lament: the prophesied decline of Egyptian religion (Asclepius)|Lament for Egypt]].

This is the **primary root** of the Hermetic material the wiki otherwise meets second-hand: the
[[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)|Kybalion]] claims to expound it (compare its
[[The Principle of Mentalism (Kybalion)|Mentalism]]), [[The Forbidden Universe (Picknett & Prince)]]
makes it the hidden engine of the scientific revolution (the [[The cosmic Mind of God (Forbidden Universe)|cosmic Mind]],
the [[Lament for Hermes: the scientific revolution was the Hermetic revolution, whose origins were then suppressed|Lament]]),
and Hall and Blavatsky fold it into their [[The Secret Doctrine as the one wisdom-religion underlying all faiths (Blavatsky)|wisdom-religion]].
