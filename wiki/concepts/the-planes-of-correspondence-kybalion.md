## Definition from Source

The Planes of Correspondence is the Kybalion's structural map of the Universe, introduced in Chapter VIII as the framework through which [[The Principle of Correspondence (Kybalion)]] operates. The Universe is divided into Three Great Planes — Physical, Mental, and Spiritual — each subdivided into Seven Minor Planes, each of which is further divided into seven sub-planes. The Kybalion emphasizes that these divisions are "more or less artificial and arbitrary": "all of the three divisions are but ascending degrees of the great scale of Life, the lowest point of which is undifferentiated Matter, and the highest point that of Spirit."

### The Three Great Planes

**I. The Great Physical Plane**
Comprises all matter and energy. Matter is understood as "Energy at a low rate of vibrations." Its Seven Minor Planes are:
1. Plane of Matter (A) — solids, liquids, gases
2. Plane of Matter (B) — subtle/radiant matter (radium, etc.)
3. Plane of Matter (C) — most tenuous matter, not suspected by ordinary science
4. Plane of Ethereal Substance — "The Ether," connecting link between Matter and Energy
5. Plane of Energy (A) — ordinary forces: heat, light, magnetism, electricity, gravitation
6. Plane of Energy (B) — "Nature's Finer Forces," operative in mental phenomena
7. Plane of Energy (C) — energy so highly organized it bears characteristics of life; available only to Spiritual Plane beings

**II. The Great Mental Plane**
Comprises all living entities from minerals to humans. Its Seven Minor Planes are:
1. Plane of Mineral Mind
2. Plane of Elemental Mind (A) — between mineral and plant, invisible to ordinary senses
3. Plane of Plant Mind
4. Plane of Elemental Mind (B) — between plant and animal
5. Plane of Animal Mind
6. Plane of Elemental Mind (C) — semi-human in intelligence
7. Plane of Human Mind — the average person of the present era occupies only the fourth sub-division

The Three Elemental Planes are described as bearing "the same relation to the Planes of Mineral, Plant, Animal and Human Mentality and Life, that the black keys on the piano do to the white keys" — necessary connectors and transitional states.

**III. The Great Spiritual Plane**
Largely beyond description from within the lower planes. Contains Seven Minor Planes inhabited by Beings so far above humans "as the latter is above the earthworm." The lower minor planes hold Masters and Adepts; higher planes hold Angelic Hosts, Archangels, and beings the Kybalion calls "The Gods." Even these highest beings exist within and are subject to the Mind of THE ALL, and are "mortal" in the sense that they belong to the Universe rather than to Absolute Spirit.

### The Fourth Dimension of Vibration

The Kybalion defines a "Plane" not as a place but as a degree of vibration — what it calls "the Fourth Dimension." The higher the rate of vibration, the higher the Plane. All Seven Hermetic Principles operate on every Plane.
