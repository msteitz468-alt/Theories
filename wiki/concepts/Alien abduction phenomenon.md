## Definition / Characterization from Source

The reported experience of being taken, usually against one's will, by non-human ("alien") beings into a strange craft and subjected to intrusive — frequently medical or reproductive — procedures. Across the principal investigators the **phenomenology is strikingly consistent**: an onset of unexplained light, a hum or vibration, paralysis, being "floated" through walls or windows, examination aboard a craft by small grey humanoids with large black eyes, tissue/sperm/ova sampling, telepathic communication, and amnesia that can be lifted under hypnotic regression.

The sources diverge on **interpretation**. Budd Hopkins frames it as a covert, physically real "research program" with the human species as subject (see [["Missing time" and the abduction syndrome]]). Whitley Strieber declines to call the beings extraterrestrial, naming them "the visitors." John Mack reframes the whole as an **ontological and spiritual phenomenon** — a "daimonic" reality bridging the physical and unseen worlds, ultimately transformative — and connects it to shamanic and cross-cultural encounter traditions documented by Mircea Eliade and [[Jacques Vallée]] (see [[Magonia]]). Skeptics (Sagan, Shermer) treat it instead as misperception, sleep paralysis, suggestion, and false memory.
