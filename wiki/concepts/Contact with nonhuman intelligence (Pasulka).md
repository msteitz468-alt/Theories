## Definition from Source

The umbrella concept of *Encounters*: "contact with nonhuman intelligence" (NHI) — Pasulka's
deliberately broad term for the experiences her witnesses report, chosen over "extraterrestrial" to
avoid the media-shaped image of humanoid aliens in spacecraft. She argues NHI contact is reported
across "almost every traditional religion" and indigenous culture (e.g. Lakota "Star Nations") as an
*ongoing lived reality*, not (as SETI frames it) a future possibility.

## Description from Source

Pasulka's method, credited to [[Jacques Vallée]], is to "turn back to the witnesses" — first-hand,
quiet, long-term field research, attentive to secrecy and disinformation. She criticizes
"confirmation bias" (the dismissal of reports that don't match the [[Whitley Strieber]] /
[[Communion (Strieber)]] "Gray alien" image) and the fear that contact would destroy religion
(arguing religions already hold categories — God, djinn, angels — for nonhuman intelligences). Across
the book's experiencer profiles, contact recurs with the same motifs:
[[The download (spontaneous cognition, Pasulka)|the download]], the
[[The organic network - noösphere (Pasulka)|organic network/noösphere]], precognition, and vivid
dreams reframed as visions. Compare the wiki's other Vallée-lineage sources
([[The Invisible College (Vallée, 1975)]], [[Messengers of Deception (Vallee)]],
[[The UFO Experience (Hynek)]]).
