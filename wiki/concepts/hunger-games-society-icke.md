## Definition and Characterisation (from the Source)

As presented in David Icke's *Everything You Need to Know But Have Never Been Told* (2018), the "Hunger Games Society" is Icke's term for the planned end-state of the Archontic elite control agenda, named after the dystopian film series depicting a society in which a tiny elite employs a vicious police/military state to subjugate a poverty-stricken majority. Icke describes it as "clearly being installed by the day."

The structure consists of three tiers:
1. **The less-than-one-percent El-ite**: Ultra-rich human-reptilian hybrid bloodline families and their associates who own and direct everything — governments, corporations, banks, media, military
2. **The police/military enforcement layer**: A professionalised, heavily militarised security apparatus maintained by manufactured terrorism and justified as protecting the public from threats that the elite itself creates
3. **The enslaved majority**: Virtually the entire human population, kept in poverty, dependency, and total surveillance, with no private property, no meaningful freedom, and no access to independent economic activity

Key mechanisms being used to construct this society (as identified in the book):
- Financial engineering (wealth concentration, engineered crashes, bail-ins, civil asset forfeiture)
- Manufactured terrorism justifying a permanent security state
- Political correctness suppressing dissent and self-censorship
- Climate change narrative justifying Agenda 21/2030 centralisation
- Transhumanism as the final technological lock
