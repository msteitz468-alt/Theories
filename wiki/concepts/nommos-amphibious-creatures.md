## Definition / Characterization from Source

The **Nommos** (collectively referred to as **Nommo** or the Nommos) are described in the mythology of the Dogon tribe of Mali as amphibious, fish-like intelligent beings who descended from the heavens to teach humanity arts, sciences, writing, and social order.

According to Robert Temple's analysis of Dogon cosmology in *The Sirius Mystery*:
* **Origin:** The Nommos originate from a planet that orbits Sirius C (*Emme Ya*), a third star in the Sirius star system. Their home world is described as a moist, watery environment.
* **Appearance:** They are described as amphibious creatures, resembling mermen or mermen-like entities with fish tails, scales, and upper bodies of humans. They are characterized as "superhuman" in their longevity and knowledge, and are referred to as "the monitors" or "the masters of the water."
* **The Descent (The Ark):** The Nommos arrived on Earth in a massive celestial "ark" that landed in water. The landing was accompanied by a thunderous, vibrating noise (described as stone blocks being struck rhythmically), a rushing wind (symbolized by the word meaning "air"), and bright fire.
* **Equivalence to Oannes:** Temple links the Nommos directly to the Sumerian and Babylonian legend of **[[Oannes]]** (or Uanna), the fish-headed creature described by the Chaldean priest Berossus who emerged from the Persian Gulf to civilize mankind.
