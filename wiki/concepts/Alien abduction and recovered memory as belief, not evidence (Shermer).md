## Definition from Source

Shermer's argument (Chapters 6 and 7) that alien abduction experiences and recovered-memory
accusations are products of identifiable psychological and social mechanisms — altered states,
hypnotic confabulation, sleep-paralysis, cultural feedback loops — rather than evidence of actual
events. He treats the two phenomena as structurally parallel: both are generated largely in the
absence of the original experience, emerge through suggestive questioning or hypnosis, spread through
media feedback loops, and follow the eight-stage pattern he identifies as a **witch-craze feedback
loop**.

## Description from Source

### Alien Abduction (Ch. 6 — "Abducted!")

Shermer opens the chapter with a first-person account of his own "abduction" during the 1983 Race
Across America, when eighty-three hours without sleep produced vivid hallucinations in which he
became convinced his support crew were aliens intent on killing him. He recalls the hallucination
"as vividly and clearly as any strong memory" — making the epistemological point that subjective
certainty is no guide to objective fact.

Shermer's composite explanation for alien abduction:

- **Altered states of consciousness** — sleep deprivation, hypnagogic and hypnopompic states (just
  before sleep and just before waking), and hypnosis all produce experiences — paralysis, floating,
  strange presences, lost time — that map precisely onto the abduction narrative.
- **False memory via hypnosis** — Psychologist Elizabeth Loftus demonstrated that false memories
  can be planted in children by suggestion alone. Professor Alvin Lawson placed students under
  hypnosis, told them repeatedly they had been abducted, and they elaborated detailed narratives.
  "Almost all alien abductions are recalled only under hypnosis, many years after the alleged event."
- **Cultural template and media feedback loop** — The "grey alien" (large bald head, elongated eyes)
  was created by NBC artists for the 1975 TV film *The UFO Incident* and has been the standard
  abductee description ever since. Strieber's *Communion* (1987), John Mack's *Abduction* (1994),
  Budd Hopkins's *Missing Time*, films like *E.T.* and *Independence Day*, and *The X-Files* all
  fed the feedback loop. "The more people who have had unusual mental experiences see and read about
  others who interpreted similar incidents as abduction, the more likely they are to convert their
  own stories."
- **Sexual content reflects human psychology, not alien behavior** — The sexual component (egg
  harvesting, sperm extraction, hybrids) recurs across centuries of similar experiences: demon and
  Satan encounters in the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries, ghost-sex in the nineteenth-century
  spiritualism era. The content reveals human fantasy patterns, not alien agendas.
- **Hume's criterion applied** — By the standard of weighing miracles, it is vastly more likely
  that millions of humans experience unusual mental states in a media-saturated UFO culture than
  that aliens are beaming people aboard spaceships undetected.

Shermer notes that the abductees he dined with after an NBC taping were "neither crazy nor ignorant
— perfectly sane, rational, intelligent folks who had in common an irrational experience." He
distinguishes between the sincerity of the experiencer and the reliability of the experience as
evidence.

### Recovered Memory as Witch Craze (Ch. 7 — "Epidemics of Accusations")

Shermer maps the recovered-memory movement against the medieval witch-craze template, identifying
eight structural features shared by both (see also the claim page on this topic). Key dynamics:

- **Fabrication via therapeutic technique** — Suggestive questioning, hypnosis, hypnotic age-
  regression, visualization, sodium amytal, and dream interpretation generate "memories" that were
  absent at the outset of therapy. Bass and Davis's *The Courage to Heal* (1988, 750,000 copies)
  made the logical error explicit: "If you think you were abused and your life shows the symptoms,
  then you were."
- **Impossible numbers** — If one-third to one-half of women were sexually abused (Bass and Davis),
  then roughly 85–100 million Americans are victims or perpetrators — "impossible."
- **Positive feedback loop → legal reversal** — The movement peaked and reversed as "retractors"
  sued therapists successfully; courts began awarding millions in damages for planted false memories.
  The East Wenatchee, Washington case (1995) and the George Franklin murder conviction reversal
  (1995) are paradigm reversals.

## What this counters in the wiki

- [[Communion (Strieber)]] — Shermer treats Strieber's account as a culturally mediated altered
  state; the grey-alien template was created by NBC, not by independent witnesses.
- [[Whitley Strieber]] — Strieber is the paradigm case of hypnagogic hallucination meeting media
  feedback; Baker showed the encounter "is a classic, textbook description of a hypnopompic
  hallucination."
- [[Missing Time (Hopkins)]] — Hopkins's hypnotic regression methodology is itself identified as
  a false-memory generator; Lawson's experiment (hypnosis + suggestion = detailed abduction
  narrative) directly undermines the evidentiary basis of the regression corpus.
- [[Encounters (Pasulka)]] — The cultural-template and media-feedback argument applies to
  contemporary believer communities that Pasulka documents.

  concept page is the Sagan companion to Shermer's parallel argument; the two sources reinforce
  each other on mechanism (hallucination, hypnosis, cultural template).
