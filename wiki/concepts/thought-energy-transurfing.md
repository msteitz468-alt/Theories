## Definition from Source

In the Transurfing model, thought energy is the force by which human consciousness interacts with the [[Alternatives Space]]. Zeland asserts that thought energy transforms possible alternatives into physical reality by "lighting up" a corresponding sector. The energy has a frequency or quality that determines which sector it resonates with.

"Thought energy transforms possible alternatives into physical reality by lighting up a sector." The frequency of thought energy must match the parameters of the target sector for a physical shift to occur.

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Selective**: attunes to sectors that match its quality/frequency; therefore negative thinking attunes to negative life lines ("you get what you do not want")
- **Realized in both positive and negative directions**: "Heart and mind united in negative expectation" is easily realized via outer intention (Ch. VII), just as positive unity realizes positive outcomes
- **Three forms aimed at goals** (Ch. VII):
  1. **Desire** — concentration on the goal itself; described as having no power to realize
  2. **Inner intention** — concentration on the process of moving toward the goal; works but demands effort
  3. **Outer intention** — concentration on how the goal realizes itself; most powerful
- **Energy given to pendulums**: when a person resonates with a pendulum's frequency, their thought energy feeds that pendulum
- **Boomerang principle**: "thoughts always return; negative thinking attunes to negative life lines" (Ch. III)

## Relationship to Importance

The level of importance attributed to an outcome amplifies the thought energy around it, creating [[Excess Potential]] and triggering [[Law of Balance]] counter-forces. Reducing importance allows thought energy to flow freely without generating resistance.
