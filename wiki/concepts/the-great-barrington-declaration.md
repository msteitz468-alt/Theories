## Definition from Source

**The Great Barrington Declaration** is a real October 4, 2020 statement, quoted in full in chapter 3 of *[[Faucian Bargain (The Most Powerful and Dangerous Bureaucrat in American History)]]*, in which the authors say "forty-four global experts, representing some of the leading universities in the world" called for an alternative to lockdowns they named **Focused Protection**.

## Key Points (as quoted in the source)

- It expresses "grave concerns about the damaging physical and mental health impacts of the prevailing COVID-19 policies" (lower childhood vaccination rates, worse cardiovascular outcomes, fewer cancer screenings, deteriorating mental health), warning these will cause "greater excess mortality in years to come," with the working class and young hardest hit; "Keeping students out of school is a grave injustice."
- It asserts "vulnerability to death from COVID-19 is more than a thousand-fold higher in the old and infirm than the young," and that "for children, COVID-19 is less dangerous than many other harms, including influenza."
- **Focused Protection**: allow those at minimal risk "to live their lives normally to build up immunity... through natural infection, while better protecting those who are at highest risk" (e.g., immunity-acquired nursing-home staff, frequent testing, delivered groceries for the elderly), with schools, businesses, and cultural life reopened.

The book uses the Declaration as evidence for [[Credentialed experts dissented from Fauci's lockdown approach]], while explicitly cautioning that "just because the narrative of those who signed... is calling for a more preferable way of life than lockdowns doesn't mean they're right."
