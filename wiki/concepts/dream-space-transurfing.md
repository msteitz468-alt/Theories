## Definition from Source

In the Transurfing model, the dream space is identical to the [[Alternatives Space]]. Dreaming is described as the soul's travel through the alternatives space, where all types of script exist. The subconscious directly connects to the alternatives space during sleep, and the soul travels through its sectors.

"The subconscious directly connects to the alternatives space and the information it contains." (Ch. VII)
"Dreaming is the soul's travel through the alternatives space, where all types of script exist." (Ch. VII)
"The brain does not store information itself. It stores the address to where the information is located in the alternatives space." (Ch. VII)

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Not illusion**: "The mind genuinely sees them" — dreams are not dismissed as mere fantasy; they are real travel through unrealized sectors
- **Adjacent sectors**: dreams typically show adjacent sectors to the current life line, containing similar scripts and scenery; they can show what could have happened or could happen
- **Intention selects script**: "intention selects the script" in the dream space — just as in waking life
- **Slower sector shifts in waking**: "in waking, material density of matter slows sector shifts" — physical reality has inertia (described as "inert, like resin") while dreams allow faster movement
- **Risk of non-return**: the source warns of a "risk of soul entering physically manifest sector and not returning" (Ch. VII Summary) — if the soul enters a fully realized sector in the dream space, it may not return
- **Lucid dreaming as outer intention training**: lucid dreaming in this context is presented as a model for accessing [[Outer Intention]] — the dream's game script is controlled by the dreamer's outer intention

## Dreams and Signs (from source)

The source explicitly states that dreams should not be interpreted as signs: "Dreams should not be interpreted as signs." (Ch. VII Summary) Dreams are not messages but experiences of adjacent alternatives.

## Hallucinations and Psychosis (from source)

Hallucinations (under drugs/alcohol) and the perceptions of psychiatric patients are explained in Ch. VIII as the subconscious accessing unrealized areas of the alternatives space — not as illness in the conventional sense but as attuning to virtual sectors.
