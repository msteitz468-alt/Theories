## Definition from Source

Hofstadter's analysis of the paranoid style's distinctive *epistemic* habit: an "elaborate concern
with demonstration," an almost obsessive accumulation of "evidence," footnotes, and bibliography —
and then, "at some critical point," a "curious leap in imagination … from the undeniable to the
unbelievable."

## Description from Source

The paranoid mentality "is far more coherent than the real world, since it leaves no room for
mistakes, failures, or ambiguities." Hofstadter's archetype is John Robison's anti-Illuminati tract:
page after page of patient detail, then suddenly "the French Revolution has taken place, and the
Illuminati have brought it about" — what is missing is "sensible judgment about what can cause a
revolution." He notes the scholarly *apparatus* (McCarthy's 313 footnotes; Welch's hundred pages of
notes) functions defensively: the paranoid "is not a receiver, he is a transmitter," amassing
evidence not to test his thesis but to fortify it. This directly characterizes the "meticulously
sourced" style of the wiki's conspiracy authors.
