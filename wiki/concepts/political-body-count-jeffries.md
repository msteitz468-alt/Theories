## Definition from Source

A recurring motif across *Hidden History*: the "body count" — long lists of witnesses, associates,
investigators, and other figures connected to a given scandal who, Jeffries argues, died suddenly,
violently, or under suspicious circumstances before they could testify or expose wrongdoing. The book
catalogs successive "body counts" by administration (e.g. the "Clinton Body Count," "Bush Body
Count," "Obama Body Count").

## Description from Source

Jeffries presents the pattern as circumstantial evidence for
[[Suppressed conspiracies and cover-ups in modern American politics (Hidden History thesis)]]: the
statistical implausibility (as he frames it) of so many inconvenient deaths clustering around
particular figures and events. The wiki records these as the author's *alleged* associations, not as
established causal claims — the deaths and their attribution to foul play are contested, and the names
involved are real individuals.
