## Definition / Characterization from Source

Paul Wallis's umbrella term in *The Scars of Eden* for the non-human visitors he believes are remembered across the world's ancestral traditions — the same beings he calls the "Powerful Ones" (plural *elohim*) in [[Escaping from Eden (Wallis)]], and which he equates with the Sumerian Anunnaki, the biblical *benei elohim*, and the "children of God" of Plato. On his account the Sky People came, "colonised our planet and interfered in our evolution to make us a more useful workforce," and engaged in an ongoing "pattern of abduction and hybridization through the ages."

Wallis stresses that the Sky People are described as **appearing human** — and often extraordinarily beautiful (he reads Genesis 18 and the Sodom "angels" as physical glimpses; he relates the modern "Nordic"/"Tall White" category to his own 1985 sighting). He treats world mythology as "indigenous memory" — "flashbacks of a patient with amnesia" — encoding real contact in pre-technological language.
