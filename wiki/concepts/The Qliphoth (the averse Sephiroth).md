# The Qliphoth (the averse Sephiroth)

## Definition from Source
The Qliphoth (Chapter XXVI) are "the evil and averse Sephiroth." Fortune states they are not independent principles or factors in the cosmic scheme, but "the unbalanced and destructive aspect of the Holy Stations themselves" — "there are, in fact, not two Trees, but one Tree, a Qliphah being the reverse of a coin of which the obverse is a Sephirah." To each archangel corresponds a devil, to each choir of angels a "cohort of demons," and each Sephirothic Sphere has its correspondence in the "Infernal Habitations."

Each Qliphah arose "primarily as an emanation of unbalanced force in the course of the evolution of the corresponding Sephirah" — an inevitable, temporary period of disequilibrium (e.g., the uncompensated severity of Geburah, prior to the emanation of Tiphareth the Redeemer, formed the Qliphah of Geburah — "the jealous God of the Old Testament and all the religious persecutions"). Fortune distinguishes "positive evil," which moves against the current of evolution, from "negative evil," which is merely uncompensated inertia or momentum not yet neutralized. The solution to evil is not suppression or destruction but "compensation and consequent absorption back into the Sphere whence it came." Below Malkuth lies a Qliphothic reservoir of "disorganised force emanated from broken-up forms," described as Purgatory rather than Hell, from which "Shells" build vehicles and from which the lower types of evil magic draw.
