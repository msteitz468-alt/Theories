## Definition from Source

The *Trimorphic Protennoia* (Codex XIII) is a first-person revelation discourse in which a divine feminine being — the **First Thought (Protennoia)**, also identified as **Barbelo** — speaks in her own voice and describes three successive descents into the world of mortals. In each descent she takes a different form corresponding to a member of the divine triad: **Voice / Father**, **Sound / Mother**, **Word / Son**. The text teaches that this threefold descent is not a defeat but a strategy of concealment: by hiding within matter and within the archons' own creation, Protennoia nullifies their bonds and recovers the scattered divine sparks ("those who are mine").

## Description from Source

The text opens with a self-proclamation that recurs three times in modified form, one for each of the three "discourses" or "parts":

> "I am Protennoia, the Thought that dwells in the Light. I am the movement that dwells in the All, she in whom the All takes its stand, the first-born among those who came to be, she who exists before the All. She (Protennoia) is called by three names, although she dwells alone, since she is perfect."

She is simultaneously invisible ("I am invisible within the Thought of the Invisible One") and immanent ("I move in every creature"). She "dwells within every Power and every eternal movement, and (in) invisible Lights and within the Archons and Angels and Demons and every soul."

**The three descents** are unfolded across the three discourses:

1. **First Descent — as Voice / Father**: Protennoia descends to the underworld and shines in the darkness, pours forth water, and plants a seed of gnosis in all creatures. "I descended to the midst of the underworld, and I shone down upon the darkness."

2. **Second Descent — as Sound / Mother**: She comes "in the likeness of a female" and speaks of the coming end of the current aeon and the beginning of a new, changeless aeon. She declares herself androgynous — "I am Mother (and) I am Father, since I copulate with myself" — and the Womb that shapes the All by giving birth to Light.

3. **Third Descent — as Word / Son**: This is the climactic and most elaborate descent. "The Third time I revealed myself to them in their tents as Word, and I revealed myself in the likeness of their shape." She hides among the archons, enters their world undetected — "I hid myself within them, and they did not know the one who empowers me" — and bestows the "Five Seals" (a Sethian baptismal initiation) upon those who belong to her. The text concludes with a remarkable statement of disguise and cosmic rescue: "I put on Jesus. I bore him from the cursed wood, and established him in the dwelling places of his Father."

**The Voice as threefold structure** is made explicit:

> "Now the Voice that originated from my Thought exists as three permanences: the Father, the Mother, the Son. Existing perceptibly as Speech, it (Voice) has within it a Word endowed with every glory, and it has three masculinities, three powers, and three names."

Protennoia's self-identification as **Barbelo** ties this text directly to the broader Sethian cosmology: she is the first emanation of the Invisible Spirit, the "Image of the Invisible Spirit," the Mother who precedes and undergirds the entire Pleroma (see [[Gnosticism: the unknowable Father, Sophia's fall, and the archon-creator (Nag Hammadi)]]).

**Relationship to the Apocryphon of John**: Scholars note that several passages of the *Trimorphic Protennoia* are strikingly close to passages in the *Apocryphon of John* (covered under Agent A's pages), particularly the Pronoia hymn that closes some versions of that text. Both texts present the First Thought descending three times to rescue the divine sparks. The Protennoia version is more lyrical, first-person, and goddess-voiced; the Apocryphon version is more narrative and cosmogonic. The closeness suggests either a shared source or literary dependence.

**Yaltabaoth appears** in the third discourse, named "Saklas, Samael, Yaltabaoth," identified as the ruler who "had taken power; who had snatched it away from the innocent one (Sophia)." The archons mistake Protennoia for their own Christ — "The Archons thought that I was their Christ" — a piece of Gnostic irony in which the true revealer is hidden in plain sight.

**Note on lacunae**: The text is relatively well-preserved compared to Marsanes and Allogenes, but several 5-line gaps occur in the third discourse ("On Fate: Two") and the colophon is abrupt.
