## Definition from Source

The Principle of Gender is the Seventh (and final) of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]] as presented in [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]]. The Three Initiates state its maxim as:

> "Gender is in everything; everything has its Masculine and Feminine Principles; Gender manifests on all planes." — THE KYBALION.

The Kybalion explicitly distinguishes Hermetic "Gender" from biological Sex. The word "Gender" is traced to the Latin root meaning "to beget; to procreate; to generate; to create; to produce" — indicating a principle of creative duality that is universal, not limited to organic life. Sex, in the text's framing, is merely one expression of Gender on the physical-organic plane.

The Three Initiates teach that the Masculine Principle acts as the directing, projecting force — it sends energy toward the Feminine Principle and initiates creative action. The Feminine Principle is described as the active creative worker: it receives the impulse, generates, and produces. Neither principle can operate without the other. Every organism, every atom, every phenomenon on every plane is said to manifest both principles in some proportion.

The Kybalion identifies the Masculine Principle with the Positive Pole of electricity and the Feminine with the Cathode (formerly called "Negative") Pole, citing contemporary science on the behavior of electrons and corpuscles as evidence. Gravitation, chemical affinity, atomic attraction, and the creation of matter are all presented as manifestations of Gender at various scales.

The Principle finds its most elaborate treatment on the Mental Plane in the companion concept [[Mental Gender (Kybalion)]].
