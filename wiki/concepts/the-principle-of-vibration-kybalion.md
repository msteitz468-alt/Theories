## Definition from Source

The Principle of Vibration is the Third of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]]. Its governing maxim is:

> "Nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates define it as embodying "the truth that Motion is manifest in everything in the Universe — that nothing is at rest — that everything moves, vibrates, and circles." Crucially, not only does everything vibrate, but the differences between all manifestations of universal power — Matter, Energy, Mind, Spirit — are due entirely to varying rates and modes of vibration.

### The Spectrum from Spirit to Matter

The Kybalion describes a single pole-to-pole continuum of vibration:

- **Spirit** occupies one pole: vibrating at such an infinite degree of intensity that it appears to be at rest (as a rapidly spinning wheel appears motionless).
- **Gross Matter** occupies the other pole: the lowest, slowest rates of vibration.
- Between these extremes lie "millions upon millions of different rates and modes of vibration," constituting all phenomena of Matter, Energy, and Mind.

The Kybalion aligns this with what it calls the "Fourth Dimension" — the dimension of vibration — which defines the [[The Planes of Correspondence (Kybalion)|Planes]]: higher rate of vibration = higher Plane.

### The Revolving-Wheel Illustration

Chapter IX employs a sustained illustration: a wheel or cylinder spinning at increasing speeds passes through successive phenomena — first silence, then sound rising through the musical scale, then heat, then visible light (red through violet), then invisible rays, then X-rays, then molecular disintegration, then dissolution into atoms and corpuscles, then Ethereal Substance. The Hermetists extend this where science stops: further increases in vibration produce mental states, then spiritual states, until the object "re-enters THE ALL, which is Absolute Spirit."

### Mental Vibration and Practical Application

The Kybalion extends the Principle beyond the physical: every thought, emotion, and mental state has "its corresponding rate and mode of vibration." Portions of these vibrations are "thrown off" and influence other minds — the basis the text ascribes to telepathy and mental influence. A practitioner who understands the Principle can "polarize his mind at any degree he wishes," controlling mental states and affecting others. This application is identified with [[Mental Transmutation (Kybalion)]] — "the science being that of Mental Transmutation, one of the branches of the Hermetic Art."

The chapter closes with the maxim: "He who understands the Principle of Vibration, has grasped the sceptre of Power."
