## Definition from Source

THE ALL is the Kybalion's name for the ultimate Substantial Reality that underlies all outward
appearances. The Kybalion quotes:

> "THAT which is the Fundamental Truth—the Substantial Reality—is beyond true naming, but the Wise
> Men call it THE ALL."
> — THE KYBALION

> "In its Essence, THE ALL is UNKNOWABLE."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates teach that human reason compels the following minimal conclusions about THE ALL:

1. **THE ALL must be ALL that REALLY IS** — nothing can exist outside it.
2. **THE ALL must be INFINITE** — Eternal in Time (always existing), infinite in Space (everywhere),
   and infinite in Power (absolute, unconditioned).
3. **THE ALL must be IMMUTABLE** — not subject to change; nothing can be added to or subtracted from it.

The text explicitly rules out identifying THE ALL with mere Matter (which "cannot manifest Life or
Mind") or with blind Energy or Force (from which "Life and Mind can never evolve"). The affirmative
answer the Kybalion gives is:

> "'THE ALL' is Infinite Living Mind—the Illumined call it SPIRIT!"
> — THE KYBALION

THE ALL is thus above Gender, above Time, above Space — "the Law, from which the Laws proceed" —
yet when it manifests on the plane of generation or creation it acts according to the Principle of
Gender in its Masculine and Feminine aspects.

Thinkers who claim personal identity with THE ALL ("I AM GOD") are treated as overreaching: "The
claim of the corpuscle that 'I am Man!' would be modest in comparison."
