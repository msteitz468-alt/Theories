## Definition / Characterization from Source

Budd Hopkins's framework for detecting UFO abductions. **"Missing time"** is an unexplained gap — typically one or two hours — between the conscious memories immediately before and after a UFO encounter, the abduction itself having been "seamlessly joined" out of awareness by what Hopkins presumes is a "post-hypnotic suggestion" from the abductors. The witness retains only the sighting (if anything), plus residual unease and sometimes unexplained physical marks.

A "red flag" for the investigator is a witness "confused about an unexplained loss of time" or reporting odd physical symptoms. The standard recovery method is **regressive hypnosis**, conducted by cooperating psychiatrists/psychologists, which "unlocks the forgotten period" and yields the abduction account. Recurring elements form the **abduction syndrome**: a craft approaching a car at night, engine/electrical failure, examination aboard the craft by humanoid beings, sample-taking, and incisions leaving scars (often dating to childhood).
