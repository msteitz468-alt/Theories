# Persian and Zoroastrian demons (Bane)

## Definition from Source

Bane characterizes Zoroastrian and ancient Persian demonology as fundamentally dualist in structure: the universe is a moral battleground between Ahura Mazda (the "Wise Lord," principle of light, truth, and good) and Ahriman / Angra Mainyu (the destructive spirit, principle of evil and darkness). The demonic pantheon is correspondingly systematic — every good Amesha Spenta (holy archangel) has a demonic counterpart, and every cosmic virtue has a named demon working against it. Bane stresses that in Zoroastrianism, all beings including demons have free will to choose between good and evil; Ahriman's evil is therefore a chosen posture, not an inherent necessity.

The primary species of demon in this tradition is the **Daevas** ("false gods" or "wrong gods"): originally ambiguous gods who could not distinguish truth from falsehood and who were rejected by Zoroaster. Over time they came to be seen as fully evil, causing disease, chaos, plague, and opposing all forms of religion. Female demonic followers of Ahriman are known as **Drug** (or Druj). The **Divs** are a related Persian species — immortal fire-demons under Ahriman's command who prey upon humans and take souls to the underworld. Bane also notes that several Zoroastrian demons (especially Asmodai/Aeshma Daeva and Jahi) were absorbed directly into Jewish and Christian demonology.

## Notable Demons

**Ahriman (Angra Mainyu)** — The supreme adversary of God and man in Zoroastrianism; creator and leader of the Daevas and all 9,999 diseases he uses to plague the earth. Described as looking like a soot-covered toad or, when outside, as a fiery snake or flying dragon. At the end of twelve millenniums, the figure Saoshyant will bring an era of peace and destroy Ahriman. In Chaldean and Persian tradition the derived name Ahrimanes refers to a demonic god who has dominion over the sun.

**Aesma Daeva (Aeshma)** — A Daeva from Persian demonology, described as one of the seven archangels of the Persians corrupted into an archfiend of wrath and lust; his personal adversary is the spirit Sraosha. He works in conjunction with Asto Vidatu, the demon of death. Bane notes that Aeshma Daeva is considered one of the direct ancestors of the Judeo-Christian Asmodeus.

**Aka Manah** — A Daeva, the personification of sensual desire; second in command to the host of Ahriman's demons, outranked only by Angra Mainyu himself. His name translates as "evil mind" or "evil thought"; his personal adversary is Vohu Manah ("good mind"), the second Amesha Spenta.

**Asto Vidatu** — A Persian and Zoroastrian demon of death whose name translates as "dissolver of bones"; he works in conjunction with Aesma Daeva. Using a noose, Asto Vidatu chases and attempts to snare the souls of the recently deceased as they ascend to Heaven.

**Az** — A Zoroastrian Daeva and Drug, the mother of all demons and sin in Persian and Zoroastrian demonology; she appears as a woman who is constantly eating but can never feel full. A powerful and destructive demon, she tries to make people forget their divine origin so that they abandon salvation.

**Bushyasta** — A Daeva from Zoroastrian mythology, the DEV of sloth; "the long-handed." Most powerful in the mornings, she can be driven off through prayer. In service under Ahriman.

**Daevas** — The primary demonic species of Zoroastrian mythology; originally gods incapable of distinguishing truth from lies, they were rejected but continued to be worshipped. Both nocturnal and diurnal, they appear as black-skinned beings and cause disease, plague, and chaos, fighting against every form of religion. To protect oneself, one must recite the Vendidad aloud, though only between sunset and sunrise.

**Div Sepid** — In ancient Iranian mythology, the "white demon," a Div that lived in a cave in the region of Mazandaran; notable in Persian epic poetry (the Shahnameh of Firdausi).

**Jahi** — The queen of Hell in Zoroastrian demonology, daughter and wife of Ahriman; the demon of lasciviousness and prostitutes. She preys primarily on women, controls menstrual cycles, and her goal is to defile all women. She is a seductress described as looking like a beautiful woman with wet curly hair and a body covered in filth, giving off a horrible stench. Her gaze is powerful enough to kill. Her name in Avestan translates as "adultery," "libertine," "menstruation," or "one who leads a licentious life."

**Nasu** — A Zoroastrian demon of corruption and corpse-pollution; associated with the defilement spread by contact with the dead.

**Saurva** — A type of Daeva in Zoroastrian demonology; the chief of the Devs. A demon of hunger who commits acts that are evil, lawless, oppressive, tyrannical, and violent. He must be rejected outright in deed, thought, and word to avoid victimization. His name in Avestan may mean "archer"; his personal adversary is the angel Khshathra Vairya.

**Zarich** — A female Daeva, the personification of aging in Zoroastrian demonology; although female, very masculine in appearance and action. One of the seven archdemons of Ahriman. Her personal adversary is Ameretat (immortality).
