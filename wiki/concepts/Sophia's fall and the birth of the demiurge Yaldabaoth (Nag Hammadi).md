## Definition from Source

The Apocryphon of John teaches that the cosmic catastrophe which produced the material world originated in an act by Sophia — one of the aeons of the Pleroma — who attempted to bring forth a likeness out of herself without the consent of the invisible Spirit and without her consort. The result was Yaldabaoth, a malformed, ignorant, arrogant demiurge who mistakenly believes himself to be the only god and who fashions the lower material cosmos using divine power stolen from his mother.

## Description from Source

The Apocryphon of John narrates: "And the Sophia of the Epinoia, being an aeon, conceived a thought from herself and the conception of the invisible Spirit and foreknowledge. She wanted to bring forth a likeness out of herself without the consent of the Spirit — he had not approved — and without her consort, and without his consideration… something came out of her which was imperfect and different from her appearance, because she had created it without her consort. And it was dissimilar to the likeness of its mother, for it has another form."

The offspring takes a monstrous shape: "When she saw (the consequences of) her desire, it changed into a form of a lion-faced serpent. And its eyes were like lightning fires which flash. She cast it away from her, outside that place, that no one of the immortal ones might see it, for she had created it in ignorance. And she surrounded it with a luminous cloud… And she called his name Yaltabaoth."

Yaldabaoth (also called Saklas and Samael) immediately appropriates his mother's light and becomes a self-sufficient creator: "This is the first archon who took a great power from his mother. And he removed himself from her and moved away from the places in which he was born." He then creates twelve archons — rulers of his own sub-realm — sharing his fire with them but withholding the light-power taken from Sophia. He fashions a hierarchy of 365 angels, each assigned to govern a part of the human body.

His defining theological error is his proclamation of sole divinity: "The archon who is weak has three names. The first name is Yaltabaoth, the second is Saklas, and the third is Samael. And he is impious in his arrogance which is in him. For he said, 'I am God and there is no other God beside me,' for he is ignorant of his strength, the place from which he had come." The text notes the self-defeating logic: "By announcing this he indicated to the angels who attended him that there exists another God. For if there were no other one, of whom would he be jealous?"

Sophia's repentance is described: "The mother began to move to and fro. She became aware of the deficiency when the brightness of her light diminished… she repented. And she was overcome by forgetfulness in the darkness of ignorance." Eventually the whole Pleroma intercedes and she is elevated above her son to the ninth realm until her deficiency is corrected.

On the Origin of the World (in the same collection) amplifies this: Pistis Sophia "wanted to create something, alone without her consort; and her product was a celestial thing… And what she had created became a product in the matter, like an aborted fetus. And it assumed a plastic form molded out of shadow, and became an arrogant beast resembling a lion."
