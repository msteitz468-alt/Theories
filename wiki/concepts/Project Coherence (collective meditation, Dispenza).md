## Definition from Source

Project Coherence is Joe Dispenza's initiative — developed in collaboration with the HeartMath Institute — to scale the coherence effects observed in his advanced workshops to global group meditation events. It is described in Chapter 13 of *Becoming Supernatural*.

### Origins in workshop research

Dispenza reports that at his advanced workshops, a random sample of 50–75 participants wore HRV (heart rate variability) monitors while the full group of 550–1,500 students collectively directed elevated emotions (love, gratitude, wholeness, joy) toward them. He claims that the monitored individuals went into heart coherence simultaneously — "at the exact same time, in the exact same meditation, on the exact same day" — across multiple events, interpreted as evidence of nonlocal, field-based heart-to-heart communication. The "Sputnik" sensor (a Russian-made environmental sensor measuring barometric, electromagnetic, and other environmental variables) reportedly showed measurable increases in collective room energy across each day of a workshop.

### The peace-gathering precedent Dispenza invokes

Dispenza draws heavily on a body of studies he attributes to Transcendental Meditation and related traditions:
- **Lebanon 1983 study**: meditators in Jerusalem claimed to produce a 76% reduction in war deaths in Lebanon on high-participation days, replicated across seven experiments over two years.
- **RAND Corporation study (1983–1985)**: ~8,000 trained meditators focused on world peace reportedly correlated with a 72% reduction in worldwide terrorism across three periods.
- **India 1987–1990**: 7,000 meditators focused on world peace, coinciding with the Cold War ending, the Berlin Wall falling, the Iran-Iraq war ending, and apartheid beginning to collapse.
- **Washington D.C. 1993**: ~2,500 meditators over June–July correlated with a statistically significant reduction in violent crime (FBI Uniform Crime Reports).
- **Global Consciousness Project / 9-11**: Princeton's random event generators showed anomalous patterns during 9/11, interpreted as evidence that collective emotional response affects the Earth's electromagnetic field.

Dispenza claims these studies are backed by "more than 50 demonstration projects and 23 peer-reviewed scientific studies" and report an average greater-than-70% reduction in crime, warfare, and terrorism. He uses the term **super radiance effect** for the threshold at which a group's size and coherence produces measurable societal effects.

### The HeartMath framework

Dispenza invokes the HeartMath Global Coherence Initiative (launched 2008) and its Global Coherence Monitoring System, which uses sensitive magnetometers to measure changes in the Earth's magnetic field correlated with human physiological states. The premise is that the heart's electromagnetic field extends beyond the body, can be sensed by others' nervous systems, and — at sufficient scale — can encode information into the Earth's field, which then distributes it globally as a "carrier wave."

### Project Coherence events

Dispenza reports launching Project Coherence global online meditations starting November 2015 via Facebook, with participation growing from ~6,000 (first event) to ~36,000 (second) to ~43,000 (third). The meditation directs participants to hold the Earth in their heart and radiate elevated emotion — love and coherence — into the planet's field. Dispenza states an intention to eventually measure global effects on the Earth's electromagnetic field and on real-world events.

### Core theoretical claim

The underlying claim is that collective human consciousness, when unified around elevated heart-centered emotions, creates constructive interference in a shared electromagnetic field. This field nonlocally influences the autonomic nervous systems of others, producing coherence where there was incoherence and potentially reducing violence, crime, and conflict at scale. Dispenza frames this as an application of the "emergence" phenomenon (schools of fish, flocks of birds acting as one mind) to human civilization.
