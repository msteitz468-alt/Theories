## Concept Definition

In Rizwan Virk's *The Simulation Hypothesis*, **The Simulation Point** is the theoretical technological threshold at which an advanced civilization develops the computational power and rendering capabilities necessary to run hyperrealistic simulations that are completely indistinguishable from base physical reality. At this point, the simulation's inhabitants (whether controlled by biological players, downloaded consciousnesses, or autonomous artificial intelligences) cannot determine that they are inside a computer-generated environment.

Virk proposes that humanity's progress in computer science, video game design, and brain-computer interfaces is tracking along an evolutionary feedback loop that will inevitably lead to this point.

## The 12 Stages on the Road to the Simulation Point

Virk categorizes the evolution of video games and computer technology into twelve distinct stages, mapping how simple text-based games eventually evolve into the Simulation Point:

| Stage | Name | Description | Key Historical / Technological Markers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage 0** | **Text Adventures and the "Game World"** | Games represent a self-contained world entirely through text. The player inputs text commands, and the computer manages the state and rules. | *Colossal Cave Adventure*, *Zork* (1970s–1980s). |
| **Stage 1** | **Early Graphical Arcade & Console Games** | The transition to graphical pixels instead of text, introducing joystick inputs and real-time movement on a 2D plane. | *Spacewar!* (1962), *Pong* (1972), *Space Invaders*, *Pac-Man*. |
| **Stage 2** | **Graphical Adventure Role-Playing Games (RPGs)** | Introduction of multi-room exploration, persistent inventories, character stats (experience points/levels), and quest-driven narrative structures. | *Ultima* series, *The Legend of Zelda* (1980s–1990s). |
| **Stage 3** | **3D Rendered MMORPGs and Virtual Worlds** | Fully 3D virtual spaces inhabited by millions of players online via customized avatars. Emphasis shifts toward social interaction and open-ended life simulation. | *Ultima Online*, *World of Warcraft*, *Second Life*, *The Sims* (1990s–Present). |
| **Stage 4** | **Immersion Using Virtual Reality (VR)** | The player transitions from looking at a 2D screen to being physically immersed in the 3D space using stereoscopic headsets and haptic controllers. | Oculus Rift/Quest, Apple Vision Pro. |
| **Stage 5** | **Photorealistic Augmented and Mixed Reality (AR/MR)** | Overlaying simulated digital graphics directly onto the physical environment, making digital icons and physical objects look indistinguishable. | *Pokémon GO*, Microsoft HoloLens, Magic Leap. |
| **Stage 6** | **Real-World Rendering: Light-Field Display & 3D Printing** | Generating holographic projections of light in physical space without screens or glasses, and converting digital assets directly into physical matter. | Light-field micro-displays, rapid 3D printing. |
| **Stage 7** | **Mind Interfaces** | Bypassing physical controllers, haptic gloves, and screens entirely by reading and writing signals directly to the brain's neural network. | Brain-computer interfaces (BCIs), Neuralink (e.g., monkey playing "mind pong"). |
| **Stage 8** | **Implanted Memories** | The capacity to write complex sensory and memory patterns directly into the brain, making a player believe they have lived a lifetime or completed a quest instantly. | Speculative / Science Fiction (e.g., *Total Recall*, *The Matrix*). |
| **Stage 9** | **Artificial Intelligence and NPCs** | Developing non-player characters (NPCs) that show human-like awareness, agency, and responses, passing the "Metaverse Turing Test." | Large Language Models, autonomous gaming agents, AGI. |
| **Stage 10** | **Downloadable Consciousness** | The ability to digitize a biological human's neural patterns and download them into a virtual avatar or simulation environment, achieving digital immortality. | Mind-uploading research, neural scanning. |
| **Stage 11** | **Reaching the Simulation Point** | The final stage where the simulation renders photorealistic, multi-sensory realities Beam-delivered directly to the mind's eye, indistinguishable from base reality. | **The Great Simulation** |
