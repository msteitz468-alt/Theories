## Definition from Source

Jacobsen defines black operations as government projects secret from Congress and from the American people. The atomic bomb (Manhattan Project) is described as the "mother of all black projects" and the parent of subsequent black operations.

Rules for black operations were written by Manhattan Project leaders. The project employed two hundred thousand people across eighty offices and dozens of plants without Congress knowing it existed until Hiroshima.

After the war, the Atomic Energy Act of 1946 created a parallel secrecy system beyond presidential executive orders.
