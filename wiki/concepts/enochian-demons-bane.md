# Enochian demons (Bane)

## Definition from Source

According to Bane's *Encyclopedia of Demons in World Religions and Cultures*, "Enochian lore" refers to the interlocking body of angelic and demonic material that flows through three distinct but related streams: (1) the apocryphal *Book of Enoch* (1 Enoch), which names twenty archangels and describes the fall of the Watcher angels; (2) the sixteenth-century Enochian magical system channeled by John Dee and Edward Kelley, which produced a structured language, hierarchies of spirits, and named Cacodaemons; and (3) Aleister Crowley's twentieth-century elaborations within his Thelemic system.

Bane identifies several categories of Enochian demonic being:

**The Watchers (Grigori)** — One of the seven orders of angels created by God to oversee the first generation of humans and teach them how to survive. Described as gigantic beings, each commanding ten angels under their authority (the Chief of Tens). Under the instigation of their leader Samiaza, the Watchers formed a pact, took human women as wives against God's will, fathered the Nephilim (semidivine offspring who were "generally violent, cannibalistic, and evil by nature"), and taught humanity dangerous arts: astrology, astronomy, botany, divination, cosmetics, mirrors, and weapon-making. Bane notes that some Nephilim became great artisans, poets, and priests. God punished all Watchers by casting them out of Heaven.

**The Enochian Cacodaemons** — Fifty-seven named Cacodaemons recorded in Enochian lore (the word "cacodaemon" first appeared in the sixteenth century). Bane notes that in most cases very little is known about them except who their angelic adversary is. They are listed as: Aax, Adi, Agb, And, Aor, Apa, Apm, Ash, Asi, Ast, Ato, Ava, Cab, Cac, Cam, Cms, Cop, Csc, Cus, Eac, Erg, Ern, Exr, Hbr, Hru, Hua, Idalam, Mgm, Miz, Mma, Moc, Mop, Mto, Oap, Odo, Oec, Oia, Ona, Onh, Onp, Pdi, Pm, Pia, Piz, Rad, Rda, Rpa, Rrb, Rrl, Rsi, Rxp, Xai, Xcz, Xdz, Xii, Xom, Xoy, and Xpa.

**The Enochian Rulers of the Lunar Mansions** — Twenty-eight demonic rulers of the lunar mansions in Enochian lore; Bane notes that sources nearly universally agree on the number but that Amnixiel and Dirachiel appear twice in some lists (possibly an error from the original source). Each ruler presides over a specific lunar mansion with associated capabilities and a zodiacal sign.

**Choronzon** — The demon of dispersion, described as "the last obstacle between the adept and enlightenment." First appearing in the writing of Edward Kelley in the sixteenth century, and again in John Dee's Enochian magic system, Choronzon was named by Aleister Crowley in the Holy Books of Thelema and in his working in the Algerian desert (some say the demon then possessed Crowley for the rest of his life). His sign is that of the crocodile. His number is 333. When he appeared to Crowley, he shouted the words said to open the gates of Hell: "Zazas, Zazas, Nasatanada, Zazas."

## Notable Named Enochian Demons

**Samiaza** — Leader and instigator of the Watcher rebellion; under his leadership the Watchers formed their pact and swore allegiance to one another before descending to take human wives. Many named Fallen Angels throughout Bane's encyclopedia are identified as having "sworn allegiance to Samiaza."

**Azazyel (Azazel)** — One of the primary Fallen Angels of the Enochian/Book of Enoch tradition; deemed the ringleader of the rebellious angels alongside Samiaza. Taught humanity how to make and use armor, dyes, jewelry, knives, make-up, shields, and swords — thereby bearing special responsibility for corrupting mankind. His punishment was the severest: the archangel Raphael was ordered to bind him, take him to a desert place of darkness, throw him in a pit face-down, hurl sharp stones upon him, and deny him light until the end of days, when he will be destroyed by fire. Those who followed him were likewise bound and buried for seventy generations.

**Abduxuel** — In Enochian lore, the twelfth of the twenty-eight rulers of the lunar mansions.

**Abrinael** — In Enochian lore, one of the twenty-eight demonic rulers of the lunar mansions; presides over the twenty-fourth mansion (Sadabatha) and is known to hinder the government.

**Amnediel** — In Enochian lore, one of the twenty-eight demonic rulers of the lunar mansions; his abilities include strengthening the power to hold captives and repel mice.

**Amnixiel** — One of the twenty-eight demonic rulers of the lunar mansions in Enochian lore; his zodiacal sign is Pisces; known for causing the loss of treasure.

**Azariel** — In Enochian and Jewish lore, one of the twenty-eight demonic rulers of the lunar mansions.

**Barbiel** — A Fallen Angel of the Order of Virtues in Enochian lore; considered an Archdemon and retrograde spirit; one of the seven Planetary Princes of Hell and one of the seven Electors of Hell; commands the lunar mansion Archaam. A diurnal and nocturnal demon who lives near water.

**Batarjal** — In Enochian lore, Batarjal ("divider of God") is one of the Fallen Angels.

**Aax** — A named Enochian Cacodaemon whose angelic counterpart is Axir. Among the fifty-seven Cacodaemons documented in Enochian lore.
