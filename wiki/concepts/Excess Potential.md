## Definition from Source

Excess potential is an energetic imbalance created when a person attributes too much importance to something — whether to their own qualities (inner importance) or to external objects and events (outer importance). Zeland asserts that the universe's [[Law of Balance]] acts to eliminate excess potential by generating balanced forces that work against the person who created it.

"Excess potential: created when too much importance attributed to something; balanced forces emerge to eliminate it." (Ch. IV)

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Created by importance**: the sole cause of excess potential is the attribution of excessive meaning or significance to anything
- **Eliminated by balanced forces**: once created, balanced forces arise specifically to neutralize the excess potential, typically by working against the person's aims in that area
- **Universal mechanism**: "All difficulties are linked to balanced forces eliminating excess potential" (Ch. IV)
- **Desire as potential**: "Desire always creates excess potential. Desire is potential by definition." (Ch. VII) — this is why pure desire does not realize goals
- **Guilt as pure excess potential**: "Guilt = pure excess potential; guilt always produces a script including punishment" (Ch. III); manipulators exploit guilt as a control thread
- **Inner importance**: overestimation of one's own virtues or shortcomings creates excess potential directed inward
- **Outer importance**: attributing huge meaning to external objects, events, or goals creates excess potential directed outward

## The Obstacle Function (from source)

"The only obstacle on the path to fulfilling your desires is the projection of artificial importance." (Ch. IV)

Importance is therefore the primary mechanism by which people obstruct their own goals. The source frames slides, fears, guilt, and pendulum control all as variants of excess potential arising from importance.

## Eliminating Excess Potential (from source)

- "Do not try to overcome obstacles; reduce their level of importance" (Ch. IV)
- [[The Guardian (Transurfing)]] monitors inner values and levels of importance
- [[Pure Intention]] — desire and action without attribution of importance — does not create excess potential
- Accepting the possibility of failure at the outset reduces importance and clears the potential (Ch. VII)
