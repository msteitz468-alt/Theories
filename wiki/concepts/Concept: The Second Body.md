## Definition and Characterization from Source

According to [[Robert Monroe (1915–1995)|Robert A. Monroe]] in *[[Journeys Out of the Body]]*, the "Second Body" is a non-physical counterpart or duplicate of the physical human form. It exists parallel to the physical body and can separate from it while consciousness remains intact. Chapter 13 ("The Second Body") provides the primary sustained treatment of this concept.

Monroe states that the form of the physical body is carried over in the Second Body in replica "down to the last hair follicle and scar, unless you deliberately think otherwise." However, he notes that the Second Body is subject to modification through thought; one may deliberately alter its form, and if habitual thought patterns differ from those developed physically, the Second Body may take "whatever form is most convenient, deliberately or otherwise."

## Key Properties and Characteristics

### Form and Structure
- Replicates the physical body in precise detail by default
- Can be deliberately transformed through thought
- Maintains a humanoid shape unless consciousness directs otherwise
- May be modified into non-human forms (Monroe speculates about transformation into quadrupeds, which he suggests might explain mythological accounts of werewolves, centaurs, etc.)

### Sensory Capabilities in Second Body
The Second Body possesses altered sensory modes compared to physical embodiment:

- **Vision**: Operates through radiation impression rather than reflected light waves. Observer can see in all directions simultaneously without turning the head. Vision can be turned "on" or "off" at will. Initially appears dim, as if in half-light or myopic, but clarifies with use.

- **Hearing**: Not received through physical ears; instead the mind receives thought-messages and translates them into understandable words. The communicating occurs at a level "above conscious awareness."

- **Touch**: Shows the most definite relationship to its physical counterpart. Can feel textures and objects.

- **Smell and Taste**: Conspicuously absent according to Monroe's observations.

- **Personality Identification**: A new mode of perception unique to non-physical state. Humans can be identified not by appearance but through "an undisguised awareness of their prime personality habits and thoughts." Monroe likens this to analyzing a star's composition by spectrograph—inner personality radiates patterns that cannot be concealed.

### Perceptual Challenges
Monroe documents that the mind tends to rationalize unfamiliar perceptions by matching them to past experience. This can produce false identifications. For example, a Van de Graaff generator was initially perceived as "a child's automobile" based on approximate size and wheel-like protuberance. Similarly, complex unfamiliar events are reconstructed by the mind to match the most similar memory-associated event.

### Mobility and Movement
- Can move through solid objects of the physical world
- Movement is controlled through thought and focused intention
- Faster movement can be achieved through greater mental effort ("stretching")
- Movement toward specific persons can be initiated through concentration on personality (not physical appearance)
- The body naturally stops moving when the mind stops concentrating on stretching toward a destination

### Consciousness and Agency
The Second Body operates under the direction of conscious thought, though Monroe notes that subconscious thoughts also influence behavior. He documents instances of unintended journeys triggered by subconscious ideation below the level of conscious awareness.

## Sexuality in the Second Body

Monroe devotes extended treatment (Chapter 15) to sexuality in the Second Body. He argues that physical sexuality is "a pale imitation or a feeble attempt to duplicate a very intimate Second State form of communion and communication which is not at all 'sexual' as we understand the term."

In the Second Body, sexual encounter involves:
- No physical interpenetration
- Full dimensional intermingling "atom for atom, throughout the entire Second Body"
- A "moment of unbearable ecstasy" followed by tranquility and equalization
- A brief, sustained electron flow between the two bodies
- Afterward: "calm and serene separation"

Monroe relates this to electrostatic attraction between opposite poles and suggests this reveals profound misunderstanding of human sexuality resulting from social conditioning and taboos.

## Memory and Identity

Monroe reports that the Second Body is accompanied by memories unrelated to physical life experience. During separation, consciousness is "flooded" with memories of places, people, and events from non-physical existence. These memories lack apparent connection to current physical life activities, past physical experience, or visits to the distant non-physical realms (Locales II and III). The source of these memories remains mysterious according to Monroe's account.
