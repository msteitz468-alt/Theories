## Definition from Source

Feder's assessment of **psychic archaeology** — the claim that clairvoyants, dowsers, or "remote
viewers" can locate sites and read the past paranormally. He argues that while such practitioners
generate impressive-sounding anecdotes, their hits are unremarkable under scrutiny (vague,
retrofitted, or no better than informed guessing) and they **fail when tested under controlled
conditions.**

## Description from Source

Feder applies the same epistemology as elsewhere: extraordinary claims require controlled,
independently verifiable results, which psychic archaeology has never delivered. It connects to the
wiki's other psychic/remote-viewing material — compare the documented (and inconclusive) military
remote-viewing programs in [[The Men Who Stare at Goats (Ronson)]] — under the umbrella
[[Pseudo-archaeology and how to detect it (Feder)]].
