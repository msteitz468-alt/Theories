## Definition from Source

Focus levels are Monroe's numbered designations for reproducible, instrumentally verifiable states of consciousness along a spectrum from normal waking awareness through deep non-physical states. They were developed through the Monroe Institute's laboratory work and form the operational framework of the Gateway Program. Each level is identified by a combination of EEG brain-wave signatures and subjective phenomenological reports.

Monroe's first book (*Journeys Out of the Body*, 1971) used "Locale I/II/III" terminology. *Far Journeys* primarily uses Focus levels, treating the old Locale system as a "misnomer" and "understatement" that he has superseded.

## Focus Level Definitions from Source

| Level | Designation | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| C-1 | Normal waking | "A clearing in a dark forest" — ordinary consciousness |
| Focus 10 | Mind awake, body asleep | EEG shows sleep waves overlaid with beta (wakefulness); physiologically verified; the first Gateway guidepost |
| Focus 12 | Expanded awareness | Five physical senses shut off; non-physical perception begins; "perspectives and overviews change drastically" |
| Focus 15 | (Referenced in participant reports) | Associated with strong vibration experiences and sense of projection "into the universe" |
| Focus 21 | (Referenced in Explorer transcripts) | "Can be either physical or nonphysical, depending on where you choose to focus the energy" |
| Focus 22–28 | Post-death bridge zone | According to an entity communicating through Explorer SHE: "your bridge. They are your levels that you enter upon death." Level 28 marks crossing the bridge, beyond which consciousness no longer assumes human form |
| Focus 27 | (Referenced in participant accounts) | Location within the rings where deceased relatives or post-human persons are encountered |
| Levels ~100+ | (Referenced in participant 1644-CM) | Described as an area of "many people milling around" who "look like holograms" — apparently deep in the ring system |

The broader entity-channeled schema (Explorer session #366, entity communicating through SHE) described a 49-level system organized in seven circles of seven levels each: plants 1–7, animals 8–14, humans 15–21, bridge 22–28, then upper "spiritual realm" circles; further nested seven-circle systems extending to infinity.

## Focus 22–28 in *Ultimate Journey* (1994)

Monroe's third book gives the post-death levels their settled definitions, used operationally in the **Lifeline** retrieval program: **22** humans in partial consciousness (coma, delirium, intoxication); **23** the recently dead who cannot accept or release the Earth Life System; **24–26** the "Belief System Territories" (the dead grouped by religious/philosophical creed); **27** the "Reception Center"/"Park," an artificial way-station easing transition; **28** beyond human thought, past which return to a physical body is precluded. See [[The afterlife "Interstate" is mapped as Focus levels 22–28, served by the Lifeline retrieval program]].

## Relationship to Old Locale System

In Chapter 10, Monroe uses the phrase "the center major ring, used to call it Locale II… what an understatement, misnomer." This signals that the Focus level system provides greater nuance than the original three-locale framework from *Journeys Out of the Body*.

## Source Context

The Focus levels are presented as navigational tools rather than metaphysical assertions. Monroe designed them for practical use in laboratory settings where subjects and monitors needed a shared reference frame. The levels are reached via Hemi-Sync audio guidance but can also be reproduced from memory once trained.
