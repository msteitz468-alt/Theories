## Definition from Source

Enochian Magick, as presented in *Success Magick*, is the angelic magical system received by
[[John Dee (1527–1608-9)]] and [[Edward Kelley]] in 1582–1584 — comprising an angelical language and
alphabet, the **Enochian Calls (or Keys)**, the names and duties of angels, the **Watchtower Table**,
and the **[[The Thirty Aethyrs (Enochian)|thirty Aethyrs]]**. Brand calls it "the essence of practical
magick" and "no longer a historical curiosity."

## Description from Source

Brand argues the system has been deliberately kept "complex to the point where it is unappealing to
use," guarded by occultists who insist it is too dangerous, too complicated, or only for contacting
God. The Gallery of Magick approach **rejects that gatekeeping**: it strips away elaborate ceremony,
provides sigils and pronunciations directly, and directs the magic at real-world ends. Brand concedes
the source material is messy and contested (Dee and Kelley's bickering, errors, and the "reformed"
Watchtower Table — see [[Which Enochian Watchtower Table is authentic — the original or Kelley's reformed Table]]),
but argues the magic works in practice regardless. Compare the technique-not-dogma stance of
[[Chaos Magic]], which similarly distils older systems.
