## Definition from Source

Blavatsky's central organising claim in *The Secret Doctrine* (1888): beneath the apparently divergent
mythologies, religions, and cosmogonies of every civilisation — Hindu, Egyptian, Chaldean, Hebrew,
Greek, Norse, Chinese, pre-Columbian — there lies a single archaic "Wisdom-Religion" or "Sacred Science,"
preserved through the ages in a universal esoteric language intelligible only to initiates. The exoteric
creeds of the world's faiths are, on this view, partial, distorted, or deliberately veiled transmissions
of that one original doctrine. Comparative symbolism — read with the esoteric key — is the method by
which Blavatsky claims to reconstruct it.

## Description from Source

Part II of Volume I (*Evolution of Symbolism*) opens with the assertion that no mythology or folk-legend
is "pure fiction" — each carries a historical or philosophical kernel. Blavatsky sharply rejects the
dominant academic interpretation of her era (Max Müller's solar-myth school) that mythologies are
merely a "disease of language" or primitive misperception. Instead she argues:

1. **One universal language of symbols.** Every ancient theology, from the oldest Vedic texts to the
   Kabbalah, employs the same esoteric ideographic language, which has "seven dialects" corresponding
   to seven mysteries of Nature. Geometry, number, astronomical symbol (zodiac, planets), and sacred
   animal all encode the same underlying truths about cosmogenesis and the nature of Spirit and Matter.

2. **One original religion.** Blavatsky writes that the "striking similitude of conception" among all
   ancient philosophies — in their hidden spirit "invariably" — is "the result of no mere coincidence,
   but of a concurrent design." She concludes that "there was, during the youth of mankind, one language,
   one knowledge, one universal religion, when there were no churches, no creeds or sects, but when
   every man was a priest unto himself."

3. **The esoteric key.** Occult Science is said to "still hold the key to all the world problems." By
   applying it to "the rare fragments of long-forgotten cosmogonies" — Egyptian, Hindu, Greek, Chaldean,
   Hebrew, Aztec — Blavatsky claims to re-establish the "once Universal Cosmogony of Secret Doctrine."
   The key, she says, "fits them all."

4. **Exoteric forms as veils.** The gods, animals, crosses, serpents, lotuses, fire-pillars, zodiacs,
   and sacred geometry found in every religion are not objects of naive worship but symbolic encodings
   of metaphysical realities about the One Absolute, its emanations (Theos), and the material cosmos
   (Kosmos). The outer (exoteric) cult is a public veil; the inner (esoteric) meaning belongs to
   the mysteries.

This forms the theosophical version of the *prisca theologia* — the Renaissance idea that a single
divine revelation underlies all ancient wisdom traditions — given a distinctively cosmological and
evolutionary cast by Blavatsky.
