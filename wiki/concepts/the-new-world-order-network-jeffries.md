## Definition from Source

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], the "New World Order" (NWO) denotes an interlocking network of elite institutions — chiefly the [[Council on Foreign Relations]], the [[Bilderberg Group (Cooper)]], and Bohemian Grove — whose stated or implied long-term goal, in Jeffries's account, is the erosion of national sovereignty and the establishment of a single world government under the control of a financial and corporate elite. The term is not treated as the exclusive property of "conspiracy theorists"; Jeffries documents its use by George H. W. Bush, Nelson Mandela, Mikhail Gorbachev, and Robert Kennedy in public speeches.

## Description from Source

Jeffries describes the CFR as "like an establishment country club — a veritable Who's Who of American policy making" whose membership spans ostensibly opposed ideological camps (Dick Cheney and Jesse Jackson both belong, per the book). The Bilderbergers are presented as more secretive: roughly two thousand of the world's most powerful men, including politicians, corporate chiefs, and royalty, meeting annually behind closed doors while the mainstream press maintains near-total silence. Bohemian Grove — a northern California retreat at which approximately 2,000 elite men gather every July — is described as involving occult ritual (the "Cremation of Care" ceremony before a 40-foot stone owl), heavy drinking, and, per some attendee accounts cited by Jeffries, homosexual activity.

Jeffries quotes several figures Jeffries regards as admissions of the agenda:

- David Rockefeller (attributed 1991 Bilderberg meeting): "We are grateful to the Washington Post, the New York Times, Time Magazine… it would have been impossible for us to develop our plans for the world if we had been subject to the bright lights of publicity…. The world is now… prepared to march toward a world government."
- Rockefeller's 2002 memoirs: "Some even believe we are part of a secret cabal working against the best interests of the United States… If that's the charge, I stand guilty, and I am proud of it."
- Denis Healey (Bilderberg founder, 2001): "To say we were striving for a one world government is exaggerated, but not wholly unfair."
- Henry Kissinger (undated): "Today America would be outraged if UN troops entered Los Angeles to restore order. Tomorrow they will be grateful…."
- James Warburg (US Senate testimony, February 17, 1950): "We shall have world government, whether or not we like it."
- Walter Cronkite (1999 UN appearance): "…we Americans will have to yield up some of our sovereignty…. I'm glad to sit here at the right hand of Satan."

Jeffries links the NWO network to the Federal Reserve's private banking cartel (see [[Federal Reserve as a banking cartel]]) and to child-sex scandal cover-ups (see [[The Franklin Cover-Up (Jeffries account)]]), arguing these are facets of the same consolidated elite power structure.
