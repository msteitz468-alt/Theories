## Definition from Source

Visualisation in Transurfing is defined precisely as "mentally picturing the realization process of a current link in the transfer chain." (Ch. VIII Summary) This distinguishes it from conventional visualisation techniques, which focus on the end goal.

"Desire is the concentration of attention on a goal. Intention is the concentration of attention on movement towards the goal. The driving force behind any action is intention and not desire." (Ch. VIII)

## Three Types of Visualisation (from source)

Ch. VIII presents three types of visualisation:

1. **Dream** — passive fantasizing; "Dreams do not come true!" — the weakest and most unreliable form; no intent to have, no intent to act; dreamer does not actually expect realization
2. **Film (goal-based)** — consciously imagining the realization of a desire as if watching a film; produces what you visualize but not as *yours* — "They end up staring at it like a beggar stares into a shop window!" — key mistake: the person orders the goal for someone else because they do not claim ownership
3. **Active participation (slide)** — person plays a role in the visualized scenario rather than watching; sets parameters of thought energy to the corresponding life line; this is what becomes a [[Slides (Transurfing)|slide]]

## The Core Distinction (from source)

The source identifies the key failure of conventional visualisation as concentrating on the end goal rather than the process:

"Visualisation of your goals is the work of desire which will not bring you a single step closer to your goal." (Ch. VIII)
"Contemplating the end result will expand the limits of your comfort zone and so in this sense it has its value, but visualising the process of moving towards your goal will significantly quicken the work of outer intention." (Ch. VIII)

The end-goal slide (type 3) is still useful — it expands the [[Comfort Zone (Transurfing)]] and attunes thought energy to the target life line. But the process visualisation is the "workhorse of Transurfing."

## Visualising the Process (from source)

"In Transurfing the visualisation practice involves imagining a mental picture of the process of moving towards your goal based on the principle that the goal must be reached sooner or later because the power of intention is working." (Ch. VIII)

Practical guidance:
- For a house: do not look at the house as a painting; enter it virtually, walk through rooms, feel the warmth, sit down to dinner — inhabit it as already yours
- For creative work: visualise the creation process — the work improving each day, you adding new qualities, it becoming a masterpiece in your hands
- "An artist creates and admires their work simultaneously." (Ch. VIII)

## Transfer Chains and Visualisation (from source)

The rule: "practice visualising the process of moving towards your goal at your current stage only." (Ch. VIII) Visualising stages far ahead in the transfer chain is ineffective and creates frustration; the process visualisation must always correspond to the current link.
