## Definition / Characterization from Source

**Pressure from Above and Pressure from Below** is a revolutionary pincer strategy described by Gary Allen and Larry Abraham as the primary tactic used by the Insiders to systematically expand the power of the federal government and force the socialization of American society.

According to *None Dare Call It Conspiracy*:
* **Origin:** The concept is derived from Communist historian Jan Kozak's analysis of the Red takeover of Czechoslovakia. Kozak described how a minority Communist party could capture control of a democratic nation by combining pressure from the government hierarchy with pressure from radical street movements.
* **The Pincer Mechanics:**
  * **Pressure from Above:** Consists of actions taken by Establishment insiders, bureaucrats, and politicians within the government. They pass regulations, executive orders, and legislation that centralize power, create new social programs, and restrict free enterprise under the guise of solving national crises.
  * **Pressure from Below:** Consists of actions taken by radical street groups, agitators, rioters, and militant activist organizations. They stage protests, demand radical reforms, and create social unrest, civil disorder, and economic disruption in the streets.
* **Strategic Outcome:** The street rioters and agitators act as pawns or shills for the conspirators above. When the mobs demand solutions to the crises they create, the Establishment insiders in government "reluctantly" respond by expanding federal control, passing more regulations, and increasing taxes. In this manner, the middle class is squeezed between the government above and the mobs below, leading to a steady, rapid transition into state socialism.
