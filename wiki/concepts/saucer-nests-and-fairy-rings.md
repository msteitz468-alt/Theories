Vallée's grouping of ground-trace phenomena: circular flattened/uprooted vegetation (the Tully 'nests' 1966, the Ohio ring 1965, the Charlton crater 1963) which he sets against the folkloric 'fairy rings' where grass grows differently and which tradition attributes to fairy dancing.
