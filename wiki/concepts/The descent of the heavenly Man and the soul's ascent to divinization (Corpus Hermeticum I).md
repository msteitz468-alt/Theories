## Definition from Source

The Hermetic anthropogony and soteriology of Poimandres. The Father-Mind gives birth to a **Man** in
his own image, whom he loves; granted authority over creation, the Man looks down, falls in love with
Nature, and descends — so the human being is uniquely **double**: mortal in body, immortal and divine in
essence. Salvation reverses the descent: at death the soul **ascends through the seven planetary
zones**, surrendering at each a vice/energy it took on, until — stripped bare — it enters the **ogdoad**,
hymns the Father with the powers, and is **"made god."**

## Description from Source

The seven surrenders (increase/decrease, machination, the illusion of longing, arrogance, unholy
presumption, evil impulses of wealth, and ambushing deceit) map the planetary spheres. Beyond the
ogdoad the soul joins powers who "enter into God" — "this is the final good for those who have received
knowledge: to be made god." Mind acts as **gatekeeper**, barring the bodily vices from the reverent.
Compare the Kybalion's [[The Principle of Mentalism (Kybalion)|Mentalism]] and the rebirth of
[[Rebirth: expelling the twelve torments by the powers of God (Corpus Hermeticum XIII)]].
