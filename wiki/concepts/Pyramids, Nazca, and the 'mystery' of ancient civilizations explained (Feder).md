## Definition from Source

Feder's treatment of the supposed "unsolvable mysteries" of ancient monuments — the Egyptian pyramids,
the Nazca lines, Easter Island, Stonehenge — which pseudo-archaeology presents as impossible without
lost super-science or alien help. Feder argues each has **well-documented human explanations** grounded
in archaeology, experiment, and the monuments' own cultural context.

## Description from Source

He shows the pyramids were built by organized human labor using known techniques (and that the
construction sequence is archaeologically traceable); that the Nazca lines are large but technically
simple ground drawings makeable with stakes and string, meant to be experienced from the ground or as
ritual pathways, *not* "alien landing strips"; and that the "mystery" framing systematically
underrates ancient engineering. This counters [[Fingerprints of the Gods (Hancock)]] and the
ancient-astronaut reading ([[The ancient-astronaut fantasy debunked (Feder)]]); see the synthesis
[[Where did civilization and advanced knowledge come from? Models compared across sources]].
