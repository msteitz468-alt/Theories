# Goetic grimoire demons (Bane)

## Definition from Source

According to Bane's *Encyclopedia of Demons in World Religions and Cultures*, the Goetia — formally the Ars Goetia, the first book of the *Lemegeton* (also called the *Lesser Key of Solomon*) — is the most systematically organized demonological text in the Western grimoire tradition. Johann Wierus (Weirus) published the foundational list in his *Pseudomonarchia Daemonum* (False Monarchy of Demons, 1583), cataloguing sixty-nine princes of Hell. Samuel L. MacGregor Mathers's 1904 edition *The Goetia: The Lesser Key of Solomon the King* added three more, bringing the canonical Goetia total to seventy-two spirits; Aleister Crowley's edition further popularized the text in the twentieth century.

The seventy-two Spirits of Solomon (also called the Spirits of the Goetia) are ranked according to an elaborate infernal nobility: **kings**, **princes**, **dukes**, **marquises**, **earls/counts**, and **presidents**. Each spirit is assigned: a specific number of legions of demons under their command; a specific appearance (animal forms, hybrid forms, human forms with specific attributes); a particular specialty or ability they will teach or perform; and in many cases an astrological or temporal window during which they are most powerful.

Bane notes that the Lemegeton has five books in total: the Ars Goetia (the 72 spirits), the Theurgia Goetia (aerial and directional spirits), the Ars Paulina, the Ars Almadel, and the Ars Nova. The Theurgia Goetia adds a parallel system of elemental princes (North, South, East, West) with their own named duke-servitors, many of whom are Aerial Devils.

The Goetia tradition is essentially an extension of Christian demonology: nearly all 72 spirits are described by Bane as Fallen Angels. The system is oriented toward practical summoning — the grimoire provides exact appearance, capabilities, method of binding, and in some cases the personal adversary saint who can banish the demon.

## Notable Goetia Spirits

**Baal (Bael)** — A king ruling in the East and one of the seventy-two Spirits of Solomon; also ranked as grand cross of the Order of the Fly. Appears as a portly three-headed beast (one head of a cat, one of a crowned man, one of a toad) with long spider legs, speaking in a hoarse voice. Commands sixty-six legions; demon of anger, hate, vengeance, and war; can bestow the power of invisibility and heighten perception. Most powerful in October.

**Agares** — Grand duke of the eastern region of Hell and prime minister of Lucifer; also listed as one of the seventy-two Spirits of Solomon and a Fallen Angel. Sources vary as to whether he serves directly under Lucifuge Rofocale or Mephistopheles. Responsible for distributing titles and prelacies.

**Aamon (Aamon)** — Grand Marquis of Hell; demon of life and reproduction. Described as having the head of a serpent and the body of a wolf (and vice versa). His name in Hebrew means "lion of God."

**Barbatos** — A Fallen Angel formerly of the Order of Virtues; ranked as count, duke, or earl depending on the edition. One of the seventy-two Spirits of Solomon and one of the three Servitors of Satanachia; also one of the four Assistants of Ashtaroth. Commands four demonic kings, thirty legions, and ten chiefs. A diurnal demon.

**Asmoday (Asmodeus the Goetic)** — Listed in the Goetia as the first and most powerful demon under the command of Amaymon, ranked as king. A Fallen Angel formerly of the Order of Angels. The demon of adultery, impurity, lechery, luxury, and sensuality; commands seventy-two legions. Appears as a well-dressed man or beautiful woman, or as a three-headed being (bull, ram, man vomiting fire) with a serpent tail, webbed goose feet, riding a dragon carrying a lance and banner. Credited with inventing carousels, dancing, music, plays, and recreational drug use.

**Astaroth** — Grand duke and treasurer of Hell; commands forty legions; a Fallen Angel and former prince of the Order of Thrones. Appears as an ugly angel riding a dragon and carrying a snake in his left hand. Will willingly answer all questions regarding past, present, and future. One of the seventy-two Spirits of Solomon.

**Buer** — One of the seventy-two Spirits of Solomon; appears as a centaur carrying bow and quiver, a five-spoke wheel that moves by rolling itself, or a man with a lion's head and five goat legs. Can only be summoned when the sun is in Sagittarius. Summoned for domestic felicity, healing the sick, and teaching philosophy and herbal medicine.

**Caim (Caym)** — In the Ars Goetia, ranked as both president and prince with the titles of Grand Master of Hell and Thrush President. A Fallen Angel formerly of the Order of Angels; one of the seventy-two Spirits of Solomon. Commands thirty legions. Appears as a blackbird, a man with a sharp sword, or a man with peacock's tail. Summoned for his willingness to answer questions.

**Stolas** — The Raven Prince, commanding twenty-six legions. Appears before his summoner as a raven or crowned owl; can take on the semblance of a man if asked. Invoked for ability to teach astronomy, prophecy based on plant study, and how to judge the value of gems. One of the Spirits of Solomon.

**Amy** — A president of Hell and one of the Fallen Angels formerly of the Order of Powers; commands thirty-six legions. Appears as a roaring fire or as a man; a nocturnal demon. Gives knowledge of astrology and liberal sciences, provides good Familiars, and reveals the locations of treasures. Unusually, it is said that after 200,000 years of banishment Amy will be allowed to return to Heaven and resume his seat in the seventh throne.

**Foras** — One of the Spirits of Solomon; appears before his summoner as a strong man. A Fallen Angel formerly of the Order of Thrones.

**Barbas (Marbas)** — One of the seventy-two Spirits of Solomon; described as a great president of Hell.
