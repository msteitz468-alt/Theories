## Definition / Characterization from Source

Domhoff's central organizing concept, adapted from C. Wright Mills. The **power elite** is the *leadership group* of the dominant class — "the corporate rich" — not a separate cabal. It consists of the people who actively govern on behalf of the wealthy few (the top 0.5% who own the country): the directors and top executives of the corporate community, the leaders of the social upper class, and the people they place in the foundations, think tanks, policy-discussion groups, and high appointive positions in the federal executive branch.

Crucially for this wiki, the power elite is **visible and documentable**, numbering in the thousands and operating through legally reported, openly studyable organizations — the opposite of the small, secret, behind-the-scenes plotters posited by conspiracy theories (see [[Domhoff: power-structure analysis is not a conspiracy theory]]). It rules not by command but by dominating the four networks through which policy is made: the corporate community ([[The corporate community]]), the social upper class ([[The social upper class]]), the policy-planning network ([[The policy-planning network]]), and the opinion-shaping network ([[The opinion-shaping network]]).
