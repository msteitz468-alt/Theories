## Definition and Characterization from Source

In [[Thomas Campbell]]'s *[[My Big TOE]]*, **AUO** (Absolute Unbounded Oneness) is the most fundamental assumption upon which the entire theoretical framework rests. It is described as a primordial, undifferentiated consciousness-potential-energy field — the One Source from which all of reality derives. Campbell explicitly states it is one of only two foundational assumptions his theory requires (the other being the [[Concept: The Fundamental Process of Evolution|Fundamental Process of evolution]]).

Campbell defines AUO as:

> "A finite primordial-consciousness-potential-energy-thing."

Despite the name "Absolute Unbounded Oneness," Campbell clarifies that neither "absolute" nor "unbounded" are literally accurate — AUO is not infinite, it is enormous but finite. The name reflects appearance from within the system rather than metaphysical precision.

## Core Properties

### High Entropy State
AUO is characterized by **high entropy** — meaning it is in a disordered, poorly organized state, with minimal internal structure, awareness, or differentiation. Campbell uses entropy (borrowed from thermodynamics) as the primary measure of consciousness quality: high entropy = dim awareness, minimal organization; low entropy = bright awareness, high organization.

AUO begins as:
- Undifferentiated potential energy
- Dimly aware at best (Campbell calls this "dim consciousness")
- Analogous to "a single biological cell floating in the primordial ooze"
- No individuated units, no structure, no purpose

### Digital Nature
AUO is described as digital — constituted of "reality cells" that can flip between states (analogous to binary bits). This digital granularity is fundamental:

> "All cellular and digital creations exhibit granularity at some level of detail. Both our reality and our perception of it are granular at the root."

The reality cells of AUO form the substrate from which all complexity eventually emerges.

### Self-Organizing Potential
Though initially dim and unstructured, AUO possesses inherent potential for self-organization. When the Fundamental Process acts on this potential, AUO begins to reduce its entropy, developing internal structure, differentiation, and eventually awareness.

### Source of All Being
AUO is "The One Source":

> "All are connected through (and are a part of) the One Source: AUO consciousness. AUO is the foundation of everything, because everything exists in the relation of AUO to itself."

Everything that exists — physical universes (PMRs), nonphysical realities (NPMRs), individuated consciousness beings — is a specialized subset of AUO's consciousness.

## Relationship to AUM

AUO does not remain static. As the Fundamental Process acts upon it, AUO evolves increasing complexity, structure, awareness, and purpose. When this evolution produces a sufficiently advanced, structured, self-aware, and purposeful consciousness, Campbell assigns the new name **AUM** ([[Concept: AUM (Absolute Unbounded Manifold)|Absolute Unbounded Manifold]]).

Campbell emphasizes there is no sharp line:
> "There is no clear dividing line between AUO and AUM. The distinction is entirely arbitrary. The extent to which AUO grows up and develops structure, organization and complex communications, as well as content, meaning, self-awareness, memory, value, and purpose is what allows AUO to emerge renamed AUM."

Campbell compares this to naming animals: we call upright primates "people" rather than "upright naked monkeys with short arms" because the degree of complexity warrants a new category.

## Philosophical Status

### Not the Traditional God
Campbell explicitly distinguishes AUO/AUM from theistic conceptions of God:

> "Don't get anthropomorphic on me here and postulate AUM as a little old man with a long white beard making people out of space-time clay on day seven."

AUO/AUM is not a person, not perfectly good, not omniscient in a traditional sense. It is an evolving system that finds the Fundamental Process applying to itself whether it wishes or not.

### Not Infinite
Despite the name, Campbell states AUO is "apparently infinite" but actually finite. The apparent infinitude is a product of scale — AUO is enormous relative to anything within it, but not genuinely boundless.

### One Source but Not Solitary
Campbell speculates whether other AUOs might exist outside the one AUO sufficient to generate all known reality, but treats this as beyond the scope of his theory:

> "All we need to build a complete theory of everything is a simple one celled monolithic singular AUO and its internal environment."

## The Two Fundamental Assumptions

Campbell explicitly states the entire My Big TOE trilogy is derived logically from only two assumptions:
1. AUO: a finite primordial-consciousness-potential-energy-thing
2. [[Concept: The Fundamental Process of Evolution|The Fundamental Process of evolution]]

> "From only those two assumptions, I intend to derive physics, the nature of your existence, your physical universe and experience, and provide a comprehensive working model of the larger reality."
