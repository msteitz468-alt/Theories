## Definition from Source

The Hermetic Axioms form the closing chapter (Ch. XV) of [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]], presented by [[The Three Initiates]] as practical maxims distilling the entire teaching into actionable principles. The chapter opens with a statement on the Law of Use:

> "The possession of Knowledge, unless accompanied by a manifestation and expression in Action, is like the hoarding of precious metals—a vain and foolish thing. Knowledge, like Wealth, is intended for Use. The Law of Use is Universal, and he who violates it suffers by reason of his conflict with natural forces." — The Kybalion.

The Kybalion insists that Hermetic teaching was never intended for mere storage or secrecy — it demands practice. The axioms collected in this chapter operationalize the Seven Principles, particularly those of Vibration, Polarity, Rhythm, and Causation. The key axioms are:

**On changing mental states through vibration:**
> "To change your mood or mental state—change your vibration." — The Kybalion.

The method: Will directs Attention; Attention changes Vibration. Cultivating the Art of Attention via Will is presented as the secret of mastery over moods.

**On destroying undesirable mental states through Polarity:**
> "To destroy an undesirable rate of mental vibration, put into operation the Principle of Polarity and concentrate upon the opposite pole to that which you desire to suppress. Kill out the undesirable by changing its polarity." — The Kybalion.

The analogy given: one does not sweep darkness from a room, but opens the shutters to admit light. Concentrating on the Positive pole dissolves the Negative.

**On Mental Transmutation:**
> "Mind (as well as metals and elements) may be transmuted from state to state; degree to degree; condition to condition; pole to pole; vibration to vibration." — The Kybalion.

**On neutralizing Rhythm:**
> "Rhythm may be neutralized by an application of the Art of Polarization." — The Kybalion.

The Hermetic adept polarizes at the Higher Self ("I Am") pole rather than the pole of personality, causing the pendulum-swing to manifest only on the lower unconscious plane.

**On Causation and Mastery:**
> "Nothing escapes the Principle of Cause and Effect, but there are many Planes of Causation, and one may use the laws of the higher to overcome the laws of the lower." — The Kybalion.

And the crowning axiom, attributed to Hermetic Masters:
> "The wise ones serve on the higher, but rule on the lower. They obey the laws coming from above them, but on their own plane, and those below them, they rule and give orders." — The Kybalion.

**The closing axiom and ultimate thesis:**
> "True Hermetic Transmutation is a Mental Art." — The Kybalion.

> "THE ALL is MIND; The Universe is Mental." — The Kybalion.

The final axiom ties all practical teaching back to [[The Principle of Mentalism (Kybalion)]]: because the Universe is wholly Mental, all real transformation is accomplished through Mental Power.
