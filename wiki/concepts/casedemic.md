## Definition from Source

**"Casedemic"** is the term used in *[[Faucian Bargain (The Most Powerful and Dangerous Bureaucrat in American History)]]* for what the authors call a pandemic of *positive test results* rather than of serious illness — driven, they argue, by over-sensitive PCR testing of asymptomatic people that inflated case counts and fueled "panic porn" used to justify lockdowns.

## Key Points

- The book ties the casedemic to **testing thresholds**: it highlights that "literally one hour after Biden was inaugurated," the **WHO** (January 20, 2021) advised that PCR sensitivity levels were too high and that asymptomatic people should not be the testing focus.
- It cites a subsequent steep fall in reported U.S. cases (a claimed ~71% decrease in ~40 days from the January 8, 2021 peak) as evidence the case surge was partly an artifact of testing metrics.
- The concept underwrites the book's argument that fear of **asymptomatic spread** was overstated (CDC's estimate that ~15% of infections were asymptomatic is cited as making asymptomatic transmission "mathematically impossible" to be the main driver).
