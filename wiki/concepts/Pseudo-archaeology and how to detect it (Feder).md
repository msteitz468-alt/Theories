## Definition from Source

The umbrella thesis of *Frauds, Myths, and Mysteries*: that the past is knowable through the
**scientific method applied to material evidence**, and that "pseudo-archaeology" — ancient astronauts,
lost continents, fringe diffusionism, psychic archaeology — fails because it ignores or distorts that
evidence. Feder argues the test is *epistemological* ("how do you know what you know?"), and that real
archaeology repeatedly explains the supposed "mysteries" without exotic causes.
