# The great antiquity of man and the critique of Darwinian evolution (Blavatsky)

## Concept Overview

Volume II, Book II, Part III (Addenda) of *The Secret Doctrine* (1888) constitutes Blavatsky's sustained engagement with the scientific anthropology of her day — particularly Darwinian and Haeckelian evolution — from the standpoint of Occult science. The section is titled "Science and the Secret Doctrine Contrasted."

Blavatsky's position rests on three interlocking theses:

1. **Radical human antiquity.** Blavatsky asserts that physical man is far older than the Quaternary age conceded by mainstream geology; she places the Fourth Root-Race (Atlanteans) in the early Eocene, claims a total sedimentation history of approximately 320 million years for the Earth's present Round, and argues that esoteric chronology is at least as defensible as the wildly divergent figures offered by scientific authorities of her era (Thomson, Lyell, Huxley, Croll, etc.).

2. **Rejection of the ape-ancestor model.** Blavatsky asserts that humanity did not descend from an anthropoid primate or any common ancestor shared with apes. Rather, the occult teaching inverts the Darwinian scheme: the great apes — specifically the anthropoid species (gorilla, chimpanzee, orang-utan) — are degenerate offshoots of early humanity, products of unions between the "mindless" Third Root-Race men and now-extinct mammals, later renewed by the Atlantean Fourth Race. She draws on the comparative anatomist de Quatrefages (and cites Huxley's own admissions) to argue that the developmental trajectory of the ape brain is *inverse* to that of man, supporting descent of ape from man rather than the reverse.

3. **Natural selection as insufficient and spiritually blind.** Blavatsky asserts that Darwinian "Natural Selection" is at best a descriptive label for the elimination of the unfit, not a creative principle. It cannot account for the origin of variations, the origin of language, or the "quasi-intelligent impulse" underlying organic development. The deeper driver, she argues, is a sub-conscious intelligence pervading matter — ultimately traceable to Dhyan-Chohanic wisdom and Fohat — an idea she finds partially corroborated by Eduard von Hartmann's "Unconscious" and similar currents in European idealist philosophy.

## Key distinctions from Darwinism

Blavatsky accepts that evolution is real and that the process is slow and gradual ("natura non facit saltum"); she does not endorse Biblical special creation. The quarrel is with the *nature* of the ancestral form and the *driver* of evolution. Occult anthropology posits that:

- The human physical frame was preceded by etheric/astral prototypes; the mammals are *post-human* in origin within the present (Fourth) Round.
- Man existed alongside, and prior to, the anthropoid mammals in the Tertiary period.
- Current "savage" races and Palaeolithic men are not primitive ancestors but *degenerations* or side-branches from more advanced earlier races (the cyclic law of rises and falls of civilization).

## Evidential strategy

Rather than claiming independent empirical proof unavailable to science, Blavatsky exploits the extreme disagreement among scientists themselves (geological timescale estimates range, in her survey, from tens of thousands to a billion years) and the absence of any discovered "missing link," arguing that occult hypotheses deserve the same standing as unverified scientific speculation.
