## Definition from Source

The umbrella concept of the book: a **magical correspondence** is an association between a magical
intention and a physical or symbolic item (a plant, stone, animal, deity, color, planet, number, or
element) used to "focus intent and direct energy" in ritual and spellwork. Kynes reframes
correspondences not as "rote tables" but as a **web** — each item linking both to an intention *and*
to the other correspondences — so a practitioner can "weave" interrelated associations (e.g. for
"love": fire → red → rose → Aphrodite → swan) to amplify and focus intent.

## Description from Source

Kynes presents the use of correspondences as **sympathetic magic** — "the power of one thing can be
used through something else connected or associated with it" (citing Isaac Bonewits) — and argues it
works because the brain itself organizes information by association (invoking Aristotle's law of
association by contiguity). This is a practical, Pagan/Wiccan expression of the broader Hermetic
doctrine of correspondence, [[The Principle of Correspondence (Kybalion)|"as above, so below"]] (one
of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]]); compare the technique-centered magic of [[Chaos Magic]] and
[[Enochian Magick (Gallery of Magick approach)]].
