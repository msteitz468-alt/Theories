## Definition from Source

The governing theme uniting the treatises: the supreme God **is Mind (Nous)**, invisible yet "seen
through the entire cosmos," the unbegotten craftsman of all; and the human is saved not by ritual works
but by **knowing** him — a contemplative **gnosis** that transforms and ultimately **divinizes** the
knower ("to be made god"). To see the invisible God one must use the invisible faculty, the mind's eye;
the ignorant remain "drunk" and asleep, while the reverent receive Mind itself.

## Description from Source

This is the doctrine the wiki meets second-hand in the [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)|Kybalion's]]
[[The Principle of Mentalism (Kybalion)|Mentalism]] ("THE ALL is Mind") and in
[[The Forbidden Universe (Picknett & Prince)|Picknett & Prince's]]
[[The cosmic Mind of God (Forbidden Universe)|cosmic Mind of God]] — both of which trace their God-as-Mind
to these Hermetic texts. It also grounds the regeneration of
[[Rebirth: expelling the twelve torments by the powers of God (Corpus Hermeticum XIII)]].
