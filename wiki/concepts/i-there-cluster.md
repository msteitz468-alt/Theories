## Definition from Source

The I-There cluster is Monroe's term for the higher-order identity formed by the totality of all incarnations of a single soul — every "I" that has ever entered physical (or non-physical) life as part of one fundamental energy unit. Each individual human life is a waveform within the I-There; the I-There simultaneously encompasses and remembers all of them.

Monroe experiences the I-There directly in Chapter 9 (Rainbow Route) by traveling through a luminous tube into the INSPEC realm: he merges progressively with successive "I" waveforms — each past or parallel self recognizing the others with great joy — until reaching a state where all known parts of his I-There are unified. At that point, the merged unity is revealed to be what Monroe had been calling an INSPEC.

The term "I-There" combines "I" (the individual self here in physical life) and "There" (Monroe's usual reference to the non-physical domain). The cluster exists "There" as an ongoing identity while individual "I" lives play out in physical reality.

## Key Structural Features from Source

- Each individual human life is one waveform within a much larger set
- The waveforms are "remarkably identical" in base pattern, with distinct variegations that, when combined across the cluster, create new modifications of the total
- Non-human incarnations (in other physical forms, on other planets) are also part of the I-There: Monroe encounters "the first I I perceive as returning from a completely nonhuman sojourn" and learns about a consciousness that had a physically controlled prehensile tail
- The I-There is incomplete until all its incarnating parts have returned; it remains "at the point" of its realm, reaching back to gather remaining parts
- When complete, the unified I-There is an INSPEC — one of many such unified wholes
- Beyond completion, the INSPEC "continues" toward what Monroe tentatively identifies as "the source of the radiation, the creative emission and return" — but communication from those who have continued is closed

## Source Context

Monroe first experiences the I-There concept earlier in the book when he decides (spring 1972, Ch. 1) to surrender control of his OBE activities to his "total self (soul?)" — a proto-I-There reference. By Chapter 9 he understands this more precisely. The INSPEC's parting instruction to Monroe is: "You are still incomplete. There are parts of you yet to be transformed, including that visiting portion so filled with curiosity." Monroe understands that his continued physical life is itself one part of the I-There's return journey.
