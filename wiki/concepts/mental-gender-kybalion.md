## Definition from Source

Mental Gender is the application of [[The Principle of Gender (Kybalion)]] to the mental plane, as taught in [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]] by [[The Three Initiates]]. It is presented as the ancient Hermetic framework that unifies and explains what modern psychology calls the "dual mind" — variously labeled objective/subjective, conscious/subconscious, voluntary/involuntary, or active/passive mind.

The Kybalion's teaching proceeds from introspection: consciousness first reports "I Am," but closer examination reveals a split into two distinct aspects — the **"I"** and the **"Me"**:

**The "Me" (Feminine Principle of Mind):**
- Corresponds to the Subjective, Sub-conscious, Involuntary, Passive mind
- Contains feelings, tastes, habits, characteristics, accumulated knowledge — the personality as known to oneself and others
- Functions as the "mental womb": capable of generating mental offspring (new thoughts, concepts, images)
- Tends toward receiving impressions; conducts the work of imagination and generation
- Without the stimulation of the "I," tends to produce only images derived from outside impressions rather than original creations
- Represents the Aspect of Becoming

**The "I" (Masculine Principle of Mind):**
- Corresponds to the Objective, Conscious, Voluntary, Active mind
- Contains and exercises the Will
- Projects Vibratory Energy toward the "Me," setting creative processes in motion
- Can stand aside and witness the "Me's" mental operations
- Does not itself generate, but directs and energizes the generative process
- Represents the Aspect of Being

The Three Initiates teach that most people are "polarized in the Feminine Principle" — dwelling almost entirely in the "Me," allowing the Masculine Will to lie inactive, and therefore being governed by the thoughts and wills of others. Strong individuals are those who activate the "I"/Will, direct their own mental generation, and can project Will-energy onto the minds of others (the mechanism underlying telepathy, hypnotism, suggestion, and personal magnetism).
