## Concept Definition

**The Great Simulation** is Rizwan Virk's term for the comprehensive, massively multiplayer virtual reality simulation that we experience as "physical reality." In this framework, the universe is modeled as a sophisticated role-playing video game. Humans are not physical biological bodies, but rather consciousnesses (players) logged into the simulation, animating virtual avatars.

Virk distinguishes between two main styles of simulation interfaces:
1. **RPG Style (Avatars):** The player exists outside the computer, projecting their consciousness into the game character (avatar). This aligns with spiritual models of an immortal soul entering a physical body.
2. **NPC Style (AI):** The character exists entirely inside the computer program as an artificial intelligence or non-player character (NPC), showing local consciousness.

## Spiritual and Mythological Parallels

Virk argues that the Great Simulation provides a technical vocabulary that makes ancient religious and mystical teachings scientifically understandable:

* **Maya and Illusion:** Eastern concepts of *Maya* (Buddhism/Hinduism) describe the physical world as a persistent illusion. In simulation terms, the physical world is a rendered graphic interface projected onto the observer's mind.
* **Karma as Game Quests:** *Karma* operates like a video game's quest engine. If a player fails a quest or behaves poorly (gaining negative points), the system logs the state. Reincarnation represents starting a new "life" (respawning) with the same character sheet to try the quest again.
* **God and Angels as Administrators:** The deities, angels, and controllers described in Western and Eastern traditions are equivalent to system administrators (SysOps), developers, and game masters. They can modify variables, pause the game, perform "adjustments" (as Philip K. Dick described), or communicate with players through in-game overlays (visions/messages).
* **NDEs as Logging Out:** Near-death experiences (NDEs) represent a temporary disconnect from the game server. The player briefly returns to the "lobby" (the non-physical realm outside the simulation), experiences a review of their play session, and is then sent back to continue their quest.
