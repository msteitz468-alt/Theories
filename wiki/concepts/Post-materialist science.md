## Definition / Characterization from Source

An umbrella for the family of arguments — advanced by scientifically credentialed authors — that the **materialist/physicalist worldview is incomplete**, and that consciousness (or mind, or the observer) is fundamental to reality rather than an accidental by-product of matter. The three books gathered here make the case from different directions:

- **Dean Radin, *The Conscious Universe*** — the empirical/parapsychological case: decades of laboratory **psi** evidence (telepathy, precognition, mind–matter interaction), assessed by **meta-analysis**, show effects that materialism says are impossible.
- **Rupert Sheldrake, *Science Set Free*** — the philosophy-of-science case: modern science rests on **ten unproven dogmas**; nature's "laws" may be evolving **habits** sustained by **morphic resonance**, and minds extend beyond brains.
- **Robert Lanza, *Biocentrism*** — the physics/cosmology case: the **observer** brings reality into being; space and time are modes of perception; life and consciousness are the ground of the cosmos, not its products.

The cluster overlaps the wiki's consciousness sources — Donald Hoffman's interface theory ([[The Case Against Reality (Why Evolution Hid the Truth from Our Eyes)]]), Thomas Campbell's *My Big TOE*, and Itzhak Bentov's *Stalking the Wild Pendulum* — and is cited approvingly by the Hermetic-revival authors (see [[Mind and consciousness as fundamental to the universe]]).
