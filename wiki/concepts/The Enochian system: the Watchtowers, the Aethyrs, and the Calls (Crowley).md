## Definition from Source

The Enochian system, as presented in *The Practice of Enochian Magick* (Liber LXXXIV Vel Chanokh), is a comprehensive magical cosmology and operative framework received by [[John Dee (1527–1608-9)]] and his scryer Edward Kelley between 1582 and 1589, organized by the Golden Dawn, and adapted by Crowley. The system maps the universe onto four elemental **Watchtowers** (or Great Watch-Towers / Great Tables), thirty concentric **Aethyrs** (Aires), and a set of nineteen **Calls (Keys)** in the Angelic (Enochian) language used to access these realms.

## Description from Source

**The Watchtowers and the Great Tables**

Liber LXXXIV presents the Enochian universe as a square — the four Watch-Towers — surrounded by thirty concentric circles (the Aethyrs), whose radii increase in geometrical proportion. The four great Watch-Towers correspond to the four elements: East/Air, West/Water, North/Earth, and South/Fire. Each tablet is subdivided by a great cross into four lesser (sub-elemental) quadrants; each quadrant contains a Calvary Cross of ten squares governing its powers.

Within each Watch-Tower, Crowley's text identifies layered hierarchies of divine names and angelic governors. The Linea Spiritus Sancti of each tablet yields three Holy Names of God. A whorl around the tablet's centre yields the Great Elemental King (e.g., RAAGIOSL for Water, BATAIVAH for Air, ICZHHCAL for Earth, EDLPRNAA for Fire). Three lines of the central cross of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost yield six Seniors per tablet — totalling twenty-four "Elders" across all four tablets, as described in the Apocalypse. Below these are the Kerubim and, beneath them, four lesser angels per sub-elemental angle; and finally, triliteral names of demons or elementals drawn from sixteen remaining squares by formula. A fifth tablet, the Black Cross (or Central Tablet / Table of Union), is attributed to Spirit and governs the whole.

The text also describes the Sigillum Dei Aemeth — the great seal — whose Holy Sevenfold Table contains seven Names of God that "not even the Angels are able to pronounce," and from which angelic names (Filiae Bonitatis, Filii Lucis, and seven great planetary angels — SABATHIEL, ZEDEKIEL, MADIMIEL, SEMELIEL, NOGAHEL, CORABIEL, LEVANAEL) are derived.

**The Forty-Eight Calls (Keys)**

Part II of the text presents the Forty-Eight Calls or Keys, described as "Most Solemn Invocations." Eighteen of these function as elemental invocations: the First Key invokes the whole Tablet of Spirit; the Second Key, the file of Spirit within it; Keys 3–6 invoke the four elemental tablets as a whole; and Keys 7–18 invoke the twelve lesser sub-elemental angles of those tablets in the order Air, Water, Earth, Fire. Each Call is given first in the Angelic (Enochian) language and then in English translation. A nineteenth Call — the Call of the Thirty Aethyrs — is distinct, used to access the Aethyrs rather than the elemental Watchtowers.

The Angelic language itself is described as a true language with grammar and syntax — not merely a collection of barbarous names — delivered by angels specifically so that the magician could speak their tongue and access the unseen universe more efficiently.

**The Thirty Aethyrs**

Surrounding and interpenetrating the fourfold Watchtower universe are thirty concentric Aethyrs (Aires), each governed by a group of Enochian angels whose names are drawn from the Watch-Tower tablets. The Aethyrs represent ascending planes of spiritual reality, corresponding roughly to the ascending Sephiroth of the Qabalistic tree. The text notes that the Aethyrs are not ordered identically to the Tree of Life, but interpenetrate and surround it, so that "only in a few places do they coincide." The lowest (30th) Aethyr, TEX, is innermost and most accessible; the highest (1st), LIL, is the most exalted. Crowley notes that the final three Aethyrs (LIL, ARN, ZON) hold the letters I, L, A, N, R, Z, N, O as key-sigils of the most exalted interpretation of the Sephiroth, with I corresponding to Kether. The total number of angels across all thirty Aethyrs is 91, the numeration of Amen.
