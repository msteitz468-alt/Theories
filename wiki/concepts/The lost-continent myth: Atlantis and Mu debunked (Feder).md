## Definition from Source

Feder's debunking of **lost advanced continents** — Atlantis, Lemuria, and Mu. He traces Atlantis to
its sole origin: **Plato's dialogues (*Timaeus* and *Critias*)**, where it is a deliberate moral
fable (a cautionary tale of a proud sea-power destroyed by the gods), not a historical report — and
shows the modern Atlantis industry descends largely from Ignatius Donnelly's 1882 speculation.

## Description from Source

He argues there is **no geological or archaeological evidence** for a sunken advanced continent —
plate tectonics and ocean-floor mapping leave no room for one, and no Atlantean artifacts exist.
Lemuria/Mu likewise began as a discarded 19th-century zoogeographic hypothesis later inflated by
occult writers. This directly counters the wiki's lost-civilization sources — see
[[Maps of the Ancient Sea Kings (Hapgood, 1966)]], [[Fingerprints of the Gods (Hancock)]],
[[The Emerald Tablets of Thoth the Atlantean (Doreal)]] — and the synthesis
[[Where did civilization and advanced knowledge come from? Models compared across sources]].
