## Definition from Source

Per [[Hidden History (Jeffries)]], the events of September 11, 2001, were not what the official account describes. Jeffries presents the 9/11 Commission Report as a whitewash analogous to the Warren Commission Report, and argues that the evidence points to government foreknowledge at minimum, and controlled demolition and other forms of active deception at maximum.

## Description from Source

Chapter 9 of *Hidden History* constitutes Jeffries's most extended treatment of a single event. He characterizes the "official conspiracy theory" (that nineteen al-Qaeda hijackers alone planned and executed the attacks) as riddled with the same kinds of anomalies he catalogues throughout the book: implausible official timelines, suppressed witness testimony, suspicious evidence handling, convenient deaths of witnesses afterward, and a complaisant mainstream press that never questioned the government's version.

Key threads in Jeffries's account:

- **NORAD stand-down**: Jeffries argues that, given established protocols for scrambling fighters against errant aircraft (documented in a 1994 GAO report citing 1,518 intercept assignments in four years, and a 1998 document promising interception within ten minutes), the failure to intercept any of the four hijacked planes — especially Flight 77, which flew unimpeded into the Pentagon nearly eighty minutes after the first hijacking was identified — is inexplicable unless the stand-down was deliberate.
- **Controlled demolition of the WTC**: Jeffries cites multiple explosives experts, demolition specialists, and NYC firefighters who said the collapses looked like planned implosions. He notes that steel cannot melt until 1535°C, that the BBC reported fires at only 800°C, and that Building 7 (which was not struck by a plane) collapsed in a symmetrical freefall. He cites the testimonies of William Rodriguez, Mike Pecoraro, and others who reported sub-basement explosions before the plane impacts above.
- **Suspect identification**: Jeffries notes that FBI Director Robert Mueller acknowledged on September 20, 2002, that there was "no legal proof to prove the identities of the suicidal hijackers." He documents multiple reports that named hijackers were found alive in Saudi Arabia and elsewhere after 9/11.
- **Cell phone impossibility**: Jeffries argues that cell phone calls from the planes were technically impossible at cruising altitude in 2001, and cites FBI court filings from the 2006 Moussaoui trial showing Barbara Olson's call as "unconnected" and lasting "0 seconds," contradicting her husband's public account.
- **Flight 93**: Jeffries presents evidence the plane was shot down by the military (an anonymous Fort Meade whistleblower, the Somerset County coroner finding no wreckage or bodies), and notes that Cleveland news reports on September 11 identified a United plane — identified as Flight 93 — landing at Cleveland Hopkins Airport.
- **Political exploitation**: Jeffries quotes Council on Foreign Relations Senator Gary Hart, the day after 9/11, urging the president to use the "disaster" to carry out "a new world order," and the neocon think tank Project for a New American Century's September 2000 document calling for "a catastrophic and catalyzing event — like a new Pearl Harbor."
- **Suspicious deaths afterward**: Jeffries catalogues a cluster of deaths of people connected to 9/11 who questioned the official story, including Barry Jennings, Danny Jowenko, Beverly Eckert, and others — framing them within [[The political "body count" pattern (Jeffries)]].
