## Definition from Source

Sutton's organizing thesis is that the Bolshevik Revolution was supported by "an alliance of statists: statist revolutionaries and statist financiers" — a syndicate of Wall Street interests, concentrated physically at the address **120 Broadway, New York City**, that financed and aided the Bolsheviks for profit and monopoly power.

## Characterization in the Source

Per Sutton, "this activity in behalf of the Bolsheviks originated in large part from a single address: 120 Broadway, New York City." Located there or adjacent:
- The **Federal Reserve Bank of New York** (120 Broadway)
- **American International Corporation** (Morgan-controlled), the "vehicle for this pro-Bolshevik activity"
- Guaranty Securities Co. (120 Broadway; [[Guaranty Trust Company]] next door at 140)
- Weinberg & Posner (where the first Soviet "ambassador" Ludwig Martens had been vice president)
- Hunt, Hill & Betts (Charles B. Hill, negotiator in the 1912 Sun Yat-sen dealings)
- John MacGregor Grant Co. (financed by Aschberg and Guaranty Trust; on the Military Intelligence black list)
- The Guggenheims, the executive heart of General Electric, and the **Bankers Club** (top floor)

Sutton explicitly connects this to Carroll Quigley: 120 Broadway "appears to be the foreign counterpart of Carroll Quigley's claim that J.P. Morgan infiltrated the domestic left. Morgan also infiltrated the international left."
