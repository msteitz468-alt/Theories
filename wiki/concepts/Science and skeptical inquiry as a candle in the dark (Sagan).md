## Definition from Source

The umbrella thesis of *The Demon-Haunted World*: that **science, paired with disciplined skepticism,
is humanity's best defense ("a candle in the dark") against superstition, pseudoscience, and
manipulation.** Sagan argues that the same credulity that once produced witch-hunts now produces UFO
abductions, faith healers, and conspiracy beliefs, and that a self-correcting, evidence-demanding,
authority-questioning method is both the cure and the foundation of a free society.

## Description from Source

The book operationalizes the thesis through several tools and case studies (each on its own page):
the **[[The Baloney Detection Kit (Sagan)|Baloney Detection Kit]]** of critical-thinking tests; the
**[[The Dragon in My Garage — the unfalsifiability test (Sagan)|"Dragon in My Garage"]]** parable on
unfalsifiable claims; **[[Pareidolia and the Face on Mars (Sagan)|pareidolia]]** (seeing faces/patterns
in noise); the explanation of **[[Alien abduction as misperception, hallucination, and cultural narrative (Sagan)|alien abduction]]** via hallucination and sleep paralysis; the
**[[The witch-craze and the demon-haunted world (Sagan)|witch-craze]]** as a cautionary parallel; and
the **[[The marriage of skepticism and wonder (Sagan)|marriage of skepticism and wonder]]**.

As the wiki's principal skeptical source, this concept and its sub-pages are the standing counterpoint
to the corpus's UFO, occult, ancient-astronaut, and conspiracy material — see
[[What is the nonhuman intelligence behind contact? Models of its nature compared]] and
[[Competing models of the UFO phenomenon compared across sources]].
