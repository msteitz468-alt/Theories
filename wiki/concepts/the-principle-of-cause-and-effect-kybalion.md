## Definition from Source

The Principle of Cause and Effect is the Sixth of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]] as taught in [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]]. The Three Initiates state its maxim as:

> "Every Cause has its Effect; every Effect has its Cause; everything happens according to Law; Chance is but a name for Law not recognized; there are many planes of causation, but nothing escapes the Law." — THE KYBALION.

The Kybalion holds that the Universe is entirely governed by Law — that what is called "Chance" is merely a label for causes that are not perceived or understood, not evidence of events outside the causal order. Nothing happens by accident; every event is the consequent of a chain of preceding events stretching back beyond the ability of human minds to trace.

The Three Initiates distinguish between being a Cause and being an Effect. The majority of people, they teach, are "mere Pawns" on the chessboard of life — moved by environment, heredity, the wills of stronger individuals, and the pull of moods and desires. The Hermetic Master, by rising to a higher plane of Causation and mastering his own mental states (through the arts of Polarity and Neutralization), becomes a Mover — a Cause rather than a mere Effect on the lower planes. He does not escape Causation on the higher planes; he obeys higher laws while ruling on lower ones.
