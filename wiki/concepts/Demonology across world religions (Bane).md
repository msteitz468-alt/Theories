## Definition from Source

The umbrella/index concept for the *Encyclopedia of Demons*: Bane's cross-cultural survey of how the
world's religions, mythologies, and folklores conceive of demons. She frames the demon as the
recurring counterpart to the angel/divine — "we need demons and the evil they represent… if there is
not an external or internal struggle to overcome, how can there be any progression?" — and traces how
demons were historically *classified and "departmentalized"*: by element (air, earth, fire, water,
plus "the underground"), then by sin, planet, astrological sign, hour, and rank (king, count, etc.),
especially in the medieval/Renaissance grimoire tradition descending from the *Testament of Solomon*.
