## Definition / Characterization from Source

Ervin Laszlo's hypothesis of a fundamental, cosmos-wide **information field** — the **Akashic field** or "A-field" — that records, stores, and conveys the "in-formation" of everything that happens, and through which all things are nonlocally interconnected. Reviving the Sanskrit *Akasha* in the idiom of quantum physics, vacuum/zero-point field theory, and systems science, Laszlo argues that this field underlies and correlates matter, life, and mind, making the universe an **integral, in-formed whole** rather than a collection of separate objects.

For Laszlo the A-field explains otherwise anomalous **"akashic experiences"** — transpersonal connection, intuitive and healing experiences, past-life and near-death reports — as instances of consciousness accessing the field. The concept is his contribution to an emerging **post-materialist** paradigm, and sits alongside Radin's psi, Sheldrake's morphic fields, and Lanza's biocentrism ([[Post-materialist science]]).
