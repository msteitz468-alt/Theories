## Definition and Characterization from Source

In Monroe's framework, **thought control** is the disciplined suppression or direction of all mental activity — both voluntary and involuntary — during the vibrational state and the OBE itself. Monroe names it explicitly as a prerequisite for the separation process:

> "This factor is thought control. In the state of vibration, you are apparently subject to every thought, both willful and involuntary, that crosses your mind. Thus you must be as close to 'no thought' or 'single thought' (concentration) as possible. If one stray idea passes through your mind, you respond instantly, and sometimes in an undesirable manner."

Monroe states he has never achieved perfect thought control: "I suspect that one is never completely free of such misdirection. At least I have not been, which may account for the many inexplicable trips to places and people I do not know. They seem to be triggered by thoughts or ideas I didn't realize I had, below the conscious level. The only approach is to do the best you can."

## Core Properties

### Applies to Both Willed and Involuntary Thoughts

The critical feature of the Second State that makes thought control necessary is that the mind responds to all thoughts, not only deliberate ones. A "stray thought" — even one lasting "just one microsecond" — immediately diverts travel toward whatever the thought concerned. Monroe describes this as the "automatic navigational system" being "too accurate."

### Thought Control as Navigation Control

In practice, thought control and directional control in the non-physical state are the same thing. Monroe's instruction for visiting a specific person requires holding only one clear mental image — the personality of the target — without any competing thought. The moment attention wavers, the traveler is redirected: "Let one small stray thought emerge dominantly for just one microsecond, and your course is deviated."

Monroe identifies "the inability to control destination" caused by thought interruption as "the chief barrier to the production of consistency and repeatability" in his experiments.

### Relationship to the Vibrational State

Thought control is introduced specifically as part of the separation process — once the vibrational state has been achieved and the practitioner is about to separate from the physical body. The instructions state: "achieve the vibrational state, and maintain complete control of your thought processes." Monroe cautions that during local familiarization exercises, one should "keep mental concentration on a single purpose without stray thought patterns."

### "Thought as Force" in Locale II

In Locale II, the significance of thought control becomes still greater, because thought is not merely a navigation tool but the operative principle of the entire environment: "Locale II is a state of being where that which we label thought is the wellspring of existence… what you think is the matrix of your action, situation, and position in this greater reality." In Locale II, uncontrolled emotions and desires — including those below conscious awareness — automatically position the traveler among entities or environments of a corresponding nature ("Like attracts like").

Monroe warns: "be absolutely sure of the results you desire, and constantly in control of the thoughts you engender."

### Thought Carry-Overs From Physical Life

Monroe also notes that habitual thought patterns acquired in physical life persist in the Second State and create minor but real complications — for example, automatically reaching for a wallet that does not exist, or "thinking" one is clothed and thereby becoming so. These habitual carry-overs reinforce the need for thought awareness: "The method of disposal is to recognize them, one by one. Once they are identified they are no longer bothersome."
