# Christian demons and the fallen angels (Bane)

## Definition from Source

According to Bane's *Encyclopedia of Demons in World Religions and Cultures*, Christian demonology built the most elaborate and taxonomically precise framework for demonic classification in Western history. It emerged gradually: Satan appears as an "instigator and accuser of man" in the Book of Job (700 B.C.E.); by the second-century apocryphal *Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs* he had become the arch-nemesis of humanity. In the fourth century, Saint Paul laid out the hierarchy of the heavenly host, and it was not until the fifth century that church authorities began systematically mapping a corresponding infernal hierarchy.

Bane identifies the **Fallen Angel** as the defining concept of Christian demonology. Fallen angels are angels who have been cast out of the grace of God for sin or transgression. She catalogues three spheres of original angels (Seraphim/Cherubim/Thrones; Dominions/Virtues/Powers; Principalities/Archangels/Angels) and notes that the rebellion was led by Lucifer — "the first and most powerful of the angels ever created" — whose sin was pride. The text that follows the fall describes God cursing and exiling the rebel angels, in some cases imprisoning them physically; their half-breed offspring with human women, the Nephilim ("to fall"), were destroyed in the great flood. Bane lists 263 named angelic beings collected from multiple traditions who are in a fallen state.

Medieval and Renaissance demonology assigned fallen angels specific ranks (king, prince, duke, count, marquis, earl, president), planets, zodiacal signs, hours of power, and demonic specialties. Francis Barrett's *The Magus* (1801) codified nine divisions: False Gods, Spirits of Lies, Vessels of Iniquity, Revengers of Evil (under Asmodeus), Deluders (under Satan), Aerial Powers (under Meririm), Furies (under Abaddon), Accusers (under Astaroth), and Tempters (under Mammon).

The word "demon" itself underwent a transformation through Christianity: originally a neutral Greek force that could be good or evil, it became under Christian influence "a being of pure evil" under control of the Devil.

## Notable Demons

**Lucifer** — Emperor of Hell and the first Fallen Angel in Christian demonology; formerly of the Order of Cherubim (also described as a Seraphim), he succumbed to pride and led the rebellion against God. His name is Latin for "light bearer." He holds the ranks of Minister of Justice of Hell, Prince of Darkness, Prince of Demons, and Prince of Pandemonium. He appears as an attractive male youth and tempts men by appealing to their pride. Most powerful on Mondays at dawn.

**Satan** — In Christian tradition (distinct from Lucifer in some medieval schemas), he is ranked as a Prince of Hell in the class of Deluders. He is the principle of resistance to the divine and the adversary of humanity; demonic hierarchies place him in command over "Deluders" — demons who mimic miracles and work with witches.

**Beelzebub** — Originally the chief god of the Philistine city of Ekron (Baal-zebub, "lord of the fly"), he was absorbed into Christianity as prime minister and sub-prince of Hell, a Fallen Angel formerly of the Order of Cherubim, and demon of gluttony, luck, money, and pride. Described as an enormous fly, a monstrous goat, or exceedingly tall with bat wings, lion tail, and fur-covered body; his howl is wolflike. He was blamed for the demonic possessions at Aix-en-Provence (1611) and Loudun (1634).

**Asmodeus** — A Fallen Angel formerly from the Order of Thrones and an Archdemon with dominion over the hierarchy Golab; demon of impurity and lechery; sub-prince of Hell. One of the most cross-cultural demons, appearing in Jewish, Persian, Zoroastrian, and numerous other demonologies. Described with three heads (bull, ram, man vomiting fire), a serpent tail, webbed feet, and riding a dragon. He invented carousels, dancing, music, plays, and recreational drug use.

**Belial** — One of the earliest of the Fallen Angels (said to be the very next created after Lucifer), a Fallen Angel of the Order of Seraphim and the Order of Virtues; he persuaded Lucifer to rebel and was the first cast out of Heaven. Demon of arrogance, deceit, hostility, and lies; described as a beautiful angel riding on a chariot of flame. Commands eighty legions. Tempts men to disloyalty and rebellion, women to gossip in church.

**Astaroth** — A grand duke and treasurer of Hell commanding forty legions; formerly a Fallen Angel and prince of the Order of Thrones. Originally an ancient Phoenician goddess (Astarte/Astoreth), she was reimagined in medieval demonology as an ugly angel riding a dragon and carrying a snake. The demon of sloth and accusation (leader of the Accusers division in Barrett's schema); willingly answers questions on the past, present, and future but gives off a deadly stench.

**Mammon** — The demon of avarice in medieval demonology; prince of tempters and Hell's ambassador to England. A Fallen Angel formerly of the Order of Thrones; described as looking wolflike. Commands all tempters — demons said to reside in every man.

**Abaddon** — One of the few demons named in the Old Testament (mentioned five times in the King James Bible); demon of anger, hate, vengeance, and war. Commands the sixth House of Hell and its demonic locust army, which will torment non-believers during the Rapture. Described as a gigantic figure veiled in black covered with whirling wheels, also as snakelike with a belly full of fire. Leader of the Furies division.

**Belphegor** — Originally a Moabite god of sexual abandon worshipped on Mount Phegor; absorbed into medieval grimoires as a Fallen Angel formerly of the Order of Principalities. Archdemon of the Togarini and ambassador to France; demon of discovery, laziness, pride, riches, sloth, and vanity. Appears as a beautiful young woman or as a naked hideous demon with a beard, horns, and sharp nails. Accepts offerings of excrement.

**Behemoth** — The nocturnal demon of indulgence in medieval demonology, holding the ranks of caretaker of wine cellars, grand cupbearer of the royal household, and steward of the devil's winery. Described as a monstrous elephant with bearlike feet; can also appear as crocodile, hippopotamus, or whale. Invoked during exorcism and cases of collective possession.

**Meririm** — An Aerial Devil and Fallen Angel; the chief of the aerial powers and lord of lightning and thunder in Hell, and one of the seven Princes of Hell. Commands the prince of air and pestilence division. Most powerful during the noon hour.
