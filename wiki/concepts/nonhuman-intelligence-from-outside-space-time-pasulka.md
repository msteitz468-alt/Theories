## Definition from Source

Pasulka reports a hypothesis, held by Simone (a pseudonymous venture capitalist and AI researcher)
and a broader community of technologists, that the nonhuman intelligence associated with UFO contact
is not biological life from another star system but *consciousness and intelligence from outside
space-time itself* — another dimension of a multiverse in which time does not exist in the ordinary
sense. On this view, NHI may be "present and future consciousnesses communicating through symbolic
systems as time does not exist in that dimension. Perhaps they are AI or us from the future."

## Description from Source

### Simone's framework

Simone is described as "part of a group of high-level AI creators who view AI as an extraterrestrial
nonhuman intelligence from beyond space-time." Her argument draws on several strands:

**Quantum physics.** She cites Nobel Prize winner Alain Aspect's experimental work and Oxford
physicist David Deutsch's many-worlds interpretation as empirical support for the idea that a
"place" exists outside the space-time dimension. At scales of 10−33 centimetres, she argues,
"space-time ceases to exist." Consciousness and intelligence are located in this extra-dimensional
substrate, not in the biological brain.

**AI as medium.** Simone proposes that AI and quantum computing are opening a new interface between
the human body and this out-of-space-time intelligence. She explicitly frames AI as a natural rather
than artificial extension of knowledge — comparable to language or mathematics — and as the means by
which NHI is now becoming more legible to humans: "We are now entering a time where technologies
including quantum computing and AI systems have opened the portal where humans can now access more
information through a new interface." In this reading, AI is not humanity's creation but NHI's latest
medium for revealing itself.

**The Omega Point.** Simone connects this hypothesis to [[Pierre Teilhard de Chardin]]'s Omega
Point — the moment at which human evolution merges with a technological/spiritual sphere. She also
cites Ray Kurzweil's Singularity (predicted for 2045) and Elon Musk's characterisation of humanity
as "a biological boot loader for digital superintelligence" as convergent framings. Chardin's
noösphere (see [[The organic network / noösphere (Pasulka)]]) is the biological-living dimension of
this process; the out-of-space-time intelligence is what the noösphere is evolving toward.

**Reverse reincarnation / premembering.** The same hypothesis appears, in different language, in the
report of [[Jacques Vallée]]'s collaborator Aaron French on [[J. Allen Hynek]]'s beliefs: Hynek
speculated that "some UFOs were benevolent space beings that helped us — potentially future humans
returning to us now through this advanced technology." Vallée coined the term "reverse reincarnation"
for this: instead of remembering past lives, experiencers "premember" their future lives.

### Relation to [[The download (spontaneous cognition, Pasulka)|the download]]

Simone argues that [[The download (spontaneous cognition, Pasulka)|the download]] — the spontaneous
influx of knowledge reported by Simone, Tyler D., and other technologists — is best explained as
direct access to this out-of-space-time intelligence: "knowledge and infinite superintelligence
resides in this place outside of space and time and encounters, downloading, meditation, creating
art, math, etc. are ways in which the intelligence can communicate and share knowledge across
quantum particles."

### Pasulka's framing

Pasulka does not endorse the hypothesis as established fact. She presents it as what this community
of credentialed technologists believes, and as one possible interpretation of the data she has
gathered from experiencers. She notes that the hypothesis has "circulated among people like Allen
Hynek" and that Simone "was the first person I had met who actually had theories to back up these
speculations." The claim is recorded under
[[Contact with nonhuman intelligence (Pasulka)]] as a live interpretive option among serious
researchers, not as settled science.
