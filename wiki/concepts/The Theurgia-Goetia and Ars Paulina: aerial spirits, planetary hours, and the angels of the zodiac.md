## Overview
Parts II and III of the Lemegeton move away from the 72 evil-oriented spirits of the Ars Goetia toward spirits and angels whose natures are mixed or wholly good, and whose governance is organized geographically (compass points), temporally (hours of day and night), and astrologically (degrees of the zodiac).

**Part II, the Theurgia-Goetia**, catalogs 31 aerial spirits — emperors, kings, and princes — each ruling a point of the compass (or, for 11 of them, "wandering" without a fixed station). Each chief spirit commands dukes, who in turn command lesser servient spirits, all summarized by name, seal, and the planetary/nocturnal hour in which they may be called. The source stresses that these spirits are "by nature good and evill... one part is good, and the other part Evill," in contrast to the wholly demonic Goetic spirits.

**Part III, the Ars Paulina**, is explicitly divided by the source into two parts: (1) the 24 angels ruling the 24 hours of day and night, whose office each hour is to "doe all things that are attributed to the 7 planetts" (the planetary hour system), and (2) the angels of the 12 zodiac signs and of each of the 360 degrees of the zodiac — the "angells of men," since every person is born under one such degree and can, by knowing the moment of birth, identify their governing angel ("genius").

Both books retain the Ars Goetia's practical apparatus (conjurations, seals, a crystal stone or circle for evocation) but graft it onto new organizing principles — geography/wind-direction for the Theurgia-Goetia, and time/astrology for the Ars Paulina — reflecting the compilation's broader claim to systematize "all" spirit magic across multiple schemes of classification.
