## Definition / Characterization from Source

John Keel's alternative to the extraterrestrial hypothesis. Rather than "shy strangers from some other galaxy," he proposes **ultraterrestrials** — "beings and forces which coexist with us but are on another time frame; that is, they operate outside the limits of our space-time continuum yet have the ability to cross over into our reality." This other world "is not a place… but is a state of energy." Keel holds such intelligences (Ivan Sanderson's "OINTs") have always been present on Earth — described in the Bible and in cave paintings — and manifest in whatever guise the era's beliefs supply: gods, angels, demons, fairies, and now spacemen and Men in Black.

Crucially, the phenomenon is **belief-driven and self-escalating**: "Once you have established a belief, the phenomenon adjusts its manifestations to support that belief and thereby escalate it. If you believe in the devil he will surely come striding down your road one rainy night and ask to use your phone." Keel relates this to "tulpas" (Tibetan thought-forms), recurring "window areas," and a percipient minority able to perceive beyond the normal visual spectrum.
