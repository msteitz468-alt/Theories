## Definition from Source

A slide is a distorted picture of reality held in the mind — a mental image or belief about oneself or the world that filters perception and, through [[Outer Intention]], is gradually realized in physical reality. Zeland uses the metaphor of a slide in a video projector: the light is the world, the slide is one's worldview/model, and the image on the screen is one's perception.

"A slide is a distorted picture of reality." (Ch. VIII)
"A slide is something you carry around in your own mind that others are not." (Ch. VIII Summary)
"Importance lies at the foundation of all slides." (Ch. VIII Summary)

## Negative Slides (from source)

A negative slide arises when a person concentrates attention on unwanted aspects of themselves — physical flaws, inadequacy, guilt. Its mechanism:

1. The slide lives in the person's mind, not in external reality — others do not share it
2. A negative slide is generated by unity between the heart and mind around a negative self-image
3. Because [[Outer Intention]] works flawlessly under conditions of heart–mind unity, the outer intention catches the person and carries them to a sector where the slide's content is expressed to its full extent
4. The transition is gradual and consistent: "light strokes... become sharper and eventually end up 'larger than life'" — if a person fears being plump they will continue to gain weight; if a birthmark bothers them it will get bigger
5. The behavioral pattern continues until the person stops attributing importance to the slide or switches to a positive slide

**Projections** (from source): the slide also causes people to project its content onto others around them. A person with a slide of inadequacy will perceive others as seeing their inadequacy. Unfair accusations from others are explained as the accuser's own slide projecting onto the accused.

## Positive Slides (from source)

A positive slide is a mental image of oneself or the world as one wishes it to be. It works by the same mechanism as the negative slide but in the constructive direction:

- Foundation: "Is it important to you to make a good impression? If so, this is the foundation of a positive slide." (Ch. VIII)
- Technique: focus on the most positive sides of one's personality and other desired attributes; the slide need not be static — it can represent movement, manner, charm
- Rule: "One thing you must avoid doing is creating an image that is a copy of someone else you believe already has the qualities you wish to embody! Your slide must be truly yours and not a copy of someone else's." (Ch. VIII)
- Working with the slide: "Do not look at the slide from the standpoint of the objective observer; delve into it, live it, at least in the virtual world." (Ch. VIII)
- Persistence: "A slide will only bring results if it is reproduced systematically over a period of time."
- Heart–mind integration: outer intention will not realize the slide immediately; it requires time; eventually the slide "becomes an integrated part of your personality, at which point it will cease to exist" once the goal is achieved

## Slides and Comfort Zone (from source)

Positive slides are the primary mechanism for expanding the [[Comfort Zone (Transurfing)]] to include previously implausible goals: "positive slides can help include the implausible into your comfort zone." (Ch. VIII)
