## Definition from Source

The Principle of Rhythm is the Fifth of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]] as presented in [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]]. The Three Initiates state its maxim as:

> "Everything flows out and in; everything has its tides; all things rise and fall; the pendulum-swing manifests in everything; the measure of the swing to the right, is the measure of the swing to the left; rhythm compensates." — THE KYBALION.

The Kybalion teaches that in everything there is a measured, to-and-from motion — a pendulum-like swing between the two poles established by [[The Principle of Polarity (Kybalion)]]. This Rhythmic swing operates on the physical plane (tides, seasons, the rise and fall of civilizations, the life cycles of suns and worlds) and equally on the mental plane (moods, emotions, periods of enthusiasm followed by depression).

The Principle carries with it what the Kybalion calls the **Law of Compensation**: the measure of the swing in one direction is equal to the measure of the swing in the other. High capacity for joy entails high capacity for suffering; what is gained on one side is balanced by what is lost on the other.

The Hermetic Masters are said to have discovered the **Law of Neutralization** — a practice of rising mentally to a Higher Plane of Consciousness so that the backward swing of the pendulum occurs on the Unconscious Plane and does not register in waking awareness. The adept thus "escapes" the effects of Rhythm without violating or destroying the Principle itself.
