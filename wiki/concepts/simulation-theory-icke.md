## Definition and Characterisation (from the Source)

As presented in David Icke's *Everything You Need to Know But Have Never Been Told* (2018), "simulation theory" as Icke applies it holds that physical reality is not solid or material but is an information system decoded by human brain-consciousness into the holographic illusion of a material world. The simulation operates on a principle identical to a video game: underlying waveform information is decoded (rendered) into the experience of being "in" a three-dimensional physical environment — but there is no more solid substance to this world than there is in a video game.

Key conceptual components:
- **The Upper Aeon**: The original, perfect, non-physical state of Infinite Awareness and pure potential
- **The Lower Aeon**: The simulation — a defective copy of the Upper Aeon generated by the Demiurge (Archontic distortion)
- **Waveform information field**: The underlying "reality" before it is decoded; the Gnostic equivalent of quantum wave functions
- **The holographic level**: The decoded, apparent physical world that humans experience
- **The Cosmic Internet**: Icke's term for the information field from which consciousness downloads and decodes reality
- **The Program**: The perception-programming system that keeps humans locked within the five-sense band of the simulation, preventing connection with Infinite Awareness

The simulation has identifiable "rules of the code" equivalent to computer game physics: laws of nature that never change regardless of location in the universe; mathematical structure that fully describes reality; a constant speed of light regardless of source.

Icke distinguishes his version from mainstream simulation theory (Bostrom, Musk) by adding the Archontic control element: the simulation was deliberately hijacked and distorted by the Archontic force to serve as a perceptual prison.
