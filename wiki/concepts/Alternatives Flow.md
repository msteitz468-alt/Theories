## Definition from Source

The alternatives flow refers to the streams of optimal cause-and-effect pathways that run through the [[Alternatives Space]] along lines of least resistance. Zeland asserts that these streams exist as inherent features of the information field's structure, and that all problems resolve themselves naturally if the person allows the flow rather than fighting it.

"When you go with the flow the world meets you halfway." (Ch. VI epigraph)

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Organized by least resistance**: "Alternatives flow organized along paths of least resistance (optimal cause-and-effect streams)" (Ch. VI)
- **Inherent optimality**: "Optimality inherent in information field structure; problems resolve themselves if flow allowed"
- **Accessible via non-importance**: importance jolts the mind out of the optimal stream; reducing importance restores access
- **Principle of harmonious movement**: "Everything should be done by the easiest and simplest means possible" (Ch. VI)
- **Pendulums cannot access it**: "Pendulums abhor streams in alternatives flow (minimal energy expenditure = no pendulum fodder)" — streams in the alternatives flow consume minimal energy, leaving nothing for pendulums

## Going With the Flow (from source)

The source distinguishes between going with the flow and passive drift:
- "Mind's greatest error = strives to control the flow itself rather than its own movement within the flow" (Ch. VI, Letting Go section)
- "By being willing to release control you will acquire more control over a situation than you had before"
- The correct relationship is to control one's own movement and attitude within the flow, not the flow itself

## Signs and the Flow (from source)

[[Guiding Signs]] indicate possible bends or turns in the alternatives flow — moments when a shift to a different life line has been initiated. Signs appear specifically when such a shift is underway, not as general omens.
