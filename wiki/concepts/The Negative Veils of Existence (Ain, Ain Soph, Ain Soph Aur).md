# The Negative Veils of Existence (Ain, Ain Soph, Ain Soph Aur)

## Definition from Source
Fortune describes the Qabalists as recognizing "four planes of manifestation, and three planes of unmanifestation, or Negative Existence." These three are, in order (deepest/most remote first): **Ain**, Negativity; **Ain Soph**, the Limitless; and **Ain Soph Aur**, the Limitless Light — "out of this last that Kether is concentrated." They are called the Three Veils of Negative Existence because they "depend back from Kether," functioning as "algebraic symbols that enable us to think of that which transcends thought, and which at the same time hide that which they represent; they are the masks of transcendent realities." She stresses that the Veils are "philosophical conventions" corresponding to "human limitations, not... cosmic conditions": the Qabalist does not found his system on the unknowable Absolute directly, but draws a veil at the point where the mind must stop, then works forward from Kether — the first "point within the reach of finite consciousness" — rather than trying to reason forward from the Absolute itself.

Negativity (Ain) implies "Being or existence of a nature which we cannot comprehend... a form of being which, according to our concepts of existence, does not exist, and yet... exists according to its own idea of existence." Though Negative Existence cannot be known directly, "all that we know as existing has its roots" in it, so its effects can be known "at one remove," as electricity's effects are known though its nature is not. Ain Soph Aur, the Limitless Light, has been likened by Mathers to "a circle whose centre is every-where and whose circumference is nowhere"; Kether "crystallises out" of it as a heated, saturated liquid crystallizes sugar upon cooling.
