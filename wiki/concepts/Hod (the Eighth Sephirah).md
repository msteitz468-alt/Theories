# Hod (the Eighth Sephirah)

## Definition from Source
Hod, meaning Glory, is the eighth Sephirah, at the foot of the Pillar of Severity. Fortune gives its attributions (Chapter XXIII) as: God-Name, Elohim Tzabaoth, "the God of Hosts"; Archangel, Michael; Order of Angels, Beni Elohim ("Sons of God"); Mundane Chakra, Kokab (Mercury); Magical Image, "an hermaphrodite"; Spiritual Experience, "Vision of Splendour"; Virtue, Truthfulness; Vice, Falsehood and Dishonesty; correspondence in the microcosm, the loins and legs; symbols, Names and Versicles and the Apron; Tarot cards, the four Eights (Swiftness, Abandoned success, Shortened force, Prudence).

Hod is the reflection of Chesed (Gedulah) via Binah, and is "the mean of the Primordial," representing form and intellect where Netzach represents force and instinct. It is "essentially the Sphere of formal magic," the sphere in which the magician's mind formulates the forms that his will, drawing on the force of Netzach, ensouls. The Archangel Michael, trampling the serpent, symbolizes the restrictive "prudence" of Hod which "shortens" primitive force. The supreme virtue of this sphere of the concrete mind is truthfulness.
