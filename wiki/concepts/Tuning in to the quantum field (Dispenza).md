## Definition from Source

Dispenza's term for the meditative practice of withdrawing attention entirely from the physical world — from body, people, objects, places, and linear time — so that consciousness "unfolds" as pure awareness into what he calls the quantum or unified field: an invisible, immaterial field of energy and information that he claims exists beyond space and time and contains all possible futures as electromagnetic frequencies.

## Description from Source

Dispenza teaches that ordinary waking consciousness is anchored to the known: a repeating loop of familiar thoughts, emotions, neurological networks, and stress hormones that keep a person broadcasting the same electromagnetic signature and drawing the same future. The path out of this loop is to become "no body, no one, no thing, no where, in no time" — a state he says is entered by slowing brain waves from high-range beta (aroused/stressed) down through alpha and into theta, withdrawing attention from every element of the outer material world, and lingering in what he describes as an "infinite black space" beyond the senses.

In that state, Dispenza claims the observer merges with the unified field, which he frames as a self-organizing intelligence that governs the laws of nature and holds all possible realities simultaneously as electromagnetic frequencies. Because the quantum field contains "all possibilities," he teaches that consciousness can select a desired potential — a new job, a healed body, a mystical experience — by combining a **clear intention** (the electrical charge sent into the field) with an **elevated emotion** such as love, gratitude, or joy (the magnetic charge). Together, intention and elevated emotion produce an electromagnetic signature; when that signature achieves a "vibrational match" with the chosen potential in the field, Dispenza teaches that the experience is drawn to the practitioner without effortful physical action.

He cites quantum physics concepts — wave function, the observer effect, electron behavior in the double-slit experiment — to support the claim that consciousness collapses infinite fields of energy into particles (experienced realities). He also references René Peoc'h's baby-chick/random-event-generator study as evidence that intention can influence matter at a distance.

Dispenza acknowledges that critics call his framework pseudoscience and that the quantum-field metaphor is contested by mainstream physicists. The book presents it as teaching, supported by his own workshop EEG measurements (e.g., showing 18% faster entry to alpha states and 62% improvement in delta/high-beta ratios over four days in a 2016 Tacoma workshop of 117 participants).

The corresponding meditation — described at the end of Chapter 3 — guides participants to rest attention across body energy centers, then expand awareness to infinite space, become no body in the present moment, hold a symbolic letter representing the desired potential, and sustain elevated emotion before surrendering the creation to a "greater mind."
