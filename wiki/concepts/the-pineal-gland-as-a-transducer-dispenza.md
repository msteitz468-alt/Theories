## Definition from Source

In Chapter 12 of *Becoming Supernatural*, Dispenza presents the pineal gland — a small, pinecone-shaped neuroendocrine gland at the center of the brain — as a "crystalline superconductor" capable of receiving and converting electromagnetic signals from beyond the ordinary senses into vivid inner experiences, operating analogously to an antenna and transducer.

### Scientific grounding Dispenza invokes

Dispenza cites a research abstract noting that the pineal gland contains tiny calcite crystals (1–20 microns) that exhibit **piezoelectric properties**: when mechanical stress is applied, they generate an electrical charge. He argues that the breathing and muscle-squeezing techniques in his meditations increase intrathecal cerebrospinal fluid pressure, compressing these crystals and triggering a pulsating electromagnetic field. This converts the gland into an "antenna" capable of tuning in to electromagnetic frequencies beyond visible light — what he identifies with the time-space or quantum unified field.

As a **transducer**, the pineal gland then converts those incoming frequencies into meaningful inner imagery and experiences, just as a radio antenna converts an electromagnetic signal into audible sound.

### Chemical cascade

Dispenza argues that as the activated pineal gland picks up higher frequencies, it transmutes melatonin into a cascade of exotic metabolites:
- **Benzodiazepines** — calm the analytical mind and suppress the amygdala's fear/survival circuitry
- **Pinolines** — powerful antioxidants, described as anti-aging and anti-cancer
- A "hibernation molecule" — slows metabolism and bodily drives
- A **phosphorescent, bioluminescent chemical** — amplifies nervous system energy, potentially explaining the high-amplitude brain measurements Dispenza reports in advanced students
- **DMT (dimethyltryptamine)** — described as among the most powerful hallucinogens known; Dispenza equates this to the active ingredient in ayahuasca, and claims the pineal produces the "whole blend" rather than DMT alone

The pituitary gland is described as a co-conspirator: once the pineal releases its upgraded metabolites, the posterior pituitary releases **oxytocin** (the "bonding hormone," associated with love, forgiveness, and suppression of the amygdala's fear response) and **vasopressin** (which raises the body's water content, creating a better conductor for high frequencies, and stabilizes the endocrine cascade).

### Experiential outcomes

Dispenza teaches that a properly activated pineal gland produces multisensory inner experiences — colors beyond the visible spectrum, luminous geometric patterns (sacred/divine geometry, cymatics-like standing waves), time dilation, encounters with non-physical beings, out-of-body experiences, and what he calls "interdimensional" realities. He frames these as the "third eye" or "inner vision" referenced in ancient traditions (e.g., Jesus's "If thine eye be single, thy whole body shall be full of light"; the Egyptian Eye of Horus; Hermes/the caduceus).

Dispenza's own definition: "The pineal gland is a crystalline superconductor that sends, as well as receives, information through the transduction of energetic vibrational signals (frequency beyond the senses, also known as the quantum field) and translates it into biological tissue (the brain and the mind) in the form of meaningful imagery, the same way as an antenna translates different channels onto a TV screen."
