## Concept Summary from Source

"The Gray Man" is the pseudonym adopted by a real but anonymized senior government scientist — head of a laboratory at a major research agency — who serves as Pasulka's central experiencer in Chapters 3 and 4 of *Encounters*. He chose the pseudonym because he despises self-promotion and social media, preferring to remain invisible. He is Australian, a former surfer from the New South Wales Central Coast, and a materials scientist with expertise in aeronautics.

The Gray Man's case is paradigmatic for Pasulka because his contact events span multiple ontological categories simultaneously: UFO sightings, angelic visitation, gray-alien encounters, poltergeist activity, sleep paralysis, lucid-dream visitations, synchronicities, and healing prayer. He does not interpret these events as extraterrestrial contact but as religious and supernatural encounters — primarily involving the warfare between good and evil. His framework places UFOs within a demonic or religious interpretive lens rather than an extraterrestrial-spacecraft one.

**Key contact events reported by Gray Man (all pseudonymous account):**

1. A luminous angelic being — later identified with Saint Michael the Archangel — appeared inches from his face, assessed him, gave him armor and a sword, and directed him to fight and defeat a dark evil figure. He insists this was not a dream.
2. A UFO sighting on Empire Bay Drive, New South Wales (likely 1994 Brisbane Waters / Gosford mass sighting), in which a large luminous object descended at extreme speed and lit the sky like day.
3. A hooded masked man materialized beside his bed; Gray Man lunged at the figure, which dissolved.
4. Three small gray beings ("gray aliens," ~3 ft tall, large black eyes) appeared in a state he describes as not-dream; he commanded them to leave in the name of Jesus.
5. Poltergeist activity in his home: a door opening and closing repeatedly; his and his severely autistic son's clothing found reversed and rebuttoned with buttons still done up in the morning — events he takes as evidence of active nonhuman interference.
6. A cascade of synchronicities involving Saint Michael (paintings, a garden statue, a therapist's correspondence with the archangel) that converged the same week he first had his angelic vision, culminating in the discovery of a decades-old Saint Michael prayer card at the bottom of a box — given to him by a deeply troubled friend he had once prayed over in a maximum-security psychiatric facility.

Pasulka uses the Gray Man's case to argue that the religious and supernatural dimensions of UFO encounters are data that mainstream research ignores, and that Vallée's information-technology interpretive framework — in which the phenomena function as a control system operating on myth and culture — provides a more adequate lens than the extraterrestrial-spacecraft hypothesis. See [[Jacques Vallée]].

The Gray Man also introduces the idea that scientists in classified aeronautics programs may privately hold deeply religious interpretations of the phenomena. He references Jack Parsons's ritual magic, Wernher von Braun's late-life Christian conversion, and the Invisible College of scientists who (per Pasulka) practice Catholic and Episcopal devotions. See [[J. Allen Hynek]].
