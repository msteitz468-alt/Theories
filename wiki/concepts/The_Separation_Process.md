## Definition and Characterization from Source

The Separation Process, detailed in Chapter 17 of *[[Journeys Out of the Body]]*, is [[Robert Monroe (1915–1995)|Robert A. Monroe]]'s systematic methodology for consciously disassociating the [[Concept: The Second Body|Second Body]] from the physical form while maintaining full awareness. Monroe emphasizes that success requires prior achievement of the [[Concept: Vibrational State|vibrational state]] and strict control of thought processes.

The fundamental principle is maintaining mental control: "In the state of vibration, you are apparently subject to every thought, both willful and involuntary, that crosses your mind. Thus you must be as close to 'no thought' or 'single thought' (concentration) as possible."

## Progressive Stages of Training

### Stage 1: Release of Extremities

This serves as initial familiarization with the Second Body without full commitment.

**Procedure:**
1. Achieve relaxation and vibrational state
2. Work with either right or left hand and arm, one at a time
3. Reach for an object beyond the physical reach (floor, wall, door, etc.)
4. Reach outward in the direction the arm is pointing—not upward or downward, but as if stretching
5. Gently push the hand farther if nothing is felt initially, continuing as if stretching the arm
6. When the hand encounters an object, examine it with touch, noting cracks, grooves, unusual details
7. Push against the object with fingertips to test resistance
8. Gently overcome resistance and push hand completely through the object
9. Identify the second object encountered on the far side
10. Carefully withdraw the hand back through the first object

**Verification Method:**
1. Decrease vibrations by thinking of the physical body and opening physical eyes
2. Make written notation of both objects felt, with precise location relative to physical hand position
3. Note small details observed during the vibrational state
4. Physically compare hand-reached objects with descriptions made in the Second Body state
5. Verify distances (should be impossible to reach with physical hand)
6. Check alignment—does a line from physical hand position through first object to second object form a straight line?

Monroe emphasizes: "If your answers are affirmative, you have had your first success. If the facts do not check out, try again another day. Almost without qualification, if you have produced the vibrational state, you can perform this exercise."

### Stage 2: Hand and Arm Conjunction Test

After demonstrating Second Body arm independence:

1. Lie on back, arms at sides or on chest
2. Gently lift arms without looking at them
3. Touch fingers together casually, remembering sensory results
4. Look at arms with closed eyes
5. With successful execution, observe both physical and non-physical arms simultaneously
6. Physical arms appear at rest at sides or chest
7. Sensory impressions align with non-physical arms positioned above the physical body

**Critical Step:** Always return non-physical arms to full conjunction with physical counterparts before discontinuing the vibrational state, "although there may be no severe aftereffect if this is not done."

### Stage 3: Local Disassociation and Familiarization

Monroe emphasizes the critical importance of remaining close to the physical body during initial separation attempts. "Whatever you may feel emotionally, keep in close proximity to the physical."

**Objectives:**
- Maintain step-by-step familiarity
- Perceive exactly what is taking place
- Avoid uncomfortable situations from uncontrolled distant journeys

**Procedure:**
1. Achieve vibrational state and maintain complete thought control
2. Remain separated no more than three feet from physical body, hovering directly above
3. Focus on the mental concept of being close to maintain proper range
4. Practice disassociation and return repeatedly (3-4 exercises minimum)
5. Return to physical each time (see Return Techniques below)
6. Note exact time on clock after each return to verify conscious continuity

**Success Milestone:** Complete cycle when able to separate, return to physical, sit up and check time, separate again, and return second time—all without loss of conscious continuity.

### Stage 4: Extended Distance Familiarization

Once comfortable with local separation:

1. Separate to slightly farther distance (up to ten feet)
2. Apply same procedures as Stage 3
3. Concentrate on single purpose without stray thoughts
4. At greater distance, mentally command vision: "think of seeing, that you can see"
5. Vision emerges without sensation of eye-opening
6. Initially vision may be dim, as in half-light or myopic—sharpness develops with use
7. Visually examine room from separated position
8. Mentally move slightly in controlled directions
9. Test mobility with arms and legs while hovering
10. Stay within prescribed range of physical body

### Stage 5: Handling Powerful Desires

Monroe identifies "the greatest problem you may face at the moment" during extended familiarization: overwhelming subjective desires.

Monroe enumerates these desires:
- Freedom (release from physical limitations and gravity)
- Sexual contact (first with loved ones, then at sensory level)
- Religious ecstasy (varying by intensity of conditioning)
- Other desires arising from unusual individual experiences

**Monroe's approach:**
- Do not label desires as evil or wrong
- Recognize them as deep, integral parts of personality
- Cannot be thought away through denial
- Must accept them as fundamental self-elements
- Keep conscious, reasoning self dominant
- Set desires aside for the moment rather than eliminate them
- Offer promise of future fulfillment—desires can understand diversion

"You cannot eliminate them, but you can set them aside for the moment. Offer the promise of future fulfillment, and you will have no resistance."

Monroe illustrates with the "Gene Autry love scene" analogy: instead of denying sexual desire, he delayed it with a promise to address it later, enabling focus on the task at hand.

**Advancement Criterion:** After dealing with desires successfully 5-7 times in near-separation condition, one is ready for distant and specific voyages.

### Stage 6: Distant Target Visitation

Prerequisites:
- Overcome most fears through repeated exercises
- Successfully managed subjective desires
- Demonstrate 5-7 successful local separations

**Critical Rules:**
- "You must 'go' to a person, not to a place."
- Select a living person well known to you
- Do not inform the target person of the attempt
- This rule prevents suggestion contamination

**Procedure:**
1. Select target person before entering vibrational state
2. Establish relaxation and vibrational state
3. Separate using chosen method
4. Move away to near distance (6-7 feet from physical)
5. With vision still in blackness, think of target person—name, personality, character
6. Do not visualize physical appearance; think of inner person
7. Slowly rotate 360 degrees
8. In rotation, feel the right direction—"an intuitive thing, a sureness that attracts you like a gentle magnet"
9. Verify direction by going past it and coming back
10. Mentally command vision and begin seeing
11. Use the "stretching" movement to propel toward target

**Movement Method—The Diver Position:**
Monroe's most effective technique involves positioning non-physical arms overhead with thumbs latched together "like a diver about to plunge into water." In this position:
- Think of the target person
- Stretch the Second Body in that direction
- Faster stretching produces faster movement
- Movement automatically stops when arrival is reached

**Return Method:**
- Think of physical body
- Reach out and stretch toward physical location
- Return occurs promptly, usually requiring no additional steps

## Return Techniques

### Normal Return
1. Feel direction and location of physical body
2. Return in calm, willful manner with no urgency
3. Think of the physical location
4. If using lift-out method: return is relatively simple; exact alignment enables movement of physical body
5. If using rotation method: move slowly toward physical, then rotate back 180 degrees to conjunction
6. Both methods produce a "slight, click-like jerk" when conjunction with physical is re-established
7. Wait moments before sitting up to avoid uneasiness
8. Sit up physically to demonstrate continuity

### Emergency Return Signal

For situations of difficulty or disorientation, Monroe provides an "infallible" backup technique:

**Formula:** "To return to the physical from wherever you are, think of your physical body. Mentally begin to move some part of your physical body."

**Specific actions:**
- Move a finger or toe
- Take a deep, deliberate breath of air
- Reactivate any of the five physical senses
- Move jaw, swallow, or move tongue
- Any act involving physical motion or physical energy

**Characteristics:**
- Return is virtually immediate when engaged
- Acts as "an automatic direction finder and rocket blast combined"
- Reintegration seems instantaneous
- However, eliminates power of choice or decision
- Should be thought of as "emergency reserve measure rather than a consistent step"

Monroe emphasizes: "Without question, some such thought action will bring you back into the physical. It is merely a question of which one works best with you."

## Consciousness and Continuity

Monroe stresses the importance of maintaining unbroken conscious awareness throughout the cycle. The ability to "separate, return to the physical, sit up and note the time, go back to the separation process, and return to the physical a second time, all without loss of conscious continuity" marks completion of the familiarization stage.

Clock notation serves as objective verification of time passage during separation, providing evidence of actual experience rather than dream or imagination.

## Thought Control and Mental Focus

Throughout the Separation Process, Monroe repeatedly emphasizes thought control as critical. Stray thoughts trigger responses, unintended movements, and misdirected journeys to unfamiliar locations. However, he acknowledges: "I suspect that one is never completely free of such misdirection. At least I have not been."
