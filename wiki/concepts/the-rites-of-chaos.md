## Definition from Source

The Rites of Chaos are five formal ritual structures presented in the *Psychonaut* section of
[[Liber Null & Psychonaut (Carroll)]] as the ceremonial toolkit of the chaos priest. Carroll
describes them as "based on the principles of gnostic new aeon shamanism" and designed to "cover
most situations that the priests of chaos may encounter." The five rites are:

1. **The Mass of Chaos** — a general sacramental rite for invocation, evocation, enchantment, or
   consecration. It has six minimum parts: preparation, statement of intent, invocation of chaos,
   invocation of Baphomet, oath, and closing.
2. **Initiation** — for admitting candidates into inner orders. Carroll insists no fixed formula
   exists; formal initiations must include an Ordeal, an Empowerment, an Induction, and critically,
   The Unexpected — an engineered surprise that "overturn[s] expectations."
3. **Exorcism** — applicable to psychic infestations of persons, places, or objects. Carroll
   distinguishes religious, psychiatric, and shamanic methods, advocating the shamanic approach of
   invoking and personifying the candidate's demons.
4. **Extreme Unction** — last rites applicable to dying or dead beings of any species, framing
   death as an initiatory event and the magician as psychopomp.
5. **Ordination** — recognition by peers that a practitioner can administer all of the above rites
   and discourse wisely on magic and mysticism.

Each rite is given "in a general form for adaptation as circumstance demands" and may be performed
by a single operator or by many participants.

### The Mass of Chaos in Detail

The Mass of Chaos is the central sacrament. Preparation includes "making ready of the site, the
erection of circles and triangles, the placing of instruments and weapons, and the administration
of any chemical or botanical elixirs." The statement of intent is intoned by the officiating
priest: "It is our will ___." The invocation of chaos uses barbarous incantation and visualization
of the eight-rayed star of chaos. Baphomet — "the representation of the terrestrial life-current"
in a "horned theriomorphic deity of androgynous, winged, reptilian, mammalian, and human aspect"
— is then invoked by the priest or priestess, who arouses the kundalini/chi/firesnake from within.
The culminating Oath is "This Is My Will," followed by consumption or concealment of the material
basis and a closing banishing.
