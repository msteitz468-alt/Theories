## Definition / Characterization from Source

The CIA's principal Cold War drug and mind-control program, authorized by director [[Allen Dulles]] in April 1953 (three days after his "brain warfare" speech), proposed by Richard Helms, and run by chemist [[Sidney Gottlieb]] within the Technical Services Staff. Per *Acid Dreams*, MK-ULTRA grew out of the earlier BLUEBIRD (1950) and ARTICHOKE (1951) projects and "quickly grew into a mammoth undertaking," encompassing LSD self-experimentation, surprise dosing of CIA personnel, the [[Operation Midnight Climax dosed unwitting citizens through CIA safehouse brothels|Midnight Climax]] safehouse experiments on unwitting citizens, CIA-funded academic research on captive subjects, and operational use abroad. Gottlieb defined its purpose as "to investigate whether and how it was possible to modify an individual's behavior by covert means." Many records were destroyed; what survived was declassified after a 1977 FOIA request and examined in the 1977 Kennedy Senate hearings.
