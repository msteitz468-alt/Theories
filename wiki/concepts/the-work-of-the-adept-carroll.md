## Definition from Source

*Liber AOM* ("The Work of the Adept") presents the second tier of Carroll's magical curriculum, superseding the initiate grade of *Liber NOX*. Carroll describes the adept as having risen "above the use of all symbolic systems save reality itself." The "playthings of the initiate — sigils, gods, demons, and the instruments of the sorcerer — become reabsorbed or retained only for teaching purposes."

The adept's work is organized under five headings (per Figure 12 in the source): **Aetherics**, **Transubstantiation**, **The Chaosphere**, **Aeonics**, and **Reincarnation**.

**Aetherics** — The adept develops direct control over aetheric forces at two levels. *Dense aether*, residing "in the belly just above the navel," enables gross feats such as levitation, fire-walking, extraordinary strength, and healing. *Subtle aether* enables telepathy, mind influence, projection of invisibility, and the projection of charisma ("an aura of fascination"). For more on the aetheric medium, see [[The Aether (Carroll)]].

**Transubstantiation** — The penultimate metamorphosis: attaining "constant magical consciousness." Carroll describes a dual discipline: complete identification with one's actions while acting ("Be In It / Identify Completely / Live It"), and residing in the void in all interstices between action. This dual awareness is held to confer "complete freedom from the consequences of one's actions."

**The Chaosphere** — A "purposely created crack in the fabric of reality through which the stuff of chaos enters our dimension." The adept constructs this psychic instrument and charges it with paradox-modulated aetheric life force. Carroll states it "will begin to supply raw chaos force to anyone coming into physical proximity with it" and that its erection at various points on earth "will tend to hasten the immanentization of the eschaton, the change of aeon."

**Aeonics** — Adepts are held to act as "agents of the fifth aeon Illuminati," working to build the technologies of the new aeon and to create "cults, orders, covens, and cabals to spread the secret wisdom." See [[The Five Aeons (Carroll's aeonics)]].

**Reincarnation** — The supreme work: *integral reincarnation*, carrying the self intact through death into a new body. Carroll presents three forms — the Red Rite (reincarnation through offspring), the Black Rite (spirit possession of an already-inhabited body), and the White Rite (aetheric transfer into a randomly selected fetus during astral flight).

Carroll frames the adept's ultimate goal as becoming "a center of creation and destruction, a living manifestation of the chaos force within the realm of duality, a complete microcosm, a god" — achieved by playing the light (expansive, creative) and dark (contracting, dissolving) forces of chaos against each other, rather than following either exclusively as in mysticism.
