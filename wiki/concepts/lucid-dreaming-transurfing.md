## Definition from Source

In the Transurfing model, lucid dreaming is the practice of becoming consciously aware within a dream — recognizing that one is dreaming while the dream continues. Zeland presents it specifically as a training ground for accessing [[Outer Intention]], since the dream environment (the [[Dream Space (Transurfing)|dream space]] = [[Alternatives Space]]) allows direct manipulation of the script through outer intention.

"A dream is like a play, and the script...is chosen by the dreamer's outer intention." (Ch. VII)

## Technique (from source)

The induction method described: "Train mind to ask 'Is this real?' 10+ times/day." (Ch. VII) — the habit of questioning reality during waking carries into the dream state, triggering awareness.

## Risk (from source)

The source explicitly warns of a risk: "risk of soul entering physically manifest sector and not returning." (Ch. VII) If the dreaming soul enters a fully realized sector of the alternatives space — a sector with maximum physical density — it may be unable to return to the waking body.

"Soul will not necessarily return if it flies into a realized sector of alternatives space." (Ch. VII Summary)

## As a Model for Outer Intention (from source)

The lucid dream is used as the primary model for understanding outer intention in waking life:
- "Outer intention only imagines an event to be true and accepts the possibility of a different script" (Ch. VII, Game Script section)
- The four types of inner intention response in a dream (flight, fight, awakening, realization) map onto waking responses to challenging situations
- The Guardian role — switching from immersed participant to witness — is described in the same terms for both dreaming and waking
