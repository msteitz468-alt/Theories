## Definition / Characterization from Source

In the tablets, the Halls of Amenti are a hidden realm "deep in Earth's heart," "far 'neath the islands of sunken Atlantis" — both "Halls of the Dead and halls of the living" — created and shielded by the Children of Light. At their center burns the Flower (a "ray of great potence"); around it sit the thirty-two Children of Light and the Seven Lords of the Cycles, with "dark Death" as a conquerable figure within. To be "made free of the Halls of Amenti" is to escape the cycle of death.

Doreal's preface notes that "in legend, the Halls of Amenti became the underworld, the Halls of the gods, where the soul passed after death for judgment." The Great Pyramid is said to be built over the entrance to Amenti.
