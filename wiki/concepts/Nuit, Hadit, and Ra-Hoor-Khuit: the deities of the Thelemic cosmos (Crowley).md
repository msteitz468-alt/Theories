## Definition from Source

The Book of the Law presents a triad of divine principles that together constitute the Thelemic cosmos: **Nuit** (infinite space and the stars), **Hadit** (the infinitely small, inmost point), and **Ra-Hoor-Khuit** (the hawk-headed warrior, the Crowned and Conquering Child). Each speaks one chapter of Liber AL. The Book of Lies ("The Glow-Worm") identifies these three as existing "above The Abyss" and as comprehending "all contradiction in themselves," and states they "are only to be understood by the Master of the Temple."

## Description from Source

### Nuit — Infinite Space

Liber AL Chapter I is voiced by Nuit. She defines herself in verse 22: "I am known to ye by my name Nuit, and…I am Infinite Space, and the Infinite Stars thereof." The injunction she derives from this is "Bind nothing! Let there be no difference made among you between any one thing & any other thing." In verse 27, the prophet addresses her: "let men speak not of Thee as One but as None; and let them speak not of thee at all, since thou art continuous!" Verse 28 renders her nature as: "None, breathed the light, faint & faery, of the stars, and two" — she is simultaneously None and Two. In verse 29: "For I am divided for love's sake, for the chance of union." She is the circumference; her body is the vault of the sky (I:32, I:64). Her number is 11, and 56 (I:24, I:60). Nuit promises "unimaginable joys on earth: certainty, not faith, while in life, upon death; peace unutterable, rest, ecstasy" (I:58).

### Hadit — The Inmost Point

Chapter II is voiced by Hadit. He opens: "I, Hadit, am the complement of Nu, my bride. I am not extended, and Khabs is the name of my House" (II:2). His spatial relationship to Nuit is described in II:3: "In the sphere I am everywhere the centre, as she, the circumference, is nowhere found." He declares: "I am the flame that burns in every heart of man, and in the core of every star. I am Life, and the giver of Life, yet therefore is the knowledge of me the knowledge of death" (II:6). He is "the axle of the wheel, and the cube in the circle" (II:7). He identifies as "the Snake that giveth Knowledge & Delight" (II:22). His numerical nature is given in II:15: "I am perfect, being Not; and my number is nine by the fools; but with the just I am eight, and one in eight: Which is vital, for I am none indeed." Hadit teaches antinomian doctrine, instructing adherents to transcend reason ("the pit called Because," II:27–33) and to embrace existence as "pure joy" (II:9). The chapter contains martial and antinomian passages — including injunctions to "stamp down the wretched & the weak" (II:21, presented as the text's doctrine, not endorsed here) — framed as expressions of the Thelemic law of strength and the rejection of pity as weakness.

### Ra-Hoor-Khuit — The Crowned and Conquering Child

Chapter III is voiced by Ra-Hoor-Khuit, who declares in verse 3: "I am a god of War and of Vengeance." He announces he has "taken his seat in the East at the Equinox of the Gods" (I:49, spoken by Nuit of him). He is described as the "Hawk-Headed Lord of Silence & of Strength" (III:70) and "the Lord of the Double Wand of Power" (III:72). Chapter III contains the most martial content in the text, including commands to "Kill and torture; spare not" (III:18) and highly antinomian passages rejecting other religious traditions (III:50–54) — all presented as the voiced doctrine of the deity within the text, flagged here as such. His sacred word is "Abrahadabra" (III:1, III:75). He also has a dual nature: "The half of the word of Heru-ra-ha, called Hoor-pa-kraat and Ra-Hoor-Khut" (III:35), indicating that Ra-Hoor-Khuit and the silent Harpocrates (Hoor-paar-kraat) are complementary aspects of a single divine being.

### The Triad in the Book of Lies

In "The Glow-Worm," the Book of Lies states: "Nuit, Hadit, Ra-Hoor-Khuit, are only to be understood by the Master of the Temple. They are above The Abyss, and contain all contradiction in themselves." Below them the text describes a "seeming duality of Chaos and Babalon" and identifies the "reflection of All" as Pan.
