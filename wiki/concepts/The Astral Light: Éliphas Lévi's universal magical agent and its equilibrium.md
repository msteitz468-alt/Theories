## Definition and Characterization from the Source
In [[Transcendental Magic (Lévi)]], the Astral Light is Éliphas Lévi's name for the "Great Magical Agent" or "Universal Agent" — a single physical-and-spiritual fluid, "immeasurably more powerful than steam," "having equilibrium for its supreme law," through which all magical, mesmeric, and much natural phenomena operate. Lévi identifies it under many other names — the alchemists' First Matter of the Great Work and Azoth, the Gnostics' "fiery body of the Holy Spirit," the Tetragram, INRI, ETHER, OD, "Magnetic Fluid," "Soul of the Earth," even "Lucifer" — and describes it physically as manifesting through caloric, light, electricity, and magnetism.

The Astral Light is "saturated with all kinds of images or reflections" of everything that has ever existed or been done; it is the medium of dreams, visions, somnambulism, apparitions, and the mechanism by which the Pentagram and other pantacles compel elementary spirits. It is morally neutral in itself — "it lends itself to good as to evil; it transmits light and propagates darkness; it may be called equally Lucifer and Lucifuge" — and is imagined as a serpent devouring its own tail, subject always to the law of two contrary movements (attraction/projection) which the initiate must master through the faculty of imagination (the Diaphane or Translucid) and a disciplined will.
