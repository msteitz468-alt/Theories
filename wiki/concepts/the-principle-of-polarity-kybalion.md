## Definition from Source

The Principle of Polarity is the Fourth of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]] as taught in [[The Kybalion (Three Initiates, 1908)]]. The Three Initiates state its maxim as:

> "Everything is dual; everything has poles; everything has its pair of opposites; like and unlike are the same; opposites are identical in nature, but different in degree; extremes meet; all truths are but half-truths; all paradoxes may be reconciled." — THE KYBALION.

The Kybalion teaches that all manifested things have "two sides," "two aspects," "two poles," a "pair of opposites" with manifold degrees between the two extremes. The crucial Hermetic insight is that apparent opposites are not different in kind but only in degree — they are the two poles of the same thing. Heat and Cold, Light and Darkness, Love and Hate, Courage and Fear, Good and Evil: all are presented as pairs of poles on a single continuous scale, not as categorically different substances.

The Three Initiates extend this principle across all planes: on the physical plane (temperature, musical scale, color spectrum), on the mental plane (emotional states), and on the metaphysical plane (Spirit and Matter as the two poles of the same substance, separated only by degree of vibration).

Because opposites share the same nature, the Kybalion teaches that Mental Transmutation is possible along any such scale — one can "slide" from one pole toward the other by changing the rate of vibration. This is the foundation of the Hermetic art of [[Mental Transmutation (Kybalion)]].
