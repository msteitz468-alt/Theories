## Definition from Source

Hofstadter's "basic element" of the paranoid style: its central image is "a vast and sinister
conspiracy, a gigantic and yet subtle machinery of influence set in motion to undermine and destroy a
way of life." The distinguishing feature is not noticing that conspiracies *occur* in history, but
treating a "vast" or "gigantic" conspiracy as **the motive force of history itself** — "History is a
conspiracy, set in motion by demonic forces of almost transcendent power."

## Description from Source

The enemy is "a perfect model of malice, a kind of amoral superman" — sinister, ubiquitous, cruel,
luxury-loving — and crucially a *free agent* who "manufactures the mechanism of history himself"
(makes crises, starts runs on banks, causes depressions). The style is **apocalyptic**: the paranoid
"traffics in the birth and death of whole worlds," lives at a perpetual turning point ("time is
running out"), and demands not compromise but an all-out crusade and total victory over an absolutely
evil foe. This is the recurring shape of the conspiracy claims catalogued across the wiki.
