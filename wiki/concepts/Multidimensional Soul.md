## Concept Definition

The **Multidimensional Soul** (or **Multidimensional Self**) is Seth's model of human identity. Rather than a singular, static entity bound to a physical body, the soul is a dynamic, evolving "gestalt of consciousness" that exists simultaneously in many dimensions, realities, and times.

The physical personality or "ego" that individuals experience in daily life is not the entirety of their self, but rather a temporary portion of the complete multidimensional identity focused intensely on three-dimensional reality.

## Core Characteristics

* **Simultaneous Reincarnation (The Spacious Present):** Reincarnational lifetimes do not occur in a linear sequence of past, present, and future. Instead, all lifetimes exist and are lived simultaneously in a "spacious present." The multidimensional self coordinates and experiences all of these incarnations at once.
* **Name Nomenclature (Ruburt and Joseph):** Seth refers to Jane Roberts as "Ruburt" and her husband Robert Butts as "Joseph." These names represent their entire multidimensional identities, spanning all their incarnations and non-physical aspects, as distinct from their physical personalities.
* **Lack of Boundaries:** The soul is not locked inside the physical skull or limited by the physical body. It communicates telepathically, travels during sleep and out-of-body states, and creates its own physical environment as a form of expression.
* **Evolving Gestalts (Seth Two):** Consciousness forms hierarchical networks. Just as human souls are gestalts of many personalities, Seth himself is part of a larger entity. He describes communicating with "Seth Two"—a highly evolved version of his own entity operating from a higher dimension of reality.
* **All-That-Is:** Every multidimensional soul is supported, maintained, and created by the inconceivable vitality of "All-That-Is" (Seth's term for the ultimate, infinite gestalt of consciousness). All souls share in the creative power of All-That-Is.
