## Overview

The **Lemegeton**, or **Lesser Key of Solomon**, is not a single work but a seventeenth-century
**compilation of five independent treatises** of ritual magic, each attributed to King [[Solomon]] and
each with its own spirit-hierarchy, ritual apparatus, and aim. As presented in
[[The Lesser Key of Solomon (Peterson)]], the five books are:

- **[[The Ars Goetia: the evocation and constraint of the 72 spirits of Solomon|Ars Goetia]]** — commanding 72 named spirits, "both good and evil," bound by Solomon in a brass vessel.
- **[[The Theurgia-Goetia and Ars Paulina: aerial spirits, planetary hours, and the angels of the zodiac|Theurgia-Goetia]]** — 31 aerial spirits of the compass, of mixed nature.
- **Ars Paulina** — the angels governing the 24 hours of the day and the 360 degrees of the zodiac (grouped with Theurgia-Goetia in the concept above).
- **[[The Almadel and Ars Notoria: the wax altar of the four Altitudes and the prayer-art of instant knowledge|Ars Almadel]]** — the angels of the four Altitudes, contacted through a wax tablet.
- **[[The Almadel and Ars Notoria: the wax altar of the four Altitudes and the prayer-art of instant knowledge|Ars Notoria]]** — the oldest component, a prayer-and-figure art for instantly gaining the arts and sciences.

The system spans the full moral range of medieval–early-modern spirit magic, from the coercive demonology
of the Goetia to the devotional theurgy of the Ars Notoria and Almadel. Peterson also documents its
lineage, appending Johann Weyer's *Pseudomonarchia Daemonum* as a direct ancestor of the Goetia.

## Constituent doctrines

- [[The Lemegeton is a 17th-century compilation of five distinct magical treatises attributed to Solomon]]
- [[The Ars Goetia catalogs 72 spirits — kings, dukes, princes, marquises, earls, and presidents — bound by Solomon in a brass vessel]]
- [[Goetic evocation compels spirits within a protective circle and triangle by divine names and the threat of the brass vessel]]
- [[The Theurgia-Goetia governs 31 aerial spirits (chief princes) of mixed good and evil nature, tied to the points of the compass]]
- [[The Ars Paulina divides magic into the angels of the 24 hours of the day and the angels ruling the 360 degrees of the zodiac]]
- [[The Almadel Art contacts the angels of the four Altitudes (Choras) through a white wax tablet and candles]]
- [[The Ars Notoria promises supernatural mastery of the arts and sciences through prescribed orations and figures (notae)]]
- [[Weyer's Pseudomonarchia Daemonum is an earlier catalogue of demons and their offices, a direct textual ancestor of the Goetia]]
