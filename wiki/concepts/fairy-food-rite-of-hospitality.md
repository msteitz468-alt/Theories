The folklore motif that food links humans and the Other: one must not eat fairy food or be unable to return; fairy food is saltless; and the exchange of food (Simonton's water-for-pancakes, biblical hospitality to angels) constitutes a sacred bond. Vallée uses Hartland's analysis of the rite of hospitality to read the Eagle River exchange as a ceremony rather than a sample collection.
