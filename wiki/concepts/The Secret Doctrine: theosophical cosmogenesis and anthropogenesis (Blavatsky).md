## Definition from Source

The umbrella framework of *The Secret Doctrine*: an ancient "wisdom-religion" — held by Blavatsky to be
the single hidden source of all the world's religions, philosophies, and (true) sciences — expounded as
a vast scheme of **cosmogenesis** (the evolution of the cosmos out of an absolute One, through cyclic
periods, guided by hierarchies of conscious powers) and **anthropogenesis** (the evolution of humanity
through seven Root-Races). It is offered as a commentary on the supposedly archaic **Stanzas of Dzyan**.

## Description from Source

The system's components (each on its own page): the
[[The Three Fundamental Propositions of the Secret Doctrine (Blavatsky)|Three Fundamental Propositions]];
[[The Stanzas of Dzyan and cosmic evolution (Blavatsky)|the Stanzas of Dzyan / cosmic evolution]] (the One,
Pralaya/Manvantara cycles, the Dhyan-Chohans / septenary hierarchies, down to the appearance of Man);
the claim that [[The Secret Doctrine as the one wisdom-religion underlying all faiths (Blavatsky)|the Secret Doctrine is the one wisdom-religion underlying all faiths]];
[[Occult science versus materialist science (Blavatsky)|occult science vs. materialist science]];
the [[The seven Root-Races and the evolution of humanity (Blavatsky)|seven Root-Races]] and
[[Lemuria and Atlantis as lost Root-Race continents (Blavatsky)|Lemuria and Atlantis]];
the re-reading of [[Fallen angels, the Serpent, and Lucifer as misunderstood cosmic forces (Blavatsky)|fallen angels and the Serpent as cosmic forces]];
and [[The great antiquity of man and the critique of Darwinian evolution (Blavatsky)|the great antiquity of man]].

As a primary root it feeds [[The Secret Teachings of All Ages (Hall)]], the Emerald Tablets tradition,
the ancient-astronaut and lost-civilization writers, and Icke's root-race material — and is countered on
Atlantis/Lemuria by [[The lost-continent myth: Atlantis and Mu debunked (Feder)]].
