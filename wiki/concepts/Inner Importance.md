## Definition from Source

Inner importance is the overestimation of one's own virtues or shortcomings. It is one of the two forms of [[Excess Potential]] identified in Ch. IV, the other being [[Outer Importance]].

"Inner importance: overestimation of own virtues or shortcomings." (Ch. IV)

## Key Properties (from source)

- **Directed inward**: where outer importance attaches to external objects or events, inner importance attaches to one's self-image — either inflating virtues (pride, arrogance) or magnifying shortcomings (inadequacy, guilt)
- **Creates excess potential**: any overestimation — whether positive or negative — constitutes a departure from equilibrium and generates the energetic imbalance that triggers [[Law of Balance]] counter-forces
- **Slides as inner importance**: [[Slides (Transurfing)]] are explicitly rooted in inner importance; the slide lives in one's head "because your appearance does not matter to anyone else for any particular reason" — the slide's foundation is the importance attributed to others' opinions of oneself

## The Guardian and Inner Importance (from source)

[[The Guardian (Transurfing)]] is the inner witness or observer who monitors inner values and levels of importance, specifically including inner importance. The Guardian role involves noticing when inner importance has been elevated and reducing it through conscious awareness rather than suppression.
