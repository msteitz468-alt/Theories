**Definition/Characterization from the Source**
In Kevin L. Gest's *The Secrets of Solomon's Temple*, "Hiram Abif" — the murdered master builder of Masonic legend, distinct from the biblical craftsman Huram-Abi sent by Hiram, King of Tyre (2 Chronicles 2:13-14) — is treated as an allegorical figure rather than a historical individual. Gest surveys competing identifications proposed by other writers (a link to Jacques de Molay's execution, an idea attributed to Plato, Elias Ashmole's supposed link to Charles I's execution, and Robert Lomas and Christopher Knight's link to Pharaoh Seqenenre Tao II) before proposing his own reading: that "Hiram Abif" personifies the heliocentric model of the solar system, understood since Solomon's era but suppressed as antireligious from roughly 100-150 BCE until its restoration by post-Copernican astronomy, and preserved meanwhile as protected "trade secret" knowledge within operative and speculative Masonry.
