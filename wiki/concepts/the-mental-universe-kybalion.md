## Definition from Source

The Mental Universe is the Kybalion's doctrine that the entire cosmos exists as a mental creation
inside the Infinite Mind of [[THE ALL (Kybalion)]]. The governing maxim:

> "The Universe is Mental—held in the Mind of THE ALL."
> — THE KYBALION

> "THE ALL creates in its Infinite Mind countless Universes, which exist for aeons of Time—and yet,
> to THE ALL, the creation, development, decline and death of a million Universes is as the time of
> the twinkling of an eye."
> — THE KYBALION

> "The Infinite Mind of THE ALL is the womb of Universes."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates arrive at this doctrine by exclusion: the Universe is neither THE ALL itself
(since THE ALL is One and immutable while the Universe is many and changing), nor "Nothing" (since
we are sensible of its existence). The only remaining option, via the Principle of Correspondence
("As above, so below"), is that THE ALL creates mentally: just as a human creates mental images
without consuming external material or subdividing itself, so THE ALL creates the Universe in its
own Mentality.

This teaching carries implications about human identity and destiny. Humans dwell within the
Infinite Mind of THE ALL ("Within the Father-Mother Mind, mortal children are at home"). Death is
not final — "it is but Birth to a new life" — and human destiny is an ascent through higher planes
across "aeons upon aeons of time," eventually returning to conscious unity with THE ALL at "the end
of the Grand Cycle of Aeons."

The chapter also addresses cosmological Gender: THE ALL manifests a Masculine Principle (Will,
standing "apart" from creation) and a Feminine Principle ("Nature," which carries out the actual
evolution of the Universe from "centres of activity" upward). THE ALL itself, however, "is but One"
and is above Gender.
