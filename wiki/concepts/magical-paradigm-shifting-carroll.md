## Definition from Source

In the *Psychonaut* section of [[Liber Null & Psychonaut (Carroll)]], Carroll presents "Magical
Paradigms" as the theoretical core of chaos magic's epistemology: the magician must be able to
"act without belief or being able to temporarily invest belief in anything." A paradigm is defined
as the set of basic postulates through which a culture or individual "interacts with its universe,"
and Carroll argues that "the universe (being the accommodating creature that she is) will tend to
provide confirmation of any paradigm one chooses to live in." This makes paradigm choice a
practical magical act, not merely a philosophical one.

### The Six Paradigms

Carroll presents six "alternative magical paradigms" as a "strange mixture of sorcery and
hyperscience":

1. **The Chaoetheric Paradigm** — the manifest universe is an island of order spewed from infinite
   chaos; a spark of that same chaos at the center of the magician's being enables magic.
2. **Probability Manipulation** — magic operates at the indeterminate point in the genesis of any
   event, engineering coincidences via the "hidden variable" of consciousness.
3. **Morphic Field Theory** — drawing on the hypothesis of formative causation: every event
   generates a morphic field predisposing future similar events. Clairvoyance taps past fields;
   sympathetic magic creates miniature fields to cause desired events.
4. **Observer-Created Universe** — following [[Austin Osman Spare]]'s approach and anticipating
   quantum non-objectivity: will and perception are ultimately identical, and sufficiently deep
   belief creates reality.
5. **The Holographic Universe** — every particle is instantaneously connected to every other
   via hidden channels (quantum inseparability); magic operates as pure information transfer
   through this web.
6. **Higher Dimensionality** — a fifth dimension, accessible to the psyche, could explain all
   occult phenomena: information moved through it "could manifest at any point in ordinary time
   or space."

Carroll notes a preference for the higher dimensionality paradigm, while stressing that all six
are operationally valid. What matters is not which paradigm is metaphysically "true" but which
enables effective magical work in the moment.

### Belief as a Tool

The deeper implication Carroll draws is that belief itself is a magical instrument. He writes that
"the magician must master the art of either acting without belief or being able to temporarily
invest belief in anything." This becomes the basis for [[Chaos Magic]]'s anti-dogmatic stance:
no paradigm is permanently adopted; each is worn like a costume for a specific operation and then
discarded. Carroll maps all paradigms onto a three-level ontology:

- **Primary Reality**: chaos, the void, acausality, quantum realm.
- **Secondary Reality**: aethers, morphic fields, gods, probability.
- **Tertiary Reality**: the physical / material world.

All paradigms share (a) some form of action at a distance and (b) a microcosm–macrocosm
equivalence ("As above, so below").
