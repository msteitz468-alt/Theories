# The Four Worlds of the Qabalah

## Definition from Source
Fortune describes four levels into which the Qabalists divide manifestation, each with its own Sephirothic correspondences (Chapter VIII, §18; Chapter IX):

- **Atziluth**, the Archetypal World, consisting of Kether alone. She rejects Mathers' rendering "Intellectual World" as misleading and insists on "Archetypal" as the exact term. It is "the natural sphere of the Sephiroth as such," the World of Emanations, where God acts directly (not through mediators). It is under the presidency of the Yod of Tetragrammaton, associated with the Wands suit of the Tarot and the element of Fire, and here the Ten Sephiroth carry ten forms of the Divine Name.
- **Briah**, the Creative World, consisting of Chokmah and Binah — the Supernal Abba and Ama, Father and Mother. In Briah, God acts "through the mediation of the Archangels," and each Sephirah here carries the name of a mighty Archangel. Correlated with the Cups suit and the element of Water.
- **Yetzirah**, the Formative World, consisting of the six central Sephiroth: Chesed, Geburah, Tiphareth, Netzach, Hod, and Yesod. Here God acts "through the Angelic Orders," each Sephirah bearing the name of a choir of angels. Correlated with the Swords suit and the element of Air.
- **Assiah**, the Material World, represented by Malkuth alone. Here God acts "through those centres which I have named the Mundane Chakras — the planets, elements, and signs of the Zodiac." Correlated with the Pentacles suit and the element of Earth.

The Sephiroth recur across all Four Worlds on "another arc or level of manifestation": the Malkuth of Atziluth gives rise to the Kether of Briah, and so on down to the Malkuth of Assiah, which in its lowest aspect abuts upon the Qliphoth. To fully understand a Sephirah one must know its correspondences in all Four Worlds plus its correspondences in the four systems of practical occultism (astrology, alchemy's elements, Tarot suits, and the fourfold Zodiac/planet triplicities) — a scheme Fortune compares to the Rosetta Stone.
