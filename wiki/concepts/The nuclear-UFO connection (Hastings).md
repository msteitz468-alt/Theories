## Definition / Characterization from Source

Robert Hastings's thesis that there is a persistent, decades-long pattern of UFO activity concentrated at **nuclear weapons facilities** — ICBM launch-control and missile-storage sites, bomber bases, weapons depots, test ranges, and nuclear power plants — beginning in the late 1940s and continuing into the twenty-first century. Documented through the on-the-record testimony of former military personnel and declassified records, the pattern includes surveillance overflights, hovering disc-shaped craft, and — most dramatically — incidents in which nuclear missiles allegedly malfunctioned or went off-alert during a UFO's presence (the 1967 Malmstrom Echo/Oscar Flight cases).

Hastings interprets the concentration as evidence that the intelligence behind UFOs is **specifically concerned with humanity's nuclear arsenal** — monitoring, and occasionally demonstrating power over, the weapons — and argues the U.S. government has covered up the incidents and discouraged witnesses from speaking. The thesis converges with Luis Elizondo's later claim that UAP monitor nuclear sites and with Richard Dolan's documentation of the November 1975 SAC-base overflights.
