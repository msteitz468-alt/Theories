## Definition from Source

The umbrella framework of the Nag Hammadi texts. **Gnosticism** holds that salvation comes through
**gnosis** (saving knowledge) of one's true divine origin; that the true God is an **unknowable Father**
beyond the cosmos, surrounded by a fullness (**Pleroma**) of aeons; that the material world was made not
by that God but by a lower, ignorant, arrogant **creator-god (Yaldabaoth/the Demiurge)** with his
**archons**, born of a fall within the Pleroma (the error of the aeon **Sophia**); and that a **divine
spark** is trapped in humanity, which the archons seek to keep ignorant and which gnosis awakens and
frees.
