## Definition / Characterization from Source

**World3** is the computer model at the heart of *The Limits to Growth* — a system-dynamics simulation that integrates global population, food production, industrial output, pollution, and nonrenewable resources, tracking their interactions through "stocks and flows and feedbacks and thresholds." It descends from MIT professor **Jay Forrester**'s World2 model (built in response to the Club of Rome's inquiry into interconnected global trends); Forrester is, the authors say, "the intellectual father of the World3 model."

The authors stress World3 generates **scenarios**, not predictions — "10 different pictures of how the twenty-first century may evolve" — to "encourage your learning, reflection, and personal choice." In nearly every business-as-usual run the result is overshoot and collapse; in scenarios that combine technical change with deliberate limits on consumption and family size (e.g. Scenario 9), the model settles into a sustainable world of roughly eight billion people.
