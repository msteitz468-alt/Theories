## Definition from Source

Feder's chapter-length refutation of the **ancient-astronaut thesis** — the claim (popularized by
Erich von Däniken, amplified by *Ancient Aliens* / Giorgio Tsoukalos) that extraterrestrials visited
Earth in the deep past and gave humanity a technological "head start." Feder's answer is "a decided
NO," and the key question he poses is *why archaeologists are skeptical.*

## Description from Source

He traces the idea's modern origin (an audacious 1963 article building on the optimism of the **Drake
equation**) and argues the supposed "evidence" collapses on inspection: artifacts and monuments are
misread, mundane explanations are ignored, and ancient peoples' own ingenuity is denied. The
underlying move — assuming non-Western ancestors *couldn't* have built their own wonders — echoes the
racism of the [[Pre-Columbian contact and the Mound Builder myth assessed (Feder)|Mound Builder myth]].
This directly counters the wiki's ancient-astronaut cluster: [[Chariots of the Gods (von Däniken)]],
[[The Twelfth Planet (Sitchin)]], [[The Sirius Mystery (Temple, 1976)]], [[The Gods of Eden (Bramley)]]
— see also [[Reptilian - Anunnaki 'gods-created-and-rule-humanity' models compared across sources]].
