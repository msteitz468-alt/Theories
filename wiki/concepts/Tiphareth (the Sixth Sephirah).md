# Tiphareth (the Sixth Sephirah)

## Definition from Source
Fortune gives Tiphareth's title as "Tiphareth, Beauty" (Hebrew: Tau, Pe, Aleph, Resh, Tau). Its Magical Images are "a majestic king. A child. A sacrificed god," situated in the centre of the Pillar of Equilibrium (the Central Pillar). Additional titles: Zoar Anpin (the Lesser Countenance), Melekh (the King), Adam, the Son, the Man.

Its Yetziratic Text calls it "the Mediating Intelligence, because in it are multiplied the influxes of the Emanations; for it causes that influence to flow into all the reservoirs of the blessings with which they themselves are united."

Attributions as given: God-Name, Tetragrammaton Aloah Va Daath. Archangel, Raphael. Order of Angels, Malachim (Kings). Mundane Chakra, Shemesh, the Sun. Spiritual Experience, Vision of the Harmony of Things; Mysteries of the Crucifixion. Virtue, Devotion to the Great Work. Vice, Pride. Correspondence in the Microcosm, the breast. Symbols: the Lamen, the Rosy Cross, the Calvary Cross, the truncated pyramid, the cube. Tarot cards, the four Sixes (Victory, Joy, Earned Success, Material Success). Colours: clear rose-pink (Atziluth), yellow (Briah), rich salmon-pink (Yetzirah), golden amber (Assiah).

Fortune identifies three keys to Tiphareth's nature: it is the centre of equilibrium of the whole Tree; it is Kether on a lower arc and Yesod on a higher arc; and it is the point of transmutation between the planes of force and the planes of form. As the Child it is the Place of Incarnation; as the Sacrificed God it bears the Mysteries of the Crucifixion, to which all sacrificed gods of the pantheons (Osiris, Dionysos, etc.) are assigned; as the King it rules Adam Kadmon, the six central Sephiroth. It is called the Christ-centre, the focussing-point of exoteric Christianity, and the sphere of the higher psychism and of the Greater Masters (Chapter XX).
