## Definition from Source

The Principle of Correspondence is the Second of [[The Seven Hermetic Principles]], attributed by the Kybalion to [[Hermes Trismegistus]]. Its governing maxim is:

> "As above, so below; as below, so above."
> — THE KYBALION

The Three Initiates describe it as embodying "the truth that there is a harmony, agreement, and correspondence between the several planes of Manifestation, Life and Being." Because all phenomena in the Universe emanate from the same source (THE ALL), the same laws, principles, and characteristics apply across every unit, combination, and Plane. Correspondence is therefore not mere analogy — it is structural identity across scales.

### Epistemological Function

The Kybalion presents Correspondence as a cognitive tool that allows the knower to move from known to unknown: by studying phenomena on one plane, one can infer truths about another. The Three Initiates illustrate this with the example of THE ALL's creative act resembling the finite artist becoming "wrapped up" in a mental creation — the difference being one of degree, not of kind. "As above, so Below; as Below, so above" licenses this inference.

### Relationship to the Planes

Chapter VIII ("The Planes of Correspondence") is devoted entirely to showing how the Principle manifests across the [[The Planes of Correspondence (Kybalion)|Three Great Planes]] (Physical, Mental, Spiritual). All Seven Hermetic Principles — Mentalism, Correspondence, Vibration, Polarity, Rhythm, Cause and Effect, Gender — operate on every Plane simultaneously. The Principle of Correspondence is what makes this universality intelligible.

### Limits Noted by the Source

The Kybalion acknowledges that Correspondence reaches only to "The Aspect of BECOMING" in THE ALL. "Back of that Aspect is 'The Aspect of BEING,' in which all Laws are lost in LAW; all Principles merge into PRINCIPLE." The question of why THE ALL creates cannot be answered by Correspondence alone.
