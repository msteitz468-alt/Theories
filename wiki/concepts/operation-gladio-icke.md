## Definition and Characterisation (from the Source)

In *Everything You Need to Know But Have Never Been Told* (2018), Operation Gladio is described by David Icke as a documented case proving that Western intelligence agencies conduct deliberate civilian terrorist attacks for political purposes. The name came "from a Roman word meaning 'stay behind.'"

Key facts as stated in Chapter 14:
- Created post-WWII by NATO and the CIA
- Based in Italy, expanding across Europe
- Official justification: preparation for armed resistance to Soviet Warsaw Pact invasion
- Real purpose: civilian terrorism to make populations demand more state security and prevent leftward political shifts

Primary documented source cited: Vincenzo Vinciguerra, serving a life sentence for the 1972 Peteano car bomb that killed three Italian police officers. His trial testimony (quoted directly): "You had to attack civilians, the people, women, children, innocent people, unknown people far from any political game. The reason was quite simple. They were supposed to force these people, the Italian public, to turn to the state to ask for greater security."

Vinciguerra further described "a secret organisation, a super-organisation with a network of communications, arms and explosives, and men trained to use them" that, "lacking a Soviet military invasion," took up the task "of preventing a slip to the left in the political balance of the country." He described terrorism "within the state itself" through the "Atlantic Alliance."

Named terrorist groups described as mobilised by this structure: Avanguardia Nazionale ('National Vanguard') and Ordine Nuovo ('New Order').

Icke uses Gladio as the documented historical precedent for his broader claim that modern ISIS/al-Qaeda terrorism is similarly state-orchestrated.
