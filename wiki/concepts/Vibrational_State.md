## Definition and Characterization from Source

According to [[Robert Monroe (1915–1995)|Robert A. Monroe]] in *[[Journeys Out of the Body]]*, the "vibrational state" is a distinct physical and energetic phenomenon that serves as the prerequisite gateway to out-of-body experience. Monroe describes it as "a surging, hissing, rhythmically pulsating wave of fiery sparks" that comes roaring into the head and sweeps throughout the body, making it "rigid and immobile."

The vibrational state is not a hallucination or imagination but rather a repeatable, objective phenomenon that Monroe treats as foundational to the separation process. He emphasizes that "there is no evidence to indicate that this vibrational state has a deleterious effect on either the mind or the physical body."

## Induction Techniques

### Basic Method
Monroe provides detailed instructions for establishing the vibrational state in Chapter 16:

1. **Preliminary affirmation**: State mentally five times: "I am dreaming. I want to remember and observe all of those matters that will be beneficial to my physical and mental being."

2. **Breathing**: Begin breathing through a half-opened mouth.

3. **Mental concentration**: Establish two imaginary lines extending from the outer sides of the closed eyes, converging at a point a foot away from the forehead. Visualize resistance or pressure where these lines meet, "as if two charged electric wires were joined, or poles of a magnet forced together."

4. **Distance extension**: Extend the convergence point outward to about three feet, maintaining the angular pressure pattern.

5. **Further extension**: Extend to six feet away (approximately 30 degrees).

6. **Directional shift**: Bend the intersection point 90 degrees upward, parallel to the body axis, above the head.

7. **Reaching and activation**: "Reach" or stretch with this point of intersection until obtaining a reaction—the characteristic surge of vibrations.

### Habituation
Once the technique is learned, Monroe notes that the full routine becomes unnecessary. A conditioned reflex develops: "You need only to think of the vibrations while in a relaxed state, and they come into being." This represents establishment of a "neuron path that can be followed again and again."

Monroe cautions that success is not guaranteed on first attempt. "The probability of success increases with each successive effort. The more often you attempt it, the more likely you are to have positive results."

## Physical Characteristics

### Frequency and Modulation
Monroe estimates the base vibrational rate at approximately 27 cycles per second. However, the frequency can be deliberately increased through mental "pulsing." 

Early in experience, vibrations may feel "rough" or as if "your body is being severely shaken right down to the molecular or atomic level." This roughness can be smoothed through frequency increase ("pulsing").

As frequency accelerates, the sensory effect transforms:
- Initial: rough, shaking sensation
- Intermediate: steady, solid effect
- Advanced: perceptible only at onset, then auto-accelerating until imperceptible
- Final stage: sensation of body warmth with slight tingling

### Control Methods
Chapter 17 provides detailed control procedures:

1. **Acclimatization**: Allow oneself to acclimate to the condition without panic. The best approach is to "do nothing" and let vibrations fade naturally (typically 5 minutes). This eliminates fear responses.

2. **Manipulation**: Once fear is eliminated, mentally direct vibrations into a ring or push them down the body to toes, then back up. Create rhythmic head-to-toe waves, allowing momentum to continue for approximately 10 seconds per circuit until fade-out.

3. **Frequency adjustment**: Practice until waves begin instantly upon mental command and maintain steady movement. Mentally "pulse" to increase frequency, smoothing the rough sensation.

4. **Objective assessment**: Monroe states that "consistent achievement of this stage is the sign that you are ready for the first physical disassociation experiments."

## Relationship to Separation

Monroe emphasizes the critical connection: "It is essential that you learn and apply this speed-up process. The faster vibration effect is the form that permits disassociation from the physical."

The vibrational state appears to be a necessary condition for consciousness to disassociate from the physical body. Once the vibrations reach sufficient frequency (described as accelerating "like a motor starting up" until the frequency is "so high that you are unable to perceive it"), the conditions for separation are met.

## Consciousness and Thought Sensitivity

A crucial characteristic is that in the vibrational state, one "is apparently subject to every thought, both willful and involuntary, that crosses your mind." Monroe stresses the importance of thought control: one must maintain "no thought" or "single thought" (concentration) focus. 

Any stray idea will produce immediate response, sometimes in undesirable manner. Monroe acknowledges: "I suspect that one is never completely free of such misdirection. At least I have not been."

## Implications and Caveats

Monroe notes an important watershed: "Beyond this point, I believe you cannot turn back. Ultimately, you will be committed to the reality of this other existence."

He elaborates: "Once you have been 'opened' to this other reality, you cannot completely shut it out again, try as you might." The vibrational state may recur spontaneously during sleep or relaxation without deliberate invocation, and eventually most practitioners "become too tired to bother" resisting and proceed with separation.

## Variables and Inconsistencies

Monroe documents that the vibrational state is not always repeatable at will despite initial success. "There are still many variables that interfere which have yet to be isolated and identified. But it does 'work' often enough to be subject to continued study."
