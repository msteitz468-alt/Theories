## Dispute as Presented in Source

Jacobsen records Bob Lazar's detailed claims about S-4 and alien technology alongside extensive scrutiny of his background (MIT denial, pandering arrest, sold film rights) and Stanton Friedman's outright fraud accusation.

## Positions

### Lazar (via Jacobsen's attribution)
Maintained his S-4 story; passed lie detector tests inconclusively but with belief in his sincerity per test administrator.

### Friedman (quoted)
"Bob Lazar is a total fraud" — cannot accept claims from someone who falsified credentials.

### Jacobsen (narrator framing)
Presents both positions without adjudicating. Notes Hayakawa filmed unexplained lights as Lazar predicted. Concludes chapter saying Lazar and Friedman "might have realized how close to the truth—something far more earthly and shocking than anyone could have imagined—they both were" but does not specify that truth in Chapter 1.
