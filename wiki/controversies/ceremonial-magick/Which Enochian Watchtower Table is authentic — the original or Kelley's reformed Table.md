Brand calls this "the most fundamental source of argument in all Enochian Magick." The Watchtower
Table is the grid from which the Divine Names, angel names, and sigils are drawn, so its accuracy
matters greatly. Per the source, Dee made early errors he corrected, but years later the Table was
"reformed" under bizarre circumstances: [[Edward Kelley]], wanting to quit, asked the angels to
release him; they responded with frightening ultimatums and ultimately the instruction that Dee and
Kelley swap wives — after which Kelley "disappeared upstairs for a while and came back with" a new
Table. This left occultists divided over which Table to trust. Brand records the dispute and the
suspicion that "Kelley dreamt up a new Table merely to deceive Dee," but declines to adjudicate it,
holding that the magic functions regardless.
