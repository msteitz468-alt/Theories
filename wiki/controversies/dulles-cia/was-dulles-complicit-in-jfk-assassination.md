## Nature of the Dispute

*The Devil's Chessboard* advances, but does not claim to prove, that Allen Dulles helped organize the assassination of President Kennedy and then steered the Warren Commission cover-up. Within the source itself there is notable tension between the strength of the thesis and the strength of the evidence Talbot is able to present.

## Positions Presented Within the Source

**1. The deep-state thesis (Talbot's argument).** A consensus formed among intelligence/financial/military elites that Kennedy "must be removed"; Dulles alone had the stature to do it; the Hunt deathbed account, Oswald's "patsy" profile, the suppressed CIA photo analysis, and Dulles's role on the Warren Commission form a circumstantial pattern. See [[The Devil's Chessboard argues a deep state removed President Kennedy]], [[E. Howard Hunt's deathbed account named a CIA plot to kill Kennedy]], [[Lee Harvey Oswald showed the signs of an intelligence "patsy"]], [[Allen Dulles steered the Warren Commission to a lone-gunman conclusion]].

**2. Talbot's own caveats.** He writes that Hunt's confession was "carefully parsed" and self-exculpatory, naming Harvey and Morales but omitting Dulles; that it is "simply not credible" LBJ dealt directly with someone of Harvey's rank; that "there is no evidence" corporate figures like David Rockefeller had foreknowledge; and that the assassination thesis rests on circumstantial pattern, not a confession naming Dulles.

**3. The official record (as reported by Talbot).** The Warren Report concluded Oswald acted alone; the 1979 House Select Committee on Assassinations found a conspiracy "involving Oswald and other unknown parties" but did not name Dulles or the CIA. HSCA chief counsel G. Robert Blakey continued to believe Oswald was guilty.
