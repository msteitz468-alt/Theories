## Nature of the Dispute

The ingested sources directly contradict each other on who among Wall Street financiers backed the Bolshevik Revolution — and specifically on the role of [[Jacob Schiff]] of [[Kuhn, Loeb & Co.]]

## Position 1 — Schiff financed the Bolsheviks (Griffin, Mullins)

- [[The Creature from Jekyll Island (Griffin)]]: Jacob Schiff "was one of the principal backers of the Bolshevik revolution" and "personally financed Trotsky's trip from New York to Russia." (See [[Wall Street financiers financed the Bolshevik Revolution]].)
- The Mullins works place Schiff/Kuhn-Loeb at the center of the New York banking group financing revolution and the Federal Reserve, as Rothschild agents.

## Position 2 — Schiff opposed the Bolsheviks; Morgan-Rockefeller promoted them (Sutton)

- [[Wall Street and the Bolshevik Revolution (Sutton)]]: Sutton cites State Department documents confirming "that the investment banker Jacob Schiff, often cited as a source of funds for the Bolshevik Revolution, was in fact against support of the Bolshevik regime. This position ... was in direct contrast to the Morgan-Rockefeller promotion of the Bolsheviks."
- Sutton attributes the documented financial support to the Morgan-controlled [[Guaranty Trust Company]] and the 120 Broadway syndicate, not to Kuhn, Loeb. (See [[The Morgan-controlled Guaranty Trust financed the Bolsheviks]].)

## The Jewish-Conspiracy Framing

Sutton (Appendix II) explicitly **rejects** the "Jewish-conspiracy theory" — examining the State Department file "Bolshevism and Judaism," Churchill's 1920 "Zionism Versus Bolshevism," and the *Protocols of Zion* material — and concludes: "There is no concrete evidence that Jews were involved in the Bolshevik Revolution because they were Jewish.... we must reject the story as spurious." He notes the proportion of foreign Jewish Bolsheviks was "less than twenty percent," most later "deported, murdered, or sent to Siberia." (See [[Sutton rejects the Jewish-conspiracy theory of the Bolshevik Revolution]].)

## Status

Per the wiki's contradiction protocol, both positions are recorded without adjudication. Note that Sutton — the most documentary of the sources, working from primary State Department records — directly disputes the Schiff claim repeated by Griffin and Mullins, and rejects the ethnic framing entirely.
